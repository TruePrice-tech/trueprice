Concrete Driveway Cost in Alabama (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in Alabama typically run $3,200–$8,800 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 3000 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 6-inch design frost line, and the state sits in no freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F0). Alabama's combination of red-clay subsoil across Birmingham, Tuscaloosa, and Montgomery requires aggressive 4-to-6-inch crushed-stone gravel base under every driveway pour to prevent clay-driven slab heave during seasonal moisture cycles — and Mobile-Baldwin coastal humidity drives rapid set times on every Gulf Coast pour, with retarder admixtures standard on every Alabama coastal residential pour during May-September high-humidity season.

Alabama climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 6-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F0 — no freeze-thaw exposure, no air-entrainment required
  • Typical residential mix: 3000 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: Moderate — periodic clay shrink-swell, standard footing reinforcement adequate
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC C — moderate seismic risk, continuous-rebar-tie footings required
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: Low — minimal winter de-icer use

Alabama licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Alabama Home Builders Licensure Board (residential >$10,000) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for driveway > 200 sq ft on new construction; HBLB residential license required above $10,000 contract

How concrete driveway costs vary in Alabama

State-specific code or insurance rule: Alabama Code §34-14A-5 makes the Alabama Home Builders Licensure Board the only state body that licenses residential concrete contractors above the $10,000 contract threshold — and the HBLB requires a Limited Specialty Construction credential with a separate examination on flatwork, footings, and post-tension foundation work, plus Alabama is one of the only Southeast states requiring a separate Alabama Department of Environmental Management Stormwater NOI on every concrete pour above 1 acre disturbed area for residential subdivisions.
