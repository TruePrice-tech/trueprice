Landscape Installation Cost in Alabama (2026)

Landscape installation in Alabama typically runs $4,200–$18,800 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 7b-9a. Alabama's warm-humid Gulf Coast climate from Mobile to the Tennessee Valley supports near year-round growing on warm-season Bermuda, zoysia, and centipede turf, with St. Augustine dominant on the Gulf Coast — but the state's red-clay subsoil across Birmingham, Tuscaloosa, and Montgomery requires aggressive 2-to-3-inch topsoil amendment and gypsum incorporation on every new bed prep, doubling typical bed-prep labor versus loamy-soil states and pushing Alabama landscape install costs above neighboring Tennessee and Mississippi norms.

Alabama climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 7b-9a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 56 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 230 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Moderate — periodic drought, advisory water-saving guidelines
  • Irrigation dependency: Moderate — periodic irrigation needed during dry season
  • Frost-line trench depth: 10-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

Alabama licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Alabama Department of Agriculture & Industries — Landscape Architects + Landscape Designer/Contractor Registration (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; ADAI Landscape Designer registration required for design work, ADAI Pesticide Applicator credential for any chemical lawn care

How landscape installation costs vary in Alabama

Alabama's warm-humid Gulf Coast climate from Mobile to the Tennessee Valley supports near year-round growing on warm-season Bermuda, zoysia, and centipede turf, with St. Augustine dominant on the Gulf Coast — but the state's red-clay subsoil across Birmingham, Tuscaloosa, and Montgomery requires aggressive 2-to-3-inch topsoil amendment and gypsum incorporation on every new bed prep, doubling typical bed-prep labor versus loamy-soil states and pushing Alabama landscape install costs above neighboring Tennessee and Mississippi norms.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Alabama Code §2-28 makes the Alabama Department of Agriculture & Industries the only state body that licenses both landscape designers and pesticide applicators under one program — every Alabama landscape contractor applying any pesticide, fertilizer, or fungicide on residential property must hold ADAI Pesticide Applicator credentials with separate categories for ornamental, turf, mosquito, and aquatic application, with the ADAI Pesticide Inspector Division actively enforcing residential complaint inspections statewide.
