Gutter Installation Cost in Alaska (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Alaska typically runs $2,400–$7,800, with G90 galvanized steel the dominant residential profile and roughly 22 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Southeast Alaska coastal panhandle from Ketchikan to Juneau receives 200+ inches/year of precipitation while Interior Alaska remains semi-arid at 11 inches/year — gutter sizing varies dramatically by region, with coastal homes requiring 6-inch K-style and triple-downspout schedules while Fairbanks and the Yukon-Kuskokwim Delta default to 5-inch profiles, and ice damming on every coastal home drives heat-cable retrofit budgets averaging $800-1,200 per home.

Alaska rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 22 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: G90 galvanized steel — dominant in high-snow-load and ice-dam corridors
  • Ground snow load: 50 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: High — sustained sub-freezing winter requires ice-and-water shield + heat-cable retrofit budget
  • Hurricane wind tier: High — 140 mph+ design wind on coastal counties
  • Tree-canopy density: Moderate — mixed canopy, gutter guards optional

Alaska licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Alaska Department of Commerce — Construction Contractors Section (official site)
  • Permit: borough building permit required for gutter installation on new construction

How gutter installation costs vary in Alaska

State-specific code or insurance rule: Alaska AS 08.18 splits Construction Contractor licensing into General Contractor with Residential Endorsement and Specialty Contractor categories — gutter installation in Alaska typically falls under the Residential Endorsement, with municipalities like Anchorage and Fairbanks requiring additional ice-dam mitigation documentation under Title 23 Mechanical Code amendments on every new gutter system installation.
