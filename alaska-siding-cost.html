Siding Replacement Cost in Alaska (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Alaska typically runs $17,500–$42,000, with cedar lap or shake wood the dominant residential cladding choice. Wind-driven rain across Southeast Alaska from Ketchikan to Juneau drives WRB and rainscreen-gap requirements on every coastal re-side, with stainless fasteners mandatory for shoreline cedar and fiber-cement to survive 200+ inches/year of rainfall — and Interior Alaska sustained sub-zero ambients drive vinyl panel brittleness, with cedar lap and shake retaining dominant residential market share over composite alternatives.

Alaska climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 7-8
  • Dominant residential cladding: Cedar lap or shake — dominant in Pacific Northwest and historic Northeast
  • Hurricane wind tier: High wind exposure — 140 mph+ design wind on coastal counties
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: Regional WUI overlay — ignition-resistant cladding required in mapped fire-hazard zones
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High-corrosion coastal — stainless or hot-dipped galvanized fasteners required within 3,000 ft of saltwater

Alaska licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Alaska Department of Commerce — Construction Contractors Section (official site)
  • Permit: borough building permit required for re-side and any envelope alteration

How siding replacement costs vary in Alaska

Wind-driven rain across Southeast Alaska from Ketchikan to Juneau drives WRB and rainscreen-gap requirements on every coastal re-side, with stainless fasteners mandatory for shoreline cedar and fiber-cement to survive 200+ inches/year of rainfall — and Interior Alaska sustained sub-zero ambients drive vinyl panel brittleness, with cedar lap and shake retaining dominant residential market share over composite alternatives.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Alaska AS 08.18 splits Construction Contractor licensing into General Contractor with Residential Endorsement (any construction including siding), Specialty Contractor (single trade categories), and Handyman exemption (under $10,000 single-project) — and unique to Alaska, contractors must additionally register with the Department of Labor and post a $25,000 surety bond for residential work statewide.
