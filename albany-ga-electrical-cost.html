Electrical questions for Albany homeowners

When should I upgrade my electrical panel?

Upgrade if your panel uses fuses instead of breakers, you are adding major appliances (EV charger, heat pump, hot tub), breakers trip frequently, or your home has less than 200-amp service.

What is knob-and-tube wiring?

Knob-and-tube is an early wiring method found in pre-1950s homes. It lacks a ground wire and cannot handle modern electrical loads. Many insurers require replacement before issuing a policy.

How much does a panel upgrade cost?

Upgrading from 100-amp to 200-amp service typically costs $1,800-$4,000 including the panel, breakers, permit, and inspection. Upgrading the utility meter base adds $500-$1,000.

How much does EV charger installation cost?

A Level 2 EV charger installation costs $500-$2,000 for labor, plus $300-$700 for the charger. If your panel needs a dedicated 240V circuit or an upgrade, add $500-$2,500.

How much does an electrician charge per hour in Albany?

Electricians in Albany typically charge $80-$150 per hour. Master electricians and emergency calls run higher, while apprentice rates are lower.

What warranty should I expect?

Expect a minimum 1-year workmanship warranty from the contractor plus manufacturer warranties on materials. Get warranty terms in writing before signing.

Should I get multiple quotes?

Always get at least 3 written quotes. Compare line-item details, not just total price. The lowest quote often omits scope items that appear as change orders later.

How do I find a good electrical contractor in Albany?

Check licensing through your state's contractor board, verify insurance, read reviews from Albany homeowners, and get at least 3 written quotes with line-item detail.

How much should I pay upfront?

Never pay more than 10-15% as a deposit before work begins. Standard payment structure: deposit, progress payment when materials arrive, final payment on completion.

What should I look for in a contractor quote?

A professional quote breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. It includes a timeline, payment schedule, warranty terms, and the contractor's license number.
