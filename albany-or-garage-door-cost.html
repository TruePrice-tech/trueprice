What Albany residents ask about garage door

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Albany?

Garage door replacement in Albany typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

What causes a garage door to be noisy?

Common causes: worn rollers (replace nylon for steel), dry hinges (lubricate with silicone spray), loose hardware, a chain-drive opener, or worn spring coils. A tune-up costs $100-$200.

What is the best type of garage door?

Insulated steel with polyurethane foam offers the best balance of durability, energy efficiency, and cost. R-value of 12-18 keeps the garage 10-20 degrees closer to house temperature.

Albany, OR Garage-door Guide

Albany's garage doors contend with the Willamette Valley's wet maritime climate. The city receives 40-45 inches of rain annually, with most falling during the October-to-April rainy season as steady drizzle rather than heavy downpours. This persistent moisture keeps garage door components in a near-constant state of dampness for six months of the year. Steel doors with compromised paint or galvanizing develop rust along the bottom panels and at the intersection of panel joints where water pools. A door with a full-width bottom weatherstrip that channels water away from the panel edge lasts significantly longer in Albany's climate.

Wood garage doors have a mixed reputation in the Willamette Valley. The damp climate causes untreated wood to swell, warp, and eventually rot, but Albany's moderate temperatures (rarely below 20F or above 95F) mean wood does not suffer the thermal-cycling stress it would in more extreme climates. Cedar and redwood doors hold up well if properly sealed and maintained every 2-3 years, but the maintenance commitment is real. Most Albany homeowners choose steel with a woodgrain texture overlay for the look with less upkeep.

The Willamette Valley sees occasional windstorms from Pacific weather systems, with gusts reaching 50-70 mph several times per winter. While Albany is not in a high-wind zone by code, lightweight non-reinforced garage doors can be damaged or torn from their tracks during these events. A mid-grade wind-braced door provides adequate protection without the cost of a full hurricane-rated system. Horizontal struts ($100-$200 retrofitted to an existing door) stiffen the panels against wind pressure at minimal cost.

The City of Albany does not require permits for standard garage door replacements. Oregon follows the OSSC (Oregon Structural Specialty Code) for structural work, but a like-for-like door swap does not trigger code review. If you are widening or changing the garage opening, a permit and structural engineering review of the header is required. Albany has minimal HOA presence compared to larger Oregon cities, so homeowners generally have free rein on door style and color.

The mid-valley garage door market is served by dealers from both Albany/Corvallis and the Salem metro. Pricing is 5-10% below the Portland average. A standard 16x7 insulated steel door runs $900-$2,000 installed. Lead times run 3-7 days for stock sizes and colors, extending to 2-4 weeks for custom orders. One Albany consideration: if your garage faces north or is shaded by trees, moss and algae growth on the door surface is common. A mildew-resistant paint finish or annual pressure washing keeps the door looking clean.
