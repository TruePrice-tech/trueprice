Alexandria solar FAQ

What is net metering?

Net metering credits you for excess electricity your panels send to the grid. Your meter runs backward during the day and forward at night. Policies and credit rates vary by state and utility.

What happens to solar panels during a power outage?

Grid-tied systems without batteries shut off during outages for safety (anti-islanding). A battery backup or hybrid inverter lets you use solar power during grid failures.

How long do solar panels last?

Modern panels are warrantied for 25-30 years and continue producing at reduced efficiency beyond that. Inverters last 10-15 years (string) or 25 years (microinverters) and are the main replacement cost.

Will solar panels survive a hurricane in Alexandria?

With the right installation, solar panels withstand 130+ mph winds and frequently survive hurricanes better than surrounding roofing materials. The keys are wind-rated racking certified for Alexandria's exposure zone, properly sealed roof penetrations, and regular maintenance to keep hardware tight. After any major storm, have your solar installer inspect racking attachments and electrical connections.

What solar incentives are available in Alexandria?

Start with the 30% federal ITC, which applies to all Alexandria solar installations with no cap. Then check DSIRE for LA-level incentives: state tax credits, utility rebates, SREC markets, property tax exemptions, and net metering terms. Each utility has different programs and rates. The combined federal-plus-state incentive stack typically reduces net cost by 40-60% in markets with strong state programs.

When is the best time to install solar in Alexandria?

The best installation window in Alexandria is fall or winter, cooler weather makes roof work safer and the system is ready for the high-production spring and summer months. Note that the process from quote to installation takes 6-12 weeks including permitting, utility approval, and scheduling. Start the process 2-3 months before your target installation date. The federal tax credit applies to the year of installation, so timing relative to tax year can matter.

Alexandria, LA Solar Guide

Alexandria receives solid solar irradiance for a mid-South location. The city averages 4.5-5.0 peak sun hours per day annually, benefiting from Central Louisiana's predominantly clear to partly cloudy skies. While not at the level of the Desert Southwest, Alexandria's solar resource is sufficient for residential solar to make financial sense, particularly as Louisiana's electricity rates have trended upward. The relatively flat terrain and modest tree canopy in many Alexandria neighborhoods provide good rooftop solar access.

Cleco Power serves Alexandria and is required under Louisiana's net metering rules to credit residential solar customers for excess generation. Louisiana's net metering policy credits excess at the utility's avoided-cost rate, which is below the full retail rate. This means the economics favor a system sized to match consumption rather than overproduce. Cleco's residential rate runs $0.10-$0.13 per kWh, which is moderate by national standards. The lower rate extends payback compared to high-rate markets but the strong solar resource partially compensates.

Louisiana offers a state solar tax credit of 50% of the first $25,000 of system cost, which is the most generous state solar credit in the country. This $12,500 maximum state credit, combined with the federal 30% ITC, can reduce the cost of a $20,000 system to as little as $5,500 in net tax credit savings. However, the Louisiana credit is subject to annual budget caps and legislative renewal, so its availability and terms may change. Verify current availability before relying on it in your financial calculations.

Central Louisiana's weather creates a few solar considerations beyond standard installation. Hurricane-force winds can reach the Alexandria area as tropical systems move inland. Solar panels are rated to withstand 140+ mph winds when properly installed with manufacturer-specified mounting hardware. Alexandria's humid climate also promotes mold and algae growth along panel frames, which is cosmetic but can spread to adjacent roof surfaces. Annual inspection of the mounting system and panel condition is recommended.

Solar installation costs in Alexandria run $2.50-$3.40 per watt before incentives. A typical 7 kW system costs $17,500-$23,800 before incentives. If the Louisiana 50% state credit is available at full value, combined with the federal 30% ITC, net costs can drop to as low as $4,500-$8,500. Even without the state credit, the federal ITC alone brings costs to $12,250-$16,660. Alexandria's solar resource produces 9,500-11,000 kWh per year from this system size. Payback with full state credit runs 3-5 years; without state credit, 10-14 years.
