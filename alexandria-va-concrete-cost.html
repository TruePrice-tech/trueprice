Common concrete questions in Alexandria

How much does concrete work cost in Alexandria?

Concrete costs in Alexandria range from $6-$12 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, patios) to $15-$30/sqft for stamped or decorative finishes, including labor and materials.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

How much does a concrete foundation cost?

A full basement foundation costs $15,000-$30,000. A slab foundation for a house costs $5,000-$15,000. A garage slab costs $2,500-$6,000. Price depends on size, depth, and soil conditions.

More questions about concrete in Alexandria

What does a concrete driveway cost per square foot?

Plain 4-inch concrete runs $6-$12 per square foot installed in 2026. Stamped concrete adds $4-$8/sqft for pattern and color. Heavy-duty 6-inch driveways for RVs or trucks cost $9-$15/sqft. A 600 sq ft two-car driveway typically lands between $5,800 and $8,100.

How much does a 1,000 sq ft concrete patio cost?

Typical 2026 ranges for a 1,000 sq ft project: plain broom-finish $8,000-$12,000, exposed-aggregate $9,500-$14,500, stamped $11,000-$16,500. Tearing out an existing slab adds $1,500-$3,000. Reinforcing wire mesh and rebar are usually $0.50-$1.00/sqft and worth it on driveways, optional on patios.

What does a slab foundation cost in Alexandria?

A monolithic slab foundation for a typical single-family house in Alexandria runs $5,000-$15,000 depending on square footage and soil conditions. A garage slab is $2,500-$6,000. Full basement foundations are a different scope entirely and run $15,000-$30,000.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Alexandria?

Driveways, patios over a certain size, and any structural slab in Alexandria require a permit and an inspection of the base prep and rebar before the pour. Sidewalks, repairs, and resurfacing usually do not. Your contractor pulls the permit — never pull it in your own name.

Local factors: concrete work in Alexandria, VA

Alexandria's housing profile. Alexandria homes are about 30 years old on average, young enough that most original systems still have useful life remaining. concrete work demand here centers on upgrades, improvements, and the occasional early failure rather than wholesale replacement.

HOA and neighborhood rules. HOA prevalence in Alexandria is moderate. If you live in a managed community, check covenants before signing a concrete work contract. Material color, visible fixture type, or warranty requirements may affect your selection.

Contractor market picture. Alexandria's moderate growth keeps the contractor pipeline moving without the extreme backlogs seen in booming metros. Off-peak scheduling can yield 8-15% savings over peak-season rates. Alexandria's large market has concrete contractors ranging from 1-person decorative specialists to large commercial outfits that also do residential. For standard flatwork (driveways, patios), mid-size companies with dedicated residential crews typically offer the best combination of pricing and quality.

Timing considerations. Alexandria allows year-round concrete work, but the ideal window is spring and fall when temperatures support proper curing without the heat-related challenges of summer. Avoid scheduling large pours during the hottest months — early morning pours are the only reliable option.

What drives cost. With Alexandria labor rates near the national median, the cost difference between a budget and premium driveway or patio pour comes down to materials and scope rather than labor premiums. Newer construction in Alexandria (averaging 30 years) means concrete work projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay.

Red flags to watch for. Be cautious of concrete work contractors in Alexandria who pressure you to sign same-day. Legitimate contractors expect you to get competing bids and will hold their price for 30 days. High-pressure sales tactics correlate with inflated pricing. Any Alexandria contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion.
