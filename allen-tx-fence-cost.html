Frequently asked fencing questions

When is the best time to install a fence?

Late spring through early fall offers the best conditions for concrete curing and working in the ground. Avoid frozen ground and extremely wet seasons. Many contractors offer off-season discounts.

How long does fence installation take?

A standard 150-200 linear foot residential fence takes 1-3 days to install. Hillside or rocky soil adds time. Post holes need 24-48 hours to cure if set in concrete.

How deep should fence posts be?

Standard rule: bury one-third of the total post length. For a 6-foot fence with 8-foot posts, dig 24-30 inches deep. In frost-prone areas, posts must go below the frost line to prevent heaving.

How much does vinyl fencing cost?

Vinyl privacy fencing costs $20-$40 per linear foot installed. A typical 150 linear foot backyard fence runs $3,000-$6,000. Vinyl never needs painting, resists rot, and lasts 25-30 years.

Can I install a fence on a slope?

Yes, using either stepped (stair-step) or racked (following the slope) installation. Racked looks smoother on gentle slopes. Stepped is better for steep grades and is structurally stronger.

What fence types does my Allen HOA allow?

Start by requesting your Allen HOA's architectural guidelines before making any fence decisions. Common restrictions: 6-foot maximum in backyards, 4 feet in front, no chain link, finished side facing outward, and approved colors only. Many communities require formal architectural review board approval. Installing without approval can result in fines and forced removal at your expense.

Allen permits: required to build a fence?

Most Allen jurisdictions require permits for fences, especially those over 6 feet. Check with your local building department before starting. You must call 811 for utility locates before digging, verify property lines (consider a survey if boundaries are unclear), and check any HOA rules. Your contractor should handle permitting as part of the project.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Allen?

Allen's rapid growth pushes fence installation costs higher than national averages: $20-$35/ft for wood privacy, $25-$45 for vinyl, $15-$25 for chain link, and $30-$60 for ornamental metal. Strong contractor demand drives labor rates up. Get at least 3 quotes since pricing spreads wider in fast-growing markets.

Allen, TX Fence Guide

Allen's fence market is dominated by HOA-governed subdivisions where the CC&Rs dictate fence materials, heights, styles, and sometimes specific approved contractors. Nearly every major Allen subdivision (Twin Creeks, The Lakes of Allen, Waterford Parks, Star Creek, Montgomery Farm) specifies cedar board-on-board construction for privacy fences, often requiring a specific stain color and cap rail. Checking your HOA's architectural guidelines before ordering materials prevents costly rebuilds when the architectural committee rejects a non-compliant fence.

Cedar board-on-board (also called shadow-box or good-neighbor fence) is the standard style in Allen because it looks identical from both sides, eliminating the good-side/bad-side issue between neighbors. A standard 6-foot cedar board-on-board fence runs $22-$32 per linear foot installed in Allen. The style uses more lumber than standard stockade fencing (roughly 30% more boards), which increases both material cost and wind resistance since the overlapping boards create a semi-open structure that allows some wind passage.

North Texas storms are the primary threat to Allen fences. Collin County sits in the heart of Tornado Alley, with severe thunderstorms bringing 60-80 mph straight-line winds and large hail multiple times per spring. Fence damage from wind events is so common that most Allen fence contractors include a wind-damage repair service. Deep posts (36 inches minimum in concrete), metal post brackets at the base, and 4x6 corner posts provide the best wind resistance. Many Allen HOAs now require steel posts with cedar wrapping for improved durability.

The City of Allen does not require permits for standard 6-foot or shorter fences in side and rear yards. Front yard fences are limited to 4 feet and require a permit if they are solid (non-see-through). Allen follows the International Building Code and has no specific wind-load requirements for residential fences, though the HOA standards in most subdivisions effectively set construction quality minimums that exceed the basic code. Pool fences must meet the IBC requirement of 48-inch minimum height with self-closing, self-latching gates.

The Allen fence market is highly competitive, with dozens of contractors serving Collin County. Cedar fence pricing in Allen runs close to the DFW metro average. A typical 150-linear-foot cedar board-on-board fence runs $3,300-$4,800 installed. Spring is peak season with 2-4 week lead times; late fall and winter installations book faster and may come with modest discounts. One Allen-specific factor: many subdivisions share rear fences between properties, and your HOA likely has rules about shared fence responsibility and approval for replacement that affect both adjacent homeowners.
