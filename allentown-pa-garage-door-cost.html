Frequently asked garage door questions

What is the difference between chain and belt drive openers?

Chain drive is cheaper ($150-$250) but louder. Belt drive ($200-$350) is whisper-quiet and ideal when bedrooms are above or adjacent to the garage. Both have similar reliability.

Should I repair or replace my garage door?

Repair if damage is limited to one or two panels and replacements are available. Replace if the door is over 15 years old, panels are no longer manufactured, or you want better insulation and security.

Does a new garage door increase home value?

A garage door replacement recovers 90-100% of its cost at resale, making it one of the highest-ROI home improvements. It also dramatically improves curb appeal.

How long does a garage door last?

Steel garage doors last 20-30 years. Wood doors last 15-20 years with maintenance. Aluminum lasts 20+ years. Springs last 7-12 years (torsion) or 5-7 years (extension), depending on usage cycles.

Allentown, PA Garage-door Guide

Allentown's Lehigh Valley climate creates a demanding environment for garage doors. The city experiences the full range of Northeast weather: hot, humid summers that promote rust and mold; cold winters with temperatures in the single digits; 30-35 inches of annual snowfall that piles against door bases; and heavy rain during spring and fall that tests weatherstripping seals. This four-season assault means every component of the door system ages faster than in milder or drier climates.

The city's housing stock includes a significant number of row homes and duplexes in Center City, the West End, and South Allentown with single-car garages accessed from rear alleys. These garages often have non-standard openings (7-foot wide, 6-foot tall) and limited headroom that restrict door type options. Low-headroom torsion spring kits and mini track systems accommodate these tight spaces but cost $100-$300 more than standard installations. Measure your opening carefully before ordering, as custom sizes add lead time and cost.

Salt damage is a significant factor in Allentown. PennDOT and city crews apply road salt heavily from November through March, and tires track salt directly onto the garage floor where it contacts the door's bottom seal and lower panels. This salt accelerates rust on unprotected steel and degrades rubber bottom seals within a single season. A vinyl or silicone bottom seal (instead of standard rubber) lasts 3-4 times longer in salt-heavy environments. Hosing down the garage floor monthly during winter to remove accumulated salt protects both the door and the concrete.

Pennsylvania does not require permits for like-for-like garage door replacements at the state level, and Allentown follows this standard. However, if you are changing the opening size, adding electrical for an opener, or modifying the structural header, a permit through the Bureau of Building Standards and Safety is required. Allentown does not have widespread HOA restrictions on garage doors in most neighborhoods, though some newer developments in the western suburbs do.

The Lehigh Valley garage door market is well-served by local dealers and the national chains. Pricing runs slightly below the Philadelphia metro average. A standard 16x7 insulated steel door runs $1,000-$2,200 installed. The market is competitive enough that getting three written quotes is straightforward. One Allentown tip: if your row home garage is accessed from an alley, verify that the installer's truck can access the rear of the property. Some Allentown alleys are too narrow for large installation vehicles, which can add labor cost if materials must be hand-carried from the street.
