Allentown solar: answers to common questions

Can I add a battery to my solar system?

Yes. Adding a home battery (10-13 kWh) costs $8,000-$15,000 installed. Batteries provide backup power during outages and can help avoid peak-rate charges in time-of-use markets.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

Do solar panels increase home value?

Owned solar systems add roughly $15,000-$20,000 to home value according to national studies. Leased systems do not add value and can complicate the sale process.

Does solar work in Allentown's cold winters?

Allentown homeowners are sometimes surprised to learn that solar panels produce electricity more efficiently in cold temperatures. While winter days are shorter, net metering banks your summer surplus as credit. Snow slides off angled panels quickly, usually within a day. Annual production is lower than desert climates, but stronger incentives and higher electricity rates in cold-climate markets often deliver comparable payback periods.

What solar incentives are available in Allentown?

All Allentown homeowners qualify for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC), which has no dollar cap. Beyond that, check your state's solar incentive database (DSIRE) for state tax credits, utility rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits (SRECs), property tax exemptions, and net metering policies. These vary by state and utility and can reduce your net cost by an additional 10-30% beyond the federal credit.

When should I install solar in Allentown?

For Allentown, schedule solar installation during late spring or summer. Weather cooperates for roof work and you maximize first-year production before winter. Keep in mind the full process (quoting, permitting, utility approval, installation) takes 6-12 weeks. Start 2-3 months before your target date. If tax-year timing matters, plan around the installation completion date since that triggers the ITC.

Allentown, PA Solar Guide

Allentown's solar market benefits from Pennsylvania's favorable net metering policy and the state's Solar Renewable Energy Certificate (SREC) program. The Lehigh Valley averages 4.0-4.5 peak sun hours per day annually, which is moderate but sufficient for solar to make strong financial sense when combined with PPL Electric Utilities' retail rates and the available incentives. Pennsylvania's geographic location in the PJM Interconnection's SREC market adds a revenue stream that significantly improves solar economics.

PPL Electric Utilities serves Allentown and is required under Pennsylvania law to offer net metering at the full retail rate for residential solar systems. With PPL's retail rates running $0.12-$0.16 per kWh, every kilowatt-hour your system produces offsets meaningful electricity cost. Net metering credits roll month to month with annual true-up, which is important in Allentown's climate since the system overproduces in summer and underproduces in winter. The annual true-up ensures you capture the value of summer excess.

Pennsylvania's SREC market provides additional income for Allentown solar system owners. For every 1,000 kWh your system produces, you earn one SREC that can be sold on the open market. SREC prices in the Pennsylvania/PJM market fluctuate but have historically ranged from $20-$45 per certificate. A typical 7 kW system in Allentown generates 8-10 SRECs per year, adding $160-$450 in annual income on top of utility bill savings. This secondary income stream shortens payback by 1-2 years.

Allentown's climate presents winter and snow considerations for solar. The Lehigh Valley averages 30-35 inches of snowfall, and panels may be covered for several days following winter storms. Panels tilted at 30+ degrees shed snow within 1-2 days in most conditions. Most Allentown solar estimates factor in a 5-8% annual production loss from snow. The older row homes in Center City and South Allentown may have limited south-facing roof area or shading from adjacent buildings, making a site assessment particularly important before committing to installation.

Solar installation costs in Allentown run $2.70-$3.60 per watt before incentives. A typical 7 kW system costs $18,900-$25,200 before the federal ITC. After the 30% ITC, costs drop to $13,230-$17,640. Pennsylvania has no state solar tax credit, so the federal ITC and SREC income are the primary incentives. Allentown's solar resource produces 8,000-9,500 kWh per year from this system size. Including SREC income, effective payback runs 8-12 years. For homeowners with south-facing roof area and 15+ years in the home, Allentown solar is a solid investment.
