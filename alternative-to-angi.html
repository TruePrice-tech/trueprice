Alternative to Angi: Free Quote Tools Without Sales Calls

By Geoff Lane, founder of Woogoro · Updated April 2026

If you are looking for a way to check contractor pricing or find help with home projects without getting buried in sales calls, here are the alternatives that actually work, organized by what you are trying to do.

Why Look for an Angi Alternative?

Angi (which now also includes HomeAdvisor) operates on a lead-generation business model. When you submit a project, your contact information is sold to multiple contractors in their network as a paid lead. Each contractor pays for the lead and is motivated to follow up quickly to win the job. The result is the experience most homeowners report: 5 to 10 phone calls, texts, and emails within the first 24 hours.

This is not unique to Angi. The same model applies to Thumbtack, HomeAdvisor (now Angi), and Networx. The pain point is real enough that "how to stop Angi calls" is a high-volume search query.

Below are alternatives organized by what you actually need to do.

If You Want to Check a Contractor Quote

Woogoro is the closest thing to a direct alternative for quote analysis. You upload a contractor quote (or describe your project), and the analyzer checks the price against city-specific cost data, verifies that the scope of work is complete, and flags concerning fine print like high deposits or warranty exclusions. Free, no email, no phone, no signup. 20 trades covered.

Angi does not have a quote analysis tool. The closest equivalent in their product is the price guide pages, which show generic national ranges rather than city-specific data.

If You Want to Estimate a Project Before Getting Quotes

Woogoro's estimate tool generates a project cost range based on your city, project type, and basic details. Useful when you want to know roughly what to budget before you start calling contractors. Same no-email, no-phone policy as the analyzer.

HomeAdvisor's True Cost Guide (now part of Angi) shows national averages and is useful as a sanity check, though the data is less granular than Woogoro's city-level estimates.

Fixr publishes detailed cost guides by project type with national and regional averages.

If You Want to Find Licensed Contractors

Direct alternatives that don't require submitting your contact info:

  • State licensing boards. Every state with contractor licensing has a public lookup tool. Search "[your state] contractor license lookup" to find it. This is the most reliable source for verifying that a contractor is actually licensed.
  • Better Business Bureau (BBB). Free directory of accredited businesses with complaint history. Filter by service type and location.
  • Google Maps. Search "[trade] near me" and review profiles. Look for contractors with 50+ reviews and a 4.5+ rating, with reviews dating back several years.
  • Nextdoor and local Facebook groups. Neighbors share unfiltered recommendations and warnings. Often the best source for small-trade jobs (handyman, plumber, electrician).
  • Trade-specific associations. Look for state chapters of NRCA (roofers), ACCA (HVAC), PHCC (plumbing), NECA (electrical), etc. These often have directories of member contractors.

If You Want Reviews of Specific Contractors

Google Maps reviews are typically the most reliable. They are difficult to remove, the volume tends to be high, and Google's review filtering is more aggressive than most platforms.

Yelp for service businesses, particularly in major metros. Older reviews are more trustworthy than recent ones.

BBB complaint history shows formal complaints and resolutions. A pattern of unresolved complaints is a strong warning sign.

Nextdoor shows neighbor experiences with specific contractors, often with more context than star ratings.

Be cautious of sites where contractors pay for placement (Angi, HomeAdvisor, Thumbtack). Top results may not reflect actual quality.

If You Want a Marketplace That Matches You to Contractors

If you genuinely want a marketplace experience and accept the sales follow-up that comes with it, the major options are:

  • Angi (largest, most service categories)
  • Thumbtack (similar model, broader project types beyond home services)
  • Networx (smaller, similar model)
  • Houzz Pro (focused on remodel and design)

All of these operate on the lead-sale model. Expect multiple contractor contacts within hours of submitting.

The Most Effective Combination

Many homeowners find that the best workflow is:

  1. Use Woogoro's estimate tool to get a budget range for your project
  2. Find 3 contractors via Google Maps, state licensing board, or Nextdoor
  3. Get 3 written quotes
  4. Use Woogoro's analyzer to check each quote against local pricing and scope standards
  5. Pick the contractor with the best combination of fair price, complete scope, and verified reputation

This avoids the lead-sale process entirely while giving you better information than any single tool would.
