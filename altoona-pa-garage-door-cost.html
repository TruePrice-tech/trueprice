Frequently asked garage door questions

What R-value should my garage door have?

R-8 is fine if the garage is detached or unheated. R-12 to R-16 is best for attached garages or if you use the space for a workshop. R-18+ is for extreme cold climates or heated garages.

Are smart garage door openers worth it?

Smart openers add $100-$200 over basic models. They let you open, close, and monitor the door from your phone, set schedules, and receive alerts. Useful for deliveries and forgetting to close.

How much does garage door spring replacement cost?

Torsion spring replacement costs $200-$400 for parts and labor. Extension springs cost $150-$300. Never attempt spring replacement yourself since the stored tension is extremely dangerous.

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

What R-value garage door do I need in Altoona?

In Altoona, the minimum practical insulation for an attached garage door is R-12, with R-16 being the better choice for most homeowners. Workshop or heated-garage applications should go R-16 to R-18. That extra $200-$500 investment recovers through energy savings in adjacent living spaces within a few years and keeps the garage itself above freezing through winter.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Altoona?

For Altoona homes averaging 48 years old, replacement is often the better investment if the door is original. A new insulated door with a modern opener costs $1,200-$3,000 and recovers 90-100% of its cost at resale. Repair makes sense for newer doors with localized damage (single panel, one spring, or opener motor replacement) when the rest of the system is sound.

Best time of year to replace a garage door in Altoona?

For Altoona, target late spring or early fall for your garage door replacement. Moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing, and off-peak scheduling often means 5-10% savings on installation. Spring is the peak rush since cold weather causes spring failures that flood installers with emergency calls.
