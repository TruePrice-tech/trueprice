Questions Altoona homeowners ask about landscaping

What are the best low-maintenance landscaping plants?

Native perennials, ornamental grasses, and drought-tolerant shrubs need the least care. Avoid high-maintenance options like hybrid tea roses, non-native annuals, and fast-growing trees that require frequent pruning.

Does landscaping increase home value?

Well-designed landscaping adds 5-15% to home value. Mature trees alone add $1,000-$10,000 each. Curb appeal landscaping recovers 100-200% of investment at resale, one of the highest ROI improvements.

How much does tree removal cost?

Small trees (under 30 feet) cost $200-$500 to remove. Medium trees (30-60 feet) cost $500-$1,000. Large trees (60-100 feet) cost $1,000-$2,000+. Stump grinding adds $100-$400 per stump.

How much does outdoor lighting cost?

Professional landscape lighting costs $2,000-$5,000 for path lights, uplights, and accent lighting. LED fixtures cost more upfront but last 50,000+ hours. Solar options cost less but provide dimmer, less reliable light.

About landscaping in Altoona, PA

Why pricing varies in Altoona. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. For landscaping specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Altoona at roughly 7% below the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Altoona has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Altoona's short growing season (May-September) compresses landscaping work into 4-5 months. Spring planting after the last frost date is critical for plant establishment before winter. Fall planting of trees and shrubs (September-October) allows root establishment before dormancy — a strategy experienced Altoona landscapers recommend for better spring survival rates. Established properties in Altoona (averaging 48 years) often have mature trees and existing landscape elements that a redesign should work around. Removing mature trees costs $500-2,000 each and eliminates decades of shade value. Design with existing assets, not against them.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most landscaping projects in Altoona require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. At 45,796 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. Altoona has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.
