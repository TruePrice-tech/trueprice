Anderson roofing: answers to common questions

How many layers of shingles can I have?

Most building codes allow a maximum of two layers. If you already have two layers, the old roof must be torn off before the new one is installed, adding $1,000-$3,000 to the cost.

Does a new roof increase home value?

A new roof recovers roughly 60-70% of its cost at resale and eliminates a major buyer objection. Homes with new roofs sell faster and with fewer inspection contingencies.

Do I need a permit for roof replacement in Anderson?

Yes. Most Anderson jurisdictions require a building permit for roof replacement. Your contractor should handle the permit and schedule inspection.

What is the difference between architectural and 3-tab shingles?

Architectural shingles are thicker, have a dimensional look, and last 25-30 years. 3-tab shingles are flat, thinner, and last 15-20 years but cost 30-40% less upfront.

How do I prevent ice dams on my Anderson roof?

Anderson's freeze-thaw cycling makes ice dams a real concern. They happen when heat from the living space warms the roof deck, melting snow that refreezes at the colder eaves. The fix is three-fold: adequate attic insulation (R-49 minimum here), continuous soffit-to-ridge airflow, and ice-and-water shield membrane on the lower 3-6 feet of every eave. A roofing quote that ignores attic conditions is missing the actual problem.

Anderson permits: required to replace my roof?

In most jurisdictions including Anderson, a building permit is required for a full roof replacement. Your contractor should pull the permit before work begins. Unpermitted roofing work can void your homeowners insurance coverage and create disclosure issues when selling the home.

When is the best time to replace a roof in Anderson?

For Anderson, aim for late spring or early fall to schedule your roof replacement. Materials install best above 45F and before snow season pricing surcharges, which means better contractor availability and potential savings of 10-15% on labor. Booking right after a major storm is the worst time since every roofer in the area will be booked and prices reflect the surge demand.
