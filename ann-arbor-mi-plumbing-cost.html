Plumbing in Ann Arbor: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Ann Arbor has moderately hard water which can reduce water heater lifespan over time. Consider a water softener or tankless unit with a descaling system to protect your investment.

❄

Freeze risk

In Ann Arbor, pipe freeze prevention is essential. Disconnect outdoor hoses before the first freeze, insulate exposed pipes, and never let your thermostat drop below 55°F — even when away. Burst pipe repairs average $1,500-3,000.

🔧

Material recommendation

For Ann Arbor homes, PEX is the go-to repiping material. Its flexibility means it handles freeze-thaw cycles better than copper or CPVC. It's also faster to install, reducing labor costs.

💡

Local tip

Older homes in Ann Arbor (pre-1970) often have galvanized or cast iron drain pipes that deteriorate over decades. A camera inspection ($150-300) can reveal hidden corrosion before it causes a major leak or backup.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Ann Arbor?

Plumber rates in Ann Arbor typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
