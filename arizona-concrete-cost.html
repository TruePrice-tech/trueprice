Concrete Driveway Cost in Arizona (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in Arizona typically run $3,400–$9,400 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 3000 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 6-inch design frost line, and the state sits in no freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F0). Arizona's combination of high alkaline soil sulfate exposure (S2 ACI 318 category) across the Phoenix, Tucson, and Mesa basins forces ASTM C150 Type II or Type V sulfate-resistant cement specification on every residential pour to prevent sulfate-driven concrete deterioration — and Arizona's expansive caliche-clay subsoil across the Sonoran Desert requires 4-to-6-inch crushed-aggregate base plus geotextile membrane separation on every Phoenix and Tucson driveway pour to prevent clay-driven slab heave during monsoon-season moisture cycles.

Arizona climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 6-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F0 — no freeze-thaw exposure, no air-entrainment required
  • Typical residential mix: 3000 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: High — expansive clay subsoil requires geotechnical investigation + reinforced footings
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S2 — severe sulfate exposure, ASTM C150 Type II/V cement required
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC B — minor seismic risk, standard tie-down requirements
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: None — no winter de-icer exposure

Arizona licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Arizona Registrar of Contractors — license CR-9 (Concrete) or CR-78 (Swimming Pools w/ concrete) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential driveway pour > 200 sq ft; ROC CR-9 specialty license required on contracts above $1,000

How concrete driveway costs vary in Arizona

Arizona's combination of high alkaline soil sulfate exposure (S2 ACI 318 category) across the Phoenix, Tucson, and Mesa basins forces ASTM C150 Type II or Type V sulfate-resistant cement specification on every residential pour to prevent sulfate-driven concrete deterioration — and Arizona's expansive caliche-clay subsoil across the Sonoran Desert requires 4-to-6-inch crushed-aggregate base plus geotextile membrane separation on every Phoenix and Tucson driveway pour to prevent clay-driven slab heave during monsoon-season moisture cycles.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Arizona Registrar of Contractors §32-1121 sets one of the lowest residential contracting thresholds in the country at $1,000 — every Arizona concrete contractor exceeding that figure must hold a CR-9 Concrete specialty license, and Arizona is one of the only Mountain West states with mandatory ASTM C150 Type II/V sulfate-resistant cement on every residential pour due to high alkaline soil sulfate exposure across the Phoenix and Tucson basins, with Arizona Department of Environmental Quality additionally requiring SWPPP notification on every concrete pour above 1 acre disturbed area.
