Fence Installation Cost in Arizona (2026)

Installing 150 linear feet of fencing in Arizona typically runs $4,800–$12,500, with 6-foot vinyl panel the dominant residential choice. Concrete-set posts must clear the 6-inch design frost line. Arizona sits inside Termite Probability and Climatic Tier 1 (the highest TPCT zone alongside Florida and Alabama) — every in-ground wood post requires CCA-treated southern yellow pine .60 or higher per Arizona Building Code, but the dominant residential material has shifted to vinyl since the 2000s due to the combination of termite pressure, sustained 110°F+ summer heat that bleaches and checks cedar within 3-5 years, and HOA architectural review that favors uniform vinyl over weathered cedar.

Arizona climate & post-design drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 6-inch design minimum for post footings
  • Dominant residential fence material: Vinyl panel — dominant in HOA-heavy Sun Belt and termite-risk corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: Low — sub-110 mph design wind, standard 8-foot post spacing
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Very high — TPCT zone 1, all in-ground wood requires PT southern yellow pine .60 CCA or alternative
  • HOA fencing-rule prevalence: High — large planned-community HOA penetration restricts material/color/height

Arizona licensing, HOA & boundary law

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Arizona Registrar of Contractors — license C-30 (Fence) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county permit typical for residential fence over 6 feet; HOA approval required in most master-planned communities

How fence installation costs vary in Arizona

Arizona sits inside Termite Probability and Climatic Tier 1 (the highest TPCT zone alongside Florida and Alabama) — every in-ground wood post requires CCA-treated southern yellow pine .60 or higher per Arizona Building Code, but the dominant residential material has shifted to vinyl since the 2000s due to the combination of termite pressure, sustained 110°F+ summer heat that bleaches and checks cedar within 3-5 years, and HOA architectural review that favors uniform vinyl over weathered cedar.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Arizona Registrar of Contractors issues a C-30 Fence specialty classification distinct from the B General Building or C-3 Carpentry credentials — making Arizona one of the few states with a dedicated fencing-specialty contractor license that requires separate examination on Arizona-specific soil-anchorage methods, HOA architectural review compliance, and view-fence masonry design for hillside subdivisions.
