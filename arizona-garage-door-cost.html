Garage Door Replacement Cost in Arizona (2026)

Garage door replacements in Arizona typically run $1,200–$4,400 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 90 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Arizona's combination of extreme summer UV exposure across the Phoenix and Tucson basins driving Sun-Belt-rated UV-stabilized polyester topcoats on every west-facing garage-door installation to prevent fade in 5-7 years, very-high TPCT termite zone 1 pressure driving pressure-treated wood jamb framing on every garage-door installation, and HOA architectural-control prevalence at 80%+ of Phoenix, Tucson, Mesa, and Scottsdale residential subdivisions requiring pre-approval review on door color, material, and style drives unique HOA-and-UV-driven material selection.

Arizona climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 90 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Very high — TPCT zone 1, all wood jamb framing requires PT southern yellow pine .60 CCA
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Very high — 80%+ of subdivisions enforce HOA architectural-committee pre-approval on door color/material/style
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: None — no salt-air corrosion exposure
  • IECC climate zone: 2B-4B

Arizona licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Arizona Registrar of Contractors — license CR-9 (Concrete) or CR-31 (Carpentry) commonly held by garage-door installers; specialty CR-65 Garage Doors available (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening on new construction; ROC specialty license required on contracts above $1,000; replacement-only installs typically permit-exempt; HOA architectural committee approval mandatory in 80%+ of Phoenix-area subdivisions

How garage door installation costs vary in Arizona

State-specific code or insurance rule: Arizona Registrar of Contractors §32-1121 sets the contracting threshold at $1,000 with ROC specialty CR-65 Garage Doors available as a dedicated credential, and Arizona is unusual among Mountain West states in HOA architectural-control prevalence — the Foundation for Community Association Research documents 80%+ of Phoenix, Tucson, Mesa, Chandler, Gilbert, and Scottsdale residential subdivisions operate HOA architectural committees with mandatory pre-approval review on garage-door color, material, and style selection plus mandatory Sun-Belt-rated UV-stabilized topcoats on every west-facing garage-door installation.
