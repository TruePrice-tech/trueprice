Gutter Installation Cost in Arizona (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Arizona typically runs $1,000–$3,800, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 13 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Arizona's 13-inch annual rainfall is the second-lowest in the U.S. after Nevada, but 50% of that volume falls during the July-September North American Monsoon in concentrated cells that overwhelm undersized gutters — Phoenix and Tucson properties without gutters routinely experience monsoon-driven foundation undermining and stucco staining, driving retrofit demand on south- and west-facing eaves that bear the highest monsoon flow.

Arizona rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 13 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 5 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: No ice-dam exposure
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: Low — sparse canopy, standard open gutters adequate

Arizona licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Arizona Registrar of Contractors — license B (general residential) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit not typically required for gutter-only installation

How gutter installation costs vary in Arizona

Arizona's 13-inch annual rainfall is the second-lowest in the U.S. after Nevada, but 50% of that volume falls during the July-September North American Monsoon in concentrated cells that overwhelm undersized gutters — Phoenix and Tucson properties without gutters routinely experience monsoon-driven foundation undermining and stucco staining, driving retrofit demand on south- and west-facing eaves that bear the highest monsoon flow.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Arizona is one of the only states where many residential properties have no gutters at all — the arid 13-inch annual rainfall and low foundation runoff risk make gutter installation optional, with homebuilders typically delivering Phoenix and Tucson new construction without gutters and homeowners installing them retrofit only where roof runoff causes documented foundation, walkway, or landscaping damage during the July-September monsoon season.
