Landscape Installation Cost in Arizona (2026)

Landscape installation in Arizona typically runs $5,200–$22,400 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with desert-adapted Sonoran or Mojave plant palette as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 5b-10a. Arizona's 13-inch annual rainfall in the Phoenix and Tucson basins makes residential landscaping the largest single residential water demand statewide — Arizona Department of Water Resources estimates 50-70% of single-family residential water use goes to outdoor irrigation, driving xeriscape and ADWR-approved low-water-use plant lists as the default Phoenix-Tucson residential aesthetic, with desert-adapted Texas Ranger, Yellow Bells, Palo Verde, and Saguaro cactus replacing ornamental turf on the majority of new-build front yards.

Arizona climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 5b-10a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 13 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 320 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Extreme — multi-year drought cycles drive xeriscape adoption + statewide turf restrictions
  • Irrigation dependency: Very high — 60%+ of residential outdoor water for irrigation
  • Frost-line trench depth: 6-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Desert-adapted — Sonoran or Mojave palette (cactus, palo verde, agave) on most front yards
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: Regional WUI defensible-space rule in mapped fire-hazard zones (CAL FIRE-style)

Arizona licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Arizona Registrar of Contractors — license CR-21 (Landscaping & Irrigation Systems) (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install on existing yards; ROC CR-21 specialty license required on contracts above $1,000

How landscape installation costs vary in Arizona

State-specific code or insurance rule: Arizona Active Management Area (AMA) statute under ARS §45-401 imposes the most restrictive residential landscape water rules in the Southwest across the Phoenix and Tucson AMAs — every new residential landscape install must comply with AMA-specific turf-area caps, Total Maximum Annual Water Allotments, and ADWR-approved low-water plant lists, with the Phoenix AMA additionally banning new ornamental turf in non-functional areas effective post-2025 ADWR rulemaking.
