Exterior Painting Cost in Arizona (2026)

Repainting a 1,500 sq ft home in Arizona typically runs $2,800–$8,200, with three-coat hard-coat stucco the dominant residential substrate driving prep scope. Arizona's 300+ annual sun-days and high-altitude UV index in Phoenix, Tucson, and Flagstaff drive aggressive chalking and fade on every south- and west-facing stucco elevation — exterior repaint cycles run 5-7 years on premium 100% acrylic elastomeric versus 8-10 years in lower-UV climates, with Sun Belt-rated UV-stabilized binders and high-PVC titanium-dioxide topcoats now standard on every Phoenix-Tucson-Mesa stucco repaint.

Arizona climate & coatings drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 2B-3B-4B
  • Dominant residential substrate: Three-coat hard-coat stucco — dominant in arid and Sun Belt corridors, drives elastomeric topcoats
  • Annual humidity tier: Very low — extreme arid, no mildew exposure
  • UV exposure: Very high — 280+ sun-days drive Sun Belt-rated UV-stabilized binders + high-PVC TiO2 topcoats
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: No salt-air corrosion exposure (interior continental)
  • Freeze-thaw cycling: Low — occasional freeze events
  • VOC architectural coatings rule: Federal default — 250 g/L flat exterior topcoat limit (EPA 40 CFR Part 59)

Arizona licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Arizona Registrar of Contractors — license CR-34 (Painting & Wall Covering) (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for repaint; ROC CR-34 specialty license required on contracts above $1,000

How exterior repaint costs vary in Arizona

Arizona's 300+ annual sun-days and high-altitude UV index in Phoenix, Tucson, and Flagstaff drive aggressive chalking and fade on every south- and west-facing stucco elevation — exterior repaint cycles run 5-7 years on premium 100% acrylic elastomeric versus 8-10 years in lower-UV climates, with Sun Belt-rated UV-stabilized binders and high-PVC titanium-dioxide topcoats now standard on every Phoenix-Tucson-Mesa stucco repaint.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Arizona Registrar of Contractors §32-1121 sets the lowest residential contracting threshold in the country at $1,000 — every Arizona painting job above that figure requires a CR-34 Painting & Wall Covering specialty license, with the ROC Recovery Fund compensating Arizona homeowners up to $30,000 per project for licensed-contractor abandonment or breach on residential repaint work.
