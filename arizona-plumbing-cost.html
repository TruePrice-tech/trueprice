Plumbing & Repipe Cost in Arizona (2026)

Plumbing repipe in Arizona typically runs $5,200–$12,800 for a whole-house 2-bath PEX repipe, with PEX-A or PEX-B cross-linked polyethylene the dominant retrofit choice and the Uniform Plumbing Code (UPC) as the adopted enforcement basis. Buried service-line trench depth must clear the 6-inch design frost line. Phoenix and Tucson groundwater runs 17-25 grains-per-gallon of dissolved calcium-magnesium carbonate, scaling tankless water heater heat exchangers and ruining unconditioned-attic storage tanks within 3-5 years; whole-house softeners or scale-control devices are now standard on Sun-Belt repipes, and unconditioned attic supply runs require thicker insulation against summer attic temperatures over 150°F.

Arizona water-quality & freeze drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 2B-3B-4B — drives freeze-protection scope on supply lines
  • Frost-line trench depth: 6-inch design minimum for buried water service
  • Water hardness profile: Very hard (10.5+ grains/gallon) — whole-house softener standard on most repipes
  • Dominant retrofit supply material: PEX-A or PEX-B cross-linked polyethylene — dominant on whole-house repipes since 2005
  • Adopted plumbing code basis: Uniform Plumbing Code (UPC) — IAPMO model adopted statewide
  • Lead service line risk tier: Low — minimal pre-1986 lead service line installations

Arizona licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Arizona Registrar of Contractors — license C-37 (Plumbing) or CR-37 (residential) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county plumbing permit required for repipe, gas line, or water-service replacement

How plumbing and repipe costs vary in Arizona

State-specific code or insurance rule: Arizona is one of three states with a Type 1 General Permit for residential greywater systems under A.A.C.
