Window Replacement Cost in Arizona (2026)

Window replacements in Arizona typically run $6,400–$16,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Southern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.4, SHGC ≤ 0.25). Arizona's combination of extreme summer UV exposure across the Phoenix and Tucson basins (340+ sun days/year) driving Sun-Belt-rated UV-stabilized vinyl frames and low-SHGC (≤0.25) glazing on every west-facing residential window installation to prevent fade and minimize cooling load, ENERGY STAR Southern climate zone driving low-SHGC (≤0.25) glazing requirement statewide, and very-high HOA architectural-control prevalence across 80%+ of Phoenix and Tucson subdivisions requiring pre-approval review on window frame color drives unique Sun-Belt-and-HOA-driven glazing selection.

Arizona climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Southern — cooling-dominant, low SHGC priority (U ≤ 0.40, SHGC ≤ 0.25)
  • IECC climate zone: 2B-4B
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.4 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: 0.25
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 90 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Moderate — selected metro historic districts with review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Moderate — 20-40% pre-1978 housing share, EPA RRP required on selected replacements

Arizona licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Arizona Registrar of Contractors — license CR-65 (Glazing) or CR-31 (Carpentry) for window installation (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification; ROC CR-65 specialty license required on contracts above $1,000; HOA architectural-committee approval mandatory in 80%+ of Phoenix, Tucson, Mesa, Chandler, and Scottsdale subdivisions

How window replacement costs vary in Arizona

State-specific code or insurance rule: Arizona Registrar of Contractors §32-1121 sets the contracting threshold at $1,000 with ROC specialty CR-65 Glazing available as a dedicated credential for window installation, and Arizona is unusual among Mountain West states in HOA architectural-control prevalence — the Foundation for Community Association Research documents 80%+ of Phoenix, Tucson, Mesa, Chandler, Gilbert, and Scottsdale residential subdivisions operate HOA architectural committees with mandatory pre-approval review on window frame color and grille pattern selection plus mandatory Sun-Belt-rated UV-stabilized topcoats on every west-facing window replacement.
