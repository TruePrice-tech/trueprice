Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in Arkansas (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in Arkansas typically run $1,800–$4,300 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2020 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Service entrance design assumes a 200A minimum on new residential construction. The Boston Mountain and Ouachita National Forest corridors face summer cumulonimbus development with cloud-to-ground lightning rates above eight strikes per square mile per year — service-entrance surge protection at the panel is increasingly written into Arkansas Department of Labor inspection lists, and rural Ozark service drops are routinely upgraded from #2 aluminum SE-cable to copper service-entrance conductors after repeated near-strike damage.

Arkansas code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2020 NEC — dwelling unit AFCI on most 120V branch circuits, GFCI expanded
  • State amendments: Arkansas adopts NEC under Ark. Code §17-28 with Department of Labor & Licensing enforcement, and the Department waives local amendments for cities under 60,000 population without a chartered inspection program
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: High — Southeast and lower Plains lightning belt
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 12-to-18-inch design depth on buried service conduit

Arkansas licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Arkansas Department of Labor & Licensing — Board of Electrical Examiners (official site)
  • Permit: state electrical permit issued by Department of Labor inspector outside chartered cities

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in Arkansas

State-specific code or insurance rule: Arkansas places electrical licensure under the state Department of Labor rather than a contractor licensing board — Ark. Code §17-28-101 makes residential electrical work a labor-regulated trade, and the Board of Electrical Examiners directly issues permits and inspects work in any unincorporated area or municipality without a chartered inspection program, with Master, Journeyman, Residential Master, and Residential Journeyman as the four separately credentialed tiers.
