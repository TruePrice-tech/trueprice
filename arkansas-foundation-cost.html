Foundation Cost in Arkansas (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in Arkansas typically run $7,800–$19,600 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with slab-on-grade as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 12-inch design frost line. Arkansas's New Madrid Seismic Zone influence across northeast Arkansas counties from Jonesboro to West Memphis forces ASCE 7-22 SDC C-D0 seismic design category requirements on every residential foundation pour in the corridor, and Arkansas's red-clay Delta subsoil across the eastern half of the state requires aggressive 4-to-6-inch crushed-stone capillary break under every slab pour, plus Ozark and Ouachita mountain pours additionally require frost-protected footing depth on every elevated residential construction site above 1,500 ft elevation where freeze-thaw exposure runs F1.

Arkansas climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 12-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Slab-on-grade — dominant in Sun Belt and Southeast on warm climates with shallow frost line
  • Expansive soil risk: Moderate — periodic clay shrink-swell, standard footing reinforcement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC C — moderate seismic risk, continuous-rebar-tie footings required
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Water table risk: Moderate — water table below frost line in most lots
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • IECC climate zone: 3A-4A
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 2 (moderate) — sub-slab depressurization rough-in recommended

Arkansas licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Arkansas Contractors Licensing Board — Residential Builder license (>$2,000) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new-construction foundation pour; Residential Builder license required above $2,000 contract; New Madrid Seismic Zone Special Inspection required in 11 northeast Arkansas counties

How foundation pour costs vary in Arkansas

State-specific code or insurance rule: Arkansas Code §17-25-101 sets one of the lowest residential contracting thresholds at $2,000 — and Arkansas is unusual among Southern states in adopting the New Madrid Seismic Zone Special Inspection Program through Arkansas Code §12-30 requiring continuous-rebar-tie footings, minimum 3500 psi compressive strength, and special-inspector observation on every residential foundation pour in Crittenden, Greene, Mississippi, Poinsett, Cross, Lee, Phillips, St. Francis, Craighead, Lawrence, and Randolph counties — the most stringent foundation-specific seismic mandate in the South Central region.
