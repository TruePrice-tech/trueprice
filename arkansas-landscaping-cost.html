Landscape Installation Cost in Arkansas (2026)

Landscape installation in Arkansas typically runs $3,800–$16,800 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 6b-8a. Arkansas's Ozark and Ouachita mountain landscapes combine acidic forest-soil substrates with intense seasonal pollen and pine-needle accumulation, requiring annual topdressing and pH amendment on every Bermuda, zoysia, and tall fescue turf install — and the Arkansas Delta clay substrate across the Eastern Arkansas counties from Jonesboro to Pine Bluff requires deep ripping plus 2-to-3-inch sand-and-organic amendment on every new lawn install, with backyard drainage swales mandatory in NRCS hydric soil mapping.

Arkansas climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 6b-8a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 50 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 220 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Low — adequate residential precipitation, minimal drought stress
  • Irrigation dependency: Moderate — periodic irrigation needed during dry season
  • Frost-line trench depth: 10-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

Arkansas licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Arkansas State Plant Board — Pesticide Applicator + Arkansas Contractors Licensing Board (Residential Remodeler >$2,000) (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; Arkansas State Plant Board Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care

How landscape installation costs vary in Arkansas

Arkansas's Ozark and Ouachita mountain landscapes combine acidic forest-soil substrates with intense seasonal pollen and pine-needle accumulation, requiring annual topdressing and pH amendment on every Bermuda, zoysia, and tall fescue turf install — and the Arkansas Delta clay substrate across the Eastern Arkansas counties from Jonesboro to Pine Bluff requires deep ripping plus 2-to-3-inch sand-and-organic amendment on every new lawn install, with backyard drainage swales mandatory in NRCS hydric soil mapping.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Arkansas Code §2-16 makes the Arkansas State Plant Board the residential pesticide applicator licensor — Arkansas requires every commercial landscape contractor applying any restricted-use pesticide on residential property to hold a State Plant Board Pesticide Applicator credential with separate categories for ornamental, turf, and right-of-way application, plus Arkansas's 1979 Right-to-Farm Act §2-4-101 limits residential nuisance complaints against neighboring agricultural pesticide drift on landscape installations adjacent to Delta row-crop operations.
