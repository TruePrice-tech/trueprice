Picking the right gutter size in Atlanta

A standard 5-inch K-style gutter in Atlanta handles about 1.2 gallons per second, fine up to roughly 2 inches per hour. When Atlanta storms exceed that rate (they do), 6-inch gutters carry 40% more water for $2-$3 more per linear foot on Midtown, Virginia-Highland, and Inman Park homes.

Atlanta downspout routing and sizing

Georgia red clay creates significant drainage challenges. The clay is nearly impermeable when saturated, which means downspout discharge pools on the surface rather than absorbing. French drains, catch basins, and graded swales are common drainage solutions in Atlanta. Ensure downspouts discharge at least 6-8 feet from the foundation, preferably into a proper drainage system.

Rule of thumb for Atlanta: one downspout per 20-30 linear feet of gutter. Each 2x3-inch downspout drains roughly 600 sq ft; step up to 3x4-inch on Midtown, Virginia-Highland, and Inman Park homes with large roof planes or steep pitches that concentrate runoff.

Common Gutter Scams in Atlanta

Midtown, Virginia-Highland, and Inman Park homeowners see these same four gutter-quote issues repeatedly.

Atlanta-specific undersizing

A Atlanta bid that specifies 5-inch gutters without reference to 50.2 inches of annual rainfall or the specific Midtown, Virginia-Highland, and Inman Park roof area being drained is underthought. Any roof plane above 1,500 sq ft emptying to a single gutter run needs 6-inch in Atlanta.

Pitch errors on Atlanta installs

Gutters should slope toward downspouts at about 1/4 inch per 10 feet of run. Flat gutters pool water, breed mosquitoes, and overflow in storms. After install, run a simple Atlanta water test: pour water at the high end and confirm steady flow to the downspout with no pooling. If water stands, pitch needs correction.

Atlanta discharge routing skipped

Gutters collect water; downspouts deliver it. Without splash blocks, extensions, or buried drainage, all that water concentrates at your foundation. Atlanta's 50.2 inches of annual rain turns that into foundation cracks, basement seepage, and eroded landscaping. A Atlanta bid silent on discharge routing is incomplete.

Seamed gutters sold as seamless

Seamless = formed on-site from one coil. Sectional = factory pieces joined with connectors. The Atlanta scam is marketing sectional gutters as "seamless" and charging the seamless premium. Seams cause the majority of gutter failures, so paying for real seamless matters — verify the coil-forming machine showed up to your job.
