Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to paint a house exterior in Atlanta?

Most homeowners in Atlanta pay between $5,000 and $10,200 for exterior painting, depending on home size and paint quality.

How many coats of paint does an exterior need?

Most exterior painting jobs require at least two coats for proper coverage and durability. A single coat may work for touch-ups or if the existing paint is in good condition with a similar color.

How long does exterior paint last?

Quality exterior paint typically lasts 5-10 years depending on climate, sun exposure, and paint quality. Premium paints like Sherwin-Williams Duration can last 10-15 years.

About exterior painting in Atlanta, GA

Why pricing varies in Atlanta. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. For exterior painting specifically, Atlanta sits within a few points of the national midpoint on our cost model.

Local conditions. Atlanta has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Use 100% acrylic latex with mildewcide; oil-based paints fail within 2-3 years on south and west exposures.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most exterior painting projects in Atlanta require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 447,841, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Atlanta's rapid recent growth has tightened the contractor market — expect longer lead times and less negotiating room than smaller markets.

Atlanta Climate and Paint Durability

Atlanta paint exposure is moderate UV (latitude 33.7N) with hot humid summers and mild winters. South-facing brick, Hardie, and wood-clapboard facades in Buckhead and Brookhaven typically last 6-8 years on premium acrylic before humidity-driven mildew overtakes gloss retention. Red-clay foundation movement cracks caulk seals over 3-5 years on slab homes. Atlanta pollen season (mid-March through April) produces a universal yellow coating that requires post-pollen wash before any paint job. Pine-tree sap drip on shaded lots creates specific staining issues.

Atlanta exterior moisture stress is severe in summer (65-85% RH) with significant mildew growth on any painted surface without mildewcide. North-facing Hardie and brick facades develop visible mildew within 12-18 months. Concrobium Mold Control and Zinsser Jomax are standard pre-wash treatments. Mildewcide-enhanced paint (SW Duration Mold Armor, Ben Moore Aura with mildewcide) is the Atlanta premium spec. Pine-sap staining under canopy oaks is specific to Decatur and Virginia-Highland yards. Power-washing frequency increases with tree canopy density.

Atlanta coatings culture emphasizes humidity-resistant acrylic latex (SW Duration Exterior, Ben Moore Aura Exterior) with heavy mildewcide loading. Oil-based alkyd is largely abandoned except on specific Victorian door and trim work. Elastomeric on stucco (SW Conflex XL) is common on Buckhead and Tuxedo Park facades. High-build primer on Hardie caulk joints. Solvent-based stain-blocker on pine-sap and tannin bleed. Sikkens-system wood stain on Inman Park and Candler Park wood trim.

Atlanta Prep Culture and Methods

Atlanta prep culture emphasizes aggressive power-washing because of pollen staining and mildew growth. Victorian and craftsman wood-clapboard requires scraping multiple paint layers and EPA RRP-certified crews on pre-1978 properties. Modern Hardie-plank tract homes typically need only surface cleaning and caulk refresh. Buckhead Mediterranean-revival stucco requires elastomeric crack-bridging before topcoat. Red-clay foundation movement on slab homes cracks caulk seams that must be refreshed. Pine-pollen contamination requires specific degreasing wash.

Atlanta Permits and Lead Paint Rules

City of Atlanta Office of Buildings does not require permits for standard exterior painting. Georgia Department of Community Affairs requires EPA RRP certification for contractors disturbing lead paint. Fulton and DeKalb County Environmental Health enforce lead-safe work practices. Atlanta Tree Ordinance requires tree-protection fencing for exterior work within critical root zones of protected oaks. Atlanta Urban Design Commission review on Inman Park, Grant Park, and Martin Luther King Jr. District adds 4-8 weeks on contributing structures.

Atlanta Seasonal Painting Windows

Atlanta exterior painting season is mid-March through mid-November with pollen-season interruption in late March to mid-April.
