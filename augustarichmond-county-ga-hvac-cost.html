HVAC in Augusta-Richmond County: what locals should know

🌡

Climate and your HVAC

Augusta-Richmond County's extreme summer heat and humidity mean your AC runs 7-8+ months a year. High-efficiency systems pay for themselves faster here than in most of the country.

⚙

System recommendation

In Augusta-Richmond County, a two-stage or variable-speed AC system runs longer at lower capacity, removing more humidity and using less energy than a single-stage unit cycling on and off.

📅

Best time to buy

The best time to replace your HVAC in Augusta-Richmond County is October through February. Contractors offer off-season pricing and can schedule sooner. Avoid June-September when demand spikes.

💡

Local tip

Many Augusta-Richmond County homes still have older R-22 refrigerant systems. If yours is one of them, now is the time to upgrade — R-22 is phased out and repair costs are skyrocketing.

Augusta-Richmond County hvac FAQ

Is a two-stage system worth the extra cost?

Two-stage and variable-speed systems run quieter, dehumidify better, and maintain more consistent temperatures. The $1,000-$2,500 premium pays back in 5-8 years through energy savings and comfort.

What is the difference between a heat pump and a furnace?

A heat pump moves heat using refrigerant and handles both heating and cooling. A furnace burns fuel to create heat and requires a separate AC unit for cooling.

Should I replace my AC and furnace at the same time?

Replacing both at once is usually more cost-effective. Matched systems run more efficiently, and you save on labor since the contractor is already on-site.
