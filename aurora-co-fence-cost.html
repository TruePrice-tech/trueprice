Common fencing questions in Aurora

What is the best fence for privacy?

A 6-foot solid board (dog-ear, board-on-board, or shadowbox) fence provides the most privacy. Board-on-board is best since it has no gaps even when boards shrink. Vinyl and composite also offer full privacy panels.

How much does a new fence cost in Aurora?

Fence costs in Aurora range from $15-$35 per linear foot for wood, $20-$40 for vinyl, $15-$30 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron, including materials and installation.

Should fence posts be set in concrete?

Yes, for most fences. Concrete-set posts are more stable and resist leaning. Some installers use compacted gravel for better drainage in wet climates, which can extend post life by reducing ground-level rot.

When is the best time to install a fence?

Late spring through early fall offers the best conditions for concrete curing and working in the ground. Avoid frozen ground and extremely wet seasons. Many contractors offer off-season discounts.

How long does fence installation take?

A standard 150-200 linear foot residential fence takes 1-3 days to install. Hillside or rocky soil adds time. Post holes need 24-48 hours to cure if set in concrete.

What shapes fence installation pricing in Aurora, CO

Why pricing varies in Aurora. Labor rates here run roughly 11% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For fence installation specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Aurora at roughly 9% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Aurora has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Freeze-thaw cycles in Aurora are the #1 killer of fence posts. Posts set above the frost line (36-48 inches in this area) heave within 1-3 winters. Proper depth, gravel drainage, and concrete footings are non-negotiable for a fence that lasts. In Aurora, material choice drives the total fence cost more than labor. A 150-linear-foot privacy fence ranges from $3,000-4,500 in pressure-treated pine to $5,500-8,500 in cedar to $6,000-10,000 in vinyl. The cost-per-year calculation often favors vinyl or composite despite the higher upfront price.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most fence installation projects in Aurora require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 345,803, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Aurora's rapid recent growth has tightened the contractor market — expect longer lead times and less negotiating room than smaller markets.

Local market conditions. Aurora's high growth rate means contractor demand consistently exceeds supply. Expect 3-6 week booking delays in season. Seasonal timing. Fence installation in Aurora requires workable ground — frozen soil makes post-hole digging impossible without equipment. The window runs April through November, with September-October offering the best combination of availability and weather.

What drives cost. With Aurora labor rates near the national median, the cost difference between a budget and premium 150-foot privacy fence comes down to materials and scope rather than labor premiums. Newer construction in Aurora (averaging 31 years) means fence installation projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay. Aurora's rapid growth means contractors can be selective about which jobs they take. Red flags to watch for. Watch for fence installation quotes in Aurora that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Check that any Aurora contractor doing fence installation carries both general liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers' compensation. Request certificates directly from the insurer, not just copies the contractor provides. Aurora's rapid growth attracts out-of-state contractors who follow the boom.
