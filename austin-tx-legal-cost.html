Legal costs in Austin, TX: what locals should know

Rate landscape

Attorney rates in Austin vary significantly by practice area and firm size. Solo practitioners and small firms typically charge 30-50% less than large firms for comparable work.

Fee structures

Many Austin attorneys offer flat fees for routine matters like uncontested divorces, wills, and LLC formations. Always ask about flat-fee options before accepting hourly billing.

Free legal help

Austin has multiple legal aid organizations offering free services for qualifying residents. Bar association referral services provide low-cost initial consultations.

Court access

Hyde Park, Travis Heights, South Congress residents can access small claims court for disputes without hiring an attorney, saving thousands in legal fees for straightforward claims.

Austin Attorney Hourly Rates by Practice Area

Attorney hourly rates in Austin range from $225-$475 for general practice, $350-$700+ for Congress Avenue and Second Street litigation firms, $200-$400 for family law, and $175-$350 for immigration. Technology and startup attorneys command $300-$600 given Austin's tech-hub growth. Solo practitioners in Round Rock, Cedar Park, and Kyle handle family law and criminal defense at the lower end.

Flat fees in Austin are standard for uncontested divorces ($1,000-$3,000), simple wills ($350-$1,500), LLC formations ($500-$1,000), traffic violations ($250-$1,200), and technology startup incorporation ($2,000-$5,000 including bylaws and equity plans). Texas title companies handle real estate closings without attorney involvement. Live-music and entertainment contract work is a niche flat-fee market in Austin.

Court Filing Costs and Small Claims in Austin

Family law petition filing costs $302. Probate filing costs $302-$370. Eviction filing costs $54. Travis County Justice Courts handle small claims and evictions.

Most Common Legal Disputes in Austin

The most common legal disputes in Austin are family law (custody, divorce, CPS cases), personal injury (auto accidents on I-35/MoPac/183, scooter injuries), landlord-tenant matters (eviction defense, rent disputes in a tight housing market), technology/employment law (non-competes, equity disputes, wrongful termination), and immigration (asylum, DACA, TPS).

Travis County District Court's civil backlog averages 12-18 months to trial. Family law cases average 8-12 months for contested custody. Justice Courts move at 30-60 days. Austin's rapid population growth has strained court capacity, and the county has added district courts in recent years to address the growing caseload.
