Healthcare in Austin, TX: what locals should know

Hospital landscape

Austin is served by multiple hospital systems competing for patients. Understanding which hospitals are in your insurance network before an emergency can save you thousands in out-of-network charges.

Insurance coverage

Check your plan's network carefully. In Austin, the difference between in-network and out-of-network charges for the same procedure can be 3-5x.

Negotiation leverage

Every Austin hospital has a financial assistance program. Ask for the self-pay rate before accepting any bill at face value. Most Austin hospitals offer 20-40% prompt-pay discounts.

Neighborhood access

Hyde Park, Travis Heights, South Congress residents have access to community health centers with sliding-fee scales for primary care, often at a fraction of ER costs.

Hospital systems and safety-net providers around Austin

Ascension Seton, Baylor Scott & White, St. David's HealthCare (HCA), and CommUnityCare Health Centers serve the Austin metro. Dell Seton Medical Center at UT is the Level I trauma center and teaching hospital. CommUnityCare is the largest FQHC in Central Texas and serves as the primary-care safety net for uninsured Travis County residents through a network of 28 clinic locations.

Travis County's uninsured rate is approximately 14%, lower than the Texas statewide rate but still significantly above the national average due to the state's refusal to expand Medicaid. An estimated 160,000 Travis County adults fall into the coverage gap between Medicaid eligibility and marketplace subsidies.
