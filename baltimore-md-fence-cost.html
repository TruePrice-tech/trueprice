Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a fence cost in Baltimore?

Most homeowners in Baltimore pay between $4,150 and $11,950 for fence installation, depending on material and yard size.

What is the cheapest fence material?

Chain link is the most affordable fencing option at $15-$25 per linear foot installed. Wood privacy fencing is the next most affordable at $25-$50 per linear foot.

How long does a wood fence last?

A well-maintained wood fence typically lasts 15-20 years. Regular staining and sealing every 2-3 years can significantly extend its lifespan. Cedar and redwood last longer than pine.

About fence installation in Baltimore, MD

Why pricing varies in Baltimore. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. For fence installation specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Baltimore at roughly 7% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Baltimore has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Cedar and pressure-treated pine both work; expect 15-20 year lifespan with annual sealing.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most fence installation projects in Baltimore require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. As a major metro of 622,104 residents, the local contractor market is deep — expect 10+ legitimate bidders on any standard residential job. Baltimore's stable population means contractor capacity rarely tightens, so off-season pricing (typically Nov-Feb) can save 10-15% on non-emergency work.
