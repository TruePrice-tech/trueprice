Frequently Asked Questions

How much does attic insulation cost in Baltimore?

Most homeowners in Baltimore pay between $1,250 and $5,450 for attic insulation, depending on type and coverage area.

Is spray foam insulation worth the extra cost?

Spray foam provides the highest R-value per inch and also acts as an air barrier. Closed-cell foam is best for moisture-prone areas, while blown-in is often the most cost-effective for attic coverage.

How much insulation do I need?

The DOE recommends R-38 to R-60 for attics in most climate zones. An energy audit can determine the exact R-value target for your home in Baltimore, MD.

Baltimore Wall Cavity Retrofit Approach

Baltimore wall assemblies vary by era: pre-1940 balloon-framed homes need dense-pack cellulose at 3.5 pcf plus horizontal firestop blocking. Zone 4A code requires R-13+5ci for walls. Do not inject closed-cell foam into brick-veneer walls because moisture trapping causes long-term damage.

Air Sealing Priorities in Baltimore

A typical pre-retrofit blower door in Baltimore reads 4,000-7,000 CFM50 because of balloon-framing leakage, unsealed laundry chutes, and open-stair chases. Priority sealing targets are: sill-plate-to-foundation joints, rim joist plane, top plate to drywall gaps, and plumbing chase penetrations. AeroBarrier is available through local applicators for challenging retrofit cases.

Energy Code Snapshot for Baltimore (Zone 4A)

Baltimore is in IECC Zone 4A requiring R-49 attic and R-13+5ci walls for new construction. Retrofit prescriptive requirements trigger when more than 50% of the envelope is opened. Local building code enforces IECC requirements through permit inspection. ACCA Manual J load calculations are required when HVAC is replaced as part of the scope.
