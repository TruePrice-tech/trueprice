Questions Baltimore homeowners ask about siding

How long does siding installation take?

A full siding replacement on an average home takes 5-10 days. Larger homes or complex designs with multiple gables, dormers, and trim details can take 2-3 weeks.

How do I maintain fiber cement siding?

Repaint every 12-15 years, caulk joints and seams every 5-7 years, keep vegetation trimmed 6 inches from the wall, and wash annually with a garden hose. Avoid power washing at high pressure.

Do I need a permit for siding replacement in Baltimore?

Most Baltimore jurisdictions require a permit if you are changing siding material or adding insulation. Like-for-like replacement without structural changes may be exempt. Check with your local building department.

What is board-and-batten siding?

Board-and-batten uses wide vertical boards with narrow strips (battens) covering the seams. It is popular in farmhouse and modern styles. Costs $8-$16/sqft installed in fiber cement or engineered wood.

Can new siding go over old siding?

Overlaying is possible but not recommended. It hides potential water damage, adds weight, and can void warranties. Tear-off adds $1,000-$3,000 but lets you inspect and repair sheathing and moisture barriers.

Baltimore-specific siding fraud patterns

Baltimore siding red flags: contractors who propose vinyl replacement on CHAP-designated historic rowhouse facades without disclosing that the Commission categorically prohibits vinyl on contributing structures (demonstrating either ignorance or disregard for the regulations that govern 37,000+ properties), formstone-removal operators who damage the underlying original brick through aggressive mechanical removal (careful hand-removal with scored chiseling preserves the 1870s-1920s brick face), siding installers who don't address the house-wrap weather-resistive barrier behind the siding (the Chesapeake humidity demands a functional WRB under all siding), and unlicensed city operators working under county registration in the jurisdictional gap created by the city-county split.
