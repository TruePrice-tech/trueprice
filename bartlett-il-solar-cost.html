Bartlett solar FAQ

Does Bartlett have any state or local solar incentives?

Incentives vary. Check the DSIRE database for Bartlett-specific rebates, SRECs, property tax exemptions, and performance-based incentives that stack on top of the federal 30% ITC.

How does the federal solar tax credit work?

The Investment Tax Credit (ITC) lets you deduct 30% of your total solar installation cost from your federal taxes. It applies to purchased systems, not leased. There is no cap on the credit amount.

How many panels do I need?

Panel count depends on your electricity usage, panel wattage, and roof sun exposure. A typical home uses 20-30 panels (400W each) to offset 80-100% of electricity consumption.

Does solar work in Bartlett's cold winters?

Solar works well in Bartlett despite the cold. Panels convert sunlight more efficiently at lower temperatures, partially offsetting shorter winter days. Net metering lets you bank summer overproduction as credits for winter months. Snow sheds from angled panels within a day or two in most cases. Higher local electricity rates and available incentives often make the payback comparable to warmer markets.

What solar incentives are available in Bartlett?

Every Bartlett homeowner installing solar qualifies for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit with no dollar limit. Beyond that, IL may offer state tax credits, utility-specific rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits, and property tax exemptions. The DSIRE database has the current list for your area. These stacked incentives typically reduce net cost by an additional 10-30%.

When should I install solar in Bartlett?

Bartlett homeowners should aim for late spring or summer to install solar. Weather cooperates for roof work and you maximize first-year production before winter. Allow 6-12 weeks from signing a contract through permitting, utility interconnection approval, and installation. Start gathering quotes 2-3 months before your target window. Remember that the 30% ITC applies to the tax year in which installation is completed.
