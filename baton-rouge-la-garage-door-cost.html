Garage Door questions for Baton Rouge homeowners

How much does garage door spring replacement cost?

Torsion spring replacement costs $200-$400 for parts and labor. Extension springs cost $150-$300. Never attempt spring replacement yourself since the stored tension is extremely dangerous.

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Baton Rouge?

Garage door replacement in Baton Rouge typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

Do I need a wind-rated garage door in Baton Rouge?

Baton Rouge building code requires it, and for good reason. A garage door is the biggest unbraced span in most homes, and a standard door failure during a hurricane creates internal pressure that can blow off the roof. Wind-rated doors handle 130+ mph loads and cost $300-$800 more than standard options. Many LA insurers offer discounts for wind-rated installations.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Baton Rouge?

For Baton Rouge homes averaging 35 years old, replacement is often the better investment if the door is original. A new insulated door with a modern opener costs $1,200-$3,000 and recovers 90-100% of its cost at resale. Repair makes sense for newer doors with localized damage (single panel, one spring, or opener motor replacement) when the rest of the system is sound.

Best time of year to replace a garage door in Baton Rouge?

For Baton Rouge, target fall or winter for your garage door replacement. Cooler weather makes the installation more comfortable and demand is lower, and off-peak scheduling often means 5-10% savings on installation. Spring is the peak rush since cold weather causes spring failures that flood installers with emergency calls.

Local Garage Door Guide for Baton Rouge, LA

Garage doors in Baton Rouge's humid climate age differently than manufacturers' rated lifespans suggest. Corrosion, weatherstrip degradation, and mildew on panel interiors all accelerate here. Hardware selection and garage ventilation matter as much as the door model itself.

Baton Rouge is in a hurricane exposure zone, which means building codes require wind-rated garage doors. A standard door will fail under hurricane-force winds, and the resulting pressure change can lift the entire roof structure. Wind-rated doors cost 20-40% more but are required by code and may lower your insurance premium. If your current door is not wind-rated, upgrading it is one of the most cost-effective storm protection investments you can make.

Many Baton Rouge homes (average age 35 years) have garage doors and openers that predate modern safety standards. Openers installed before 1993 may lack auto-reverse sensors, which are now required by federal law. If your opener does not have photo-eye sensors at the base of the door, replace the opener regardless of whether it still functions since this is a serious safety and liability issue.
