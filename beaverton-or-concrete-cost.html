Beaverton concrete FAQ

How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

How much does concrete work cost in Beaverton?

Concrete costs in Beaverton range from $6-$12 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, patios) to $15-$30/sqft for stamped or decorative finishes, including labor and materials.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

Local conditions. Beaverton has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Beaverton's constant moisture promotes moss and algae growth on concrete surfaces. Annual pressure washing and sealer application maintain appearance and prevent the biological growth that makes walkways slippery. Standard mixes work well — freeze-thaw additives are unnecessary in this mild climate. Homes in Beaverton averaging 32 years old typically have stable sub-grades that simplify concrete work. Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Beaverton require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. At 93,542 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. The Beaverton market has grown at a steady pace, producing a solid base of experienced contractors without the severe backlogs of booming cities.
