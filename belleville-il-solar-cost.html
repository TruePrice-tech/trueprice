Frequently asked solar questions

Can I add a battery to my solar system?

Yes. Adding a home battery (10-13 kWh) costs $8,000-$15,000 installed. Batteries provide backup power during outages and can help avoid peak-rate charges in time-of-use markets.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

Do solar panels increase home value?

Owned solar systems add roughly $15,000-$20,000 to home value according to national studies. Leased systems do not add value and can complicate the sale process.

Is solar worth it in Belleville?

Solar is a strong investment for most Belleville homes, with typical payback in 6-10 years followed by 15-20 years of free electricity. The 30% federal tax credit cuts the net cost substantially. Your specific return depends on roof orientation, shading, energy consumption, and local utility rates. Three quotes with site-specific production estimates give you the data to decide.

What solar incentives are available in Belleville?

Every Belleville homeowner installing solar qualifies for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit with no dollar limit. Beyond that, IL may offer state tax credits, utility-specific rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits, and property tax exemptions. The DSIRE database has the current list for your area. These stacked incentives typically reduce net cost by an additional 10-30%.

When should I install solar in Belleville?

Belleville homeowners should aim for early spring or fall to install solar. Moderate weather conditions for installation and you avoid the peak-demand scheduling crunch. Allow 6-12 weeks from signing a contract through permitting, utility interconnection approval, and installation. Start gathering quotes 2-3 months before your target window. Remember that the 30% ITC applies to the tax year in which installation is completed.
