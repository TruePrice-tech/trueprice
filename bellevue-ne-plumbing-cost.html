Plumbing in Bellevue: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Bellevue has moderately hard water which can reduce water heater lifespan over time. Consider a water softener or tankless unit with a descaling system to protect your investment.

❄

Freeze risk

Bellevue gets cold enough for frozen pipes to be a regular concern. Insulate pipes in crawlspaces, garages, and exterior walls. When temperatures drop below 20°F, let faucets drip slightly overnight.

🔧

Material recommendation

PEX piping is the top recommendation for repiping in Bellevue. It's flexible, resists freezing better than rigid pipes (it expands slightly), and typically costs 30-40% less than copper to install.

💡

Local tip

Many Bellevue homes built before 1970 have galvanized steel pipes that corrode from the inside out. If your water pressure has been steadily dropping or you see rusty water, a whole-house repipe is likely needed.
