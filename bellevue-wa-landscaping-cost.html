Bellevue landscaping FAQ

How much does mulch cost?

Bulk mulch costs $25-$50 per cubic yard delivered. Plan 3 inches deep and 1 yard covers 108 sqft. A 1,000 sqft bed needs about 10 yards ($250-$500). Professional installation adds $30-$50/yard.

How much does a sprinkler system cost?

A residential irrigation system costs $2,500-$5,000 for a quarter-acre lot. Smart controllers add $100-$300 and reduce water usage 20-40%. Drip irrigation zones for beds cost $500-$1,000 extra.

Should I hire a landscaper or landscape architect?

A landscaper handles installation and maintenance. A landscape architect designs complex projects and manages grading, drainage, and permits. For projects over $15,000 or involving structural elements, an architect is worthwhile.

How much does lawn installation cost?

Sod costs $1-$2 per square foot installed. Hydroseeding costs $0.05-$0.10/sqft. Traditional seeding costs $0.10-$0.25/sqft but takes 6-8 weeks to establish. A 5,000 sqft lawn costs $5,000-$10,000 in sod.

More questions about landscaping in Bellevue

How much does mulch cost per yard?

Bulk mulch costs $25-$50 per cubic yard delivered. Plan 3 inches deep — one cubic yard covers about 108 sq ft. A 1,000 sq ft bed needs roughly 10 yards ($250-$500 in materials) plus $30-$50/yard for professional spreading and edging.

What does a sprinkler system cost in Bellevue?

A residential irrigation system in Bellevue costs $2,500-$5,000 for a quarter-acre lot with 4-6 zones. Smart wifi controllers add $100-$300 and cut water usage 20-40%. Drip-irrigation zones for planting beds cost $500-$1,000 extra and are worth it on shrubs and perennials.

How much does sod installation cost?

Sod costs $1-$2 per square foot installed including soil prep and rolling. Hydroseeding runs $0.05-$0.10/sqft and establishes in 4-6 weeks. Traditional broadcast seeding is the cheapest at $0.10-$0.25/sqft but needs 6-8 weeks and frequent watering. A 5,000 sq ft lawn lands between $5,000 and $10,000 in sod.

Do I need a permit for landscaping in Bellevue?

Most landscaping in Bellevue — planting, mulching, sod, basic irrigation — does not need a permit. Retaining walls over 4 feet, drainage tied to the storm system, tree removal in some neighborhoods, and any grading that changes lot drainage usually do. Confirm with your landscaper before they start digging.

Local factors: landscaping in Bellevue, WA

What local homes look like in Bellevue. With homes averaging just 31 years old, Bellevue has a relatively new building stock. Most landscaping work involves upgrades and efficiency improvements rather than end-of-life replacements. Code compliance is rarely an issue since these homes were built to recent standards.

Compliance and HOA notes. HOA rules affect some but not all Bellevue homeowners. If applicable, confirm your HOA's requirements for landscaping before getting quotes. This avoids the situation where your preferred material or color gets rejected after you have already signed a contract.

Local trade economics. With steady but not explosive growth, Bellevue's contractor market offers reasonable availability. Most shops can schedule work within 1-3 weeks, and shopping for quotes is straightforward. Bellevue's large market supports full-service landscape design firms, installation-only crews, and maintenance companies. For projects over $8,000, a dedicated design firm with installation capability typically provides better results than splitting design and installation across separate companies.

Seasonal timing. Bellevue offers a longer landscaping season than cold-climate markets. The busiest period is March through June when spring planting demand peaks. Fall (October-December) is an excellent time for plantings, hardscape projects, and irrigation system installation at lower competition and often better pricing.

What drives cost. The biggest factor in Bellevue landscaping project pricing is labor cost, running 42% above national benchmarks. For a full yard makeover, that premium alone accounts for $1680-3360 in additional cost. Newer construction in Bellevue (averaging 31 years) means landscaping project projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay.

Red flags to watch for. In Bellevue, verify your landscaping project contractor pulls the permit themselves — never pull it in your own name. If they ask you to pull the permit, they may not be properly licensed to do the work. Be cautious of landscaping project contractors in Bellevue who pressure you to sign same-day. Legitimate contractors expect you to get competing bids and will hold their price for 30 days. High-pressure sales tactics correlate with inflated pricing.
