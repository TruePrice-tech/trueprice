Bellingham roofing: answers to common questions

Why do roofing quotes vary so much?

Quotes differ based on material quality, labor assumptions, flashing work, ventilation, and warranty coverage. The cheapest bid frequently skips items that become change orders mid-project.

How do I know if my roof deck needs replacing?

Signs include sagging between rafters, soft or spongy spots when walked on, visible water stains from the attic, and rotted or delaminating plywood. Your roofer checks this during tear-off.

What roofing material is best for Bellingham?

Material choice depends on Bellingham's climate, your budget, and desired lifespan. Architectural shingles offer the best balance of cost and durability for most homeowners.

What is a roofing square?

A roofing square equals 100 square feet. A typical 2,000 sqft roof is about 20 squares. Contractors price materials and labor per square.

How long does a roof last in Bellingham?

Expect architectural asphalt shingles to last 20-25 years in Bellingham, with 3-tab shingles at 15-20 years, metal roofing at 40-60, and tile at 50+. The biggest lifespan variable here is UV exposure since it accelerates granule loss and material breakdown. Adequate attic ventilation and lighter-colored materials both help counteract this.

Bellingham permits: required to replace my roof?

Most Bellingham jurisdictions require a permit for a complete roof replacement. This is the contractor's responsibility to obtain before work starts. Skipping permits creates real risks: insurance coverage gaps, code violation liability, and mandatory disclosure when selling.

When is the best time to replace a roof in Bellingham?

The optimal window for roof replacement in Bellingham is late summer or early fall, stable weather allows proper curing and contractors are less backlogged than spring. Scheduling during off-peak periods can save 10-15% on labor costs since contractors have more availability and may offer competitive pricing to fill their calendar. Avoid scheduling immediately after a major storm event when contractor demand (and prices) spike.
