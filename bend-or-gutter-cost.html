What Bend residents ask about gutter

How much do new gutters cost in Bend?

Gutters in Bend cost $6-$15 per linear foot for aluminum seamless, $8-$20 for steel, and $15-$30 for copper. A typical home (150-200 linear feet) runs $1,200-$3,000 for aluminum.

What causes gutters to pull away from the house?

Common causes: rotted fascia board behind the gutter, failed or spaced-too-far-apart hangers, ice and snow weight, clogged gutters holding heavy water, and improper original installation.

Do gutters protect the foundation?

Yes. Gutters prevent concentrated roof runoff from eroding soil next to the foundation and saturating the ground. Homes without gutters have 3-5x more foundation moisture problems than homes with properly functioning gutter systems.

What Bend homeowners should know about gutter installation

Why pricing varies in Bend. Labor rates here run roughly 15% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. Our cost model shows gutter installation in Bend tracking right at the national baseline.

Bend's site and climate factors. Bend has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Ice dams are the primary gutter concern in Bend. Heated gutter cables ($400-800 installed per 50 feet) prevent the ice backup that tears gutters off eaves and drives water behind siding. Ice-and-water shield membrane on the eave edge (6+ feet up from the gutter line) provides the secondary defense. Seamless aluminum gutters are the Bend standard: $6-12 per linear foot installed. Copper ($20-40/ft) and zinc ($15-25/ft) offer 50+ year lifespans but at 3-4x the cost. For most homeowners, aluminum with a quality finish provides the best value at 20-30 years before replacement.

Bend paperwork, inspections, and licensing. Most gutter installation projects in Bend require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Who's doing the work in Bend. With 81,236 people, Bend's contractor pool is mid-sized. You will find 4-6 established shops for most trades, which provides enough competition for fair pricing. Bend's expansion has outpaced trade workforce growth. The practical effect: longer booking windows and firmer pricing than you would find in slower-growth cities.
