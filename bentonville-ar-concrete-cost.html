What Bentonville residents ask about concrete

What is stamped concrete?

Stamped concrete uses patterned mats pressed into wet concrete to mimic brick, stone, slate, or wood. It costs $12-$25/sqft installed. It requires resealing every 2-3 years to maintain appearance and prevent wear.

What causes concrete to crack?

Common causes: shrinkage during curing, frost heave from inadequate base prep, tree roots, heavy vehicle loads beyond design, poor control joint placement, and water pooling due to improper grading.

Can cracked concrete be repaired?

Hairline cracks can be sealed with flexible caulk or epoxy for $1-$5 per linear foot. Wider cracks may need patching compound. If the slab has heaved or settled significantly, mudjacking ($5-$10/sqft) or replacement is needed.

How long does concrete last?

Properly poured and maintained concrete lasts 30-50 years. Driveways see 25-30 years with normal use. Sealing every 2-3 years and controlling drainage significantly extend the lifespan.

How much does a concrete patio cost?

A 300 sqft concrete patio costs $1,800-$3,600 for basic broom finish. Stamped patterns add $3-$8/sqft. Colored concrete adds $1-$3/sqft. An exposed aggregate finish adds $2-$4/sqft.

Where Bentonville's pricing comes from. Bentonville's labor market runs about 13% below the national median for skilled trades. This wage differential is the primary driver of the area's below-average project costs. On concrete work, Bentonville comes in about 8% under the national average based on our metro pricing data.

Local conditions in Bentonville. Bentonville has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Bentonville's freeze-thaw cycles and moderate rainfall both affect concrete performance. Air-entrained mix is standard for outdoor flatwork, and expansion joints every 10-12 feet prevent the cracking that temperature swings cause. Proper drainage away from slabs prevents the water infiltration that freeze-thaw cycles exploit. Standard concrete in Bentonville runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Bentonville permits and code requirements. Permits are usually required for concrete work in Bentonville; verify the specifics with the local building department before committing.

The Bentonville contractor pool. At 40,167 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. The Bentonville area's booming growth means contractors have more work than they can handle. This shifts pricing power toward the contractor side of the table.
