Common landscaping questions in Bentonville

Does landscaping increase home value?

Well-designed landscaping adds 5-15% to home value. Mature trees alone add $1,000-$10,000 each. Curb appeal landscaping recovers 100-200% of investment at resale, one of the highest ROI improvements.

How much does tree removal cost?

Small trees (under 30 feet) cost $200-$500 to remove. Medium trees (30-60 feet) cost $500-$1,000. Large trees (60-100 feet) cost $1,000-$2,000+. Stump grinding adds $100-$400 per stump.

How much does outdoor lighting cost?

Professional landscape lighting costs $2,000-$5,000 for path lights, uplights, and accent lighting. LED fixtures cost more upfront but last 50,000+ hours. Solar options cost less but provide dimmer, less reliable light.

How much does a retaining wall cost?

Retaining walls cost $20-$50 per square face foot for concrete block, $25-$60 for natural stone, and $15-$30 for timber. A 50 linear foot wall at 3 feet tall runs $3,000-$9,000 installed.

What is xeriscaping?

Xeriscaping uses drought-tolerant plants, efficient irrigation, and permeable surfaces to minimize water use. It cuts outdoor water consumption 50-75% and is increasingly required in water-restricted regions.

Where Bentonville's pricing comes from. Bentonville's labor market runs about 13% below the national median for skilled trades. This wage differential is the primary driver of the area's below-average project costs. On landscaping, Bentonville comes in about 10% under the national average based on our metro pricing data.

Local conditions in Bentonville. Bentonville has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Bentonville's moderate climate supports a wide range of landscaping options. The mix of native perennials and structured plantings performs well with standard irrigation. Avoid high-water exotic species that struggle with summer humidity and winter dormancy transitions. A complete landscaping project in Bentonville typically breaks down as: plant material (30-40%), hardscape/materials (20-30%), labor (25-35%), and design (5-10%). For budget-conscious homeowners, phasing the project over 2-3 seasons lets plants establish naturally and reduces upfront costs.

Bentonville permits and code requirements. Permits are usually required for landscaping in Bentonville; verify the specifics with the local building department before committing.

The Bentonville contractor pool. At 40,167 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. The Bentonville area's booming growth means contractors have more work than they can handle. This shifts pricing power toward the contractor side of the table.
