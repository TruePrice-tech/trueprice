Frequently asked gutter questions

How long do gutters last?

Aluminum gutters last 20-30 years. Steel lasts 15-20 years. Copper lasts 50+ years. Vinyl lasts 10-15 years. Seamless gutters outlast sectional because they have fewer leak-prone joints.

Should I repair or replace my gutters?

Repair if damage is limited to a few sections, joints, or downspouts. Replace if gutters sag along most of their length, have multiple leaks, are rusting through, or the fascia behind them is rotting.

How much do new gutters cost in Billings?

Gutters in Billings cost $6-$15 per linear foot for aluminum seamless, $8-$20 for steel, and $15-$30 for copper. A typical home (150-200 linear feet) runs $1,200-$3,000 for aluminum.

What causes gutters to pull away from the house?

Common causes: rotted fascia board behind the gutter, failed or spaced-too-far-apart hangers, ice and snow weight, clogged gutters holding heavy water, and improper original installation.

gutter installation pricing explained: Billings, MT

Why pricing varies in Billings. Labor rates here run roughly 11% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Billings has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Billings's heavy snow loads require commercial-grade gutter hangers on 18-inch centers (standard is 24-36 inches). Ice dam cables ($600-1,200 installed per 50 feet) are essential on north-facing eaves. Some homeowners skip gutters entirely due to ice load damage — this creates foundation drainage problems that are more expensive than the gutter damage they avoid. Seamless aluminum gutters are the Billings standard: $6-12 per linear foot installed. Copper ($20-40/ft) and zinc ($15-25/ft) offer 50+ year lifespans but at 3-4x the cost. For most homeowners, aluminum with a quality finish provides the best value at 20-30 years before replacement.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most gutter installation projects in Billings require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 109,059, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Billings has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.
