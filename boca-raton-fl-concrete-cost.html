Frequently asked concrete questions

Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Boca Raton?

In Boca Raton, permits are typically required for driveways, sidewalks, and any concrete work near property lines or public right-of-way. Backyard patios may be exempt depending on size. Check local requirements.

What is stamped concrete?

Stamped concrete uses patterned mats pressed into wet concrete to mimic brick, stone, slate, or wood. It costs $12-$25/sqft installed. It requires resealing every 2-3 years to maintain appearance and prevent wear.

More questions about concrete in Boca Raton

What does a concrete driveway cost per square foot?

Plain 4-inch concrete runs $6-$12 per square foot installed in 2026. Stamped concrete adds $4-$8/sqft for pattern and color. Heavy-duty 6-inch driveways for RVs or trucks cost $9-$15/sqft. A 600 sq ft two-car driveway typically lands between $5,800 and $8,100.

How much does a 1,000 sq ft concrete patio cost?

Typical 2026 ranges for a 1,000 sq ft project: plain broom-finish $8,000-$12,000, exposed-aggregate $9,500-$14,500, stamped $11,000-$16,500. Tearing out an existing slab adds $1,500-$3,000. Reinforcing wire mesh and rebar are usually $0.50-$1.00/sqft and worth it on driveways, optional on patios.

What does a slab foundation cost in Boca Raton?

A monolithic slab foundation for a typical single-family house in Boca Raton runs $5,000-$15,000 depending on square footage and soil conditions. A garage slab is $2,500-$6,000. Full basement foundations are a different scope entirely and run $15,000-$30,000.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Boca Raton?

Driveways, patios over a certain size, and any structural slab in Boca Raton require a permit and an inspection of the base prep and rebar before the pour. Sidewalks, repairs, and resurfacing usually do not. Your contractor pulls the permit — never pull it in your own name.

Local factors: concrete work in Boca Raton, FL

The Boca Raton housing picture. Local market conditions. Rapid growth in Boca Raton has stretched the local contractor workforce thin. When to schedule. Boca Raton allows year-round concrete work, but the ideal window is spring and fall when temperatures support proper curing without the heat-related challenges of summer. Avoid scheduling large pours during the hottest months — early morning pours are the only reliable option.

What drives cost. With Boca Raton labor rates near the national median, the cost difference between a budget and premium driveway or patio pour comes down to materials and scope rather than labor premiums.
