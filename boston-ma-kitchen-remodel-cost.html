Frequently asked kitchen remodel questions

Should I hire a general contractor or manage subs myself?

A GC adds 15-25% overhead but manages scheduling, permits, and quality across trades. Self-managing saves money but requires significant time and construction knowledge to coordinate plumbing, electrical, and carpentry.

How long does a kitchen remodel take?

A cosmetic refresh takes 2-4 weeks. A mid-range remodel runs 6-10 weeks. A full gut renovation with layout changes takes 12-16 weeks. Permit delays and backordered materials can add 2-4 weeks.

What is the average cost per square foot for a kitchen remodel?

Budget remodels run $75-$150/sqft, mid-range $150-$300/sqft, and high-end $300-$500+/sqft. A 150 sqft kitchen at mid-range would cost $22,500-$45,000.

Should I get multiple quotes?

Always get at least 3 written quotes. Compare line-item details, not just total price. The lowest quote often omits scope items that appear as change orders later.

How do I find a good kitchen remodel contractor in Boston?

Check licensing through your state's contractor board, verify insurance, read reviews from Boston homeowners, and get at least 3 written quotes with line-item detail.

How much should I pay upfront?

Never pay more than 10-15% as a deposit before work begins. Standard payment structure: deposit, progress payment when materials arrive, final payment on completion.

Boston Kitchen Stock and Typical Layouts

Boston kitchen stock runs from 1900s-1920s triple-decker multifamily in Dorchester, Roxbury, and South Boston (90-130 sqft with back-stair service kitchen) through 1940s-60s Cape and split-level in Newton, Brookline, and Needham (140-200 sqft) to 2000s suburban colonial-revival in Weston, Wellesley, and Lexington (250-400 sqft). Back Bay and South End brownstone condos have 80-130 sqft kitchens. Cambridge and Somerville triple-deckers have compact 90-120 sqft kitchens with original pantry.

Triple-decker construction means each floor is a separate unit and plumbing stacks cross unit boundaries. Wall-removal requires structural engineering letters filed with ISD. Brownstone bearing walls in Back Bay and South End are extremely thick (18-24 inches) and require extensive masonry work for any opening. Boston frost depth of 48 inches affects any exterior chase footing work. HOA architectural review in Weston and Wellesley is informal but peer-pressure-enforced through neighborhood aesthetics.
