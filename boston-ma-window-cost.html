Boston-specific window fraud patterns

Boston window fraud patterns concentrate around missing CSL (Construction Supervisor License) when the job involves structural opening modification. Many Boston contractors hold HIC registration but not CSL, and the two are distinct. Structural work (enlarging, reducing, or reframing openings) requires CSL, and a mass.gov lookup confirms the specific license class and effective dates. Boston's Mass Save rebate program also requires verified licensing, and rebate applications for work performed without proper licensing are rejected post-install, leaving the homeowner responsible for the full cost.

Boston window scam patterns and contractor verification

Boston window scam patterns: contractors holding only HIC registration but not Construction Supervisor License (CSL) where structural opening work is involved (both are required for structural work), Back Bay Purple Glass claims without documented manganese-chemistry source, "Mass Save rebate-qualified" claims with products that fail ENERGY STAR Northern-zone verification, and pre-1978 home work without MA Deleading compliance. Legitimate Boston installers provide written HIC registration AND CSL numbers (both verifiable at mass.gov), MA Deleader license on pre-1978 child-occupied work, and both licenses active at contract-signing date.
