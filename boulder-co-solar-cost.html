Solar questions for Boulder homeowners

How long does solar take to pay for itself in Boulder?

Payback in Boulder depends on electricity rates, sun hours, and incentives. Most systems pay back in 6-10 years and then generate free electricity for another 15-20 years.

What is the difference between string inverters and microinverters?

String inverters are cheaper but the whole array drops output when one panel is shaded. Microinverters optimize each panel independently, cost 15-20% more, and last longer.

Should I buy or lease solar panels?

Buying maximizes long-term savings and increases home value. Leasing eliminates upfront cost but the leasing company keeps the tax credit, and panels may complicate home sales.

Does solar work in Boulder's cold winters?

Cold weather actually improves solar panel efficiency since photovoltaic cells perform better at lower temperatures. Boulder's shorter winter days reduce seasonal output, but net metering lets you bank summer surplus for winter use. Snow typically clears from tilted panels within 1-2 days. Between higher electricity rates and incentive programs, the financial return in Boulder often matches sunbelt installations.

What solar incentives are available in Boulder?

Start with the 30% federal ITC, which applies to all Boulder solar installations with no cap. Then check DSIRE for CO-level incentives: state tax credits, utility rebates, SREC markets, property tax exemptions, and net metering terms. Each utility has different programs and rates. The combined federal-plus-state incentive stack typically reduces net cost by 40-60% in markets with strong state programs.

What's the best season to install solar in Boulder?

The best installation window in Boulder is late spring or summer, weather cooperates for roof work and you maximize first-year production before winter. Note that the process from quote to installation takes 6-12 weeks including permitting, utility approval, and scheduling. Start the process 2-3 months before your target installation date. The federal tax credit applies to the year of installation, so timing relative to tax year can matter.
