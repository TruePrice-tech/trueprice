Roofing questions for Bountiful homeowners

Do I need a permit for roof replacement in Bountiful?

Yes. Most Bountiful jurisdictions require a building permit for roof replacement. Your contractor should handle the permit and schedule inspection.

What is the difference between architectural and 3-tab shingles?

Architectural shingles are thicker, have a dimensional look, and last 25-30 years. 3-tab shingles are flat, thinner, and last 15-20 years but cost 30-40% less upfront.

Does homeowners insurance cover roof replacement?

Insurance covers sudden damage (storms, falling trees) but not wear-and-tear or maintenance neglect. Verify your policy covers replacement cost, not depreciated value.

How do I prevent ice dams on my Bountiful roof?

The root cause of ice dams is heat leaking into the attic, not the roof material itself. In Bountiful, you need R-49+ attic insulation and continuous ventilation from soffit to ridge to keep the roof deck cold. Any roofing quote here should include ice and water shield on at least the first 3-6 feet from every eave. Replacing the roof without fixing the thermal envelope underneath just delays the same problem.

Bountiful permits: required to replace my roof?

Yes, Bountiful generally requires a building permit for full roof replacements. Your roofer should pull this as part of the job. Without a permit, your insurance coverage may be compromised and you face disclosure obligations if you sell the home.

When is the best time to replace a roof in Bountiful?

Bountiful homeowners should target late spring or early fall for roof replacement work. Off-season scheduling typically saves 10-15% compared to peak demand periods, especially the weeks immediately following severe weather events.
