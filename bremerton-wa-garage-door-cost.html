Frequently asked garage door questions

What is the best type of garage door?

Insulated steel with polyurethane foam offers the best balance of durability, energy efficiency, and cost. R-value of 12-18 keeps the garage 10-20 degrees closer to house temperature.

Can I insulate my existing garage door?

Yes. Retrofit insulation kits ($100-$200 DIY) add R-4 to R-8. However, adding weight to a non-insulated door may require spring adjustment. A new insulated door is often a better investment.

How much does a garage door opener cost?

A new opener installed costs $250-$600 for chain or belt drive. Smart openers with Wi-Fi and battery backup run $400-$800 installed. Replacing the opener alone takes 2-3 hours.

What is the difference between chain and belt drive openers?

Chain drive is cheaper ($150-$250) but louder. Belt drive ($200-$350) is whisper-quiet and ideal when bedrooms are above or adjacent to the garage. Both have similar reliability.

How often should I maintain my garage door in Bremerton?

Twice-yearly lubrication of springs, hinges, rollers, and tracks with silicone spray keeps the mechanism running smoothly. Test the safety auto-reverse with a 2x4 under the door at least annually. Inspect weatherstripping for wear, and tighten all mounting hardware since door vibration loosens bolts gradually. Budget $100-$200 for a professional tune-up every 2-3 years.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Bremerton?

Given that Bremerton homes average 35 years old, many original garage doors are due for replacement rather than repair. A new insulated door and modern opener runs $1,200-$3,000 and recoups 90-100% at resale. Repairs make financial sense for doors under 15 years old with specific damage, but throwing money at an aging system usually delays an inevitable replacement.

When should I replace a garage door in Bremerton?

Bremerton homeowners get the best pricing and selection by scheduling garage door replacement during spring or early fall. Dry conditions for proper weatherstrip adhesion, and you can save 5-10% versus peak season pricing. Spring demand surges from cold-weather spring failures, so plan ahead to avoid the rush.
