Common fencing questions in Caldwell

Who owns the fence between two properties?

Typically the homeowner who installed it. If the fence sits exactly on the property line, both neighbors share responsibility. Always get a survey before building to confirm the boundary.

What is the difference between dog-ear and flat-top fence?

Dog-ear pickets have angled cuts at the top for water runoff and a traditional look. Flat-top pickets have a clean, modern appearance but hold slightly more water, which can accelerate rot.

Is a Caldwell permit needed to build a fence?

Most Caldwell jurisdictions require a permit for fences over 6 feet tall. Many require permits for any fence. You also need to check setback requirements, HOA rules, and call 811 for utility locates before digging.

Does a fence increase property value?

A well-built fence adds 1-5% to property value depending on material and neighborhood norms. Privacy fencing adds more value in neighborhoods where it is expected. Chain link adds minimal value.

How deep should fence posts be in Caldwell?

Frost heave is the main threat to fence posts in Caldwell. Posts need to reach at least 6 inches below the local frost line, typically 36-48 inches total depth. Use 8-10 inches of compacted gravel at the bottom for drainage, then concrete to grade. Cutting depth to save cost is a false economy since shallow posts in Caldwell start leaning within 2-3 winters.

Is a Caldwell permit needed to build a fence?

Check with Caldwell's building department, but permits are generally required for fences, especially those above 6 feet. Before digging post holes, you need utility locates (call 811) and confirmed property lines. If boundaries are uncertain, a survey ($300-$600) prevents expensive neighbor disputes. Your contractor should pull the permit.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Caldwell?

Expect to pay $20-$35/ft for wood privacy fencing in Caldwell, $25-$45 for vinyl, $15-$25 for chain link, and $30-$60 for aluminum or wrought iron.
