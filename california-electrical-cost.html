Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in California (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in California typically run $3,200–$7,800 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2020 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Service entrance design assumes a 200A minimum on new residential construction. Pacific Gas & Electric and Southern California Edison Public Safety Power Shutoff (PSPS) events across the Hayward, San Andreas, and Diablo fault zones have made whole-house transfer switches and battery backup interconnection a standard line item on coastal and foothill panel upgrades — the 2022 CEC requires bonding and grounding of any battery storage system to the main service ground, with seismic flexible conduit at every motor or load-center connection in mapped liquefaction zones.

California code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2020 NEC — dwelling unit AFCI on most 120V branch circuits, GFCI expanded
  • State amendments: California Electrical Code (CCR Title 24 Part 3, 2022 edition) is based on 2020 NEC with Title 24 Part 6 energy and PV interconnection amendments — solar-ready, EV-ready, and battery-ready conduit are mandatory on new residential construction
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: Low — Pacific coastal and high desert minimal flash density
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 18-to-24-inch design depth on buried service conduit

California licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: California Contractors State License Board — C-10 (Electrical) (official site)
  • Permit: city electrical permit required; Title 24 + CALGreen efficiency and solar-readiness verification on new fixtures

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in California

Pacific Gas & Electric and Southern California Edison Public Safety Power Shutoff (PSPS) events across the Hayward, San Andreas, and Diablo fault zones have made whole-house transfer switches and battery backup interconnection a standard line item on coastal and foothill panel upgrades — the 2022 CEC requires bonding and grounding of any battery storage system to the main service ground, with seismic flexible conduit at every motor or load-center connection in mapped liquefaction zones.

State-specific code or insurance rule: California is the only state where every new single-family home built since the 2019 Title 24 cycle must include photovoltaic generation sized to annual electricity demand under CCR Title 24 Part 6 §150.1(c)14 — and Senate Bill 49 plus Title 24 §150.0(o) layer EV-ready and battery-ready raceway requirements onto every panel upgrade, so CSLB C-10 contractors routinely upsize a 100A service to 200A specifically to meet PV + EVSE + battery demand factors regardless of immediate equipment install.
