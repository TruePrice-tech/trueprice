Garage Door Replacement Cost in California (2026)

Garage door replacements in California typically run $1,600–$5,400 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 100 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. California's combination of Pacific salt-spray on every coastal exposure from Crescent City to San Diego driving stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track replacement at 7-10 year intervals on every coastal residential garage-door installation, CAL FIRE Tier 3 WUI overlay across the Sierra foothills additionally requiring Class A fire-rated garage-door specification on every WUI residential property, very-high HOA architectural-control prevalence across 60%+ of California subdivisions requiring pre-approval review on door color/material/style, and high TPCT termite zone 2 pressure across central and southern California drives unique fire-and-corrosion-and-HOA-driven garage-door material selection.

California climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 100 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): High — TPCT zone 2, PT wood jamb framing required
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Very high — 80%+ of subdivisions enforce HOA architectural-committee pre-approval on door color/material/style
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High — coastal salt-spray drives stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track requirement at 5-7 year intervals
  • IECC climate zone: 3B-4B

California licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: California Contractors State License Board (CSLB) — D-28 Doors, Gates & Activating Devices specialty (official site)
  • Permit: city building permit required for new garage-door opening on new construction; CSLB D-28 specialty license required above $500 contract; replacement-only installs require permit if structural modification involved; HOA architectural committee approval mandatory in 60%+ of California subdivisions; CALGreen Title 24 Part 11 mandatory recycled-content review on every door installation

How garage door installation costs vary in California

State-specific code or insurance rule: California Contractors State License Board (CSLB) §7058 imposes one of the most credential-segmented residential contractor licensing schemes in the country with a dedicated D-28 Doors, Gates & Activating Devices specialty classification covering garage-door installation, gate-operator installation, and access-control system installation, and CALGreen Title 24 Part 11 separately requires recycled-content review on every garage-door installation specifying the source-reduction or recycled-content percentage of the steel skin, plus California Energy Code Title 24 Part 6 mandates UL-listed photo-eye reversing-edge sensors and listing on every UL 325-compliant residential opener installed in California.
