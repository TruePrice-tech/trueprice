Gutter Installation Cost in California (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in California typically runs $1,400–$5,400, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 22 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. California's 22-inch state-average annual rainfall masks dramatic regional variation from Crescent City's 65 inches/year to Death Valley's 2 inches/year — coastal Northern California requires 6-inch K-style and triple-downspout schedules while inland Central Valley and Southern California default to 5-inch — and CAL FIRE Tier 3 WUI overlay across the Sierra foothills, North Coast, and Santa Monica Mountains has driven ember-resistant gutter guard retrofit on tens of thousands of homes since the 2017-2020 fire seasons.

California rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 22 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 10 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: Low — occasional freeze events, minimal ice-dam history
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: Moderate — mixed canopy, gutter guards optional

California licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: California Contractors State License Board — B (General Building) or C-43 (Sheet Metal) for gutter work (official site)
  • Permit: city building permit not typically required for gutter-only installation; CAL FIRE WUI screening required in mapped fire-hazard zones

How gutter installation costs vary in California

California's 22-inch state-average annual rainfall masks dramatic regional variation from Crescent City's 65 inches/year to Death Valley's 2 inches/year — coastal Northern California requires 6-inch K-style and triple-downspout schedules while inland Central Valley and Southern California default to 5-inch — and CAL FIRE Tier 3 WUI overlay across the Sierra foothills, North Coast, and Santa Monica Mountains has driven ember-resistant gutter guard retrofit on tens of thousands of homes since the 2017-2020 fire seasons.

State-specific code or insurance rule: California Building Code Chapter 7A §706A.4 requires WUI-mapped properties to install ember-resistant metal gutter screens or filled-mesh gutter guards as a condition of certificate of occupancy in CAL FIRE-mapped State Responsibility Area — eliminating open-top K-style installations on every Sonoma, Napa, El Dorado, San Bernardino, and Riverside county WUI overlay property and driving aftermarket gutter-guard retrofit on every existing WUI home.
