HVAC Replacement Cost in California (2026)

HVAC replacement in California typically runs $11,400–$26,800 for a complete 3-ton system replacement, with natural-gas furnaces with central AC as the dominant heating choice and roughly 2,300 heating degree days against 1,400 cooling degree days driving the seasonal load mix. California's eight climate zones span coastal 3C (Bay Area, near-zero cooling load) through inland 3B Central Valley (Fresno-Bakersfield, 4,500+ CDD) — Title 24 prescriptively allows different equipment minimums by zone, and wildland-urban-interface filtration (MERV 13+) is now standard on retrofits in CAL FIRE high-severity zones.

California climate & load drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 3B-3C-4B-5B
  • Annual load split: Cooling-dominant — annual cooling load exceeds heating load (2,300 HDD / 1,400 CDD)
  • Dominant heating fuel: Natural gas — piped utility supply through state pipeline network
  • Dominant cooling system: Central air conditioning paired with separate heating equipment

California licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: California Contractors State License Board — C-20 (Warm-Air Heating, Ventilating & Air-Conditioning) (official site)
  • Permit: permit + Title 24 HERS verification required

How HVAC replacement costs vary in California

California's eight climate zones span coastal 3C (Bay Area, near-zero cooling load) through inland 3B Central Valley (Fresno-Bakersfield, 4,500+ CDD) — Title 24 prescriptively allows different equipment minimums by zone, and wildland-urban-interface filtration (MERV 13+) is now standard on retrofits in CAL FIRE high-severity zones.

State-specific code or insurance rule: California SB 1206 (2018) caps refrigerant Global Warming Potential at 750 for new stationary AC systems by 2025 — phasing out R-410A faster than the federal AIM Act timeline — and Title 24 Part 6 mandates HERS-rater field verification of duct leakage and refrigerant charge on every change-out.
