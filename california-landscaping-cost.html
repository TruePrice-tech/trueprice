Landscape Installation Cost in California (2026)

Landscape installation in California typically runs $7,200–$32,000 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with Pacific NW or California Mediterranean plant palette as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 5a-11a. California's combination of multi-year drought cycles, the 2022 SGMA groundwater pumping restrictions, and CAL FIRE Tier 3 WUI defensible-space rules across the Sierra foothills, North Coast, and Santa Monica Mountains has fundamentally restructured residential landscape design statewide — drought-tolerant California-native plant communities, Mediterranean aromatic herbs, and decomposed-granite hardscape now dominate the typical Bay Area, LA, and Central Valley front-yard install, with CAL FIRE ember-resistant Zone 0 cleanup driving complete shrub removal within 5 ft of every WUI structure.

California climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 5a-11a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 22 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 280 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Extreme — multi-year drought cycles drive xeriscape adoption + statewide turf restrictions
  • Irrigation dependency: Very high — 60%+ of residential outdoor water for irrigation
  • Frost-line trench depth: 6-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Coastal-mediterranean — Pacific NW natives or California Mediterranean palette
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: Statewide WUI defensible-space requirement on every property in fire-hazard zones

California licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: California Contractors State License Board — C-27 (Landscaping) (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install on existing yards; CSLB C-27 license required above $500 contract; CDFA Pest Control Business License required for any pesticide application; CAL FIRE defensible-space inspection mandatory in WUI-mapped State Responsibility Area

How landscape installation costs vary in California

California's combination of multi-year drought cycles, the 2022 SGMA groundwater pumping restrictions, and CAL FIRE Tier 3 WUI defensible-space rules across the Sierra foothills, North Coast, and Santa Monica Mountains has fundamentally restructured residential landscape design statewide — drought-tolerant California-native plant communities, Mediterranean aromatic herbs, and decomposed-granite hardscape now dominate the typical Bay Area, LA, and Central Valley front-yard install, with CAL FIRE ember-resistant Zone 0 cleanup driving complete shrub removal within 5 ft of every WUI structure.

State-specific code or insurance rule: California Government Code §65595 and the Model Water Efficient Landscape Ordinance (MWELO) impose the most restrictive residential landscape water rules in the country — every new California residential landscape installation greater than 500 sq ft must submit a Landscape Documentation Package showing Water Budget compliance with ETo-based plant factors, hydrozone separation, and CIMIS-verified irrigation efficiency, with cities including Los Angeles, San Diego, San Jose, Sacramento, and Fresno separately enforcing turf-replacement rebate programs and ornamental-turf removal mandates on non-functional residential turf areas.
