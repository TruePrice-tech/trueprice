Roof Replacement Cost in California (2026)

Roof replacement in California typically runs $10,800–$29,000 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. Wildland-urban-interface zones in CAL FIRE high-severity hazard areas require Class A fire-rated assemblies under Chapter 7A of the California Building Code; asphalt with code-listed underlayment or non-combustible coverings (tile, metal) are standard.

California licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required

  • Permit: permit and Title 24 cool-roof compliance required

How roof replacement costs vary in California

Wildland-urban-interface zones in CAL FIRE high-severity hazard areas require Class A fire-rated assemblies under Chapter 7A of the California Building Code; asphalt with code-listed underlayment or non-combustible coverings (tile, metal) are standard.

State-specific code or insurance rule: California Title 24 Part 6 mandates cool-roof minimum solar reflectance and thermal emittance values — non-compliant materials cannot be installed in low-slope re-roofs in most climate zones.
