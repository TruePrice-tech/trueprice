Siding Replacement Cost in California (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in California typically runs $14,500–$36,500, with three-coat hard-coat stucco the dominant residential cladding choice. California climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 3B-3C-4B-5B
  • Dominant residential cladding: Three-coat hard-coat stucco — dominant in arid and Mediterranean Sun Belt
  • Hurricane wind tier: Low wind exposure — sub-110 mph design wind
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: Statewide ignition-resistant cladding required in WUI overlay
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High-corrosion coastal — stainless or hot-dipped galvanized fasteners required within 3,000 ft of saltwater

California licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: California Contractors State License Board — B (General Building) or C-35 (Lathing & Plastering) for stucco / C-61 D-3 for siding (official site)
  • Permit: city building permit required; CAL FIRE WUI map verification mandatory in mapped fire-hazard zones

How siding replacement costs vary in California

Pacific salt-haze deposition from Monterey to San Diego corrodes galvanized fasteners on coastal re-sides within 5-7 years, mandating Type 304 or Type 316 stainless on all shoreline fiber-cement, cedar, and stucco lath installations — and CAL FIRE Tier 3 WUI overlay across the Sierra foothills, North Coast, and Santa Monica Mountains has expanded fiber-cement plank dominance over vinyl by an order of magnitude on Sonoma, Napa, and El Dorado county retrofits since the 2017-2020 fire seasons.

State-specific code or insurance rule: California Building Code Chapter 7A makes ignition-resistant cladding mandatory on every new and re-sided home in CAL FIRE-mapped State Responsibility Area Wildland-Urban Interface zones — eliminating wood lap, wood shake, and untreated cedar from the WUI overlay, and forcing fiber-cement, stucco, brick veneer, or stone veneer on the cladding side, with WUI-rated soffit vents and ember-resistant attic ventilation on the assembly side.
