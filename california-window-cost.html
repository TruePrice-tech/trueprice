Window Replacement Cost in California (2026)

Window replacements in California typically run $7,400–$22,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR South-Central climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.32, SHGC ≤ 0.25). California's combination of California Title 24 Part 6 climate-zone-specific U-factor and SHGC mandates driving the most stringent prescriptive window energy requirements in the country, CAL FIRE Tier 3 WUI overlay across the Sierra foothills additionally requiring tempered-or-multi-pane glazing on every WUI residential property, persistent Pacific salt-spray on every coastal exposure driving stainless-hardware-and-aluminum-clad-wood frame replacement at 7-10 year intervals, very-high HOA architectural-control prevalence across 60%+ of California subdivisions, and high pre-1978 housing share driving EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every San Francisco, Oakland, Berkeley, Los Angeles, and Sacramento historic-neighborhood window replacement drives unique CA-Title-24-and-WUI-driven glazing selection.

California climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: South-Central — mixed climate, balanced U-factor and lower SHGC (U ≤ 0.32, SHGC ≤ 0.40)
  • IECC climate zone: 3B-4B
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.32 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: 0.25
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 100 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: High — multiple state-mapped historic districts with mandatory review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): High — 40-60% pre-1978 housing share, EPA RRP required on most replacements

California licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: California Contractors State License Board (CSLB) — C-17 Glazing specialty (official site)
  • Permit: city building permit required for window opening modification; CSLB C-17 specialty license required above $500 contract; California Title 24 Part 6 mandatory above-IECC-baseline U-factor and SHGC; CALGreen Title 24 Part 11 mandatory recycled-content review; HOA architectural committee approval mandatory in 60%+ of California subdivisions

How window replacement costs vary in California

State-specific code or insurance rule: California Title 24 Part 6 (California Energy Code) imposes the most aggressive prescriptive window U-factor and SHGC requirements in the country with 16 separate climate zones each with distinct U-0.30-to-U-0.32 maximum U-factors and SHGC ≤ 0.25 maximums, plus the California Contractors State License Board (CSLB) C-17 Glazing specialty license is one of the most credential-segmented residential window contractor licensing schemes in the country, and CALGreen Title 24 Part 11 separately requires recycled-content review on every window installation specifying source-reduction or recycled-content percentage of the vinyl or aluminum frame, with mandatory NFRC labeling on every California residential window install.
