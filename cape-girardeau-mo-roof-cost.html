Cape Girardeau roofing: answers to common questions

What is the difference between architectural and 3-tab shingles?

Architectural shingles are thicker, have a dimensional look, and last 25-30 years. 3-tab shingles are flat, thinner, and last 15-20 years but cost 30-40% less upfront.

Does homeowners insurance cover roof replacement?

Insurance covers sudden damage (storms, falling trees) but not wear-and-tear or maintenance neglect. Verify your policy covers replacement cost, not depreciated value.

What time of year is cheapest for roofing in Cape Girardeau?

Late fall and winter are typically slower seasons in Cape Girardeau, so contractors may offer better pricing. Spring and summer are peak demand, meaning longer waits and higher labor costs.

Why do roofing quotes vary so much?

Quotes differ based on material quality, labor assumptions, flashing work, ventilation, and warranty coverage. The cheapest bid frequently skips items that become change orders mid-project.

How long does a roof last in Cape Girardeau?

Expect architectural asphalt shingles to last 20-25 years in Cape Girardeau, with 3-tab shingles at 15-20 years, metal roofing at 40-60, and tile at 50+. The biggest lifespan variable here is UV exposure since it accelerates granule loss and material breakdown. Adequate attic ventilation and lighter-colored materials both help counteract this.

Cape Girardeau permits: required to replace my roof?

Most Cape Girardeau jurisdictions require a permit for a complete roof replacement. This is the contractor's responsibility to obtain before work starts. Skipping permits creates real risks: insurance coverage gaps, code violation liability, and mandatory disclosure when selling.

When is the best time to replace a roof in Cape Girardeau?

The optimal window for roof replacement in Cape Girardeau is early fall, after summer storms subside and before winter weather complicates scheduling. Scheduling during off-peak periods can save 10-15% on labor costs since contractors have more availability and may offer competitive pricing to fill their calendar. Avoid scheduling immediately after a major storm event when contractor demand (and prices) spike.

Should I repair or replace my roof?

If damage covers less than 30% of the roof and the roof is under 15 years old, repair is usually sufficient. Beyond that, replacement provides better long-term value.

Should I choose the lowest roofing quote?

Not always. Lower bids sometimes exclude flashing, ventilation upgrades, or disposal costs that appear later as change orders. Compare scope, not just price.
