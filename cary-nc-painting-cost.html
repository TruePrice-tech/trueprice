Painting questions for Cary homeowners

How much does interior painting cost per room?

A typical room costs $300-$700 for walls and ceiling. A full interior (5-7 rooms) runs $2,500-$6,000. Trim, doors, and closets add $100-$300 per room.

Is spray painting better than brush and roller?

Spraying is faster and gives a smoother finish on large, flat surfaces. Brush and roller are better for detail work and touchups. Many pros spray first, then back-roll for adhesion.

How often should a house be painted?

Wood siding needs repainting every 5-7 years. Stucco and fiber cement last 8-12 years between coats. Vinyl and aluminum siding rarely need painting. Climate, sun exposure, and paint quality affect timing.

What should I look for in a painting quote?

A professional quote specifies: paint brand and sheen, number of coats, prep work included, surface repairs, primer usage, timeline, warranty, and what is excluded (trim, doors, ceilings).

Local factors: exterior painting in Cary, NC

What local homes look like in Cary. Cary's housing stock averages 20 years old, relatively recent construction that was largely built to modern code. exterior painting projects here tend to be simpler: fewer surprise compliance issues, quicker permit turnaround, and contractors who can quote from memory.

Compliance and HOA notes. HOA oversight is the norm across Cary's residential communities. exterior painting projects require advance approval of materials, colors, and sometimes contractors. Build 2-4 weeks of review time into your project schedule.

Local trade economics. Rapid growth in Cary has stretched the local contractor workforce thin. Lead times of 3-6 weeks are common during peak season, and the best-reviewed companies book out even further. Planning ahead is the single biggest way to control costs. Cary's large market supports both premium and budget painting contractors. Prices vary 30-50% for the same scope — the difference is usually prep quality, not paint brand. Ask every bidder to detail their prep process in writing.

Seasonal timing. Cary offers a longer painting season than cold-climate cities but still has optimal windows. Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) provide the ideal combination of temperature and humidity for proper paint adhesion and curing.

What drives cost. With Cary labor rates near the national median, the cost difference between a budget and premium whole-house exterior paint job comes down to materials and scope rather than labor premiums. Newer construction in Cary (averaging 20 years) means exterior painting projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay. Cary's rapid growth means contractors can be selective about which jobs they take. Off-season scheduling and flexible timelines give you better leverage on pricing than trying to rush a project during peak demand.

Red flags to watch for. Any Cary contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion. Watch for exterior painting quotes in Cary that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Lump-sum bids hide margin and make change orders impossible to evaluate. Cary's rapid growth attracts out-of-state contractors who follow the boom. Verify any unfamiliar company's local licensing, physical address, and track record. Fly-by-night operations leave when the market cools.
