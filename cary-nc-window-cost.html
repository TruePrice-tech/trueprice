Cary window replacement FAQ

How much energy do new windows actually save?

Replacing single-pane windows saves 25-30% on heating and cooling costs. Upgrading from older double-pane to modern Low-E double-pane saves 10-15%. Payback takes 10-15 years through energy savings alone.

How much does window replacement cost in Cary?

Window replacement in Cary costs $400-$1,200 per window installed, depending on size, frame material, glass type, and accessibility. A whole-house project (10-15 windows) runs $5,000-$15,000.

What is the difference between full-frame and insert replacement?

Insert replacement fits a new window into the existing frame, is faster, and costs less. Full-frame replacement removes everything down to the studs, costs 30-50% more, but fixes hidden damage.

Should I replace window screens too?

New windows almost always include new screens. If reusing inserts, check screen mesh for holes and frame corners for separation. Replacement screens cost $25-$75 each.

Do I need a permit for window replacement in Cary?

In Cary, permits are typically required when changing window size or adding new openings. Same-size replacements usually do not need permits, but check your local building department.

What local homes look like in Cary. Local trade economics. Rapid growth in Cary has stretched the local contractor workforce thin. Cary's large market has both national window companies (Renewal by Andersen, Pella) and independent installers using manufacturer-direct windows. What drives cost. Labor costs in Cary track within a few points of the national average for window replacement, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. Cary's relatively young housing stock (20 years average) simplifies most window replacement projects. Red flags to watch for. Any Cary contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. In fast-growing Cary, some contractors take on more work than they can handle.
