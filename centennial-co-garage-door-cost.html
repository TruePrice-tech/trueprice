Centennial garage door FAQ

How much does garage door spring replacement cost?

Torsion spring replacement costs $200-$400 for parts and labor. Extension springs cost $150-$300. Never attempt spring replacement yourself since the stored tension is extremely dangerous.

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Centennial?

Garage door replacement in Centennial typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

Centennial, CO garage door replacement market overview

Why pricing varies in Centennial. Labor rates here run roughly 11% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For garage door replacement specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Centennial at roughly 8% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Centennial has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Insulated garage doors (R-12 minimum, R-18+ preferred) make a measurable difference in Centennial's winters, especially for attached garages with living space above. Uninsulated doors allow cold air infiltration that increases heating costs and can freeze exposed plumbing in the garage. A standard 2-car garage door in Centennial costs $800-2,500 for the door and $300-600 for professional installation. Opener replacement adds $250-500. The biggest pricing lever is material: stamped steel ($800-1,200), insulated steel ($1,200-1,800), wood composite ($1,800-3,000), and real wood ($2,500-5,000+).

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most garage door replacement projects in Centennial require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 106,114, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Centennial has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.

garage door replacement in Centennial, CO: the local picture

Home stock in Centennial. With average home age near 36 years, Centennial homes are at the typical first-replacement milestone for major systems. HOA & compliance. HOAs are common in Centennial. For garage door replacement, factor in 2-4 weeks of HOA architectural review before starting, and collect pre-approval of materials in writing. HOA rejections after project start can be expensive.

Local market conditions. Centennial has moderate construction activity. Contractors are busy but accessible with 1-3 weeks' notice. Off-season quotes (contractor-dependent) can run 8-15% below peak rates. Centennial's large market has dedicated garage door companies, big-box retailer installation services, and independent contractors. Dedicated garage door companies typically offer the widest selection and fastest service but price 10-20% above big-box options for equivalent products.

Seasonal timing. Garage door replacement in Centennial isn't highly seasonal — it's a 4-6 hour indoor/outdoor job that works year-round. However, spring (March-May) is the busiest period as homeowners address winter damage and start curb-appeal projects. Off-peak months (July-September, November-January) offer faster scheduling.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Centennial track within a few points of the national average for garage door replacement, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. At 36 years average home age, Centennial properties are hitting their first major replacement cycle for systems and components. garage door replacement demand is at peak levels in this age band, which keeps contractor schedules full but pricing competitive.

Red flags to watch for. Any Centennial contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag.
