Plumbing questions for Chapel Hill homeowners

Is trenchless sewer repair worth the extra cost?

Trenchless repair costs 20-40% more but avoids excavating your yard, driveway, or landscaping. It is faster and causes less property damage, often making it cheaper overall.

How much does it cost to replace a water heater?

A standard 50-gallon tank water heater runs $1,200-$2,500 installed. Tankless units cost $2,500-$5,000 but last roughly twice as long and cut energy bills 20-30%.

What does a plumbing inspection include?

A thorough inspection covers supply lines, drain lines, water heater condition, fixture connections, shutoff valves, water pressure testing, and a visual check for leaks and corrosion.

How often should I have my plumbing inspected in Chapel Hill?

Schedule a plumbing inspection at least once a year in Chapel Hill. Focus areas: water heater condition (8-12 year typical life), flexible supply lines under sinks (replace braided steel lines after 10 years), and the main sewer line. For homes over 20 years old, a camera inspection of the sewer line ($150-$400) every 3-5 years catches problems before they surface inside.

What should a plumbing quote include in Chapel Hill?

In Chapel Hill, a solid plumbing quote includes labor hours, specified materials and brands, any permit costs, cleanup and disposal fees, and warranty terms. The contractor's license and insurance should be listed. Get three quotes scoped identically to compare pricing fairly on jobs over $500.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Chapel Hill?

The sweet spot for scheduling plumbing in Chapel Hill is early fall. Between summer water-heater demand and winter freeze calls, meaning better contractor availability and fairer pricing. Since emergency plumbing runs 50-100% above scheduled rates, proactive maintenance during off-peak periods is a straightforward way to save.
