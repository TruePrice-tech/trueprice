HVAC in Charleston: what locals should know

🌡

Climate and your HVAC

In Charleston, summer temperatures regularly exceed 95°F with high humidity, putting enormous strain on cooling systems. Investing in a high-efficiency unit with a good dehumidification rating is critical.

⚙

System recommendation

In Charleston, a two-stage or variable-speed AC system runs longer at lower capacity, removing more humidity and using less energy than a single-stage unit cycling on and off.

📅

Best time to buy

The best time to replace your HVAC in Charleston is October through February. Contractors offer off-season pricing and can schedule sooner. Avoid June-September when demand spikes.

💡

Local tip

Many Charleston homes still have older R-22 refrigerant systems. If yours is one of them, now is the time to upgrade — R-22 is phased out and repair costs are skyrocketing.

What affects HVAC cost in Charleston

  • System type (AC only, heat pump, furnace, full system)
  • SEER efficiency rating (14-20+)
  • Home size and tonnage requirement
  • Ductwork condition and modifications
  • Equipment brand (Carrier, Trane, Goodman, etc.)
  • Local labor rates in Charleston, SC

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new HVAC system cost in Charleston?

Most homeowners in Charleston pay between $8,373 and $24,035 for HVAC replacement, depending on system type and efficiency.

Should I replace my AC and furnace at the same time?

Replacing both at once is usually more cost-effective than separate replacements. Matched systems run more efficiently and most contractors offer bundle pricing.

What SEER rating should I choose?

14 SEER is the minimum federal requirement. 16 SEER offers a good balance of efficiency and cost. 18-20+ SEER provides the best energy savings but costs significantly more upfront.
