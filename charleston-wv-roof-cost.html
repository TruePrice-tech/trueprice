Charleston roofing FAQ

What is the most affordable roofing option?

Three-tab asphalt shingles are the most affordable at $3-5 per square foot installed. Architectural shingles cost $4-7/sqft but last 10-15 years longer, making them a better value over time.

How much does a new roof cost in Charleston?

Most homeowners in Charleston pay between $8,000 and $16,000 for a new roof, depending on size, material, and complexity.

How many layers of shingles can I have?

Most building codes allow a maximum of two layers. If you already have two layers, the old roof must be torn off before the new one is installed, adding $1,000-$3,000 to the cost.

Does a new roof increase home value?

A new roof recovers roughly 60-70% of its cost at resale and eliminates a major buyer objection. Homes with new roofs sell faster and with fewer inspection contingencies.

Do I need a permit for roof replacement in Charleston?

Yes. Most Charleston jurisdictions require a building permit for roof replacement. Your contractor should handle the permit and schedule inspection.

How long does a roof last in Charleston?

In Charleston's climate, architectural asphalt shingles typically last 20-25 years, standard 3-tab shingles 15-20 years, metal roofing 40-60 years, and tile 50+ years. These estimates assume proper ventilation and no major storm damage. UV exposure is the primary degradation factor here, so lighter-colored materials and adequate attic ventilation both extend lifespan meaningfully.

Do I need a permit to replace my roof in Charleston?

A permit is typically required for roof replacement in Charleston. The contractor handles the permit process. Going without one risks voiding your homeowners insurance and creates legal complications at resale since you must disclose unpermitted work.

What's the best season to replace a roof in Charleston?

Schedule your Charleston roof replacement during early fall if possible. After summer storms subside and before winter weather complicates scheduling, and off-peak timing often means lower installation costs since contractors compete harder for work. Expect to pay a 10-15% premium if you schedule during the post-storm rush when demand peaks.

Does homeowners insurance cover roof replacement?

Insurance covers sudden damage (storms, falling trees) but not wear-and-tear or maintenance neglect. Verify your policy covers replacement cost, not depreciated value.

Should I repair or replace my roof?

If damage covers less than 30% of the roof and the roof is under 15 years old, repair is usually sufficient. Beyond that, replacement provides better long-term value.

Should I choose the lowest roofing quote?

Not always. Lower bids sometimes exclude flashing, ventilation upgrades, or disposal costs that appear later as change orders. Compare scope, not just price.
