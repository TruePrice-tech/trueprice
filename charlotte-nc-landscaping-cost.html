Frequently asked landscaping questions

Should I hire a landscaper or landscape architect?

A landscaper handles installation and maintenance. A landscape architect designs complex projects and manages grading, drainage, and permits. For projects over $15,000 or involving structural elements, an architect is worthwhile.

How much does lawn installation cost?

Sod costs $1-$2 per square foot installed. Hydroseeding costs $0.05-$0.10/sqft. Traditional seeding costs $0.10-$0.25/sqft but takes 6-8 weeks to establish. A 5,000 sqft lawn costs $5,000-$10,000 in sod.

How much does a paver patio cost?

Paver patios cost $12-$25 per square foot installed, depending on paver material and pattern complexity. A 300 sqft patio runs $3,600-$7,500. Permeable pavers cost 15-20% more but help with drainage.

Soil conditions and amendments in Charlotte

Charlotte Piedmont red clay soils derived from saprolite are acidic (pH 4.5-5.5) and high in iron oxide. Mecklenburg County saprolite breaks down under compaction, which is why reputable crews over-compact base courses beyond nominal Proctor requirements. McGill SoilBuilder compost and Piedmont Farms amendments dominate the Carolinas market. NCDA Agronomic Soil Testing (free for NC residents) provides the baseline. Lime applications raise pH on most clay-heavy lots. Pine-fines compost is the standard clay-breaking amendment.

Lawn and turf choices: a Charlotte guide

The Charlotte metro sits on the transition zone between warm and cool-season turf, which complicates lawn decisions for homeowners. Warm-season Bermuda (Tifway 419, Celebration) dominates sunny new-construction tract homes. Tall fescue (Kentucky 31, Rebel blends) dominates shaded Dilworth, Myers Park, and Elizabeth yards. Zoysia (Emerald, Meyer, Zeon, Empire) is gaining on moderate-sun lots. St Augustine appears on some shaded lots. Fescue struggles in July-August heat but recovers with fall overseed.

Charlotte: tree services and ordinances

Charlotte Tree Ordinance (Chapter 21, Article V) protects heritage trees on private lots and requires a Tree Save Plan on certain development. Mecklenburg County Tree Protection rules extend to unincorporated areas. Licensed arborists through ISA charge $700-$3,200 per mature tree removal. Ice-storm cleanup (2022 Christmas freeze, 2024 winter storms) spikes pricing 30-50 percent. NC Certified Plant Professional (NCAN) and Certified Arborist credentials are regional baselines. Pine-bark beetle treatments drive preventive workload on mature pine stands.

Charlotte's seasonal maintenance rhythm

Charlotte landscape maintenance season runs March through November active. Warm-season grasses green up late-March and peak June-September. Pre-emergent weed control applications happen mid-February and mid-September. Fall overseed of fescue happens late-August through mid-October. Pollen season (mid-March through April) produces a yellow coating on hardscape surfaces. Ice-storm emergency cleanup creates episodic December-February workload spikes. Hurricane-remnant cleanup (September) adds occasional workload.

Stormwater rules: Charlotte edition

Local market conditions. Charlotte's high growth rate means contractor demand consistently exceeds supply. Expect 3-6 week booking delays in season. Charlotte's large market supports full-service landscape design firms, installation-only crews, and maintenance companies.
