Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to paint a house exterior in Charlotte?

Most homeowners in Charlotte pay between $4,900 and $9,950 for exterior painting, depending on home size and paint quality.

How many coats of paint does an exterior need?

Most exterior painting jobs require at least two coats for proper coverage and durability. A single coat may work for touch-ups or if the existing paint is in good condition with a similar color.

How long does exterior paint last?

Quality exterior paint typically lasts 5-10 years depending on climate, sun exposure, and paint quality. Premium paints like Sherwin-Williams Duration can last 10-15 years.

About exterior painting in Charlotte, NC

Why pricing varies in Charlotte. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. For exterior painting specifically, Charlotte sits within a few points of the national midpoint on our cost model.

Local conditions. Charlotte has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. 100% acrylic latex with mildew-resistant primer is the regional standard; budget for repaint every 7-10 years.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most exterior painting projects in Charlotte require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. As a major metro of 792,862 residents, the local contractor market is deep — expect 10+ legitimate bidders on any standard residential job. Charlotte's rapid recent growth has tightened the contractor market — expect longer lead times and less negotiating room than smaller markets.

Charlotte Climate and Paint Durability

Charlotte sits on the Piedmont plateau at 750 feet elevation with moderate UV (latitude 35.2N) and brutal summer humidity that holds 80-90% RH through July and August. South-facing brick veneer and Hardie-plank in Ballantyne, Waxhaw, and the Lake Norman corridor fade predictably over 7-9 years, while north-facing elevations in tree-heavy Dilworth and Plaza Midwood grow visible Cladosporium mildew within 10-14 months. The 2022 Christmas arctic blast cracked caulk seams across thousands of Piedmont homes when temps plunged from 60F to 9F in 18 hours. Pine and oak pollen blankets the entire metro from mid-March through late April, requiring a dedicated pre-paint power wash before any exterior work can begin.

Piedmont humidity hammers painted surfaces relentlessly from May through September. North-facing Hardie, brick, and wood-clapboard in Dilworth, Plaza Midwood, and NoDa grow visible Cladosporium and Aureobasidium mildew within 10-14 months without mildewcide-enhanced topcoat. Concrobium Mold Control and Zinsser Jomax House Surface Cleaner are mandatory pre-wash treatments. Charlotte's specification default is mildewcide-loaded acrylic: SW Duration Mold Armor or Ben Moore Aura Exterior with factory-added mildewcide. Pine-straw accumulation against foundation walls in Cotswold and Sardis holds moisture against painted surfaces and accelerates peeling.

Charlotte Siding Materials and Substrates

Brick veneer dominates Charlotte at roughly 60 percent of residential stock, driven by the Piedmont's deep red-clay soils that historically supplied local brick kilns. Wood clapboard survives on 1920s-1940s craftsman bungalows in Dilworth, Plaza Midwood, and NoDa, requiring periodic scraping and priming against tannin bleed. Stucco appears on a handful of Mediterranean-revival homes in Myers Park and Eastover but is far less common than in Atlanta or Tampa. Board-and-batten cedar accents on Lake Norman waterfront custom builds are a Charlotte-specific luxury aesthetic.

Charlotte Prep Culture and Methods

Charlotte prep starts with the pollen wash: every exterior surface gets a 1,500-2,000 PSI rinse with sodium-percarbonate detergent after the April pollen season subsides. Dilworth and Plaza Midwood bungalows built before 1978 require EPA RRP-certified lead-safe crews; Charlotte's 1920s craftsman housing stock carries 3-7 layers of legacy lead paint. Newer Hardie-plank tract homes in Ballantyne need only surface cleaning and perimeter caulk refresh. Piedmont red-clay foundation shrink-swell cracks caulk seams at the sill plate and window trim within 3-4 years on slab-on-grade construction, creating a recurring prep cycle unique to the Charlotte clay belt.

Charlotte Permits and Lead Paint Rules

Mecklenburg County Code Enforcement does not require permits for standard exterior painting. NC DHHS requires EPA RRP certification for contractors disturbing lead paint on pre-1978 structures. Charlotte-Mecklenburg Historic Landmarks Commission review on contributing structures in Dilworth, Fourth Ward, Wesley Heights, and Hermitage Court adds 4-8 weeks for exterior color-change approval. Charlotte's post-2016 Tree Save Ordinance requires tree-protection fencing during exterior work within the critical root zone of any tree exceeding 8-inch DBH.

Charlotte Seasonal Painting Windows

Charlotte exterior painting runs mid-April (after pollen subsides) through mid-November. The June-August window loses 25-30 percent of scheduled workdays to afternoon Piedmont thunderstorms and oppressive humidity that prevents proper film formation before dew point hits. September through mid-November is the Charlotte prime window: stable temps in the 65-80F range, dropping humidity, and minimal storm disruption. Winter interior jobs fill schedules December through February. Contractor backlogs peak at 8-12 weeks in the September-October sweet spot.
