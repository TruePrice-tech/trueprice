Questions Charlottesville homeowners ask about fencing

How tall can I build my fence?

Most municipalities allow 6 feet in backyards and 3-4 feet in front yards without a variance. Some HOAs restrict fence height further. Always check local zoning before building.

What is the cheapest fence material?

Chain link is the cheapest at $10-$20 per linear foot installed. Pressure-treated pine is the cheapest wood option at $15-$25/ft. Vinyl and composite start at $20-$30/ft but never need painting.

What is the best fence for privacy?

A 6-foot solid board (dog-ear, board-on-board, or shadowbox) fence provides the most privacy. Board-on-board is best since it has no gaps even when boards shrink. Vinyl and composite also offer full privacy panels.

How long does a wood fence last in Charlottesville?

In Charlottesville's climate, a properly installed and maintained pressure-treated fence lasts 15-20 years. Cedar lasts 15-25 years. Untreated pine may only last 5-8 years. Staining or sealing every 2-3 years and keeping vegetation trimmed away from the base are the two most effective ways to extend your fence's lifespan.

Charlottesville permits: required to build a fence?

Fence permits are required in most Charlottesville jurisdictions, particularly for heights over 6 feet. Before breaking ground: call 811 for utility locates, verify property lines (a survey costs $300-$600 if boundaries are unclear), and check HOA requirements. A professional contractor handles the permit process as part of the project.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Charlottesville?

Per-foot costs for fence installation in Charlottesville: wood privacy $15-$30, vinyl $20-$40, chain link $10-$20, and aluminum or wrought iron $25-$55. A standard 150-foot backyard fence runs $2,250-$6,000 total. Always compare 3 quotes with the same scope and materials to ensure you are getting fair pricing.
