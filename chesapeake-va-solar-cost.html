Solar questions for Chesapeake homeowners

How does the federal solar tax credit work?

The Investment Tax Credit (ITC) lets you deduct 30% of your total solar installation cost from your federal taxes. It applies to purchased systems, not leased. There is no cap on the credit amount.

How many panels do I need?

Panel count depends on your electricity usage, panel wattage, and roof sun exposure. A typical home uses 20-30 panels (400W each) to offset 80-100% of electricity consumption.

How much does solar cost in Chesapeake?

Most homeowners in Chesapeake pay $15,000-$30,000 before incentives for a 6-10kW system. After the 30% federal tax credit, net cost drops to $10,500-$21,000.

Will solar panels damage my roof?

Properly installed panels do not damage roofs. Reputable installers use flashing and sealant at every penetration point. If your roof needs replacement within 5-10 years, do it before installing solar.

Will solar panels survive a hurricane in Chesapeake?

Solar panels on properly rated racking systems handle hurricanes well, often outlasting the roof itself during high-wind events. The critical factors: racking must be certified for Chesapeake's wind speed zone (130+ mph), all roof penetrations need proper flashing and sealant, and ongoing maintenance keeps connections tight. Schedule a post-storm inspection with your installer after any significant hurricane event.

What solar incentives are available in Chesapeake?

Every Chesapeake homeowner installing solar qualifies for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit with no dollar limit. Beyond that, VA may offer state tax credits, utility-specific rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits, and property tax exemptions. The DSIRE database has the current list for your area. These stacked incentives typically reduce net cost by an additional 10-30%.

When should I install solar in Chesapeake?

Chesapeake homeowners should aim for early spring or fall to install solar. Moderate weather conditions for installation and you avoid the peak-demand scheduling crunch. Allow 6-12 weeks from signing a contract through permitting, utility interconnection approval, and installation. Start gathering quotes 2-3 months before your target window. Remember that the 30% ITC applies to the tax year in which installation is completed.
