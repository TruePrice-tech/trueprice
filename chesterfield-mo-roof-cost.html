Frequently asked roofing questions

What is the difference between architectural and 3-tab shingles?

Architectural shingles are thicker, have a dimensional look, and last 25-30 years. 3-tab shingles are flat, thinner, and last 15-20 years but cost 30-40% less upfront.

Does homeowners insurance cover roof replacement?

Insurance covers sudden damage (storms, falling trees) but not wear-and-tear or maintenance neglect. Verify your policy covers replacement cost, not depreciated value.

What time of year is cheapest for roofing in Chesterfield?

Late fall and winter are typically slower seasons in Chesterfield, so contractors may offer better pricing. Spring and summer are peak demand, meaning longer waits and higher labor costs.

Why do roofing quotes vary so much?

Pricing spreads come from material tiers, labor estimates, flashing scope, ventilation choices, and warranty terms. Budget bids tend to leave out scope that reappears as extras later.

How long does a roof last in Chesterfield?

For Chesterfield homeowners, here are realistic roof lifespans: 3-tab asphalt shingles last 15-20 years, architectural shingles 20-25, standing seam metal 40-60, and clay or concrete tile 50+. UV degradation is the primary wear factor in this climate. Proper ventilation keeps the roof deck from overheating, and lighter material colors reflect more UV, both extending service life.

Do I need a permit to replace my roof in Chesterfield?

In most jurisdictions including Chesterfield, a building permit is required for a full roof replacement. Your contractor should pull the permit before work begins. Unpermitted roofing work can void your homeowners insurance coverage and create disclosure issues when selling the home.

What's the best season to replace a roof in Chesterfield?

For Chesterfield, aim for early fall to schedule your roof replacement. After summer storms subside and before winter weather complicates scheduling, which means better contractor availability and potential savings of 10-15% on labor. Booking right after a major storm is the worst time since every roofer in the area will be booked and prices reflect the surge demand.

Should I repair or replace my roof?

If damage covers less than 30% of the roof and the roof is under 15 years old, repair is usually sufficient. Beyond that, replacement provides better long-term value.

Should I choose the lowest roofing quote?

Not always. Lower bids sometimes exclude flashing, ventilation upgrades, or disposal costs that appear later as change orders. Compare scope, not just price.
