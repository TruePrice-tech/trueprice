HVAC in Cheyenne: what locals should know

🌡

Climate and your HVAC

Cheyenne sees some of the coldest temperatures in the lower 48. Dual-fuel systems (heat pump + gas furnace backup) are gaining popularity for their efficiency in moderate cold while maintaining gas reliability for extreme days.

⚙

System recommendation

In Cheyenne's extreme cold, a gas furnace rated 96%+ AFUE is the safest choice. Standard heat pumps lose efficiency below 20°F, though cold-climate heat pumps now work down to -15°F.

📅

Best time to buy

Don't gamble on an aging furnace in Cheyenne's winters. If your system is 15+ years old, schedule a replacement in early fall when you can plan rather than panic.

💡

Local tip

In Cheyenne, a furnace failure in January can be dangerous. Keep an emergency plan: know a reliable HVAC company, consider a backup heat source, and don't ignore warning signs like strange noises or uneven heating.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new HVAC system cost in Cheyenne?

Most homeowners in Cheyenne pay between $8,160 and $23,345 for HVAC replacement, depending on system type and efficiency.

Should I replace my AC and furnace at the same time?

Replacing both at once is usually more cost-effective than separate replacements. Matched systems run more efficiently and most contractors offer bundle pricing.

What SEER rating should I choose?

14 SEER is the minimum federal requirement. 16 SEER offers a good balance of efficiency and cost. 18-20+ SEER provides the best energy savings but costs significantly more upfront.
