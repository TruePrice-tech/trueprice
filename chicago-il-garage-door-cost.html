Common garage door questions in Chicago

What causes a garage door to be noisy?

Common causes: worn rollers (replace nylon for steel), dry hinges (lubricate with silicone spray), loose hardware, a chain-drive opener, or worn spring coils. A tune-up costs $100-$200.

What is the best type of garage door?

Insulated steel with polyurethane foam offers the best balance of durability, energy efficiency, and cost. R-value of 12-18 keeps the garage 10-20 degrees closer to house temperature.

Can I insulate my existing garage door?

Yes. Retrofit insulation kits ($100-$200 DIY) add R-4 to R-8. However, adding weight to a non-insulated door may require spring adjustment. A new insulated door is often a better investment.

What R-value garage door do I need in Chicago?

In Chicago, the minimum practical insulation for an attached garage door is R-12, with R-16 being the better choice for most homeowners. Workshop or heated-garage applications should go R-16 to R-18. That extra $200-$500 investment recovers through energy savings in adjacent living spaces within a few years and keeps the garage itself above freezing through winter.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Chicago?

For Chicago homes averaging 50 years old, replacement is often the better investment if the door is original. A new insulated door with a modern opener costs $1,200-$3,000 and recovers 90-100% of its cost at resale. Repair makes sense for newer doors with localized damage (single panel, one spring, or opener motor replacement) when the rest of the system is sound.

When is the best time to replace a garage door in Chicago?

For Chicago, target late spring or early fall for your garage door replacement. Moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing, and off-peak scheduling often means 5-10% savings on installation. Spring is the peak rush since cold weather causes spring failures that flood installers with emergency calls.
