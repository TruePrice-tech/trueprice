Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a gutter cost in Chicago?

Most homeowners in Chicago pay between $1,150 and $1,700 for gutter installation, depending on material and yard size.

What is the cheapest gutter material?

Aluminum gutters are the most affordable option at $6-$12 per linear foot installed. Vinyl is comparable at $5-$11 per linear foot but less durable in extreme climates.

How long does a wood gutter last?

A well-maintained wood gutter typically lasts 15-20 years. Regular staining and sealing every 2-3 years can significantly extend its lifespan. Cedar and redwood last longer than pine.

Ice and freeze risk in Chicago

Freeze risk: very high.

Ice dams are one of the most common and expensive gutter-related problems in Chicago. When attic heat melts roof snow, the meltwater refreezes in gutters and at eave edges, backing up under shingles and causing interior water damage. Heated gutter cables ($500-$1,500 installed) are strongly recommended for homes with a history of ice dam formation. Proper attic insulation (R-49+) and balanced ventilation address the root cause, but heated cables provide immediate protection while insulation upgrades are planned.

Chicago gutter cleaning schedule

Recommended frequency: three times per year (late spring after seed drop, mid-October, and late November after final leaf drop).

Chicago's dense urban tree canopy means gutters fill rapidly in fall. The combination of heavy leaf drop and freeze risk makes pre-winter cleaning critical. Gutter neglect in October leads directly to ice dam formation in December.
