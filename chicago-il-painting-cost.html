What Chicago residents ask about painting

Do I need to prime before painting?

Prime over bare wood, drywall patches, stains, dark colors being covered with light ones, and previously unpainted surfaces. Paint-and-primer combos work for simple color changes on already-painted walls.

Should I hire a painter or do it myself?

DIY saves 60-70% on cost but takes 3-5x longer and usually looks less polished. Hire a pro for exteriors, high ceilings, detailed trim work, or any spray application. DIY works fine for single interior rooms.

How much does it cost to paint kitchen cabinets?

Professional cabinet painting costs $3,000-$7,000 for an average kitchen. The process includes removing doors, degreasing, sanding, priming, spraying 2-3 coats, and reinstalling hardware.

How much does interior painting cost per room?

A typical room costs $300-$700 for walls and ceiling. A full interior (5-7 rooms) runs $2,500-$6,000. Trim, doors, and closets add $100-$300 per room.

Is spray painting better than brush and roller?

Spraying is faster and gives a smoother finish on large, flat surfaces. Brush and roller are better for detail work and touchups. Many pros spray first, then back-roll for adhesion.

How do I find a good painting contractor in Chicago?

Check licensing through your state's contractor board, verify insurance, read reviews from Chicago homeowners, and get at least 3 written quotes with line-item detail.

How much should I pay upfront?

Never pay more than 10-15% as a deposit before work begins. Standard payment structure: deposit, progress payment when materials arrive, final payment on completion.

What should I look for in a contractor quote?

A professional quote breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. It includes a timeline, payment schedule, warranty terms, and the contractor's license number.

Do I need a permit for exterior painting in Chicago?

Most exterior painting projects in Chicago require a permit. Your contractor should pull the permit and schedule the inspection. Never pull permits in your own name.

What shapes exterior painting pricing in Chicago, IL

Why pricing varies in Chicago. Labor rates here run roughly 47% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For exterior painting specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Chicago at roughly 22% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Chicago has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Freeze-thaw cycles in Chicago are the primary driver of exterior paint failure. Water penetrates hairline cracks, freezes, expands, and peels paint from the substrate. Annual touch-up of cracks and gaps extends full repaint intervals by 2-3 years. Chicago homes from the 1976s era commonly have multiple paint layers that need proper scraping and priming before recoating. Shortcuts on prep show within 2 years. A quality prep job is 60% of a good paint job.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most exterior painting projects in Chicago require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. As a major metro of 2,718,782 residents, the local contractor market is deep — expect 10+ legitimate bidders on any standard residential job. Chicago has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.

Chicago Climate and Paint Durability

Chicago paint exposure is moderate UV (latitude 41.9N) but aggressive freeze-thaw cycling (85+ cycles per year) that stresses film flexibility. South-facing wood clapboard on Wicker Park three-flats typically lasts 5-7 years on premium acrylic; north-facing elevations get 8-10. Lake-effect wind-driven rain on Rogers Park and Edgewater east-facing facades accelerates moisture intrusion through failed caulk joints. Salt carryover from CDOT heavy salting damages any paint film within 3-4 feet of street level throughout winter.

Chicago exterior moisture stress is moderate with high seasonal variability: summer humidity (65-80%) followed by aggressive freeze-thaw winters. North-facing brownstone and brick facades develop black algae within 2-3 years of paint. Concrobium Mold Control and Zinsser Jomax are the standard pre-wash treatments. Mildewcide-enhanced paint (SW Duration Mold Armor, Ben Moore Aura with mildewcide) is the Chicago premium spec for shaded elevations. Ice-dam-related ceiling damage often requires interior repaint after ice-melt events.

Chicago Prep Culture and Methods

Chicago prep culture emphasizes power-washing aggressively because of Lake Michigan-moisture-driven algae and mildew. Bungalow-belt brick facades require masonry-primer-compatible acrylic (SW Loxon or equivalent). Three-flat wood-clapboard repaint requires scraping multiple paint layers; pre-1978 properties require EPA RRP-certified crews for lead safety. Post-war aluminum-siding work requires Direct-to-Metal (DTM) primer. Gangway (side-yard) access constraints in dense neighborhoods force staging from the front stoop, adding time.

Chicago Permits and Lead Paint Rules

Chicago Department of Buildings does not require permits for standard exterior painting. Chicago Residential Landlord and Tenant Ordinance (RLTO) requires lead disclosure for pre-1978 rentals. EPA RRP-certified crews are mandatory for any painting disturbing 6+ sqft in pre-1978 residential. Landmarks Commission review applies to designated properties. Cook County Environmental Health enforces lead-safe work practices on child-occupied residences. CDPH Lead Safe Housing Registry is tracked.
