Chicago siding FAQ

How long does siding installation take?

A full siding replacement on an average home takes 5-10 days. Larger homes or complex designs with multiple gables, dormers, and trim details can take 2-3 weeks.

How do I maintain fiber cement siding?

Repaint every 12-15 years, caulk joints and seams every 5-7 years, keep vegetation trimmed 6 inches from the wall, and wash annually with a garden hose. Avoid power washing at high pressure.

Do I need a permit for siding replacement in Chicago?

Most Chicago jurisdictions require a permit if you are changing siding material or adding insulation. Like-for-like replacement without structural changes may be exempt. Check with your local building department.

What is board-and-batten siding?

Board-and-batten uses wide vertical boards with narrow strips (battens) covering the seams. It is popular in farmhouse and modern styles. Costs $8-$16/sqft installed in fiber cement or engineered wood.

Can new siding go over old siding?

Overlaying is possible but not recommended. It hides potential water damage, adds weight, and can void warranties. Tear-off adds $1,000-$3,000 but lets you inspect and repair sheathing and moisture barriers.

Chicago climate impact on siding

Chicago siding faces the most severe freeze-thaw cycling of any major US metro: 70-100 freeze-thaw cycles per year, sub-zero arctic plunges, 30+ inch snow events, and summer heat-index peaks above 100F. Freeze-thaw cycling drives caulk and sealant failure; Chicago-specific spec requires 35-year silicone sealants at all penetrations and transitions. Thin-gauge vinyl cracks on impact at sub-zero ambient, which is why the Chicago minimum vinyl spec is .046" or heavier. Insulated vinyl with permanently bonded EPS foam backing is increasingly specified for both thermal performance and impact resistance.

Chicago has no coastal saltwater exposure but Lake Michigan shoreline properties (Rogers Park, Edgewater, Hyde Park, South Shore) experience freeze-thaw moisture cycling on lakefront elevations. Chicago DOT winter road-salt spray across lakefront neighborhoods corrodes fasteners and aluminum trim within 3-5 years on uncoated galvanized steel. Stainless-steel 304 or 316 fasteners are the durable lake-effect spec. Harbor and marina-adjacent properties see additional corrosion exposure. Lake-effect snow-load on framed siding at roof-wall transitions drives structural-reinforcement requirements on some tear-off renovations.
