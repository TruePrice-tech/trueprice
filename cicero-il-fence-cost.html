Questions Cicero homeowners ask about fencing

How long does fence installation take?

A standard 150-200 linear foot residential fence takes 1-3 days to install. Hillside or rocky soil adds time. Post holes need 24-48 hours to cure if set in concrete.

How deep should fence posts be?

Standard rule: bury one-third of the total post length. For a 6-foot fence with 8-foot posts, dig 24-30 inches deep. In frost-prone areas, posts must go below the frost line to prevent heaving.

How much does vinyl fencing cost?

Vinyl privacy fencing costs $20-$40 per linear foot installed. A typical 150 linear foot backyard fence runs $3,000-$6,000. Vinyl never needs painting, resists rot, and lasts 25-30 years.

How deep should fence posts be in Cicero?

Cicero's freeze-thaw cycling will push shallow fence posts out of the ground within a couple of winters. Set posts 36-48 inches deep, at least 6 inches past the frost line. Bottom the hole with 8-10 inches of compacted gravel for drainage, then fill with concrete to grade. There is no shortcut on depth in this climate; it is the single most important factor in long-term fence stability.

Are permits required to build a fence in Cicero?

In Cicero, fence permits are standard for installations over 6 feet and sometimes required for any fence. Three things to handle before installation: call 811 for utility marking, confirm property boundaries (budget $300-$600 for a survey if needed), and review any HOA rules. The fence contractor should manage the permitting as part of the project scope.
