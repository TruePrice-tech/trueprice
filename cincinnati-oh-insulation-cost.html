Frequently Asked Questions

How much does attic insulation cost in Cincinnati?

Most homeowners in Cincinnati pay between $1,250 and $5,400 for attic insulation, depending on type and coverage area.

Is spray foam insulation worth the extra cost?

Spray foam provides the highest R-value per inch and also acts as an air barrier. Closed-cell foam is best for moisture-prone areas, while blown-in is often the most cost-effective for attic coverage.

How much insulation do I need?

The DOE recommends R-38 to R-60 for attics in most climate zones. An energy audit can determine the exact R-value target for your home in Cincinnati, OH.

Cincinnati Wall Cavity Retrofit Approach

Cincinnati wall assemblies vary by era: pre-1940 balloon-framed homes need dense-pack cellulose at 3.5 pcf plus horizontal firestop blocking. Zone 4A code requires R-13+5ci for walls. Do not inject closed-cell foam into brick-veneer walls because moisture trapping causes long-term damage.

Air Sealing Priorities in Cincinnati

A typical pre-retrofit blower door in Cincinnati reads 4,000-7,000 CFM50 because of balloon-framing leakage, unsealed laundry chutes, and open-stair chases. Priority sealing targets are: sill-plate-to-foundation joints, rim joist plane, top plate to drywall gaps, and plumbing chase penetrations. AeroBarrier is available through local applicators for challenging retrofit cases.
