Questions Cincinnati homeowners ask about insulation

What R-value do I need in my attic?

DOE recommendations: R-38 to R-60 in cold climates (zones 4-8) and R-30 to R-38 in warm climates (zones 1-3). Most existing homes have R-19 or less. Adding insulation to code reduces heating/cooling costs 15-25%.

How do I know if my home needs more insulation?

Check attic insulation depth (less than 10 inches of fiberglass means you are under R-30), feel for cold spots on walls in winter, notice ice dams on the roof, or get an energy audit with thermal imaging ($200-$400).

How much does insulation cost in Cincinnati?

Insulation costs in Cincinnati range from $1-$2/sqft for blown-in attic insulation, $1.50-$3.50/sqft for batt insulation in walls, and $2-$4/sqft for spray foam. A full attic (1,000 sqft) runs $1,000-$2,500.

Can I add insulation over existing insulation?

Yes, in most cases. Adding blown-in cellulose or unfaced fiberglass over existing attic insulation is common and effective. Never compress existing insulation and do not add a vapor barrier on top of existing insulation.

Local trade economics. Cincinnati has moderate construction activity. Contractors are busy but accessible with 1-3 weeks' notice. Off-season quotes can run 8-15% below peak rates. Cincinnati's large market has dedicated insulation contractors who do nothing but insulation work — they're faster, cheaper, and more knowledgeable about building science than general contractors. For spray foam specifically, verify the contractor is manufacturer-certified (Icynene, Demilec, etc.).

Seasonal timing. Schedule insulation upgrades in Cincinnati for late summer or early fall — before heating season starts but while attic temperatures allow comfortable work.
