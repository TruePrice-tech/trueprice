What Cleveland residents ask about concrete

How much does a concrete patio cost?

A 300 sqft concrete patio costs $1,800-$3,600 for basic broom finish. Stamped patterns add $3-$8/sqft. Colored concrete adds $1-$3/sqft. An exposed aggregate finish adds $2-$4/sqft.

How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

More questions about concrete in Cleveland

What does a concrete driveway cost per square foot?

Plain 4-inch concrete runs $6-$12 per square foot installed in 2026. Stamped concrete adds $4-$8/sqft for pattern and color. Heavy-duty 6-inch driveways for RVs or trucks cost $9-$15/sqft. A 600 sq ft two-car driveway typically lands between $5,800 and $8,100.

How much does a 1,000 sq ft concrete patio cost?

Typical 2026 ranges for a 1,000 sq ft project: plain broom-finish $8,000-$12,000, exposed-aggregate $9,500-$14,500, stamped $11,000-$16,500. Tearing out an existing slab adds $1,500-$3,000. Reinforcing wire mesh and rebar are usually $0.50-$1.00/sqft and worth it on driveways, optional on patios.

What does a slab foundation cost in Cleveland?

A monolithic slab foundation for a typical single-family house in Cleveland runs $5,000-$15,000 depending on square footage and soil conditions. A garage slab is $2,500-$6,000. Full basement foundations are a different scope entirely and run $15,000-$30,000.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Cleveland?

Driveways, patios over a certain size, and any structural slab in Cleveland require a permit and an inspection of the base prep and rebar before the pour. Sidewalks, repairs, and resurfacing usually do not. Your contractor pulls the permit — never pull it in your own name.

About concrete work in Cleveland, OH

Where Cleveland's pricing comes from. At 12% above national average labor rates, Cleveland's contractor pricing starts from a higher baseline than most US markets. The gap reflects both local wages and regional demand for skilled trades. On concrete work, Cleveland runs about 9% above the US average based on our pricing data across metro areas.

Local conditions in Cleveland. Cleveland has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Cleveland for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Standard concrete in Cleveland runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Cleveland permits and code requirements. Permits are usually required for concrete work in Cleveland; verify the specifics with the local building department before committing.

The Cleveland contractor pool. With a population of 390,113, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. The Cleveland area's flat growth keeps contractor availability high year-round. Seasonal discounts of 10-15% are realistic for flexible project timelines.

Local factors: concrete work in Cleveland, OH

Home stock in Cleveland. Cleveland's housing stock, averaging 55 years, is at the age where second-generation replacements are becoming necessary. Original upgrades from the 1990s-2000s are now aging out themselves. concrete work projects here often uncover previous work that was done to older code standards.

HOA & compliance. The absence of widespread HOA governance in Cleveland means fewer approval delays for concrete work work. You can move straight from permits to construction without waiting for committee review.

How the local trade market looks. Cleveland's flat growth pattern keeps contractor pricing competitive. You will likely find more willingness to negotiate and shorter lead times than in booming cities. However, fewer contractors means fewer quotes to compare, so check references carefully. Cleveland's large market has concrete contractors ranging from 1-person decorative specialists to large commercial outfits that also do residential. For standard flatwork (driveways, patios), mid-size companies with dedicated residential crews typically offer the best combination of pricing and quality.

Best months to book. The concrete season in Cleveland runs May through October when overnight temps stay above 40°F consistently. Spring is peak demand as homeowners address winter frost damage. Fall pours (September-October) offer better contractor availability and fewer weather delays.

Local neighborhood signal. In Cleveland, the highest volume of concrete work work tends to come out of Tremont, Ohio City, Detroit-Shoreway, and Lakewood — larger lots, older homes, and higher home values all drive replacement demand. Contractors familiar with these neighborhoods often already know the typical building stock, permit nuances, and HOA quirks.

What drives cost. Red flags to watch for. Check that any Cleveland contractor doing concrete work carries both general liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers' compensation. For older Cleveland homes (average 55 years), beware of concrete work quotes that don't mention code compliance.
