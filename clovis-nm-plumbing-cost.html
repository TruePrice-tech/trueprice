Frequently asked plumbing questions

What is the difference between copper and PEX piping?

Copper lasts 50+ years, resists bacteria, and has proven durability. PEX costs 60% less, installs faster, resists freezing better, and requires fewer joints. Both are code-compliant.

Is trenchless sewer repair worth the extra cost?

Trenchless repair costs 20-40% more but avoids excavating your yard, driveway, or landscaping. It is faster and causes less property damage, often making it cheaper overall.

How much does it cost to replace a water heater?

A standard 50-gallon tank water heater runs $1,200-$2,500 installed. Tankless units cost $2,500-$5,000 but last roughly twice as long and cut energy bills 20-30%.

What does a plumbing inspection include?

A thorough inspection covers supply lines, drain lines, water heater condition, fixture connections, shutoff valves, water pressure testing, and a visual check for leaks and corrosion.

How much does a plumber charge per hour in Clovis?

Plumbers in Clovis typically charge $75-$150 per hour. Emergency and after-hours calls add a $100-$200 trip fee on top of the hourly rate.

Does Clovis's hard water damage plumbing?

It does. The mineral content in Clovis's water supply leaves calcium and magnesium deposits inside pipes, tanks, and fixtures. Water heaters lose 20-30% efficiency as scale builds on heating elements. Fixture aerators clog and valves stick. A whole-house softener ($1,500-$3,000 installed) prevents the buildup. Descale your water heater yearly to extend its life by 3-5 years.

What should a plumbing quote include in Clovis?

A professional plumbing quote in Clovis should itemize: labor hours, material costs and brands, permit fees if applicable, cleanup, and warranty terms. The quote should include the plumber's license number and proof of insurance. Get three written quotes for any job over $500 to ensure fair pricing.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Clovis?

Clovis homeowners should target fall or spring for planned plumbing work. Moderate temperatures make excavation work easier and contractor schedules are less congested, which translates to better availability and pricing. Emergency service runs 50-100% more than scheduled jobs, so proactive replacement of aging equipment is the financially smarter path.
