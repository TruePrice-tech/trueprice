Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

About concrete work in Coeur d'Alene, ID

Why pricing varies in Coeur d'Alene. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. For concrete work specifically, Coeur d'Alene sits within a few points of the national midpoint on our cost model.

Local conditions. Coeur d'Alene has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Coeur d'Alene for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Homes in Coeur d'Alene averaging 33 years old typically have stable sub-grades that simplify concrete work. New pours integrate cleanly with existing slabs and foundations without the settling corrections older properties need.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Coeur d'Alene require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. At 46,402 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. Coeur d'Alene's rapid recent growth has tightened the contractor market — expect longer lead times and less negotiating room than smaller markets.
