Questions College Station homeowners ask about plumbing

What is a backflow preventer and do I need one?

A backflow preventer stops contaminated water from flowing back into the clean supply. Most municipalities require them on irrigation systems and commercial properties. Some require annual testing.

Do I need a plumbing permit in College Station?

Most College Station jurisdictions require permits for new lines, water heater installs, and sewer work. Minor repairs like faucet swaps and toilet replacements typically do not need permits.

How long do plumbing fixtures typically last?

Faucets last 15-20 years, toilets 25-50 years, water heaters 8-12 years (tank) or 15-20 years (tankless), and supply lines should be replaced every 20-25 years.

What is the difference between copper and PEX piping?

Copper lasts 50+ years, resists bacteria, and has proven durability. PEX costs 60% less, installs faster, resists freezing better, and requires fewer joints. Both are code-compliant.

Is trenchless sewer repair worth the extra cost?

Trenchless repair costs 20-40% more but avoids excavating your yard, driveway, or landscaping. It is faster and causes less property damage, often making it cheaper overall.

How often should I have my plumbing inspected in College Station?

Annual inspections are the standard recommendation for College Station homes. Key things your plumber should check: water heater age and condition (failure typically comes at 8-12 years), supply line connections at every fixture, and drain performance. Homes older than 20 years benefit from a sewer camera inspection ($150-$400) every few years since underground lines deteriorate silently.

What should a plumbing quote include in College Station?

A College Station plumbing estimate should detail: scope of work, material specifications, labor costs, permit fees if needed, waste disposal, and warranty. Check that the plumber's license number and insurance are included. Compare three written quotes for any project over $500; make sure the scope matches across all three.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in College Station?

For non-emergency plumbing in College Station, schedule during fall or winter, demand drops after summer's peak water-heater season and before spring's outdoor plumbing projects. Emergency calls cost 50-100% more than scheduled work, so replacing aging water heaters, supply lines, and fixtures proactively during off-peak months saves significantly versus waiting for a failure.
