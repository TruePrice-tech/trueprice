Plumbing in Collierville: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Collierville has moderate water hardness — not extreme, but enough to warrant annual water heater flushing. A whole-house filter can improve taste and reduce minor buildup in pipes and fixtures.

❄

Freeze risk

While Collierville doesn't see extreme cold regularly, freezing temps do occur. Insulate outdoor faucets and any pipes in unheated spaces. Disconnect garden hoses before the first frost.

🔧

Material recommendation

PEX piping is the most popular repipe material in Collierville. It's flexible, quiet, and typically costs 30-40% less than copper. Copper remains a good choice if you prefer the durability and resale appeal.

💡

Local tip

In Collierville, homes from the 1970s-80s era are hitting the age where original plumbing starts failing. Water heaters last 8-12 years, and supply pipes 40-60 years. If you're in that window, budget for upgrades.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Collierville?

Plumber rates in Collierville typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
