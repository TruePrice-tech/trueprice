Concrete Driveway Cost in Colorado (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in Colorado typically run $3,800–$10,400 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 4000 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 36-inch design frost line, and the state sits in severe freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F2). Colorado's combination of expansive bentonitic clay subsoil across the Front Range and Eastern Plains, F2 (severe) freeze-thaw exposure across the state, and 36-inch frost depth forces aggressive expansive-soil mitigation on every Colorado residential foundation pour — Front Range subdivisions in Aurora, Castle Rock, and Highlands Ranch routinely use post-tension foundation specifications instead of standard slab-on-grade, and Colorado road de-icing salt exposure on every grade-level driveway edge drives 6-7% air-entrainment specification on every residential pour to prevent freeze-thaw spalling.

Colorado climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 36-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F2 — severe freeze-thaw + de-icer exposure, 5-7% air-entrainment required
  • Typical residential mix: 4000 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: Very high — bentonitic clay subsoil drives post-tension foundation requirement
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S2 — severe sulfate exposure, ASTM C150 Type II/V cement required
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC B — minor seismic risk, standard tie-down requirements
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: High — winter de-icing salt drives air-entrainment + corrosion-protected rebar

Colorado licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide concrete license — Denver, Colorado Springs, Aurora issue municipal contractor credentials (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential driveway pour > 100 sq ft; mandatory soils report disclosure required on every Colorado residential foundation pour above 5% slope per CO Soil Disclosure Act

How concrete driveway costs vary in Colorado

Colorado's combination of expansive bentonitic clay subsoil across the Front Range and Eastern Plains, F2 (severe) freeze-thaw exposure across the state, and 36-inch frost depth forces aggressive expansive-soil mitigation on every Colorado residential foundation pour — Front Range subdivisions in Aurora, Castle Rock, and Highlands Ranch routinely use post-tension foundation specifications instead of standard slab-on-grade, and Colorado road de-icing salt exposure on every grade-level driveway edge drives 6-7% air-entrainment specification on every residential pour to prevent freeze-thaw spalling.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Colorado Soil Disclosure Act (CRS §6-6.5) imposes the most restrictive expansive-soil disclosure regime in the country — every Colorado residential property seller must provide a written disclosure of swelling soil risk to every buyer, and every Colorado residential foundation pour must include a Colorado Geological Survey-recognized soils report identifying expansive bentonitic clay subsoil risk, plus Colorado Front Range subdivisions routinely require post-tension foundation specifications instead of standard slab-on-grade due to bentonite-driven slab heave.
