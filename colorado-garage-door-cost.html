Garage Door Replacement Cost in Colorado (2026)

Garage door replacements in Colorado typically run $1,300–$4,600 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 105 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Colorado's combination of high-elevation freeze-thaw cycling across the Front Range driving sealant degradation on every garage-door bottom seal at 4-6 year intervals, road de-icing salt exposure on every grade-level garage-door bottom edge driving stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track requirement on every Front Range install, and high HOA architectural-control prevalence across 70%+ of Aurora, Castle Rock, Highlands Ranch, and Parker residential subdivisions plus Colorado state energy code R-16 minimum mandate drives unique cold-climate-and-HOA-driven garage-door specification.

Colorado climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 105 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-16 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Low — minimal termite pressure
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: High — 60-80% of subdivisions enforce HOA architectural-committee pre-approval
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: None — no salt-air corrosion exposure
  • IECC climate zone: 5B-7

Colorado licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide garage-door license — Denver, Colorado Springs, Aurora, and other home-rule municipalities issue local Building Contractor credentials (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening; no state license required; replacement-only installs typically permit-exempt; HOA architectural committee approval mandatory in 70%+ of Front Range subdivisions; Colorado state energy code amendments mandate R-16 minimum garage-door insulation

How garage door installation costs vary in Colorado

State-specific code or insurance rule: Colorado Revised Statutes §38-33.3 (Colorado Common Interest Ownership Act) provides one of the most enforceable HOA architectural-control statutes in the country with mandatory pre-approval review on garage-door color/material/style across 70%+ of Front Range subdivisions in Aurora, Castle Rock, Highlands Ranch, Parker, Centennial, and Lone Tree, and Colorado state energy code amendments separately mandate R-16 minimum garage-door insulation on every new-construction residential install — one of the highest mandatory R-value tiers below Alaska and Minnesota's R-19.
