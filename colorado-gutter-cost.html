Gutter Installation Cost in Colorado (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Colorado typically runs $1,200–$4,500, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 17 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Colorado's 17-inch state-average annual rainfall masks dramatic Front Range vs Western Slope variation, with Denver and Colorado Springs receiving 14-16 inches/year primarily as summer thunderstorm bursts that drive monsoon-style downspout overflow on undersized 5-inch K-style — and Front Range hailstones with 2-inch+ diameter routinely bend aluminum K-style profiles, driving Class 4 impact-rated gutter material adoption on Denver and Colorado Springs hail-prone ZIP codes.

Colorado rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 17 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 30 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: Moderate — periodic ice-dam events on north-facing eaves
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: Moderate — mixed canopy, gutter guards optional

Colorado licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide gutter contractor license — Denver, Colorado Springs, Aurora issue municipal credentials (official site)
  • Permit: city building permit not typically required for gutter-only installation

How gutter installation costs vary in Colorado

State-specific code or insurance rule: Colorado HB 16-1005 (Rain Barrel Bill) is the most restrictive residential rainwater harvesting law in the country — Colorado homeowners are limited to two 55-gallon rain barrels per single-family residence, capturing only roof-runoff water for outdoor irrigation use, with the strict cap reflecting Colorado's prior-appropriation water rights doctrine that treats roof runoff as belonging to downstream water rights holders.
