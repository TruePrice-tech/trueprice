What Columbia residents ask about plumbing

Why does my water heater make popping noises?

Popping or rumbling sounds come from sediment buildup at the bottom of the tank. Flushing the tank annually prevents this and extends the heater's lifespan by 2-3 years.

How do I know if I have a slab leak?

Signs include unexplained spikes in your water bill, warm spots on the floor, the sound of running water when fixtures are off, and cracks in the foundation or damp baseboards.

What is a backflow preventer and do I need one?

A backflow preventer stops contaminated water from flowing back into the clean supply. Most municipalities require them on irrigation systems and commercial properties. Some require annual testing.

How often should I have my plumbing inspected in Columbia?

Schedule a plumbing inspection at least once a year in Columbia. Focus areas: water heater condition (8-12 year typical life), flexible supply lines under sinks (replace braided steel lines after 10 years), and the main sewer line. For homes over 20 years old, a camera inspection of the sewer line ($150-$400) every 3-5 years catches problems before they surface inside.

What should a plumbing quote include in Columbia?

In Columbia, a solid plumbing quote includes labor hours, specified materials and brands, any permit costs, cleanup and disposal fees, and warranty terms. The contractor's license and insurance should be listed. Get three quotes scoped identically to compare pricing fairly on jobs over $500.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Columbia?

The sweet spot for scheduling plumbing in Columbia is early fall. Between summer water-heater demand and winter freeze calls, meaning better contractor availability and fairer pricing. Since emergency plumbing runs 50-100% above scheduled rates, proactive maintenance during off-peak periods is a straightforward way to save.
