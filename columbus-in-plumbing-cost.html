Plumbing in Columbus: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Hard water is a major issue in Columbus. If you notice white crusty deposits on faucets, your water heater is fighting the same buildup inside. A softener system typically costs $1,000-2,500 installed and protects all your plumbing.

❄

Freeze risk

In Columbus, pipe freezing is a moderate-to-high risk during the coldest months. Make sure you know where your main water shutoff valve is — quick action when a pipe bursts can save thousands in damage.

🔧

Material recommendation

For Columbus homes, PEX is the go-to repiping material. Its flexibility means it handles freeze-thaw cycles better than copper or CPVC. It's also faster to install, reducing labor costs.

💡

Local tip

Many Columbus homes built before 1970 have galvanized steel pipes that corrode from the inside out. If your water pressure has been steadily dropping or you see rusty water, a whole-house repipe is likely needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Columbus?

Plumber rates in Columbus typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
