Columbus Utility Rate and Net Metering Structure

AEP Ohio residential solar operates under Ohio's net metering framework (ORC 4928.671) with credits at the generation component of the retail rate (approximately 60-70 percent of the full retail rate) rather than full retail 1-for-1. This is less favorable than full retail net metering states. Ohio Solar Renewable Energy Credits (Ohio SRECs) trade at $10-$30 per MWh through the PJM GATS platform, providing additional revenue. Columbia Gas of Ohio service is unrelated to solar economics. The combination of partial-retail net metering plus Ohio SRECs determines Columbus solar payback.

The flat topography means consistent AEP Ohio feeder capacity without the transformer-capacity variations that hillside cities face. Columbus Solar Incentives and Tax Credits

Ohio stacks a moderate incentive set: the 30 percent federal ITC, Ohio SRECs worth $10-$30 per MWh through PJM GATS (generating $1,500-$4,500 additional revenue for a typical 6-8 kW system over 15 years), and Ohio property tax exemption for solar improvements under ORC 5709.53. Ohio charges sales tax on solar equipment (5.75 percent state plus local). AEP Ohio has no direct solar rebate. Federal ITC plus Ohio SRECs reduce effective net cost of a $22,000 system to $12,000-$14,000 over the SREC period. The SREC market provides meaningful revenue that generic Midwest states lack.

Columbus Solar Permitting Process

City of Columbus Department of Building and Zoning Services issues solar permits through the online portal with 2-4 week turnaround. German Village Commission approval must precede the city permit for contributing structures. Dublin, Upper Arlington, and Westerville each have separate portals with 1-3 week turnaround. Ohio electrical contractor license is required. AEP Ohio interconnection application can be filed in parallel with the permit process to avoid serial delay. The flat topography rarely triggers structural engineering review, keeping permit costs lower than hillside markets.

Columbus Battery Storage Options and Rebates
