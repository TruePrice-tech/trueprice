Common window replacement questions in Columbus

Should I replace window screens too?

New windows almost always include new screens. If reusing inserts, check screen mesh for holes and frame corners for separation. Replacement screens cost $25-$75 each.

Do I need a permit for window replacement in Columbus?

In Columbus, permits are typically required when changing window size or adding new openings. Same-size replacements usually do not need permits, but check your local building department.

What U-factor should I look for?

Lower U-factor means better insulation. Aim for 0.25-0.30 in cold climates and 0.30-0.40 in mild climates. Energy Star requirements vary by region.

What is Low-E glass?

Low-E (low emissivity) coating reflects infrared heat while allowing visible light through. It reduces heat gain in summer and heat loss in winter, cutting energy costs 10-25%.

Are bay and bow windows more expensive?

Bay windows cost $1,500-$4,000 installed and bow windows $2,500-$5,500. Both require structural support and add 3-4x the cost of a standard same-sized window.

Licensing and code rules within Columbus

Columbus window permits are issued by the Department of Building and Zoning Services (BZS). Because Ohio lacks a dedicated glazing credential, BZS verifies contractor municipal registration, bonding, and insurance at application. The German Village Commission is the defining Columbus regulatory layer: it controls all exterior modifications on contributing structures across the 233-acre German Village Historic District. The Commission mandates specific window materials (wood or Commission-approved fiberglass exclusively; vinyl is categorically banned), sash geometry, meeting-rail proportions, muntin layouts, and exterior paint palettes. Commission applications require scaled drawings plus physical material samples and typically take 4-8 weeks for a decision. Denials mean redesign and resubmission, so a pre-application consultation with Commission staff is strongly advisable. Federal EPA RRP rules apply to all pre-1978 properties, encompassing nearly every home in German Village, Victorian Village, and Italian Village. BZS separately enforces Ohio Residential Code energy requirements, checking NFRC label data on every installed unit at final inspection.
