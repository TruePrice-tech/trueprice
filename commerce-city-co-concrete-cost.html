Frequently asked concrete questions

How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

How much does concrete work cost in Commerce City?

Concrete costs in Commerce City range from $6-$12 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, patios) to $15-$30/sqft for stamped or decorative finishes, including labor and materials.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

More questions about concrete in Commerce City

What does a concrete driveway cost per square foot?

Plain 4-inch concrete runs $6-$12 per square foot installed in 2026. Stamped concrete adds $4-$8/sqft for pattern and color. Heavy-duty 6-inch driveways for RVs or trucks cost $9-$15/sqft. A 600 sq ft two-car driveway typically lands between $5,800 and $8,100.

How much does a 1,000 sq ft concrete patio cost?

Typical 2026 ranges for a 1,000 sq ft project: plain broom-finish $8,000-$12,000, exposed-aggregate $9,500-$14,500, stamped $11,000-$16,500. Tearing out an existing slab adds $1,500-$3,000. Reinforcing wire mesh and rebar are usually $0.50-$1.00/sqft and worth it on driveways, optional on patios.

What does a slab foundation cost in Commerce City?

A monolithic slab foundation for a typical single-family house in Commerce City runs $5,000-$15,000 depending on square footage and soil conditions. A garage slab is $2,500-$6,000. Full basement foundations are a different scope entirely and run $15,000-$30,000.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Commerce City?

Driveways, patios over a certain size, and any structural slab in Commerce City require a permit and an inspection of the base prep and rebar before the pour. Sidewalks, repairs, and resurfacing usually do not. Your contractor pulls the permit — never pull it in your own name.

About concrete work in Commerce City, CO

Where Commerce City's pricing comes from. At 11% above national average labor rates, Commerce City's contractor pricing starts from a higher baseline than most US markets. The gap reflects both local wages and regional demand for skilled trades. On concrete work, Commerce City runs about 8% above the US average based on our pricing data across metro areas.

Local conditions in Commerce City. Commerce City has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Commerce City for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Standard concrete in Commerce City runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Commerce City permits and code requirements. Permits are usually required for concrete work in Commerce City; verify the specifics with the local building department before committing.

The Commerce City contractor pool. At 49,799 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. The Commerce City market has grown at a steady pace, producing a solid base of experienced contractors without the severe backlogs of booming cities.

Local factors: concrete work in Commerce City, CO

How the local trade market looks. With steady but not explosive growth, Commerce City's contractor market offers reasonable availability. Most shops can schedule work within 1-3 weeks, and shopping for quotes is straightforward. In Commerce City (49,799 residents), concrete work is often done by small local companies or general contractors. Best months to book. The concrete season in Commerce City runs May through October when overnight temps stay above 40°F consistently. Spring is peak demand as homeowners address winter frost damage. Fall pours (September-October) offer better contractor availability and fewer weather delays.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Commerce City track within a few points of the national average for concrete work, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. Commerce City's relatively young housing stock (34 years average) simplifies most concrete work projects. Red flags to watch for. Any Commerce City contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag.
