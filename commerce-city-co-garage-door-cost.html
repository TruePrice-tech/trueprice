Frequently asked garage door questions

Are smart garage door openers worth it?

Smart openers add $100-$200 over basic models. They let you open, close, and monitor the door from your phone, set schedules, and receive alerts. Useful for deliveries and forgetting to close.

How much does garage door spring replacement cost?

Torsion spring replacement costs $200-$400 for parts and labor. Extension springs cost $150-$300. Never attempt spring replacement yourself since the stored tension is extremely dangerous.

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Commerce City?

Garage door replacement in Commerce City typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

What causes a garage door to be noisy?

Common causes: worn rollers (replace nylon for steel), dry hinges (lubricate with silicone spray), loose hardware, a chain-drive opener, or worn spring coils. A tune-up costs $100-$200.

What R-value garage door do I need in Commerce City?

Commerce City's winters demand serious insulation. An R-12 to R-16 door is the baseline for attached garages, with R-16 to R-18 recommended if you use the space as a workshop or heat it. The $200-$500 upgrade cost pays back through lower heating bills in rooms adjacent to the garage within 3-5 years.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Commerce City?

Given that Commerce City homes average 34 years old, many original garage doors are due for replacement rather than repair. A new insulated door and modern opener runs $1,200-$3,000 and recoups 90-100% at resale. Repairs make financial sense for doors under 15 years old with specific damage, but throwing money at an aging system usually delays an inevitable replacement.

What's the best season to replace a garage door in Commerce City?

Commerce City homeowners get the best pricing and selection by scheduling garage door replacement during late spring or early fall. Moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing, and you can save 5-10% versus peak season pricing. Spring demand surges from cold-weather spring failures, so plan ahead to avoid the rush.

Local Garage Door Guide for Commerce City, CO

Cold winters push Commerce City garage doors into two failure modes: thermal losses through uninsulated panels and spring breakage during deep-cold cycles. An R-12+ door and a torsion spring rated for the local temperature range both pay for themselves through avoided replacement and emergency service calls.

Many Commerce City homes (average age 34 years) have garage doors and openers that predate modern safety standards. Openers installed before 1993 may lack auto-reverse sensors, which are now required by federal law. If your opener does not have photo-eye sensors at the base of the door, replace the opener regardless of whether it still functions since this is a serious safety and liability issue.
