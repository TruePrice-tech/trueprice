How to compare auto repair quotes correctly

Auto repair estimates vary wildly because shops use different labor rate books, charge different diagnostic fees, and use different parts tiers. A $400 estimate and an $800 estimate for the same job usually come down to OEM vs aftermarket parts and labor hours quoted.

Flat-rate labor (from Mitchell or AllData) means you pay the book time regardless of actual hours. This can help or hurt you — ask whether the estimate is flat-rate or actual hours.

Compare parts warranty, labor warranty, and diagnostic fee treatment (waived if work proceeds, or charged separately). Itemized estimates beat lump-sum every time.

Most important fields to compare

  • Total estimate
  • Parts breakdown (OEM vs aftermarket)
  • Labor hours & hourly rate
  • Diagnostic fee
  • Parts warranty
  • Labor warranty
  • Loaner / shuttle availability
  • Itemized vs flat-rate
  • Shop supplies / disposal fees
  • Estimated completion time
