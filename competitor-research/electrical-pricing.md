# Electrical Vertical — Pricing Reference

Sources: HomeGuide, Angi, HomeAdvisor, Fixr, Forbes Home, Bob Vila, This Old House, Generac dealer pricing, ChargePoint installer network, NEC code references. Numbers reflect 2025 national pricing and should be regionally adjusted (see multipliers section).

---

## 1. Service Panel Upgrade

The single most common big-ticket electrical job. Always requires a permit and inspection. Always requires a load calculation per NEC 220.83.

| Job | Typical Range | Average | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 100A → 200A (same location) | $1,800 – $3,500 | $2,500 | Most common upgrade |
| 100A → 200A (relocation) | $2,500 – $5,000 | $3,800 | Adds masonry/conduit work |
| 200A new install | $1,300 – $3,000 | $2,200 | New construction |
| 200A → 400A | $4,000 – $8,000 | $5,500 | Often needs new meter base + utility coordination |
| Meter base replacement | $500 – $1,200 | $850 | Often required when upgrading |
| Mast / weather head replacement | $300 – $800 | $500 | Often bundled |
| Service drop coordination (utility) | $0 – $500 | varies | Some utilities free, others charge |

### Brand pricing (200A panel hardware only)

| Brand / Line | Panel cost (200A, 40-space) | Tier |
|---|---|---|
| Square D Homeline | $180 – $260 | Budget / residential workhorse |
| Square D QO | $300 – $480 | Premium residential, tighter trip curve |
| Eaton BR | $170 – $250 | Budget |
| Eaton CH (Cutler-Hammer) | $290 – $450 | Premium |
| Siemens (residential) | $180 – $290 | Budget/mid |
| GE / ABB (Powermark) | $170 – $260 | Budget |
| Span Smart Panel | $3,500 – $4,500 | Smart/luxury (panel only) |
| Leviton Smart Load Center | $1,800 – $2,800 | Smart |

Federal Pacific Stab-Lok and Zinsco panels are NOT brands you upgrade TO — they are panels you upgrade FROM. Both have documented failure-to-trip issues; insurance carriers increasingly refuse to write policies on homes with them.

### Material vs labor split (panel upgrade)

- Material (panel, breakers, meter base, wire, lugs, grounding): 30 – 40%
- Labor (4 – 8 hours licensed electrician + 1 helper): 50 – 60%
- Permit + inspection: 5 – 10%

## 10. Knob & Tube and Aluminum Wiring

### Knob & tube replacement

| Scope | Range |
|---|---|
| Partial (one room or circuit) | $1,500 – $4,000 — but see red flags doc, insurance won't honor |
| Whole house (1,500 sq ft) | $8,000 – $15,000 |
| Whole house (2,500 sq ft) | $14,000 – $25,000 |
| Whole house (3,500+ sq ft, lath & plaster) | $22,000 – $40,000 |

### Aluminum wiring remediation (pre-1972 branch circuits)

| Method | Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| COPALUM crimp (CPSC-approved) | $50 – $85 per connection | $2,500 – $6,500 typical home |
| AlumiConn purple wire nut (CPSC alternative) | $25 – $45 per connection | $1,200 – $3,500 typical home |
| Full rewire | See whole-house rewire above | Only sure fix |
| Pigtailing with regular wire nuts + NoAlox | DO NOT — not CPSC approved |

## 12. Labor & Hourly Rates

| Region | Licensed electrician $/hr | Master electrician $/hr |
|---|---|---|
| Rural Midwest / South | $60 – $95 | $90 – $140 |
| Suburban national avg | $85 – $130 | $120 – $175 |
| Major metros (Chicago, Boston, Denver) | $110 – $165 | $150 – $220 |
| NYC, SF, Seattle, Honolulu | $140 – $220 | $185 – $300 |
| Service call minimum (most companies) | $75 – $200 flat |
| Trip charge (national franchises) | $59 – $129 |

National franchises (Mr. Electric, Mister Sparky) typically charge 20 – 45% more than local independents and use flat-rate menu pricing rather than time-and-materials. Power Home Remodeling does electrical only as part of larger jobs; their pricing is generally 30 – 60% above local independents.
