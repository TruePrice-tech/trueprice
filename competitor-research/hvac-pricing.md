# HVAC Pricing Research (2026)

Source synthesis: Trane.com pricing guide, HomeGuide, HomeAdvisor/Angi, PickHVAC, Carrier.com, EnergySage, Budget Heating, NuWatt Energy, Modernize, ACDirect. All prices fully installed unless noted, USD, national averages. Adjust by regional multiplier (see bottom).

## 1. Brand tiers and per-ton installed pricing

A residential 3-ton (36,000 BTU) split system is the reference point. National 2026 installed range across all brands and tiers: roughly $5,000 (builder-grade Goodman/Heil, basic 14.3 SEER2, easy swap) to $18,000+ (Lennox Signature / Trane XV / Carrier Infinity variable-speed in tight retrofit).

Per-ton installed averages below assume a like-for-like AC swap on existing ductwork at a 3-ton size. Heat pumps add roughly $1,500-$3,000 over straight AC. Furnace+AC combos add $2,500-$5,000 for the furnace.

### Premium tier (variable-speed, communicating, 18-20+ SEER2)
- **Trane XV (XV18, XV20i)** — $2,800-$3,800/ton installed. 3-ton system $9,500-$13,500. Best inverter compressor reputation, 12-yr parts w/ registration.
- **Carrier Infinity (24VNA0, 26VNA)** — $2,800-$3,900/ton. 3-ton $10,000-$14,000. Greenspeed inverter, communicating wall control required.
- **Lennox Signature / Dave Lennox (XC25, SL28XCV)** — $3,000-$4,500/ton. 3-ton $11,000-$16,500. Highest SEER2 on the market (up to 28 SEER2 historically), but proprietary parts and fewer dealers.
- **American Standard Platinum (AccuComfort Platinum 20)** — $2,800-$3,800/ton. 3-ton $9,500-$13,500. Trane sister brand, same Tyler TX factory, ~5-10% cheaper than Trane badge for same chassis.
- **Bryant Evolution (189BNV, 187BNC)** — $2,700-$3,700/ton. 3-ton $9,000-$13,000. Carrier-built, same internals as Infinity, badge discount.
- **Daikin Fit / DZ20VC** — $2,600-$3,600/ton. 3-ton $8,800-$12,500. Slim side-discharge cabinet, 12-yr full unit replacement warranty if registered.
- **Mitsubishi Hyper-Heat (P-Series ducted, M-Series multi-zone)** — $4,000-$6,000/ton ducted, $5,000-$8,000/ton ductless multi-zone. Cold-climate inverter leader, rated to -13°F.

### Mid tier (two-stage, 16-17 SEER2)
- **Trane XL16i / XL18i** — $2,200-$2,900/ton. 3-ton $7,500-$10,500.
- **Carrier Performance (24ACB7, 24ACC7)** — $2,200-$2,900/ton. 3-ton $7,500-$10,500.
- **Lennox Elite (XC16, EL18XCV)** — $2,300-$3,000/ton. 3-ton $7,800-$10,800.
- **American Standard Gold (Silver/Gold 17)** — $2,100-$2,800/ton. 3-ton $7,200-$10,000.
- **Bryant Preferred (127ANA, 187BNC)** — $2,100-$2,800/ton.
- **Rheem Prestige (RA17, RP17)** — $2,000-$2,700/ton. 3-ton $7,000-$9,800.
- **Ruud Ultra (UA17, UP17)** — $2,000-$2,700/ton. Rheem twin badge, ~5% cheaper.
- **York Affinity (YXV, YXT)** — $2,000-$2,700/ton. 3-ton $6,800-$9,500.
- **Amana ASXC18** — $1,900-$2,600/ton. Goodman premium twin.

### Budget / value tier (single-stage, 14.3-15.2 SEER2)
- **Goodman GSXC / GSX14** — $1,500-$2,200/ton. 3-ton $5,000-$7,500. Daikin-owned, 10-yr unit replacement warranty.
- **Amana ASX14** — $1,600-$2,300/ton. ~5% premium over Goodman badge.
- **Rheem Classic (RA14AZ, RA15AZ)** — $1,700-$2,400/ton. 3-ton $5,800-$8,000.
- **Heil / Tempstar / Comfortmaker (ICP / Carrier value brands)** — $1,600-$2,300/ton. 3-ton $5,500-$7,800.
- **York LX** — $1,700-$2,400/ton.
- **Trane XR14 / XR16** — $1,900-$2,600/ton. Cheapest Trane badge.
- **Carrier Comfort (24ACC6)** — $1,900-$2,600/ton.
- **Lennox Merit (13ACX, ML14XC1)** — $1,900-$2,700/ton.

## 3. SEER2 efficiency tier cost adders

Federal minimum since Jan 1 2023: 14.3 SEER2 (south), 13.4 SEER2 (north). Each step up the ladder adds roughly:

| SEER2 | Adder vs 14.3 baseline (3-ton) | Compressor type |
|---|---|---|
| 14.3 | $0 | Single stage |
| 15.2 | +$400-$700 | Single stage |
| 16 | +$800-$1,400 | Single or 2-stage |
| 17 | +$1,400-$2,200 | 2-stage |
| 18 | +$2,000-$3,200 | 2-stage / inverter |
| 19-20 | +$3,000-$4,800 | Variable-speed inverter |
| 21+ | +$4,500-$7,000 | Premium inverter, communicating |

Variable-speed compressor premium alone (vs comparable 2-stage): roughly $1,500-$2,800 added.

## 4. Ductless mini-split per-head and multi-zone

- **Single zone (1 outdoor + 1 head)**: $3,800-$6,800 installed. Mitsubishi/Daikin premium, $4,500-$7,800.
- **Each additional head (multi-zone)**: $1,500-$2,500 installed (equipment $400-$1,800 + 4-8 hrs labor + line set).
- **2-zone**: $6,500-$10,500. **3-zone**: $8,500-$13,000. **4-zone**: $11,000-$16,500. **5-zone**: $13,500-$19,500.
- **Concealed ducted cassette per zone**: +$800-$1,500 over wall-mount.
- **Hyper-Heat / cold-climate premium**: +$1,000-$2,000 per zone.

## 5. Ductwork

| Work | $/linear ft installed |

| Like-for-like replacement | $25-$55 |
| Retrofit into home with no ducts | $30-$50 |

Typical whole-home duct replacement on a 3-ton system: $5,000-$12,000. Adds $5,000-$16,000 total when bundled with new HVAC.

## 6. Required design and add-ons

- **Manual J load calculation (ACCA)**: $150-$500 standalone, often baked into quote. *Skipping it is the #1 red flag.* Manual S equipment selection and Manual D duct design should accompany.
- **AHRI matched-system certificate**: Free, must be on the proposal — proves the indoor coil + outdoor condenser + air handler are tested as a matched pair. No certificate = no manufacturer warranty and no rebates.
- **Pad / composite platform**: $75-$250.
- **Refrigerant line set (new or replaced)**: $400-$1,200 (line-set flush only: $150-$300).
- **Electrical disconnect + whip**: $150-$400.
- **Condensate pump / float switch**: $100-$300.
- **Permit + inspection**: $100-$500 depending on jurisdiction.
- **Old equipment haul-off**: $100-$300 (should be included; common surprise add).
- **Smart thermostat (Nest, Ecobee, Honeywell T9)**: $200-$450 supplied & installed; communicating thermostats for variable-speed systems are proprietary, $400-$900.

### Indoor air quality add-ons
- UV germicidal lamp in plenum: $400-$900
- Whole-home media filter cabinet (MERV 13-16): $400-$1,000
- HEPA bypass filter: $1,200-$2,500
- Whole-home dehumidifier (Aprilaire 1850, Santa Fe): $2,200-$3,800
- Whole-home humidifier: $400-$900
- iWave / bipolar ionizer: $700-$1,500 (efficacy disputed)
- ERV/HRV (fresh-air ventilation): $1,800-$4,500

## 8. National franchise vs local independent premium

National franchises and chain consolidators typically run 20-40% over a comparable local independent quote on the same equipment, sometimes more on premium tier sales due to commissioned in-home sales reps. Equipment is identical; the gap is overhead, marketing, and sales commissions.

| Brand | Type | Typical pricing posture |
|---|---|---|
| One Hour Heating & Air | Franchise (Authority Brands, ~400 locations) | +25-40% over local |
| ARS / Rescue Rooter | Corporate-owned (~70 locations) | +20-35% |
| Service Experts | Corporate (Enercare-owned, ~90 locations) | +20-35%, pushes Lennox |
| Horizon Services | Regional chain (Mid-Atlantic) | +25-40% |
| Sears Home Services | National | +30-50%, often subcontracted |
| Aire Serv | Franchise (Neighborly) | +15-30% |
| Michael & Son / Len The Plumber | Regional consolidators | +20-35% |
| Local independent (NATE-certified, manufacturer-authorized) | Reference | Baseline |

The franchise premium can sometimes be justified by 24/7 dispatch, financing, and warranty backstop if the contractor goes out of business; for a routine planned replacement, the local independent is almost always the better value provided they are NATE-certified and manufacturer-authorized.

## 9. Quick "is this quote sane" rules of thumb

- 3-ton mid-tier 16 SEER2 AC swap on good ducts: **$7,500-$10,500**. Anything over $13K needs justification.
- 3-ton ducted heat pump 17 SEER2: **$10,000-$14,000**.
- 3-ton variable-speed premium tier: **$11,000-$16,000**.
- Per-ton all-in for the most common job (mid-tier replacement): **$2,200-$3,200/ton**.
- Mini-split per zone: **$3,500-$5,500 for the first head, $1,800-$2,500 for each additional**.
