# Kitchen Remodel Regulations + Rebates Reference (2026)

For Woogoro kitchen vertical. All rules current as of April 2026. Build agents should surface these at the right point in the funnel (typically after ZIP and home age are captured).

## 1. Building Permits — When Required

**Almost always required for kitchen remodels.** Triggers:

| Work type | Permit needed? |
|---|---|
| Paint, hardware swap, appliance replacement (like-for-like, no electrical change) | No |
| Cabinet replacement only, same layout | Usually no, but check local |
| New countertops only | No |
| Flooring replacement | Usually no |
| Any plumbing relocation (sink, dishwasher, ice maker line) | **Yes** — plumbing permit |
| Any electrical change (new circuits, panel upgrade, recessed lights) | **Yes** — electrical permit |
| Wall removal or framing | **Yes** — building permit + structural review if load-bearing |
| Gas line work (new gas range, pot filler, gas cooktop) | **Yes** — gas/mechanical permit |
| Range hood ducting through roof or exterior wall | **Yes** — mechanical permit |
| Window or door changes | **Yes** — building permit |

### Permit cost ranges (combined kitchen permit, 2026)

| Region | Cost |
|---|---|
| Rural / small town | $200 - $500 |
| Mid-size city | $500 - $1,500 |
| Boston, Philly | $500 - $2,000 |
| Chicago | $500 - $1,500 |
| Houston, Dallas, Phoenix | $400 - $1,200 |
| Miami-Dade | $400 - $1,500 |
| Los Angeles, SF Bay | $1,500 - $5,000 (+ Title 24 docs $500-$1,500) |
| NYC (DOB filings, expediter) | $1,500 - $8,000 |
| Seattle | $800 - $2,500 |

**Critical:** "Pulling a permit later" after work is done is possible in some jurisdictions but triggers double fees, retroactive inspections, and can require partial demo. Working without a required permit voids most homeowner insurance coverage on the work and creates a disclosure obligation at sale.

Source: Sweeten, Angi permit data, Cal Build Remodel 2026.

## 2. EPA Lead Renovation, Repair and Painting (RRP) Rule — Pre-1978 Homes

### Federal requirement
Any contractor disturbing **more than 6 sqft of interior painted surface** in a home built **before 1978** must be **EPA RRP Lead-Safe Certified**. Kitchen remodels almost always trigger this (drywall, cabinet removal, framing exposure all disturb painted surfaces).

### Requirements
- Contractor firm must hold EPA Firm Certification ($300 fee, 5-yr renewal).
- At least one Certified Renovator on staff (8-hr EPA-approved course).
- Must give homeowner the **"Renovate Right"** EPA pamphlet before work starts and get signed receipt.
- Must follow lead-safe work practices: containment, plastic sheeting, HEPA vacuum, prohibited methods (no open-flame burning, no power sanding without HEPA shroud, no high-temp heat guns >1100°F).
- Records retained 3 years.

### Penalties
Up to **$37,500 per violation per day**. EPA actively enforces; multiple high-profile cases against contractors and homeowners (yes, homeowners can be co-liable if they hired uncertified).

### Practical implication for the estimator
- If home age is pre-1978, add line item: **"Lead-safe RRP work practices + testing: $400-$1,500"**
- Surface a contractor RRP cert check ("Ask your contractor for EPA RRP firm cert number and verify at epa.gov/lead/firm-search")
- States with their own programs (mostly more strict): AL, GA, IA, KS, MA, MS, NC, OK, OR, RI, UT, WA, WI

Source: EPA.gov Lead RRP Program, NAHB Q&A, NCHH RRP FAQ.

## 3. Asbestos Testing — Pre-1980 Homes

### What contains asbestos in a kitchen
- **Vinyl floor tile and sheet flooring** (1950s-1980s) — VERY common
- **Mastic / floor adhesive** under tile
- **Popcorn / textured ceilings** (pre-1978)
- **Pipe insulation** (older homes)
- **Drywall joint compound** (some pre-1980)
- **Cement board / millboard** behind ranges or near hood vents

### Rule
Federal AHERA + NESHAP and most state rules require testing before disturbing **any suspect material in pre-1980 homes**. Removal must be done by a licensed asbestos abatement contractor in most states.

### Costs
- Sample test (PLM): $25-$75/sample, typically 3-6 samples = $100-$400
- Inspection by licensed inspector: $250-$800
- Abatement (if positive): $1,500-$5,000 typical kitchen flooring removal; popcorn ceiling $1,000-$3,000; whole-room $5k-$15k

### State-strict states
CA (DOSH), MA, NY, NJ, CT, IL, MN, OR, WA enforce strictly with permits and notifications required.

### Practical implication
Pre-1980 home → estimator adds: **"Asbestos testing $100-$400 + potential abatement $1,500-$5,000 if positive"**

## 4. Energy Star + Federal IRA Tax Credits (2026 Status)

### MAJOR 2026 CHANGE — 25C Credit ENDED
The Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit (Section 25C) — which gave 30% back up to $2,000 on heat pump water heaters and $1,200/yr on other efficiency upgrades — **ended December 31, 2025** for new installations under recent legislation. Anything installed on or before 12/31/2025 can still be claimed on 2025 taxes filed in 2026, but **new 2026 installs do not qualify federally**.

### What still exists in 2026
1. **HEEHRA (High-Efficiency Electric Home Rebate Act) — POINT-OF-SALE rebates**
   - Funded by IRA, administered state-by-state. Most states launched 2024-2025.
   - Income-qualified: <80% AMI gets up to 100% rebate; 80-150% AMI gets 50%.
   - Covers: heat pump water heater (up to $1,750), induction/electric stove (up to $840), heat pump dryer (up to $840), electrical panel upgrade (up to $4,000), wiring (up to $2,500), insulation/air sealing (up to $1,600).
   - **Kitchen-relevant items: induction cooktops/ranges + heat pump water heater + panel upgrades.**
   - Status by state varies — check energy.gov/save/rebates and your state energy office.

2. **State-specific programs (top 15 utility/state rebates relevant to kitchens, 2026):**
   | Program | Region | Kitchen-relevant rebate |
   |---|---|---|
   | TECH Clean California | CA | $1,000-$3,100 heat pump water heater |
   | BayREN Home+ | SF Bay | $300-$2,500 induction range, $1,000 HPWH |
   | LADWP | LA | $500-$1,500 efficient appliance bundles |
   | SCE / SoCalGas | Southern CA | $300-$1,000 induction cooktop |
   | NYSERDA | NY | $700 induction, $1,000-$2,500 HPWH |
   | Mass Save | MA | $750 HPWH, $500 induction |
   | Efficiency Maine | ME | $750 HPWH, $500 induction |
   | Efficiency Vermont | VT | $600 HPWH |
   | Energy Trust of Oregon | OR | $500-$1,500 HPWH, $300 induction |
   | Focus on Energy | WI | $300-$700 HPWH |
   | Xcel Energy | CO/MN | $400-$1,200 HPWH |
   | ComEd | IL | $200-$500 efficient appliance |
   | Eversource | CT/MA/NH | $400-$1,200 HPWH |
   | Duke Energy | NC/SC/FL | $200-$500 HPWH |
   | Austin Energy | Austin TX | $400-$1,200 HPWH |

3. **ENERGY STAR appliance** required to qualify for almost all rebates above. Look for ENERGY STAR Most Efficient label for highest rebate tier.

4. **LED lighting** has no federal credit but most utilities offer $5-$50 rebates per fixture or instant in-store discounts.

### Practical implication
Estimator should ask: "Switching to induction range or installing a heat pump water heater?" → if yes and income qualified → surface HEEHRA + state rebate adders. Show net cost after rebate.

## 6. HOA / Condo / Co-op Approval

### Common requirements for kitchen remodels in attached/multi-family
- **Alteration agreement** — formal contract between owner and association covering scope, contractor insurance, work hours, common-area protection, restoration obligation.
- **Plans submission** — typically architect-stamped drawings.
- **Insurance** — contractor must name association as additional insured ($1M-$5M GL minimum).
- **Engineer letter** for any wall removal (most condo walls are non-bearing but the association requires proof).
- **Working hours** — usually weekdays 8am-5pm or 9am-5pm only; no weekends in many co-ops.
- **Elevator protection / freight elevator scheduling** — fees $250-$2,500.
- **Application fee** — $250-$1,500 typical, NYC co-ops $1,000-$5,000+.
- **Approval timeline** — 2-12 weeks. Skipping = stop-work order, fines, potential reversal of work.

### NYC co-op note
NYC co-op alteration agreements are notoriously strict. Many require licensed architects, expediters, and large insurance limits. Budget **$3,000-$10,000** in soft costs just for board approval and filings on top of DOB permits.

## 7. ADA / Universal Design / Aging-in-Place

### What it means in a kitchen
- 36" min clearance, 48" preferred between counters
- Lower countertop sections (28-34" vs standard 36") for seated use
- Pull-out shelves, drawer-based storage
- Lever faucets, touchless faucets
- Side-opening wall ovens
- Induction cooktop (no flame, auto-shutoff)
- Contrasting edges, task lighting
- Anti-scald single-handle faucets

### Federal incentives
- **No federal tax credit** specifically for aging-in-place kitchen mods.
- **HSA/FSA reimbursement** possible if doctor-prescribed for medical necessity (e.g., wheelchair user).
- **Medicare** does NOT cover home modifications.
- **VA HISA grant** — up to $6,800 for service-connected veterans, $2,000 non-service-connected, for medically necessary mods.

### State tax credits / incentives (2026)
| State | Credit |
|---|---|
| **Virginia** | Up to $5,000 livable home tax credit (refundable) for qualifying retrofits |
| **Georgia** | Up to $500 retrofit credit |
| **Maryland** | Accessible Homes for Seniors loan/grant program |
| **Colorado** | Some local senior modification grants |
| **New Jersey** | NJ Accessible Home Tax Credit pilot |
| **Connecticut** | Property tax abatement for some accessibility mods |

### Practical implication
If user indicates "aging in place" or "wheelchair access," surface VA HISA + state credit + relevant Universal Design line items.

## 8. Historic District / Landmark Approval

### When it applies
- Home is in a designated local, state, or National Register historic district
- Home is individually landmarked
- Some federal historic tax credit properties

### What requires approval
- Window changes visible from street
- Exterior changes (chimney mods for new range hood, exterior vents)
- Sometimes interior changes if they affect significant features (rare)

### Process
- **Certificate of Appropriateness (COA)** from local historic preservation commission
- Review timeline: 4-12 weeks
- Cost: $0-$500 application
- Restrictions: may dictate window style, materials, vent locations
- **Federal Historic Rehabilitation Tax Credit (20%)** available only for income-producing properties (rentals); does NOT apply to owner-occupied homes
- Some states (e.g., MD, VA, MO, NC, IA, WI) offer state historic credits for owner-occupied: 10-25% of qualified rehab

### Practical implication
Estimator should ask: "Is your home in a historic district or landmarked?" → if yes, add COA timeline + potentially state historic credit info.

## 9. Plumbing & Electrical Code Triggers (2026 IRC/IPC/NEC)

### Kitchen-specific NEC requirements (often missed by DIY/sketchy contractors)
- **GFCI protection** required on all kitchen receptacles serving countertop
- **AFCI protection** required on most kitchen branch circuits (NEC 2017+)
- **Two 20-amp small appliance circuits** minimum for countertop receptacles
- **Dedicated circuit** for dishwasher, disposal, microwave (often shared in older homes — code violation)
- **Range circuit:** 40-50 amp 240V dedicated
- **Receptacle spacing:** no point along countertop more than 24" from a receptacle; islands must have at least one
- **Tamper-resistant receptacles** (TR) required
- **Under-cabinet/over-counter lighting** must be on switched circuit

### IPC plumbing
- **Air gap or air break** required for dishwasher drain (CA strict)
- **Water hammer arrestors** on quick-close valves
- **Backflow prevention** on pot filler installations
- **Vent stack** for new sink locations

These trigger inspection failures if missed; if a contractor's bid does not show electrical/plumbing rough-in inspection line items, it's a red flag.
