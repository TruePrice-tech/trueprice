# Landscaping install pricing reference (2026)

Scope: install-only landscape work. Excludes recurring maintenance (mowing, fert/weed) and fence installs (covered by fencing vertical). Numbers below are 2026 nationally-blended installed prices unless noted. Use regional multipliers at the end to localize.

## Per-subcategory details

### Sod installation
- Material vs labor split: roughly 55-65% material / 35-45% labor on flat lots. Steeper or fenced-in yards push labor to 50%.
- Per-pallet wholesale: Bermuda $180-260, Fescue $200-315, St Augustine $250-380, Zoysia $300-450. A pallet covers ~450 sq ft.
- Crew/time: 2-3 person crew installs ~3,000-5,000 sq ft per day after prep. Prep (sod cut, till, level, starter fert) is the slow step and is often a separate $0.40-$0.80/sf line item.
- Top suppliers: SiteOne Landscape Supply (national), Ewing Outdoor Supply, John Deere Landscapes (now SiteOne), local sod farms (always cheapest, fresher cut, contractors prefer).
- Customer pitfalls: removal of existing lawn ($0.50-$1.20/sf) and grading are usually NOT in headline quotes. Watering for 2-3 weeks is on the homeowner.

### Hydroseeding
- $0.10-$0.35/sf installed; sweet spot $0.18 for 5,000-15,000 sf jobs.
- 60-80% cheaper than sod for the same area, but 3-6 week germination and you cannot walk on it.
- Best ROI on lots >7,500 sf where sod becomes cost-prohibitive.

### Sprinkler / irrigation
- Per-zone economics:
  - 4-zone basic (small flat lawn): $2,400-$4,800 total, ~$600-$1,200/zone
  - 6-zone mid (typical 1/4 acre): $4,200-$8,400, ~$700-$1,400/zone
  - 12-zone large (1/2 acre, mixed beds + lawn): $9,500-$18,000, ~$800-$1,500/zone
- Drip vs spray: drip costs ~$300-$500/zone less in materials but more labor per linear foot. Net cost similar. Big water savings (30-50%), WaterSense recognized.
- Smart controllers: Rachio 3 (~$230 retail), Hunter Hydrawise HC (~$240), Rain Bird ESP-TM2 with LNK Wi-Fi (~$200). Install adder typically $180-$550 over basic clock controller.
- Brand tiering: Hunter and Rain Bird are the contractor defaults (best parts availability, longest warranty). Toro is common in commercial. Orbit is the box-store DIY tier — most pros refuse to warranty Orbit installs.
- WaterSense: EPA WaterSense-labeled controllers can qualify for utility rebates ($50-$200 in CA, AZ, NV, TX, CO, FL).

### Tree install
- Pricing rule of thumb: ~$150-$250 per caliper inch for the tree, plus $100-$400 install labor for sub-4" caliper, more for larger.
- Small tree (1-2" cal, 5-8 ft): $150-$425 installed
- Medium (2-3" cal): $350-$800 installed
- Large (3-4" cal): $650-$1,800 installed
- Specimen (4-6" cal): $1,400-$4,500 installed
- XL specimen (6"+ cal, 20+ ft, crane required): $3,500-$12,000+ installed
- Plant warranty norms: 1-year replacement warranty is standard IF the homeowner follows the watering schedule. Almost no landscapers offer 2+ year warranties on plant material. Bartlett Tree Experts and a few premium installers offer extended warranties for a 10-15% upcharge.
- Top suppliers: SiteOne, local wholesale nurseries (Monrovia, Bailey's, Iseli for conifers), local growers (always cheapest for natives).

### Shrubs, perennials, annuals
- 3-5 gal shrub: $35-$110 installed (material is $15-$50)
- 7-15 gal shrub: $85-$240 installed
- 1-gal perennials: $14-$35 installed; bulk planting (50+) drops to $10-$18/each
- Annual flats: $30-$70/flat installed; HOAs often spec twice-yearly rotation

### Hardscape — pavers
- Concrete paver patio installed: $18-$38/sf for mid-grade (Belgard Cambridge, Pavestone Plaza), $24-$62/sf for premium (Techo-Bloc Blu, Belgard Mirage, large-format)
- Belgard material only: $3-$7/sf. Techo-Bloc material only: $5-$12/sf. Pavestone (box-store/contractor): $2-$5/sf.
- Stamped concrete patio: $14-$32/sf installed. Cheaper than pavers up front, but cracks within 5-10 years and cannot be repaired invisibly.
- Walkways: 20-30% premium per sf vs patio of same material because of more cuts and edge restraints.
- Retaining walls:
  - Under 3 ft, segmental gravity: $50-$140/lf
  - 3-4 ft with geogrid + drainage: $160-$320/lf
  - 4-6 ft engineered (permit + stamped plans): $380-$700/lf
  - Boulder walls: $25-$55/sf face area
- Top brands: Belgard (Oldcastle), Techo-Bloc (premium Quebec-based, best color retention), Pavestone (Quikrete-owned, value tier), Unilock (premium), Cambridge Pavingstones (Northeast). ICPI-certified installers command 10-20% premium and are required for most manufacturer warranties.

### Drainage
- French drain (exterior, 18-24" depth): $25-$80/lf installed. Pipe + sock + 3/4" washed stone + fabric.
- Interior basement French drain: $40-$85/lf (separate scope, often a waterproofer not a landscaper)
- Dry well: $800-$3,500 installed depending on volume and soil percolation
- Dry creek bed: $30-$95/lf installed (decorative river rock + boulders + filter fabric)
- Swale (graded only): $8-$25/lf
- Almost always priced as a flat package once site visit happens. Per-foot rates are starting points.

### Landscape design fees
- Designer (no architect license): $75-$250/hr, most charge $100-$150
- Landscape architect (licensed): $125-$350/hr
- Per-project flat fees:
  - Concept plan only: $300-$1,200
  - Full residential plan with plant list and hardscape detail: $1,500-$9,500
  - Estate / multi-acre: $10,000-$25,000+
- Common structure: design fee is 8-15% of expected build budget. Many design-build firms credit 50-100% of the design fee back if you hire them to install.
- When bundled vs separate: design-build firms (BrightView, Lambert, most regional firms) bundle design free or credit it. Boutique designers charge separately and let you bid the install.

### Outdoor lighting
- Per fixture installed: $135-$385, sweet spot $220-$280 for low-voltage LED path/spot
- Transformer + smart controller: $375-$1,400 installed. Sized at 125-150% of total fixture wattage.
- Typical 12-fixture residential package: $2,800-$5,500 turnkey
- Brands: FX Luminaire (Hunter, top tier), Kichler (mid), Vista Pro, Volt (DIY-friendly direct), Hinkley. Pros mark up retail 20-40% but include the warranty.
- 40-60% of total project cost is labor (trenching, wire runs, low-voltage hookup).

### Mulching (initial install / large quantity)
- Hardwood mulch installed (delivered + spread): $70-$135/cu yd
- Premium dyed/cedar/cypress: $90-$180/cu yd
- Bulk discount: jobs over 10 yards drop into the $60-$85/yd range
- Coverage: 1 cu yd covers ~100 sf at 3" depth
- Blower-truck install for large estate jobs: $40-$60/yd labor adder, but 3-5x faster

## Local independent vs national franchise

- BrightView, Yellowstone, Ruppert, US Lawns, ValleyCrest (now part of BrightView) are commercial-first. Their residential install pricing typically runs 15-30% above a comparable local design-build firm.
- National players win on: insurance, warranty backing, multi-property HOA contracts, large-scale logistics.
- Local independents win on: price, design creativity, native plant knowledge, willingness to do small or weird jobs.
- The biggest pricing gap is on simple installs (sod, mulch, basic plant beds) where the local crew may be 30-40% cheaper. The gap narrows on complex hardscape and engineered retaining walls where insurance and stamped plans matter.
- ICPI-certified, ASLA-member, or NALP-certified independents charge a 10-20% premium over uncertified locals but are still cheaper than national franchises on residential.
