# Siding Pricing Research

Compiled from HomeAdvisor, Angi, Fixr, Forbes Home, Bob Vila, This Old House, James Hardie pro guides, Remodeling Magazine Cost vs Value 2024, and contractor forums (Contractor Talk, JLC, Reddit r/HomeImprovement / r/Construction). Pricing reflects 2025-2026 installed pricing for a typical 1,500-2,500 sqft single-family home (roughly 1,500-2,500 sqft of wall area, often expressed in 15-25 "squares" of 100 sqft each). Tear-off included unless noted.

A typical 2,000 sqft home has roughly 2,000-2,500 sqft of wall area (gables, dormers add 10-20%). Multiply the per-sqft above by 2,200 sqft for an order-of-magnitude project total.

## Vinyl

Workhorse of US residential remodels - roughly 26-28% of new home siding installs (Census C30 / Principia data). Cheapest, lowest maintenance, and the easiest install which is why labor is the smallest share.

Brand tiers (most retailers carry 2-3 brands under different private labels - they all come from a handful of plants):
- **CertainTeed (Saint-Gobain)**: Monogram, Cedar Impressions, MainStreet. Monogram is the volume premium .044 panel.
- **Mastic / Ply Gem (Cornerstone Building Brands)**: Quest, Carvedwood, Ovation, Structure (insulated).
- **Royal Building Products (Westlake)**: Estate, Haven, Woodland.
- **Variform (Ply Gem)**: Contractor / value lines, frequently sold through ABC Supply.
- **Norandex**: Polar Wall, Sagebrush; common in Midwest.
- **Alside (Associated Materials)**: Charter Oak, Prodigy (insulated), Conquest (builder).
- **Crane Performance Siding**: Solid Core, Board & Batten. Now part of Exterior Portfolio / Royal.

Panel thickness is the single most useful spec to check on a quote: builder/.040 vs standard .042 vs premium .044-.046. A sub-.042 panel is a tell that the salesman is hiding the line.

Installed cost rule of thumb: ~$3.50-5.50/sqft material+labor for builder grade, ~$6-9/sqft for premium .044+, ~$9-13/sqft for shake/scallop accents.

## Insulated vinyl

Standard vinyl with a contoured EPS foam backer (typically by Progressive Foam Technologies) laminated or fitted to the panel back. Adds R-2 to R-4. Brands: CertainTeed CedarBoards, Mastic Structure, Alside Prodigy, Norandex Polar Wall Plus.

Installed: **$9-14/sqft**. Premium of about $2-3/sqft over equivalent non-insulated vinyl. Practical benefits: rigidity (resists denting/oilcanning), modest energy savings, sound dampening. The R-value alone rarely pays back; the rigidity and look are the real value.

Important: insulated vinyl is the only vinyl that has any chance of qualifying for the IRA 25C insulation tax credit, and only if the manufacturer publishes the certification - see siding-regulations-rebates.md.

## Fiber cement

Dominated by **James Hardie** (60%+ US market share). Competitors: **Allura** (Plycem/Elementia), **Nichiha** (panels and architectural), **Plycem**, **Cembrit/Equitone** (commercial).

Hardie product lines:
- HardiePlank lap (most common)
- HardieShingle
- HardiePanel (vertical/board-and-batten)
- Artisan (premium thicker, hand-finished look)
- HZ5 (north of climate line) vs HZ10 (south, hot/humid)
- ColorPlus factory finish vs primed-and-field-painted (~$1-2/sqft adder for ColorPlus)

Installed: **$10-18/sqft** for HardiePlank ColorPlus. Artisan runs $16-22/sqft. Labor is meaningful here - planks are heavy, dust-controlled cutting required (silica OSHA rule), and special blind-nailing patterns.

**Elite Preferred Contractor** premium: Hardie's top installer tier carries roughly a **10-25% labor premium** over non-program installers, but unlocks extended labor warranty backing and better factory warranty terms. Worth it on full-house jobs.

LP SmartSide is the fast-growing alternative - lighter, easier to cut, fewer fastener requirements, and ships pre-primed. Installers often quote it $1-3/sqft below Hardie for similar panel coverage.

## Wood

- **Cedar bevel/lap**: $9-16/sqft installed for clear-grade Western Red Cedar.
- **Cedar shingles/shakes** (hand-nailed): $11-22/sqft.
- **Redwood**: $14-22/sqft, supply-constrained, mostly West Coast.
- **Pine (primed)**: $7-11/sqft, requires aggressive paint maintenance every 5-7 years.

Maintenance: stain/seal every 3-5 years, full repaint every 7-10. Lifetime cost typically exceeds fiber cement once maintenance is included. Sold mostly for historic/architectural reasons.

## Engineered wood (LP SmartSide and friends)

LP SmartSide is the dominant brand. Treated wood strands + zinc borate + resin, factory primed. Comes in lap, panel, trim, and soffit. Fast install: nail gun, standard wood blades, no silica.

Installed: **$8.50-15/sqft**. Strong warranty (5/50 prorated). Growing share - frequently the "Hardie alternative" pitch from independents.

## Aluminum

Largely historic on residential post-1980. Still used for soffit, fascia, custom trim wraps, and some commercial. Installed: $7-13/sqft when sourced as siding. Easily dented, fades. Recyclable (~$0.30-0.50/lb scrap value at tear-off).

## Steel siding

Niche but growing premium category:
- **Quality Edge** (Trudefinition, TruCedar) - steel lap with embossed wood look
- **ABC Seamless** - on-site roll-formed seamless steel, dealer network model
- **TruLog Siding** - steel log-cabin look
- **Edco**, **Rollex**

Installed: $11-19/sqft. Big sell is hail/dent resistance vs vinyl, fire resistance, and 40-year+ finish warranties (Kynar/PVDF coating).

## Stucco

- **Traditional 3-coat (hard-coat)**: lath, scratch, brown, finish coat over weather-resistive barrier (now typically two layers of building paper, "drainage plane" code in CA/OR/WA). $9-16/sqft.
- **One-coat stucco**: $7-11/sqft. Fiber-reinforced single base coat.
- **EIFS / synthetic stucco**: foam board + base coat + mesh + finish. $9-17/sqft. Modern "drainable EIFS" (Dryvit Outsulation Plus, Sto Therm) avoids the 1990s class-action moisture issues.

Hot zones for stucco: AZ, NM, TX, CA, FL. Outside the Sun Belt, stucco usually means re-stucco over existing.

## Brick and stone veneer

- **Brick veneer**: $14-30/sqft. Includes flashing, weeps every 24", brick ties, lintels at openings. Most full-house projects are not pure brick - they're brick wainscot + siding above.
- **Manufactured stone veneer (MSV)**: $14-28/sqft. Dominant brands: Eldorado Stone (Westlake Royal), Coronado Stone, Cultured Stone (Boral/Westlake), ProVia. The Cost vs Value Report repeatedly puts MSV at the **highest ROI of any exterior remodel** (~150% recouped).
- **Natural thin stone veneer**: $25-60/sqft.

Stone is rarely whole-house. It's a 10-25% accent on gables, columns, water tables.

## Trim and accessory cost (the line nobody itemizes)

Trim, J-channel, corner posts, drip edge, light blocks, vent blocks, and starter strip typically run **15-25% of total project cost**. On a $30,000 vinyl job that's $4,500-7,500. Hardie/LP jobs trend higher (20-30%) because of dedicated trim boards (HardieTrim, LP Trim & Fascia). When a quote shows a suspiciously low per-sqft number, trim is usually the missing line.

## Removal of old siding

- Vinyl tear-off: **$0.50-1.50/sqft**.
- Wood lap tear-off: **$1.00-2.50/sqft**.
- Stucco demo (hard-coat with lath): **$2.00-4.50/sqft** - this is the expensive one.
- Brick demo: $4-8/sqft.
- Cement-asbestos siding: **$4-12/sqft** for licensed abatement (regulated - see regulations doc).

Disposal/dump fees are usually folded in but should be a line item.

## House wrap install

- **Tyvek HomeWrap (DuPont)**: $0.30-0.50/sqft material, $0.50-1.00 installed including tape and flashing.
- **Tyvek DrainWrap / CommercialWrap D**: $0.60-1.00/sqft installed; recommended in stucco and wet climates.
- **Typar (Berry Global)**: similar pricing, often $0.05-0.10 cheaper per sqft.
- **Hydrogap / Benjamin Obdyke**: drainage-plane wraps, slight premium.
- **Felt (#15 or #30)**: legacy stucco backing.

A wrap line should always exist on a quote. If the existing wrap is staying, that should be explicitly stated and justified.

## Insulation backing

- **1/4" fanfold under vinyl**: $0.40-0.80/sqft. Cosmetic flatness, minimal R-value (R-1).
- **1/2" - 1" rigid foam (XPS/polyiso)**: $1.50-3.50/sqft installed. R-3 to R-6.
- **Continuous exterior insulation 1.5"-2" polyiso**: $3.00-6.00/sqft installed. R-9 to R-13. Required by IECC in some climate zones for thermal-break compliance.
- **Insulated vinyl panels**: bundled into the panel cost (R-2 to R-4).

## Soffit and fascia replacement

- Vinyl soffit + aluminum fascia wrap: **$8-16/linear foot** of eave.
- Wood replacement before wrap: $4-10/lf rotted run.
- Aluminum coil-stock fascia wrap: $4-8/lf.
- A typical 2,000 sqft home has 150-250 lf of eave. Budget $2,000-4,500.

Often quoted separately or hidden in "trim". Always confirm soffit/fascia is included or excluded in writing.

## National installers vs local independents

Top national/regional siding installers:
- **Power Home Remodeling Group** (Mastic exclusive)
- **Long Roofing & Home Solutions**
- **Sears Home Services / Renewal by Andersen partner network**
- **Lowe's Installed Services** (subcontracted)
- **Home Depot Installed Services** (subcontracted)
- **ABC Seamless** (steel)
- **Champion Windows & Home Exteriors**
- **West Shore Home**
- **Erie Home**

National pricing premium over comparable local independent: typically **25-40%** for the same materials, occasionally up to **60-75%** in top-pressure in-home sales scenarios. The premium pays for marketing, the in-home sales rep commission (often 8-12% of contract), and headquarters overhead. Their materials are the same as anyone else's - usually a private-label of a major mill (Power = Mastic, ABC = mill-direct steel, etc.).

Sears and the box-store programs are subcontracted to local crews, then marked up 30-50% for the brand. Quality varies by which sub picked up the job that week.

## James Hardie Elite Preferred premium

Hardie's contractor program tiers:
1. **Hardie Contractor Alliance** (basic registered installer)
2. **Preferred Contractor**
3. **Elite Preferred Contractor** (top tier, vetted, training, mystery-shop QA)

Elite Preferred premium: roughly **10-25% higher labor cost** than a non-program installer doing the same Hardie job. Justification: extended labor warranty, factory-backing of installation defects, priority warranty claims, and (genuinely) better install quality. On a $40,000 Hardie job that's $4,000-10,000 more.

## Regional multipliers

Compared to US average baseline (1.00):
- San Francisco Bay Area: 1.45-1.65
- New York metro / Long Island / NJ: 1.35-1.55
- Boston / Eastern MA: 1.30-1.50
- Seattle: 1.25-1.45
- Los Angeles / Orange County: 1.25-1.45
- Washington DC / Northern VA: 1.20-1.40
- Denver: 1.10-1.25
- Chicago: 1.05-1.20
- Phoenix: 1.00-1.15
- Atlanta: 0.95-1.10
- Dallas / Houston: 0.95-1.10
- Twin Cities: 1.00-1.15
- Florida (Tampa/Orlando): 0.95-1.10; Miami 1.10-1.25 (HVHZ code adders)
- Rural Midwest / Deep South: 0.80-0.95

Coastal hurricane zones (FL HVHZ, coastal NC/SC, TX coastal counties) add **5-15% on top** for wind-rated panels and fastening schedules.

## Material vs labor split (typical)

| Material | Material % | Labor % |
|---|---|---|
| Vinyl (builder) | 35-45% | 55-65% |
| Vinyl (premium / insulated) | 45-55% | 45-55% |
| LP SmartSide | 45-55% | 45-55% |
| Fiber cement (Hardie) | 40-50% | 50-60% |
| Cedar / shake | 50-60% | 40-50% |
| Stucco (3-coat) | 25-35% | 65-75% |
| Brick veneer | 35-45% | 55-65% |
| Stone veneer | 40-50% | 50-60% |

Anything where the contractor quotes "material is X, labor is Y" and the labor share looks unusually low is a flag - the labor is hidden in markup somewhere else.
