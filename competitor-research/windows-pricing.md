# Windows brand + installed pricing reference (2026)

Scope: per-window INSTALLED pricing (glass + frame + labor, standard double-hung ~3'x5', average region). Ranges reflect low-end small/basic options through premium large/upgraded options. National average baseline 2026: ~$550-750 per window installed across all brands (survey of 16 brands, avg $554/window).

## Per-brand details

### Andersen Windows
- **100 Series** (Fibrex composite): $400-1,500 installed. Entry; sold at Home Depot. Limited colors/sizes. 10-yr glass / 10-yr non-glass warranty.
- **200 Series** (vinyl-clad wood): $450-2,500 installed. Limited configurations; bridge between 100 and 400.
- **400 Series** (vinyl-clad wood): $500-3,000 installed. Flagship wood-clad. 20-yr glass / 10-yr non-glass.
- **A-Series** (Fibrex+wood composite, architectural): $1,100-4,000 installed. Premium architectural line. 20/10 warranty.
- Install method: sold through dealers/lumberyards/Home Depot; install by independent contractors. Usually insert/pocket, full-frame available.
- Sales notes: Non-pressure; distribution model, prices negotiable with dealer. Reputation solid (ENERGY STAR certified across lines).

### Pella
- **250 Series** (vinyl): $450-1,350 installed. Entry vinyl, ENERGY STAR options.
- **350 Series** (reinforced vinyl): $550-1,600 installed. Most efficient vinyl line, triple-pane available.
- **Lifestyle Series** (wood-clad): $600-2,000 installed. Mid-premium wood with aluminum clad exterior.
- **Reserve Series** (wood-clad, traditional/contemporary): $1,200-3,500 installed. High-end custom.
- **Architect Series** (wood-clad premium): $1,200-3,500. Historical replications.
- Install method: Pella Windows & Doors showrooms offer full-service install (direct); also sold at Lowes. Full-frame + pocket both offered.
- Warranty: limited lifetime on vinyl frame, 20-yr on glass, 10-yr on non-glass. Transferable one time.
- Sales notes: In-home showroom reps can be pushy on upsell; Lowes channel is no-pressure but install quality varies. ConsumerAffairs 2-3 stars average.

### Marvin
- **Essential** (Ultrex fiberglass): $900-1,200 installed. Streamlined, cost-conscious fiberglass.
- **Elevate** (Ultrex fiberglass ext / wood int): $1,000-2,400 installed. Wood interior for aesthetic upgrade.
- **Signature Collection** (Ultimate/Modern, wood + alum clad): $1,200-2,500+ installed. Premium architectural.
- Install method: Dealer network; independent installers. Insert and full-frame both common.
- Warranty: 20-yr glass, 10-yr non-glass, transferable.
- Sales notes: Strong reputation; fewer complaints than Pella/Andersen. Energy Star partner. Higher floor price means not for budget shoppers.

### Renewal by Andersen
- **All lines** (Fibrex composite only — proprietary Andersen material): $1,000-3,500 installed, typical mid $1,500-2,000.
- Operates as a separate full-service subsidiary from Andersen (factory-direct, manufacture+measure+install bundled).
- Install method: Always full-frame replacement with branded installers (not subcontractors in most markets).
- Warranty: 20-yr glass, 10-yr non-glass, 2-yr install. Transferable. Voided by non-Andersen glass service.
- Sales notes: Notorious for high-pressure in-home sales, 3-hour presentations, same-day discounts that expire. ConsumerAffairs ~3.5 stars. Price typically 2-3x equivalent dealer Andersen due to bundled direct model. Reddit consensus: good product, overpriced relative to shopping around.

### Window World
- **"Comfort World" (rebadged, typically MI Windows / Simonton / Alside depending on franchise)**: $300-550 installed, national average $373/window (Nov 2025 survey).
- Frame: vinyl only.
- Install method: Franchise model; subcontracted installers paid per window -> speed incentive.
- Warranty: "Limited lifetime" on product, 1-yr install. Transferable one time, voided by non-authorized service.
- Sales notes: Cheapest national chain; 1.9/5 average consumer rating. Common complaints: install shortcuts, no-show service calls, product is lowest-bidder MI Windows. Franchises vary wildly in quality.

### Champion Windows
- **Comfort 365 vinyl** (their only window, factory-direct): $450-1,300 installed, avg ~$474/window (Nov 2025 survey). Marketing prices often quoted $1,600-2,000 before "discounts".
- Frame: vinyl only, proprietary Comfort 365 glass package.
- Install method: Factory-direct, W-2 employee installers in most markets. Always insert/pocket.
- Warranty: Limited lifetime, non-transferable in many states.
- Sales notes: Same-day sales policy; reps trained to inflate price 50%+ then "discount" on the spot. Won't honor lower price if homeowner waits. High BBB complaint volume. Product is mid-grade vinyl — OK quality, bad buying experience.

### Milgard
- **Style Line** (vinyl, budget): $450-900 installed. Narrow frame, budget.
- **Trinsic** (vinyl, contemporary): $440-1,100 installed. Narrowest sightlines in vinyl class.
- **Tuscany** (vinyl, traditional): $485-1,295 installed. Traditional profile.
- **Montecito** (vinyl, value): $400-850 installed. Cost-optimized.
- **Ultra Series** (fiberglass): $700-1,600 installed. Fiberglass option.
- Install method: Dealer network, primarily West Coast / Southwest / Mountain states. Independent installers.
- Warranty: Full lifetime with glass breakage, transferable. Among best in industry.
- Sales notes: Strong West-Coast reputation. Not widely available East. ENERGY STAR partner. Owned by MITER Brands (same parent as MI Windows).

### Simonton
- **5500 (Reflections)** (vinyl): $370-885 installed. Mainstream vinyl.
- **6500 (Impressions)** (vinyl): $470-1,145 installed. **Home Depot exclusive.**
- **9800 Impressions** (vinyl premium): $700-1,400 installed. Top line.
- Frame: vinyl only.
- Install method: Dealer and Home Depot installs; independent subs.
- Warranty: Double lifetime (transferable to next owner at 100%), glass breakage included. Best-in-class warranty.
- Sales notes: Solid mid-tier reputation, widely available nationally. Owned by Cornerstone Building Brands. 2025 Consumer Reports mid-pack.

### Jeld-Wen
- **Builders Vinyl V2500** (vinyl): $250-550 installed. New-construction grade, not recommended for premium replacements.
- **Premium Vinyl V4500** (vinyl): $375-750 installed. Upgraded vinyl, decent mid-budget.
- **Premium Atlantic Aluminum** (aluminum impact): $70-85/sqft ~= $600-1,400/window. FL impact/coastal market.
- **Siteline** (wood-clad): $600-1,500 installed. Lower-volume wood line.
- Install method: Primarily sold new-construction through lumber yards, also Home Depot. Limited replacement dealer network.
- Warranty: 20-yr on vinyl components, 10-yr on glass. Non-transferable on most lines.
- Sales notes: Reputation weakened post-2020; Consumer Reports mid/low. Better for new builds than replacement. Frequent leak complaints in homeowner forums.

### ProVia
- **Aspect** (vinyl, entry): $650-950 installed. Entry ProVia vinyl.
- **Endure** (vinyl, premium): $1,100-1,800 installed. Top-rated vinyl; heavy frame, triple-pane option.
- **Aeris** (wood-clad interior / vinyl ext): $1,000-2,500 installed. Wood interior for aesthetic premium.
- Install method: Sold only through certified ProVia dealers (no big-box). Independent dealer-installers.
- Warranty: Lifetime limited, transferable one time, glass breakage included on Endure/Aeris.
- Sales notes: Highly regarded — "the premium vinyl without the Renewal price tag". Amish-country Ohio manufacturer, low complaint volume. Dealer network thinner outside Midwest/East.

### Soft-Lite
- **Barrington** (vinyl, entry): $600-900 installed.
- **Pro** (vinyl, mid): $700-1,000 installed.
- **Classic** (vinyl): $800-1,100 installed.
- **Elements** (vinyl): $850-1,455 installed. Mid-premium.
- **Imperial LS** (vinyl premium, wood interior option): $1,000-1,600+ installed. Top line; one homeowner quote $2,873/window for ultra-custom.
- Install method: Dealer-only, mostly Ohio/Great Lakes/Mid-Atlantic/Northeast. Not available in West.
- Warranty: Double lifetime transferable with glass breakage.
- Sales notes: Well-regarded regional premium vinyl brand, heavy frame with triple-pane capability. Reddit/HomeImprovement consistently recommends over Renewal at half the price (where available).

### Alside / Atrium (value tier)
- **Alside Mezzo** (vinyl): $450-750 installed. Mid-value vinyl; 4.2/5 rating. Frame won't support triple-pane (weakness).
- **Alside Sheffield / 8000** (vinyl): $350-625 installed. Builder-grade.
- **Atrium Series 450 / 8700** (Ply Gem, vinyl): $275-500 installed. Bottom-tier builder/landlord grade.
- Install method: Sold through lumberyards and independent installers; never factory-direct.
- Warranty: Limited lifetime, non-transferable on Alside; Atrium limited 10-20yr.
- Sales notes: Mezzo is genuine value sweet spot. Sheffield/Atrium should be avoided for owner-occupied primary residence — landlord/flip grade.

## Frame material price floors (independent of brand, installed/window)

- **Vinyl**: $300 low (builder-grade Alside/Atrium/Jeld-Wen V2500) — $900 typical mid — $1,600 high (Soft-Lite Imperial, ProVia Endure)
- **Fiberglass**: $700 low (Milgard Ultra) — $1,200 mid (Marvin Essential) — $2,400 high (Marvin Elevate)
- **Wood-clad (alum or vinyl clad exterior / wood interior)**: $600 low (Pella Lifestyle) — $1,500 mid — $3,500+ high (Pella Reserve, Marvin Signature, Andersen A-Series)
- **Aluminum**: $500 low — $900 mid — $1,400+ high (coastal impact/Jeld-Wen Premium Atlantic; mostly FL/coastal)
- **Composite (Fibrex, Integrity)**: $1,000 low (RbA entry) — $1,700 mid — $3,500 high (RbA premium, Andersen A-Series)

## Install method premium

- **Pocket/insert replacement** (new window slides into existing frame): **baseline** ($600-1,000/window typical)
- **Full-frame replacement** (tear out to rough opening, new frame + trim + flashing): **+40% to +100%** ($1,200-2,500/window typical). Average premium $600-1,000/window over insert. Required when existing frame is rotted, out of square, or when enlarging opening.

## Region multipliers (national average = 1.00)

- **Northeast** (NY, NJ, MA, CT, PA): **1.15-1.20** (higher labor, prevailing wage in some markets, historic home premiums)
- **West** (CA, WA, OR): **1.10-1.15** (CA labor + Title 24 energy compliance upgrades)
- **Mountain** (CO, UT, AZ, NV): **0.95-1.05** (roughly national avg)
- **Midwest** (IL, OH, MI, IN, WI): **0.95-1.05** (national avg; higher in Chicago)
- **Southeast** (FL, GA, NC, SC, TN): **0.90-1.00** nationally, but **1.15-1.30** in FL coastal counties due to impact/hurricane code
- **South Central** (TX, OK, AR, LA): **0.85-0.95** (lowest labor rates nationally)
