Concrete Driveway Cost in Connecticut (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in Connecticut typically run $4,200–$11,400 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 4000 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 42-inch design frost line, and the state sits in severe freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F2). Connecticut's pyrrhotite-contaminated aggregate crisis affecting 41 northeastern Connecticut towns is the most acute residential concrete crisis in the country — Connecticut Department of Housing has cataloged over 30,000 affected homes with crumbling foundations from pyrrhotite-bearing aggregate sourced from a single quarry between 1983-2015, and every modern Connecticut residential foundation pour now requires aggregate source certification under the Connecticut Crumbling Foundation Law plus mandatory testing on suspect-source aggregate to prevent further pyrrhotite-driven foundation failure.

Connecticut climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 42-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F2 — severe freeze-thaw + de-icer exposure, 5-7% air-entrainment required
  • Typical residential mix: 4000 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: Low — sandy or stable subsoil, standard slab-on-grade design
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC B — minor seismic risk, standard tie-down requirements
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: Very high — heavy de-icer use drives 0.40 max w/c + epoxy-coated rebar

Connecticut licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Connecticut Department of Consumer Protection — Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration (official site)
  • Permit: city or town building permit required for residential driveway pour > 200 sq ft; HIC registration required above $200 contract

How concrete driveway costs vary in Connecticut

State-specific code or insurance rule: Connecticut General Statutes §20-419 creates the lowest-threshold mandatory Home Improvement Contractor registration in the country at $200 contract value — every Connecticut residential concrete contractor exceeding that figure must hold a HIC registration, with the DCP Guaranty Fund compensating homeowners up to $25,000 per project for HIC-contractor abandonment, and Connecticut additionally enforces the Connecticut Crumbling Foundation Law (CT Gen. Stat. §16a-44a) restricting pyrrhotite-contaminated aggregate on every residential foundation pour in 41 affected northeastern Connecticut towns.
