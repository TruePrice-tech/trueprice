Garage Door Replacement Cost in Connecticut (2026)

Garage door replacements in Connecticut typically run $1,500–$5,200 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 130 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Connecticut climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 130 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating at 110-140 mph design wind
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Moderate — periodic termite pressure, PT framing recommended
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Moderate — suburban HOA prevalence on newer subdivisions
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: Moderate — bayfront salt-spray, stainless hardware recommended
  • IECC climate zone: 5A

Connecticut licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Connecticut Department of Consumer Protection — Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration (official site)
  • Permit: city or town building permit required for new garage-door opening; HIC registration required above $200 contract; replacement-only installs typically permit-exempt; coastal Atlantic counties mandate DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating on every garage-door installation

How garage door installation costs vary in Connecticut

Connecticut's combination of 130 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed across Atlantic coastal counties driving DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating on every coastal residential garage-door installation, persistent Long Island Sound salt-spray on every Fairfield, New Haven, Middlesex, and New London county exposure driving stainless-hardware replacement, F2 freeze-thaw cycling driving sealant degradation on every garage-door bottom seal at 4-6 year intervals, and 42-inch frost depth drives aggressive coastal-wind-and-cold-climate-driven material selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Connecticut General Statutes §20-419 creates the lowest-threshold mandatory Home Improvement Contractor registration in the country at $200 contract value covering every residential garage-door installation, with the DCP Guaranty Fund compensating homeowners up to $25,000 per project for HIC-contractor abandonment, and Connecticut Atlantic coastal counties (Fairfield, New Haven, Middlesex, New London) separately mandate DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating on every garage-door installation to address Long Island Sound hurricane exposure with 130 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed.
