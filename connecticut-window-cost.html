Window Replacement Cost in Connecticut (2026)

Window replacements in Connecticut typically run $7,400–$19,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Northern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.3, no SHGC limit). Connecticut's combination of 130 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed across Atlantic coastal counties driving ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated window mandate on every coastal residential install, persistent Long Island Sound salt-spray on every Fairfield, New Haven, Middlesex, and New London county exposure, very-high pre-1978 housing share (60%+ across the state, the highest in the country) driving mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every Connecticut residential window replacement, and ENERGY STAR Northern climate zone U-0.30 mandate drives unique coastal-and-historic-housing-driven glazing selection.

Connecticut climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Northern — coldest climates, U-factor priority (U ≤ 0.27 in coldest tier)
  • IECC climate zone: 5A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.3 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: No limit (cold climate)
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 130 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: Mandatory ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated in coastal counties only
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: High — multiple state-mapped historic districts with mandatory review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Very high — 60%+ pre-1978 housing share, mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every replacement

Connecticut licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Connecticut Department of Consumer Protection — Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration (official site)
  • Permit: city or town building permit required for window opening modification; HIC registration required above $200 contract; replacement-only same-size installs typically permit-exempt; Atlantic coastal counties mandate ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated windows on every coastal residential install

How window replacement costs vary in Connecticut

State-specific code or insurance rule: Connecticut General Statutes §20-419 creates the lowest-threshold mandatory Home Improvement Contractor registration in the country at $200 contract value covering every residential window installation, with the DCP Guaranty Fund compensating homeowners up to $25,000 per project for HIC-contractor abandonment, and Connecticut Atlantic coastal counties (Fairfield, New Haven, Middlesex, New London) separately mandate ASTM E1996 large-missile impact resistance on every coastal residential window installation to address Long Island Sound hurricane exposure with 130 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed, plus very-high pre-1978 housing share statewide driving mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every Connecticut residential window replacement.
