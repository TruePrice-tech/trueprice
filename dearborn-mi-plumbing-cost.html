Common plumbing questions in Dearborn

How long do plumbing fixtures typically last?

Faucets last 15-20 years, toilets 25-50 years, water heaters 8-12 years (tank) or 15-20 years (tankless), and supply lines should be replaced every 20-25 years.

What is the difference between copper and PEX piping?

Copper lasts 50+ years, resists bacteria, and has proven durability. PEX costs 60% less, installs faster, resists freezing better, and requires fewer joints. Both are code-compliant.

Is trenchless sewer repair worth the extra cost?

Trenchless repair costs 20-40% more but avoids excavating your yard, driveway, or landscaping. It is faster and causes less property damage, often making it cheaper overall.

How much does it cost to replace a water heater?

A standard 50-gallon tank water heater runs $1,200-$2,500 installed. Tankless units cost $2,500-$5,000 but last roughly twice as long and cut energy bills 20-30%.

How do I prevent frozen pipes in Dearborn?

Frozen pipes are a major expense in Dearborn and largely preventable. Wrap all exposed pipes in foam insulation, add heat tape to runs in unheated spaces, and maintain at least 55F indoor temperature through winter. Opening under-sink cabinets during extreme cold lets warm air circulate around supply lines. For properties that sit empty in winter, a professional drain-and-winterize service is essential.

What should a plumbing quote include in Dearborn?

A Dearborn plumbing estimate should detail: scope of work, material specifications, labor costs, permit fees if needed, waste disposal, and warranty. Check that the plumber's license number and insurance are included. Compare three written quotes for any project over $500; make sure the scope matches across all three.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Dearborn?

For non-emergency plumbing in Dearborn, schedule during late spring or summer, frozen ground complicates any excavation and emergency freeze calls dominate plumber schedules in winter. Emergency calls cost 50-100% more than scheduled work, so replacing aging water heaters, supply lines, and fixtures proactively during off-peak months saves significantly versus waiting for a failure.
