Frequently asked roofing questions

How long does a roof replacement take?

Most residential roofs take 1-3 days to replace. Complex roofs with multiple layers, steep pitch, or extensive damage can take 4-7 days.

What is the most affordable roofing option?

Three-tab asphalt shingles are the most affordable at $3-5 per square foot installed. Architectural shingles cost $4-7/sqft but last 10-15 years longer, making them a better value over time.

How much does a new roof cost in Dearborn?

Most homeowners in Dearborn pay between $8,000 and $16,000 for a new roof, depending on size, material, and complexity.

How many layers of shingles can I have?

Most building codes allow a maximum of two layers. If you already have two layers, the old roof must be torn off before the new one is installed, adding $1,000-$3,000 to the cost.

How do I prevent ice dams on my Dearborn roof?

Preventing ice dams in Dearborn is primarily an insulation and ventilation issue, not a roofing one. Start with R-49 or better attic insulation and seal all air leaks from the living space into the attic. Ensure continuous soffit-to-ridge ventilation keeps the roof deck uniformly cold. Ice and water shield membrane on the first 3-6 feet from eaves provides backup protection. Fix the heat loss first or new roofing alone will not solve the problem.

Are permits required to replace my roof in Dearborn?

Most Dearborn jurisdictions require a permit for a complete roof replacement. This is the contractor's responsibility to obtain before work starts. Skipping permits creates real risks: insurance coverage gaps, code violation liability, and mandatory disclosure when selling.

Best time of year to replace a roof in Dearborn?

The optimal window for roof replacement in Dearborn is late spring or early fall, materials install best above 45F and before snow season pricing surcharges. Scheduling during off-peak periods can save 10-15% on labor costs since contractors have more availability and may offer competitive pricing to fill their calendar. Avoid scheduling immediately after a major storm event when contractor demand (and prices) spike.

Does homeowners insurance cover roof replacement?

Insurance covers sudden damage (storms, falling trees) but not wear-and-tear or maintenance neglect. Verify your policy covers replacement cost, not depreciated value.

Should I repair or replace my roof?

If damage covers less than 30% of the roof and the roof is under 15 years old, repair is usually sufficient. Beyond that, replacement provides better long-term value.
