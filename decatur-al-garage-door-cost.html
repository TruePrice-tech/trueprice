Common garage door questions in Decatur

How much does garage door spring replacement cost?

Torsion spring replacement costs $200-$400 for parts and labor. Extension springs cost $150-$300. Never attempt spring replacement yourself since the stored tension is extremely dangerous.

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Decatur?

Garage door replacement in Decatur typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

How do I keep my garage cool in Decatur?

Start with an insulated door rated R-8 or higher, which drops garage temperatures 10-20 degrees versus uninsulated panels. Choose a light color to reflect solar heat. A radiant barrier on the ceiling helps further. For workspace use, a small ventilation fan or ductless mini-split provides real comfort without the expense of fully insulating the garage structure.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Decatur?

Given that Decatur homes average 37 years old, many original garage doors are due for replacement rather than repair. A new insulated door and modern opener runs $1,200-$3,000 and recoups 90-100% at resale. Repairs make financial sense for doors under 15 years old with specific damage, but throwing money at an aging system usually delays an inevitable replacement.

Best time of year to replace a garage door in Decatur?

Decatur homeowners get the best pricing and selection by scheduling garage door replacement during fall or winter. Cooler weather makes the installation more comfortable and demand is lower, and you can save 5-10% versus peak season pricing. Spring demand surges from cold-weather spring failures, so plan ahead to avoid the rush.
