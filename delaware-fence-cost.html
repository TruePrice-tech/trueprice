Fence Installation Cost in Delaware (2026)

Installing 150 linear feet of fencing in Delaware typically runs $5,200–$13,500, with 6-foot vinyl panel the dominant residential choice. Concrete-set posts must clear the 24-inch design frost line. Delaware Bay and Atlantic coastal salt-haze deposition across Sussex County corrodes standard galvanized hardware on chain-link and ornamental aluminum within 5-7 years, mandating stainless or aluminum components on shoreline retrofits — and Sussex County beach-area HOAs commonly restrict fence height and materials to preserve view corridors and aesthetic uniformity, driving vinyl dominance over wood on Rehoboth, Bethany, and Fenwick installations.

Delaware climate & post-design drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 24-inch design minimum for post footings
  • Dominant residential fence material: Vinyl panel — dominant in HOA-heavy Sun Belt and termite-risk corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — 110–140 mph design wind drives 8-foot post spacing
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Moderate — periodic termite pressure
  • HOA fencing-rule prevalence: Moderate — suburban HOA prevalence on newer subdivisions

Delaware licensing, HOA & boundary law

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Delaware Division of Revenue — Business License (fencing contractor) (official site)
  • Permit: county permit typical for residential fence over 6 feet

How fence installation costs vary in Delaware

State-specific code or insurance rule: Delaware is one of the few East Coast states with no specialty fencing contractor licensing — every fencing contractor operates under a Delaware Division of Revenue Business License only, with no examination, bond, or board oversight, and Delaware Code Title 25 §1306 (the Delaware Spite Fence Statute) makes any fence over 8 feet built primarily to annoy a neighbor subject to civil action for nuisance abatement.
