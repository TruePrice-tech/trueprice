Plumbing in Denton: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Hard water in Denton can shorten water heater life and reduce efficiency. Annual tank flushing helps, but a water softener is the best long-term solution for protecting all your plumbing fixtures.

❄

Freeze risk

Freezing is uncommon in Denton, but the risk isn't zero. During rare cold snaps, homes here are actually MORE vulnerable because plumbing isn't typically winterized. Keep pipe insulation on hand for outdoor runs.

🔧

Material recommendation

For Denton's hard water, PEX piping is ideal — it doesn't corrode or develop pinhole leaks like copper can in mineral-rich water. It's also the most cost-effective option for whole-house repiping.

💡

Local tip

In Denton, homes from the 1970s-80s era are hitting the age where original plumbing starts failing. Water heaters last 8-12 years, and supply pipes 40-60 years. If you're in that window, budget for upgrades.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Denton?

Plumber rates in Denton typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
