Vehicle inspections and warranty protections in Denver

Colorado requires a biennial emissions test in the Denver metro area (Adams, Arapahoe, Boulder, Broomfield, Denver, Douglas, and Jefferson counties) for most vehicles. No general safety inspection exists. The emissions test costs $25 at state-operated AIR stations. Vehicles that fail must be repaired and retested. The state offers a repair cost waiver after $715 in qualifying repair expenditures for vehicles that still cannot pass.

Colorado's Lemon Law covers new vehicles with defects reported within the manufacturer's express warranty period. Colorado's Consumer Protection Act covers deceptive auto-repair practices. Colorado does not license general auto mechanics at the state level but Denver requires city business licensing. Shops must provide written estimates on request.
