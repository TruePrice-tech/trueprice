Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a concrete driveway cost in Denver?

Most homeowners in Denver pay between $4,900 and $10,400 for a concrete driveway, depending on size and finish type.

Is stamped concrete worth the extra cost?

Stamped concrete costs 30-50% more than plain concrete but can mimic the look of pavers, brick, or natural stone at a fraction of the cost. It adds significant curb appeal and value.

How long does a concrete driveway last?

A properly installed and sealed concrete driveway typically lasts 25-30 years. Regular sealing every 2-3 years and prompt crack repair can extend its lifespan further.

Climate Impact on Denver Concrete

Denver sees approximately 120 freeze-thaw cycles per year, among the highest residential counts in the country because of the day-night temperature swing at altitude. CDOT and city plows apply magnesium chloride, which is more corrosive to concrete than rock salt. Intense high-altitude UV also degrades sealers 20-30 percent faster than sea-level markets.

About concrete work in Denver, CO

Why pricing varies in Denver. Labor rates here run roughly 11% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For concrete work specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Denver at roughly 10% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Denver has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained mix (5-7%) is mandatory; sealers extend service life on driveways exposed to road salt.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Denver require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. As a major metro of 649,495 residents, the local contractor market is deep — expect 10+ legitimate bidders on any standard residential job. Denver's rapid recent growth has tightened the contractor market — expect longer lead times and less negotiating room than smaller markets.
