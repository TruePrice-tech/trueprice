Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new garage door cost in Denver?

Most homeowners in Denver pay between $1,063 and $4,370 for a new garage door installed, depending on size and material.

Should I replace the opener when I get a new garage door?

If your opener is more than 10 years old, replacing it with the door is usually more cost-effective. New openers are quieter, more secure, and include modern safety features.

How long does garage door installation take?

A standard garage door installation takes 3-5 hours. Custom doors or jobs requiring structural modifications may take a full day.

About garage door replacement in Denver, CO

Why pricing varies in Denver. Labor rates here run roughly 11% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For garage door replacement specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Denver at roughly 8% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Denver has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Insulated doors (R-13 minimum, R-18+ preferred) cut heating costs and prevent freeze damage to garage plumbing.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most garage door replacement projects in Denver require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. As a major metro of 649,495 residents, the local contractor market is deep — expect 10+ legitimate bidders on any standard residential job. Denver's rapid recent growth has tightened the contractor market — expect longer lead times and less negotiating room than smaller markets.
