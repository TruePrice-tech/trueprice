Denver Plumbing Licensing and Permits

Colorado licenses plumbers at the state level through DORA (Department of Regulatory Agencies) Division of Professions and Occupations. There are Journeyman and Master tiers. The City and County of Denver requires additional registration through Denver Community Planning and Development. Colorado's licensing requirements are among the strictest in the western states. Verify at dora.colorado.gov. Note that Colorado does not have reciprocity with neighboring states, so a plumber licensed in Utah or New Mexico cannot legally work in Denver.

Freeze Risk and Winterization in Denver

Denver's winters bring sustained sub-freezing temperatures from November through March with periodic arctic plunges well below zero. Frozen pipes are a routine winter hazard. Most Denver homes are well-insulated compared to Sun Belt construction, but older homes and homes with unfinished basements still experience freeze damage regularly. Exterior Woodford Model 17 frost-free hose bibs are standard, and any pipe running through unheated space needs foam insulation. The February 2019 polar vortex and the December 2022 bomb cyclone are recent reference events.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Denver?

Plumber rates in Denver typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
