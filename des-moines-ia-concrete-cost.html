Des Moines concrete: answers to common questions

What is the best time to pour concrete?

Pour when temperatures stay between 50-80F for at least 3 days. Spring and fall are ideal. Avoid pouring in rain, direct hot sun, or below freezing. Cold and heat both compromise cure quality.

How thick should a concrete driveway be?

Residential driveways should be 4 inches thick for cars and 5-6 inches for heavy vehicles like RVs. A 6-inch compacted gravel base underneath is essential. Thickened edges (6 inches) prevent cracking at the perimeter.

Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Des Moines?

In Des Moines, permits are typically required for driveways, sidewalks, and any concrete work near property lines or public right-of-way. Backyard patios may be exempt depending on size. Check local requirements.

Des Moines's site and climate factors. Des Moines has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Des Moines for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Older properties in Des Moines (averaging 46 years) often have settling concrete that affects drainage. Re-pouring may require sub-grade correction (compaction, drainage tile) that new construction doesn't need. Budget 10-20% extra for site preparation on established properties.
