Plumbing in Des Plaines: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Des Plaines has moderately hard water which can reduce water heater lifespan over time. Consider a water softener or tankless unit with a descaling system to protect your investment.

❄

Freeze risk

Des Plaines gets cold enough for frozen pipes to be a regular concern. Insulate pipes in crawlspaces, garages, and exterior walls. When temperatures drop below 20°F, let faucets drip slightly overnight.

🔧

Material recommendation

PEX piping is the top recommendation for repiping in Des Plaines. It's flexible, resists freezing better than rigid pipes (it expands slightly), and typically costs 30-40% less than copper to install.

💡

Local tip

Many Des Plaines homes built before 1970 have galvanized steel pipes that corrode from the inside out. If your water pressure has been steadily dropping or you see rusty water, a whole-house repipe is likely needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Des Plaines?

Plumber rates in Des Plaines typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
