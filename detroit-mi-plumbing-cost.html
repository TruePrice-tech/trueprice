Plumbing in Detroit, MI: what locals should know

☁️

Climate & site factors

The water in Detroit is on the harder side. You may notice mineral spots on fixtures. For water heaters, this means flushing the tank annually is essential to prevent sediment buildup. For Detroit homes, PEX is the go-to repiping material. Its flexibility means it handles freeze-thaw cycles better than copper or CPVC. It's also faster to install, reducing labor costs.

🏠

The local housing stock averages around 52 years old, which affects job scope on most plumbing projects in Detroit. Older homes often need code upgrades that newer construction does not.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Detroit?

Plumber rates in Detroit typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
