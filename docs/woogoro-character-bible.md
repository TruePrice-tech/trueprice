# Woogoro Character Bible

**Status:** v0.2 draft — approved by Lane 2026-04-23. Not locked until art is commissioned against it.

This is the canonical source of truth for Woogoro names, personalities, backstories, outfit catalogs, and visual conventions. Art commissions, marketing copy, in-app dialogue, and blog bylines should all reference this file.

---

## Universal conventions

- **Cast size:** 20 vertical Woogoros (one per home-services vertical) + **Iris** the shire keeper / narrator (21 total characters).
- **Base art (already commissioned):** each of the 20 vertical Woogoros has a "naked / cozy" base render with their vertical-specific fur color. Iris has a canonical base.
- **Outfits per vertical Woogoro:** 5 baked renders.
  - 1× **Work outfit** (vertical prop + trade gear — replaces the old vertical-Trudy art on cost-guide pages)
  - 4× **character-specific outfits** (each reveals a different facet of personality)
- **Iris's art set:** 1 canonical pose (hat + Fair Scepter) + 5 emotion variants (Happy, Sad, Excited, Concerned, Pensive — replacing the old Trudy emotion set).
- **Total render budget at launch:** 20 × 5 = 100 outfit renders + 6 Iris renders = **106 new renders**.

**Aesthetic lock (Rainbow reservation):**
- **Only Iris** wears rainbow. She is the shire keeper, her rainbow fur reflects all 20 trades because she taught each of them everything they know. No vertical Woogoro has a "Rainbow outfit" in their catalog — that would dilute Iris's unique identity.
- Future annual event ("Shire Day") is the only exception: a one-off seasonal render where all Woogoros wear rainbow to honor Iris. Not part of the launch catalog.

**Antagonists (Overcharge Goblins):** deferred. Character archetypes exist in working notes but are not being commissioned for launch. Product stays cozy/positive (Finch-pattern) for v1.

## The Narrator — Iris (Rainbow Woogoro)

- **Role:** Shire keeper. Narrator of the product. Appears on homepage, onboarding, result pages, emotion states, blog bylines. **Replaces Trudy end-to-end.**
- **Trait:** Warm, wise, quietly fierce. A matriarch who has seen every quote ever passed through the shire and remembers the fair ones by heart. Not stern-elder — more *"badass grandmother who bakes bread and also once chased a goblin out with a broom."*
- **Catchphrase:** *"No price is too small to examine."* (Alternates: *"I've seen this one before."* / *"Let's take a closer look."*)
- **Backstory:** The oldest Woogoro in the shire, and the only one who isn't tied to a single vertical — her rainbow fur reflects all 20 trades, because she taught each of them everything they know. Wields the **Fair Scepter** (her pricing wand): gentle chime when a quote is honest, warning flash when something's off.
- **Visual canon (always present):**
  - Floppy rainbow bucket hat with pompoms (non-negotiable signature silhouette)
  - Fair Scepter — wand with a glowing receipt/price-tag charm at the tip (ties magic to pricing utility; ages better than a generic wand)
- **Art set needed:**
  1. **Canonical pose** — hat + scepter, gentle smile, paws folded. Used for default/footer/logo contexts.
  2. **Happy** — celebrating a fair price
  3. **Sad** — noticed a user got overcharged
  4. **Excited** — welcoming a new shire member (onboarding moment)
  5. **Concerned** — scepter flashing warning on a red-flag quote
  6. **Pensive** — reviewing paperwork, glasses on, thinking

## Writing-style guide for in-app Woogoro dialogue

- **Voice:** Warm, direct, plain-spoken. Adults helping adults.
- **Never condescending.** Woogoros are experts in their trade, not tutors explaining basics to children.
- **Trade-accurate without being jargony.** Pip can mention "O-rings" once in a while because he's obsessed; he shouldn't give a technical lecture.
- **Personality shows through word choice.** Pip hedges ("I think maybe…"). Rex is blunt ("Yeah, that's a bad gasket"). Scout is calm ("Deep breath — let me look"). Gus grumbles ("Third one this week, pal").
- **No stereotypes or accent-coding** (no cowboy "pardner" speak for Rex, no "mmhmm" for Maple, etc.).
- **Iris never gives vertical-specific advice.** She guides users *to* the right Woogoro ("Pip wants to take a look at that quote for you") and narrates *around* their specialty.
