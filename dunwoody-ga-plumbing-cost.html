Plumbing in Dunwoody: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

The soft water in Dunwoody is a plus for plumbing longevity. Water heaters last longer and pipes stay cleaner. Focus your plumbing budget on other priorities like pipe material and drainage.

❄

Freeze risk

Dunwoody rarely freezes, but when it does, exposed pipes are especially vulnerable because homes here aren't built for cold weather. Insulate outdoor pipes and know your shutoff valve location just in case.

🔧

Material recommendation

For repiping in Dunwoody, PEX is the value pick and copper is the premium choice. Both work well in your climate. PEX installs faster (lower labor cost), while copper has a longer track record.

💡

Local tip

Homes built in Dunwoody during the 1970s-80s often have copper pipes that may be developing pinhole leaks after 40+ years. If you're seeing green staining at joints, it's time for an inspection.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Dunwoody?

Plumber rates in Dunwoody typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
