Plumbing in Durham, NC: what locals should know

Climate & site factors

Durham's soft water means less mineral buildup in your plumbing system. You generally don't need a water softener, though a basic sediment filter can improve water quality and protect fixtures. For repiping in Durham, PEX is the value pick and copper is the premium choice. Both work well in your climate. PEX installs faster (lower labor cost), while copper has a longer track record.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Durham?

Plumber rates in Durham typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
