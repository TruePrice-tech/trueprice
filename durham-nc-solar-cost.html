What affects solar cost in Durham

  • System size (kW) based on energy usage
  • Panel type (standard vs. premium efficiency)
  • Roof condition, angle, and shading
  • Inverter type (string vs. microinverters)
  • Battery storage (Tesla Powerwall, Enphase, etc.)
  • Local labor rates in Durham, NC
  • Net metering and local incentive availability

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do solar panels cost in Durham?

Most homeowners in Durham pay between $15,130 and $73,260 before the 30% federal tax credit. After the credit, costs drop significantly.

Is the 30% federal solar tax credit still available?

Yes. The federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) provides a 30% tax credit on the total cost of your solar installation through 2032. It drops to 26% in 2033 and 22% in 2034.

How long does solar installation take?

Physical installation typically takes 1-3 days. However, the full process from contract signing to power-on can take 2-3 months due to permitting and utility interconnection.
