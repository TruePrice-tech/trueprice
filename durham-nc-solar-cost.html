Questions Durham homeowners ask about solar

Do solar panels increase home value?

Owned solar systems add roughly $15,000-$20,000 to home value according to national studies. Leased systems do not add value and can complicate the sale process.

How long does solar take to pay for itself in Durham?

Payback in Durham depends on electricity rates, sun hours, and incentives. Most systems pay back in 6-10 years and then generate free electricity for another 15-20 years.

What is the difference between string inverters and microinverters?

String inverters are cheaper but the whole array drops output when one panel is shaded. Microinverters optimize each panel independently, cost 15-20% more, and last longer.

Should I buy or lease solar panels?

Buying maximizes long-term savings and increases home value. Leasing eliminates upfront cost but the leasing company keeps the tax credit, and panels may complicate home sales.

Does Durham have any state or local solar incentives?

Incentives vary. Check the DSIRE database for Durham-specific rebates, SRECs, property tax exemptions, and performance-based incentives that stack on top of the federal 30% ITC.

Will solar panels survive a hurricane in Durham?

With the right installation, solar panels withstand 130+ mph winds and frequently survive hurricanes better than surrounding roofing materials. The keys are wind-rated racking certified for Durham's exposure zone, properly sealed roof penetrations, and regular maintenance to keep hardware tight. After any major storm, have your solar installer inspect racking attachments and electrical connections.

What solar incentives are available in Durham?

Start with the 30% federal ITC, which applies to all Durham solar installations with no cap. Then check DSIRE for NC-level incentives: state tax credits, utility rebates, SREC markets, property tax exemptions, and net metering terms. Each utility has different programs and rates. The combined federal-plus-state incentive stack typically reduces net cost by 40-60% in markets with strong state programs.

When is the best time to install solar in Durham?

The best installation window in Durham is early spring or fall, moderate weather conditions for installation and you avoid the peak-demand scheduling crunch. Note that the process from quote to installation takes 6-12 weeks including permitting, utility approval, and scheduling. Start the process 2-3 months before your target installation date. The federal tax credit applies to the year of installation, so timing relative to tax year can matter.
