Frequently asked roofing questions

What time of year is cheapest for roofing in Eagan?

Late fall and winter are typically slower seasons in Eagan, so contractors may offer better pricing. Spring and summer are peak demand, meaning longer waits and higher labor costs.

Why do roofing quotes vary so much?

Quotes differ based on material quality, labor assumptions, flashing work, ventilation, and warranty coverage. The cheapest bid frequently skips items that become change orders mid-project.

How do I know if my roof deck needs replacing?

Signs include sagging between rafters, soft or spongy spots when walked on, visible water stains from the attic, and rotted or delaminating plywood. Your roofer checks this during tear-off.

How long does a roof last in Eagan?

For Eagan homeowners, here are realistic roof lifespans: 3-tab asphalt shingles last 15-20 years, architectural shingles 20-25, standing seam metal 40-60, and clay or concrete tile 50+. UV degradation is the primary wear factor in this climate. Proper ventilation keeps the roof deck from overheating, and lighter material colors reflect more UV, both extending service life.

Eagan permits: required to replace my roof?

In most jurisdictions including Eagan, a building permit is required for a full roof replacement. Your contractor should pull the permit before work begins. Unpermitted roofing work can void your homeowners insurance coverage and create disclosure issues when selling the home.

When is the best time to replace a roof in Eagan?

For Eagan, aim for late summer or early fall to schedule your roof replacement. Stable weather allows proper curing and contractors are less backlogged than spring, which means better contractor availability and potential savings of 10-15% on labor. Booking right after a major storm is the worst time since every roofer in the area will be booked and prices reflect the surge demand.
