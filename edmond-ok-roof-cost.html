What Edmond residents ask about roofing

What roofing material is best for Edmond?

Material choice depends on Edmond's climate, your budget, and desired lifespan. Architectural shingles offer the best balance of cost and durability for most homeowners.

What is a roofing square?

A roofing square equals 100 square feet. A typical 2,000 sqft roof is about 20 squares. Contractors price materials and labor per square.

Should I repair or replace my roof?

If damage covers less than 30% of the roof and the roof is under 15 years old, repair is usually sufficient. Beyond that, replacement provides better long-term value.

Do I need impact-resistant shingles in Edmond?

Given Edmond's position in an active hail corridor, Class 4 impact-resistant shingles are a smart investment. Standard shingles here often fail early from repeated hail strikes. IR-rated materials handle the punishment significantly better, and most OK insurers offer 15-25% premium discounts that offset the 10-20% higher material cost within a few years.

Are permits required to replace my roof in Edmond?

Most Edmond jurisdictions require a permit for a complete roof replacement. This is the contractor's responsibility to obtain before work starts. Skipping permits creates real risks: insurance coverage gaps, code violation liability, and mandatory disclosure when selling.

Best time of year to replace a roof in Edmond?

The optimal window for roof replacement in Edmond is early fall, after summer storms subside and before winter weather complicates scheduling. Scheduling during off-peak periods can save 10-15% on labor costs since contractors have more availability and may offer competitive pricing to fill their calendar. Avoid scheduling immediately after a major storm event when contractor demand (and prices) spike.
