What is the most expensive part of a kitchen remodel?

Cabinets are typically the largest single expense at 30-40% of the total budget. Countertops are second at 10-15%, followed by labor, appliances, and flooring.

Should I hire a general contractor or manage subs myself?

A GC adds 15-25% overhead but manages scheduling, permits, and quality across trades. Self-managing saves money but requires significant time and construction knowledge to coordinate plumbing, electrical, and carpentry.

How long does a kitchen remodel take?

A cosmetic refresh takes 2-4 weeks. A mid-range remodel runs 6-10 weeks. A full gut renovation with layout changes takes 12-16 weeks. Permit delays and backordered materials can add 2-4 weeks.

El Paso: kitchen stock and typical layouts

El Paso's kitchen remodel market reflects the Chihuahuan Desert border city's distinct housing stock: 1920s-40s adobe homes in Sunset Heights and Kern Place have original Territorial-style kitchens with thick walls, deep window reveals, and Saltillo tile floors that renovation architects preserve as character elements while modernizing cabinetry and countertops. 1960s-80s tract homes across the Northeast and Eastside have standard builder-grade kitchens ripe for mid-range renovation at $25,000-$50,000. The Far Westside's rapid growth adds newer homes where cosmetic upgrades ($10,000-$25,000) address builder-grade finishes. El Paso's border economy keeps labor rates 20-25% below DFW, making kitchen remodels more affordable here than in most major Texas metros. Fort Bliss military housing operates under DOD renovation standards separate from civilian market conditions.

El Paso's layout challenges are uniquely split: adobe homes in Sunset Heights and Kern Place have 12-18 inch thick walls that are effectively immovable (demolishing adobe walls produces enormous debris and dust, costs $3,000-$8,000 per wall, and eliminates the thermal mass that is the adobe's primary functional advantage). Kitchen expansion in adobe homes typically involves annexing adjacent rooms rather than removing walls. Tract homes from the 1960s-80s have standard wood-frame or block construction with removable interior walls, making open-plan conversions straightforward at $2,000-$5,000 for header installation. The Fort Bliss military-housing stock has fixed floor plans that DOD renovation contracts cannot alter.

El Paso-area plumbing, water, and rough-in

El Paso Water Utilities' very hard water (250-350 ppm) from the Rio Grande and Hueco Bolson aquifer is the most aggressive municipal supply among these metros for kitchen fixture and appliance impact. Dishwasher spray arms clog within 6-12 months, faucet cartridges calcify in 2-3 years, and garbage disposal components scale rapidly. Point-of-use RO under the kitchen sink ($250-$400 installed) is effectively mandatory for drinking water quality and protects the refrigerator ice maker and water dispenser from the mineral content. Whole-house water softener ($1,000-$3,000) is the companion investment that extends all kitchen plumbing component life to normal durations.

Kitchen remodels in El Paso nearly always trigger water heater assessment because the very hard water (250-350 ppm) degrades water heaters faster than any other metro in this group. At $0.90/therm gas from Texas Gas Service, gas tankless water heaters (Rinnai, Navien) at $2,500-$3,500 installed provide continuous hot water and resist mineral damage better than tank heaters (the flow-through design flushes deposits rather than accumulating them). Biannual descaling ($200-$300) is mandatory at this hardness level. Tank heaters last only 6-8 years versus 10-12 nationally without annual flushing.
