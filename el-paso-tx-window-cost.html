What El Paso residents ask about window replacement

What type of replacement window is best?

Vinyl frames offer the best value with low maintenance and good insulation. Fiberglass is more durable and paintable. Wood has the best aesthetics but needs ongoing maintenance. Aluminum is cheapest but conducts heat.

How much energy do new windows actually save?

Replacing single-pane windows saves 25-30% on heating and cooling costs. Upgrading from older double-pane to modern Low-E double-pane saves 10-15%. Payback takes 10-15 years through energy savings alone.

How much does window replacement cost in El Paso?

Window replacement in El Paso costs $400-$1,200 per window installed, depending on size, frame material, glass type, and accessibility. A whole-house project (10-15 windows) runs $5,000-$15,000.

What is the difference between full-frame and insert replacement?

Insert replacement fits a new window into the existing frame, is faster, and costs less. Full-frame replacement removes everything down to the studs, costs 30-50% more, but fixes hidden damage.

Energy performance spec within El Paso

El Paso window energy performance is dominated by solar heat gain: the Chihuahuan Desert's 300+ clear days at 3,800-foot elevation deliver the most intense ground-level solar radiation of any major metro in this group. South and west-facing windows are the primary cooling-load pathway, making ultra-low SHGC glass (0.20-0.25) the most impactful specification on those exposures. The 90-degree diurnal temperature swing (100F afternoon to 40F predawn) creates a secondary thermal-performance demand: U-factor under 0.30 prevents the overnight heat loss that forces heating systems to work in the same climate that requires aggressive cooling during the day. This dual-performance requirement is unique to high-desert markets.

At El Paso Electric's $0.12/kWh rate (among the lowest in the Southwest), window energy savings produce slower payback than high-rate markets: a full-house window upgrade saves $200-$400 annually on a typical home, producing 12-16 year payback on energy alone. UV-filtering glass ($40-$60 per window premium) prevents the furniture, floor, and fabric fading that the intense desert UV causes -- a preservation value that the energy-payback calculation alone does not capture.
