Frequently asked solar questions

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

Do solar panels increase home value?

Owned solar systems add roughly $15,000-$20,000 to home value according to national studies. Leased systems do not add value and can complicate the sale process.

How long does solar take to pay for itself in Elgin?

Payback in Elgin depends on electricity rates, sun hours, and incentives. Most systems pay back in 6-10 years and then generate free electricity for another 15-20 years.

What is the difference between string inverters and microinverters?

String inverters are cheaper but the whole array drops output when one panel is shaded. Microinverters optimize each panel independently, cost 15-20% more, and last longer.

Does solar work in Elgin's cold winters?

Cold weather actually improves solar panel efficiency since photovoltaic cells perform better at lower temperatures. Elgin's shorter winter days reduce seasonal output, but net metering lets you bank summer surplus for winter use. Snow typically clears from tilted panels within 1-2 days. Between higher electricity rates and incentive programs, the financial return in Elgin often matches sunbelt installations.

What solar incentives are available in Elgin?

Start with the 30% federal ITC, which applies to all Elgin solar installations with no cap. Then check DSIRE for IL-level incentives: state tax credits, utility rebates, SREC markets, property tax exemptions, and net metering terms. Each utility has different programs and rates. The combined federal-plus-state incentive stack typically reduces net cost by 40-60% in markets with strong state programs.

Best time of year to install solar in Elgin?

The best installation window in Elgin is late spring or summer, weather cooperates for roof work and you maximize first-year production before winter. Note that the process from quote to installation takes 6-12 weeks including permitting, utility approval, and scheduling. Start the process 2-3 months before your target installation date. The federal tax credit applies to the year of installation, so timing relative to tax year can matter.
