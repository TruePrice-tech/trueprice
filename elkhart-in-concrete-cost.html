Questions Elkhart homeowners ask about concrete

Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Elkhart?

In Elkhart, permits are typically required for driveways, sidewalks, and any concrete work near property lines or public right-of-way. Backyard patios may be exempt depending on size. Check local requirements.

What is stamped concrete?

Stamped concrete uses patterned mats pressed into wet concrete to mimic brick, stone, slate, or wood. It costs $12-$25/sqft installed. It requires resealing every 2-3 years to maintain appearance and prevent wear.

What Elkhart's local context means for pricing. Elkhart has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Elkhart for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters.
