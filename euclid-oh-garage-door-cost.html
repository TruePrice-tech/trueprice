Frequently asked garage door questions

How much does garage door spring replacement cost?

Torsion spring replacement costs $200-$400 for parts and labor. Extension springs cost $150-$300. Never attempt spring replacement yourself since the stored tension is extremely dangerous.

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Euclid?

Garage door replacement in Euclid typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

What R-value garage door do I need in Euclid?

Euclid's winters demand serious insulation. An R-12 to R-16 door is the baseline for attached garages, with R-16 to R-18 recommended if you use the space as a workshop or heat it. The $200-$500 upgrade cost pays back through lower heating bills in rooms adjacent to the garage within 3-5 years.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Euclid?

Given that Euclid homes average 44 years old, many original garage doors are due for replacement rather than repair. A new insulated door and modern opener runs $1,200-$3,000 and recoups 90-100% at resale. Repairs make financial sense for doors under 15 years old with specific damage, but throwing money at an aging system usually delays an inevitable replacement.

When is the best time to replace a garage door in Euclid?

Euclid homeowners get the best pricing and selection by scheduling garage door replacement during late spring or early fall. Moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing, and you can save 5-10% versus peak season pricing. Spring demand surges from cold-weather spring failures, so plan ahead to avoid the rush.
