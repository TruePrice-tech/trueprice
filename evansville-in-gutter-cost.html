Frequently asked gutter questions

How do I know if my gutters are failing?

Signs include: water spilling over during rain, visible sagging or pulling away from the fascia, rust spots or holes, peeling paint on fascia or soffits, and erosion or pooling at the foundation.

How long do gutters last?

Aluminum gutters last 20-30 years. Steel lasts 15-20 years. Copper lasts 50+ years. Vinyl lasts 10-15 years. Seamless gutters outlast sectional because they have fewer leak-prone joints.

Should I repair or replace my gutters?

Repair if damage is limited to a few sections, joints, or downspouts. Replace if gutters sag along most of their length, have multiple leaks, are rusting through, or the fascia behind them is rotting.

Evansville, IN gutter installation market overview

Why pricing varies in Evansville. Labor rates here run roughly 10% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Evansville has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Ice dams are the primary gutter concern in Evansville. Heated gutter cables ($400-800 installed per 50 feet) prevent the ice backup that tears gutters off eaves and drives water behind siding. Ice-and-water shield membrane on the eave edge (6+ feet up from the gutter line) provides the secondary defense. Seamless aluminum gutters are the Evansville standard: $6-12 per linear foot installed. Copper ($20-40/ft) and zinc ($15-25/ft) offer 50+ year lifespans but at 3-4x the cost. For most homeowners, aluminum with a quality finish provides the best value at 20-30 years before replacement.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most gutter installation projects in Evansville require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 120,310, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Evansville has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.
