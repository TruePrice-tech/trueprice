What Evansville residents ask about roofing

What roofing material is best for Evansville?

Material choice depends on Evansville's climate, your budget, and desired lifespan. Architectural shingles offer the best balance of cost and durability for most homeowners.

What is a roofing square?

A roofing square equals 100 square feet. A typical 2,000 sqft roof is about 20 squares. Contractors price materials and labor per square.

Should I repair or replace my roof?

If damage covers less than 30% of the roof and the roof is under 15 years old, repair is usually sufficient. Beyond that, replacement provides better long-term value.

How do I prevent ice dams on my Evansville roof?

Preventing ice dams in Evansville is primarily an insulation and ventilation issue, not a roofing one. Start with R-49 or better attic insulation and seal all air leaks from the living space into the attic. Ensure continuous soffit-to-ridge ventilation keeps the roof deck uniformly cold. Ice and water shield membrane on the first 3-6 feet from eaves provides backup protection. Fix the heat loss first or new roofing alone will not solve the problem.

Do I need a permit to replace my roof in Evansville?

Most Evansville jurisdictions require a permit for a complete roof replacement. This is the contractor's responsibility to obtain before work starts. Skipping permits creates real risks: insurance coverage gaps, code violation liability, and mandatory disclosure when selling.

What's the best season to replace a roof in Evansville?

The optimal window for roof replacement in Evansville is late spring or early fall, materials install best above 45F and before snow season pricing surcharges. Scheduling during off-peak periods can save 10-15% on labor costs since contractors have more availability and may offer competitive pricing to fill their calendar. Avoid scheduling immediately after a major storm event when contractor demand (and prices) spike.

Does homeowners insurance cover roof replacement?

Insurance covers sudden damage (storms, falling trees) but not wear-and-tear or maintenance neglect. Verify your policy covers replacement cost, not depreciated value.
