Frequently asked concrete questions

What is the best time to pour concrete?

Pour when temperatures stay between 50-80F for at least 3 days. Spring and fall are ideal. Avoid pouring in rain, direct hot sun, or below freezing. Cold and heat both compromise cure quality.

How thick should a concrete driveway be?

Residential driveways should be 4 inches thick for cars and 5-6 inches for heavy vehicles like RVs. A 6-inch compacted gravel base underneath is essential. Thickened edges (6 inches) prevent cracking at the perimeter.

Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Florence?

In Florence, permits are typically required for driveways, sidewalks, and any concrete work near property lines or public right-of-way. Backyard patios may be exempt depending on size. Check local requirements.

About concrete work in Florence, AL

Where Florence's pricing comes from. Florence's labor market runs about 11% below the national median for skilled trades. This wage differential is the primary driver of the area's below-average project costs. On concrete work, Florence comes in about 14% under the national average based on our metro pricing data.

Local conditions in Florence. Florence has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Concrete in Florence cures differently than in dry climates — high humidity slows evaporation, which actually improves curing strength but extends the timeline. The bigger concern is expansive clay soils common in this region, which cause heave and cracking. Proper sub-base preparation (4-6 inches of compacted gravel) is non-negotiable. Standard concrete in Florence runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Florence permits and code requirements. Permits are usually required for concrete work in Florence; verify the specifics with the local building department before committing.

The Florence contractor pool. At 40,059 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. The Florence area's flat growth keeps contractor availability high year-round. Seasonal discounts of 10-15% are realistic for flexible project timelines.
