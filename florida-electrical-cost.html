Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in Florida (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in Florida typically run $2,200–$5,400 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2020 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Service entrance design assumes a 200A minimum on new residential construction. Florida ranks first in the United States for cloud-to-ground lightning flash density at 12-15 strikes per square mile annually, driving Type 1 service-entrance SPD installation as a near-universal panel-upgrade line item — and the HVHZ wind-borne debris zone in Miami-Dade and Broward forces meter bases and exterior service equipment to pass FBC Product Approval impact testing, ruling out the standard pancake-style meter sockets common across the Southeast.

Florida code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2020 NEC — dwelling unit AFCI on most 120V branch circuits, GFCI expanded
  • State amendments: Florida Building Code 2023 (FBC 8th edition) adopts 2020 NEC with HVHZ wind-borne debris protection on Miami-Dade and Broward service equipment, Product Approval mandatory on every panel and meter base
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: Very high — central Florida and Gulf Coast lightning corridor (10+ flashes/sq mi/yr)
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 12-to-18-inch design depth on buried service conduit

Florida licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Florida Department of Business & Professional Regulation — Electrical Contractors Licensing Board (EC license) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county electrical permit required; FBC Product Approval verification on all service equipment in HVHZ counties

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in Florida

State-specific code or insurance rule: Florida is the only state where every electrical service equipment component — meter base, main breaker panel, exterior disconnect — must carry a Florida Product Approval (FL #) under FBC §1714 on Miami-Dade and Broward HVHZ projects, and the FBC requires service equipment installed within 1 mile of the coastline to use stainless-steel mounting hardware and NEMA 4X enclosures regardless of inland code defaults.
