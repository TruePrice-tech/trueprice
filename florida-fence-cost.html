Fence Installation Cost in Florida (2026)

Installing 150 linear feet of fencing in Florida typically runs $5,400–$14,200, with 6-foot vinyl panel the dominant residential choice. Concrete-set posts must clear the 0-inch design frost line. Florida HVHZ wind-borne debris exposure in Miami-Dade and Broward forces 6-foot post-spacing maximum and concrete-sleeve installation on every residential fence under FBC §1714 — plus Termite Probability and Climatic Tier 1 (the highest TPCT zone) drives near-universal vinyl, aluminum, or composite material selection over wood, and coastal salt-haze deposition mandates stainless mounting hardware on every shoreline installation within 1 mile of tidewater.

Florida climate & post-design drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 0-inch design minimum for post footings
  • Dominant residential fence material: Vinyl panel — dominant in HOA-heavy Sun Belt and termite-risk corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: HVHZ — Miami-Dade engineered post-spacing and concrete-set required
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Very high — TPCT zone 1, all in-ground wood requires PT southern yellow pine .60 CCA or alternative
  • HOA fencing-rule prevalence: High — large planned-community HOA penetration restricts material/color/height

Florida licensing, HOA & boundary law

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Florida Department of Business & Professional Regulation — Construction Industry Licensing Board (Specialty Structure or Building Contractor) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for fence installation; HVHZ Product Approval verification mandatory in Miami-Dade and Broward; HOA approval required in most master-planned communities

How fence installation costs vary in Florida

State-specific code or insurance rule: Florida Statute §163.3164(7) and the Statewide HOA Disclosure Act make Florida the most HOA-regulated state in the country — with over 50% of Florida residential subdivisions enforcing architectural review controls including specific approved fencing materials (typically only vinyl or aluminum picket), maximum heights, and color palettes, and the Marketable Record Title Act limiting HOA enforcement to 30 years unless re-recorded — making HOA approval a pre-construction step on every Florida residential fence install in master-planned communities.
