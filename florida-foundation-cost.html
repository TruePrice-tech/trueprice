Foundation Cost in Florida (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in Florida typically run $9,400–$28,800 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with slab-on-grade as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 0-inch design frost line. Florida's combination of Atlantic and Gulf hurricane wind uplift requirements driving HVHZ Miami-Dade and Broward NOA-rated foundation tie-down schedules at 175 mph design wind, persistent year-round humidity driving rapid concrete set times requiring retarder admixtures on every pour, and Florida's high water table across the entire peninsula requiring waterproof admixtures plus capillary-break crushed-stone on every below-grade pour, plus Central Florida sinkhole-prone karst limestone requiring sinkhole-disclosure-and-investigation under FL Statute §501.140 on every residential transfer drives the most specialized residential foundation pour requirements in the Southeast.

Florida climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 0-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Slab-on-grade — dominant in Sun Belt and Southeast on warm climates with shallow frost line
  • Expansive soil risk: Low — sandy or stable subsoil, standard slab-on-grade design
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC A — no seismic design considerations
  • Hurricane wind tier: HVHZ — Miami-Dade NOA-rated foundation tie-down at 175 mph design wind
  • Water table risk: Very high — water within 5 ft of grade, sump-pump + waterproofing membrane required on every basement
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • IECC climate zone: 1A-2A
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 2 (moderate) — sub-slab depressurization rough-in recommended

Florida licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Florida Department of Business & Professional Regulation — Construction Industry Licensing Board (Certified Residential Contractor or Specialty Foundation Contractor) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential foundation pour; FL DBPR specialty license required statewide; HVHZ permit + Florida Product Approval (FL #) NOA verification mandatory in Miami-Dade and Broward; sinkhole disclosure required under FL Statute §501.140

How foundation pour costs vary in Florida

State-specific code or insurance rule: Florida Building Code §1804 + §1810 imposes the most restrictive coastal hurricane-zone foundation requirements in the country — every HVHZ residential foundation pour in Miami-Dade and Broward counties requires Florida Product Approval (FL #) compliance with Notice of Acceptance (NOA) testing for hurricane wind uplift up to 175 mph, plus continuous epoxy-coated rebar tie schedules and minimum 4500 psi compressive strength on hurricane-zone foundations, and Florida Statute §501.140 (Sinkhole Disclosure) separately requires every Florida property seller to disclose any sinkhole activity on every residential property transfer with optional homeowner's-insurance Sinkhole Loss Coverage endorsement.
