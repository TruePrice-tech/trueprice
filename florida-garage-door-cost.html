Garage Door Replacement Cost in Florida (2026)

Garage door replacements in Florida typically run $1,400–$6,400 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 175 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Florida climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 175 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: HVHZ — Miami-Dade NOA-rated and DASMA 115-tested at 175 mph design wind plus large-missile impact resistance
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Very high — TPCT zone 1, all wood jamb framing requires PT southern yellow pine .60 CCA
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Very high — 80%+ of subdivisions enforce HOA architectural-committee pre-approval on door color/material/style
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High — coastal salt-spray drives stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track requirement at 5-7 year intervals
  • IECC climate zone: 1A-2A

Florida licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Florida DBPR Construction Industry Licensing Board — Specialty Garage Door Contractor (CV-C-1518) or Certified Building Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for every garage-door installation in Miami-Dade and Broward counties (HVHZ); Florida Product Approval (FL #) NOA verification mandatory on every HVHZ install; FL DBPR Specialty Garage Door Contractor license required statewide

How garage door installation costs vary in Florida

Florida's HVHZ Miami-Dade and Broward 175 mph design wind speed plus large-missile impact resistance requirement under FBC §1620 + ANSI/DASMA 115 drives the most expensive residential garage-door installation requirements in the country at $4,000-$8,000 for a Miami-Dade NOA-rated D-rated impact-resistant residential garage door, plus persistent Atlantic and Gulf salt-spray on every Florida coastal exposure driving stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track replacement at 5-8 year intervals, very-high TPCT termite zone 1 pressure forcing pressure-treated wood jamb framing, and very-high HOA architectural-control prevalence across 80%+ of Florida subdivisions drives unique HVHZ-and-coastal-driven material selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Florida Building Code §1620 + ANSI/DASMA 115 imposes the most restrictive coastal hurricane-zone garage-door requirements in the country — every HVHZ residential garage-door installation in Miami-Dade and Broward counties requires Florida Product Approval (FL #) compliance with Notice of Acceptance (NOA) testing for hurricane wind uplift up to 175 mph plus large-missile impact resistance per ASTM E1996 (D-rated), and FL DBPR separately licenses a Specialty Garage Door Contractor (license code CV-C-1518) with mandatory examination on Florida Building Code wind-pressure calculations, FBC 1626 large-missile impact testing, and HVHZ NOA submittal documentation — the only dedicated state-level garage-door specialty license in the U.S.
