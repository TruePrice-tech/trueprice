Landscape Installation Cost in Florida (2026)

Landscape installation in Florida typically runs $4,400–$18,800 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 8b-11a. Florida's combination of 365-day growing season, sandy fast-draining soils across the entire peninsula, and persistent summer humidity drives the highest residential irrigation dependency in the Southeast — and the state's St. Augustinegrass turf monoculture across single-family residential subdivisions from Jacksonville to Naples requires fungicide-rotation programs to manage take-all root rot and gray leaf spot, driving Florida residential turf-care service contracts toward 8-12 monthly visits per year versus 4-6 in moderate-climate states.

Florida climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 8b-11a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 54 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 365 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Moderate — periodic drought, advisory water-saving guidelines
  • Irrigation dependency: Very high — 60%+ of residential outdoor water for irrigation
  • Frost-line trench depth: 0-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

Florida licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Florida Department of Agriculture & Consumer Services — Limited Lawn & Ornamental Pest Control License + DBPR Construction (no separate landscape license) (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install on existing yards; FDACS Limited Lawn & Ornamental Pest Control License required for any chemical lawn care; HOA approval typical in deed-restricted communities

How landscape installation costs vary in Florida

Florida's combination of 365-day growing season, sandy fast-draining soils across the entire peninsula, and persistent summer humidity drives the highest residential irrigation dependency in the Southeast — and the state's St. Augustinegrass turf monoculture across single-family residential subdivisions from Jacksonville to Naples requires fungicide-rotation programs to manage take-all root rot and gray leaf spot, driving Florida residential turf-care service contracts toward 8-12 monthly visits per year versus 4-6 in moderate-climate states.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Florida Statute §373.185 (Florida-Friendly Landscaping Act) protects the right of every Florida homeowner to install Florida-Friendly Landscaping (xeriscape, native plants, low-water-use vegetation) regardless of HOA covenants, restrictions, or deed-recorded landscape requirements — overriding HOA mandates in deed-restricted communities and forcing every Florida HOA architectural review committee to approve Florida-Friendly Landscaping applications meeting the FFL principles published by UF/IFAS Extension.
