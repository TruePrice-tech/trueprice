Exterior Painting Cost in Florida (2026)

Repainting a 1,500 sq ft home in Florida typically runs $2,800–$8,400, with three-coat hard-coat stucco the dominant residential substrate driving prep scope. Florida's combination of 75%+ year-round humidity, 290+ sun-days, and Gulf and Atlantic salt-spray drives the most aggressive exterior coating-failure environment in the continental United States — Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach coastal stucco repaints typically run on a 5-7 year cycle versus 10-12 years in moderate climates, with elastomeric mid-coat over alkali-resistant primer now the dominant Florida residential coatings spec to bridge the inevitable hairline stucco cracks driven by hurricane-load building flex and persistent thermal expansion.

Florida climate & coatings drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 1A-2A
  • Dominant residential substrate: Three-coat hard-coat stucco — dominant in arid and Sun Belt corridors, drives elastomeric topcoats
  • Annual humidity tier: Very high — 75%+ year-round drives mildew bloom + biocide-additive coatings
  • UV exposure: Very high — 280+ sun-days drive Sun Belt-rated UV-stabilized binders + high-PVC TiO2 topcoats
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High-corrosion coastal — marine-grade alkyd primer + stainless fastener replacement on every coastal repaint
  • Freeze-thaw cycling: No freeze exposure
  • VOC architectural coatings rule: Federal default — 250 g/L flat exterior topcoat limit (EPA 40 CFR Part 59)

Florida licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Florida Department of Business & Professional Regulation — Construction Industry Licensing Board (Certified or Registered Painting Contractor) (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for repaint; FL DBPR painting contractor license required statewide; HOA approval typical in deed-restricted communities

How exterior repaint costs vary in Florida

State-specific code or insurance rule: Florida Statute §489.105 makes Florida one of only a handful of states that licenses Painting Contractors as a separately scoped category under the Construction Industry Licensing Board (CILB) — Certified Painting Contractors carry statewide jurisdiction while Registered Painting Contractors are limited to the local-jurisdiction examination county only, with both tiers subject to the CILB's recovery-fund disclosure on every residential repaint contract above $2,500.
