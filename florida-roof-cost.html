Roof Replacement Cost in Florida (2026)

Roof replacement in Florida typically runs $9,500–$28,000 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. Miami-Dade and Broward counties form the High-Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) with 175-mph design winds, mandatory product approval (NOA), and secondary water barrier requirements — coastal Panhandle counties carry 150-mph design loads.

Florida climate & code drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 1A-2A
  • Hurricane wind tier: High-Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) — Miami-Dade and Broward counties under 175-mph design wind
  • Hail risk tier: Low — minimal severe hail history
  • Ground snow load: 0 psf — no design snow load
  • Dominant residential roof material: asphalt shingle

Florida licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Florida CILB — Certified Roofing Contractor (CCC) for statewide; Registered Roofing Contractor (RC) for local (official site)
  • Permit: permit required statewide; HVHZ has separate product approval

How roof replacement costs vary in Florida

State-specific code or insurance rule: Florida Statute §489.147 (anti-AOB law) and the 25% Rule under §708.1.1.1 of the Florida Building Code interact: if more than 25% of a roof in a 12-month period is damaged or repaired, the entire roof must be brought up to current code — a defining cost driver in Miami-Dade and Broward HVHZ jurisdictions where Notice of Acceptance (NOA) product approval is mandatory.
