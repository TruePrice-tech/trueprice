Siding Replacement Cost in Florida (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Florida typically runs $11,500–$31,000, with three-coat hard-coat stucco the dominant residential cladding choice. Florida HVHZ wind-borne debris exposure in Miami-Dade and Broward forces Notice of Acceptance fastener schedules on every cladding component — and Atlantic salt-haze on the Treasure Coast and Gulf salt-haze on Tampa Bay, Sarasota, and Naples corrodes galvanized fasteners on coastal re-sides within 3-5 years, mandating stainless on all shoreline stucco lath, fiber-cement, and brick veneer wall ties within 1 mile of the coastline.

Florida climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 1A-2A
  • Dominant residential cladding: Three-coat hard-coat stucco — dominant in arid and Mediterranean Sun Belt
  • Hurricane wind tier: High-Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) — Miami-Dade and Broward fastener-test approval mandatory
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: No statewide WUI cladding mandate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High-corrosion coastal — stainless or hot-dipped galvanized fasteners required within 3,000 ft of saltwater

Florida licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Florida Department of Business & Professional Regulation — Construction Industry Licensing Board (Certified or Registered Building Contractor) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required; Florida Product Approval (FL #) verification on all cladding components in HVHZ counties

How siding replacement costs vary in Florida

State-specific code or insurance rule: Florida Building Code §1714 requires every cladding component — siding panels, trim, fasteners, weather-resistant barrier — to carry a Florida Product Approval (FL #) on Miami-Dade and Broward HVHZ projects, with Notice of Acceptance (NOA) testing for wind pressures up to 175 mph and missile-impact resistance, eliminating the standard fiber-cement and cedar fastener schedules used elsewhere in the country.
