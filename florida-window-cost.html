Window Replacement Cost in Florida (2026)

Window replacements in Florida typically run $8,400–$32,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Southern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.4, SHGC ≤ 0.25). Florida's HVHZ Miami-Dade and Broward 175 mph design wind speed plus large-missile impact resistance requirement under FBC §1620 + ASTM E1996 D-rated drives the most expensive residential window installation requirements in the country at $15,000-$45,000 for full-house Miami-Dade NOA-rated D-rated impact-resistant residential windows, plus statewide impact-resistant requirement on every coastal-county installation, ENERGY STAR Southern climate zone driving low-SHGC (≤0.25) glazing requirement statewide, persistent Atlantic and Gulf salt-spray driving aluminum-clad-wood or fiberglass frame replacement at 5-7 year intervals, and FL Statute §553.844 Wind Loss Mitigation insurance discount eligibility for impact-rated glazing drives unique HVHZ-and-Wind-Loss-Mitigation-driven glazing selection.

Florida climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Southern — cooling-dominant, low SHGC priority (U ≤ 0.40, SHGC ≤ 0.25)
  • IECC climate zone: 1A-2A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.4 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: 0.25
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 175 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: Statewide ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Moderate — selected metro historic districts with review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Low — sub-20% pre-1978 housing share, RRP rarely triggered

Florida licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Florida DBPR Construction Industry Licensing Board — Specialty Glass and Glazing Contractor (SCC131151) or Certified Building Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for every window installation in Miami-Dade and Broward counties (HVHZ); Florida Product Approval (FL #) NOA verification mandatory on every HVHZ install; FL DBPR Specialty Glass and Glazing Contractor license required statewide; impact-resistant glazing mandatory on every coastal-county residential install

How window replacement costs vary in Florida

State-specific code or insurance rule: Florida Building Code §1620 + ASTM E1996 imposes the most restrictive coastal hurricane-zone window requirements in the country — every HVHZ residential window installation in Miami-Dade and Broward counties requires Florida Product Approval (FL #) compliance with Notice of Acceptance (NOA) testing for hurricane wind uplift up to 175 mph plus large-missile impact resistance per ASTM E1996 (D-rated 9-lb 2x4 impact at 50 fps), and FL DBPR separately licenses a Specialty Glass and Glazing Contractor (SCC131151) with mandatory examination on Florida Building Code wind-pressure calculations, FBC 1626 large-missile impact testing, and HVHZ NOA submittal documentation — the only dedicated state-level glazing specialty license for HVHZ work in the U.S., plus FL Statute §553.844 Wind Loss Mitigation insurance discount eligibility for impact-rated glazing on every Florida residential install.
