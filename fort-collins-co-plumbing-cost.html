Plumbing in Fort Collins: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Fort Collins has moderately hard water which can reduce water heater lifespan over time. Consider a water softener or tankless unit with a descaling system to protect your investment.

❄

Freeze risk

Fort Collins gets cold enough for frozen pipes to be a regular concern. Insulate pipes in crawlspaces, garages, and exterior walls. When temperatures drop below 20°F, let faucets drip slightly overnight.

🔧

Material recommendation

In Fort Collins's cold climate, PEX piping offers a key advantage: it can expand slightly when water freezes inside, making it more resistant to burst pipes than rigid copper or CPVC.

💡

Local tip

Homes built in Fort Collins during the 1970s-80s often have copper pipes that may be developing pinhole leaks after 40+ years. If you're seeing green staining at joints, it's time for an inspection.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Fort Collins?

Plumber rates in Fort Collins typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
