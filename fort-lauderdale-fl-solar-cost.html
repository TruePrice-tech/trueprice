Frequently asked solar questions

What is net metering?

Net metering credits you for excess electricity your panels send to the grid. Your meter runs backward during the day and forward at night. Policies and credit rates vary by state and utility.

What happens to solar panels during a power outage?

Grid-tied systems without batteries shut off during outages for safety (anti-islanding). A battery backup or hybrid inverter lets you use solar power during grid failures.

How long do solar panels last?

Modern panels are warrantied for 25-30 years and continue producing at reduced efficiency beyond that. Inverters last 10-15 years (string) or 25 years (microinverters) and are the main replacement cost.

Will solar panels survive a hurricane in Fort Lauderdale?

Solar panels on properly rated racking systems handle hurricanes well, often outlasting the roof itself during high-wind events. The critical factors: racking must be certified for Fort Lauderdale's wind speed zone (130+ mph), all roof penetrations need proper flashing and sealant, and ongoing maintenance keeps connections tight. Schedule a post-storm inspection with your installer after any significant hurricane event.

What solar incentives are available in Fort Lauderdale?

Every Fort Lauderdale homeowner installing solar qualifies for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit with no dollar limit. Beyond that, FL may offer state tax credits, utility-specific rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits, and property tax exemptions. The DSIRE database has the current list for your area. These stacked incentives typically reduce net cost by an additional 10-30%.

When is the best time to install solar in Fort Lauderdale?

Fort Lauderdale homeowners should aim for fall or winter to install solar. Cooler weather makes roof work safer and the system is ready for the high-production spring and summer months. Allow 6-12 weeks from signing a contract through permitting, utility interconnection approval, and installation. Start gathering quotes 2-3 months before your target window. Remember that the 30% ITC applies to the tax year in which installation is completed.
