Frequently asked concrete questions

How long does concrete last?

Properly poured and maintained concrete lasts 30-50 years. Driveways see 25-30 years with normal use. Sealing every 2-3 years and controlling drainage significantly extend the lifespan.

How much does a concrete patio cost?

A 300 sqft concrete patio costs $1,800-$3,600 for basic broom finish. Stamped patterns add $3-$8/sqft. Colored concrete adds $1-$3/sqft. An exposed aggregate finish adds $2-$4/sqft.

How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

What Fort Myers homeowners should know about concrete work

Why pricing varies in Fort Myers. Labor rates here run roughly 13% below the national average per BLS metro data, which is the main reason this city's pricing sits below the national midpoint. Our analysis shows concrete work in Fort Myers running roughly 8% below the US baseline.

Fort Myers's site and climate factors. Fort Myers has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Concrete in Fort Myers cures differently than in dry climates — high humidity slows evaporation, which actually improves curing strength but extends the timeline. The bigger concern is expansive clay soils common in this region, which cause heave and cracking. Proper sub-base preparation (4-6 inches of compacted gravel) is non-negotiable. Standard concrete in Fort Myers runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Fort Myers paperwork, inspections, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Fort Myers require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Who's doing the work in Fort Myers. With 68,190 people, Fort Myers's contractor pool is mid-sized. You will find 4-6 established shops for most trades, which provides enough competition for fair pricing. Fort Myers's measured growth has built a reliable contractor ecosystem. Lead times are manageable and pricing competition is healthy.

Local factors: concrete work in Fort Myers, FL

Fort Myers's housing profile. Fort Myers homes are about 34 years old on average, young enough that most original systems still have useful life remaining. concrete work demand here centers on upgrades, improvements, and the occasional early failure rather than wholesale replacement.

HOA and neighborhood rules. HOA prevalence in Fort Myers is moderate. If you live in a managed community, check covenants before signing a concrete work contract. Material color, visible fixture type, or warranty requirements may affect your selection.

Contractor market picture. Fort Myers's moderate growth keeps the contractor pipeline moving without the extreme backlogs seen in booming metros. Off-peak scheduling can yield 8-15% savings over peak-season rates. In Fort Myers (68,190 residents), concrete work is often done by small local companies or general contractors. For decorative work (stamped, colored), verify the contractor has a portfolio of completed projects in your specific pattern and color — decorative concrete expertise varies widely.

Timing considerations. Fort Myers allows year-round concrete work, but the ideal window is spring and fall when temperatures support proper curing without the heat-related challenges of summer. Avoid scheduling large pours during the hottest months — early morning pours are the only reliable option.

What drives cost. Fort Myers homeowners benefit from labor rates 13% below national medians. For a driveway or patio pour, this translates to savings of $390-780 compared to the national average. Newer construction in Fort Myers (averaging 34 years) means concrete work projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay.

Red flags to watch for. Any Fort Myers contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion. Watch for concrete work quotes in Fort Myers that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Lump-sum bids hide margin and make change orders impossible to evaluate.
