Fort Myers fencing: answers to common questions

How much does vinyl fencing cost?

Vinyl privacy fencing costs $20-$40 per linear foot installed. A typical 150 linear foot backyard fence runs $3,000-$6,000. Vinyl never needs painting, resists rot, and lasts 25-30 years.

Can I install a fence on a slope?

Yes, using either stepped (stair-step) or racked (following the slope) installation. Racked looks smoother on gentle slopes. Stepped is better for steep grades and is structurally stronger.

Who owns the fence between two properties?

Typically the homeowner who installed it. If the fence sits exactly on the property line, both neighbors share responsibility. Always get a survey before building to confirm the boundary.

What is the difference between dog-ear and flat-top fence?

Dog-ear pickets have angled cuts at the top for water runoff and a traditional look. Flat-top pickets have a clean, modern appearance but hold slightly more water, which can accelerate rot.

Do I need a permit to build a fence in Fort Myers?

Most Fort Myers jurisdictions require a permit for fences over 6 feet tall. Many require permits for any fence. You also need to check setback requirements, HOA rules, and call 811 for utility locates before digging.

What fence material lasts longest in Fort Myers's climate?

Fort Myers's humidity and heat are hard on wood fences. Vinyl and aluminum fences last 10-15 years longer because they do not rot, attract termites, or absorb moisture. If wood is your preference, pressure-treated pine or cedar hold up best but require staining or sealing every 2-3 years. Composite fencing is a middle-ground option that looks like wood but handles the humidity like vinyl.

Do I need a permit to build a fence in Fort Myers?

In Fort Myers, fence permits are standard for installations over 6 feet and sometimes required for any fence. Three things to handle before installation: call 811 for utility marking, confirm property boundaries (budget $300-$600 for a survey if needed), and review any HOA rules. The fence contractor should manage the permitting as part of the project scope.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Fort Myers?

Fence installation in Fort Myers typically costs $15-$30 per linear foot for wood privacy, $20-$40 for vinyl, $10-$20 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron. Local Fencing Guide for Fort Myers, FL

Fort Myers's humid climate is unkind to wood fences but treats vinyl, aluminum, and composites well. Wood is workable if you commit to pressure-treated lumber, elevate the bottom rail 2 inches above grade, and re-seal on a regular cadence -- but most homeowners find the lifetime cost lower with non-wood materials here.

Some Fort Myers neighborhoods have HOA fence restrictions. If your property is in a managed community, check the CC&Rs for height limits, material restrictions, and color requirements before committing to a design. Even without an HOA, most Fort Myers zoning codes restrict front-yard fence height to 3-4 feet.

As a hurricane-exposure area, Fort Myers homeowners should consider wind resistance when choosing a fence style. Solid privacy fences act as sails in high winds and are more likely to fail. Shadow-box or spaced-picket designs allow wind to pass through while still providing partial privacy. Post spacing of 6 feet (instead of 8) with deeper footings adds meaningful wind resistance.
