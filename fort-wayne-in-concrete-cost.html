What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

Fort Wayne, IN: concrete work cost factors

Why pricing varies in Fort Wayne. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. For concrete work specifically, Fort Wayne sits within a few points of the national midpoint on our cost model.

Local conditions. Fort Wayne has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Fort Wayne for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Standard concrete in Fort Wayne runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Fort Wayne require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 256,496, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Fort Wayne has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.

Seasonal timing. The concrete season in Fort Wayne runs May through October when overnight temps stay above 40°F consistently. Spring is peak demand as homeowners address winter frost damage. Fall pours (September-October) offer better contractor availability and fewer weather delays.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Fort Wayne track within a few points of the national average for concrete work, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners.
