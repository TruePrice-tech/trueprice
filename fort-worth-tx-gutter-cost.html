Why gutter sizing matters in Fort Worth

Standard 5-inch K-style gutters move about 1.2 gallons per second, adequate for moderate rainfall up to 2 inches per hour. Fort Worth rainfall intensity routinely exceeds that threshold, so 6-inch oversized gutters with 40% more capacity are worth the $2-$3 per linear foot upcharge in TCU/West Cliff, Arlington Heights, and Fairmount.

Getting downspouts right in Fort Worth

Proper downspout routing away from foundations prevents settlement and moisture intrusion. Downspout extensions of 4-6 feet from the foundation and connection to underground drainage are standard Fort Worth practice.

Rule of thumb for Fort Worth: one downspout per 20-30 linear feet of gutter. Each 2x3-inch downspout drains roughly 600 sq ft; step up to 3x4-inch on TCU/West Cliff, Arlington Heights, and Fairmount homes with large roof planes or steep pitches that concentrate runoff.

Fort Worth Gutter Quote Red Flags

The Fort Worth gutter-industry patterns worth flagging before signing anything.

Undersized gutters for Fort Worth rainfall

A Fort Worth bid that specifies 5-inch gutters without reference to 34 inches of annual rainfall or the specific TCU/West Cliff, Arlington Heights, and Fairmount roof area being drained is underthought. Any roof plane above 1,500 sq ft emptying to a single gutter run needs 6-inch in Fort Worth.

Flat Fort Worth gutters as installed

Standard pitch is 1/4 inch per 10 feet of run toward the downspout. Flat gutters pool, attract mosquitoes, and overflow during Fort Worth downpours. Test with a garden hose after install — pooling anywhere means the hanger heights need re-setting.

Downspouts dumping at the foundation

Fort Worth downspouts need splash blocks, extensions, or buried drain tile — without them, 34 inches of annual rain lands at the foundation each year. Foundation problems, wet basements, and landscape erosion follow. Fort Worth bids that ignore discharge routing are missing half the job.

Sectional gutters priced as seamless

True seamless gutters are roll-formed on-site from continuous coil using a portable forming machine — seams only at corners, downspout outlets, and end caps. If a Fort Worth contractor quotes "seamless" but plans to splice 10- or 20-foot factory sections, that's sectional product. Seams are the #1 failure point in gutter systems, and paying seamless pricing for sectional product is the most common Fort Worth gutter scam.
