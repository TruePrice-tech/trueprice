Permits and electrical scope for Fort Worth homeowners

Development Services issues kitchen remodel permits online with 1-3 business day turnaround for standard scope. Texas has no equivalent to California's Title 24 energy-code kitchen requirements, which simplifies the electrical permitting process. Induction cooktop conversion from gas requires a 50-amp 240V circuit ($300-$600 in the Fort Worth market, below California pricing). Atmos Energy coordinates gas-line capping on conversions. Panel upgrades from 100 to 200 amps ($2,000-$3,500) are common when kitchen remodel scope includes induction cooktop, EV charger circuit, and whole-home generator pre-wiring -- the post-Uri trifecta that defines Fort Worth's electrical-upgrade demand pattern.

Total: 8-14 weeks for mid-range scope. The DFW Metroplex's deep contractor pool keeps scheduling manageable at 3-4 weeks ahead for quality GCs. Post-Uri kitchen-electrification projects add Atmos Energy gas-capping coordination (3-5 business days) and potential Oncor panel-upgrade scheduling (2-6 weeks).

Plumbing, water, and rough-in throughout Fort Worth

Fort Worth Water's moderate hardness (150-200 ppm) produces visible white scale on kitchen fixtures within 18-24 months and clogs faucet aerators annually. Homeowners investing in kitchen remodel quality should consider point-of-use RO ($300-$500) for the kitchen sink to protect the investment in premium fixtures. The February 2021 Uri freeze demonstrated that kitchen plumbing on exterior walls is vulnerable: supply lines froze and burst in thousands of Fort Worth kitchens during the 4-day sub-zero event. Post-Uri, relocating kitchen supply lines away from exterior walls ($500-$1,500 additional scope) has become a recommended practice during gut renovations.

Kitchen remodels in Fort Worth frequently bundle water heater upgrades. At Atmos Energy's $0.95/therm gas rate, gas tankless water heaters (Rinnai, Navien) at $2,500-$4,000 installed are the dominant upgrade, providing continuous hot water and freeing the closet or garage space the old tank occupied. Electric heat-pump water heaters are less common because the low gas rate favors gas economics. Post-Uri, some homeowners specify dual-fuel water heater setups (gas primary with electric backup) for the same grid-outage-resilience logic that drives dual-fuel HVAC systems in this market.
