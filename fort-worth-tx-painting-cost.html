Fort Worth painting FAQ

How much does paint prep work cost?

Prep is 50-70% of the total job cost. Scraping, sanding, caulking, priming, and repairs can cost $1,500-$4,000 on an exterior. Skipping prep leads to peeling paint within 1-2 years.

Do I need to prime before painting?

Prime over bare wood, drywall patches, stains, dark colors being covered with light ones, and previously unpainted surfaces. Paint-and-primer combos work for simple color changes on already-painted walls.

Should I hire a painter or do it myself?

DIY saves 60-70% on cost but takes 3-5x longer and usually looks less polished. Hire a pro for exteriors, high ceilings, detailed trim work, or any spray application. DIY works fine for single interior rooms.

How much does it cost to paint kitchen cabinets?

Professional cabinet painting costs $3,000-$7,000 for an average kitchen. The process includes removing doors, degreasing, sanding, priming, spraying 2-3 coats, and reinstalling hardware.

Fort Worth's climate and paint durability

Fort Worth's paint failure mode is a three-way attack: UV intensity during the 20+ days above 100F accelerates pigment fading on south and west exposures, the February 2021 Winter Storm Uri demonstrated that freeze events can crack paint films on surfaces that had never experienced thermal contraction below 20F, and the severe hail corridor produces impact damage that chips and craters paint surfaces at points of hail strike. The combination means Fort Worth exterior paint faces more diverse degradation pathways than most US metros. The Blackland Prairie humidity during summer (dew points in the low 70s) promotes mildew on shaded north-facing surfaces that are also the most protected from UV -- so the surfaces that hold paint color best are also the most likely to grow mildew. This humidity-UV duality requires balancing UV-resistant formulations (south/west) with mildewcide-enhanced products (north/shaded).

Fort Worth's summer humidity (dew points in the low 70s) produces mildew growth on north-facing and shaded surfaces within 18-24 months of painting if mildewcide-enhanced products are not used. The standard treatment protocol: power wash with 3-5% sodium hypochlorite solution to kill existing mildew before repainting, then topcoat with mildewcide-enhanced acrylic (Sherwin-Williams SuperPaint with antimicrobial additive or Benjamin Moore Regal with built-in mold resistance). South-facing surfaces face the opposite problem: UV rather than mildew is the degradation pathway. The paint-product strategy should vary by exposure: UV-resistant on south/west, mildewcide-enhanced on north/east. This exposure-specific approach is rarely discussed by contractors who default to one product everywhere.

Fort Worth-area permits and lead paint rules

Fort Worth does not require permits for exterior painting unless structural repair is involved. Texas has no statewide painting contractor license, but reputable operators carry general liability insurance and EPA RRP certification for pre-1978 work. The Landmarks Commission review on Fairmount-Southside contributing structures is the primary regulatory constraint. EPA RRP compliance is federal and applies regardless of state licensing requirements: any work on pre-1978 homes that disturbs lead paint requires RRP-certified contractors, lead-safe practices, and proper disposal documentation.

Fort Worth: seasonal painting windows

Fort Worth's painting season: optimal exterior painting runs October through May when temperatures stay below 95F and humidity allows proper curing. June through August extreme heat (100F+) restricts work to early-morning hours (before 10am) on sun-exposed surfaces -- afternoon application in direct sun above 95F causes latex to skin and peel. The brief 1-2 week cold snaps in January-February require monitoring nighttime temperatures: latex paint needs overnight temperatures above 35F for proper film formation. The post-hail-storm spring season (April-June) creates demand surges for touch-up and repaint as homeowners address hail-impact damage simultaneously with insurance-claim roofing work.

Fort Worth-area common contractor upsells

Fort Worth painting upsells worth accepting: caulking all trim joints ($300-$700, essential in the clay-soil-movement market), mildewcide-enhanced primer on north-facing surfaces ($200-$400, prevents the mildew growth that Fort Worth's summer humidity promotes), and full hardboard-edge sealing on pre-1985 homes ($300-$600, prevents the moisture delamination that destroys hardboard siding). Upsells to decline: unnecessary "premium" clear-coat sealers on quality acrylic latex, "hail-resistant paint" marketing claims (paint cannot resist hail impact regardless of formulation), and excessive prep on post-2000 fiber cement or LP SmartSide that needs only power washing and spot-priming.
