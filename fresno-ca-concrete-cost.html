Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

Localized expansive-clay pockets in southeastern Fresno and along old river channels require additional sub-base preparation. Climate Impact on Fresno Concrete

Fresno's Central Valley climate creates a split concrete-performance environment: the extreme dry-summer heat (30-45 days above 100F) is the dominant placement and curing challenge, while the Tule fog season (November-February) creates a secondary curing challenge from persistent damp, cold conditions that prevent proper film formation on sealers and coatings. UV intensity at ground level accelerates surface chalking and sealer degradation. Agricultural dust deposits on concrete surfaces require periodic pressure washing. The positive: no freeze-thaw spalling (freezing events are rare and brief), no road-salt exposure, and the stable alluvium sub-base produces minimal settlement.

Fresno's concrete-damage events are less dramatic than other metros in this group. The December 2022-January 2023 atmospheric-river flooding produced standing water against concrete flatwork across low-lying central and south Fresno that created sub-base erosion beneath cracks and joints, manifesting as settlement months later. The extreme summer heat produces thermal-cracking damage on concrete placed without proper hot-weather protocol (retarder, early-morning placement, curing compound) -- this is the most common preventable concrete failure in the Fresno market. Seismic risk is moderate but has not produced a significant concrete-damaging event in modern records.
