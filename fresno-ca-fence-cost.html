Fencing questions for Fresno homeowners

How deep should fence posts be?

Standard rule: bury one-third of the total post length. For a 6-foot fence with 8-foot posts, dig 24-30 inches deep. In frost-prone areas, posts must go below the frost line to prevent heaving.

How much does vinyl fencing cost?

Vinyl privacy fencing costs $20-$40 per linear foot installed. A typical 150 linear foot backyard fence runs $3,000-$6,000. Vinyl never needs painting, resists rot, and lasts 25-30 years.

Can I install a fence on a slope?

Yes, using either stepped (stair-step) or racked (following the slope) installation. Racked looks smoother on gentle slopes. Stepped is better for steep grades and is structurally stronger.

Who owns the fence between two properties?

Typically the homeowner who installed it. If the fence sits exactly on the property line, both neighbors share responsibility. Always get a survey before building to confirm the boundary.

Fresno Soil and Climate Impact

Fresno's San Joaquin Valley alluvium provides generally stable conditions for fence-post setting with standard 24-30 inch depth. The sandy-loam and silt deposits support concrete footings well. Localized expansive-clay pockets in southeastern Fresno and along old river channels may require deeper posts (36 inches) with gravel drainage. The flat terrain and absence of rocky substrate make post-hole digging straightforward. The shallow water table in some central Fresno areas means post holes may encounter water during the winter wet season; drain-gravel at the footing base manages this.

Fresno's Central Valley climate creates distinctive fencing challenges: 30-45 days above 100F with peaks of 112-115F bleach and dry wood surfaces faster than most US markets, requiring UV-resistant stain every 2-3 years. The extreme heat warps green lumber within weeks of installation. The Tule fog season (November-February) creates the opposite problem: persistent damp 40-degree conditions promote mold growth on north-facing fence surfaces and on shaded portions of fence panels. The split-climate year means wood fencing in Fresno faces both UV degradation AND moisture damage -- a dual-threat combination that few markets experience at this intensity. The agricultural dust that permeates the Valley embeds in stain and sealer surfaces.
