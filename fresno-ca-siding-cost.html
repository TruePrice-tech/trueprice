Fresno siding FAQ

What goes under siding?

From inside out: wall sheathing (plywood or OSB), a weather-resistant barrier (house wrap like Tyvek), then siding. Some installations add rigid foam insulation between the house wrap and siding.

Does new siding increase home value?

New siding recovers 70-80% of its cost at resale. Fiber cement siding recoups more than vinyl. Beyond ROI, it eliminates a major curb appeal concern and passes inspection without issues.

Can siding be repaired instead of fully replaced?

Individual damaged sections can be replaced if matching material is available. If damage covers more than 20-30% of the wall area, full replacement is more cost-effective and looks better.

How long does siding installation take?

A full siding replacement on an average home takes 5-10 days. Larger homes or complex designs with multiple gables, dormers, and trim details can take 2-3 weeks.

How do I maintain fiber cement siding?

Repaint every 12-15 years, caulk joints and seams every 5-7 years, keep vegetation trimmed 6 inches from the wall, and wash annually with a garden hose. Avoid power washing at high pressure.

Fresno-specific siding fraud patterns

Fresno siding red flags: stucco contractors who don't specify elastomeric coating for the extreme Central Valley heat (standard acrylic paint chalks and fails within 3-4 years on south and west exposures during the 30-45 days above 100F), T1-11 and hardboard repair that doesn't address the underlying moisture damage caused by Tule fog season and failed edge-sealing (painting over rotted substrate wastes the paint investment because the rot continues behind the new film), and pricing significantly above Valley competitors without justifiable scope or specification difference. CalRecycle debris-diversion compliance adds legitimate cost; any contractor who doesn't mention it is either absorbing it or dumping illegally.

Understanding siding upsells to accept and decline in Fresno

Fresno siding upsells worth accepting: elastomeric stucco coating ($0.75-$1.50/sqft premium, handles the extreme heat better than standard acrylic), T1-11/hardboard replacement with fiber cement rather than overlay (eliminates the moisture-damaged substrate), and Tule-fog-season mildew-resistant primer on north-facing surfaces. Upsells to decline: vinyl in the extreme Valley heat (degrades faster than in temperate markets), and "premium" products at inflated pricing without Valley-competitive benchmarks.
