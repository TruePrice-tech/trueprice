Questions Fresno homeowners ask about window replacement

Are bay and bow windows more expensive?

Bay windows cost $1,500-$4,000 installed and bow windows $2,500-$5,500. Both require structural support and add 3-4x the cost of a standard same-sized window.

How long do replacement windows last?

Vinyl windows last 20-30 years, fiberglass 30-40 years, wood 30+ years with maintenance, and aluminum 20-25 years. Failed seals (foggy glass) are the most common early failure.

How do I know my windows need replacing?

Replace when you see fog between panes (failed seal), feel drafts with windows closed, frames are rotting or warping, locks no longer engage, or energy bills are rising with no other explanation.

Fresno energy performance spec: overview

Fresno window energy performance is dominated by the extreme cooling load: 30-45 days above 100F with peaks of 112-115F make solar heat gain through windows the single largest contributor to cooling costs. South and west-facing windows on the 1970s-90s tract homes across Woodward Park, Clovis, and northeast Fresno transmit enough solar energy to add $30-$60/month to summer PG&E bills at peak rates. Low-SHGC glass (0.22-0.27) on south and west exposures is the highest-ROI window specification in the Central Valley because it directly reduces the 4-5 ton AC system's runtime during the 10-14 hour daily cooling cycle that the 100F+ heat demands.

At PG&E's $0.26/kWh with TOU peak pricing at $0.45-$0.55/kWh during summer afternoons, Fresno window upgrades produce the fastest payback in PG&E territory: a full-house upgrade from single-pane to double-pane Low-E saves $500-$900 annually. The TOU rate structure adds urgency: the highest electricity rates coincide with the hours when poorly performing windows admit the most solar energy, creating a double penalty that efficient windows eliminate. This TOU-solar-gain interaction makes Fresno's window-upgrade economics more compelling than the moderate base rate ($0.26/kWh) alone suggests.

Fresno-area rebates and tax credit stacking

Fresno rebate stacking: PG&E Central Valley-specific programs offer $50-$150 per qualifying window (reflecting the higher cooling loads that make efficiency investments more cost-effective in the Valley). BayREN and SJVAPCD stack additional incentives. Federal 25C tax credit (30% up to $600 annually) applies. CalRecycle compliance adds documentation but not cost to the rebate process. The combined PG&E/BayREN/SJVAPCD/25C stack offsets 10-20% of a typical window project, and the faster payback from the extreme cooling-load reduction makes the rebate a bonus on top of already-strong economics.

What failing windows look like: a Fresno breakdown

Fresno's 1970s-90s tract homes have single-pane or early double-pane aluminum-frame windows that are functionally obsolete in the extreme Central Valley heat: the metal frames conduct heat directly into the interior, and the absent or degraded Low-E coating transmits solar energy at maximum intensity. Full-frame replacement with vinyl thermal-break frames and Low-E glass produces dramatic comfort improvement -- homeowners consistently report that the AC system runs 2-3 fewer hours per day after window replacement. A Fresno guide: glass package choices

Glass options for Fresno: double-pane Low-E with SHGC under 0.25 is the minimum specification for south and west exposures. Triple-pane on west exposures (where afternoon sun delivers the most intense heat during PG&E's TOU peak period) produces the highest ROI of any window upgrade in the Fresno market because it simultaneously reduces peak-rate electricity consumption and extends the AC system's service life by reducing run-time hours. Fresno and condensation and interior moisture management

Fresno's dry Central Valley climate produces minimal condensation during the main season. The Tule fog season (November-February) is the exception: persistent damp 40-degree conditions can produce exterior condensation on high-performance Low-E glass and interior condensation on original single-pane windows. The Tule fog condensation pattern is unique to the San Joaquin Valley and does not appear in any other California market. Standard double-pane Low-E eliminates interior condensation during fog season. The morning fog moisture on exterior glass surfaces evaporates as the fog lifts (typically by noon).

Understanding wind, storm, and durability requirements in Fresno

Fresno faces minimal window-storm risk: the sheltered Valley floor produces no significant wind events, hail is rare, and seismic risk is moderate but has not produced a major window-damaging event in modern records. The primary window-degradation mechanism is heat-driven seal failure: south and west-facing windows absorb enough solar energy to push seal temperatures above the manufacturer's rated service limit during the 112-115F peak days, accelerating argon-gas loss and producing the inter-pane condensation (fogging) that indicates seal failure. This heat-driven degradation produces a Fresno-specific failure timeline: 8-12 years on sun-exposed windows versus 15-20 years nationally.

Impact-rated window rules and insurance math for Fresno homeowners

Fresno has no impact-rated glass requirements: the sheltered Valley floor produces negligible wind and hail risk. The absence of impact requirements means Fresno homeowners can allocate their window budget entirely to thermal performance rather than splitting between thermal and impact.
