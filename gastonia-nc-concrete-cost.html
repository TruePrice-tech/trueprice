Common concrete questions in Gastonia

How much does a concrete patio cost?

A 300 sqft concrete patio costs $1,800-$3,600 for basic broom finish. Stamped patterns add $3-$8/sqft. Colored concrete adds $1-$3/sqft. An exposed aggregate finish adds $2-$4/sqft.

How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

More questions about concrete in Gastonia

What does a concrete driveway cost per square foot?

Plain 4-inch concrete runs $6-$12 per square foot installed in 2026. Stamped concrete adds $4-$8/sqft for pattern and color. Heavy-duty 6-inch driveways for RVs or trucks cost $9-$15/sqft. A 600 sq ft two-car driveway typically lands between $5,800 and $8,100.

How much does a 1,000 sq ft concrete patio cost?

Typical 2026 ranges for a 1,000 sq ft project: plain broom-finish $8,000-$12,000, exposed-aggregate $9,500-$14,500, stamped $11,000-$16,500. Tearing out an existing slab adds $1,500-$3,000. Reinforcing wire mesh and rebar are usually $0.50-$1.00/sqft and worth it on driveways, optional on patios.

What does a slab foundation cost in Gastonia?

A monolithic slab foundation for a typical single-family house in Gastonia runs $5,000-$15,000 depending on square footage and soil conditions. A garage slab is $2,500-$6,000. Full basement foundations are a different scope entirely and run $15,000-$30,000.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Gastonia?

Driveways, patios over a certain size, and any structural slab in Gastonia require a permit and an inspection of the base prep and rebar before the pour. Sidewalks, repairs, and resurfacing usually do not. Your contractor pulls the permit — never pull it in your own name.

Local factors: concrete work in Gastonia, NC

Home stock in Gastonia. With homes averaging just 30 years old, Gastonia has a relatively new building stock. How the local trade market looks. Gastonia is growing fast, and the construction trades have not kept pace with housing demand. This means longer wait times, less willingness to negotiate on price, and fewer available quotes to compare. Start your search early. In Gastonia (73,209 residents), concrete work is often done by small local companies or general contractors. Best months to book. Gastonia allows year-round concrete work, but the ideal window is spring and fall when temperatures support proper curing without the heat-related challenges of summer. Avoid scheduling large pours during the hottest months — early morning pours are the only reliable option.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Gastonia track within a few points of the national average for concrete work, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. Gastonia's relatively young housing stock (30 years average) simplifies most concrete work projects. High construction demand in Gastonia creates a seller's market for concrete work contractors. Booking 3-4 weeks ahead is typical; emergency or rush jobs carry 15-25% premiums.

Red flags to watch for. Watch for concrete work quotes in Gastonia that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately.
