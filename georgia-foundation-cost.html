Foundation Cost in Georgia (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in Georgia typically run $8,400–$19,400 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with slab-on-grade as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 6-inch design frost line. Georgia's red-clay Piedmont subsoil across the Atlanta-Athens-Macon corridor requires aggressive 4-to-6-inch crushed-stone capillary break under every slab pour to prevent clay-driven slab heave during seasonal moisture cycles, and Georgia's high mature-deciduous tree canopy across the Atlanta-Athens corridor combined with persistent root-driven slab uplift on existing foundations drives standard root-barrier and continuous expansion-joint installation on every Atlanta and Athens residential foundation, plus Georgia coastal Chatham, Bryan, Liberty, McIntosh, Glynn, and Camden counties require Atlantic hurricane wind tie-down detailing on every coastal residential foundation pour.

Georgia climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 6-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Slab-on-grade — dominant in Sun Belt and Southeast on warm climates with shallow frost line
  • Expansive soil risk: Moderate — periodic clay shrink-swell, standard footing reinforcement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC C — moderate seismic risk, continuous-rebar-tie footings required
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — 110-140 mph design wind, standard tie-down
  • Water table risk: Moderate — water table below frost line in most lots
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • IECC climate zone: 2A-3A
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 2 (moderate) — sub-slab depressurization rough-in recommended

Georgia licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Georgia State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors — residential-basic or residential-light (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential foundation pour on new construction; residential-basic or residential-light contractor license required above $2,500 contract; Georgia EPD Stormwater NOI required on every disturbed-acre residential subdivision pour

How foundation pour costs vary in Georgia

State-specific code or insurance rule: Georgia Code §43-41 created the State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors in 2008, making Georgia one of the most recent Southeast states to centralize residential contractor licensing — every Georgia foundation contractor exceeding the $2,500 contract threshold must hold a residential-basic or residential-light license, plus Georgia Code §44-3 separately enforces the Georgia Property Owners Association Act requiring HOA-approved foundation contractor selection on every covered residential property in deed-restricted Atlanta-area communities, and Georgia EPD Stormwater requires erosion-and-sediment plan plus NOI on every Atlanta subdivision lot foundation pour above 1 acre disturbed area.
