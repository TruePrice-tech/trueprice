Garage Door Replacement Cost in Georgia (2026)

Garage door replacements in Georgia typically run $1,100–$4,200 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 115 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Georgia's combination of Atlantic hurricane wind exposure across coastal counties (Chatham, Bryan, Liberty, McIntosh, Glynn, Camden) driving DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating on every coastal residential garage-door installation, very-high TPCT termite zone 1 pressure across the entire state driving pressure-treated wood jamb framing on every Atlanta, Athens, Macon, Augusta, and Savannah residential install, persistent Gulf-Atlantic humidity driving rapid corrosion on standard galvanized springs at 5-7 year intervals, and high HOA architectural-control prevalence across Atlanta-metro subdivisions drives unique hurricane-and-termite-driven material selection.

Georgia climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 115 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating at 110-140 mph design wind
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Chain-drive — dominant on detached or budget installs, durable and economical
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Very high — TPCT zone 1, all wood jamb framing requires PT southern yellow pine .60 CCA
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: High — 60-80% of subdivisions enforce HOA architectural-committee pre-approval
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: Moderate — bayfront salt-spray, stainless hardware recommended
  • IECC climate zone: 2A-3A

Georgia licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Georgia State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors — residential-light or residential-basic (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening; residential-light or residential-basic contractor license required above $2,500 contract; replacement-only installs typically permit-exempt; Georgia coastal Atlantic counties mandate DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating

How garage door installation costs vary in Georgia

Georgia's combination of Atlantic hurricane wind exposure across coastal counties (Chatham, Bryan, Liberty, McIntosh, Glynn, Camden) driving DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating on every coastal residential garage-door installation, very-high TPCT termite zone 1 pressure across the entire state driving pressure-treated wood jamb framing on every Atlanta, Athens, Macon, Augusta, and Savannah residential install, persistent Gulf-Atlantic humidity driving rapid corrosion on standard galvanized springs at 5-7 year intervals, and high HOA architectural-control prevalence across Atlanta-metro subdivisions drives unique hurricane-and-termite-driven material selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Georgia Code §43-41 created the State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors in 2008 with the residential-light contractor license dedicated to projects under 5,000 sq ft and the residential-basic license covering full new-construction work — every Georgia garage-door contractor exceeding the $2,500 contract threshold must hold one of these credentials, plus Georgia Code §44-3 separately enforces the Georgia Property Owners Association Act requiring HOA architectural-committee pre-approval on every Atlanta-metro garage-door installation in deed-restricted communities.
