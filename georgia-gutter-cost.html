Gutter Installation Cost in Georgia (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Georgia typically runs $1,100–$4,200, with 6-inch oversized K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 50 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Georgia's 50-inch annual rainfall concentrated in spring thunderstorm cells and summer tropical-driven downbursts pushes 6-inch oversized K-style as standard residential profile — and Atlanta-area mature pine and oak canopy drives heavy needle and leaf accumulation that requires 3-4 unguarded gutter cleanings per year, with gutter-guard retrofit demand near-universal on long-tenure suburban properties.

Georgia rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 50 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 6-inch oversized K-style seamless aluminum — dominant in high-rainfall and steep-pitch corridors
  • Ground snow load: 5 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: Low — occasional freeze events, minimal ice-dam history
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — 110–140 mph design wind
  • Tree-canopy density: High — mature deciduous canopy drives gutter-guard demand and 2x annual cleaning frequency

Georgia licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Georgia State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction

How gutter installation costs vary in Georgia

Georgia's 50-inch annual rainfall concentrated in spring thunderstorm cells and summer tropical-driven downbursts pushes 6-inch oversized K-style as standard residential profile — and Atlanta-area mature pine and oak canopy drives heavy needle and leaf accumulation that requires 3-4 unguarded gutter cleanings per year, with gutter-guard retrofit demand near-universal on long-tenure suburban properties.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Georgia State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors issues Residential Light Commercial Contractor and Residential Basic Contractor as two separately tested credentials for gutter and exterior work — and unique to Georgia, the Board requires Continuing Education in stormwater management for any contractor working in metro Atlanta DEKALB and Cobb county MS4 jurisdictions, where larger residential gutter systems must discharge into approved infiltration features rather than directly to streets.
