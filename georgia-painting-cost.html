Exterior Painting Cost in Georgia (2026)

Repainting a 1,500 sq ft home in Georgia typically runs $2,600–$7,600, with fiber-cement plank (Hardie or LP SmartSide-style) the dominant residential substrate driving prep scope. Georgia's 75%+ summer humidity combined with Atlanta-Athens heavy pine-pollen surge in the April-May window and high mature-deciduous tree canopy across the Piedmont drives mildew bloom and pollen contamination on every residential exterior coating system — fiber-cement substrates with mildewcide-additive 100% acrylic primer-and-topcoat have become the dominant Atlanta and Savannah suburban repaint spec, with Coastal Georgia salt-air on Tybee and St. Simons mandating marine-grade alkyd primer.

Georgia climate & coatings drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 2A-3A-4A
  • Dominant residential substrate: Fiber-cement plank (Hardie or LP SmartSide-style) — dominant post-2000 Sun Belt subdivision substrate
  • Annual humidity tier: Very high — 75%+ year-round drives mildew bloom + biocide-additive coatings
  • UV exposure: High — long sun seasons drive south-facing fade in 5-7 years on standard topcoats
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: Moderate-corrosion coastal — marine primer recommended on bayfront and tidal exposures
  • Freeze-thaw cycling: Moderate — periodic freeze events, standard caulk formulations adequate
  • VOC architectural coatings rule: Federal default — 250 g/L flat exterior topcoat limit (EPA 40 CFR Part 59)

Georgia licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Georgia State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for repaint; residential-basic or residential-light contractor license required above $2,500 contract

How exterior repaint costs vary in Georgia

State-specific code or insurance rule: Georgia Code §43-41 created the State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors in 2008, making Georgia one of the most recent Southeast states to centralize residential contractor licensing — every Georgia painting contractor exceeding the $2,500 contract threshold must hold a residential-basic or residential-light license, with mandatory continuing education on EPA RRP lead-safe procedures for Atlanta, Savannah, and Macon historic-district repaint work.
