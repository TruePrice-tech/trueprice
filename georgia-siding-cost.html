Siding Replacement Cost in Georgia (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Georgia typically runs $9,200–$25,500, with fiber-cement plank (Hardie or LP SmartSide-style) the dominant residential cladding choice. Atlanta-area red Piedmont clay seasonal swelling and shrinking shears stucco substrate cracks on slab-on-grade construction, driving fiber-cement plank dominance over hard-coat stucco — and South Georgia coastal Savannah and Brunswick salt-haze deposition mandates stainless fasteners on shoreline cedar and fiber-cement to survive within 1 mile of tidewater.

Georgia climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 2A-3A-4A
  • Dominant residential cladding: Fiber-cement plank — dominant in WUI fire-zone and high-humidity Sun Belt
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate wind exposure — 110–140 mph design wind on coastal counties
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: No statewide WUI cladding mandate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: Moderate-corrosion coastal — stainless fasteners recommended on bayfront and tidal exposures

Georgia licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Georgia State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for full re-side and structural sheathing work

How siding replacement costs vary in Georgia

State-specific code or insurance rule: Georgia State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors issues Residential Light Commercial Contractor and Residential Basic Contractor as two separately tested credentials — Residential Basic is restricted to single-family detached up to four stories with no pre-engineered structural systems, while Light Commercial extends to multi-family up to four units, with siding work falling under both as no separate cladding-only credential exists in Georgia.
