Window Replacement Cost in Georgia (2026)

Window replacements in Georgia typically run $5,200–$13,800 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR South-Central climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.32, SHGC ≤ 0.25). Georgia's combination of Atlantic hurricane wind exposure across coastal counties (Chatham, Bryan, Liberty, McIntosh, Glynn, Camden) driving ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated window mandate on every coastal residential install, ENERGY STAR South-Central climate zone driving low-SHGC (≤0.25) and U-factor (≤0.32) requirement statewide, persistent Gulf-Atlantic humidity driving rapid sealant degradation on every south-facing window at 5-7 year intervals, and high HOA architectural-control prevalence across Atlanta-metro subdivisions drives moderate hurricane-and-Southern-climate-zone-driven glazing selection.

Georgia climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: South-Central — mixed climate, balanced U-factor and lower SHGC (U ≤ 0.32, SHGC ≤ 0.40)
  • IECC climate zone: 2A-3A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.32 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: 0.25
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 115 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: Mandatory ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated in coastal counties only
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Moderate — selected metro historic districts with review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Moderate — 20-40% pre-1978 housing share, EPA RRP required on selected replacements

Georgia licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Georgia State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors — residential-light or residential-basic (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification; residential-light or residential-basic contractor license required above $2,500 contract; replacement-only same-size installs typically permit-exempt; Georgia coastal Atlantic counties mandate ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated windows

How window replacement costs vary in Georgia

Georgia's combination of Atlantic hurricane wind exposure across coastal counties (Chatham, Bryan, Liberty, McIntosh, Glynn, Camden) driving ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated window mandate on every coastal residential install, ENERGY STAR South-Central climate zone driving low-SHGC (≤0.25) and U-factor (≤0.32) requirement statewide, persistent Gulf-Atlantic humidity driving rapid sealant degradation on every south-facing window at 5-7 year intervals, and high HOA architectural-control prevalence across Atlanta-metro subdivisions drives moderate hurricane-and-Southern-climate-zone-driven glazing selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Georgia Code §43-41 created the State Licensing Board for Residential and General Contractors in 2008 with the residential-light contractor license dedicated to projects under 5,000 sq ft and the residential-basic license covering full new-construction work — every Georgia window contractor exceeding the $2,500 contract threshold must hold one of these credentials, plus Georgia Coastal Atlantic counties (Chatham, Bryan, Liberty, McIntosh, Glynn, Camden) separately require ASTM E1996 large-missile impact resistance on every coastal residential window installation to address Atlantic hurricane exposure with 115 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed.
