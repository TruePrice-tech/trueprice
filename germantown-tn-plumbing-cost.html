Frequently asked plumbing questions

What is the difference between copper and PEX piping?

Copper lasts 50+ years, resists bacteria, and has proven durability. PEX costs 60% less, installs faster, resists freezing better, and requires fewer joints. Both are code-compliant.

Is trenchless sewer repair worth the extra cost?

Trenchless repair costs 20-40% more but avoids excavating your yard, driveway, or landscaping. It is faster and causes less property damage, often making it cheaper overall.

How much does it cost to replace a water heater?

A standard 50-gallon tank water heater runs $1,200-$2,500 installed. Tankless units cost $2,500-$5,000 but last roughly twice as long and cut energy bills 20-30%.

What does a plumbing inspection include?

A thorough inspection covers supply lines, drain lines, water heater condition, fixture connections, shutoff valves, water pressure testing, and a visual check for leaks and corrosion.

How much does a plumber charge per hour in Germantown?

Plumbers in Germantown typically charge $75-$150 per hour. Emergency and after-hours calls add a $100-$200 trip fee on top of the hourly rate.

How often should I have my plumbing inspected in Germantown?

A yearly plumbing check in Germantown prevents the kind of surprises that cost thousands. The priority items: water heater (8-12 year lifespan, check anode rod and sediment), braided steel supply lines under sinks (replace after 10 years regardless of appearance), and main sewer line condition. Camera sewer inspections ($150-$400) every 3-5 years are standard practice for older homes.

What should a plumbing quote include in Germantown?

A professional plumbing quote in Germantown should itemize: labor hours, material costs and brands, permit fees if applicable, cleanup, and warranty terms. The quote should include the plumber's license number and proof of insurance. Get three written quotes for any job over $500 to ensure fair pricing.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Germantown?

Germantown homeowners should target early fall for planned plumbing work. Between summer water-heater demand and winter freeze calls, which translates to better availability and pricing. Emergency service runs 50-100% more than scheduled jobs, so proactive replacement of aging equipment is the financially smarter path.
