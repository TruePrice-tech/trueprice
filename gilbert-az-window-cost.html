Window Replacement questions for Gilbert homeowners

What is the difference between double-pane and triple-pane windows?

Triple-pane windows add 15-25% better insulation over double-pane but cost 25-35% more. They pay back faster in extreme climates (very cold winters or very hot summers).

What is argon gas fill in windows?

Argon is an inert gas sealed between panes that insulates better than air. It improves R-value by 15-20% and is standard on most quality double and triple-pane windows. It slowly leaks over 15-20 years.

Should I replace all windows at once?

Replacing all windows at once saves 10-15% on labor versus doing them individually. It also ensures uniform appearance and consistent energy performance throughout the home.

Ask contractors about tune-ups and partial upgrades before committing to a replacement.

Local market conditions. Rapid growth in Gilbert has stretched the local contractor workforce thin. Gilbert's large market has both national window companies (Renewal by Andersen, Pella) and independent installers using manufacturer-direct windows. National brands quote 40-60% higher than independents for comparable products — the difference is marketing overhead, not glass quality.

Seasonal timing. Gilbert allows year-round window installation, but spring and fall are optimal — moderate temperatures mean less thermal stress during the swap and more comfortable working conditions for installers. Summer heat can cause sealants to cure too fast.
