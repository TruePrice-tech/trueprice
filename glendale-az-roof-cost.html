Frequently asked roofing questions

Does a new roof increase home value?

A new roof recovers roughly 60-70% of its cost at resale and eliminates a major buyer objection. Homes with new roofs sell faster and with fewer inspection contingencies.

Do I need a permit for roof replacement in Glendale?

Yes. Most Glendale jurisdictions require a building permit for roof replacement. Your contractor should handle the permit and schedule inspection.

What is the difference between architectural and 3-tab shingles?

Architectural shingles are thicker, have a dimensional look, and last 25-30 years. 3-tab shingles are flat, thinner, and last 15-20 years but cost 30-40% less upfront.

Does homeowners insurance cover roof replacement?

Insurance covers sudden damage (storms, falling trees) but not wear-and-tear or maintenance neglect. Verify your policy covers replacement cost, not depreciated value.

What time of year is cheapest for roofing in Glendale?

Late fall and winter are typically slower seasons in Glendale, so contractors may offer better pricing. Spring and summer are peak demand, meaning longer waits and higher labor costs.

How long does a roof last in Glendale?

Roof lifespan in Glendale depends on material and maintenance: architectural shingles run 20-25 years, 3-tab shingles 15-20, metal 40-60, and tile 50 or more. UV radiation breaks down materials faster than most homeowners expect, so lighter colors and proper attic ventilation both add years. These numbers assume no major storm damage and regular maintenance.

Are permits required to replace my roof in Glendale?

Yes, Glendale generally requires a building permit for full roof replacements. Your roofer should pull this as part of the job. Without a permit, your insurance coverage may be compromised and you face disclosure obligations if you sell the home.

Best time of year to replace a roof in Glendale?

Glendale homeowners should target fall or early spring for roof replacement work. Summer surface temperatures make installation difficult and can affect adhesive curing, giving you the best combination of weather conditions and contractor pricing. Off-season scheduling typically saves 10-15% compared to peak demand periods, especially the weeks immediately following severe weather events.
