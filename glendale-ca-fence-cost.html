Frequently asked fencing questions

How deep should fence posts be?

Standard rule: bury one-third of the total post length. For a 6-foot fence with 8-foot posts, dig 24-30 inches deep. In frost-prone areas, posts must go below the frost line to prevent heaving.

How much does vinyl fencing cost?

Vinyl privacy fencing costs $20-$40 per linear foot installed. A typical 150 linear foot backyard fence runs $3,000-$6,000. Vinyl never needs painting, resists rot, and lasts 25-30 years.

Can I install a fence on a slope?

Yes, using either stepped (stair-step) or racked (following the slope) installation. Racked looks smoother on gentle slopes. Stepped is better for steep grades and is structurally stronger.

Who owns the fence between two properties?

Typically the homeowner who installed it. If the fence sits exactly on the property line, both neighbors share responsibility. Always get a survey before building to confirm the boundary.

What fence types does my Glendale HOA allow?

Every Glendale HOA has its own fence rules, but typical restrictions include height limits (6 feet backyard, 4 feet front), material restrictions (no chain link is common), color requirements (earth tones or white), and mandatory outward-facing finished sides. You will usually need architectural review board approval before installation begins. Get the current guidelines from your HOA management company and submit your design before committing to materials.

Are permits required to build a fence in Glendale?

Check with Glendale's building department, but permits are generally required for fences, especially those above 6 feet. Before digging post holes, you need utility locates (call 811) and confirmed property lines. If boundaries are uncertain, a survey ($300-$600) prevents expensive neighbor disputes. Your contractor should pull the permit.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Glendale?

In Glendale, budget $15-$30 per linear foot for wood privacy fencing, $20-$40 for vinyl, $10-$20 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron.
