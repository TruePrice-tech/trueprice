Plumbing in Grand Forks: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

The extremely hard water in Grand Forks causes heavy scale buildup in pipes and appliances. Water heaters, dishwashers, and faucets all suffer. A water softener is practically a necessity here.

❄

Freeze risk

Grand Forks's severe winters make frozen pipes a serious risk. Insulate all exposed pipes, especially in crawlspaces and exterior walls. Keep cabinet doors open during deep freezes and know your main shutoff valve location.

🔧

Material recommendation

PEX piping is the top recommendation for repiping in Grand Forks. It's flexible, resists freezing better than rigid pipes (it expands slightly), and typically costs 30-40% less than copper to install.

💡

Local tip

Homes built in Grand Forks during the 1970s-80s often have copper pipes that may be developing pinhole leaks after 40+ years. If you're seeing green staining at joints, it's time for an inspection.
