What Grand Junction residents ask about roofing

Why do roofing quotes vary so much?

Bid-to-bid variation comes down to material grade, labor assumptions, flashing work, ventilation upgrades, and warranty tier. Lower bids often exclude items that appear as change orders later.

How do I know if my roof deck needs replacing?

Signs include sagging between rafters, soft or spongy spots when walked on, visible water stains from the attic, and rotted or delaminating plywood. Your roofer checks this during tear-off.

What roofing material is best for Grand Junction?

Material choice depends on Grand Junction's climate, your budget, and desired lifespan. Architectural shingles offer the best balance of cost and durability for most homeowners.

What is a roofing square?

A roofing square equals 100 square feet. A typical 2,000 sqft roof is about 20 squares. Contractors price materials and labor per square.

Do I need impact-resistant shingles in Grand Junction?

Absolutely. Hail damage is one of the most frequent roof insurance claims in Grand Junction. Class 4 impact-resistant shingles absorb hail strikes that would crack or dislodge standard materials. While they cost 10-20% more upfront, the 15-25% insurance discount most CO carriers offer for IR-rated roofs typically pays back the difference in 3-5 years, not counting the avoided replacement costs.

Is a Grand Junction permit needed to replace my roof?

In most jurisdictions including Grand Junction, a building permit is required for a full roof replacement. Your contractor should pull the permit before work begins. Unpermitted roofing work can void your homeowners insurance coverage and create disclosure issues when selling the home.

When should I replace a roof in Grand Junction?

For Grand Junction, aim for spring or fall to schedule your roof replacement. Moderate temperatures allow proper material adhesion and curing, which means better contractor availability and potential savings of 10-15% on labor. Booking right after a major storm is the worst time since every roofer in the area will be booked and prices reflect the surge demand.
