Grand Rapids concrete FAQ

Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Grand Rapids?

In Grand Rapids, permits are typically required for driveways, sidewalks, and any concrete work near property lines or public right-of-way. Backyard patios may be exempt depending on size. Check local requirements.

What is stamped concrete?

Stamped concrete uses patterned mats pressed into wet concrete to mimic brick, stone, slate, or wood. It costs $12-$25/sqft installed. It requires resealing every 2-3 years to maintain appearance and prevent wear.

Grand Rapids, MI wage data for contractors

According to BLS data, cement masons and concrete finishers in the Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood, MI metro earn a median of $28.19/hr (13% above the national median of $25.00/hr). Additionally, construction laborers in the area earn $23.24/hr, 5% above the $22.08/hr national rate.

Higher contractor wages explain much of why concrete work costs in Grand Rapids exceed the national average. With labor making up 40-60% of a typical concrete work project, local wage data is one of the most reliable indicators of what your quote will look like.

Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS), most recent annual release.
