Questions Great Falls homeowners ask about landscaping

How much does a retaining wall cost?

Retaining walls cost $20-$50 per square face foot for concrete block, $25-$60 for natural stone, and $15-$30 for timber. A 50 linear foot wall at 3 feet tall runs $3,000-$9,000 installed.

What is xeriscaping?

Xeriscaping uses drought-tolerant plants, efficient irrigation, and permeable surfaces to minimize water use. It cuts outdoor water consumption 50-75% and is increasingly required in water-restricted regions.

Do I need a permit for landscaping in Great Falls?

Most landscaping in Great Falls does not require permits. Exceptions include retaining walls over 4 feet, grading that changes drainage, connecting to city water for irrigation, and work near property lines.

How often should I replace mulch?

Replenish organic mulch annually (top off to 3 inches). Full replacement every 2-3 years prevents matting and fungal buildup. Rock and rubber mulch last 5-10 years but do not improve soil.

What is the cheapest way to landscape a yard?

Seed instead of sod, use native plants from local nurseries, mulch with free municipal compost, and phase the project over 2-3 seasons. A basic DIY front yard makeover can be done for $500-$1,500.

What shapes landscaping pricing in Great Falls, MT

Why pricing varies in Great Falls. Labor rates here run roughly 10% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For landscaping specifically, Great Falls sits within a few points of the national midpoint on our cost model.

Local conditions. Great Falls has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Landscaping in Great Falls is constrained by USDA hardiness zones that limit plant selection to cold-hardy natives. The growing season is short — stick to zone-appropriate perennials and spring planting after the last frost to ensure survival through the first winter. Newer developments in Great Falls typically start with minimal landscaping — builder-grade sod and a few foundation shrubs. A comprehensive landscape plan costs $3,500-12,000 and typically adds 5-15% to home value, making it one of the highest-ROI exterior investments.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most landscaping projects in Great Falls require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. At 59,351 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. Great Falls's stable population means contractor capacity rarely tightens, so off-season pricing (typically Nov-Feb) can save 10-15% on non-emergency work.

landscaping insights for Great Falls, MT

Home stock in Great Falls. Great Falls's housing stock averages 28 years old — relatively recent construction that was largely built to modern code. landscaping projects here tend to be simpler: fewer surprise compliance issues, quicker permit turnaround, and contractors who can quote from memory.

HOA & compliance. HOA involvement is uncommon in Great Falls, so most landscaping projects avoid architectural review delays. City permitting alone governs the process.

Local market conditions. Great Falls has a stable, lower-growth construction market, which usually means more competitive contractor pricing than fast-growing metros. The tradeoff: smaller contractor pool, so reputation and reviews matter more — a few bad actors can dominate the local landscape. In Great Falls (59,351 residents), landscaping is often handled by small local crews who do both installation and maintenance. For larger design-build projects, consider firms from the nearest larger metro that service your area.

Seasonal timing. The landscaping season in Great Falls runs April through October. Spring planting (April-May) is the busiest period as homeowners and contractors rush to install before summer heat. Fall planting (September-October) is the insider move — plants establish roots before dormancy and emerge stronger the following spring.

What drives cost. With Great Falls labor rates near the national median, the cost difference between a budget and premium full yard makeover comes down to materials and scope rather than labor premiums. Newer construction in Great Falls (averaging 28 years) means landscaping project projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay.

Red flags to watch for. Any Great Falls contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion. Watch for landscaping project quotes in Great Falls that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Lump-sum bids hide margin and make change orders impossible to evaluate.
