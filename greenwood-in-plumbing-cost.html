Plumbing in Greenwood: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

The extremely hard water in Greenwood causes heavy scale buildup in pipes and appliances. Water heaters, dishwashers, and faucets all suffer. A water softener is practically a necessity here.

❄

Freeze risk

In Greenwood, pipe freezing is a moderate-to-high risk during the coldest months. Make sure you know where your main water shutoff valve is — quick action when a pipe bursts can save thousands in damage.

🔧

Material recommendation

PEX piping is the top recommendation for repiping in Greenwood. It's flexible, resists freezing better than rigid pipes (it expands slightly), and typically costs 30-40% less than copper to install.

💡

Local tip

Many Greenwood homes built before 1970 have galvanized steel pipes that corrode from the inside out. If your water pressure has been steadily dropping or you see rusty water, a whole-house repipe is likely needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Greenwood?

Plumber rates in Greenwood typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
