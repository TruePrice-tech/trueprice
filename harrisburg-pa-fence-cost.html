Frequently asked fencing questions

How deep should fence posts be?

Standard rule: bury one-third of the total post length. For a 6-foot fence with 8-foot posts, dig 24-30 inches deep. In frost-prone areas, posts must go below the frost line to prevent heaving.

How much does vinyl fencing cost?

Vinyl privacy fencing costs $20-$40 per linear foot installed. A typical 150 linear foot backyard fence runs $3,000-$6,000. Vinyl never needs painting, resists rot, and lasts 25-30 years.

Can I install a fence on a slope?

Yes, using either stepped (stair-step) or racked (following the slope) installation. Racked looks smoother on gentle slopes. Stepped is better for steep grades and is structurally stronger.

Who owns the fence between two properties?

Typically the homeowner who installed it. If the fence sits exactly on the property line, both neighbors share responsibility. Always get a survey before building to confirm the boundary.

How deep should fence posts be in Harrisburg?

In Harrisburg's cold climate, fence posts should extend at least 6 inches below the frost line to prevent heaving. For most of Harrisburg, this means 36-48 inches deep with 8-10 inches of compacted gravel at the bottom for drainage and concrete from there to grade. Shallow posts in freeze-prone areas will lean within 2-3 winters regardless of how well the rest of the fence is built.

Are permits required to build a fence in Harrisburg?

Fence permits are required in most Harrisburg jurisdictions, particularly for heights over 6 feet. Before breaking ground: call 811 for utility locates, verify property lines (a survey costs $300-$600 if boundaries are unclear), and check HOA requirements. A professional contractor handles the permit process as part of the project.

Per-foot costs for fence installation in Harrisburg: wood privacy $15-$30, vinyl $20-$40, chain link $10-$20, and aluminum or wrought iron $25-$55. Local Fencing Guide for Harrisburg, PA

Fence installation in Harrisburg requires cold-climate construction methods. Post holes must extend below the local frost line (typically 36-48 inches) to prevent heaving, and concrete footings need time to cure before freezing temperatures arrive. Wood species selection matters more here since pressure-treated southern pine handles freeze-thaw cycling well, while cedar provides natural rot resistance without the chemical treatment.

Some Harrisburg neighborhoods have HOA fence restrictions. If your property is in a managed community, check the CC&Rs for height limits, material restrictions, and color requirements before committing to a design. Even without an HOA, most Harrisburg zoning codes restrict front-yard fence height to 3-4 feet.

Many Harrisburg properties (average home age 48 years) have aging or deteriorated fences that predate current building codes. Replacement often requires updated post depth, property line verification, and sometimes a survey to confirm boundaries before new fence construction begins. Budget $300-$600 for a property survey if boundary markers are not clearly visible.
