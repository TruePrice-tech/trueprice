Common fencing questions in Hattiesburg

Can I install a fence on a slope?

Yes, using either stepped (stair-step) or racked (following the slope) installation. Racked looks smoother on gentle slopes. Stepped is better for steep grades and is structurally stronger.

Who owns the fence between two properties?

Typically the homeowner who installed it. If the fence sits exactly on the property line, both neighbors share responsibility. Always get a survey before building to confirm the boundary.

What is the difference between dog-ear and flat-top fence?

Dog-ear pickets have angled cuts at the top for water runoff and a traditional look. Flat-top pickets have a clean, modern appearance but hold slightly more water, which can accelerate rot.

What fence material lasts longest in Hattiesburg's climate?

For maximum lifespan in Hattiesburg's climate, vinyl and aluminum fences are the clear winners, lasting 10-15 years longer than wood due to immunity from rot, termites, and moisture. Cedar and pressure-treated pine are the best wood options but need sealing every 2-3 years. Composite fencing offers wood-grain appearance with weather resistance closer to vinyl.

Hattiesburg permits: required to build a fence?

Most Hattiesburg jurisdictions require permits for fences, especially those over 6 feet. Check with your local building department before starting. You must call 811 for utility locates before digging, verify property lines (consider a survey if boundaries are unclear), and check any HOA rules. Your contractor should handle permitting as part of the project.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Hattiesburg?

Hattiesburg fence installation rates: wood privacy $15-$30/ft, vinyl $20-$40, chain link $10-$20, and ornamental metal $25-$55. For a typical 150 linear foot backyard, that works out to $2,250-$6,000 depending on material choice. Three quotes with identical scope give you a reliable pricing range.
