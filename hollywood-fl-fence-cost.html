What Hollywood residents ask about fencing

When is the best time to install a fence?

Late spring through early fall offers the best conditions for concrete curing and working in the ground. Avoid frozen ground and extremely wet seasons. Many contractors offer off-season discounts.

How long does fence installation take?

A standard 150-200 linear foot residential fence takes 1-3 days to install. Hillside or rocky soil adds time. Post holes need 24-48 hours to cure if set in concrete.

How deep should fence posts be?

Standard rule: bury one-third of the total post length. For a 6-foot fence with 8-foot posts, dig 24-30 inches deep. In frost-prone areas, posts must go below the frost line to prevent heaving.

How much does vinyl fencing cost?

Vinyl privacy fencing costs $20-$40 per linear foot installed. A typical 150 linear foot backyard fence runs $3,000-$6,000. Vinyl never needs painting, resists rot, and lasts 25-30 years.

Can I install a fence on a slope?

Yes, using either stepped (stair-step) or racked (following the slope) installation. Racked looks smoother on gentle slopes. Stepped is better for steep grades and is structurally stronger.

Understanding fence installation costs in Hollywood, FL

Why pricing varies in Hollywood. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. For fence installation specifically, Hollywood sits within a few points of the national midpoint on our cost model.

Local conditions. Hollywood has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Wood fences in Hollywood face a double threat: UV degradation and moisture rot. Pressure-treated pine rated for ground contact (UC4A) is the minimum standard — cedar, which performs well in dry climates, rots in 5-7 years here without aggressive sealing. Budget for annual sealing or staining to reach a 15-year lifespan. In Hollywood, material choice drives the total fence cost more than labor. A 150-linear-foot privacy fence ranges from $3,000-4,500 in pressure-treated pine to $5,500-8,500 in cedar to $6,000-10,000 in vinyl. The cost-per-year calculation often favors vinyl or composite despite the higher upfront price.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most fence installation projects in Hollywood require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 146,526, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Hollywood has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.

What Hollywood, FL homeowners should know about fence installation

Home stock in Hollywood. Hollywood's housing stock averages 25 years old — relatively recent construction that was largely built to modern code. fence installation projects here tend to be simpler: fewer surprise compliance issues, quicker permit turnaround, and contractors who can quote from memory.

HOA & compliance. HOAs are common in Hollywood. For fence installation, factor in 2-4 weeks of HOA architectural review before starting, and collect pre-approval of materials in writing. HOA rejections after project start can be expensive.

Local market conditions. Hollywood has moderate construction activity. Contractors are busy but accessible with 1-3 weeks' notice. Off-season quotes (contractor-dependent) can run 8-15% below peak rates. Hollywood's large market supports both dedicated fence companies and general contractors who do fencing. Dedicated fence installers typically quote 15-25% less because they buy materials in bulk and run crews that do nothing but fence work.

Seasonal timing. In Hollywood, fence contractors are busiest March through June when homeowners want fencing before summer. Fall (September-November) offers better availability and occasionally lower prices as outdoor project demand fades.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Hollywood track within a few points of the national average for fence installation, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. Hollywood's relatively young housing stock (25 years average) simplifies most fence installation projects. Modern code compliance, standard dimensions, and accessible construction reduce both time and cost versus older homes.

Red flags to watch for. Check that any Hollywood contractor doing fence installation carries both general liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers' compensation. Request certificates directly from the insurer, not just copies the contractor provides. In Hollywood, verify your fence installation contractor pulls the permit themselves — never pull it in your own name. If they ask you to pull the permit, they may not be properly licensed to do the work.
