What Houston residents ask about concrete

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Houston?

In Houston, permits are typically required for driveways, sidewalks, and any concrete work near property lines or public right-of-way. Backyard patios may be exempt depending on size. Check local requirements.

What is stamped concrete?

Stamped concrete uses patterned mats pressed into wet concrete to mimic brick, stone, slate, or wood. It costs $12-$25/sqft installed. It requires resealing every 2-3 years to maintain appearance and prevent wear.

What causes concrete to crack?

Common causes: shrinkage during curing, frost heave from inadequate base prep, tree roots, heavy vehicle loads beyond design, poor control joint placement, and water pooling due to improper grading.

Can cracked concrete be repaired?

Hairline cracks can be sealed with flexible caulk or epoxy for $1-$5 per linear foot. Wider cracks may need patching compound. If the slab has heaved or settled significantly, mudjacking ($5-$10/sqft) or replacement is needed.

Concrete in Houston cures differently than in dry climates — high humidity slows evaporation, which actually improves curing strength but extends the timeline. The bigger concern is expansive clay soils common in this region, which cause heave and cracking. Proper sub-base preparation (4-6 inches of compacted gravel) is non-negotiable. Subgrade and mix design throughout Houston

Beaumont clay and Lissie Formation silts sit under most of the Houston metro with Plasticity Index values frequently above 40. The 4-6 inch seasonal vertical soil movement Houston is infamous for is why nearly every residential slab here is post-tensioned. Raised pier-and-beam houses in the Heights, Montrose, and Woodland Heights avoid the worst of the shrink-swell cycle.

Houston residential spec is 3,500 psi with Type II cement to resist sulfate-rich clay, and 1/2-inch post-tensioned strand spaced 48-60 inches on a chair grid for structural slabs. US Concrete, Tilcon Martin Marietta, and Southern Star Concrete dominate the delivery radius inside Beltway 8.

Residential work in Houston rarely uses conventional rebar for structural slabs; PT strand is the dominant standard. Non-structural flatwork like carport pads or rear patios still uses #4 rebar on 18-inch centers with a full grid of plastic chairs.

Climate Impact on Houston Concrete

Houston essentially does not freeze in a statistically meaningful way (about 2 freeze-thaw cycles a year). The degradation threats are humidity, hurricane-season saturation of subgrade, and chloride exposure in coastal Seabrook and Clear Lake zones. TxDOT uses brine rather than rock salt during occasional icing events.

How the local trade market looks. Houston is growing fast, and the construction trades have not kept pace with housing demand. This means longer wait times, less willingness to negotiate on price, and fewer available quotes to compare. Start your search early. Houston's large market has concrete contractors ranging from 1-person decorative specialists to large commercial outfits that also do residential. For standard flatwork (driveways, patios), mid-size companies with dedicated residential crews typically offer the best combination of pricing and quality.

Best months to book. Houston allows year-round concrete work, but the ideal window is spring and fall when temperatures support proper curing without the heat-related challenges of summer. Avoid scheduling large pours during the hottest months — early morning pours are the only reliable option.
