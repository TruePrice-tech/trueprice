Garage Door questions for Houston homeowners

Are smart garage door openers worth it?

Smart openers add $100-$200 over basic models. They let you open, close, and monitor the door from your phone, set schedules, and receive alerts. Useful for deliveries and forgetting to close.

How much does garage door spring replacement cost?

Torsion spring replacement costs $200-$400 for parts and labor. Extension springs cost $150-$300. Never attempt spring replacement yourself since the stored tension is extremely dangerous.

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Houston?

Garage door replacement in Houston typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

What causes a garage door to be noisy?

Common causes: worn rollers (replace nylon for steel), dry hinges (lubricate with silicone spray), loose hardware, a chain-drive opener, or worn spring coils. A tune-up costs $100-$200.

Do I need a wind-rated garage door in Houston?

Absolutely. Houston's hurricane-zone building code mandates wind-rated garage doors. The garage opening is typically the weakest structural point in a home during high winds. When a non-rated door fails, the resulting pressure change can lift the roof. Wind-rated options cost $300-$800 more and often qualify for insurance premium reductions that offset the added cost.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Houston?

Given that Houston homes average 36 years old, many original garage doors are due for replacement rather than repair. A new insulated door and modern opener runs $1,200-$3,000 and recoups 90-100% at resale. Repairs make financial sense for doors under 15 years old with specific damage, but throwing money at an aging system usually delays an inevitable replacement.

When should I replace a garage door in Houston?

Houston homeowners get the best pricing and selection by scheduling garage door replacement during fall or winter. Cooler weather makes the installation more comfortable and demand is lower, and you can save 5-10% versus peak season pricing. Spring demand surges from cold-weather spring failures, so plan ahead to avoid the rush.

Local Garage Door Guide for Houston, TX

Houston's heat and humidity drive garage door failure modes that drier climates rarely see. Weatherstrips harden and crack, steel tracks develop rust streaks, and insulated panels can delaminate over time. Aluminum or galvanized hardware and properly vented garages both extend service life here.

Houston is in a hurricane exposure zone, which means building codes require wind-rated garage doors. A standard door will fail under hurricane-force winds, and the resulting pressure change can lift the entire roof structure. Wind-rated doors cost 20-40% more but are required by code and may lower your insurance premium. If your current door is not wind-rated, upgrading it is one of the most cost-effective storm protection investments you can make.

Many Houston homes (average age 36 years) have garage doors and openers that predate modern safety standards. Openers installed before 1993 may lack auto-reverse sensors, which are now required by federal law. If your opener does not have photo-eye sensors at the base of the door, replace the opener regardless of whether it still functions since this is a serious safety and liability issue.

HOA restrictions in many Houston neighborhoods dictate garage door style, color, and sometimes material. Before ordering a replacement door, get your HOA's architectural guidelines in writing and submit your selection for approval. Retroactive violations can result in fines and forced replacement at your expense.

Houston's fast-growing market means garage door installers stay busy, particularly during spring and fall. Schedule your replacement 2-4 weeks in advance and request a firm installation date in writing to avoid delays.
