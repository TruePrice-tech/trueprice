Common kitchen remodel questions in Houston

How much does it cost to move kitchen plumbing?

Moving a sink or dishwasher 3-5 feet costs $500-$1,500. Moving plumbing to a different wall or adding a kitchen island with plumbing costs $2,000-$5,000 depending on access and distance.

What kitchen layout is most efficient?

The work triangle (sink, stove, fridge within 4-9 feet of each other) remains the gold standard. L-shaped and U-shaped layouts are most efficient. Galley kitchens work well for single-cook households.

Do I need a permit for a kitchen remodel in Houston?

In Houston, permits are required if you move plumbing, add electrical circuits, or change the structural layout. Cosmetic updates like paint, hardware, and countertop swaps do not need permits.

Houston's permits and electrical scope

Houston Public Works issues the kitchen permit via the ProjectDox portal with 1-3 business day turnaround on residential. CenterPoint Energy panel upgrades to 200A run $2,800-$5,200; most post-1990 houses already have 200A. Texas is a non-NEC-amended state that requires GFCI protection on every kitchen receptacle per the 2020 NEC. Gas work is inspected by the Houston Gas Administration separately from electrical; both inspections are needed before drywall closure.

Houston ProjectDox runs 1-3 business days on residential kitchen permits, making it one of the fastest major-city permit portals in the country. Surrounding Harris County (unincorporated), Sugar Land, Pearland, and The Woodlands each have separate portals with varying turnaround. Deed-restricted neighborhoods in River Oaks, Memorial Villages, and West U add private architectural review that runs another 2-6 weeks and often requires committee meetings with specific submission dates.

Countertop trends and local stone yards: Houston edition

Granite is still the Houston default because of the local fabrication industry in Stafford and Pasadena and because humidity does not damage properly sealed granite the way it cracks quartz seams. Ant Granite, Custom Granite, and Levantina Houston stock the slab yards. Quartz is gaining in Memorial and River Oaks through Caesarstone and Silestone. Leathered granite is a Houston-specific preference for hiding water spotting under humid conditions.

Plumbing, water, and rough-in: Houston edition

Houston water is hard and chloraminated; the City of Houston Public Utilities draws from the Trinity River and Lake Houston. Post-tensioned slab homes require ground-penetrating radar or X-ray before any plumbing relocation through the slab, which adds $600-$1,500 to a sink move. Master plumbers (TSBPE-licensed) charge $110-$160/hour and the licensing is rigorously enforced. Pier-and-beam Heights houses allow easy underside access that cuts plumbing relocation cost in half.

Houston kitchen remodels on pier-and-beam often reveal a gas tank in a utility closet that the remodel redirects to the laundry room or an exterior closet. Slab houses in Meyerland often have an attic-mounted tank that floods ceilings when it fails; proactive kitchen-era swap to a tankless (Rinnai RU199 or Navien NPE-240A) costs $2,800-$4,800 installed. Houston Energy Efficiency Alliance and CenterPoint Energy rebates cover $150-$600 of the gas-tankless swap.
