Houston medical bills: the Texas Medical Center, Harris Health safety net, and energy-industry insurance

Houston's medical landscape is unique in the country: the Texas Medical Center (TMC) is the largest medical complex in the world, with over 60 institutions including Houston Methodist, Memorial Hermann, MD Anderson Cancer Center, Texas Children's Hospital, Baylor College of Medicine, and the Harris Health System's Ben Taub Hospital. The concentration of academic and specialty care produces extraordinary depth for complex conditions but also produces unusually high pricing across the board. For routine care, Houston's hospital systems outside the TMC core (CHI St. Luke's Health, HCA Houston Healthcare, Memorial Hermann community campuses) offer 20-40 percent below TMC academic premium pricing.

Texas's failure to expand Medicaid means Houston's uninsured rate is around 21 percent. Harris Health System (the Harris County safety-net) provides charity care to uninsured Harris County residents through Ben Taub Hospital, LBJ Hospital, and a network of community-based clinics. The Harris Health Financial Assistance Program covers patients up to 200 percent of FPL with full charity care, with sliding scales above 200 percent FPL. The federally qualified health centers serving Houston (Legacy Community Health, Vecino Health Centers, Spring Branch Community Health Center, plus several others) provide sliding-fee primary care.

The energy industry creates an unusual Houston insurance market. Major Houston employers (ExxonMobil, Chevron, ConocoPhillips, Halliburton, plus dozens of mid-size energy companies) provide comprehensive insurance with broad networks and low cost-sharing. Houston's tech and medical-research employers also support well-insured workforce populations. The well-insured commercial market subsidizes the TMC's research and teaching missions and supports premium pricing across Houston hospitals. International medical tourism is also significant; MD Anderson, Texas Children's, and Houston Methodist all have international patient services for global patients seeking US specialty care.

Texas SB 1264 (2019) provides surprise billing protections; the Texas Department of Insurance handles billing complaints. The Texas Medical Disclosure Panel and the Texas AG's Consumer Protection Division also handle billing fraud. For Houston patients receiving surprise out-of-network bills, dispute the bill in writing with the hospital, file a complaint with TDI, and invoke No Surprises Act protections for ERISA-covered care. Self-pay rates at Houston hospitals are typically 30-50 percent below the chargemaster, and prompt-pay discounts of 25-40 percent are routine.

When should I use Texas Medical Center hospitals vs community hospitals in Houston?

The Texas Medical Center (TMC) is the world's largest medical complex with extraordinary specialty depth at MD Anderson (cancer), Texas Children's (pediatrics), Houston Methodist (cardiology), and Memorial Hermann's TMC campus (trauma, transplant). For complex conditions, rare diseases, cancer care, advanced cardiac procedures, transplant surgery, complex pediatric care, or experimental treatments, TMC institutions are the right clinical choice despite higher pricing. For routine care, Houston's hospital systems outside the TMC core (CHI St. Luke's Health, HCA Houston Healthcare, Memorial Hermann community campuses, Memorial Hermann Texas Medical Center campus minus the academic services) offer 20-40 percent below TMC academic premium pricing. Insurance networks vary by employer plan; many Houston employer plans include both TMC and community hospital networks at different cost-sharing levels. Verify your plan's preferred network before scheduling elective procedures.

What is Harris Health and how do I qualify for charity care?

Harris Health System is the Harris County safety-net hospital system, anchored by Ben Taub Hospital and Lyndon B. Johnson Hospital. Harris Health provides charity care to uninsured Harris County residents through the Harris Health Financial Assistance Program. Eligibility: Harris County residence (proof required: lease, utility bill, voter registration), household income below 200 percent of FPL for full charity care (sliding scales above), and US citizenship or qualifying immigration status (DACA recipients qualify). To enroll: visit harrishealth.org or call the Financial Assistance enrollment line. Application processing typically takes 30-60 days. Harris Health provides comprehensive primary care, specialty care, hospital care, and behavioral health services. For non-emergency conditions, Harris Health enrollment provides much lower out-of-pocket costs than traditional uninsured care at TMC institutions or community hospitals. Ben Taub serves as the Houston region's safety-net trauma center.

Balance billing protections and patient rights near Houston

Texas's SB 1264 (2019) provides balance billing protections for emergency services and for out-of-network providers at in-network facilities. The law uses a baseball-style arbitration process for billing disputes between insurers and providers. The federal No Surprises Act covers ERISA employer plans that the state law cannot reach. The Texas Department of Insurance enforces the state protections.

Texas has not expanded Medicaid under the ACA, leaving approximately 1.4 million adults in the coverage gap statewide. Texas Medicaid covers pregnant women, children, and disabled adults, but non-disabled adults without dependent children earn too much for Medicaid and too little for marketplace subsidies. Harris County's uninsured population relies heavily on Harris Health System's charity care programs.
