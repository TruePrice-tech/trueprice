What Houston residents ask about painting

How much does it cost to paint kitchen cabinets?

Professional cabinet painting costs $3,000-$7,000 for an average kitchen. The process includes removing doors, degreasing, sanding, priming, spraying 2-3 coats, and reinstalling hardware.

How much does interior painting cost per room?

A typical room costs $300-$700 for walls and ceiling. A full interior (5-7 rooms) runs $2,500-$6,000. Trim, doors, and closets add $100-$300 per room.

Is spray painting better than brush and roller?

Spraying is faster and gives a smoother finish on large, flat surfaces. Brush and roller are better for detail work and touchups. Many pros spray first, then back-roll for adhesion.

How often should a house be painted?

Wood siding needs repainting every 5-7 years. Stucco and fiber cement last 8-12 years between coats. Vinyl and aluminum siding rarely need painting. Climate, sun exposure, and paint quality affect timing.

Climate and paint durability in Houston

Houston paint exposure is moderate UV (latitude 29.8N) but extreme humidity stress year-round (60-85% RH). South-facing Hardie-plank and stucco facades in Bellaire and West U typically last 6-8 years on premium acrylic before mildew intrusion overtakes gloss retention. Humid subtropical climate promotes mildew and algae faster than any other major Texas metro. Heights pier-and-beam wood clapboard lasts 4-6 years on less-than-premium product. Hurricane-season wind-driven rain (Harvey 2017, Beryl 2024) creates episodic wash-out damage on any freshly painted surface.

Houston exterior moisture stress is severe year-round. Humidity above 70% sustains mildew growth on any painted surface without mildewcide. North-facing Hardie and stucco facades develop visible mildew within 12-18 months of paint. Concrobium Mold Control and Zinsser Jomax House Surface Cleaner are standard pre-wash treatments. Mildewcide-enhanced paint (SW Duration Mold Armor, Ben Moore Aura Exterior with mildewcide) is the Houston premium spec. Pine-pollen staining is a March-April specific issue.

Prep culture and methods: Houston edition

Houston prep culture emphasizes aggressive power-washing because humidity-driven mildew is universal. Hardie-plank tract homes typically need only surface cleaning and spot-priming. Heights pre-1978 bungalows require EPA RRP-certified crews for lead safety. Memorial Villages stucco work requires elastomeric crack-bridging (Dryvit Finest, Sto Premium) before topcoat. Clay-foundation-movement-driven stucco hairlines are a specific Houston prep concern that must be addressed with elastomeric before painting. Pine-tree-sap stains on siding need specific stain-blocking primer.

Houston: permits and lead paint rules

Decoding exterior painting costs in Houston, TX

Why pricing varies in Houston. Contractor labor rates in Houston are roughly average for the US. The real pricing variation between quotes here comes down to scope interpretation, material tiers, and contractor overhead. On exterior painting, Houston's pricing closely mirrors the national average in our data.

Local conditions. Houston has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Mildew and algae staining are the #1 paint complaint in Houston. Any exterior paint job here must use 100% acrylic latex with mildewcide additive — skipping the mildewcide saves $50-100 per gallon but guarantees black streaking within 18 months. Houston homes averaging 36 years old typically have 1-3 paint layers in good condition. Prep is simpler — power wash, spot-prime bare spots, and apply 2 coats of finish. This keeps the overall project cost lower than older homes with heavy prep requirements.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most exterior painting projects in Houston require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. As a major metro of 2,195,914 residents, the local contractor market is deep. Expect 10+ legitimate bidders on any standard residential job. The Houston area's booming growth means contractors have more work than they can handle. This shifts pricing power toward the contractor side of the table.

Houston, TX area exterior painting: local intel

Home stock in Houston. Houston's homes, averaging 36 years, are entering the window where original-equipment replacements become unavoidable. This is the sweet spot for exterior painting work since systems are aging but the homes are modern enough to avoid major code-upgrade surprises.

HOA & compliance. If you live in one of Houston's many HOA-governed neighborhoods, get architectural approval before signing a exterior painting contract. The committee's material and color restrictions may narrow your options, so check first and avoid paying for an unapproved choice.

Local market conditions. Houston is growing fast, and the construction trades have not kept pace with housing demand. This means longer wait times, less willingness to negotiate on price, and fewer available quotes to compare. Start your search early. Houston's large market supports both premium and budget painting contractors. Prices vary 30-50% for the same scope — the difference is usually prep quality, not paint brand. Ask every bidder to detail their prep process in writing.

Seasonal timing. In Houston, the best painting conditions are October through March — cooler temperatures and lower humidity allow proper curing. Summer painting is possible but requires early-morning starts and careful attention to direct sun exposure during application.

Neighborhood patterns. In Houston, the highest volume of exterior painting work tends to come out of The Heights, Montrose, River Oaks, and West University — larger lots, older homes, and higher home values all drive replacement demand. Contractors familiar with these neighborhoods often already know the typical building stock, permit nuances, and HOA quirks.

Geography note. Buffalo Bayou, Space Center Houston, Hermann Park no zoning code citywide, which creates an unusually mixed housing fabric and makes comparable pricing tricky between adjacent blocks These geographic realities subtly shape contractor pricing and availability in Houston.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Houston track within a few points of the national average for exterior painting, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. At 36 years average home age, Houston properties are hitting their first major replacement cycle for systems and components. exterior painting demand is at peak levels in this age band, which keeps contractor schedules full but pricing competitive. High construction demand in Houston creates a seller's market for exterior painting contractors. Booking 3-4 weeks ahead is typical; emergency or rush jobs carry 15-25% premiums.

Red flags to watch for. Watch for exterior painting quotes in Houston that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Lump-sum bids hide margin and make change orders impossible to evaluate. Check that any Houston contractor doing exterior painting carries both general liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers' compensation. Request certificates directly from the insurer, not just copies the contractor provides. In fast-growing Houston, some contractors take on more work than they can handle. Ask about their current project count — a reputable exterior painting contractor runs 2-4 jobs simultaneously, not 10-15.
