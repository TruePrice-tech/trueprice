How Much Does Attic Insulation Cost?

Attic insulation in 2026 costs $1.50-$5.50 per sq ft installed. Blown cellulose $1.50-$2.50, blown fiberglass $1.20-$2.20, open-cell spray foam $2.50-$4.50, closed-cell spray foam $3.50-$5.50. A 1,500 sq ft attic to R-49: $2,800-$5,800 for blown, $5,500-$8,500 for spray foam.

R-Value Targets by Climate Zone

The Department of Energy's recommended attic R-values in 2026:

  • Zones 1-2 (Hot south): R-30 to R-49
  • Zones 3-4 (Mid-South, Mid-Atlantic, Pacific NW): R-38 to R-49
  • Zones 5-7 (Northeast, Midwest, Mountain): R-49 to R-60

For most of the U.S., R-49 is the practical target — diminishing returns kick in above R-49 in attics, and the marginal cost gets steeper. Going from R-30 to R-49 captures most of the available savings; going from R-49 to R-60 captures another 10-15% with significantly more material.

What Moves the Price Beyond Square Footage

Existing insulation removal. If the attic has rodent contamination, water damage, or vermiculite (which may contain asbestos and requires testing): removal and disposal can run $1,500-$5,000 on top of new insulation. Most healthy existing insulation can be topped up without removal, saving this cost.

Air sealing. Insulation works best with air sealing — sealing gaps around plumbing penetrations, recessed lights, attic hatches, and dropped soffits. Standalone air sealing: $400-$1,200. Bundled with new insulation: typically $200-$600 add-on. Worth doing — air sealing is often more impactful than the marginal R-value beyond R-30.

Access difficulty. Standard attic access (pull-down stairs, walkable space): standard pricing. Tight access (small hatch, knee-walls, scuttle hole only): +15-30% labor. Cathedral ceilings or vaulted attics with no air gap require entirely different approach (typically spray foam against the rafters) — separate quote category.

Baffles for soffit vents. Required to maintain ventilation when blowing insulation against the eaves. $5-$15 per baffle, 8-20 baffles per typical attic. Should be itemized on the quote.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does attic insulation cost in 2026?

$1.50-$5.50 per sq ft installed depending on type. Blown cellulose $1.50-$2.50, fiberglass $1.20-$2.20, open-cell spray foam $2.50-$4.50, closed-cell spray foam $3.50-$5.50.

How much does blown-in attic insulation cost?

$1.20-$2.50 per sq ft installed. For a 1,500 sq ft attic adding R-30 over existing: $1,800-$3,200. Full top-up to R-49: $2,800-$5,800.

What R-value do I need for an attic?

DOE 2026 targets: R-30 to R-49 (hot south), R-38 to R-49 (mid-South), R-49 to R-60 (cold North). R-49 is the most common target nationally.

Is spray foam attic insulation worth the cost?

Worth it for rim joists, vaulted ceilings, and sealed attics. For straightforward top-up over a flat ceiling, blown insulation is more cost-effective. Closed-cell foam is 2-3x cellulose per R-value but adds air sealing.

How much will attic insulation save on energy bills?

R-19 to R-49 typically saves 10-20% on heating, 5-15% on cooling in cold climates. Payback period 6-12 years on a $3,500 job for a $2,500/year heating bill.
