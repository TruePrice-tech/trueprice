How Much Does an Electrician Cost?

Licensed electricians charge $75 to $150 per hour in 2026 for residential work. Most jobs run $200 to $5,000 total: panel upgrades $2,500-$4,500, EV charger installation $600-$2,500, ceiling fan install $200-$450, full home rewire $8,000-$15,000. Permits add $50-$300 and are required for any panel or new-circuit work.

Hourly Rate vs Job Pricing

Like plumbers, most reputable electricians use flat-rate pricing on common jobs and hourly billing on diagnostic or unusual work. The 2026 hourly range for a licensed electrician is $75-$150, with the median around $115/hour. High-cost metros run $130-$180; rural and small-market work runs $65-$95.

Service-call fees are typically $75-$150 just to dispatch the truck. Reputable shops credit toward repair work; less reputable shops stack a separate diagnostic fee on top.

Permits Are Not Optional

Any work involving the panel, new circuits, service changes, or wiring upgrades requires a permit and inspection. Permit fees run $50-$300 depending on jurisdiction; inspection fees add another $50-$150. A licensed electrician pulls the permit; a "handyman" or unlicensed worker can't.

The cost of skipping permits — beyond fines if caught — is real. Unpermitted electrical work voids most homeowners insurance for fire damage. It also has to be disclosed at home sale, where it can become a financing obstacle for buyers. If a quote is significantly lower than competitors and excludes permits, that's almost always why.

Common areas where unpermitted work shows up: basement finishes (added circuits without permit), DIY hot tub installs (240V circuit not inspected), garage conversions (added outlets/lighting). When buying a home, ask the seller for permit records on any electrical work done in the past 10 years.

EV Charger Install: What Drives the Price

Level 2 EV charger installation cost varies more than most electrical jobs because it depends entirely on three factors: distance from the panel, panel capacity, and whether utility upgrades are needed.

Easy install ($600-$900): 200-amp panel in garage with at least 50 amps spare capacity, charger mounting within 10 feet of panel, exposed conduit acceptable. Most attached-garage homes built post-2000 fall here.

Moderate install ($1,000-$1,800): Panel in basement or utility room, charger mounting 30-60 feet away, conduit run through finished walls or attic. Requires more wire and more labor for the run.

Hard install ($1,800-$2,500): Long conduit run across the house, exterior conduit, or panel in inconvenient location. Often needs a sub-panel near the charger location.

Plus panel upgrade ($2,500-$4,500 additional): If the existing panel is at capacity (very common in 100-amp panels), it has to be upgraded before the charger circuit can be added. This is the single biggest cost surprise on EV charger quotes.

The federal Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling Property Credit (Section 30C) covers 30% of EV charger install cost up to $1,000 for residential installations through 2032.

How to Tell If Your Electrician Quote Is Fair

Three quotes from licensed local electricians for the same scope should cluster within 15-20%. A quote significantly lower usually means: no permits included, "handyman" labor rate for what's actually a licensed job, or material substitution (cheaper aluminum wire vs copper, off-brand breakers vs manufacturer-matched).

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an electrician cost per hour in 2026?

Licensed electricians charge $75 to $150 per hour in 2026 for residential work. Apprentices run $40-$70/hour. Master electricians and specialty work (low-voltage, fire alarm, solar interconnect) run $120-$200/hour. Most companies have a minimum service charge of $100-$200 even for short visits.

How much does it cost to upgrade an electrical panel?

A 200-amp panel upgrade runs $2,500 to $4,500 in 2026, including the new panel, breakers, meter base if needed, permit, and inspection. Upgrading to 400-amp service runs $5,500-$9,500.

What does it cost to install an EV charger?

Level 2 EV charger installation runs $600 to $2,500 depending on distance from the panel and whether the panel has spare 240V capacity. The federal credit covers 30% of install cost up to $1,000.

How much does it cost to rewire a house?

Full rewire of a 2,000 sq ft home runs $8,000 to $15,000 in 2026. Cost is driven by accessibility — finished walls require fishing wire through cavities or opening drywall, which adds 30-50% over an unfinished basement install.

Do electricians require permits?

Yes for any work involving the panel, new circuits, service changes, or aluminum wiring. Permits typically cost $50-$300 plus inspection fees of $50-$150. Work without permits can void homeowners insurance and create issues at home sale.
