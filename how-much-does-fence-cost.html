How Much Does a Fence Cost?

Fence cost in 2026 per linear foot installed: chain link $18-$30, wood $25-$45, vinyl $30-$55, aluminum $35-$65, wrought iron $40-$80, composite $45-$75. A typical 1/4 acre backyard (200-300 linear feet) runs $5,000-$13,500 for wood, $6,000-$16,500 for vinyl. Gates are priced separately at $200-$800 each.

What Moves the Price Beyond Material

Height. 4-foot fence is the baseline residential height (front yards, decorative). 6-foot is standard privacy. 8-foot is rare and often requires a permit and engineering. 6-foot is roughly 35-50% more than 4-foot for the same material.

Posts and post spacing. Standard residential post spacing is 6-8 feet. Closer spacing (5-6 feet) increases material and labor about 10-15% but is appropriate for windy areas, slope, or premium privacy fencing. Concrete-footed posts are standard; pounded steel posts are cheaper but only suitable for chain link.

Slope and ground. Flat ground with normal soil: standard pricing. Sloped ground (stepped or racked panels): +10-20% labor. Rocky or hard clay soil that requires augering or jackhammering for post holes: +15-30%. The contractor should walk your property and price these explicitly — beware quotes given over the phone without a site visit for any non-flat property.

Gates. Standard 4-foot pedestrian gate: $200-$400. Double drive gate (10-12 feet wide): $500-$1,200 for wood/vinyl, $1,500-$3,000 for wrought iron. Self-closing hardware adds $80-$200 per gate. Pool-code gates (for pool enclosure compliance) have specific latch and self-closing requirements; expect $300-$600 each.

Removal of existing fence. $3-$8 per linear foot, plus disposal fees. If the existing fence has concrete footings to remove, expect the higher end. Some contractors offer free removal if you're using them for the new install — worth asking.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a fence cost per linear foot?

2026 installed pricing: chain link $18-$30, wood $25-$45, vinyl $30-$55, aluminum $35-$65, wrought iron $40-$80, composite $45-$75. Add 10-25% for steep slope, hard ground, or existing-fence removal.

How much does a fence cost for a typical backyard?

1/4 acre lot fenced on three sides is 200-300 linear feet. Chain link $3,600-$9,000, wood $5,000-$13,500, vinyl $6,000-$16,500, wrought iron $8,000-$24,000. Gates priced separately at $200-$800 each.

What's the cheapest fence material?

Chain link at $18-$30/linear foot installed. For privacy: pressure-treated wood at $25-$35/linear foot. Vinyl is more expensive upfront but often cheapest on a 25-year basis (no maintenance, 30+ year life).

Do I need a permit to install a fence?

Usually yes — especially for fences over 6 feet, front-yard fences, or HOA neighborhoods. Permits run $50-$200. Check city code for height limits and setbacks.

How long does fence installation take?

200-300 linear feet typical: 1-3 days chain link, 2-4 days wood, 1-3 days vinyl, 3-5 days wrought iron. Concrete needs 24-48 hours to cure before panels go on.
