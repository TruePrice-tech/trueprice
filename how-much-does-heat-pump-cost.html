How Much Does Heat Pump Cost

HVAC costs $3,700 to $11,500 nationally in 2026 for a typical project. Pricing depends on size of system (tonnage), efficiency (SEER2 rating), and whether ductwork already exists. Most homeowners pay toward the middle of that range for standard scope, with premium materials or custom work pushing prices higher.

These ranges reflect national averages. Your actual cost depends on size of system (tonnage), efficiency (SEER2 rating), and whether ductwork already exists. City-specific pricing varies up to 30% in higher-cost metros (San Francisco, Seattle, Boston, New York) and 10-15% lower in smaller markets (Memphis, Little Rock, Des Moines).

What Should Be Included

A complete hvac quote should itemize:

  • equipment
  • labor
  • permits
  • disposal of old unit
  • start-up and calibration
  • basic duct adjustments

If any line is missing or lumped into a generic "labor" charge, ask the contractor to break it out. Scope omissions are the most common way quotes come in artificially low.

Red Flags to Watch in Quotes

Experienced homeowners watch for these specific patterns that signal a quote needs follow-up questions:

  • deposits over 30%
  • refrigerant or permit fees "TBD"
  • vague model numbers
  • missing manufacturer warranty details
