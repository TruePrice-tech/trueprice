How Much Does a Kitchen Remodel Cost?

Kitchen remodels in 2026 fall into three tiers: minor refresh $19,000-$30,000, mid-range full remodel $30,000-$60,000, upscale $75,000-$150,000+. National average is around $40,000. Cabinets typically consume 30-35% of the budget; appliances 14-20%; labor 20-30%. Layout changes that move plumbing, electrical, or walls add $5,000-$15,000 over an in-place remodel.

Where the Money Actually Goes

For a typical $50,000 mid-range remodel, the breakdown is:

  • Cabinets — 30-35% ($15,000-$17,500). The single biggest line item. Stock cabinets ($150-$250 per linear foot installed), semi-custom ($250-$500), full custom ($500-$1,200+). A typical 25-foot linear-foot kitchen needs $6,250-$12,500 in stock cabinets or $12,500-$25,000 in semi-custom.
  • Appliances — 14-20% ($7,000-$10,000). Mid-range package (refrigerator, range, dishwasher, microwave, hood) runs $5,000-$10,000. Premium package ($12,000-$25,000). Built-in or commercial-style appliances ($25,000+).
  • Labor — 20-30% ($10,000-$15,000). General contractor labor for demolition, install, finishing. Skilled trades (plumber, electrician) usually included; specialty work (custom tile, fabricator coordination) may be extra.
  • Countertops — 10-15% ($5,000-$7,500). Quartz $50-$110/sq ft installed; granite $50-$120; butcher block $40-$70; concrete $80-$140; marble $60-$200; soapstone $70-$150. Typical kitchen has 30-50 sq ft of counter.
  • Flooring — 7-10% ($3,500-$5,000). LVT/LVP $4-$10/sq ft installed; ceramic tile $7-$15; hardwood $8-$18; engineered wood $7-$14.
  • Plumbing/electrical/lighting — 5-8% ($2,500-$4,000). New under-cabinet lighting, recessed cans, possibly new sink/disposal, possibly new outlets for code (kitchens require GFCI on all counter outlets).
  • Backsplash — 2-4% ($1,000-$2,000). Subway tile $10-$20/sq ft installed; mosaic $15-$30; natural stone $25-$50.
  • Permits and design — 2-5% ($1,000-$2,500). Permit ($200-$1,500 depending on jurisdiction); design services if used ($1,000-$5,000 for room-only design, $5,000-$15,000 for full project design).

Where Remodels Actually Overrun (And How to Prevent It)

Scope creep. Once walls open and the project momentum is rolling, owners often decide to add or change items. "Since the wall is open, can we also...?" is the most expensive sentence in remodeling. Lock all decisions before demolition starts; treat any mid-project addition as a deliberate change order with cost approval.

Hidden conditions. Water damage behind cabinets, knob-and-tube wiring discovered during electrical work, asbestos in older flooring, undersized plumbing rough-in. These can't be fully predicted. Budget 15-25% contingency on top of the contractor's quote specifically for unknowns.

Decision delays. Selecting cabinets, countertops, tile, fixtures, appliances takes longer than people expect. Custom cabinets have 8-12 week lead times; if you decide on cabinets after demolition starts, the kitchen sits unfinished while you wait. Make all material selections before starting; finalize colors/finishes within 2 weeks of project start.

Appliance availability. Specialty appliances (Sub-Zero refrigerators, Wolf ranges, certain dishwashers) have 6-16 week lead times in 2026. Order before construction starts.

Cabinet damage. Cabinets damaged during shipping or install can delay completion by weeks. Inspect cabinets immediately on delivery; reject any with damage rather than installing and dealing with replacement later.

What Should Be in a Kitchen Remodel Quote

A complete kitchen remodel quote should specify, line by line:

  • Demolition and disposal scope
  • Cabinet brand, line, door style, finish, and quantity (with linear footage)
  • Countertop material, edge profile, and square footage
  • Appliance package (each appliance specified by brand and model, or "owner-supplied" with installation included)
  • Flooring material and square footage
  • Tile backsplash material, installation pattern, and square footage
  • Lighting fixtures (under-cabinet, recessed, pendant) by quantity and type
  • Plumbing rough-in changes (if any) — every fixture relocation adds $500-$1,500
  • Electrical rough-in changes — new circuits, GFCI requirements, panel work if needed
  • Drywall repair and painting
  • Permits and inspection fees
  • Project timeline with milestone dates
  • Payment schedule (typical: 10-25% deposit, draws at milestones, 10% holdback until punch list complete)
  • Allowances vs fixed-cost items (allowances are estimates; if you exceed, you pay the difference)
  • Change-order process and pricing
  • Warranty terms (1 year on workmanship is standard; manufacturer warranties on appliances/materials separate)

How to Tell If Your Kitchen Remodel Quote Is Fair

Three quotes from licensed contractors with similar reputations should cluster within 15-25% for the same scope. Wider spreads usually mean different scope assumptions, different material grades, or one contractor pricing high to deter the project.

Allowances are where comparison gets tricky — one contractor may quote $50,000 with a $5,000 cabinet allowance while another quotes $55,000 with a $10,000 cabinet allowance. The first will balloon when you select real cabinets; the second is honest about typical cabinet cost. Compare apples to apples by specifying real cabinet brand/line in each quote rather than relying on allowances.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a kitchen remodel cost in 2026?

Minor refresh runs $19,000-$30,000. Mid-range full remodel runs $30,000-$60,000. Upscale runs $75,000-$150,000+. National average around $40,000.

What is the breakdown of kitchen remodel costs?

For a typical $50,000 mid-range remodel: cabinets 30-35%, countertops 10-15%, appliances 14-20%, labor 20-30%, flooring 7-10%, plumbing/electrical/lighting 5-8%, permits and design 2-5%.

Are kitchen remodels worth the cost?

At resale: minor remodels recoup 70-85% of cost; mid-range 60-70%; upscale 50-60%. If selling within 2 years, minor refresh has the best ROI.

Where do kitchen remodels go over budget?

Three places: scope creep (mid-project additions), hidden conditions (water damage, undersized electrical), and decision delays (extends crew time). Budget 15-25% contingency.

How long does a kitchen remodel take?

Minor refresh: 2-3 weeks. Mid-range full remodel: 6-10 weeks. Upscale or layout change: 10-16 weeks. Custom cabinets are usually the long-pole item.
