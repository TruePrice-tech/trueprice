How Much Does a New Roof Cost in 2026?

A full roof replacement typically costs between $8,500 and $25,000 depending on material, roof size, and where you live. Here is what you should expect to pay and how to make sure you are getting a fair deal.

National Average Roof Replacement Cost

In 2026, the national average for a complete roof replacement falls between $12,000 and $15,000 for a standard single-story home with asphalt shingles. That number shifts significantly based on material choice, roof complexity, and your local labor market.

Roof Cost by Material

Material is the single biggest factor in what you will pay. Here is how the most common options compare for a typical 2,000 square foot roof:

Material Cost Range Lifespan
3-Tab Asphalt Shingles $7,000 – $11,000 15 – 20 years
Architectural Shingles $9,000 – $16,000 25 – 30 years
Standing Seam Metal $15,000 – $30,000 40 – 70 years
Clay or Concrete Tile $18,000 – $35,000 50 – 100 years
Wood Shake $14,000 – $25,000 20 – 40 years
Slate $25,000 – $50,000+ 75 – 150 years

Architectural shingles have become the default choice for most homeowners. They cost slightly more than basic 3-tab shingles but last significantly longer and carry better wind ratings. Factors That Affect Roof Replacement Cost

Beyond material and size, several other variables can push your quote higher or lower:

  • Roof pitch: Steeper roofs require more safety equipment and slow the crew down. A 12/12 pitch roof can cost 20-30% more than a low-slope roof.
  • Layers to remove: Tearing off an existing layer adds $1,000 to $2,500. Two layers means even more labor.
  • Decking repairs: Rotted or damaged decking (plywood sheathing) typically costs $50 to $100 per sheet to replace, and you will not know the full extent until the old shingles come off.
  • Complexity: Dormers, valleys, skylights, chimneys, and multiple levels all increase labor time and material waste.
  • Location: Labor rates vary dramatically. A roof in San Francisco can cost twice as much as the same roof in rural Alabama. Check our city pricing guides for local numbers.
  • Permits and code requirements: Some municipalities require permits ($200 to $500) and specific underlayment or ventilation upgrades.
  • Time of year: Late fall and winter are typically slower seasons for roofers, which can mean better pricing.

When Should You Replace Your Roof?

Not every roof problem requires a full replacement. But there are clear signals that repairs will not cut it anymore:

  • Your roof is approaching or past its expected lifespan (20+ years for asphalt shingles).
  • You see widespread curling, cracking, or missing shingles — not just a few isolated spots.
  • Granule loss is heavy (check your gutters for sandy buildup).
  • You notice sagging in the roofline, which can indicate structural issues.
  • Multiple leaks have appeared, especially after storms.

How to Save Money on a New Roof

A roof is one of the biggest home expenses, but there are legitimate ways to bring the cost down without cutting corners:

  • Get at least three quotes. Prices for the same scope can vary by 30% or more between contractors. Make sure each quote covers the same materials and scope of work.
  • Time your project. Booking in late fall or winter — when roofers have lighter schedules — can sometimes yield 10-15% savings.
  • Ask about material tiers. Some contractors default to premium materials when a mid-grade product would perform just as well for your climate and home.
  • Check for manufacturer rebates. Shingle manufacturers occasionally run promotions or rebates, especially in spring.
  • Review every line item. Upload your quote to our quote analyzer to spot overcharges and missing items before you sign.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average cost of a new roof in 2026?

Most homeowners pay between $8,500 and $25,000 for a full roof replacement in 2026. The national average is around $12,000 to $15,000 for a standard asphalt shingle roof on a 1,700 to 2,200 square foot home. Material choice, location, and roof complexity all influence the final price.

How long does a roof replacement take?

A typical asphalt shingle roof replacement takes 1 to 3 days for an average-sized home. Metal or tile roofs may take longer due to the additional labor involved. Weather delays can extend the timeline, so plan for a buffer if you are scheduling during rainy season.

Does a new roof increase home value?

Yes. A new roof typically recovers 60% to 70% of its cost at resale, according to industry estimates. Beyond the dollar value, a new roof makes a home significantly easier to sell and can lower insurance premiums in many states.
