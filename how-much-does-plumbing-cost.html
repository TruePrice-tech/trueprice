How Much Does Plumbing Cost

Plumbing costs $450 to $8,500 nationally in 2026 for a typical project. Pricing depends on whether the job is a repair, a replacement, or a full repipe, plus material choice (PEX vs copper) and accessibility of existing pipes. Most homeowners pay toward the middle of that range for standard scope, with premium materials or custom work pushing prices higher.

Your actual cost depends on whether the job is a repair, a replacement, or a full repipe, plus material choice (PEX vs copper) and accessibility of existing pipes. City-specific pricing varies up to 30% in higher-cost metros (San Francisco, Seattle, Boston, New York) and 10-15% lower in smaller markets (Memphis, Little Rock, Des Moines).

What Should Be Included

A complete plumbing quote should itemize:

  • labor time
  • materials (pipe, fittings, fixtures)
  • permits if applicable
  • disposal of old materials
  • pressure testing

If any line is missing or lumped into a generic "labor" charge, ask the contractor to break it out. Scope omissions are the most common way quotes come in artificially low.

Red Flags to Watch in Quotes

Experienced homeowners watch for these specific patterns that signal a quote needs follow-up questions:

  • hourly rate without cap
  • no parts itemization
  • deposits over 30%
  • "upon arrival" diagnostic fee without credit
