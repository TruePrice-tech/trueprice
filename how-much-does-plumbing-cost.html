How Much Does Plumbing Cost?

Plumbing in 2026 falls into three tiers. Small jobs (clogs, leaks, single fixtures): $150-$650. Mid jobs (water heater, toilet, single-bathroom repipe): $400-$3,500. Big jobs (whole-home repipe, sewer line, slab leak): $4,500-$18,000. Plumber hourly rate is $85-$185 with $75-$200 service-call fees.

Plumbing Cost by Job Tier

Most "how much does plumbing cost" questions are really questions about a specific job — and plumbing job costs cluster into three useful tiers.

Tier 1: Small jobs ($150-$650)

One-visit jobs where a single plumber fixes a single thing in 30 minutes to 2 hours. Examples: clogged drain ($150-$450), leaky faucet repair ($150-$300), garbage disposal install ($250-$500), toilet flapper replacement ($150-$280), single fixture replacement ($300-$650). These usually include the service call fee in the flat-rate quote.

Tier 2: Mid jobs ($400-$3,500)

Half-day to full-day jobs requiring some planning, possibly a permit, and 1-2 plumbers. Examples: water heater replacement ($1,200-$3,500), toilet replacement ($400-$900), single-bathroom rough-in ($1,800-$3,500), sewer line snake/clear ($400-$1,200), sump pump replacement ($650-$1,500), tankless water heater install ($2,500-$5,500). These are the bills people most commonly query — and the tier with the most quote variance, so getting a second quote is worth the time.

Tier 3: Big jobs ($4,500-$18,000)

Multi-day projects that often touch structural elements (walls, floors) and require permits. Examples: whole-home repipe in PEX ($4,500-$9,500), whole-home repipe in copper ($8,000-$18,000), main sewer line replacement ($4,500-$15,000), slab leak repair ($1,500-$8,000+ depending on access), full bathroom rough-in for new construction ($4,500-$9,500). Always get 3 quotes for jobs in this tier.

Plumber Hourly Rates in 2026

Licensed plumbers charge $85-$185/hour, with $130/hour as the rough national median. Apprentices and helpers run $40-$70/hour. Master plumbers and specialty work (gas lines, backflow certification, commercial) run $150-$250/hour. Geography matters: Bay Area, NYC metro, Boston sit at the top of the range; smaller markets sit at the bottom.

Where Plumbing Bills Inflate

Three patterns explain most "why is my plumbing bill so high" complaints:

Parts markup. Plumbing parts are wholesale-priced; service plumbers typically mark up 100-300% over wholesale. Industry-standard, but the markup should be visible on the bill — a line that says "parts: $480" without itemization is hiding markup. A reasonable bill lists each part with quantity and unit price.

Time padding. Hourly billing rounded up. A 90-minute job billed as 2 hours is normal; a 90-minute job billed as 3.5 hours is padding. Standard book times exist for common jobs — if the bill claims significantly more time than book, ask why.

Scope creep. Discoveries during the job ("the shut-off valve was corroded; the supply line behind the wall was leaking") added without prior approval. Reasonable practice: any added scope over $100 should be approved in writing before continuing. Unsigned change orders are a question to ask now, not later.

How to Get a Fair Plumbing Quote

For non-emergency work, get 3 quotes for the same scope. They should land within 15-20% of each other; an outlier high or low usually indicates different scope assumptions or a contractor who really wants (or doesn't want) the job. For emergency work, vet on the phone: ask service-call fee, credit policy, hourly rate, and whether they offer flat-rate pricing on common jobs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does plumbing cost in 2026?

Three tiers: small jobs $150-$650, mid jobs $400-$3,500, big jobs $4,500-$18,000. Hourly labor rate $85-$185, service-call fees $75-$200.

What's the average plumbing repair cost?

Median 2026 plumbing repair is $400-$650. About 70% of residential calls fall in this range. Water heaters and main sewer issues are the two most common drivers above median.

How much does it cost to replumb a house?

$4,500-$9,500 for PEX; $8,000-$18,000 for copper. PEX is the most common 2026 choice, faster to install and roughly half the cost of copper.

Is plumbing more expensive on weekends?

Yes. Weekend, evening, and holiday rates are typically 1.5-2x weekday hourly. Service call fees may also be elevated. Wait until a weekday for non-emergencies.

How can I tell if my plumbing quote is too high?

Check hourly rate vs $85-$185 local range, parts markup vs wholesale (100-300% is standard, more is excessive), and whether scope creep over $100 is approved in writing.
