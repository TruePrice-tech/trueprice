How Much New Roof Cost

Roof replacement costs $9,000 to $30,000 nationally in 2026 for a typical project. Pricing depends on square footage of roof (usually 1.2× house sq ft), material chosen, and complexity of the roofline. Most homeowners pay toward the middle of that range for standard scope, with premium materials or custom work pushing prices higher.

These ranges reflect national averages. Your actual cost depends on square footage of roof (usually 1.2× house sq ft), material chosen, and complexity of the roofline. City-specific pricing varies up to 30% in higher-cost metros (San Francisco, Seattle, Boston, New York) and 10-15% lower in smaller markets (Memphis, Little Rock, Des Moines).

What Should Be Included

A complete roof replacement quote should itemize:

  • tear-off of old roofing
  • underlayment
  • flashing
  • ice/water shield
  • ridge vents
  • disposal

If any line is missing or lumped into a generic "labor" charge, ask the contractor to break it out. Scope omissions are the most common way quotes come in artificially low.

Red Flags to Watch in Quotes

Experienced homeowners watch for these specific patterns that signal a quote needs follow-up questions:

  • no tear-off line item
  • flashing excluded
  • underlayment not specified
  • warranty from contractor under 5 years
