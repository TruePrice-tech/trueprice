How Much Does a New Roof Cost?

By Geoff Lane, founder of Woogoro · Updated April 2026

A new roof costs $9,000 to $16,000 in 2026 for a typical 2,000 sq ft home with architectural asphalt shingles, or $15,000 to $30,000 with standing-seam metal. Per "square" (100 sq ft of roof), pricing runs $350-$600 for shingles and $600-$1,200 for metal. The biggest cost variable isn't size — it's complexity (valleys, dormers, multiple levels) and whether the existing deck needs replacing.

Why "Per Square" Matters More Than Total Cost

Roofers price by the "square" — 100 square feet of roof surface — and that's the right unit to compare quotes. Two contractors quoting a $14,000 total for the same house may have very different per-square pricing if one is including roof deck replacement and the other isn't. Always ask for the per-square breakdown.

Roof surface is meaningfully larger than your home's floor area. A 2,000 sq ft single-story home with a moderate 6:12 pitch has roughly 25 squares of roof (including overhangs). A two-story 2,000 sq ft home has only ~14 squares because the second floor sits on top of the first. This is why a larger floor plan with two stories often costs less to roof than a smaller single-story sprawl.

What Drives Variation Within Each Material

Roof complexity. Cut-up rooflines with valleys, dormers, hips, and intersecting planes take 30-50% more labor than a simple gable. Complexity drives more flashing, more cuts, more underlayment, more waste — and more places for water to find a way in if installation isn't tight.

Roof pitch. Steep roofs (above 9:12) require harness work, slower pacing, and sometimes special equipment. Expect 15-25% premium over a 6:12 walkable pitch. Very low pitches (under 4:12) require special underlayment for water resistance, adding 5-15%.

Tear-off vs overlay. Tear-off (removing existing shingles to expose deck) costs $1-$2 per sq ft on top of the new roof. Overlay (laying new shingles over old) is cheaper but only allowed if the deck and existing shingles are in good shape, and most jurisdictions limit you to one overlay before requiring tear-off. Overlay also adds weight and shortens new shingle life.

Deck replacement. If the roof deck (plywood or OSB sheathing) is rotted from leaks, it has to be replaced before new roofing goes on. Partial deck replacement runs $70-$120 per sheet of plywood. Whole-roof deck replacement on a 25-square home can add $3,000-$6,000.

What Should Be in a Roofing Quote

A complete roofing quote itemizes:

  • Tear-off and disposal (or overlay if applicable) — usually broken out as line item
  • Roof deck inspection with a per-sheet rate for any rotted plywood replacement
  • Underlayment — synthetic preferred over felt; specified by manufacturer name
  • Ice and water shield on eaves, valleys, around penetrations (required by code in cold climates)
  • Drip edge on eaves and rakes (required by most building codes since 2012)
  • Flashing at chimneys, walls, vents — replaced new, not reused (reused flashing is the #1 source of post-install leaks)
  • Ridge vents for attic ventilation — sized to NFA (net free area) requirements
  • Shingles — manufacturer and product line specified, not just "30-year shingles"
  • Cleanup and disposal — including magnetic sweep for nails

If any of these are missing or lumped under generic "labor," ask for them to be itemized. Missing ice and water shield, undersized ventilation, and reused flashing are the three most common scope omissions that bite homeowners later.

Insurance Claim vs Out-of-Pocket: When to Use Which

If your roof is failing from age (worn shingles, granule loss, age beyond warranty), insurance won't cover it. Replacement is out-of-pocket and you're shopping competitively across contractors.

If your roof has sudden damage (hail, wind, fallen tree) and your policy includes wind/hail coverage at replacement cost, an insurance claim usually makes more sense than out-of-pocket. Your insurance typically covers full replacement value minus your deductible. Get the claim approved BEFORE picking a contractor — the adjuster's scope-of-loss document defines what's covered.

Be cautious of "storm chaser" contractors who knock on doors after a major weather event, promise to handle the insurance claim, and offer to "eat the deductible." Eating the deductible is insurance fraud in most states; it can void your claim and put you on the hook for the full repair. Reputable contractors collect the deductible from you separately.

How to Tell If Your Roofing Quote Is Fair

Three quotes from licensed local contractors that cluster within 15% of each other suggests you're seeing fair market pricing. A quote that's 30%+ below the others is usually missing scope (excluded tear-off, no ice and water shield, "TBD" deck repair, reused flashing). A quote that's 30%+ above the others is either a high-end contractor with longer warranty or simply pricing high.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new roof cost in 2026?

For a typical 2,000 sq ft single-story home with a moderate-pitch roof, expect $9,000 to $16,000 for architectural asphalt shingles and $15,000 to $30,000 for standing-seam metal. Tile or slate runs $20,000 to $60,000+. The single biggest variable is roof complexity — multi-level homes with valleys, dormers, and chimneys can run 30-50% more than a simple gable roof of the same square footage.

What is a "square" in roofing?

A roofing "square" equals 100 square feet of roof surface. A typical 2,000 sq ft home has roughly 22-30 squares of roof (more than the floor area because roof surface is angled and includes overhangs). Most contractors price by the square. Asphalt shingles in 2026 run $350-$600 per square installed; standing-seam metal runs $600-$1,200 per square.

Should I file an insurance claim for a new roof?

Only if you have storm damage that meets your policy's threshold. Insurance covers sudden damage (hail, wind, fallen trees), not gradual wear or age. If you have hail damage and your policy is replacement-cost (not actual-cash-value), insurance typically covers full replacement minus your deductible. Be cautious of contractors who promise to "eat the deductible" — that's insurance fraud in most states.

How much does it cost to replace a roof on a 1500 sq ft house?

For a 1,500 sq ft single-story home (typically 18-22 squares of roof), asphalt shingle replacement runs $7,000-$13,000. Metal roof replacement runs $13,000-$24,000. Smaller homes don't scale linearly — minimum job costs (mobilization, dump fees, permits) mean you don't save 25% by being 25% smaller. Per-square-foot cost actually rises slightly on smaller jobs.

How long should a new roof last?

3-tab asphalt shingles last 15-20 years. Architectural (dimensional) asphalt shingles last 20-30 years. Premium asphalt shingles (impact-rated) last 30-50 years. Standing-seam metal lasts 40-70 years. Concrete tile lasts 50+ years. Real-world life is heavily affected by attic ventilation — poorly vented attics shorten any roof's life by 25-40% from heat and moisture damage.
