How to compare roofing quotes

The cheapest roofing quote is not always the best value. A fair comparison looks at total price, cost per square, materials, scope, warranty, flashing, ventilation, and cleanup so you can compare apples to apples.

Start with price per square, not just total price

A roofing square equals 100 square feet of roof area. Two quotes can have very different totals simply because the roof measurements are different. Before comparing anything else, check the total roof size and calculate price per square.

What to compare in every roofing quote

1. Material quality

Compare the exact shingle, metal, or tile product. One contractor may be quoting a lower tier product that looks similar but performs differently.

2. Tear off and disposal

Make sure removal of the old roof, dump fees, and cleanup are included. Missing disposal costs can make a quote look artificially low.

3. Underlayment and leak protection

Check whether synthetic underlayment, ice and water shield, and starter materials are clearly listed.

4. Flashing work

Ask whether step flashing, pipe boots, chimney flashing, wall flashing, and drip edge are included or simply reused.

5. Ventilation

Ventilation is often ignored in low quotes. Ridge vent, intake, and attic airflow can affect both roof life and warranty value.

6. Warranty

Separate workmanship warranty from manufacturer warranty. A long manufacturer warranty does not automatically mean labor issues are covered.

Quick roofing quote checklist

Roof size and measurements clearly stated
Material brand and product line listed
Tear off and disposal included
Underlayment specified
Flashing details included
Ventilation work specified
Cleanup and magnet sweep included
Workmanship warranty listed separately
Permit responsibility explained
Payment schedule clearly stated

Example of a bad comparison

Quote Total price Looks cheap? What might be missing
Contractor A $11,500 Yes Flashing, ventilation, disposal, better underlayment
Contractor B $14,400 No Full tear off, upgraded materials, warranty, cleanup

A low quote can become the most expensive job if scope gets added later or workmanship problems show up after installation.
