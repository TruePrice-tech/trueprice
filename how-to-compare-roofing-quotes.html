How to Compare Roofing Quotes

Many homeowners receive multiple roofing quotes and assume the cheapest one is the best value. That is often a mistake. The right way to compare roofing quotes is to compare the full scope of work, not just the headline price.

What to Compare First

  • Total project price
  • Roofing material and brand
  • Tear off and disposal scope
  • Underlayment details
  • Flashing replacement
  • Ventilation work
  • Workmanship and manufacturer warranty

Why the Lowest Quote Can Be Misleading

A low quote can look attractive until you realize it excludes decking assumptions, drip edge, ridge vent, chimney flashing, or disposal. Some contractors also use vague wording that makes change orders more likely later.

Questions to Ask Before Choosing a Contractor

  • What exactly is included in tear off and disposal?
  • Are flashing details fully specified?
  • What underlayment is being used?
  • Is ridge ventilation included?
  • What is the workmanship warranty?
  • How are decking repairs handled?
