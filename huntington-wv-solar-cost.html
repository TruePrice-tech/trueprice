Solar questions for Huntington homeowners

What is the difference between string inverters and microinverters?

String inverters are cheaper but the whole array drops output when one panel is shaded. Microinverters optimize each panel independently, cost 15-20% more, and last longer.

Should I buy or lease solar panels?

Buying maximizes long-term savings and increases home value. Leasing eliminates upfront cost but the leasing company keeps the tax credit, and panels may complicate home sales.

Does Huntington have any state or local solar incentives?

Incentives vary. Check the DSIRE database for Huntington-specific rebates, SRECs, property tax exemptions, and performance-based incentives that stack on top of the federal 30% ITC.

How does the federal solar tax credit work?

The Investment Tax Credit (ITC) lets you deduct 30% of your total solar installation cost from your federal taxes. It applies to purchased systems, not leased. There is no cap on the credit amount.

Is solar worth it in Huntington?

Solar is a strong investment for most Huntington homes, with typical payback in 6-10 years followed by 15-20 years of free electricity. The 30% federal tax credit cuts the net cost substantially. Your specific return depends on roof orientation, shading, energy consumption, and local utility rates. Three quotes with site-specific production estimates give you the data to decide.

What solar incentives are available in Huntington?

Every Huntington homeowner installing solar qualifies for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit with no dollar limit. Beyond that, WV may offer state tax credits, utility-specific rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits, and property tax exemptions. The DSIRE database has the current list for your area. These stacked incentives typically reduce net cost by an additional 10-30%.

When is the best time to install solar in Huntington?

Huntington homeowners should aim for early spring or fall to install solar. Moderate weather conditions for installation and you avoid the peak-demand scheduling crunch. Allow 6-12 weeks from signing a contract through permitting, utility interconnection approval, and installation. Start gathering quotes 2-3 months before your target window. Remember that the 30% ITC applies to the tax year in which installation is completed.
