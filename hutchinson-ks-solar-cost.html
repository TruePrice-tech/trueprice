Hutchinson solar: answers to common questions

What is the difference between string inverters and microinverters?

String inverters are cheaper but the whole array drops output when one panel is shaded. Microinverters optimize each panel independently, cost 15-20% more, and last longer.

Should I buy or lease solar panels?

Buying maximizes long-term savings and increases home value. Leasing eliminates upfront cost but the leasing company keeps the tax credit, and panels may complicate home sales.

Does Hutchinson have any state or local solar incentives?

Incentives vary. Check the DSIRE database for Hutchinson-specific rebates, SRECs, property tax exemptions, and performance-based incentives that stack on top of the federal 30% ITC.

How does the federal solar tax credit work?

The Investment Tax Credit (ITC) lets you deduct 30% of your total solar installation cost from your federal taxes. It applies to purchased systems, not leased. There is no cap on the credit amount.

Is solar worth it in Hutchinson?

The numbers work well for most Hutchinson homeowners. Typical payback runs 6-10 years, after which the system generates essentially free electricity for another 15-20 years. The 30% federal tax credit applies to the full installation cost with no dollar cap. Roof orientation, shading, usage patterns, and utility rate structure all affect individual payback. Start with the 30% federal ITC, which applies to all Hutchinson solar installations with no cap. Then check DSIRE for KS-level incentives: state tax credits, utility rebates, SREC markets, property tax exemptions, and net metering terms. Each utility has different programs and rates. What's the best season to install solar in Hutchinson?

The best installation window in Hutchinson is spring or fall, comfortable working temperatures and strong solar production begins immediately. Note that the process from quote to installation takes 6-12 weeks including permitting, utility approval, and scheduling. Start the process 2-3 months before your target installation date. The federal tax credit applies to the year of installation, so timing relative to tax year can matter.
