What Should an HVAC Quote Include?

  • Equipment (condenser + air handler or furnace)
  • Refrigerant line set
  • Thermostat (programmable or smart)
  • Ductwork modifications if needed
  • Electrical disconnect and wiring
  • Equipment pad
  • Condensate drain line
  • Filter rack
  • Permits and inspections
  • Old equipment disposal and refrigerant recovery
  • Warranty (equipment + labor)
  • Manual J load calculation

SEER Rating: Is Higher Worth It?

Higher SEER costs more upfront but saves on energy bills. Here is the tradeoff for a 3-ton central AC:

SEEREquipment + InstallEst. Annual Energy CostAnnual Savings vs 14Payback Period
14 SEER$3,300 - $5,500~$1,100Baseline--
16 SEER$3,700 - $6,300~$960~$140/yr3-6 years
18 SEER$4,800 - $8,000~$850~$250/yr6-10 years
20+ SEER$6,000 - $10,000~$770~$330/yr8-14 years

Sweet spot for most homeowners: 16 SEER. Best value with reasonable payback. Go 18+ only if you plan to stay 10+ years (note: the $2,000 federal 25C heat pump credit expired Dec 31, 2025; state and utility rebates may still apply).

Geothermal: The Premium Option

Geothermal heat pumps use the earth's constant temperature for heating and cooling. Highest upfront cost but lowest operating cost.

Home SizeTotal Installed CostAfter 30% Tax CreditAnnual Savings vs Gas
