Gutter Installation Cost in Idaho (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Idaho typically runs $1,100–$4,400, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 19 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Idaho mountain elevations from McCall to Sun Valley face heavy snow-load and ice-dam exposure with sustained sub-freezing winter weather — heavier-gauge aluminum K-style and steel-bracket spacing under 24 inches dominate mountain residential installations, and heat-cable retrofit budgets averaging $700-1,100 per home are standard on north-facing mountain eaves to prevent ice-dam-driven roof leaks.

Idaho rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 19 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 30 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: High — sustained sub-freezing winter requires ice-and-water shield + heat-cable retrofit budget
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: Moderate — mixed canopy, gutter guards optional

Idaho licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Idaho Contractors Board (registration only, no exam) (official site)
  • Permit: state or city building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction

How gutter installation costs vary in Idaho

State-specific code or insurance rule: Idaho Contractors Board issues a registration credential with no examination requirement — every gutter contractor must register with the Board, post a financial-responsibility statement, and disclose criminal history, but Idaho has no skills-based competency testing for gutter or general residential contracting, leaving construction-quality verification to municipal inspectors and homeowner contract review.
