Roof Replacement Cost in Idaho (2026)

Roof replacement in Idaho typically runs $7,800–$19,500 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. Sun Valley, McCall, and Bonner County mountain communities carry ground snow loads above 80 psf; engineered ridge ventilation and step-flashing at log-home profiles are the dominant cost drivers in upper-elevation work.

Idaho climate & code drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 5B-6B
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Hail risk tier: Low — minimal severe hail history
  • Ground snow load: 35 psf — moderate snow load, ice-and-water shield required
  • Dominant residential roof material: asphalt shingle

Idaho licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Idaho Contractors Board — registration only, no exam (official site)
  • Permit: city/county permit required

How roof replacement costs vary in Idaho

Sun Valley, McCall, and Bonner County mountain communities carry ground snow loads above 80 psf; engineered ridge ventilation and step-flashing at log-home profiles are the dominant cost drivers in upper-elevation work.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Idaho Code §54-5208 requires every roofer to be registered with a $4,000 cash bond or surety equivalent — no exam, but proof of liability and workers' compensation insurance must be filed before work begins.
