Foundation Cost in Illinois (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in Illinois typically run $21,800–$48,400 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with full basement pour as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 42-inch design frost line. Illinois's combination of 42-inch frost depth across Chicago and northern Illinois, F2 freeze-thaw exposure driving 5-7% air-entrainment specification, high Lake Michigan water table across Cook and Lake counties driving sump-pump plus dual backwater-valve installation on every basement pour, statewide radon zone 1 designation forcing sub-slab depressurization rough-in on every residential foundation, and southern Illinois New Madrid Seismic Zone influence in St. Clair, Madison, Monroe, Randolph, Jackson, Williamson, and Pulaski counties forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC C-D0 seismic detailing makes Illinois one of the most foundation-engineering-intensive states in the country.

Illinois climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 42-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Full basement — dominant in Upper Midwest, Northeast, and Mid-Atlantic on freeze-thaw + radon-zone-1 sites
  • Expansive soil risk: Moderate — periodic clay shrink-swell, standard footing reinforcement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC C — moderate seismic risk, continuous-rebar-tie footings required
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Water table risk: High — periodic water table within footing depth, sump-pump rough-in standard
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S1 — moderate sulfate exposure, ASTM C150 Type II cement required
  • IECC climate zone: 5A-6A
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 1 (highest) — passive sub-slab depressurization rough-in required on every basement and slab pour

Illinois licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide foundation contractor license — Cook County, City of Chicago, and most home-rule municipalities issue Building Contractor licenses (official site)
  • Permit: city building permit + Cook County Stormwater Management permit required on every residential foundation pour; Illinois Plumbing Code mandatory backwater valve and sump-pump on every basement pour; Chicago radon-resistant new construction mandatory under City Ordinance 18-6-A

How foundation pour costs vary in Illinois

Illinois's combination of 42-inch frost depth across Chicago and northern Illinois, F2 freeze-thaw exposure driving 5-7% air-entrainment specification, high Lake Michigan water table across Cook and Lake counties driving sump-pump plus dual backwater-valve installation on every basement pour, statewide radon zone 1 designation forcing sub-slab depressurization rough-in on every residential foundation, and southern Illinois New Madrid Seismic Zone influence in St. Clair, Madison, Monroe, Randolph, Jackson, Williamson, and Pulaski counties forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC C-D0 seismic detailing makes Illinois one of the most foundation-engineering-intensive states in the country.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Illinois is one of the few Midwest states without a statewide residential builder license — Cook County, City of Chicago, and most home-rule municipalities issue municipal Building Contractor licenses but no statewide examination or bond is required, and Illinois Plumbing Code 77 IL Adm Code 890 separately mandates a backwater valve plus dual sump-pump installation on every Illinois residential basement pour to prevent combined-sewer backup flooding, with City of Chicago Ordinance 18-6-A additionally requiring radon-resistant new construction sub-slab depressurization rough-in on every Chicago residential basement and slab pour.
