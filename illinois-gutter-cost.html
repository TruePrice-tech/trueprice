Gutter Installation Cost in Illinois (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Illinois typically runs $1,500–$5,800, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 39 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Illinois's 39-inch annual rainfall concentrated in spring and summer thunderstorm cells overwhelms undersized 5-inch K-style on Chicago-area suburbs with mature oak and elm canopy — and Lake Michigan lake-effect winter snow-load on north-facing Cook and Lake County eaves drives gutter-bracket spacing under 24 inches and ice-cable retrofit budgets averaging $500-900 per home.

Illinois rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 39 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 25 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: Moderate — periodic ice-dam events on north-facing eaves
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: Moderate — mixed canopy, gutter guards optional

Illinois licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide gutter contractor license — Chicago and Cook County issue local credentials (official site)
  • Permit: Chicago building permit not typically required for gutter-only installation; suburban Cook County issues independently

How gutter installation costs vary in Illinois

State-specific code or insurance rule: Illinois 415 ILCS 5/22.18b (Roof Drainage Act) is the only state law specifically requiring residential gutter discharge to be directed away from neighboring property and storm sewers when those storm sewers are at-capacity — making Illinois homeowners subject to civil action by neighbors and municipalities for roof runoff that contributes to flooding on adjacent parcels, with Chicago and Cook County enforcing the rule through stormwater management ordinances.
