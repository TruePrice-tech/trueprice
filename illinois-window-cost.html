Window Replacement Cost in Illinois (2026)

Window replacements in Illinois typically run $7,400–$19,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Northern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.3, no SHGC limit). Illinois's combination of 42-inch frost depth across Chicago and northern Illinois, F2 freeze-thaw cycling driving sealant degradation, persistent Lake Michigan salt-spray-equivalent (winter de-icing salt + lake-effect humidity) on every Cook, Lake, and DuPage county exposure, very-high pre-1978 housing share (50%+ across the state) driving mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every Illinois residential window replacement, City of Chicago Landmark Designation review on every window replacement within Chicago's 60+ designated historic districts, and southern Illinois New Madrid Seismic Zone influence drives unique cold-climate-and-historic-Chicago-driven glazing selection.

Illinois climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Northern — coldest climates, U-factor priority (U ≤ 0.27 in coldest tier)
  • IECC climate zone: 5A-6A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.3 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: No limit (cold climate)
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 105 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: High — multiple state-mapped historic districts with mandatory review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Very high — 60%+ pre-1978 housing share, mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every replacement

Illinois licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide window license — Cook County, City of Chicago, and most home-rule municipalities issue Building Contractor licenses (official site)
  • Permit: city building permit required for window opening modification on new construction; Cook County and Chicago municipal licenses required; replacement-only same-size installs typically permit-exempt; Chicago Landmark Designation review required on every historic-district install

How window replacement costs vary in Illinois

State-specific code or insurance rule: Illinois is one of the few Midwest states without a statewide residential builder license — Cook County, City of Chicago, and most home-rule municipalities issue municipal Building Contractor licenses with Chicago specifically requiring a separate Class B General Contractor license with bond and examination, plus City of Chicago Landmark Designation Ordinance separately requires Commission on Chicago Landmarks review on every window replacement within Chicago's 60+ designated historic districts (Old Town, Lincoln Park East, Hyde Park, Pullman, Bronzeville, etc.) to ensure profile, glazing, and grille-pattern continuity with the surrounding 19th-century architectural overlay, with mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every Chicago, Aurora, Joliet, Naperville pre-1978 residential window replacement.
