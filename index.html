How to Compare Roofing Quotes

Many homeowners receive multiple roofing estimates and struggle to determine which contractor is offering the best value. The TruePrice roofing quote comparison tool helps analyze estimates by price per square, materials, and warranty coverage.

Check price per square

Roofing contractors often price by the square, which equals 100 square feet. Comparing quotes by price per square helps normalize different bid formats.

Review material differences

A lower quote may use lower grade materials, while a higher quote may include upgraded shingles, underlayment, or ventilation components.

Compare warranty value

Contractor workmanship warranties and manufacturer warranties can materially change the value of a roofing quote.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions homeowners ask when evaluating roofing quotes.

What is a roofing square?

One roofing square equals 100 square feet of roof area. Contractors often use price per square to quote jobs.

How do I know if a roofing quote is too high?

Compare it against a fair range based on your roof size, materials, and job complexity. Then compare contractor quotes side by side.

Should I pick the cheapest roofer?

Not automatically. Very low quotes can reflect weaker materials, missing line items, or thin margins that create future risk.

What should be included in a roofing quote?

A strong quote should clearly identify material type, tear off, disposal, underlayment, flashing, ventilation, warranty, and total price.
