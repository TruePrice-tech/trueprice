Foundation Cost in Indiana (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in Indiana typically run $16,400–$38,400 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with full basement pour as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 36-inch design frost line. Indiana's combination of 36-inch frost depth across northern Indiana, F2 freeze-thaw exposure driving 5-7% air-entrainment specification, and statewide radon zone 1 designation across the northern half (39 of 92 counties classified zone 1) drives sub-slab depressurization rough-in on every northern Indiana residential foundation pour, with the Indianapolis metro plus Hamilton, Hancock, Hendricks, Johnson, and Madison counties additionally requiring continuous footing reinforcement on every residential foundation pour to manage F2 freeze-thaw exposure across the Central Indiana glacial-till plain.

Indiana climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 36-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Full basement — dominant in Upper Midwest, Northeast, and Mid-Atlantic on freeze-thaw + radon-zone-1 sites
  • Expansive soil risk: Low — sandy or stable subsoil, standard slab-on-grade design
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC B — minor seismic risk, standard tie-down requirements
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Water table risk: Moderate — water table below frost line in most lots
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • IECC climate zone: 5A
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 1 (highest) — passive sub-slab depressurization rough-in required on every basement and slab pour

Indiana licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide foundation contractor license — Marion County (Indianapolis), Hamilton County, and other home-rule municipalities issue local Builder Contractor credentials (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential foundation pour; IN Indiana Building Code 675 IAC mandatory; statewide Indiana radon zone 1 designation across northern half drives sub-slab depressurization rough-in

How foundation pour costs vary in Indiana

Indiana's combination of 36-inch frost depth across northern Indiana, F2 freeze-thaw exposure driving 5-7% air-entrainment specification, and statewide radon zone 1 designation across the northern half (39 of 92 counties classified zone 1) drives sub-slab depressurization rough-in on every northern Indiana residential foundation pour, with the Indianapolis metro plus Hamilton, Hancock, Hendricks, Johnson, and Madison counties additionally requiring continuous footing reinforcement on every residential foundation pour to manage F2 freeze-thaw exposure across the Central Indiana glacial-till plain.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Indiana is one of the few large states without a statewide residential builder license — Marion County, Hamilton County, and other Indiana home-rule municipalities issue local Builder Contractor credentials, but no centralized state board oversees foundation contractor competency or bond requirements, leaving Indianapolis and Indianapolis suburb homeowners to rely on municipal license history rather than a state-board examination check, plus Indiana Code §16-41-39 separately enforces statewide radon-resistant new construction in 39 northern Indiana counties (radon zone 1) requiring sub-slab depressurization rough-in on every residential basement and slab pour.
