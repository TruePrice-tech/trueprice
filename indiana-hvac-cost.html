HVAC Replacement Cost in Indiana (2026)

HVAC replacement in Indiana typically runs $7,900–$18,000 for a complete 3-ton system replacement, with natural-gas furnaces with central AC as the dominant heating choice and roughly 5,500 heating degree days against 1,300 cooling degree days driving the seasonal load mix. Indiana's coal-region utility rate structure (NIPSCO, Duke, Vectren, AES Indiana) keeps natural gas heat at 30-40% of the operating cost of electric resistance heat, which makes pure heat-pump retrofits a slow sell — dual-fuel systems (HP + gas backup at a 25-35°F balance point) are the regional sweet spot.

Indiana climate & load drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A
  • Annual load split: Heating-dominant — annual heating load exceeds cooling load (5,500 HDD / 1,300 CDD)
  • Dominant heating fuel: Natural gas — piped utility supply through state pipeline network
  • Dominant cooling system: Central air conditioning paired with separate heating equipment

Indiana licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: Indiana — no statewide HVAC license; Indianapolis (Marion County), Fort Wayne, and Evansville require local plumbing/HVAC certification with municipal exams (official site)
  • Permit: municipal mechanical permit required

How HVAC replacement costs vary in Indiana

Indiana's coal-region utility rate structure (NIPSCO, Duke, Vectren, AES Indiana) keeps natural gas heat at 30-40% of the operating cost of electric resistance heat, which makes pure heat-pump retrofits a slow sell — dual-fuel systems (HP + gas backup at a 25-35°F balance point) are the regional sweet spot.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Indiana has no statewide HVAC contractor license — installer credentialing falls entirely to municipal building departments, with Indianapolis requiring a separate plumbing/HVAC class certificate and bond, while smaller counties accept any registered general contractor for mechanical work over $1,000.
