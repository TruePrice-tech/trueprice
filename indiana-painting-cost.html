Exterior Painting Cost in Indiana (2026)

Repainting a 1,500 sq ft home in Indiana typically runs $2,800–$7,600, with wood lap or shake siding the dominant residential substrate driving prep scope. Indiana's combination of 60%+ summer humidity, central-Midwest pre-1978 housing stock concentration in Indianapolis, Gary, and South Bend, and 80+ annual freeze-thaw cycles drives EPA RRP Lead-Safe Certified work on the majority of urban Indiana residential repaint scope — and central Indiana corn-pollen surge in late July contaminates fresh latex topcoats on Indianapolis-Lafayette suburban repaints, requiring contractor scheduling around the pollen window.

Indiana climate & coatings drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A
  • Dominant residential substrate: Wood lap or shake — dominant pre-1990 substrate, drives full prep + caulk + prime + 2-coat
  • Annual humidity tier: High — extended summer humidity drives mildewcide-additive topcoats
  • UV exposure: Moderate — typical continental UV, standard binder packages adequate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: No salt-air corrosion exposure (interior continental)
  • Freeze-thaw cycling: High — 60-100 freeze-thaw cycles drive sealant inspection on every repaint
  • VOC architectural coatings rule: Federal default — 250 g/L flat exterior topcoat limit (EPA 40 CFR Part 59)

Indiana licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide painting license — Indianapolis, Fort Wayne, Evansville issue municipal contractor credentials (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for repaint; Indianapolis Class A Improvement Contractor registration required for any city contract above $5,000

How exterior repaint costs vary in Indiana

Indiana's combination of 60%+ summer humidity, central-Midwest pre-1978 housing stock concentration in Indianapolis, Gary, and South Bend, and 80+ annual freeze-thaw cycles drives EPA RRP Lead-Safe Certified work on the majority of urban Indiana residential repaint scope — and central Indiana corn-pollen surge in late July contaminates fresh latex topcoats on Indianapolis-Lafayette suburban repaints, requiring contractor scheduling around the pollen window.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Indiana has no statewide painting or general contractor licensing — Indiana Code §25-2.7 explicitly preempts state credentials in favor of municipal regulation, with Indianapolis Department of Business & Neighborhood Services, Fort Wayne Neighborhood Code Enforcement, and Evansville Building Commission each maintaining separate Class A/B contractor registration schedules requiring $10,000 surety bonds on residential exterior contracting work.
