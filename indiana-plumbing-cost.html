Plumbing & Repipe Cost in Indiana (2026)

Plumbing repipe in Indiana typically runs $5,100–$12,900 for a whole-house 2-bath PEX repipe, with PEX-A or PEX-B cross-linked polyethylene the dominant retrofit choice and the International Plumbing Code (IPC) as the adopted enforcement basis. Buried service-line trench depth must clear the 32-inch design frost line. Indiana's limestone and dolomite aquifer system delivers groundwater with calcium-magnesium hardness frequently exceeding 20 grains-per-gallon across central and southern counties, scaling water-heater elements and reducing tankless heat-exchanger life — and Gary, Hammond, and East Chicago's pre-1986 lead service line corridor mirrors the broader Lake Michigan industrial-belt LSL exposure.

Indiana water-quality & freeze drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A — drives freeze-protection scope on supply lines
  • Frost-line trench depth: 32-inch design minimum for buried water service
  • Water hardness profile: Very hard (10.5+ grains/gallon) — whole-house softener standard on most repipes
  • Dominant retrofit supply material: PEX-A or PEX-B cross-linked polyethylene — dominant on whole-house repipes since 2005
  • Adopted plumbing code basis: International Plumbing Code (IPC) — ICC model adopted statewide
  • Lead service line risk tier: High — pre-1986 lead service line corridor under active LCRR replacement

Indiana licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Indiana Plumbing Commission (Professional Licensing Agency) — Journey Plumber / Plumbing Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: city or county plumbing permit required for repipe and water-heater installation

How plumbing and repipe costs vary in Indiana

State-specific code or insurance rule: Indiana Code §25-28.5-1-9 makes the Plumbing Contractor license dependent on first holding a Journey Plumber credential plus four years of post-license experience — a layered-credential pathway stricter than most Midwestern states, with the Plumbing Commission separately empowered under §25-28.5-1-19 to deny reciprocity from any state lacking equivalent journey-license requirements.
