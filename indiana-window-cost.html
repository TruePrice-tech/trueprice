Window Replacement Cost in Indiana (2026)

Window replacements in Indiana typically run $5,800–$14,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Northern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.3, no SHGC limit). Indiana's combination of 36-inch frost depth across northern Indiana, F2 freeze-thaw cycling, ENERGY STAR Northern climate zone driving low-U-factor (≤0.30) glazing requirement statewide, high pre-1978 housing share (40%+ across the state) driving mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every Indiana residential window replacement, and southern Indiana Tornado Alley exposure drives moderate cold-climate-and-historic-housing-driven glazing selection.

Indiana climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Northern — coldest climates, U-factor priority (U ≤ 0.27 in coldest tier)
  • IECC climate zone: 5A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.3 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: No limit (cold climate)
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 110 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Moderate — selected metro historic districts with review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): High — 40-60% pre-1978 housing share, EPA RRP required on most replacements

Indiana licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide window license — Marion County (Indianapolis), Hamilton County, and other home-rule municipalities issue local Builder Contractor credentials (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification; municipal license required; replacement-only same-size installs typically permit-exempt; high pre-1978 housing share drives mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on Indianapolis-area replacements

How window replacement costs vary in Indiana

Indiana's combination of 36-inch frost depth across northern Indiana, F2 freeze-thaw cycling, ENERGY STAR Northern climate zone driving low-U-factor (≤0.30) glazing requirement statewide, high pre-1978 housing share (40%+ across the state) driving mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every Indiana residential window replacement, and southern Indiana Tornado Alley exposure drives moderate cold-climate-and-historic-housing-driven glazing selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Indiana Code §22-12 leaves residential window licensing entirely to municipal Building Departments with Marion County, Hamilton County, Allen County, and Lake County issuing local Builder Contractor credentials but no centralized state board oversees window contractor competency or bond requirements, leaving Indianapolis-area homeowners to rely on municipal license history rather than a state-board examination check, plus EPA RRP lead-safe certification mandatory on every Indiana residential window replacement in pre-1978 housing across Indianapolis, Fort Wayne, Evansville, South Bend, and Gary historic neighborhoods.
