Plumbing in Indianapolis: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Indianapolis has very hard water, which accelerates mineral buildup in water heaters and reduces their lifespan by 2-4 years. A whole-house water softener or tankless unit with a descaling system is strongly recommended.

❄

Freeze risk

In Indianapolis, pipe freezing is a moderate-to-high risk during the coldest months. Make sure you know where your main water shutoff valve is — quick action when a pipe bursts can save thousands in damage.

🔧

Material recommendation

PEX piping is the top recommendation for repiping in Indianapolis. It's flexible, resists freezing better than rigid pipes (it expands slightly), and typically costs 30-40% less than copper to install.

💡

Local tip

Many Indianapolis homes built before 1970 have galvanized steel pipes that corrode from the inside out. If your water pressure has been steadily dropping or you see rusty water, a whole-house repipe is likely needed.
