Common solar questions in Indianapolis

Can I add a battery to my solar system?

Yes. Adding a home battery (10-13 kWh) costs $8,000-$15,000 installed. Batteries provide backup power during outages and can help avoid peak-rate charges in time-of-use markets.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

Do solar panels increase home value?

Owned solar systems add roughly $15,000-$20,000 to home value according to national studies. Leased systems do not add value and can complicate the sale process.

Indianapolis Utility Rate and Net Metering Structure

AES Indiana residential solar operates under Indiana's Excess Distributed Generation (EDG) tariff (IC 8-1-40), which replaced 1-for-1 net metering in 2022 with credits at 125 percent of the average wholesale rate (approximately $0.04-$0.05 per kWh) rather than the retail rate of $0.12-$0.14. This is among the least favorable distributed generation tariffs in the Midwest and dramatically extends payback versus pre-2022 installations. CenterPoint Energy Indiana gas service is unrelated to solar economics. Sizing solar to 85-90 percent of annual consumption minimizes low-value exports under the EDG structure.
