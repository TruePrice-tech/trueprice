Interior Painting Cost Per Square Foot

Interior painting costs $2 to $6 per square foot of floor area for walls only with two coats. $3 to $8 with ceilings included. $5 to $10 with trim, doors, and detailed prep. The range reflects paint grade (builder vs premium), coat count (single vs two), prep complexity (smooth walls vs damage repair), and whether trim and ceilings are in scope.

Floor Area vs Wall Area: Why This Matters

Most painters quote by floor area in residential work because it's an easier number to capture. But what they're actually painting is wall area, which is 2.5-3.5x larger than floor area in most homes. A 1,500 sq ft home with 8-foot ceilings has roughly 4,500-5,000 sq ft of wall surface.

This matters when comparing quotes from different painters. One painter quoting $3.50 per sq ft of floor area is roughly equivalent to another painter quoting $1.10 per sq ft of wall area. Always confirm which basis the contractor is using before comparing prices.

For commercial or high-end work, painters typically quote per sq ft of wall area directly. Residential work usually quotes floor area or by-room.

The Difference Between One Coat and Two Coats

Two coats is the professional standard for any color change or covering darker existing paint over lighter. One coat is acceptable only when refreshing the same color over a clean, sound surface — and even then, two coats produces a meaningfully better finish.

Two-coat work costs roughly 60-80% more than one-coat. The prep is the same in both cases (cleaning, taping, masking, drop cloths, repair); only the application doubles. So a $3,000 one-coat job becomes a $5,000-$5,400 two-coat job.

Watch for quotes that say "one coat" for a color change. Either the painter is cutting corners, or the price is misleading because the customer will need to pay for a second coat once the first one shows uneven coverage.

Paint Quality: When to Pay More

Builder-grade paint (Behr Premium Plus, Valspar 4000, Sherwin-Williams ProClassic) costs $30-$50/gallon. Acceptable for low-traffic areas (closets, guest bedrooms, basements). Decent finish; touches up well; expected lifespan 5-7 years.

Mid-grade paint (Behr Marquee, Sherwin-Williams Emerald, Benjamin Moore Regal Select) costs $50-$70/gallon. Better coverage (often legitimate one-coat for color match), more washable, lasts 7-10 years. Standard for most professional residential work in 2026.

Premium paint (Sherwin-Williams Duration or Aura, Benjamin Moore Aura, Farrow & Ball) costs $70-$120/gallon. Best coverage, color depth, and durability. Worth the upgrade for kitchens, bathrooms, kids' rooms, hallways — areas with frequent washing or wear.

Premium paint adds $0.50-$1.50 per sq ft of floor area to the total. On a 1,500 sq ft home, that's $750-$2,250 — a meaningful upgrade that's often worth it for high-use areas and skippable for low-use ones.

Where Per-Sq-Ft Quotes Hide Costs

Trim work. Trim painting (baseboards, door casings, window casings, crown molding) is usually quoted separately or excluded from "walls only" pricing. Trim adds $1-$3 per linear foot in most homes — an average home has 200-400 linear feet of trim, so this adds $200-$1,200. Always ask whether trim is included.

Doors. Painting interior doors adds $50-$150 per door. A typical home has 8-15 interior doors. Hollow-core doors paint faster than solid-core; both surfaces of a door doubles the cost. Painting just the visible side is acceptable but unusual professionally.

Damage repair. Filling nail holes is included in standard prep. Patching gouges, repairing drywall tape failure, or covering water stains is extra. A heavy-prep room (older home with multiple repair areas) can add $200-$600 to a single room.

Furniture moving. Painters typically expect rooms to be empty or with furniture moved to the center and covered. Heavy furniture moving is a separate charge ($50-$150 per room).

How to Compare Painting Quotes

Three painters quoting the same scope (same rooms, same coats, same paint grade, similar prep) should cluster within 20% of each other. Painters legitimately vary in productivity and overhead, so a wider spread isn't unusual. But quotes 30%+ apart usually mean different scope assumptions.

Get each quote in writing with: rooms included, walls/ceilings/trim/doors included or excluded, paint brand and product line, coat count, prep work scope, and furniture moving expectations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does interior painting cost per square foot in 2026?

$2 to $6 per square foot of floor area for walls only with two coats. $3 to $8 per square foot with ceilings included. $5 to $10 per square foot with trim, doors, prep work for damaged walls, or premium paint.

Is interior painting priced by floor area or wall area?

Most painters quote by floor area in homes (easier measurement) but actually paint wall area, which is typically 2.5-3.5x floor area. Always confirm which measurement basis the contractor is using.

What does 'one coat' vs 'two coats' paint cost?

Two coats is standard for any color change. Two-coat work costs roughly 60-80% more than one-coat. Quotes promising 'one coat' on a color change should be questioned — either corners are being cut or you'll pay for the second coat later.

Does paint quality matter for cost?

Yes meaningfully. Builder-grade ($40-$50/gallon) is fine for low-traffic areas. Premium paint ($70-$90/gallon) covers in fewer coats, lasts longer, washes better. Adds $0.50-$1.50 per sq ft — worth it for high-traffic areas, skippable for closets.

How much does it cost to paint a 1500 sq ft house interior?

For 1,500 sq ft with 8-foot ceilings: walls only, two coats, basic prep, mid-grade paint runs $3,000-$6,500. With ceilings included: $4,500-$9,000. With trim, doors, and detailed prep: $6,500-$13,500.
