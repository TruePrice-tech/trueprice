Exterior Painting Cost in Iowa (2026)

Repainting a 1,500 sq ft home in Iowa typically runs $2,600–$7,200, with vinyl panel (trim painting only on most homes) the dominant residential substrate driving prep scope. Iowa's 90+ annual freeze-thaw cycles combined with intense central-plains corn-pollen and soybean-dust contamination during the August-September harvest season measurably soils fresh latex topcoats on every Iowa exterior repaint — and Iowa's vinyl-siding-dominant residential stock since the 1990s shifts the typical Iowa painting scope toward trim-only painting on most homes built post-1995, with full exterior repaint demand concentrated in Des Moines, Cedar Rapids, and Davenport historic neighborhoods.

Iowa climate & coatings drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 5A-6A
  • Dominant residential substrate: Vinyl panel — paint scope reduced to wood trim, doors, and shutters on most homes
  • Annual humidity tier: High — extended summer humidity drives mildewcide-additive topcoats
  • UV exposure: Moderate — typical continental UV, standard binder packages adequate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: No salt-air corrosion exposure (interior continental)
  • Freeze-thaw cycling: Very high — 100+ annual freeze-thaw cycles drive caulk-and-recaulk on every repaint
  • VOC architectural coatings rule: Federal default — 250 g/L flat exterior topcoat limit (EPA 40 CFR Part 59)

Iowa licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Iowa Workforce Development — Construction Contractor Registration (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for repaint; Iowa Construction Contractor Registration required statewide above $2,000 annual gross

How exterior repaint costs vary in Iowa

Iowa's 90+ annual freeze-thaw cycles combined with intense central-plains corn-pollen and soybean-dust contamination during the August-September harvest season measurably soils fresh latex topcoats on every Iowa exterior repaint — and Iowa's vinyl-siding-dominant residential stock since the 1990s shifts the typical Iowa painting scope toward trim-only painting on most homes built post-1995, with full exterior repaint demand concentrated in Des Moines, Cedar Rapids, and Davenport historic neighborhoods.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Iowa Code §91C requires every painting contractor performing more than $2,000 in annual gross construction work in Iowa to register with Iowa Workforce Development — Iowa is unusual in tying contractor registration to annual gross rather than per-project value, capturing every full-time Iowa painting contractor with no examination, no surety bond requirement, and no recovery fund, leaving consumer recourse to the Iowa Attorney General Consumer Protection Division.
