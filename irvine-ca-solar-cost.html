Irvine solar: answers to common questions

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

Do solar panels increase home value?

Owned solar systems add roughly $15,000-$20,000 to home value according to national studies. Leased systems do not add value and can complicate the sale process.

How long does solar take to pay for itself in Irvine?

Payback in Irvine depends on electricity rates, sun hours, and incentives. Most systems pay back in 6-10 years and then generate free electricity for another 15-20 years.

What is the difference between string inverters and microinverters?

String inverters are cheaper but the whole array drops output when one panel is shaded. Microinverters optimize each panel independently, cost 15-20% more, and last longer.

How much solar energy can I produce in Irvine?

Irvine is one of the best locations in the country for residential solar production. A standard 8kW system produces 12,000-14,000 kWh annually, 25-35% more than average U.S. markets. Most Irvine homeowners can cover 80-100% of their electricity needs with a properly sized system. Plan for future consumption increases from EVs or pool equipment when determining system size.

What solar incentives are available in Irvine?

Start with the 30% federal ITC, which applies to all Irvine solar installations with no cap. Then check DSIRE for CA-level incentives: state tax credits, utility rebates, SREC markets, property tax exemptions, and net metering terms. Each utility has different programs and rates. The combined federal-plus-state incentive stack typically reduces net cost by 40-60% in markets with strong state programs.

When should I install solar in Irvine?

The best installation window in Irvine is fall or winter, cooler temperatures make attic work comfortable and your system starts producing before peak summer rates. Note that the process from quote to installation takes 6-12 weeks including permitting, utility approval, and scheduling. Start the process 2-3 months before your target installation date. The federal tax credit applies to the year of installation, so timing relative to tax year can matter.
