Plumbing in Irving: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

The water in Irving is on the harder side. You may notice mineral spots on fixtures. For water heaters, this means flushing the tank annually is essential to prevent sediment buildup.

❄

Freeze risk

Irving rarely freezes, but when it does, exposed pipes are especially vulnerable because homes here aren't built for cold weather. Insulate outdoor pipes and know your shutoff valve location just in case.

🔧

Material recommendation

For Irving's hard water, PEX piping is ideal — it doesn't corrode or develop pinhole leaks like copper can in mineral-rich water. It's also the most cost-effective option for whole-house repiping.

💡

Local tip

Homes built in Irving during the 1970s-80s often have copper pipes that may be developing pinhole leaks after 40+ years. If you're seeing green staining at joints, it's time for an inspection.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Irving?

Plumber rates in Irving typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
