Frequently asked landscaping questions

How much does outdoor lighting cost?

Professional landscape lighting costs $2,000-$5,000 for path lights, uplights, and accent lighting. LED fixtures cost more upfront but last 50,000+ hours. Solar options cost less but provide dimmer, less reliable light.

How much does a retaining wall cost?

Retaining walls cost $20-$50 per square face foot for concrete block, $25-$60 for natural stone, and $15-$30 for timber. A 50 linear foot wall at 3 feet tall runs $3,000-$9,000 installed.

What is xeriscaping?

Xeriscaping uses drought-tolerant plants, efficient irrigation, and permeable surfaces to minimize water use. It cuts outdoor water consumption 50-75% and is increasingly required in water-restricted regions.

Do I need a permit for landscaping in Jackson?

Most landscaping in Jackson does not require permits. Exceptions include retaining walls over 4 feet, grading that changes drainage, connecting to city water for irrigation, and work near property lines.

More questions about landscaping in Jackson

How much does mulch cost per yard?

Bulk mulch costs $25-$50 per cubic yard delivered. Plan 3 inches deep — one cubic yard covers about 108 sq ft. A 1,000 sq ft bed needs roughly 10 yards ($250-$500 in materials) plus $30-$50/yard for professional spreading and edging.

What does a sprinkler system cost in Jackson?

A residential irrigation system in Jackson costs $2,500-$5,000 for a quarter-acre lot with 4-6 zones. Smart wifi controllers add $100-$300 and cut water usage 20-40%. Drip-irrigation zones for planting beds cost $500-$1,000 extra and are worth it on shrubs and perennials.

How much does sod installation cost?

Sod costs $1-$2 per square foot installed including soil prep and rolling. Hydroseeding runs $0.05-$0.10/sqft and establishes in 4-6 weeks. Traditional broadcast seeding is the cheapest at $0.10-$0.25/sqft but needs 6-8 weeks and frequent watering. A 5,000 sq ft lawn lands between $5,000 and $10,000 in sod.

Do I need a permit for landscaping in Jackson?

Most landscaping in Jackson — planting, mulching, sod, basic irrigation — does not need a permit. Retaining walls over 4 feet, drainage tied to the storm system, tree removal in some neighborhoods, and any grading that changes lot drainage usually do. Confirm with your landscaper before they start digging.
