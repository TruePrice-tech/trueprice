Questions Johns Creek homeowners ask about solar

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

Do solar panels increase home value?

Owned solar systems add roughly $15,000-$20,000 to home value according to national studies. Leased systems do not add value and can complicate the sale process.

How long does solar take to pay for itself in Johns Creek?

Payback in Johns Creek depends on electricity rates, sun hours, and incentives. Most systems pay back in 6-10 years and then generate free electricity for another 15-20 years.

What is the difference between string inverters and microinverters?

String inverters are cheaper but the whole array drops output when one panel is shaded. Microinverters optimize each panel independently, cost 15-20% more, and last longer.

Is solar worth it in Johns Creek?

Solar is a strong investment for most Johns Creek homes, with typical payback in 6-10 years followed by 15-20 years of free electricity. The 30% federal tax credit cuts the net cost substantially. Your specific return depends on roof orientation, shading, energy consumption, and local utility rates. Three quotes with site-specific production estimates give you the data to decide.

What solar incentives are available in Johns Creek?

Every Johns Creek homeowner installing solar qualifies for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit with no dollar limit. Beyond that, GA may offer state tax credits, utility-specific rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits, and property tax exemptions. The DSIRE database has the current list for your area. These stacked incentives typically reduce net cost by an additional 10-30%.

Best time of year to install solar in Johns Creek?

Johns Creek homeowners should aim for fall or winter to install solar. Cooler weather makes roof work safer and the system is ready for the high-production spring and summer months. Allow 6-12 weeks from signing a contract through permitting, utility interconnection approval, and installation. Start gathering quotes 2-3 months before your target window. Remember that the 30% ITC applies to the tax year in which installation is completed.
