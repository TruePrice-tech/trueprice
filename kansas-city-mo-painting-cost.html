Most exterior painting jobs require at least two coats for proper coverage and durability. A single coat may work for touch-ups or if the existing paint is in good condition with a similar color.

Kansas City Climate and Paint Durability

Kansas City paint exposure combines moderate UV (latitude 39.1N) with 80 freeze-thaw cycles and 3-5 significant hail events per year that make it one of the most demanding paint environments in the Midwest. The May 2024 supercell hail event damaged exterior paint across Johnson County KS and southern KCMO. Severe hail routinely pits and cracks exterior coatings, driving repaint cycles as short as 3-4 years on exposed west-facing elevations. The metro straddles the Missouri-Kansas state line, creating different contractor licensing requirements, different building codes, and different historic preservation rules on each side of State Line Road.

KC exterior moisture stress is moderate with 39 inches of annual rainfall and summer humidity (60-80%). North-facing brick and clapboard facades develop mildew within 2-3 years without mildewcide. Expansive shale clay traps water against foundation walls, creating lower-wall efflorescence and paint failure in the bottom 12-18 inches. Concrobium Mold Control and Zinsser Jomax are the standard pre-wash treatments. Hail-damaged paint films admit moisture through impact craters that accelerate substrate deterioration if not repainted promptly.
