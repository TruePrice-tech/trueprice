Frequently Asked Questions

How much do solar panels cost in Kansas City?

Most homeowners in Kansas City pay between $13,005 and $63,140 before the 30% federal tax credit. After the credit, costs drop significantly.

Is the 30% federal solar tax credit still available?

Yes. The federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) provides a 30% tax credit on the total cost of your solar installation through 2032. It drops to 26% in 2033 and 22% in 2034.

How long does solar installation take?

Physical installation typically takes 1-3 days. However, the full process from contract signing to power-on can take 2-3 months due to permitting and utility interconnection.

Kansas City Utility Rate and Net Metering Structure

Evergy serves both the Missouri and Kansas sides of the KC metro, but net metering terms differ by state. Missouri's net metering statute (RSMo 386.890) provides 1-for-1 retail-rate credits. Kansas net metering under KSA 66-1265 provides credits at the avoided-cost rate (approximately 40-50 percent of retail), significantly less favorable. A KCMO-side system generates 40-50 percent more export value than an identical system in Overland Park. This cross-state tariff asymmetry is the single most important economic factor in KC solar and makes state-line confirmation critical before any quote.

Kansas City Interconnection and Grid Approval

Evergy interconnection for residential systems under 25 kW runs 4-8 weeks through the Evergy Net Metering portal (same portal for both states but different rate applications). KCMO Permits and Inspections Division issues solar permits for the Missouri side with 2-4 week turnaround. Overland Park, Olathe, and Lenexa on the Kansas side each have separate permit portals with 1-3 week turnaround. KCMO Historic Preservation Commission review on Country Club Plaza, Pendleton Heights, and Janssen Place contributing structures can add 4-6 weeks. Kansas-side municipalities have minimal historic protections.

Kansas City Solar Installer Landscape

The KC installer market is led by Good Energy Solutions (Lawrence KS-based), SunPower dealers, Sunrun, and Tesla Energy. Local firms include KC Solar and Sun Source Energy. The dual-state metro means installers need familiarity with both Missouri and Kansas permitting and net metering. Missouri requires electrical contractor licensing through the Division of Professional Registration; Kansas requires contractor registration through the AG's office. Kansas City Solar Incentives and Tax Credits

The 30 percent federal ITC is the anchor incentive on both sides. Missouri exempts solar from state sales tax (RSMo 144.030) and provides property tax exemption (RSMo 137.100) for 25 years. Kansas charges sales tax on solar (6.5 percent state plus local) and provides no property tax exemption. Evergy has phased out direct solar rebates on both sides. The net result: a KCMO-side installation saves $3,000-$5,000 more over the system life than an identical KS-side installation due to Missouri's superior net metering, sales tax exemption, and property tax exemption. This cross-state advantage is critical for any KC solar decision.

Kansas City Roof Types and Mounting Implications

KC rooftop stock is heavily composition shingle on post-1960 suburban housing in Overland Park, Olathe, and Lee's Summit. Country Club Plaza Spanish Revival commercial-adjacent residential uses tile roofing that adds 15-20 percent install cost. Brookside and Waldo bungalows have simple gable roofs with good solar-viable south-facing planes. Slate on Hyde Park and Valentine Victorian homes adds 25-30 percent. The 3-5 hail events per year make Class 4 hail-rated module selection important; some insurance carriers (State Farm, American Family) require hail-rated modules for homeowner insurance continuity in the KC hail corridor.

Kansas City HOA Rules and Solar Access Law

Missouri does not have a statewide solar access law. Kansas has limited solar access protections. KCMO Historic Preservation Commission reviews exterior modifications on Country Club Plaza, Pendleton Heights, and Janssen Place. Kansas-side master-planned communities (Corinth, Somerset, Park Place) have architectural review committees that can impose meaningful restrictions. The dual-state regulatory asymmetry means solar access protections differ block-by-block near State Line Road. HOA rules in Leawood and Prairie Village tend to be more restrictive than KCMO neighborhoods.

Kansas City Solar Permitting Process

KCMO Permits and Inspections issues solar permits with 2-4 week turnaround. Kansas-side municipalities (Overland Park, Olathe, Lenexa) each have separate portals with 1-3 week turnaround. Missouri electrical contractor license is required for MO-side work; Kansas contractor registration for KS-side work. An installer working both sides needs credentials in both states. KCMO Historic Preservation Commission review adds 4-6 weeks on designated properties. Evergy interconnection application should be filed in parallel with permitting to avoid serial delay.

Kansas City Battery Storage Options and Rebates

Battery storage adoption in KC is driven by severe weather resilience (the May 2024 hail event, annual derecho-season outages, and February 2021 Uri) and the cross-state net metering asymmetry. For Kansas-side systems where export credits are only 40-50 percent of retail, battery storage improves economics by shifting exported energy to self-consumption. Tesla Powerwall 3 and Enphase IQ Battery 10 dominate. Federal ITC at 30 percent applies. Neither Missouri nor Kansas offers a state battery rebate. Evergy has explored demand-response pilot programs.

Kansas City Climate Risks for Solar Installations

KC climate risks for solar are spring hail (3-5 events per year, with the May 2024 supercell producing documented module damage across Johnson County), severe thunderstorm wind uplift (ASCE 7-16 design wind 115 mph), tornado risk in the central US tornado corridor, and snow loading averaging 18 inches annual snowfall. Class 4 hail-rated module glass is increasingly required by KC-area insurance carriers. Hail-impact testing per IEC 61215 and UL 61730 is the standard spec. The March 2022 tornado outbreak reinforced the need for hurricane-rated racking fastening.

Kansas City Solar Project Timeline

Full timeline from contract signing to PTO in KC runs 10-16 weeks: 1-2 weeks site assessment and engineering, 2-4 weeks permitting (varies by municipality and state), 1-2 days installation, 1-2 weeks inspection, 4-8 weeks Evergy interconnection. The March-May installation window captures the full summer production peak but overlaps with hail season. Some installers defer spring installs to June-July to avoid post-hail rework. Fall (September-October) is an alternative window after hail season ends.
