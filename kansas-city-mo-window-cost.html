Energy performance spec for Kansas City

Kansas City sits in IECC climate zone 4A, a heating-dominant zone where window thermal performance directly affects winter energy bills. ENERGY STAR v7 targets Kansas City replacement windows at U-factor 0.27 maximum and SHGC 0.40 maximum. Evergy may offer $50-$75 per window rebates for qualifying ENERGY STAR installations.

Kansas City buildings with older single-pane windows lose significant heat through glass conductance. Triple-pane IGUs raise interior surface temperature above 60F and eliminate condensation damage on frames. Whole-house window replacement in Kansas City typically reduces HVAC energy use 15-25% versus single-pane or failed-seal IGU windows.

What failing windows look like in Kansas City

Glass package choices for Kansas City

Kansas City glass packages should target triple-pane with Low-E 272 on south exposures (passive, maximizing solar gain) and Low-E 366 on west exposures (solar control). Argon fill is baseline; krypton fill upgrades performance on triple-pane.

Wind, storm, and durability requirements for Kansas City

Kansas City storm considerations include winter wind pressure, ice loading on frames, and temperature differential stress on seals. Impact-rated glass is available for storm-prone locations.
