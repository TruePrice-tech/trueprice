Frequently asked concrete questions

How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

A Kenosha homeowner's guide to concrete work

How Kenosha pricing is built. Contractor labor in Kenosha commands a 18% premium over the national average, driven by local cost of living and trade demand. This wage differential flows directly into every quote you receive. For concrete work specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Kenosha at roughly 13% above the national midpoint.

What Kenosha's local context means for pricing. Kenosha has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Kenosha for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Standard concrete in Kenosha runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Permits, codes, and licensing in Kenosha. In Kenosha, concrete work projects typically require a permit. Confirm the current local process before signing a contract.

Kenosha's trade contractor landscape. At 99,889 residents, Kenosha has a moderate contractor base. Most homeowners can find 3-5 qualified bidders without searching beyond the immediate area. Moderate growth in Kenosha supports a balanced contractor market. Enough work to keep good shops busy, enough competition to keep pricing reasonable.
