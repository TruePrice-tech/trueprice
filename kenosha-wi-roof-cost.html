Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new roof cost in Kenosha?

Most homeowners in Kenosha pay around $13,900 for a new roof. The full range is $5,650 to $111,400 depending on roof size and materials.

Is roofing more expensive in Kenosha than average?

Roofing in Kenosha is 38% above the national average. Local labor rates, material availability, and building code requirements all influence pricing.

Why do roofing quotes vary so much?

Roofing estimates vary depending on labor assumptions, material brands, flashing work, ventilation upgrades, and warranty coverage included in the proposal.

Should I always choose the lowest roofing quote?

Not always. Lower bids sometimes exclude items like flashing, ventilation upgrades, or disposal costs that appear later as change orders.

What should a roofing quote include?

A complete quote should include tear-off, underlayment, flashing, ice and water shield, drip edge, ventilation, ridge vent, starter strip, ridge cap, decking inspection, disposal/cleanup, and the permit. If any of these are missing, ask your contractor why.

Do I need to worry about snow load on my roof in Kenosha?

Kenosha gets significant snowfall, and roofs must be designed to handle the weight. Ice dams are also a concern. Proper ventilation, ice and water shield along eaves, and adequate insulation help prevent costly damage.

My home in Kenosha is older. Does that affect the cost?

Often yes. Homes in Kenosha average around 49 years old. Older roofs may need additional decking repair, updated ventilation, or code-required upgrades that add to the base replacement cost.
