Foundation Cost in Kentucky (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in Kentucky typically run $14,400–$31,800 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with full basement pour as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 24-inch design frost line. Kentucky's karst limestone subsoil across 38 of 120 counties (the highest concentration of karst in the eastern U.S.) drives mandatory sinkhole disclosure under KY Statute §324.360 on every Kentucky residential property transfer, and Kentucky's combination of statewide radon zone 1 designation, New Madrid Seismic Zone influence across the western Jackson Purchase counties forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC C-D0 seismic detailing, and 24-inch frost depth across the Bluegrass Region drives aggressive geotechnical investigation, sub-slab depressurization rough-in, and continuous footing reinforcement on every Kentucky residential foundation pour.

Kentucky climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 24-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Full basement — dominant in Upper Midwest, Northeast, and Mid-Atlantic on freeze-thaw + radon-zone-1 sites
  • Expansive soil risk: Moderate — periodic clay shrink-swell, standard footing reinforcement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC C — moderate seismic risk, continuous-rebar-tie footings required
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Water table risk: Moderate — water table below frost line in most lots
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • IECC climate zone: 4A
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 1 (highest) — passive sub-slab depressurization rough-in required on every basement and slab pour

Kentucky licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Kentucky Department of Housing, Buildings & Construction — no statewide foundation contractor license but mandatory state-issued Builder License for residential general contractors above 1,500 sq ft new construction (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential foundation pour; Kentucky karst-zone sinkhole disclosure required under KY Statute §324.360; statewide radon zone 1 mandates sub-slab depressurization rough-in
