Garage door replacements in Kentucky typically run $1,100–$4,100 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 110 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Kentucky climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 110 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Chain-drive — dominant on detached or budget installs, durable and economical
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Moderate — periodic termite pressure, PT framing recommended
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Low — limited HOA garage-door restrictions outside metro subdivisions
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: None — no salt-air corrosion exposure
  • IECC climate zone: 4A

Kentucky licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Kentucky Department of Housing, Buildings & Construction — Builder License for residential general contractors above 1,500 sq ft new construction (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening; KY DHBC Builder License required for new construction above 1,500 sq ft; replacement-only installs typically permit-exempt; western KY counties (within New Madrid Seismic Zone) require seismic-detailed garage-door header attachment
