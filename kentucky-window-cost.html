Window Replacement Cost in Kentucky (2026)

Window replacements in Kentucky typically run $4,800–$12,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR North-Central climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.32, SHGC ≤ 0.4). Kentucky's combination of 24-inch frost depth across the Bluegrass Region, F2 freeze-thaw cycling, Kentucky New Madrid Seismic Zone influence across the western Jackson Purchase counties forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC C-D0 seismic-detailed window header attachment, ENERGY STAR North-Central climate zone driving moderate U-factor (≤0.32) requirement statewide, and high pre-1978 housing share across Louisville, Lexington, Bowling Green, and Owensboro historic neighborhoods drives moderate seismic-and-historic-housing-driven glazing selection.

Kentucky climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: North-Central — heating-dominant, balanced U-factor and SHGC (U ≤ 0.32, SHGC ≤ 0.40)
  • IECC climate zone: 4A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.32 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: 0.4
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 110 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Moderate — selected metro historic districts with review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): High — 40-60% pre-1978 housing share, EPA RRP required on most replacements

Kentucky licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Kentucky Department of Housing, Buildings & Construction — Builder License for residential general contractors above 1,500 sq ft new construction (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification; KY DHBC Builder License required for new construction above 1,500 sq ft; replacement-only same-size installs typically permit-exempt; western KY counties (within New Madrid Seismic Zone) require seismic-detailed window header attachment

How window replacement costs vary in Kentucky

State-specific code or insurance rule: Kentucky Department of Housing, Buildings & Construction §198B operates a Builder License only for residential general contractors performing new-construction work above 1,500 sq ft footprint, leaving window replacement-only contractors entirely outside state credential review, plus Kentucky New Madrid Seismic Zone influence across western Kentucky counties (Hickman, Fulton, Carlisle, Ballard, McCracken, Graves) forces ASCE 7-22 SDC C-D0 seismic-detailed window header attachment on every western Kentucky residential window installation.
