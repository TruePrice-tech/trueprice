What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

How much does a concrete foundation cost?

About concrete work in Kentwood, MI

Why pricing varies in Kentwood. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. Our cost model shows concrete work in Kentwood tracking right at the national baseline.

Local conditions. Kentwood has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Kentwood for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Older properties in Kentwood (averaging 47 years) often have settling concrete that affects drainage. Re-pouring may require sub-grade correction (compaction, drainage tile) that new construction doesn't need. Budget 10-20% extra for site preparation on established properties.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Kentwood require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With 50,233 people, Kentwood's contractor pool is mid-sized. You will find 4-6 established shops for most trades, which provides enough competition for fair pricing. Kentwood's measured growth has built a reliable contractor ecosystem. Lead times are manageable and pricing competition is healthy.

Kentwood, MI contractor wages: BLS data

According to BLS data, cement masons and concrete finishers in the Grand Rapids-Wyoming-Kentwood, MI metro earn a median of $28.19/hr (13% above the national median of $25.00/hr). Construction laborers earn $23.24/hr (5% above the $22.08/hr national median).

These higher labor costs are the primary reason concrete work quotes in Kentwood run above the national average. Labor typically represents 40-60% of a concrete work quote, so even a few percentage points above or below the national median shows up in your bottom line.
