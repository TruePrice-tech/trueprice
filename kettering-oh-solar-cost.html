Solar questions for Kettering homeowners

Does Kettering have any state or local solar incentives?

Incentives vary. Check the DSIRE database for Kettering-specific rebates, SRECs, property tax exemptions, and performance-based incentives that stack on top of the federal 30% ITC.

How does the federal solar tax credit work?

The Investment Tax Credit (ITC) lets you deduct 30% of your total solar installation cost from your federal taxes. It applies to purchased systems, not leased. There is no cap on the credit amount.

How many panels do I need?

Panel count depends on your electricity usage, panel wattage, and roof sun exposure. A typical home uses 20-30 panels (400W each) to offset 80-100% of electricity consumption.

Does solar work in Kettering's cold winters?

Cold weather actually improves solar panel efficiency since photovoltaic cells perform better at lower temperatures. Kettering's shorter winter days reduce seasonal output, but net metering lets you bank summer surplus for winter use. Snow typically clears from tilted panels within 1-2 days. Between higher electricity rates and incentive programs, the financial return in Kettering often matches sunbelt installations.

What solar incentives are available in Kettering?

Start with the 30% federal ITC, which applies to all Kettering solar installations with no cap. Then check DSIRE for OH-level incentives: state tax credits, utility rebates, SREC markets, property tax exemptions, and net metering terms. Each utility has different programs and rates. The combined federal-plus-state incentive stack typically reduces net cost by 40-60% in markets with strong state programs.

When is the best time to install solar in Kettering?

The best installation window in Kettering is late spring or summer, weather cooperates for roof work and you maximize first-year production before winter. Note that the process from quote to installation takes 6-12 weeks including permitting, utility approval, and scheduling. Start the process 2-3 months before your target installation date. The federal tax credit applies to the year of installation, so timing relative to tax year can matter.
