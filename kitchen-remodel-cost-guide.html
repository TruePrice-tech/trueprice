Kitchen Remodel Cost Guide

A kitchen remodel is one of the most popular home improvement projects, and for good reason. It is where families spend a significant amount of time, and an updated kitchen adds both daily comfort and resale value. However, costs can vary dramatically depending on the scope of work, materials, and your local market. This 2026 guide covers average prices, explains where your budget goes, and provides strategies for maximizing your return.

Factors That Affect Your Kitchen Remodel Cost

  • Kitchen size. A 100-square-foot galley kitchen costs significantly less than a 250-square-foot open-concept layout. More square footage means more cabinets, countertop, flooring, and labor hours.
  • Layout changes. Keeping the existing layout (sink, stove, and refrigerator in their current positions) avoids expensive plumbing and electrical relocation. Moving a sink or adding an island with plumbing can add $3,000–$8,000.
  • Cabinet quality. Stock cabinets from big-box stores cost $100–$300 per linear foot installed. Semi-custom cabinets run $300–$700, and fully custom cabinetry can exceed $1,000 per linear foot.
  • Countertop material. Laminate is the most affordable at $15–$40 per square foot. Butcher block runs $40–$70. Quartz and granite range from $50–$120 per square foot. Marble and quartzite can exceed $150.
  • Region. Kitchen remodel costs in major metro areas (New York, San Francisco, Boston) run 20–35% above the national average. Smaller cities in the Midwest and South tend to be below the baseline.
  • Structural work. Removing a wall to create an open floor plan adds $3,000–$15,000 depending on whether it is load-bearing. This also triggers additional permitting and engineering.

How to Save Money on a Kitchen Remodel

A smart kitchen remodel prioritizes impact per dollar. Here are proven strategies to reduce costs without sacrificing the result.

  • Keep the existing layout. The single biggest cost saver is leaving plumbing and gas lines where they are. A same-layout remodel can save $5,000–$15,000 compared to a reconfigured kitchen.
  • Reface instead of replace cabinets. If your cabinet boxes are structurally sound, refacing the doors and drawer fronts with new hardware delivers a fresh look at 40–60% of replacement cost.
  • Choose mid-range appliances. The jump from mid-range to premium appliances can add $10,000–$20,000 with marginal performance gains for most home cooks. Brands like Bosch, KitchenAid, and Samsung offer excellent value.
  • Get at least three quotes. Three bids help you identify fair pricing and catch scope differences. Make sure each contractor itemizes cabinets, countertops, labor, and materials separately.
  • Time your project wisely. Late fall and winter are typically slower for kitchen contractors. You may get better scheduling and pricing by avoiding the spring and summer rush.
  • Handle demolition yourself. If you are comfortable with basic tools, removing old cabinets, countertops, and flooring yourself can save $1,000–$3,000 in labor. Just confirm with your contractor first.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a kitchen remodel cost in 2026?

A mid-range kitchen remodel costs $30,000–$65,000 for a standard-sized kitchen. Minor refreshes start around $8,000, while high-end renovations with custom cabinets and premium appliances can exceed $150,000.

What is the most expensive part of a kitchen remodel?

Cabinets are typically the largest expense, accounting for 25–35% of the total budget. Custom cabinetry can easily cost $25,000–$40,000 for a mid-sized kitchen. Choosing stock or semi-custom cabinets is one of the most effective ways to control costs.

How long does a kitchen remodel take?

A minor refresh takes 2–4 weeks. A mid-range remodel typically runs 6–10 weeks. A full gut renovation with structural changes can take 12–16 weeks or longer, especially if custom cabinets have a lead time of 8–12 weeks.

Does a kitchen remodel increase home value?

Yes. A mid-range kitchen remodel recovers roughly 60–75% of its cost at resale, and a minor refresh can recover 80% or more. Beyond the dollar return, an updated kitchen makes a home more attractive to buyers and can shorten time on market.

Should I remodel my kitchen before selling?

If your kitchen is seriously outdated (1980s or 1990s style), a minor to mid-range update is usually worth the investment. If the kitchen was updated in the last 10–15 years, cosmetic touches like new hardware and fresh paint may be sufficient.

Can I remodel a kitchen for under $20,000?

Yes, but you will need to keep the existing layout and focus on cosmetic updates. Painting or refacing cabinets, installing laminate countertops, updating lighting and hardware, and adding a new backsplash can transform a kitchen for $10,000–$18,000.
