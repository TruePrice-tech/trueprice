What Knoxville residents ask about fencing

Can I install a fence on a slope?

Yes, using either stepped (stair-step) or racked (following the slope) installation. Racked looks smoother on gentle slopes. Stepped is better for steep grades and is structurally stronger.

Who owns the fence between two properties?

Typically the homeowner who installed it. If the fence sits exactly on the property line, both neighbors share responsibility. Always get a survey before building to confirm the boundary.

What is the difference between dog-ear and flat-top fence?

Dog-ear pickets have angled cuts at the top for water runoff and a traditional look. Flat-top pickets have a clean, modern appearance but hold slightly more water, which can accelerate rot.

How long does a wood fence last in Knoxville?

Wood fence lifespan in Knoxville: pressure-treated pine lasts 15-20 years with maintenance, cedar 15-25, and untreated pine just 5-8. The two things that extend life most are regular staining or sealing (every 2-3 years) and keeping plants and soil cleared from the bottom of the fence to promote air circulation and reduce moisture contact.

Knoxville permits: required to build a fence?

Check with Knoxville's building department, but permits are generally required for fences, especially those above 6 feet. Before digging post holes, you need utility locates (call 811) and confirmed property lines. If boundaries are uncertain, a survey ($300-$600) prevents expensive neighbor disputes. Your contractor should pull the permit.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Knoxville?

In Knoxville, budget $15-$30 per linear foot for wood privacy fencing, $20-$40 for vinyl, $10-$20 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron. A 150 linear foot backyard project totals $2,250-$6,000 depending on material. Collect 3 written quotes scoped identically to compare apples to apples.

Local Fencing Guide for Knoxville, TN

A fence in Knoxville has to handle four seasons of weathering. Humid summers work on rot and insects; cold winters stress post footings; shoulder seasons bring freeze-thaw cycling. The combination rewards mid-grade material choices and proper installation technique rather than cheapest-option builds.

Some Knoxville neighborhoods have HOA fence restrictions. If your property is in a managed community, check the CC&Rs for height limits, material restrictions, and color requirements before committing to a design. Even without an HOA, most Knoxville zoning codes restrict front-yard fence height to 3-4 feet.

Many Knoxville properties (average home age 36 years) have aging or deteriorated fences that predate current building codes. Replacement often requires updated post depth, property line verification, and sometimes a survey to confirm boundaries before new fence construction begins. Budget $300-$600 for a property survey if boundary markers are not clearly visible.
