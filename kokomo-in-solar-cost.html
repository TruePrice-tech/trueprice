Kokomo solar FAQ

How many panels do I need?

Panel count depends on your electricity usage, panel wattage, and roof sun exposure. A typical home uses 20-30 panels (400W each) to offset 80-100% of electricity consumption.

How much does solar cost in Kokomo?

Most homeowners in Kokomo pay $15,000-$30,000 before incentives for a 6-10kW system. After the 30% federal tax credit, net cost drops to $10,500-$21,000.

Will solar panels damage my roof?

Properly installed panels do not damage roofs. Reputable installers use flashing and sealant at every penetration point. If your roof needs replacement within 5-10 years, do it before installing solar.

How much roof space do I need for solar?

Each 400W panel needs about 18 square feet. A 8kW system (20 panels) requires roughly 360 square feet of unshaded, south-facing roof space. East and west orientations work but produce 10-15% less.

What is net metering?

Net metering credits you for excess electricity your panels send to the grid. Your meter runs backward during the day and forward at night. Policies and credit rates vary by state and utility.

Does solar work in Kokomo's cold winters?

Solar works well in Kokomo despite the cold. Panels convert sunlight more efficiently at lower temperatures, partially offsetting shorter winter days. Net metering lets you bank summer overproduction as credits for winter months. Snow sheds from angled panels within a day or two in most cases. Higher local electricity rates and available incentives often make the payback comparable to warmer markets.

What solar incentives are available in Kokomo?

Every Kokomo homeowner installing solar qualifies for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit with no dollar limit. Beyond that, IN may offer state tax credits, utility-specific rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits, and property tax exemptions. The DSIRE database has the current list for your area. These stacked incentives typically reduce net cost by an additional 10-30%.

What's the best season to install solar in Kokomo?

Kokomo homeowners should aim for late spring or summer to install solar. Weather cooperates for roof work and you maximize first-year production before winter. Allow 6-12 weeks from signing a contract through permitting, utility interconnection approval, and installation. Start gathering quotes 2-3 months before your target window. Remember that the 30% ITC applies to the tax year in which installation is completed.

How much should I pay upfront?

Never pay more than 10-15% as a deposit before work begins. Standard payment structure: deposit, progress payment when materials arrive, final payment on completion.

What should I look for in a contractor quote?

A professional quote breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. It includes a timeline, payment schedule, warranty terms, and the contractor's license number.

Do I need a permit for solar installation in Kokomo?

Most solar installation projects in Kokomo require a permit. Your contractor should pull the permit and schedule the inspection. Never pull permits in your own name.

What are common red flags in contractor quotes?

Red flags include: no line-item detail, same-day signing pressure, request for more than 30% upfront, no license or insurance verification, and verbal-only warranties.
