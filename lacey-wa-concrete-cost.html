Lacey concrete: answers to common questions

Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Lacey?

In Lacey, permits are typically required for driveways, sidewalks, and any concrete work near property lines or public right-of-way. Backyard patios may be exempt depending on size. Check local requirements.

What is stamped concrete?

Stamped concrete uses patterned mats pressed into wet concrete to mimic brick, stone, slate, or wood. It costs $12-$25/sqft installed. It requires resealing every 2-3 years to maintain appearance and prevent wear.

Why pricing varies in Lacey. Local conditions. Lacey has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Lacey's constant moisture promotes moss and algae growth on concrete surfaces. Annual pressure washing and sealer application maintain appearance and prevent the biological growth that makes walkways slippery. Standard mixes work well — freeze-thaw additives are unnecessary in this mild climate. Standard concrete in Lacey runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways).
