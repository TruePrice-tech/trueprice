Frequently asked concrete questions

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

How much does concrete work cost in Lafayette?

Concrete costs in Lafayette range from $6-$12 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, patios) to $15-$30/sqft for stamped or decorative finishes, including labor and materials.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

How much does a concrete foundation cost?

A full basement foundation costs $15,000-$30,000. A slab foundation for a house costs $5,000-$15,000. A garage slab costs $2,500-$6,000. Price depends on size, depth, and soil conditions.

How long before I can drive on new concrete?

Wait 24-48 hours for foot traffic, 7 days for vehicle traffic, and 30 days for full cure. Avoid deicing salt for the first winter. New concrete reaches 90% strength in 7 days and full strength in 28 days.

A Lafayette homeowner's guide to concrete work

How Lafayette pricing is built. BLS data puts Lafayette's skilled trade wages near the national midpoint. The local pricing you see is shaped more by material costs, permit fees, and competition than by labor rate differences. For concrete work specifically, Lafayette sits within a few points of the national midpoint on our cost model.

What Lafayette's local context means for pricing. Lafayette has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Lafayette for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Older properties in Lafayette (averaging 48 years) often have settling concrete that affects drainage. Re-pouring may require sub-grade correction (compaction, drainage tile) that new construction doesn't need. Budget 10-20% extra for site preparation on established properties.

Permits, codes, and licensing in Lafayette. In Lafayette, concrete work projects typically require a permit. Confirm the current local process before signing a contract.

Lafayette's trade contractor landscape. At 70,373 residents, Lafayette has a moderate contractor base. Most homeowners can find 3-5 qualified bidders without searching beyond the immediate area. Moderate growth in Lafayette supports a balanced contractor market. Enough work to keep good shops busy, enough competition to keep pricing reasonable.

Lafayette, IN trade wages and labor costs

Higher contractor wages explain much of why concrete work costs in Lafayette exceed the national average. With labor making up 40-60% of a typical concrete work project, local wage data is one of the most reliable indicators of what your quote will look like.
