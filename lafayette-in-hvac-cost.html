Frequently asked hvac questions

How long does a new HVAC system last?

A properly maintained system lasts 15-20 years. Air conditioners and heat pumps last 12-17 years, while gas furnaces can reach 20-25 years with annual maintenance.

How long does HVAC installation take in Lafayette?

A standard residential HVAC replacement takes 1-2 days. Complex jobs involving ductwork modification or system-type changes can take 3-5 days.

How much does a new HVAC system cost in Lafayette?

Most homeowners in Lafayette pay between $4,000 and $14,000 for a new HVAC system, depending on equipment type, efficiency rating, and home size.

Local factors: HVAC replacement in Lafayette, IN

The Lafayette housing picture. With average home age near 48 years, Lafayette homes are at the typical first-replacement milestone for major systems. Original furnaces, AC units, water heaters, and roofs installed when the homes were built are aging out. HVAC replacement is hitting peak demand in this age band.

Neighborhood rulebook. About half of Lafayette's newer subdivisions have HOA governance. Check whether your community requires architectural review for HVAC replacement work. Even when HOAs exist here, approval timelines tend to be shorter than in heavily regulated markets.

Local market conditions. The Lafayette contractor market is balanced: enough work to keep shops busy, but enough competition that homeowners can still get timely service. Booking 1-3 weeks ahead typically secures your preferred contractor. Lafayette homeowners should consider a programmable or smart thermostat with their new system. Setting back temperatures while away or sleeping can save 10-15% on heating bills.

When to schedule. Lafayette HVAC contractors are busiest December through February for heating and June through August for cooling. Book your replacement during the shoulder seasons for better pricing and faster scheduling.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Lafayette track within a few points of the national average for HVAC replacement, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. At 48 years average home age, Lafayette properties are hitting their first major replacement cycle for systems and components. HVAC replacement demand is at peak levels in this age band, which keeps contractor schedules full but pricing competitive.

Red flags to watch for. Any Lafayette contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion. Watch for HVAC replacement quotes in Lafayette that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Lump-sum bids hide margin and make change orders impossible to evaluate.
