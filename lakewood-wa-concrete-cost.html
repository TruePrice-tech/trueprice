Frequently asked concrete questions

How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

How much does concrete work cost in Lakewood?

Concrete costs in Lakewood range from $6-$12 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, patios) to $15-$30/sqft for stamped or decorative finishes, including labor and materials.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

About concrete work in Lakewood, WA

Why pricing varies in Lakewood. Labor rates here run roughly 26% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For concrete work specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Lakewood at roughly 18% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Lakewood has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Lakewood's constant moisture promotes moss and algae growth on concrete surfaces. Annual pressure washing and sealer application maintain appearance and prevent the biological growth that makes walkways slippery. Standard mixes work well — freeze-thaw additives are unnecessary in this mild climate. Standard concrete in Lakewood runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Lakewood require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. At 59,097 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. Lakewood has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.

Lakewood, WA concrete work: local considerations

Home stock in Lakewood. Lakewood's housing stock averages 29 years old — relatively recent construction that was largely built to modern code. concrete work projects here tend to be simpler: fewer surprise compliance issues, quicker permit turnaround, and contractors who can quote from memory.

HOA & compliance. HOA prevalence in Lakewood is moderate. If you're in a managed community, check covenants before signing a concrete work contract — material color, visible fixture type, or warranty requirements may affect your selection.

Local market conditions. Lakewood has moderate construction activity. Contractors are busy but accessible with 1-3 weeks' notice. Off-season quotes (contractor-dependent) can run 8-15% below peak rates. In Lakewood (59,097 residents), concrete work is often done by small local companies or general contractors. For decorative work (stamped, colored), verify the contractor has a portfolio of completed projects in your specific pattern and color — decorative concrete expertise varies widely.

Seasonal timing. Lakewood allows year-round concrete work, but the ideal window is spring and fall when temperatures support proper curing without the heat-related challenges of summer. Avoid scheduling large pours during the hottest months — early morning pours are the only reliable option.

What drives cost. The biggest factor in Lakewood concrete work pricing is labor cost, running 26% above national benchmarks. For a driveway or patio pour, that premium alone accounts for $1040-2080 in additional cost. Lakewood's relatively young housing stock (29 years average) simplifies most concrete work projects. Modern code compliance, standard dimensions, and accessible construction reduce both time and cost versus older homes.

Red flags to watch for. Check that any Lakewood contractor doing concrete work carries both general liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers' compensation. Request certificates directly from the insurer, not just copies the contractor provides.
