Lancaster roofing: answers to common questions

How many layers of shingles can I have?

Most building codes allow a maximum of two layers. If you already have two layers, the old roof must be torn off before the new one is installed, adding $1,000-$3,000 to the cost.

Does a new roof increase home value?

A new roof recovers roughly 60-70% of its cost at resale and eliminates a major buyer objection. Homes with new roofs sell faster and with fewer inspection contingencies.

Do I need a permit for roof replacement in Lancaster?

Yes. Most Lancaster jurisdictions require a building permit for roof replacement. Your contractor should handle the permit and schedule inspection.

What is the difference between architectural and 3-tab shingles?

Architectural shingles are thicker, have a dimensional look, and last 25-30 years. 3-tab shingles are flat, thinner, and last 15-20 years but cost 30-40% less upfront.

How do I prevent ice dams on my Lancaster roof?

Ice dams form when heat escapes through the roof, melting snow that refreezes at the eaves. Prevention starts with proper attic insulation (R-49 or higher for Lancaster's climate zone) and continuous soffit-to-ridge ventilation. Your roofing quote should include ice and water shield membrane on the first 3-6 feet from all eaves. If your current roof has ice dam damage, address the insulation and ventilation before replacing the roof or the problem will recur.

Is a Lancaster permit needed to replace my roof?

A permit is typically required for roof replacement in Lancaster. The contractor handles the permit process. Going without one risks voiding your homeowners insurance and creates legal complications at resale since you must disclose unpermitted work.

When should I replace a roof in Lancaster?

Schedule your Lancaster roof replacement during late spring or early fall if possible. Materials install best above 45F and before snow season pricing surcharges, and off-peak timing often means lower installation costs since contractors compete harder for work. Expect to pay a 10-15% premium if you schedule during the post-storm rush when demand peaks.
