All cities with landscaping cost data

738 U.S. cities across 50 states · data updated 2026

Landscape installation pricing depends on whether the line item is plants (per-plant, retail-driven), turf (per-square-foot sod vs hydroseed), or hardscape (per-square-foot patio/walkway). Sod runs $1.50–$2.50/sqft installed for tall fescue in the transition zone; Bermuda and St. Augustine are higher in the Deep South because plug establishment is labor-thicker. Hardscape swings on materials (poured concrete vs flagstone vs paver) and irrigation tie-in (a drip system with a controller adds $1,500–$3,500 to any plant-heavy job). Drainage and grading work — the silent big-ticket — typically averages 15–25% of the total scope on a sloped lot but doesn't appear on a glance-bid. City pages below break out dominant turf type, irrigation norms, and the grading-vs-flat split that swings the line-item total at bid time.
