Frequently asked siding questions

Should I paint or replace my siding?

Paint if siding is structurally sound with no rot or warping. Painting costs $3,000-$7,000 and lasts 5-10 years. If paint peels repeatedly or siding has damage, replacement is the better long-term investment.

Is vinyl or fiber cement siding better?

Fiber cement resists fire, impact, and insects better than vinyl, and holds paint longer. Vinyl costs 30-40% less, never needs painting, and handles moisture well. Choose based on budget and climate.

What is the cheapest siding option?

Vinyl is the most affordable at $4-$8/sqft installed. Aluminum is similar at $4-$9/sqft. Both are low-maintenance, but vinyl has largely replaced aluminum due to better impact resistance and color retention.

What siding material lasts the longest?

Fiber cement (James Hardie) lasts 40-50 years with minimal maintenance. Vinyl lasts 25-40 years. Engineered wood lasts 20-30 years. Natural wood lasts 20-40 years but requires repainting every 5-7 years.

Contractor market picture. In a mature market like Laredo, contractors rely on repeat business and referrals rather than new-construction volume. Timing considerations. Laredo allows year-round siding installation, but spring and fall offer the ideal combination of moderate temperatures and lower humidity for sealant curing. Summer pours heat against fresh caulk and can cause it to skin over before bonding properly.

What drives cost. Laredo homeowners benefit from labor rates 25% below national medians. For a whole-house re-siding, this translates to savings of $750-1500 compared to the national average. Newer construction in Laredo (averaging 28 years) means siding replacement projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay.

Red flags to watch for. Any Laredo contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag.
