Frequently asked solar questions

What is the difference between string inverters and microinverters?

String inverters are cheaper but the whole array drops output when one panel is shaded. Microinverters optimize each panel independently, cost 15-20% more, and last longer.

Should I buy or lease solar panels?

Buying maximizes long-term savings and increases home value. Leasing eliminates upfront cost but the leasing company keeps the tax credit, and panels may complicate home sales.

Does Laredo have any state or local solar incentives?

Incentives vary. Check the DSIRE database for Laredo-specific rebates, SRECs, property tax exemptions, and performance-based incentives that stack on top of the federal 30% ITC.

How does the federal solar tax credit work?

The Investment Tax Credit (ITC) lets you deduct 30% of your total solar installation cost from your federal taxes. It applies to purchased systems, not leased. There is no cap on the credit amount.

Is solar worth it in Laredo?

The numbers work well for most Laredo homeowners. Typical payback runs 6-10 years, after which the system generates essentially free electricity for another 15-20 years. The 30% federal tax credit applies to the full installation cost with no dollar cap. Roof orientation, shading, usage patterns, and utility rate structure all affect individual payback. What solar incentives are available in Laredo?

Start with the 30% federal ITC, which applies to all Laredo solar installations with no cap. Then check DSIRE for TX-level incentives: state tax credits, utility rebates, SREC markets, property tax exemptions, and net metering terms. Each utility has different programs and rates. The combined federal-plus-state incentive stack typically reduces net cost by 40-60% in markets with strong state programs.

When should I install solar in Laredo?

The best installation window in Laredo is fall or winter, cooler weather makes roof work safer and the system is ready for the high-production spring and summer months. Note that the process from quote to installation takes 6-12 weeks including permitting, utility approval, and scheduling. Start the process 2-3 months before your target installation date. The federal tax credit applies to the year of installation, so timing relative to tax year can matter.
