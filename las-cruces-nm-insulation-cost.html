Las Cruces insulation: answers to common questions

How much does insulation cost in Las Cruces?

Insulation costs in Las Cruces range from $1-$2/sqft for blown-in attic insulation, $1.50-$3.50/sqft for batt insulation in walls, and $2-$4/sqft for spray foam. A full attic (1,000 sqft) runs $1,000-$2,500.

Can I add insulation over existing insulation?

Yes, in most cases. Adding blown-in cellulose or unfaced fiberglass over existing attic insulation is common and effective. Never compress existing insulation and do not add a vapor barrier on top of existing insulation.

Should I remove old insulation before adding new?

Remove if the existing insulation is wet, moldy, pest-contaminated, or vermiculite (potential asbestos). Otherwise, add new insulation on top. Removal costs $1-$2/sqft and is usually unnecessary.

Local conditions. Las Cruces has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Radiant barriers and reflective roof coatings outperform added attic insulation in Las Cruces's cooling-dominant climate. The intense solar radiation on the roof surface is the primary heat source — reflecting it before it enters the attic is more effective than trying to insulate against it. Insulation costs in Las Cruces vary by material: blown fiberglass ($1-1.50/sqft at R-38), blown cellulose ($1.25-2/sqft at R-38), spray foam open-cell ($1.50-2.50/sqft at R-19), and spray foam closed-cell ($2.50-4/sqft at R-21). For attic retrofits, blown-in materials offer the best cost-per-R-value.
