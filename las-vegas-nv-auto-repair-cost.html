Las Vegas Vehicle Inspections and Warranty Protections

Nevada requires a biennial emissions test in Clark County (Las Vegas metro) for most gasoline-powered vehicles. The test fee is $18-$25 depending on the station. No general safety inspection exists. Vehicles that fail must be repaired and retested. Nevada's DMV offers a repair cost exemption after $450 in qualifying emissions-repair expenditures.

Nevada's Lemon Law covers new vehicles with defects reported within the manufacturer's express warranty period or 18 months. Nevada's Deceptive Trade Practices Act covers auto-repair fraud. Nevada requires repair shops to provide written estimates and obtain customer authorization. The state does not license general auto mechanics.
