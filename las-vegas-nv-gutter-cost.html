Common Gutter Scams in Las Vegas

Summerlin, Henderson, and Green Valley homeowners see these same four gutter-quote issues repeatedly.

Las Vegas-specific undersizing

A Las Vegas bid that specifies 5-inch gutters without reference to 4.2 inches of annual rainfall or the specific Summerlin, Henderson, and Green Valley roof area being drained is underthought. Any roof plane above 1,500 sq ft emptying to a single gutter run needs 6-inch in Las Vegas.

Pitch errors on Las Vegas installs

Correct slope for Las Vegas gutters: 1/4 inch per 10 feet of run toward each downspout. Flat sections pool water and overflow during storms. A five-minute post-install hose test catches the problem — any standing water means the hangers need adjusting.

Las Vegas discharge routing skipped

Discharge routing is the half of a Las Vegas gutter project contractors most often skip. Given 4.2 inches of Las Vegas rainfall annually, dumping water at the foundation causes foundation movement, basement moisture, and yard erosion. A proper Las Vegas bid addresses where the water goes, not just how it gets off the roof.

Seamed gutters sold as seamless

Real seamless means the installer rolls the full gutter run on your Las Vegas property from continuous coil stock; seams appear only at corners, outlets, and end caps. A "seamless" Las Vegas bid that mentions joining factory-cut sections with splice connectors is sectional gutter at seamless pricing — the single most common install-side scam.
