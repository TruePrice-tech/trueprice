Las Vegas Kitchen Stock and Typical Layouts

Vegas kitchen stock runs from 1990s-2000s Summerlin, Anthem, and Henderson master-planned tract homes (220-380 sqft great-room kitchens) through 1970s-80s Green Valley and Paradise older ranches (150-210 sqft) to 2010s-onward Inspirada, Cadence, and Mountains Edge new-construction (280-450 sqft open concept). Downtown and John S. Park 1950s ranches are smaller at 130-180 sqft with original single-wall layouts. Condo-tower kitchens on The Strip (Turnberry, Panorama, Veer) are lock-and-leave style at 90-150 sqft.

Slab-on-grade construction across every Vegas tract means sink relocation requires 4-6 inch concrete saw-cutting. Post-tensioned slabs in 1990s-2010s tracts require PT-cable X-ray before any cut. Great-room ceilings in Summerlin, Anthem, and Inspirada limit hood-vent runs because roof truss structure constrains chase boxes. HOA architectural committees in gated Summerlin villages control hood-vent placement and color finishes; MacDonald Highlands specifically requires committee approval on any exterior chase.

Las Vegas Permits and Electrical Scope

Clark County Building Department and City of Las Vegas Building and Safety issue permits depending on jurisdiction, with 2-4 week turnaround. NV Energy panel upgrades to 200A run $2,400-$4,800. Nevada adopted the 2020 NEC and requires AFCI/GFCI combo protection on kitchen receptacle circuits. Henderson, North Las Vegas, and Boulder City each run separate portals. HOA architectural committee review often takes longer than the building permit itself in Summerlin, Anthem, and MacDonald Highlands.

Clark County Building runs 2-4 weeks on straightforward kitchen permits. City of Las Vegas runs 2-3 weeks. Henderson runs 2-3 weeks with stricter energy-code review. North Las Vegas runs fastest at 1-2 weeks. HOA review adds 2-8 weeks on top and master-planned community committees often meet only monthly. Summerlin HOA review and the associated architectural committee in MacDonald Highlands can stretch to 8-10 weeks with multiple revision cycles.

Las Vegas Plumbing, Water, and Rough-In

Las Vegas Valley Water District delivers hard water from Lake Mead with TDS 400-550 ppm; point-of-entry softeners are common on any permitted kitchen upgrade. Slab-on-grade construction dominates every Vegas tract, which forces saw-cutting for any sink relocation. Master plumbers (NSCB-licensed, C-1 classification) charge $95-$135/hour. The Nevada State Plumbing Code amends the UPC; SNWA (Southern Nevada Water Authority) coin-operated smart meters affect any fixture-count change on permit.

Vegas kitchen remodels typically encounter a 40-50 gallon gas tank in a garage alcove. NV Energy rebates pay $400-$1,000 for heat-pump water heater swaps; the hot garage environment (120F+ summers) makes heat-pump water heaters exceptionally efficient. Rheem ProTerra is the common selection. Tankless (Rinnai RUR199 or Navien NPE-240A) is the luxury Strip-tower swap, running $3,200-$5,500 installed. Hard water mandates annual descaling on tankless and the HOA often restricts exterior vent locations.

Las Vegas's rapid recent growth has tightened the contractor market — expect longer lead times and less negotiating room than smaller markets.
