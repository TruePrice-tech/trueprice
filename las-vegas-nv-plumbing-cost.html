Plumbing in Las Vegas: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

The extremely hard water in Las Vegas causes heavy scale buildup in pipes and appliances. Water heaters, dishwashers, and faucets all suffer. A water softener is practically a necessity here.

❄

Freeze risk

While freeze events are rare in Las Vegas, they can catch homeowners off guard. The 2021 winter storm showed that even warm-climate homes need basic freeze protection for exposed pipes and outdoor fixtures.

🔧

Material recommendation

For Las Vegas's hard water, PEX piping is ideal — it doesn't corrode or develop pinhole leaks like copper can in mineral-rich water. It's also the most cost-effective option for whole-house repiping.

💡

Local tip

Las Vegas's newer housing stock means most homes don't need repiping yet. Your biggest plumbing expense is likely water heater replacement ($1,200-3,500) or upgrading to a tankless unit for endless hot water.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Las Vegas?

Plumber rates in Las Vegas typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
