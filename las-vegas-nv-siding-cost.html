Las Vegas climate impact on siding

Wind-driven sand abrasion separates Vegas exterior damage from other desert metros. Haboob events (spring dust storms with 40-60 mph gusts) sandblast south and west stucco surfaces, creating micro-pitting that traps moisture during monsoon events and accelerates delamination. The fix is elastomeric acrylic coating rated for abrasion resistance (Dryvit Outsulation or LaHabra EcoShield), reapplied every 8-12 years rather than standard re-coat cycles. Summer surface temperatures on dark-toned stucco approach 170F, which is 10-15 degrees hotter than the same wall orientation in Tucson because of the valley-floor heat-island effect from 2.2 million residents on a 600 square mile basin.

Vegas has no coastal saltwater exposure but monsoon-season dust-storm debris and Mojave blowing dust abrade stucco finish. Dust-storm debris removal annually prevents accelerated paint-film degradation. Fastener corrosion is minimal in dry desert climate; galvanized steel performs adequately for 25-30 year service life. Monsoon-driven microburst damage to siding (particularly stucco crack propagation) is the dominant Vegas weather-driven concern. UV degradation is the dominant durability factor: unprotected paint chalks within 4-6 years, vinyl degrades within 8-12 years, dark stucco fades within 6-10 years.
