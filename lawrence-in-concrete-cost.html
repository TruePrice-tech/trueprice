Lawrence concrete FAQ

How much does a concrete patio cost?

A 300 sqft concrete patio costs $1,800-$3,600 for basic broom finish. Stamped patterns add $3-$8/sqft. Colored concrete adds $1-$3/sqft. An exposed aggregate finish adds $2-$4/sqft.

How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

Should I seal my concrete?

A Lawrence homeowner's guide to concrete work

How Lawrence pricing is built. Contractor labor in Lawrence commands a 10% premium over the national average, driven by local cost of living and trade demand. This wage differential flows directly into every quote you receive. For concrete work specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Lawrence at roughly 6% above the national midpoint.

What Lawrence's local context means for pricing. Lawrence has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Lawrence for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Standard concrete in Lawrence runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Permits, codes, and licensing in Lawrence. In Lawrence, concrete work projects typically require a permit. Confirm the current local process before signing a contract.

Lawrence's trade contractor landscape. At 47,135 residents, Lawrence has a moderate contractor base. Most homeowners can find 3-5 qualified bidders without searching beyond the immediate area. Moderate growth in Lawrence supports a balanced contractor market. Enough work to keep good shops busy, enough competition to keep pricing reasonable.

Local factors: concrete work in Lawrence, IN

The Lawrence housing picture. The typical Lawrence home is around 51 years old. Most systems are past their first-replacement cycle and approaching their second. Expect concrete work quotes to include mentions of upgraded electrical service, ductwork adjustments, or insulation retrofits that newer homes wouldn't need.

Neighborhood rulebook. About half of Lawrence's newer subdivisions have HOA governance. Check whether your community requires architectural review for concrete work work. Even when HOAs exist here, approval timelines tend to be shorter than in heavily regulated markets.

Local market conditions. The Lawrence contractor market is balanced: enough work to keep shops busy, but enough competition that homeowners can still get timely service. Booking 1-3 weeks ahead typically secures your preferred contractor. In Lawrence (47,135 residents), concrete work is often done by small local companies or general contractors. For decorative work (stamped, colored), verify the contractor has a portfolio of completed projects in your specific pattern and color — decorative concrete expertise varies widely.

When to schedule. The concrete season in Lawrence runs May through October when overnight temps stay above 40°F consistently. Spring is peak demand as homeowners address winter frost damage. Fall pours (September-October) offer better contractor availability and fewer weather delays.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Lawrence track within a few points of the national average for concrete work, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. The 51-year average home age in Lawrence means most concrete work projects encounter at least one behind-the-wall surprise. Experienced local contractors price this risk in; lowball bids from out-of-area contractors often don't.

Red flags to watch for. Check that any Lawrence contractor doing concrete work carries both general liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers' compensation. Request certificates directly from the insurer, not just copies the contractor provides. In Lawrence, verify your concrete work contractor pulls the permit themselves — never pull it in your own name. If they ask you to pull the permit, they may not be properly licensed to do the work. In Lawrence, concrete work on homes over 41 years old should include a contingency line item (10-15% of total).
