What Lawrence residents ask about landscaping

How much does lawn installation cost?

Sod costs $1-$2 per square foot installed. Hydroseeding costs $0.05-$0.10/sqft. Traditional seeding costs $0.10-$0.25/sqft but takes 6-8 weeks to establish. A 5,000 sqft lawn costs $5,000-$10,000 in sod.

How much does a paver patio cost?

Paver patios cost $12-$25 per square foot installed, depending on paver material and pattern complexity. A 300 sqft patio runs $3,600-$7,500. Permeable pavers cost 15-20% more but help with drainage.

How much does landscaping cost in Lawrence?

Basic landscaping in Lawrence costs $3,000-$8,000 for design and installation. Full yard transformations with hardscaping, irrigation, and mature plantings run $15,000-$50,000+.

What are the best low-maintenance landscaping plants?

Native perennials, ornamental grasses, and drought-tolerant shrubs need the least care. Avoid high-maintenance options like hybrid tea roses, non-native annuals, and fast-growing trees that require frequent pruning.

More questions about landscaping in Lawrence

How much does mulch cost per yard?

Bulk mulch costs $25-$50 per cubic yard delivered. Plan 3 inches deep — one cubic yard covers about 108 sq ft. A 1,000 sq ft bed needs roughly 10 yards ($250-$500 in materials) plus $30-$50/yard for professional spreading and edging.

What does a sprinkler system cost in Lawrence?

A residential irrigation system in Lawrence costs $2,500-$5,000 for a quarter-acre lot with 4-6 zones. Smart wifi controllers add $100-$300 and cut water usage 20-40%. Drip-irrigation zones for planting beds cost $500-$1,000 extra and are worth it on shrubs and perennials.

How much does sod installation cost?

Sod costs $1-$2 per square foot installed including soil prep and rolling. Hydroseeding runs $0.05-$0.10/sqft and establishes in 4-6 weeks. Traditional broadcast seeding is the cheapest at $0.10-$0.25/sqft but needs 6-8 weeks and frequent watering. A 5,000 sq ft lawn lands between $5,000 and $10,000 in sod.

Do I need a permit for landscaping in Lawrence?

Most landscaping in Lawrence — planting, mulching, sod, basic irrigation — does not need a permit. Retaining walls over 4 feet, drainage tied to the storm system, tree removal in some neighborhoods, and any grading that changes lot drainage usually do. Confirm with your landscaper before they start digging.
