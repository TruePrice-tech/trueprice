Questions Lenexa homeowners ask about plumbing

What is the difference between copper and PEX piping?

Copper lasts 50+ years, resists bacteria, and has proven durability. PEX costs 60% less, installs faster, resists freezing better, and requires fewer joints. Both are code-compliant.

Is trenchless sewer repair worth the extra cost?

Trenchless repair costs 20-40% more but avoids excavating your yard, driveway, or landscaping. It is faster and causes less property damage, often making it cheaper overall.

How much does it cost to replace a water heater?

A standard 50-gallon tank water heater runs $1,200-$2,500 installed. Tankless units cost $2,500-$5,000 but last roughly twice as long and cut energy bills 20-30%.

What does a plumbing inspection include?

A thorough inspection covers supply lines, drain lines, water heater condition, fixture connections, shutoff valves, water pressure testing, and a visual check for leaks and corrosion.

How much does a plumber charge per hour in Lenexa?

Plumbers in Lenexa typically charge $75-$150 per hour. Emergency and after-hours calls add a $100-$200 trip fee on top of the hourly rate.

Does Lenexa's hard water damage plumbing?

Yes. Hard water in the Lenexa area deposits mineral scale inside pipes, reduces water heater efficiency by 20-30%, and shortens fixture lifespan. A water softener ($1,500-$3,000 installed) or whole-house water conditioner addresses the problem at the source. Descaling your water heater annually adds 3-5 years to its lifespan. If you notice white buildup on faucets or reduced shower pressure, scale is the likely cause.

What should a plumbing quote include in Lenexa?

Every Lenexa plumbing quote should break down: labor, materials with brand names, permit fees where required, disposal, and warranty coverage. Verify the plumber's license number and insurance are documented. For any job over $500, three written quotes with identical scope give you a reliable pricing baseline.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Lenexa?

Plan your Lenexa plumbing projects for spring or fall. Moderate temperatures and lower demand than summer peak, and you avoid the emergency premium that adds 50-100% to the bill. Water heaters, supply lines, and fixtures nearing end-of-life are worth replacing on your schedule rather than theirs.
