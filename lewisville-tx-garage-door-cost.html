Garage Door questions for Lewisville homeowners

How long does a garage door last?

Steel garage doors last 20-30 years. Wood doors last 15-20 years with maintenance. Aluminum lasts 20+ years. Springs last 7-12 years (torsion) or 5-7 years (extension), depending on usage cycles.

How long does garage door installation take?

A standard single or double door replacement takes 3-5 hours. Custom doors or jobs requiring structural framing changes take 1-2 days. Most installations are completed in a single visit.

How much does a garage door replacement cost in Lewisville?

A new garage door in Lewisville costs $800-$4,000 for a standard door installed, or $2,500-$8,000+ for a premium insulated or carriage-style door with a new opener.

What R-value should my garage door have?

R-8 is fine if the garage is detached or unheated. R-12 to R-16 is best for attached garages or if you use the space for a workshop. R-18+ is for extreme cold climates or heated garages.

How do I keep my garage cool in Lewisville?

Start with an insulated door rated R-8 or higher, which drops garage temperatures 10-20 degrees versus uninsulated panels. Choose a light color to reflect solar heat. A radiant barrier on the ceiling helps further. For workspace use, a small ventilation fan or ductless mini-split provides real comfort without the expense of fully insulating the garage structure.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Lewisville?

Given that Lewisville homes average 34 years old, many original garage doors are due for replacement rather than repair. A new insulated door and modern opener runs $1,200-$3,000 and recoups 90-100% at resale. Repairs make financial sense for doors under 15 years old with specific damage, but throwing money at an aging system usually delays an inevitable replacement.

When should I replace a garage door in Lewisville?

Lewisville homeowners get the best pricing and selection by scheduling garage door replacement during fall or winter. Cooler weather makes the installation more comfortable and demand is lower, and you can save 5-10% versus peak season pricing. Spring demand surges from cold-weather spring failures, so plan ahead to avoid the rush.
