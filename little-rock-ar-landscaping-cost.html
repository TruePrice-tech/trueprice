Common landscaping questions in Little Rock

How much does outdoor lighting cost?

Professional landscape lighting costs $2,000-$5,000 for path lights, uplights, and accent lighting. LED fixtures cost more upfront but last 50,000+ hours. Solar options cost less but provide dimmer, less reliable light.

How much does a retaining wall cost?

Retaining walls cost $20-$50 per square face foot for concrete block, $25-$60 for natural stone, and $15-$30 for timber. A 50 linear foot wall at 3 feet tall runs $3,000-$9,000 installed.

What is xeriscaping?

Xeriscaping uses drought-tolerant plants, efficient irrigation, and permeable surfaces to minimize water use. It cuts outdoor water consumption 50-75% and is increasingly required in water-restricted regions.

Do I need a permit for landscaping in Little Rock?

Most landscaping in Little Rock does not require permits. Exceptions include retaining walls over 4 feet, grading that changes drainage, connecting to city water for irrigation, and work near property lines.

Neighborhood rulebook. HOA involvement is uncommon in Little Rock, so most landscaping projects avoid architectural review delays. City permitting alone governs the process.

Local market conditions. Little Rock's moderate growth keeps the contractor pipeline moving without the extreme backlogs seen in booming metros. When to schedule. Little Rock offers a longer landscaping season than cold-climate markets. The busiest period is March through June when spring planting demand peaks. Fall (October-December) is an excellent time for plantings, hardscape projects, and irrigation system installation at lower competition and often better pricing.
