Common concrete questions in Long Beach

How thick should a concrete driveway be?

Residential driveways should be 4 inches thick for cars and 5-6 inches for heavy vehicles like RVs. A 6-inch compacted gravel base underneath is essential. Thickened edges (6 inches) prevent cracking at the perimeter.

Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Long Beach?

In Long Beach, permits are typically required for driveways, sidewalks, and any concrete work near property lines or public right-of-way. Backyard patios may be exempt depending on size. Check local requirements.

What is stamped concrete?

Stamped concrete uses patterned mats pressed into wet concrete to mimic brick, stone, slate, or wood. It costs $12-$25/sqft installed. It requires resealing every 2-3 years to maintain appearance and prevent wear.

What causes concrete to crack?

Common causes: shrinkage during curing, frost heave from inadequate base prep, tree roots, heavy vehicle loads beyond design, poor control joint placement, and water pooling due to improper grading.

Any structural concrete in Long Beach must meet the California Building Code's Seismic Design Category D requirements (the most stringent standard for this soil type). The 1994 Northridge earthquake (6.7M, 25 miles north) produced ground-motion-induced concrete cracking across Long Beach from wave propagation along the Newport-Inglewood Fault. Oil-field subsidence in Signal Hill has historically tilted and cracked flatwork on the hilltop's most affected properties.

Who pulls the permit? Development Services issues concrete permits with same-week turnaround. CSLB licensing is mandatory. Standard residential flatwork requires an over-the-counter permit. Retaining walls over 4 feet and structural slabs require engineering review with seismic-design-category verification. CalRecycle 65% debris diversion applies. Heritage Coalition review may apply to visible hardscape on designated landmark properties in Belmont Heights, Bluff Park, and Drake Park. Signal Hill (independent city) has a separate permit authority.

When to Pour Concrete in Long Beach

Long Beach's concrete season: year-round pouring is feasible because the marine-moderated climate provides comfortable working conditions in all months. Optimal windows: March through June and September through November when marine-layer moisture is moderate and temperatures support ideal curing. June Gloom (May-June marine layer) delays morning work on coastal properties until surfaces dry. Santa Ana wind events (October-February) deposit dust on wet concrete and create application challenges during gusty conditions. The absence of freeze risk and the minimal rain disruption (average 12 inches annual precipitation) make Long Beach the most scheduling-flexible concrete market among these 10 metros.
