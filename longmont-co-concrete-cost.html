Longmont concrete FAQ

What is the best time to pour concrete?

Pour when temperatures stay between 50-80F for at least 3 days. Spring and fall are ideal. Avoid pouring in rain, direct hot sun, or below freezing. Cold and heat both compromise cure quality.

How thick should a concrete driveway be?

Residential driveways should be 4 inches thick for cars and 5-6 inches for heavy vehicles like RVs. A 6-inch compacted gravel base underneath is essential. Thickened edges (6 inches) prevent cracking at the perimeter.

Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Longmont?

In Longmont, permits are typically required for driveways, sidewalks, and any concrete work near property lines or public right-of-way. Backyard patios may be exempt depending on size. Check local requirements.

About concrete work in Longmont, CO

Where Longmont's pricing comes from. At 11% above national average labor rates, Longmont's contractor pricing starts from a higher baseline than most US markets. The gap reflects both local wages and regional demand for skilled trades. On concrete work, Longmont runs about 8% above the US average based on our pricing data across metro areas.

Local conditions in Longmont. Longmont has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Longmont for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Homes in Longmont averaging 39 years old typically have stable sub-grades that simplify concrete work. New pours integrate cleanly with existing slabs and foundations without the settling corrections older properties need.

Longmont permits and code requirements. Permits are usually required for concrete work in Longmont; verify the specifics with the local building department before committing.

The Longmont contractor pool. At 89,919 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. The Longmont area's booming growth means contractors have more work than they can handle. This shifts pricing power toward the contractor side of the table.
