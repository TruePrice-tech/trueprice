Garage Door questions for Longmont homeowners

Should I repair or replace my garage door?

Repair if damage is limited to one or two panels and replacements are available. Replace if the door is over 15 years old, panels are no longer manufactured, or you want better insulation and security.

Does a new garage door increase home value?

A garage door replacement recovers 90-100% of its cost at resale, making it one of the highest-ROI home improvements. It also dramatically improves curb appeal.

How long does a garage door last?

Steel garage doors last 20-30 years. Wood doors last 15-20 years with maintenance. Aluminum lasts 20+ years. Springs last 7-12 years (torsion) or 5-7 years (extension), depending on usage cycles.

How long does garage door installation take?

A standard single or double door replacement takes 3-5 hours. Custom doors or jobs requiring structural framing changes take 1-2 days. Most installations are completed in a single visit.

How much does a garage door replacement cost in Longmont?

A new garage door in Longmont costs $800-$4,000 for a standard door installed, or $2,500-$8,000+ for a premium insulated or carriage-style door with a new opener.

What R-value garage door do I need in Longmont?

Longmont's winters demand serious insulation. An R-12 to R-16 door is the baseline for attached garages, with R-16 to R-18 recommended if you use the space as a workshop or heat it. The $200-$500 upgrade cost pays back through lower heating bills in rooms adjacent to the garage within 3-5 years.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Longmont?

Given that Longmont homes average 39 years old, many original garage doors are due for replacement rather than repair. A new insulated door and modern opener runs $1,200-$3,000 and recoups 90-100% at resale. Repairs make financial sense for doors under 15 years old with specific damage, but throwing money at an aging system usually delays an inevitable replacement.

What's the best season to replace a garage door in Longmont?

Longmont homeowners get the best pricing and selection by scheduling garage door replacement during late spring or early fall. Moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing, and you can save 5-10% versus peak season pricing. Spring demand surges from cold-weather spring failures, so plan ahead to avoid the rush.

Local Garage Door Guide for Longmont, CO

Cold winters push Longmont garage doors into two failure modes: thermal losses through uninsulated panels and spring breakage during deep-cold cycles. An R-12+ door and a torsion spring rated for the local temperature range both pay for themselves through avoided replacement and emergency service calls.
