Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a gutter cost in Los Angeles?

Most homeowners in Los Angeles pay between $1,050 and $1,600 for gutter installation, depending on material and yard size.

What is the cheapest gutter material?

Aluminum gutters are the most affordable option at $6-$12 per linear foot installed. Vinyl is comparable at $5-$11 per linear foot but less durable in extreme climates.

How long does a wood gutter last?

A well-maintained wood gutter typically lasts 15-20 years. Regular staining and sealing every 2-3 years can significantly extend its lifespan. Cedar and redwood last longer than pine.

Los Angeles rainfall and gutter drainage

14.9 inches
annual rainfall in Los Angeles

Los Angeles receives less than 15 inches of rain annually, but nearly all of it falls between November and March. When it rains in LA, it often comes as intense atmospheric river events that can deliver 3-5 inches in 24 hours. This feast-or-famine pattern means gutters sit dry for months and then must handle extreme volume with no warning.

Hillside homes in the foothills face particular drainage challenges. Improperly routed downspouts can contribute to slope erosion and mudslide risk. LA building code requires downspout discharge to be directed away from foundations and slopes, and many hillside homes need extended drainage runs to approved discharge points.

Ice and freeze risk in Los Angeles

Freeze risk: none.

Freeze risk is essentially zero in Los Angeles. Heated gutters are unnecessary and any contractor suggesting them is either uninformed or upselling. The only cold-climate gutter concern in LA is for mountain-adjacent homes in areas like La Canada Flintridge or Mt. Wilson, where occasional frost can occur.

Los Angeles gutter cleaning schedule

Recommended frequency: once per year (late October before rainy season).

Even though LA gets little rain, dry-season dust and debris accumulation can clog gutters and downspouts before the first major storm. A single thorough cleaning in October ensures the system is ready when the rains arrive. Homes near eucalyptus trees may need a second cleaning in mid-winter.

Best time to buy gutters in Los Angeles

Best months: May through September (dry season, low demand).

Worst months: November through February (rainy season, emergency repairs spike demand).
