Rainfall and Drainage in Los Angeles

14.9 inches
annual rainfall in Los Angeles

Los Angeles receives less than 15 inches of rain annually, but nearly all of it falls between November and March. When it rains in LA, it often comes as intense atmospheric river events that can deliver 3-5 inches in 24 hours. This feast-or-famine pattern means gutters sit dry for months and then must handle extreme volume with no warning.

Picking the right gutter size in Los Angeles

A standard 5-inch K-style gutter in Los Angeles handles about 1.2 gallons per second, fine up to roughly 2 inches per hour. When Los Angeles storms exceed that rate (they do), 6-inch gutters carry 40% more water for $2-$3 more per linear foot on Silver Lake, Echo Park, and Sherman Oaks homes.

Downspout placement for Los Angeles homes

Hillside homes in the foothills face particular drainage challenges. Improperly routed downspouts can contribute to slope erosion and mudslide risk. LA building code requires downspout discharge to be directed away from foundations and slopes, and many hillside homes need extended drainage runs to approved discharge points.

Los Angeles homes typically need one downspout per 20-30 linear feet of gutter run. A standard 2x3-inch downspout handles about 600 sq ft of roof area; 3x4-inch oversized downspouts move substantially more water and earn their upcharge on larger roof planes common in Silver Lake, Echo Park, and Sherman Oaks.

Los Angeles Guard Selection Guide

Los Angeles's tree canopy is low to moderate; the prevalent species are palm, eucalyptus, jacaranda, pepper tree. Guard performance hinges on local debris type, and Silver Lake, Echo Park, and Sherman Oaks homes generally share the same mix.

Primary debris types in Los Angeles

Eucalyptus leaves and bark strips, jacaranda flowers in June, palm fronds, and fine dust accumulation during dry months.

Recommended guard style

Reverse-curve or surface-tension guards work well in LA's low-debris environment and shed the occasional heavy rain effectively; micro-mesh can actually over-filter in dusty conditions, causing surface pooling.

Guard cost comparison for Los Angeles

Expect $7-$15/LF for micro-mesh in Los Angeles, $4-$8/LF for screen, $10-$18/LF for reverse-curve, $2-$5/LF for foam (don't). National-brand pricing from LeafFilter or Gutter Helmet lands $15-$35/LF with warranty bundled; a local Los Angeles installer offering the same micro-mesh quality typically beats that by 30-50%.

No gutter guard system is actually maintenance-free, regardless of type. Even top-tier micro-mesh needs surface brushing every 1-2 years to clear fine pollen and dust buildup in Los Angeles. Any Los Angeles contractor pitching "never clean your gutters again" is overselling.

Freeze risk level for Los Angeles: none.

Freeze risk is essentially zero in Los Angeles. Heated gutters are unnecessary and any contractor suggesting them is either uninformed or upselling. Red Flags in Gutter Quotes: What Los Angeles Homeowners Should Watch For

Silver Lake, Echo Park, and Sherman Oaks homeowners see these same four gutter-quote issues repeatedly.

Los Angeles-specific undersizing

At 14.9 inches of annual rain in Los Angeles, a bid that defaults to 5-inch gutters without discussing Silver Lake, Echo Park, and Sherman Oaks roof area or peak rainfall rate is cutting corners. Roof planes over 1,500 sq ft draining to one run want 6-inch capacity in Los Angeles's rain profile.

Pitch errors on Los Angeles installs

Gutters should slope toward downspouts at about 1/4 inch per 10 feet of run. Flat gutters pool water, breed mosquitoes, and overflow in storms. After install, run a simple Los Angeles water test: pour water at the high end and confirm steady flow to the downspout with no pooling. If water stands, pitch needs correction.

Missing drainage on Los Angeles bids

Los Angeles downspouts need splash blocks, extensions, or buried drain tile — without them, 14.9 inches of annual rain lands at the foundation each year. Foundation problems, wet basements, and landscape erosion follow. Los Angeles bids that ignore discharge routing are missing half the job.

Seamed gutters sold as seamless

Seamless = formed on-site from one coil. Sectional = factory pieces joined with connectors. The Los Angeles scam is marketing sectional gutters as "seamless" and charging the seamless premium. Seams cause the majority of gutter failures, so paying for real seamless matters — verify the coil-forming machine showed up to your job.

Los Angeles Gutter Installation: Seasonal Timing

Best months

May through September (dry season, low demand)

Most LA homeowners do not think about gutters until the first big rainstorm exposes a problem. Contractors are flooded with emergency calls during atmospheric river events. Planning your gutter work during dry season gives you better pricing and contractor availability.

Peak pricing / low availability

November through February (rainy season, emergency repairs spike demand)

Los Angeles peak months bring 10-20% labor surcharges and waitlists. Storm-emergency gutter work is always premium-priced — it doesn't follow the regular seasonal pattern.
