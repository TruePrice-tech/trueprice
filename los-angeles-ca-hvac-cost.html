Los Angeles HVAC permits and licensing

Permit authority. LADBS (LA Department of Building and Safety).

Express Permit available at LADBS counter for like-for-like residential replacements, typically same-day. California Title 24 Part 6 energy compliance docs (CF-1R, CF-2R, CF-3R) are mandatory and must be filed by a HERS rater for any ductwork changes

LA City requires HERS testing and Title 24 CF-3R compliance certification at final inspection; any contractor skipping HERS fails the LADBS signoff and you lose the LADWP rebate

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new HVAC system cost in Los Angeles?

Most homeowners in Los Angeles pay between $4,403 and $13,685 for HVAC replacement, depending on system type and efficiency.

Should I replace my AC and furnace at the same time?

Replacing both at once is usually more cost-effective than separate replacements. Matched systems run more efficiently and most contractors offer bundle pricing.

What SEER rating should I choose?

14 SEER is the minimum federal requirement. 16 SEER offers a good balance of efficiency and cost. 18-20+ SEER provides the best energy savings but costs significantly more upfront.
