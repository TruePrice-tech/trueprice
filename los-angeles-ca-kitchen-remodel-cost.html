Los Angeles Permits and Electrical Scope

LADBS issues the kitchen permit through ePlanLA with a plumbing sub-permit for any fixture relocation. Los Angeles Plumbing, Water, and Rough-In

LADWP water runs 380-450 ppm TDS, which is hard enough that unfiltered water stains unsealed stone within months. Point-of-entry softeners are common on Westside remodels despite the Porter Ranch and West Valley outflow restrictions tied to chloride discharge. The Uniform Plumbing Code governs rough-in; LA County amended UPC requires 12-inch from fixture centerline to sidewall which is tighter than the national model code. Slab homes in the San Fernando Valley add core-drilling costs of $600-$1,800 for relocating a sink.

LA kitchen remodels often swap a 40-gallon gas tank for a heat-pump water heater (Rheem ProTerra, AO Smith HPTU) to qualify for the LADWP Consumer Rebate Program ($1,200) plus IRA 25C credit. SoCalGas customers on the West Side are increasingly swapping to all-electric to front-run the next round of Building Decarbonization Ordinance amendments. Installation in a tight laundry closet adds $800-$1,800 versus a garage-located swap.
