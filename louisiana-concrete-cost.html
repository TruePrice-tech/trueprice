Concrete Driveway Cost in Louisiana (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in Louisiana typically run $3,000–$8,400 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 3500 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 6-inch design frost line, and the state sits in no freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F0). Louisiana's combination of alluvial Mississippi River Delta soils across South Louisiana from Baton Rouge to New Orleans, persistent year-round humidity driving rapid concrete set times requiring retarder admixtures, and hurricane wind uplift requirements force post-tension foundation specifications on the majority of Louisiana residential foundation pours — and Louisiana's high water table across the entire state requires waterproof admixtures on every residential basement and below-grade pour, plus continuous epoxy-coated rebar tie schedules on every coastal Louisiana foundation pour to manage Gulf-driven chloride penetration.

Louisiana climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 6-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F0 — no freeze-thaw exposure, no air-entrainment required
  • Typical residential mix: 3500 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: High — expansive clay subsoil requires geotechnical investigation + reinforced footings
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S1 — moderate sulfate exposure, ASTM C150 Type II cement required
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC A — no seismic design considerations
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: Low — minimal winter de-icer use

Louisiana licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Louisiana State Licensing Board for Contractors — Residential Building Contractor or Specialty (official site)
  • Permit: city or parish building permit required for residential concrete pour; LSLBC residential license required above $7,500 contract; coastal hurricane wind-zone post-tension foundation requirements

How concrete driveway costs vary in Louisiana

Louisiana's combination of alluvial Mississippi River Delta soils across South Louisiana from Baton Rouge to New Orleans, persistent year-round humidity driving rapid concrete set times requiring retarder admixtures, and hurricane wind uplift requirements force post-tension foundation specifications on the majority of Louisiana residential foundation pours — and Louisiana's high water table across the entire state requires waterproof admixtures on every residential basement and below-grade pour, plus continuous epoxy-coated rebar tie schedules on every coastal Louisiana foundation pour to manage Gulf-driven chloride penetration.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Louisiana RS §37:2156 requires every Louisiana concrete contractor exceeding the $7,500 residential threshold to hold a Louisiana State Licensing Board for Contractors residential license — and Louisiana Building Code §1804 separately requires post-tension foundation specifications on every residential foundation pour in alluvial South Louisiana parishes from Baton Rouge to New Orleans due to highly variable Mississippi River Delta soil conditions, with LSLBC additionally enforcing Louisiana Code §38:2212 statewide concrete-supplier certification on every residential pour above 5 cu yd to prevent fly-ash and slag substitution.
