Why gutter sizing matters in Louisville

A standard 5-inch K-style gutter in Louisville handles about 1.2 gallons per second, fine up to roughly 2 inches per hour. When Louisville storms exceed that rate (they do), 6-inch gutters carry 40% more water for $2-$3 more per linear foot on Highlands, Crescent Hill, and Old Louisville homes.

Getting downspouts right in Louisville

The Highlands and Cherokee Triangle have early-20th-century homes where original terra-cotta drain tiles connect downspouts directly into the combined sewer system, a practice now discouraged by MSD (Metropolitan Sewer District) due to overflow risk. Modern Louisville practice routes downspout extensions 5-7 feet from the foundation into yard-grade dispersal or dedicated storm-drain connections separate from the sanitary sewer.

Louisville homes typically need one downspout per 20-30 linear feet of gutter run. A standard 2x3-inch downspout handles about 600 sq ft of roof area; 3x4-inch oversized downspouts move substantially more water and earn their upcharge on larger roof planes common in Highlands, Crescent Hill, and Old Louisville.

Which Gutter Guards Actually Work in Louisville

Louisville's tree canopy is moderate to heavy; the prevalent species are Kentucky coffeetree, white oak, tulip poplar. Guard performance hinges on local debris type, and Highlands, Crescent Hill, and Old Louisville homes generally share the same mix.

Gutter guard pricing in Louisville

Micro-mesh guards run $7-$15/LF installed in Louisville. Screen guards $4-$8/LF. Surface-tension (reverse curve) $10-$18/LF. Foam inserts $2-$5/LF (avoid — they trap debris and promote mold). National brands like LeafFilter and Gutter Helmet charge $15-$35/LF, which bakes in warranty and marketing overhead; local Louisville installers using equivalent micro-mesh typically charge 30-50% less.

No gutter guard system is actually maintenance-free, regardless of type. Even top-tier micro-mesh needs surface brushing every 1-2 years to clear fine pollen and dust buildup in Louisville. Any Louisville contractor pitching "never clean your gutters again" is overselling.

Winter gutter basics in Louisville

Cold-weather damage to Louisville gutters is rare but not zero. The non-negotiable item is pitch — at 1/4 inch per 10 feet, water flows out rather than sitting long enough to freeze during rare Louisville cold events.

Louisville Gutter Quote Red Flags

The Louisville gutter-industry patterns worth flagging before signing anything.

Undersized gutters for Louisville rainfall

Louisville receives 45 inches of rain annually. A contractor bidding standard 5-inch gutters without mentioning Louisville rainfall intensity or your specific roof area is sizing for convenience, not performance. Homes in Highlands, Crescent Hill, and Old Louisville with roof areas over 1,500 sq ft draining to a single run should default to 6-inch gutters.

Flat Louisville gutters as installed

Gutters should slope toward downspouts at about 1/4 inch per 10 feet of run. Flat gutters pool water, breed mosquitoes, and overflow in storms. After install, run a simple Louisville water test: pour water at the high end and confirm steady flow to the downspout with no pooling. If water stands, pitch needs correction.

Downspouts dumping at the foundation

Discharge routing is the half of a Louisville gutter project contractors most often skip. Given 45 inches of Louisville rainfall annually, dumping water at the foundation causes foundation movement, basement moisture, and yard erosion. A proper Louisville bid addresses where the water goes, not just how it gets off the roof.

Sectional gutters priced as seamless

Seamless = formed on-site from one coil. Sectional = factory pieces joined with connectors. The Louisville scam is marketing sectional gutters as "seamless" and charging the seamless premium. Seams cause the majority of gutter failures, so paying for real seamless matters — verify the coil-forming machine showed up to your job.
