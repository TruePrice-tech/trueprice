Questions Lubbock homeowners ask about concrete

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

How much does a concrete foundation cost?

A full basement foundation costs $15,000-$30,000. A slab foundation for a house costs $5,000-$15,000. A garage slab costs $2,500-$6,000. Price depends on size, depth, and soil conditions.

How long before I can drive on new concrete?

Wait 24-48 hours for foot traffic, 7 days for vehicle traffic, and 30 days for full cure. Avoid deicing salt for the first winter. New concrete reaches 90% strength in 7 days and full strength in 28 days.

What is the best time to pour concrete?

Pour when temperatures stay between 50-80F for at least 3 days. Spring and fall are ideal. Avoid pouring in rain, direct hot sun, or below freezing. Cold and heat both compromise cure quality.

More questions about concrete in Lubbock

What does a concrete driveway cost per square foot?

Plain 4-inch concrete runs $6-$12 per square foot installed in 2026. Stamped concrete adds $4-$8/sqft for pattern and color. Heavy-duty 6-inch driveways for RVs or trucks cost $9-$15/sqft. A 600 sq ft two-car driveway typically lands between $5,800 and $8,100.

How much does a 1,000 sq ft concrete patio cost?

Typical 2026 ranges for a 1,000 sq ft project: plain broom-finish $8,000-$12,000, exposed-aggregate $9,500-$14,500, stamped $11,000-$16,500. Tearing out an existing slab adds $1,500-$3,000. Reinforcing wire mesh and rebar are usually $0.50-$1.00/sqft and worth it on driveways, optional on patios.

What does a slab foundation cost in Lubbock?

A monolithic slab foundation for a typical single-family house in Lubbock runs $5,000-$15,000 depending on square footage and soil conditions. A garage slab is $2,500-$6,000. Full basement foundations are a different scope entirely and run $15,000-$30,000.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Lubbock?

Driveways, patios over a certain size, and any structural slab in Lubbock require a permit and an inspection of the base prep and rebar before the pour. Sidewalks, repairs, and resurfacing usually do not. Your contractor pulls the permit — never pull it in your own name.

About concrete work in Lubbock, TX

Where Lubbock's pricing comes from. Lubbock's labor market runs about 21% below the national median for skilled trades. This wage differential is the primary driver of the area's below-average project costs. On concrete work, Lubbock comes in about 12% under the national average based on our metro pricing data.

Local conditions in Lubbock. Lubbock has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Lubbock's dry climate requires proper curing attention — concrete that dries too fast develops surface cracks. Keep newly poured concrete wet for 7 days using curing compound or plastic sheeting. Air-entrained mix is recommended for any surfaces exposed to winter freezes. Standard concrete in Lubbock runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Lubbock permits and code requirements. Permits are usually required for concrete work in Lubbock; verify the specifics with the local building department before committing.

The Lubbock contractor pool. With a population of 239,538, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. The Lubbock market has grown at a steady pace, producing a solid base of experienced contractors without the severe backlogs of booming cities.

How the local trade market looks. With steady but not explosive growth, Lubbock's contractor market offers reasonable availability. Best months to book. Lubbock allows year-round concrete work, but the ideal window is spring and fall when temperatures support proper curing without the heat-related challenges of summer. Avoid scheduling large pours during the hottest months — early morning pours are the only reliable option.

What drives cost. Lubbock homeowners benefit from labor rates 21% below national medians. For a driveway or patio pour, this translates to savings of $630-1260 compared to the national average. Newer construction in Lubbock (averaging 28 years) means concrete work projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay.

Red flags to watch for. Watch for concrete work quotes in Lubbock that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately.
