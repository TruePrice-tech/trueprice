Questions Lubbock homeowners ask about concrete

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

How much does a concrete foundation cost?

A full basement foundation costs $15,000-$30,000. A slab foundation for a house costs $5,000-$15,000. A garage slab costs $2,500-$6,000. Price depends on size, depth, and soil conditions.

How long before I can drive on new concrete?

Wait 24-48 hours for foot traffic, 7 days for vehicle traffic, and 30 days for full cure. Avoid deicing salt for the first winter. New concrete reaches 90% strength in 7 days and full strength in 28 days.

What is the best time to pour concrete?

Pour when temperatures stay between 50-80F for at least 3 days. Spring and fall are ideal. Avoid pouring in rain, direct hot sun, or below freezing. Cold and heat both compromise cure quality.

What warranty should I expect?

Expect a minimum 1-year workmanship warranty from the contractor plus manufacturer warranties on materials. Get warranty terms in writing before signing.

Should I get multiple quotes?

Always get at least 3 written quotes. Compare line-item details, not just total price. The lowest quote often omits scope items that appear as change orders later.

Check licensing through your state's contractor board, verify insurance, read reviews from Lubbock homeowners, and get at least 3 written quotes with line-item detail.

Never pay more than 10-15% as a deposit before work begins. Standard payment structure: deposit, progress payment when materials arrive, final payment on completion.

About concrete work in Lubbock, TX

Why pricing varies in Lubbock. Labor rates here run roughly 21% below the national average per BLS metro data, which is the main reason this city's pricing sits below the national midpoint. For concrete work specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Lubbock at roughly 13% below the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Lubbock has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Lubbock's dry climate requires proper curing attention — concrete that dries too fast develops surface cracks. Keep newly poured concrete wet for 7 days using curing compound or plastic sheeting. Air-entrained mix is recommended for any surfaces exposed to winter freezes. Standard concrete in Lubbock runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Lubbock require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 239,538, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Lubbock has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.

Lubbock, TX concrete work: local considerations

Home stock in Lubbock. Lubbock's housing stock averages 28 years old — relatively recent construction that was largely built to modern code. concrete work projects here tend to be simpler: fewer surprise compliance issues, quicker permit turnaround, and contractors who can quote from memory.

HOA & compliance. HOAs are common in Lubbock. For concrete work, factor in 2-4 weeks of HOA architectural review before starting, and collect pre-approval of materials in writing. HOA rejections after project start can be expensive.

Local market conditions. Lubbock has moderate construction activity. Contractors are busy but accessible with 1-3 weeks' notice. Off-season quotes (contractor-dependent) can run 8-15% below peak rates. Lubbock's large market has concrete contractors ranging from 1-person decorative specialists to large commercial outfits that also do residential. For standard flatwork (driveways, patios), mid-size companies with dedicated residential crews typically offer the best combination of pricing and quality.

Seasonal timing. Lubbock allows year-round concrete work, but the ideal window is spring and fall when temperatures support proper curing without the heat-related challenges of summer. Avoid scheduling large pours during the hottest months — early morning pours are the only reliable option.

What drives cost. Lubbock homeowners benefit from labor rates 21% below national medians. For a driveway or patio pour, this translates to savings of $630-1260 compared to the national average. Newer construction in Lubbock (averaging 28 years) means concrete work projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay.

Red flags to watch for. Watch for concrete work quotes in Lubbock that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Lump-sum bids hide margin and make change orders impossible to evaluate. Check that any Lubbock contractor doing concrete work carries both general liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers' compensation. Request certificates directly from the insurer, not just copies the contractor provides.
