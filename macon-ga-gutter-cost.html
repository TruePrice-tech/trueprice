What Macon residents ask about gutter

How much do new gutters cost in Macon?

Gutters in Macon cost $6-$15 per linear foot for aluminum seamless, $8-$20 for steel, and $15-$30 for copper. A typical home (150-200 linear feet) runs $1,200-$3,000 for aluminum.

What causes gutters to pull away from the house?

Common causes: rotted fascia board behind the gutter, failed or spaced-too-far-apart hangers, ice and snow weight, clogged gutters holding heavy water, and improper original installation.

Do gutters protect the foundation?

Yes. Gutters prevent concentrated roof runoff from eroding soil next to the foundation and saturating the ground. Homes without gutters have 3-5x more foundation moisture problems than homes with properly functioning gutter systems.

Where Macon's pricing comes from. Macon's labor market runs about 16% below the national median for skilled trades. Local conditions in Macon. Macon has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Gutters in Macon must handle heavy summer downpours and constant debris from vigorous plant growth. Oversize to 6-inch K-style minimum and add kickout flashing where gutters meet siding — undersized 5-inch gutters overflow during the intense rain events common here. Leaf guards are essential with the year-round vegetation. Gutter guard systems in Macon range from $5-8 per linear foot for mesh screens to $15-25 for micro-mesh or reverse-curve systems. The payoff depends on tree cover — homes with heavy canopy cover recoup the investment in 3-5 years through eliminated cleaning costs ($150-300 per cleaning, 2-4 times per year).
