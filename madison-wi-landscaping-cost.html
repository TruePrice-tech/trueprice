Frequently asked landscaping questions

How much does outdoor lighting cost?

Professional landscape lighting costs $2,000-$5,000 for path lights, uplights, and accent lighting. LED fixtures cost more upfront but last 50,000+ hours. Solar options cost less but provide dimmer, less reliable light.

How much does a retaining wall cost?

Retaining walls cost $20-$50 per square face foot for concrete block, $25-$60 for natural stone, and $15-$30 for timber. A 50 linear foot wall at 3 feet tall runs $3,000-$9,000 installed.

What is xeriscaping?

Xeriscaping uses drought-tolerant plants, efficient irrigation, and permeable surfaces to minimize water use. It cuts outdoor water consumption 50-75% and is increasingly required in water-restricted regions.

Do I need a permit for landscaping in Madison?

Most landscaping in Madison does not require permits. Exceptions include retaining walls over 4 feet, grading that changes drainage, connecting to city water for irrigation, and work near property lines.

Local factors: landscaping in Madison, WI

Madison's housing profile. With average home age near 47 years, Madison homes are at the typical first-replacement milestone for major systems. Original furnaces, AC units, water heaters, and roofs installed when the homes were built are aging out. landscaping is hitting peak demand in this age band.

HOA and neighborhood rules. About half of Madison's newer subdivisions have HOA governance. Check whether your community requires architectural review for landscaping work. Even when HOAs exist here, approval timelines tend to be shorter than in heavily regulated markets.

Contractor market picture. The Madison contractor market is balanced: enough work to keep shops busy, but enough competition that homeowners can still get timely service. Booking 1-3 weeks ahead typically secures your preferred contractor. Madison's large market supports full-service landscape design firms, installation-only crews, and maintenance companies. For projects over $8,000, a dedicated design firm with installation capability typically provides better results than splitting design and installation across separate companies.

Timing considerations. The landscaping season in Madison runs April through October. Spring planting (April-May) is the busiest period as homeowners and contractors rush to install before summer heat. Fall planting (September-October) is the insider move — plants establish roots before dormancy and emerge stronger the following spring.

What drives cost. The biggest factor in Madison landscaping project pricing is labor cost, running 24% above national benchmarks. For a full yard makeover, that premium alone accounts for $960-1920 in additional cost. Madison's housing stock averages 47 years — the age where original installations start failing and code requirements have evolved. Most landscaping project quotes will include some code-catch-up items that newer homes wouldn't need.

Red flags to watch for. Any Madison contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion. Watch for landscaping project quotes in Madison that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Lump-sum bids hide margin and make change orders impossible to evaluate.
