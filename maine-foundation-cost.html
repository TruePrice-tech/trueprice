Foundation Cost in Maine (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in Maine typically run $19,400–$44,400 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with full basement pour as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 60-inch design frost line. Maine's combination of 60-inch frost depth across the northern half (Aroostook County and Penobscot County), F3 freeze-thaw exposure driving 6-8% air-entrainment specification, statewide radon zone 1 designation forcing sub-slab depressurization rough-in on every residential basement pour, and Maine coastal Atlantic salt-spray on every Cumberland, York, Lincoln, Knox, Waldo, Hancock, and Washington county exposure drives aggressive frost-protected footing depth (5-foot minimum in Aroostook), corrosion-protected reinforcement on every coastal pour, and continuous capillary-break crushed-stone on every Maine residential foundation.

Maine climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 60-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Full basement — dominant in Upper Midwest, Northeast, and Mid-Atlantic on freeze-thaw + radon-zone-1 sites
  • Expansive soil risk: Low — sandy or stable subsoil, standard slab-on-grade design
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC B — minor seismic risk, standard tie-down requirements
  • Hurricane wind tier: Low — sub-110 mph design wind, minimal tie-down detailing
  • Water table risk: Moderate — water table below frost line in most lots
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • IECC climate zone: 6A-7
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 1 (highest) — passive sub-slab depressurization rough-in required on every basement and slab pour

Maine licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: Maine has no statewide residential builder license — Maine Office of Professional & Financial Regulation oversees specialty trades only (official site)
  • Permit: town or municipal building permit required for residential foundation pour; no state foundation contractor license; Maine MUBEC adoption mandates IRC + IECC compliance; statewide radon zone 1 mandates sub-slab depressurization rough-in
