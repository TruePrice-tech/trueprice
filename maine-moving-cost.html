Moving Cost in Maine (2026)

Hiring movers in Maine for a local 2-bedroom 50-mile household-goods move typically runs $500–$1,400, with moderate state intrastate regulation under the Maine Public Utilities Commission (MPUC) — Transportation Division. Maine's combination of MPUC Transportation Division intrastate HHG regulation under 35-A M.R.S.A. § 7102, $18.45/hr BLS Laborers and Movers mean wage, seasonal vacation-home migration patterns producing one of the most seasonally-volatile move markets in the country, Portland + Bangor + Augusta metro market concentration, voluntary blanket tariff filing regime, MPUC complaint and disciplinary authority, and significant Greater Portland-to-Boston-suburban-NH outbound migration patterns in addition to inbound vacation-home moves.

Maine intrastate regulator, tariff & valuation drivers

  • Intrastate regulator type: Public Utility Commission (PUC) regulation
  • State regulator name: Maine Public Utilities Commission (MPUC) — Transportation Division
  • Tariff filing requirement: Voluntary blanket tariff filing — carriers may file or charge market rates
  • Valuation disclosure (FVP vs RVP): Federal FMCSA Carmack Amendment Released Value rule applies (no state supplement)
  • Intrastate license requirement: Yes — state intrastate operating authority permit required
  • Overall regulation intensity: Moderate — state operating authority + voluntary tariff filing regime
  • Typical mover hourly labor rate (BLS SOC 53-7062 mean): $18.45/hr

Maine mover licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Maine Public Utilities Commission (MPUC) — Transportation Division (official site)
  • Permit: Maine PUC Certificate of Public Convenience and Necessity for intrastate household-goods carriers under 35-A M.R.S.A. § 7102; voluntary blanket tariff filing; minimum federal liability + cargo coverage; MPUC complaint and disciplinary authority

How household-goods moving costs vary in Maine

Maine's combination of MPUC Transportation Division intrastate HHG regulation under 35-A M.R.S.A. § 7102, $18.45/hr BLS Laborers and Movers mean wage, seasonal vacation-home migration patterns producing one of the most seasonally-volatile move markets in the country, Portland + Bangor + Augusta metro market concentration, voluntary blanket tariff filing regime, MPUC complaint and disciplinary authority, and significant Greater Portland-to-Boston-suburban-NH outbound migration patterns in addition to inbound vacation-home moves.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Maine Public Utilities Commission (MPUC) Transportation Division regulates intrastate household-goods carriers under 35-A M.R.S.A. § 7102 — MPUC operates a moderate intrastate regulatory framework requiring a Certificate of Public Convenience and Necessity for any intrastate household-goods carrier with voluntary blanket tariff filing, and Maine's seasonal vacation-home migration patterns (Greater Portland and Mid-Coast Maine receive significant May-October seasonal inbound moves followed by November-April outbound moves to FL/AZ/NC) produce one of the most seasonally-volatile intrastate move markets in the country.
