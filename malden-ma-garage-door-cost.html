Garage Door questions for Malden homeowners

What R-value should my garage door have?

R-8 is fine if the garage is detached or unheated. R-12 to R-16 is best for attached garages or if you use the space for a workshop. R-18+ is for extreme cold climates or heated garages.

Are smart garage door openers worth it?

Smart openers add $100-$200 over basic models. They let you open, close, and monitor the door from your phone, set schedules, and receive alerts. Useful for deliveries and forgetting to close.

How much does garage door spring replacement cost?

Torsion spring replacement costs $200-$400 for parts and labor. Extension springs cost $150-$300. Never attempt spring replacement yourself since the stored tension is extremely dangerous.

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

What R-value garage door do I need in Malden?

For Malden's cold winters, an R-12 to R-16 insulated door is recommended for attached garages. If you heat your garage or use it as a workshop, step up to R-16 or R-18. The extra $200-$500 for higher insulation pays back in 3-5 years through reduced heating costs in adjacent rooms and prevents freezing issues with items stored in the garage.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Malden?

With Malden homes averaging 57 years, many still have original garage doors past their useful life. A full replacement ($1,200-$3,000 with a new opener) typically returns 90-100% at resale. Repair is worth it only for newer doors with isolated issues like a single broken spring, damaged panel, or failing opener motor. If the door is original to the house, replacement is the better investment.

Best time of year to replace a garage door in Malden?

The ideal window for a Malden garage door replacement is late spring or early fall. Moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing, plus you get access to a wider range of styles and colors. Avoid spring if you can, since that is when winter-related spring breakages create peak demand and longer wait times.
