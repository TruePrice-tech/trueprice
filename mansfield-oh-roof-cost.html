Frequently asked roofing questions

What is a roofing square?

A roofing square equals 100 square feet. A typical 2,000 sqft roof is about 20 squares. Contractors price materials and labor per square.

Should I repair or replace my roof?

If damage covers less than 30% of the roof and the roof is under 15 years old, repair is usually sufficient. Beyond that, replacement provides better long-term value.

Should I choose the lowest roofing quote?

Not always. Lower bids sometimes exclude flashing, ventilation upgrades, or disposal costs that appear later as change orders. Compare scope, not just price.

How long does a roof replacement take?

Most residential roofs take 1-3 days to replace. Complex roofs with multiple layers, steep pitch, or extensive damage can take 4-7 days.

How do I prevent ice dams on my Mansfield roof?

Ice dams form when heat escapes through the roof, melting snow that refreezes at the eaves. Prevention starts with proper attic insulation (R-49 or higher for Mansfield's climate zone) and continuous soffit-to-ridge ventilation. Your roofing quote should include ice and water shield membrane on the first 3-6 feet from all eaves. If your current roof has ice dam damage, address the insulation and ventilation before replacing the roof or the problem will recur.

Is a Mansfield permit needed to replace my roof?

A permit is typically required for roof replacement in Mansfield. The contractor handles the permit process. Going without one risks voiding your homeowners insurance and creates legal complications at resale since you must disclose unpermitted work.

When should I replace a roof in Mansfield?

Schedule your Mansfield roof replacement during late spring or early fall if possible. Materials install best above 45F and before snow season pricing surcharges, and off-peak timing often means lower installation costs since contractors compete harder for work. Expect to pay a 10-15% premium if you schedule during the post-storm rush when demand peaks.
