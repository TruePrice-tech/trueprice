Gutter Installation Cost in Maryland (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Maryland typically runs $1,200–$4,500, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 44 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Maryland's 44-inch annual rainfall combined with high mature-deciduous tree canopy across Montgomery, Prince George's, and Baltimore counties drives near-universal gutter-guard retrofit demand on long-tenure suburban properties — and the Chesapeake Bay coastal flood-elevation overlay drives below-grade downspout-discharge re-routing on every Anne Arundel and St. Mary's county tidewater retrofit.

Maryland rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 44 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 25 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: Moderate — periodic ice-dam events on north-facing eaves
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — 110–140 mph design wind
  • Tree-canopy density: High — mature deciduous canopy drives gutter-guard demand and 2x annual cleaning frequency

Maryland licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Maryland Home Improvement Commission (MHIC) (official site)
  • Permit: county building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction; Baltimore City separate inspection

How gutter installation costs vary in Maryland

Maryland's 44-inch annual rainfall combined with high mature-deciduous tree canopy across Montgomery, Prince George's, and Baltimore counties drives near-universal gutter-guard retrofit demand on long-tenure suburban properties — and the Chesapeake Bay coastal flood-elevation overlay drives below-grade downspout-discharge re-routing on every Anne Arundel and St. Mary's county tidewater retrofit.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Maryland Home Improvement Commission (MHIC) requires every contractor performing residential exterior work above $1,000 to hold an MHIC license — and unique to Maryland, the MHIC administers a Guaranty Fund that compensates homeowners up to $30,000 per project for licensed-contractor abandonment or negligence, funded by MHIC license fees, with claims reviewed by an Administrative Law Judge under MHIC §8-405.
