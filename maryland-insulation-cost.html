Attic Insulation Upgrade Cost in Maryland (2026)

Attic insulation upgrades in Maryland typically run $1,900–$7,200 for a 1,500 sq ft attic upgrade R-30 to R-49 blown cellulose, with blown cellulose loose-fill the dominant residential retrofit material at the IECC R-49 attic minimum. Maryland's combination of 4,800 annual heating degree days, 1,300 cooling degree days, and Chesapeake Bay watershed humidity creates a balanced heating-cooling insulation profile — and Maryland's pre-1978 housing-stock concentration in Baltimore rowhouse districts triggers combination air-sealing + dense-pack cellulose wall cavity + blown-cellulose attic upgrades on the majority of urban Maryland insulation retrofit scope, with brick-veneer rowhouse design requiring specialty narrow-cavity dense-pack cellulose installation through interior wall penetrations.

Maryland energy code & climate-load drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A
  • Required attic R-value: R-49 (IECC 2021 prescriptive minimum)
  • Required wall R-value: R-20 (above-grade wood-frame)
  • Adopted energy code basis: IECC 2021 — current cycle, full mandatory R-49 attic + R-20 wall + air-sealing requirements
  • Stretch code available: Yes — opt-in or mandatory above-baseline pathway
  • Annual heating + cooling load: 4,800 HDD / 1,300 CDD
  • Vapor barrier requirement: Warm-side vapor retarder required (typical IECC zones 5+)
  • Dominant attic insulation material: Blown cellulose loose-fill — dominant in heating-dominant Northeast and Upper Midwest
  • IRA HOMES + HEER rebate status: Live — IRA HOMES + HEER rebates currently available to residential homeowners

Maryland licensing, rebates & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Maryland Home Improvement Commission (MHIC) — Home Improvement Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: permit required for attic insulation retrofit per Maryland Building Performance Standards; MHIC license required above $500 contract

How attic insulation upgrade costs vary in Maryland

State-specific code or insurance rule: Maryland Building Performance Standards (Annotated Code §12-503) imposes the most restrictive statewide energy code in the Mid-Atlantic — Maryland adopted IECC 2021 with state-specific air-sealing requirements that exceed IECC baseline, plus Maryland HB 831 (2022) established the Maryland Energy Administration EmPOWER Maryland weatherization program providing rebates for attic insulation upgrades layered with IRA HOMES + HEER funding, doubling effective Maryland residential insulation rebate amounts in 2024-2025.
