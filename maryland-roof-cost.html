Roof Replacement Cost in Maryland (2026)

Roof replacement in Maryland typically runs $9,000–$23,500 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. Eastern Shore (Worcester, Wicomico, Somerset) sits inside the Atlantic hurricane envelope with 130-mph design wind; Western Maryland (Garrett, Allegany) carries a 50-psf snow load and routine ice-dam exposure.

Maryland climate & code drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate wind exposure — 110–140 mph design wind on coastal counties
  • Hail risk tier: Low — minimal severe hail history
  • Ground snow load: 30 psf — moderate snow load, ice-and-water shield required
  • Dominant residential roof material: asphalt shingle

Maryland licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Maryland Home Improvement Commission — MHIC license required for any roofing job over $500 (official site)
  • Permit: county permit (Montgomery, Prince George's, Baltimore, Anne Arundel)

How roof replacement costs vary in Maryland

Eastern Shore (Worcester, Wicomico, Somerset) sits inside the Atlantic hurricane envelope with 130-mph design wind; Western Maryland (Garrett, Allegany) carries a 50-psf snow load and routine ice-dam exposure.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Maryland's Guaranty Fund (MD Code Bus. Reg. §8-405) reimburses homeowners up to $20,000 per licensed-contractor dispute — verifying MHIC license status before signing is the highest-leverage homeowner-side check in the state.
