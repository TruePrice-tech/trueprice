Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in Massachusetts (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in Massachusetts typically run $2,900–$7,000 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2023 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Service entrance design assumes a 200A minimum on new residential construction. Cape Cod and South Shore coastal salt-haze deposition shortens galvanized service-mast life to 7-10 years, mandating stainless mounting hardware and NEMA 4X meter sockets on shoreline upgrades — and aging Boston, Worcester, and Springfield triple-decker housing stock with knob-and-tube branch wiring forces complete rewire scope on most pre-1950 panel upgrades to satisfy 527 CMR 12 §250 grounding electrode replacement requirements.

Massachusetts code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2023 NEC — current cycle with expanded GFCI on dwelling outdoor and 250V circuits, EV charging requirements
  • State amendments: Massachusetts Electrical Code (527 CMR 12) adopts 2023 NEC with state amendments preserving Mass-specific apprentice ratios, AFCI on dwelling unit branch circuits, and arc-fault TRs
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: Moderate — typical interior continental flash density
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 30-inch+ design depth — buried service conduit must clear hard frost line

Massachusetts licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Massachusetts Board of State Examiners of Electricians (official site)
  • Permit: municipal electrical permit required for panel upgrade and any service work; CSL filing required separately

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in Massachusetts

State-specific code or insurance rule: Massachusetts 527 CMR 12 is one of the most heavily amended NEC adoptions in the country — the Massachusetts Electrical Code retains state-specific apprentice-to-journeyman ratios, mandatory continuing-education requirements every cycle, and prohibition of multi-wire branch circuits in dwelling unit AFCI installations — and Master Electrician (A), Journeyman (B), and Master Restricted (C) are three separately tested Board credentials.
