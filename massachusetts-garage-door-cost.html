Massachusetts climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 130 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating at 110-140 mph design wind
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Moderate — periodic termite pressure, PT framing recommended
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Moderate — suburban HOA prevalence on newer subdivisions
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High — coastal salt-spray drives stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track requirement at 5-7 year intervals
  • IECC climate zone: 5A

Massachusetts licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Massachusetts Office of Consumer Affairs & Business Regulation — Construction Supervisor License (CSL) + Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) Registration (official site)
  • Permit: city or town building permit required for new garage-door opening; CSL required for new-construction; HIC registration required for any home-improvement contract above $1,000; MA State Building Code 780 CMR 9th Edition mandatory; Massachusetts Stretch Code adoption in 280 of 351 municipalities driving above-baseline R-value

How garage door installation costs vary in Massachusetts

Massachusetts's combination of 48-inch frost depth across western MA, F2-F3 freeze-thaw cycling driving sealant degradation on every garage-door bottom seal at 3-5 year intervals, persistent Atlantic salt-spray on every Plymouth, Barnstable, Dukes, Nantucket, and Essex county exposure driving stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track replacement at 5-7 year intervals, 130 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed across coastal counties driving DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating, and Massachusetts Stretch Code adoption in 280 of 351 municipalities drives unique cold-climate-and-coastal-and-stretch-code-driven material selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Massachusetts requires both a Construction Supervisor License (CSL) for new-construction garage-door work plus a Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) Registration for replacement work above $1,000 contract — making MA one of the only states with dual licensing for new-construction-vs-replacement garage-door work, with the MA Office of Consumer Affairs Guaranty Fund compensating affected homeowners up to $10,000 per HIC-contractor abandonment claim, and Massachusetts Stretch Code adoption in 280 of 351 municipalities separately mandates above-IECC-2021-baseline R-13 minimum garage-door insulation on every residential installation in the adopting jurisdictions.
