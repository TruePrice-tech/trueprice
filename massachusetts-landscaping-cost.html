Landscape Installation Cost in Massachusetts (2026)

Landscape installation in Massachusetts typically runs $4,800–$20,800 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 5b-7a. Massachusetts's combination of acidic glacial-till soils across the Berkshires, Connecticut River Valley, and South Coast, mature deciduous canopy across suburban Boston from Newton to Concord to Wellesley, and 175-day growing season requires lime application as a standard component of every Massachusetts turf install — and the state's sandy outwash soils across Cape Cod, Martha's Vineyard, and Nantucket combined with restrictive Cape Cod Commission Land Use Bylaw require nitrogen-fixing native plants and reduced-fertilizer landscape design on every Cape and Islands residential property to protect coastal pond and estuary water quality.

Massachusetts climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 5b-7a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 47 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 175 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Moderate — periodic drought, advisory water-saving guidelines
  • Irrigation dependency: Low — irrigation optional, established plants survive on rainfall
  • Frost-line trench depth: 48-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

Massachusetts licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Massachusetts Department of Agricultural Resources Pesticide Program + Massachusetts Office of Consumer Affairs HIC registration (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; HIC registration required above $1,000 contract; MDAR Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care; municipal turf pesticide ordinances in 30+ Massachusetts towns

How landscape installation costs vary in Massachusetts

Massachusetts's combination of acidic glacial-till soils across the Berkshires, Connecticut River Valley, and South Coast, mature deciduous canopy across suburban Boston from Newton to Concord to Wellesley, and 175-day growing season requires lime application as a standard component of every Massachusetts turf install — and the state's sandy outwash soils across Cape Cod, Martha's Vineyard, and Nantucket combined with restrictive Cape Cod Commission Land Use Bylaw require nitrogen-fixing native plants and reduced-fertilizer landscape design on every Cape and Islands residential property to protect coastal pond and estuary water quality.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Massachusetts MGL Ch 132B §6F imposes the Massachusetts Children's & Families' Protection Act on every residential pesticide application — every Massachusetts commercial landscape contractor applying restricted-use pesticides on residential property within 75 ft of a child-occupied structure must provide 72-hour advance written notification, plus 30+ Massachusetts towns including Newton, Cambridge, Marblehead, and Wellesley have separately adopted municipal turf-pesticide ordinances banning synthetic pesticide application on residential lawns, making Massachusetts the most restrictive Northeast state on residential landscape pesticide use after Connecticut.
