Exterior Painting Cost in Massachusetts (2026)

Repainting a 1,500 sq ft home in Massachusetts typically runs $3,800–$10,800, with wood lap or shake siding the dominant residential substrate driving prep scope. Massachusetts's combination of 100+ annual freeze-thaw cycles in interior climate zone 5A-6A, North Atlantic salt-spray on Cape Cod and the South Shore, and the highest pre-1978 housing-stock density in the country drives the most expensive average exterior repaint cycle in New England — Boston, Cambridge, Worcester, and Springfield wood-lap repaints typically average 30-50% higher per-square-foot than national norms once MassDPH lead-safe procedures and HIC Guaranty Fund participation are factored into contractor pricing.

Massachusetts climate & coatings drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 5A-6A
  • Dominant residential substrate: Wood lap or shake — dominant pre-1990 substrate, drives full prep + caulk + prime + 2-coat
  • Annual humidity tier: Moderate — typical continental humidity, standard topcoat formulations adequate
  • UV exposure: Moderate — typical continental UV, standard binder packages adequate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: Moderate-corrosion coastal — marine primer recommended on bayfront and tidal exposures
  • Freeze-thaw cycling: Very high — 100+ annual freeze-thaw cycles drive caulk-and-recaulk on every repaint
  • VOC architectural coatings rule: OTC Phase II — 100 g/L flat exterior topcoat limit (Northeast/Mid-Atlantic)

Massachusetts licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Massachusetts Office of Consumer Affairs — Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration; MassDPH Lead-Safe Renovator required for pre-1978 work (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for repaint; HIC registration required above $1,000 contract; MassDPH Lead-Safe Renovator required for pre-1978 prep

How exterior repaint costs vary in Massachusetts

Massachusetts's combination of 100+ annual freeze-thaw cycles in interior climate zone 5A-6A, North Atlantic salt-spray on Cape Cod and the South Shore, and the highest pre-1978 housing-stock density in the country drives the most expensive average exterior repaint cycle in New England — Boston, Cambridge, Worcester, and Springfield wood-lap repaints typically average 30-50% higher per-square-foot than national norms once MassDPH lead-safe procedures and HIC Guaranty Fund participation are factored into contractor pricing.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Massachusetts 105 CMR 460 imposes the most restrictive lead-paint regime in the country on rental housing — every Massachusetts repaint that disturbs pre-1978 painted surfaces in housing units occupied by children under six must achieve a MassDPH Letter of Compliance (deleading) or Letter of Interim Control, requiring a licensed deleader plus MassDPH project notification, and the Massachusetts HIC Guaranty Fund compensates homeowners up to $10,000 per project for HIC-contractor breach on residential repaint contracts.
