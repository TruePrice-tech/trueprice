Roof Replacement Cost in Massachusetts (2026)

Roof replacement in Massachusetts typically runs $11,200–$28,500 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. Cape Cod, Nantucket, and Martha's Vineyard sit on the 140-mph wind-design line and require enhanced fastening (six nails, ring-shank) and storm-rated starter strips; salt exposure shortens galvanized fastener life on ocean-facing slopes.

Massachusetts climate & code drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 5A
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate wind exposure — 110–140 mph design wind on coastal counties
  • Hail risk tier: Low — minimal severe hail history
  • Ground snow load: 35 psf — moderate snow load, ice-and-water shield required
  • Dominant residential roof material: asphalt shingle

Massachusetts licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Massachusetts Office of Consumer Affairs — Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration; CSL (Construction Supervisor License) for structural work (official site)
  • Permit: town/city permit required statewide

How roof replacement costs vary in Massachusetts

State-specific code or insurance rule: Massachusetts has the country's most consumer-protective home-improvement framework: the Guaranty Fund (M.G.L. c.142A §5) reimburses up to $10,000 per claim against registered contractors, and the Attorney General's office actively prosecutes unregistered roofers under Chapter 93A.
