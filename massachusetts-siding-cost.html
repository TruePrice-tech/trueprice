Siding Replacement Cost in Massachusetts (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Massachusetts typically runs $12,500–$32,500, with vinyl panel the dominant residential cladding choice. Cape Cod, North Shore, and South Shore Atlantic salt-haze deposition corrodes galvanized fasteners on shoreline re-sides within 5-7 years, mandating stainless on all coastal cedar lap, fiber-cement, and brick-veneer wall ties — and aging Boston, Cambridge, and Worcester triple-decker housing stock with cedar lap, asbestos-cement shingle, and original 1900s vinyl reaching end-of-life forces complete envelope re-side scope including WRB upgrade and rainscreen retrofit.

Massachusetts climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 5A
  • Dominant residential cladding: Vinyl panel — dominant low-budget retrofit choice in non-WUI, non-coastal corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: High wind exposure — 140 mph+ design wind on coastal counties
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: No statewide WUI cladding mandate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High-corrosion coastal — stainless or hot-dipped galvanized fasteners required within 3,000 ft of saltwater

Massachusetts licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Massachusetts Office of Consumer Affairs and Business Regulation — Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration + Construction Supervisor License (CSL) (official site)
  • Permit: town building permit required; CSL holder must sign off on structural work

How siding replacement costs vary in Massachusetts

State-specific code or insurance rule: Massachusetts requires both a Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration AND a Construction Supervisor License (CSL) — the HIC registration is for the contracting business entity while the CSL is the personal credential for the individual responsible for code compliance, with both required on every residential re-side and the CSL holder personally liable for envelope code violations under 780 CMR R107.
