How much does concrete work cost in Memphis?

Concrete costs in Memphis range from $6-$12 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, patios) to $15-$30/sqft for stamped or decorative finishes, including labor and materials.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

How much does a concrete foundation cost?

A full basement foundation costs $15,000-$30,000. A slab foundation for a house costs $5,000-$15,000. A garage slab costs $2,500-$6,000. Price depends on size, depth, and soil conditions.

What Memphis homeowners should know about concrete work

Why pricing varies in Memphis. Labor rates here run roughly 8% below the national average per BLS metro data, which is the main reason this city's pricing sits below the national midpoint. Our analysis shows concrete work in Memphis running roughly 5% below the US baseline.

Memphis's site and climate factors. Memphis has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Memphis's freeze-thaw cycles and moderate rainfall both affect concrete performance. Air-entrained mix is standard for outdoor flatwork, and expansion joints every 10-12 feet prevent the cracking that temperature swings cause. Proper drainage away from slabs prevents the water infiltration that freeze-thaw cycles exploit. Homes in Memphis averaging 37 years old typically have stable sub-grades that simplify concrete work. New pours integrate cleanly with existing slabs and foundations without the settling corrections older properties need.

Memphis paperwork, inspections, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Memphis require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Who's doing the work in Memphis. With 653,450 residents, Memphis supports one of the deeper contractor pools in the region. Competition is strong, and getting 5-10 bids on a standard project is realistic. Memphis's stable population base means the contractor market is not stretched thin. Homeowners benefit from competitive pricing and shorter lead times.

Local factors: concrete work in Memphis, TN

Memphis's housing profile. At an average of 37 years, Memphis housing is squarely in the first-replacement cycle. The original components installed during construction are reaching end of life. concrete work demand is strong in this age range, and contractors are familiar with the common building patterns.

HOA and neighborhood rules. HOA involvement is uncommon in Memphis, so most concrete work projects avoid architectural review delays. City permitting alone governs the process.

Contractor market picture. In a mature market like Memphis, contractors rely on repeat business and referrals rather than new-construction volume. This tends to produce fair pricing and motivated service, but the smaller contractor pool means a bad choice is harder to recover from. Memphis's large market has concrete contractors ranging from 1-person decorative specialists to large commercial outfits that also do residential. For standard flatwork (driveways, patios), mid-size companies with dedicated residential crews typically offer the best combination of pricing and quality.

Timing considerations. Memphis allows year-round concrete work, but the ideal window is spring and fall when temperatures support proper curing without the heat-related challenges of summer. Avoid scheduling large pours during the hottest months — early morning pours are the only reliable option.

Neighborhood patterns. In Memphis, the highest volume of concrete work work tends to come out of Midtown, Cooper-Young, East Memphis, and Germantown — larger lots, older homes, and higher home values all drive replacement demand. Contractors familiar with these neighborhoods often already know the typical building stock, permit nuances, and HOA quirks.

Geography and layout. Beale Street, Graceland, Mississippi River Memphis sits in the New Madrid Seismic Zone; major earthquakes are rare but possible and affect commercial structural work These geographic realities subtly shape contractor pricing and availability in Memphis.

What drives cost. With Memphis labor rates near the national median, the cost difference between a budget and premium driveway or patio pour comes down to materials and scope rather than labor premiums. Memphis's housing stock averages 37 years — the age where original installations start failing and code requirements have evolved. Most concrete work quotes will include some code-catch-up items that newer homes wouldn't need.

Red flags to watch for. Check that any Memphis contractor doing concrete work carries both general liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers' compensation. Request certificates directly from the insurer, not just copies the contractor provides. In Memphis, verify your concrete work contractor pulls the permit themselves — never pull it in your own name. If they ask you to pull the permit, they may not be properly licensed to do the work.
