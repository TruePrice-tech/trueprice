Standard 5-inch K-style gutters move about 1.2 gallons per second, adequate for moderate rainfall up to 2 inches per hour. Memphis rainfall intensity routinely exceeds that threshold, so 6-inch oversized gutters with 40% more capacity are worth the $2-$3 per linear foot upcharge in Midtown, Cooper-Young, and East Memphis.

Common Gutter Scams in Memphis

Midtown, Cooper-Young, and East Memphis homeowners see these same four gutter-quote issues repeatedly.

Memphis-specific undersizing

A Memphis bid that specifies 5-inch gutters without reference to 54 inches of annual rainfall or the specific Midtown, Cooper-Young, and East Memphis roof area being drained is underthought. Any roof plane above 1,500 sq ft emptying to a single gutter run needs 6-inch in Memphis.

Pitch errors on Memphis installs

Standard pitch is 1/4 inch per 10 feet of run toward the downspout. Flat gutters pool, attract mosquitoes, and overflow during Memphis downpours. Test with a garden hose after install — pooling anywhere means the hanger heights need re-setting.

Memphis discharge routing skipped

Memphis downspouts need splash blocks, extensions, or buried drain tile — without them, 54 inches of annual rain lands at the foundation each year. Foundation problems, wet basements, and landscape erosion follow. Memphis bids that ignore discharge routing are missing half the job.

Seamed gutters sold as seamless

True seamless gutters are roll-formed on-site from continuous coil using a portable forming machine — seams only at corners, downspout outlets, and end caps. If a Memphis contractor quotes "seamless" but plans to splice 10- or 20-foot factory sections, that's sectional product. Seams are the #1 failure point in gutter systems, and paying seamless pricing for sectional product is the most common Memphis gutter scam.
