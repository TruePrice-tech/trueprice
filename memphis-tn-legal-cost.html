Legal costs in Memphis, TN: what locals should know

Rate landscape

Attorney rates in Memphis vary significantly by practice area and firm size. Solo practitioners and small firms typically charge 30-50% less than large firms for comparable work.

Fee structures

Many Memphis attorneys offer flat fees for routine matters like uncontested divorces, wills, and LLC formations. Always ask about flat-fee options before accepting hourly billing.

Free legal help

Memphis has multiple legal aid organizations offering free services for qualifying residents. Bar association referral services provide low-cost initial consultations.

Court access

Midtown, Cooper-Young, East Memphis residents can access small claims court for disputes without hiring an attorney, saving thousands in legal fees for straightforward claims.

Memphis legal costs: FedEx logistics practice, the tristate market, and Tennessee Bar oversight

Memphis's legal market is shaped by the city's logistics-industry concentration (FedEx HQ, AutoZone HQ, International Paper, plus the broader logistics workforce), the bistate Tennessee-Mississippi-Arkansas tristate market, and the smaller-than-Nashville commercial economy. Major Memphis firms include Baker Donelson, Bass Berry & Sims, Burch Porter & Johnson, Glankler Brown, Lewis Thomason, and Wyatt Tarrant & Combs. Partner rates at Memphis's largest firms run $400-$800 per hour; mid-size firms offer $300-$600 partner rates. Solo and small-firm rates run $200-$400 per hour for general practice.

The FedEx logistics practice creates substantial specialty legal work. FedEx WorldPort at Memphis International is the company's largest cargo hub, and FedEx's Memphis HQ generates aviation regulatory matters, supply-chain contracts, labor and employment (FedEx's pilot and ground crew workforce), international trade and customs, and complex commercial litigation involving freight and logistics. Several Memphis firms have built dedicated logistics industry practices. AutoZone's Memphis HQ creates additional corporate legal work in retail, supply chain, and IP.

The Tennessee Board of Professional Responsibility regulates attorney conduct in Tennessee. Tennessee follows modified comparative negligence with a 50 percent bar rule. Tennessee's contingency-fee rules cap personal injury attorney fees at 33 1/3-40 percent of recovery. Memphis Bar Association maintains a Lawyer Referral Service. Memphis Area Legal Services and Community Legal Center provide pro bono and reduced-fee representation for low-income residents. The University of Memphis Cecil C. Humphreys School of Law operates legal clinics covering various practice areas.

The tristate market means many Memphis attorneys handle work across Tennessee, Mississippi (Olive Branch, Southaven, the broader DeSoto County area), and Arkansas (West Memphis, Crittenden County). Each state has its own bar admission requirements, but federal court matters and bistate commercial transactions can be handled with single representation. For Mississippi-side matters, Memphis attorneys often coordinate with Mississippi-licensed counsel for state court appearances.

How does the FedEx logistics practice work in Memphis?

FedEx's Memphis HQ creates a deep logistics-industry legal practice. Practice areas where Memphis firms have particular expertise: aviation regulatory (FAA matters, airport regulations, international air freight), supply-chain contracts (the just-in-time logistics industry produces complex contract disputes), labor and employment (FedEx's pilot, ground crew, and driver workforce creates frequent labor matters), international trade and customs (CBP regulations, free trade agreements, sanctions compliance), and complex commercial litigation involving freight and logistics. Major Memphis firms (Baker Donelson, Bass Berry & Sims, Glankler Brown) maintain dedicated logistics practices. Many Memphis attorneys have substantial in-house experience at FedEx, the Memphis-Shelby County Airport Authority, or other logistics companies. For non-logistics matters, Memphis firms offer general corporate, M&A, real estate, and litigation services at competitive rates well below Nashville or Chicago.

What are typical Memphis attorney rates by practice area?

Memphis attorney rates run below Nashville. Typical 2025 rates: solo practitioners and small firms $200-$400/hour for general practice, mid-size firm partners $300-$600/hour, BigLaw partners $400-$800/hour at the largest firms (Baker Donelson, Bass Berry & Sims), logistics industry specialists $400-$700/hour, IP and tax attorneys $300-$600/hour, family law attorneys $200-$400/hour, criminal defense $250-$500/hour. Personal injury cases run on contingency (33 1/3-40 percent of recovery). Strategies to save: for routine matters, flat-fee solo practitioners are typically most cost-effective; for logistics matters, Memphis firms have unusually deep specialty expertise; for low-income Memphis residents, Memphis Area Legal Services provides free representation.

Court Filing Costs and Small Claims across Memphis

Shelby County Circuit Court civil filing fees start at $250. General Sessions Court handles claims up to $25,000 with filing fees of $60-$115. Family law filing costs $250-$350. Eviction filing costs $47-$60 in General Sessions. The Shelby County courthouse complex on Adams Avenue handles most filings. Filing fees in Memphis are moderate for a metro of this size.

Shelby County General Sessions Court handles claims up to $25,000, Tennessee's generous small claims limit. Cases are heard at the Shelby County courthouse. Filing fees run $60-$115. Attorneys are permitted. The high monetary limit means most consumer, contractor, and landlord-tenant disputes can be resolved without Circuit Court filing costs. Appeals go to Circuit Court for trial de novo.

Memphis-area mediation and arbitration options

The Mediation Center of Memphis provides community mediation. AAA Memphis handles commercial arbitration. Tennessee courts mandate mediation in many cases. The Shelby County General Sessions Court offers a mediation program for landlord-tenant disputes. Tennessee's ADR Commission certifies mediators and publishes annual statistics on mediation outcomes.

Mediation and arbitration typically resolve Memphis disputes faster and at lower cost than full litigation. Ask any Memphis attorney whether alternative dispute resolution is appropriate for your case before committing to a courtroom timeline.

Most Common Legal Disputes: a Memphis breakdown

The most common legal disputes in Memphis are criminal defense (the largest share of the docket given crime rates), family law (custody, divorce, orders of protection), landlord-tenant matters (eviction defense in a city with high renter population), personal injury (auto accidents on I-40/I-55/I-240, FedEx corridor trucking accidents), and consumer debt collection defense. Shelby County courts handle over 250,000 cases annually.

Shelby County Circuit Court's civil backlog averages 14-20 months to trial. Family law cases average 10-14 months for contested custody. General Sessions moves at 30-60 days. The criminal docket consumes a disproportionate share of judicial resources in Memphis, which slows civil case resolution. Tennessee's mandatory mediation helps divert some cases from the backlog.
