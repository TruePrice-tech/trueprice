Healthcare in Memphis, TN: what locals should know

Hospital landscape

Memphis is served by multiple hospital systems competing for patients. Understanding which hospitals are in your insurance network before an emergency can save you thousands in out-of-network charges.

Insurance coverage

Check your plan's network carefully. In Memphis, the difference between in-network and out-of-network charges for the same procedure can be 3-5x.

Negotiation leverage

Every Memphis hospital has a financial assistance program. Ask for the self-pay rate before accepting any bill at face value. Most Memphis hospitals offer 20-40% prompt-pay discounts.

Neighborhood access

Midtown, Cooper-Young, East Memphis residents have access to community health centers with sliding-fee scales for primary care, often at a fraction of ER costs.

Memphis: hospital systems and safety-net providers

Methodist Le Bonheur Healthcare, Baptist Memorial Health Care, St. Jude Children's Research Hospital, and Regional One Health serve the Memphis metro. Regional One Health is the county-owned safety-net hospital and Level I trauma center for Shelby County, the busiest trauma center in Tennessee. St. Jude provides free pediatric cancer care to families regardless of insurance.

Shelby County's uninsured rate is approximately 11%, driven by Tennessee's refusal to expand Medicaid. The coverage gap disproportionately affects Memphis's low-income Black population in neighborhoods like Whitehaven, Frayser, and Orange Mound. An estimated 80,000 Shelby County adults lack health insurance coverage.

Average Medical Procedure Costs across Memphis

An ER visit at Methodist University averages $1,900-$3,200, while Regional One's ER charges $900-$1,800 for comparable acuity. MRI at Baptist Memphis runs $900-$2,000; freestanding imaging centers in Germantown and Collierville offer the same scan for $280-$550. Cardiac catheterization at Methodist costs $12,000-$28,000 before insurance negotiated rates.
