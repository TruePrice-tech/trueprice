Memphis Sewer Infrastructure

Memphis sewer infrastructure varies by neighborhood age. Sewer line inspection with video camera ($200-$400) is recommended before purchasing older homes.

Memphis receives 54 inches of annual precipitation. Heavy rainfall regularly overwhelms aging storm drains and causes basement or crawlspace flooding. French drains, sump pumps, and proper grading protect against foundation water intrusion.

Pipe Materials Inside Memphis Walls

Memphis pre-1960 homes may still have galvanized supply lines and cast-iron drains. Post-1970 homes typically have copper supply. Modern retrofits use PEX-a (Uponor AquaPEX) for supply and ABS or PVC for drain-waste-vent. Midtown and Cooper-Young housing stock reflects distinct pipe-material eras.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Memphis?

Plumber rates in Memphis typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
