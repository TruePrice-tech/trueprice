Questions Mentor homeowners ask about garage door

How long does a garage door last?

Steel garage doors last 20-30 years. Wood doors last 15-20 years with maintenance. Aluminum lasts 20+ years. Springs last 7-12 years (torsion) or 5-7 years (extension), depending on usage cycles.

How long does garage door installation take?

A standard single or double door replacement takes 3-5 hours. Custom doors or jobs requiring structural framing changes take 1-2 days. Most installations are completed in a single visit.

How much does a garage door replacement cost in Mentor?

A new garage door in Mentor costs $800-$4,000 for a standard door installed, or $2,500-$8,000+ for a premium insulated or carriage-style door with a new opener.

What R-value should my garage door have?

R-8 is fine if the garage is detached or unheated. R-12 to R-16 is best for attached garages or if you use the space for a workshop. R-18+ is for extreme cold climates or heated garages.

What R-value garage door do I need in Mentor?

For Mentor's cold winters, an R-12 to R-16 insulated door is recommended for attached garages. If you heat your garage or use it as a workshop, step up to R-16 or R-18. The extra $200-$500 for higher insulation pays back in 3-5 years through reduced heating costs in adjacent rooms and prevents freezing issues with items stored in the garage.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Mentor?

With Mentor homes averaging 47 years, many still have original garage doors past their useful life. A full replacement ($1,200-$3,000 with a new opener) typically returns 90-100% at resale. Repair is worth it only for newer doors with isolated issues like a single broken spring, damaged panel, or failing opener motor. If the door is original to the house, replacement is the better investment.

When should I replace a garage door in Mentor?

The ideal window for a Mentor garage door replacement is late spring or early fall. Moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing, plus you get access to a wider range of styles and colors. Avoid spring if you can, since that is when winter-related spring breakages create peak demand and longer wait times.
