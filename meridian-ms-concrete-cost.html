Meridian concrete: answers to common questions

How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

What Meridian homeowners should know about concrete work

Why pricing varies in Meridian. Labor rates here run roughly 14% below the national average per BLS metro data, which is the main reason this city's pricing sits below the national midpoint. Our analysis shows concrete work in Meridian running roughly 9% below the US baseline.

Meridian's site and climate factors. Meridian has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Concrete in Meridian cures differently than in dry climates — high humidity slows evaporation, which actually improves curing strength but extends the timeline. The bigger concern is expansive clay soils common in this region, which cause heave and cracking. Proper sub-base preparation (4-6 inches of compacted gravel) is non-negotiable. Standard concrete in Meridian runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Meridian paperwork, inspections, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Meridian require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Who's doing the work in Meridian. With 40,921 people, Meridian's contractor pool is mid-sized. You will find 4-6 established shops for most trades, which provides enough competition for fair pricing. Meridian's stable population base means the contractor market is not stretched thin. Homeowners benefit from competitive pricing and shorter lead times.
