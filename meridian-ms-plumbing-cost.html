Plumbing in Meridian: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

The soft water in Meridian is a plus for plumbing longevity. Water heaters last longer and pipes stay cleaner. Focus your plumbing budget on other priorities like pipe material and drainage.

❄

Freeze risk

Freezing is uncommon in Meridian, but the risk isn't zero. During rare cold snaps, homes here are actually MORE vulnerable because plumbing isn't typically winterized. Keep pipe insulation on hand for outdoor runs.

🔧

Material recommendation

PEX piping is the most popular repipe material in Meridian. It's flexible, quiet, and typically costs 30-40% less than copper. Copper remains a good choice if you prefer the durability and resale appeal.

💡

Local tip

In Meridian, homes from the 1970s-80s era are hitting the age where original plumbing starts failing. Water heaters last 8-12 years, and supply pipes 40-60 years. If you're in that window, budget for upgrades.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Meridian?

Plumber rates in Meridian typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
