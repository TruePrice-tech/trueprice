What Mesquite residents ask about concrete

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

How much does concrete work cost in Mesquite?

Concrete costs in Mesquite range from $6-$12 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, patios) to $15-$30/sqft for stamped or decorative finishes, including labor and materials.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

How much does a concrete foundation cost?

A full basement foundation costs $15,000-$30,000. A slab foundation for a house costs $5,000-$15,000. A garage slab costs $2,500-$6,000. Price depends on size, depth, and soil conditions.

How long before I can drive on new concrete?

Wait 24-48 hours for foot traffic, 7 days for vehicle traffic, and 30 days for full cure. Avoid deicing salt for the first winter. New concrete reaches 90% strength in 7 days and full strength in 28 days.

Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. Local conditions. Mesquite has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Concrete in Mesquite cures differently than in dry climates — high humidity slows evaporation, which actually improves curing strength but extends the timeline. The bigger concern is expansive clay soils common in this region, which cause heave and cracking. Proper sub-base preparation (4-6 inches of compacted gravel) is non-negotiable. Standard concrete in Mesquite runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Mesquite require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. Mesquite's 143,484 residents support a healthy contractor market. Competition keeps pricing fair, though the pool is not so large that you can skip due diligence on references. Mesquite, TX-specific concrete work considerations

Home stock in Mesquite. Mesquite homes are about 31 years old on average, young enough that most original systems still have useful life remaining. concrete work demand here centers on upgrades, improvements, and the occasional early failure rather than wholesale replacement.

HOA & compliance. HOAs are common in Mesquite. For concrete work, factor in 2-4 weeks of HOA architectural review before starting, and collect pre-approval of materials in writing. HOA rejections after project start can be expensive.

Local market conditions. Mesquite's moderate growth keeps the contractor pipeline moving without the extreme backlogs seen in booming metros. Off-peak scheduling can yield 8-15% savings over peak-season rates. Mesquite's large market has concrete contractors ranging from 1-person decorative specialists to large commercial outfits that also do residential. For standard flatwork (driveways, patios), mid-size companies with dedicated residential crews typically offer the best combination of pricing and quality.

Seasonal timing. Mesquite allows year-round concrete work, but the ideal window is spring and fall when temperatures support proper curing without the heat-related challenges of summer. Avoid scheduling large pours during the hottest months — early morning pours are the only reliable option.

What drives cost. With Mesquite labor rates near the national median, the cost difference between a budget and premium driveway or patio pour comes down to materials and scope rather than labor premiums. Newer construction in Mesquite (averaging 31 years) means concrete work projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay.

Red flags to watch for. Check that any Mesquite contractor doing concrete work carries both general liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers' compensation. Request certificates directly from the insurer, not just copies the contractor provides. In Mesquite, verify your concrete work contractor pulls the permit themselves — never pull it in your own name. If they ask you to pull the permit, they may not be properly licensed to do the work.
