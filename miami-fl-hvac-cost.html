Miami's utility rates and HVAC economics

Efficiency targets. SEER 18 minimum, AFUE 80% minimum for Miami's climate.

HVAC permits and licensing in Miami

Permit authority. Miami-Dade County Building Department.

Miami-Dade requires all equipment to carry a High Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) product approval number, which is meaningfully more restrictive than statewide Florida Product Approval. Florida Class A or Class B mechanical contractor license required, and the inspector verifies wind-load tie-down hardware on the condenser pad

HVHZ product approval numbers must appear on the permit application; a Florida-statewide product approval number that is not HVHZ-approved gets the permit rejected automatically

Questions Miami homeowners ask about hvac

How long does HVAC installation take in Miami?

A standard residential HVAC replacement takes 1-2 days. Complex jobs involving ductwork modification or system-type changes can take 3-5 days.

How much does a new HVAC system cost in Miami?

Most homeowners in Miami pay between $4,000 and $14,000 for a new HVAC system, depending on equipment type, efficiency rating, and home size.

Is a two-stage system worth the extra cost?

Two-stage and variable-speed systems run quieter, dehumidify better, and maintain more consistent temperatures. The $1,000-$2,500 premium pays back in 5-8 years through energy savings and comfort.
