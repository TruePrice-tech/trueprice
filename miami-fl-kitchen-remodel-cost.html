Frequently asked kitchen remodel questions

How much should I pay upfront?

Never pay more than 10-15% as a deposit before work begins. Standard payment structure: deposit, progress payment when materials arrive, final payment on completion.

What should I look for in a contractor quote?

A professional quote breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. It includes a timeline, payment schedule, warranty terms, and the contractor's license number.

Do I need a permit for kitchen remodel in Miami?

Most kitchen remodel projects in Miami require a permit. Your contractor should pull the permit and schedule the inspection. Never pull permits in your own name.

Miami Kitchen Stock and Typical Layouts

Miami kitchen stock runs from 1920s MiMo (Miami Modern) single-family homes in Morningside and Miami Shores (130-180 sqft) through 1950s-70s coral-rock and CBS block construction in Coral Gables and Miami Beach (140-210 sqft) to 2000s-onward condo-tower kitchens in Brickell, Edgewater, and Sunny Isles (80-140 sqft). Coconut Grove bungalows have pier-and-beam 120-160 sqft kitchens with period built-ins. Doral and Kendall tract homes have 220-380 sqft great-room kitchens.

CBS (concrete block and stucco) construction in 1950s-onward Miami homes means any wall-removal requires masonry core-saw work with a licensed structural engineer letter. High-rise condo tower kitchens have plumbing slab-penetration rules that force sink locations to pre-existing risers. HVHZ requirements add structural detailing cost to any connection between kitchen addition and main envelope. Coastal Construction Control Line jurisdiction applies to any Miami Beach and Sunny Isles parcel seaward of the line.

Miami Permits and Electrical Scope

Miami-Dade County Building Department and City of Miami Planning issue permits depending on jurisdiction, with 4-8 week turnaround. Florida Power and Light panel upgrades to 200A run $2,800-$5,200. Florida amends the NEC; all kitchen receptacles require GFCI protection and AFCI is required on lighting circuits. The High-Velocity Hurricane Zone (HVHZ) applies to all Miami-Dade and Broward residential work; appliances tied to the structural envelope need Florida Product Approval and Miami-Dade Notice of Acceptance (NOA).

Miami-Dade County Building runs 4-8 weeks on kitchen remodels. City of Miami runs 3-5 weeks. Miami Beach runs 5-8 weeks with strict HVHZ review. Coral Gables runs 4-6 weeks and is notoriously strict on Mediterranean-revival design guidelines. Key Biscayne runs 4-6 weeks with Coastal Construction Control Line layering. Historic district review through the Miami Historic Preservation Board adds 4-8 weeks on any exterior-visible work in MiMo Historic District, Morningside, and Coral Gables core.

Miami Plumbing, Water, and Rough-In

Miami-Dade Water and Sewer Department delivers water from the Biscayne Aquifer with TDS 180-280 ppm. Saltwater intrusion is a concern on barrier-island parcels. Master plumbers (Florida DBPR-licensed) charge $95-$140/hour. The Florida Building Code Plumbing (based on IPC) requires specific freeze-protection detailing on exterior lines even in Miami because of occasional cold snaps. CBS (concrete-block-stucco) construction forces masonry coring for any new fixture location, adding $400-$900 per penetration.

Miami kitchen remodels typically encounter a 40-50 gallon electric tank in a garage or utility closet (natural gas is less common than other metros). Florida Power and Light rebates pay $300-$800 for heat-pump water heater swaps. Rheem ProTerra and AO Smith Voltex perform exceptionally well in Miami's warm garage environment. Tankless electric is rare because of the amp-draw requirements; tankless gas installed through an exterior vent is the alternative on single-family homes with gas service.

Local factors: kitchen remodel in Miami, FL

Home stock in Miami. Miami's housing stock averages 28 years old — relatively recent construction that was largely built to modern code. kitchen remodel projects here tend to be simpler: fewer surprise compliance issues, quicker permit turnaround, and contractors who can quote from memory.

HOA & compliance. HOAs are common in Miami. For kitchen remodel, factor in 2-4 weeks of HOA architectural review before starting, and collect pre-approval of materials in writing. HOA rejections after project start can be expensive.

Local market conditions. Miami has moderate construction activity. Contractors are busy but accessible with 1-3 weeks' notice. Off-season quotes (contractor-dependent) can run 8-15% below peak rates. Miami's large market (417,650 residents) supports both general contractors and kitchen-specific specialists. Specialists typically quote 10-15% lower than GCs for the same scope because they have established cabinet and countertop supplier relationships with volume pricing.

Seasonal timing. Lead times for custom cabinets in Miami run 6-10 weeks from order to delivery. Factor this into your timeline — the construction phase can't start until cabinets arrive. Stock and semi-custom options ship in 2-4 weeks and cut the total project timeline significantly.

Neighborhood patterns. In Miami, the highest volume of kitchen remodel work tends to come out of Coconut Grove, Coral Gables, Brickell, and Wynwood — larger lots, older homes, and higher home values all drive replacement demand. Contractors familiar with these neighborhoods often already know the typical building stock, permit nuances, and HOA quirks.

Geography note. South Beach, Vizcaya, Wynwood Walls These geographic realities subtly shape contractor pricing and availability in Miami.

What drives cost. With Miami labor rates near the national median, the cost difference between a budget and premium mid-range kitchen renovation comes down to materials and scope rather than labor premiums. Newer construction in Miami (averaging 28 years) means kitchen remodel projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay.

Red flags to watch for. In Miami, verify your kitchen remodel contractor pulls the permit themselves — never pull it in your own name. If they ask you to pull the permit, they may not be properly licensed to do the work. Be cautious of kitchen remodel contractors in Miami who pressure you to sign same-day.
