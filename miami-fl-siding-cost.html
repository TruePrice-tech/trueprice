Common siding questions in Miami

Is vinyl or fiber cement siding better?

Fiber cement resists fire, impact, and insects better than vinyl, and holds paint longer. Vinyl costs 30-40% less, never needs painting, and handles moisture well. Choose based on budget and climate.

What is the cheapest siding option?

Vinyl is the most affordable at $4-$8/sqft installed. Aluminum is similar at $4-$9/sqft. Both are low-maintenance, but vinyl has largely replaced aluminum due to better impact resistance and color retention.

What siding material lasts the longest?

Fiber cement (James Hardie) lasts 40-50 years with minimal maintenance. Vinyl lasts 25-40 years. Engineered wood lasts 20-30 years. Natural wood lasts 20-40 years but requires repainting every 5-7 years.

How do I know my siding needs replacing?

Replace when you see warping, buckling, cracks, or holes; soft spots indicating rot; bubbling paint that keeps returning; high energy bills from poor insulation; or mold and mildew that will not clean away.

How much does siding replacement cost in Miami?

Siding replacement in Miami costs $6,000-$18,000 for an average home. Vinyl is the most affordable ($4-$8/sqft installed), while fiber cement ($8-$14/sqft) and wood ($10-$18/sqft) cost more.

Climate impact on siding: Miami edition

Miami siding faces mandatory HVHZ wind-uplift exposure, annual Atlantic hurricane season (June-November), tropical UV degradation, and salt spray in coastal-adjacent zones. Hurricane Andrew (1992) and Hurricane Irma (2017) both drove code tightening. Stucco failure in Miami typically traces to window or penetration sealant failure followed by moisture intrusion behind the CBS-stucco assembly; the dominant Miami failure pattern is hidden moisture damage surfacing years after original installation.

Miami coastal zones (all of Miami Beach, Key Biscayne, Bal Harbour, Surfside, Sunny Isles, Fisher Island, and Atlantic-facing properties) experience severe salt-air corrosion. Stainless steel 316 fasteners are the durable Miami coastal spec (even 304 stainless can pit in severe salt exposure); galvanized steel pits and stains within 2-4 years. Marine-coated aluminum, bronze, or copper trim are coastal-durable. Hurricane wind-borne debris testing per Florida Building Code R609 and Miami-Dade HVHZ requirements drive product selection. Salt-air also degrades paint film; coastal-grade paints with specific chemistry extend service.

Miami-specific siding fraud patterns

Miami-specific siding fraud patterns concentrate on hurricane-season AOB manipulation and NOA substitution.
