Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in Michigan (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in Michigan typically run $2,300–$5,500 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2017 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Michigan code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2017 NEC — older adoption cycle, local amendments common
  • State amendments: Michigan adopts 2017 NEC under PA 217 of 1956 (Skilled Trades Regulation Act) with Bureau of Construction Codes enforcement and three-tier licensing
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: Moderate — typical interior continental flash density
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 30-inch+ design depth — buried service conduit must clear hard frost line

Michigan licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs — Bureau of Construction Codes (Electrical Licensing Section) (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal electrical permit required for panel upgrade and any service work

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in Michigan

Upper Peninsula winter ambient sustains -20°F to -40°F across Houghton, Marquette, and Sault Ste. Marie service drops, brittling PVC conduit fittings and forcing rigid metal conduit on exterior service masts above 45° latitude — and Lake Michigan and Lake Superior lake-effect freeze-thaw drives ground frost depth to 60 inches in the U.P., requiring service-entrance conduit buried below the frost line with rigid schedule 80 PVC.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Michigan Public Act 217 of 1956 (the Skilled Trades Regulation Act) issues Master Electrician, Journeyman, Sign Specialist, Fire Alarm Specialist, and Electrical Contractor as five separately tested credentials — and unique to Michigan, the Electrical Contractor license is a business-entity credential that requires a designated Master Electrician on file, with the Master credential separately renewable on an individual basis.
