Gutter Installation Cost in Michigan (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Michigan typically runs $1,100–$4,200, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 33 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Michigan's 35 psf state-average snow load reaches 60+ psf in the Upper Peninsula's Houghton and Marquette counties — driving heavier-gauge aluminum K-style and steel-bracket spacing under 18 inches on U.P. residential installations — and Lake Michigan and Lake Superior lake-effect winter snow drives ice-dam exposure on every north-facing eave with heat-cable retrofit budgets averaging $700-1,100 per home.

Michigan rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 33 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 35 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: High — sustained sub-freezing winter requires ice-and-water shield + heat-cable retrofit budget
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: High — mature deciduous canopy drives gutter-guard demand and 2x annual cleaning frequency

Michigan licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs — Residential Builder License (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction

How gutter installation costs vary in Michigan

State-specific code or insurance rule: Michigan Public Act 299 of 1980 (Occupational Code Article 24) requires every Residential Builder and Residential Maintenance & Alteration Contractor to hold a Michigan LARA-issued license for any residential project exceeding $600 — and unique to Michigan, the Maintenance & Alteration credential is restricted to four scope categories (Carpentry, Concrete, Excavation, Insulation) with siding and gutter work falling under Carpentry, requiring per-scope examination at credential issuance.
