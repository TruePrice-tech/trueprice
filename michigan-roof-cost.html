Roof Replacement Cost in Michigan (2026)

Roof replacement in Michigan typically runs $8,000–$21,500 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. Upper Peninsula (Marquette, Houghton) carries ground snow loads of 70–90 psf and lake-effect ice-dam frequency unmatched in the Lower 48; Lower Peninsula freeze-thaw counts top 60 cycles per winter, making valley flashing and ice-and-water shield selection a long-term cost driver.

Michigan licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs — Residential Builder license required for jobs over $600 (official site)
  • Permit: municipal permit required

How roof replacement costs vary in Michigan

Upper Peninsula (Marquette, Houghton) carries ground snow loads of 70–90 psf and lake-effect ice-dam frequency unmatched in the Lower 48; Lower Peninsula freeze-thaw counts top 60 cycles per winter, making valley flashing and ice-and-water shield selection a long-term cost driver.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Michigan's Residential Builders Act (Public Act 299 of 1980) makes it a misdemeanor to roof for compensation without a license on jobs above $600 — and bars unlicensed contractors from collecting in court even for completed work.
