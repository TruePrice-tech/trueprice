Siding Replacement Cost in Michigan (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Michigan typically runs $9,200–$25,000, with vinyl panel the dominant residential cladding choice. Lake Michigan, Lake Superior, and Lake Huron shoreline salt-haze deposition (from winter de-icing brine entrainment) plus freshwater wind-driven rain corrode galvanized fasteners on shoreline re-sides — and Upper Peninsula sustained sub-zero ambients across Houghton, Marquette, and Sault Ste. Marie drive vinyl panel brittleness, with cedar lap and engineered wood retaining significant market share over composite alternatives in the U.P.

Michigan climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 5A-6A-7
  • Dominant residential cladding: Vinyl panel — dominant low-budget retrofit choice in non-WUI, non-coastal corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate wind exposure — 110–140 mph design wind on coastal counties
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: No statewide WUI cladding mandate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: Low salt-air corrosion — standard galvanized fasteners adequate

Michigan licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs — Residential Builder License (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal building permit required for full re-side and envelope sheathing work

How siding replacement costs vary in Michigan

State-specific code or insurance rule: Michigan Public Act 299 of 1980 (Occupational Code Article 24) requires every Residential Builder and Residential Maintenance & Alteration Contractor to hold a Michigan LARA-issued license for any residential project exceeding $600 — and unique to Michigan, the Maintenance & Alteration credential is restricted to four specific scope categories (Carpentry, Concrete, Excavation, Insulation) chosen at examination, with each scope listed individually on the contractor's license card and verified by the building department on every permit application.
