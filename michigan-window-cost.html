Window Replacement Cost in Michigan (2026)

Window replacements in Michigan typically run $5,800–$14,800 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Northern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.3, no SHGC limit). Michigan's combination of 42-inch frost depth across the Upper Peninsula and northern Lower Peninsula, F2 freeze-thaw cycling driving sealant degradation, persistent Lake Michigan and Lake Huron salt-spray-equivalent (winter de-icing salt + lake-effect humidity) on every Great Lakes shoreline exposure, very-high pre-1978 housing share (60%+ across MI) driving mandatory EPA RRP plus Detroit Lead Safe Renovation Ordinance enforcement on every Detroit, Flint, Saginaw, and Grand Rapids residential window replacement, and Michigan EGLE High-Risk Erosion Area shoreline-setback requirements drives unique cold-climate-and-Detroit-lead-safe-driven glazing selection.

Michigan climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Northern — coldest climates, U-factor priority (U ≤ 0.27 in coldest tier)
  • IECC climate zone: 5A-6A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.3 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: No limit (cold climate)
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 105 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Moderate — selected metro historic districts with review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Very high — 60%+ pre-1978 housing share, mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every replacement

Michigan licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Michigan Department of Licensing & Regulatory Affairs (LARA) — Residential Builder License + Maintenance & Alteration Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification; LARA Residential Builder license required for new-construction; LARA Maintenance & Alteration Contractor license required for replacement work; replacement-only same-size installs typically permit-exempt; Detroit Lead Safe Renovation Ordinance separately enforces RRP-equivalent on every Detroit residential window replacement

How window replacement costs vary in Michigan

Michigan's combination of 42-inch frost depth across the Upper Peninsula and northern Lower Peninsula, F2 freeze-thaw cycling driving sealant degradation, persistent Lake Michigan and Lake Huron salt-spray-equivalent (winter de-icing salt + lake-effect humidity) on every Great Lakes shoreline exposure, very-high pre-1978 housing share (60%+ across MI) driving mandatory EPA RRP plus Detroit Lead Safe Renovation Ordinance enforcement on every Detroit, Flint, Saginaw, and Grand Rapids residential window replacement, and Michigan EGLE High-Risk Erosion Area shoreline-setback requirements drives unique cold-climate-and-Detroit-lead-safe-driven glazing selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Michigan Construction Code Act of 1972 (PA 230) requires every Michigan window contractor to hold a LARA Residential Builder License plus complete a 60-hour Builder Pre-Licensure education program — making Michigan one of the most education-intensive residential builder licensing schemes in the country, plus Michigan Maintenance & Alteration Contractor License (MAC) separately covers replacement-only window work, and Detroit Lead Safe Renovation Ordinance (Detroit City Code §9-1-301) separately enforces RRP-equivalent lead-safe-certification mandate on every Detroit residential window replacement in pre-1978 housing — one of the most aggressive municipal lead-safe enforcement programs in the country with very-high pre-1978 housing share statewide.
