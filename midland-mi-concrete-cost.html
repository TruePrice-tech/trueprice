Midland concrete: answers to common questions

Can cracked concrete be repaired?

Hairline cracks can be sealed with flexible caulk or epoxy for $1-$5 per linear foot. Wider cracks may need patching compound. If the slab has heaved or settled significantly, mudjacking ($5-$10/sqft) or replacement is needed.

How long does concrete last?

Properly poured and maintained concrete lasts 30-50 years. Driveways see 25-30 years with normal use. Sealing every 2-3 years and controlling drainage significantly extend the lifespan.

How much does a concrete patio cost?

A 300 sqft concrete patio costs $1,800-$3,600 for basic broom finish. Stamped patterns add $3-$8/sqft. Colored concrete adds $1-$3/sqft. An exposed aggregate finish adds $2-$4/sqft.

How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

A Midland homeowner's guide to concrete work

How Midland pricing is built. BLS data puts Midland's skilled trade wages near the national midpoint. The local pricing you see is shaped more by material costs, permit fees, and competition than by labor rate differences. For concrete work specifically, Midland sits within a few points of the national midpoint on our cost model.

What Midland's local context means for pricing. Midland has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Midland for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Standard concrete in Midland runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Permits, codes, and licensing in Midland. In Midland, concrete work projects typically require a permit. Confirm the current local process before signing a contract.

Midland's trade contractor landscape. At 42,181 residents, Midland has a moderate contractor base. Most homeowners can find 3-5 qualified bidders without searching beyond the immediate area. Moderate growth in Midland supports a balanced contractor market. Enough work to keep good shops busy, enough competition to keep pricing reasonable.

Midland, MI: trade wage overview

According to BLS data, cement masons and concrete finishers in the Midland, MI metro earn a median of $28.17/hr (13% above the national median of $25.00/hr). Additionally, construction laborers in the area earn $22.92/hr, 4% above the $22.08/hr national rate.

Higher contractor wages explain much of why concrete work costs in Midland exceed the national average. With labor making up 40-60% of a typical concrete work project, local wage data is one of the most reliable indicators of what your quote will look like.

Local market conditions. The Midland contractor market is balanced: enough work to keep shops busy, but enough competition that homeowners can still get timely service. When to schedule. The concrete season in Midland runs May through October when overnight temps stay above 40°F consistently. Spring is peak demand as homeowners address winter frost damage. Fall pours (September-October) offer better contractor availability and fewer weather delays.

What drives cost. With Midland labor rates near the national median, the cost difference between a budget and premium driveway or patio pour comes down to materials and scope rather than labor premiums. Midland's housing stock averages 45 years — the age where original installations start failing and code requirements have evolved. Red flags to watch for. In Midland, verify your concrete work contractor pulls the permit themselves — never pull it in your own name.
