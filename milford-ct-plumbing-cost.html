Plumbing in Milford: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

The soft water in Milford is a plus for plumbing longevity. Water heaters last longer and pipes stay cleaner. Focus your plumbing budget on other priorities like pipe material and drainage.

❄

Freeze risk

Frozen pipes happen in Milford most winters. The most vulnerable spots are pipes in exterior walls, unheated basements, and garages. Pipe insulation is cheap ($0.50-1/ft) and prevents expensive burst-pipe repairs.

🔧

Material recommendation

PEX piping is the top recommendation for repiping in Milford. It's flexible, resists freezing better than rigid pipes (it expands slightly), and typically costs 30-40% less than copper to install.

💡

Local tip

Many Milford homes built before 1970 have galvanized steel pipes that corrode from the inside out. If your water pressure has been steadily dropping or you see rusty water, a whole-house repipe is likely needed.
