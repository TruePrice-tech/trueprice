Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a concrete driveway cost in Minneapolis?

Most homeowners in Minneapolis pay between $5,600 and $11,950 for a concrete driveway, depending on size and finish type.

Is stamped concrete worth the extra cost?

Stamped concrete costs 30-50% more than plain concrete but can mimic the look of pavers, brick, or natural stone at a fraction of the cost. It adds significant curb appeal and value.

How long does a concrete driveway last?

A properly installed and sealed concrete driveway typically lasts 25-30 years. Regular sealing every 2-3 years and prompt crack repair can extend its lifespan further.

About concrete work in Minneapolis, MN

Why pricing varies in Minneapolis. Labor rates here run roughly 41% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For concrete work specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Minneapolis at roughly 20% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Minneapolis has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained mix and proper curing blankets are mandatory; salt damage shortens driveway lifespan, so use sealer.

Contractor market. With a population of 400,070, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Minneapolis has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.
