Concrete Driveway Cost in Minnesota (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in Minnesota typically run $3,800–$10,200 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 4000 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 60-inch design frost line, and the state sits in most severe freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F3). Minnesota's combination of 60-inch frost depth in interior climate zones 6A-7, F3 (most severe) freeze-thaw exposure across the entire state, and -40°F winter low temperatures drives the most aggressive residential concrete cold-weather pour requirements in the Lower 48 — and Minnesota road de-icing salt exposure on every grade-level driveway edge from Minnesota Department of Transportation snow operations plus Twin Cities lake-aerosol from 10,000+ regional lakes drives 6%+ air-entrainment plus 0.40 maximum water-cement ratio on every residential pour.

Minnesota climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 60-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F3 — most severe freeze-thaw + heavy de-icer use, 6-8% air-entrainment + 0.40 max w/c required
  • Typical residential mix: 4000 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: Low — sandy or stable subsoil, standard slab-on-grade design
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC A — no seismic design considerations
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: Very high — heavy de-icer use drives 0.40 max w/c + epoxy-coated rebar

Minnesota licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Minnesota Department of Labor & Industry — Residential Building Contractor or Specialty (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential driveway pour > 200 sq ft; DLI residential contractor license required above $15,000 annual gross

How concrete driveway costs vary in Minnesota

State-specific code or insurance rule: Minnesota Statute §326B requires every Minnesota concrete contractor performing more than $15,000 in annual gross residential construction work to hold a Minnesota Department of Labor & Industry Residential Building Contractor or Concrete Specialty license — plus Minnesota's Concrete Reinforcing Steel Institute (CRSI) Hot Weather and Cold Weather Concrete Specifications (incorporated into MN Building Code) require continuous concrete-cure-temperature monitoring on every residential pour during November-March cold-weather window, the most restrictive cold-weather concrete pour regime in the country.
