Fence Installation Cost in Minnesota (2026)

Installing 150 linear feet of fencing in Minnesota typically runs $5,200–$13,500, with 6-foot western red cedar privacy the dominant residential choice. Concrete-set posts must clear the 48-inch design frost line. Minnesota's 48-to-60-inch frost line in International Falls and the Iron Range (the deepest ground frost in the contiguous U.S.) drives mandatory 4-to-6-foot post-hole depths and concrete-anchored installations on every northern residential fence — and Twin Cities and Duluth metros' aggressive winter de-icing brine corrodes standard galvanized chain-link mesh within 12-15 years, with PVC-coated alternatives now standard on suburban installs.

Minnesota climate & post-design drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 48-inch design minimum for post footings
  • Dominant residential fence material: Western red cedar privacy — dominant in moderate-climate suburban subdivisions
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — 110–140 mph design wind drives 8-foot post spacing
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Low — minimal termite pressure
  • HOA fencing-rule prevalence: Moderate — suburban HOA prevalence on newer subdivisions

Minnesota licensing, HOA & boundary law

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Minnesota Department of Labor & Industry — Residential Building Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal permit typical for residential fence over 6 feet

How fence installation costs vary in Minnesota

State-specific code or insurance rule: Minnesota Statutes §326B.802 requires every Residential Building Contractor and Residential Remodeler to hold a Department of Labor & Industry license — and Minnesota Statutes §344 (the Partition Fence Act) makes adjoining landowners equally responsible for constructing and maintaining a 'lawful fence' along agricultural-parcel boundaries — with county fence viewers (a court-appointed three-person panel) holding binding authority to arbitrate disputes and order specific construction methods when neighbors cannot agree.
