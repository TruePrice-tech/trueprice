Foundation Cost in Minnesota (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in Minnesota typically run $22,400–$52,400 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with full basement pour as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 60-inch design frost line. Minnesota's combination of 60-inch frost depth across the northern half (Itasca, Cook, Lake, St. Louis, Koochiching, Beltrami, Roseau, Lake of the Woods counties), F3 freeze-thaw exposure driving 6-8% air-entrainment specification, Minnesota State Building Code 1322 mandatory radon-resistant new construction passive sub-slab depressurization on every residential foundation pour, and Minnesota Energy Code R402.2 above-IECC-baseline foundation insulation R-value drives aggressive frost-protected footing depth (60 inches+ in the north), continuous radon-control rough-in, and high-R-value foundation insulation on every Minnesota residential foundation.

Minnesota climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 60-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Full basement — dominant in Upper Midwest, Northeast, and Mid-Atlantic on freeze-thaw + radon-zone-1 sites
  • Expansive soil risk: Low — sandy or stable subsoil, standard slab-on-grade design
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC A — no seismic design considerations
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Water table risk: Moderate — water table below frost line in most lots
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • IECC climate zone: 6A-7
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 1 (highest) — passive sub-slab depressurization rough-in required on every basement and slab pour

Minnesota licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Minnesota Department of Labor & Industry — Residential Building Contractor License + Residential Remodeler License (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential foundation pour; MN DLI Residential Building Contractor license required for new-construction foundation; Minnesota State Building Code 1322 Radon Control mandatory passive sub-slab depressurization on every residential foundation; Minnesota Energy Code R402 foundation insulation R-value above IECC baseline

How foundation pour costs vary in Minnesota

State-specific code or insurance rule: Minnesota State Building Code 1322 (Radon Control) is the most stringent residential radon-resistant new construction mandate in the country — every Minnesota residential foundation pour above 1,000 sq ft footprint requires a passive sub-slab depressurization system with a 4-inch crushed-stone capillary break, 6-mil polyethylene vapor retarder, sealed-and-caulked floor penetrations, and a 3-inch-or-larger PVC vent stack from the sub-slab to above the roofline, plus Minnesota Energy Code R402.2 separately requires above-IECC-2021-baseline foundation insulation R-value (R-15 continuous below-grade or R-19 cavity) on every Minnesota residential basement and slab pour.
