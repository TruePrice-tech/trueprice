Garage Door Replacement Cost in Minnesota (2026)

Garage door replacements in Minnesota typically run $1,500–$5,000 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 115 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Minnesota's combination of 60-inch frost depth across the northern half (Itasca, Cook, Lake, St. Louis, Koochiching, Beltrami, Roseau, Lake of the Woods counties), -40°F winter low temperatures driving Minnesota Energy Code R402 mandatory R-19 garage-door insulation as the highest mandatory R-value tier in the lower 48, F3 freeze-thaw cycling driving aggressive sealant replacement at 3-4 year intervals, and statewide Tornado Alley exposure across the southern half drives extreme cold-climate-and-tornado-driven material selection.

Minnesota climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 115 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-19 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Low — minimal termite pressure
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Moderate — suburban HOA prevalence on newer subdivisions
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: None — no salt-air corrosion exposure
  • IECC climate zone: 6A-7

Minnesota licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Minnesota Department of Labor & Industry — Residential Building Contractor License + Residential Remodeler License (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening; MN DLI Residential Building Contractor license required; replacement-only installs typically permit-exempt; Minnesota Energy Code R402 mandatory R-19 garage-door insulation on every residential install — the highest mandatory R-value mandate in the lower 48

How garage door installation costs vary in Minnesota

State-specific code or insurance rule: Minnesota Energy Code R402.2.13 imposes the highest mandatory garage-door insulation R-value tier in the lower 48 at R-19 — every Minnesota residential garage-door installation requires a polyurethane or polystyrene-foam-core door with minimum R-19 thermal resistance plus continuous weather-stripping at the perimeter, top, and bottom-seal interface, with the Minnesota Department of Labor & Industry mandating a Residential Building Contractor License examination covering Minnesota Energy Code R402 specifically, and Minnesota State Building Code 1322 separately requires sub-slab depressurization radon-control rough-in interaction with garage-attached interior air-sealing details.
