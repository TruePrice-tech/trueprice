Gutter Installation Cost in Minnesota (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Minnesota typically runs $1,300–$5,000, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 28 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Minnesota's 50 psf ground snow load combined with International Falls and Iron Range sustained sub-zero winter weather drives heavier-gauge aluminum K-style and steel-bracket spacing under 18 inches on northern residential installations — and ice-dam formation on every north-facing eave drives near-universal heat-cable retrofit with budgets averaging $700-1,200 per home in the Twin Cities and Duluth metros.

Minnesota rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 28 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 50 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: High — sustained sub-freezing winter requires ice-and-water shield + heat-cable retrofit budget
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: Moderate — mixed canopy, gutter guards optional

Minnesota licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Minnesota Department of Labor & Industry — Residential Building Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction

How gutter installation costs vary in Minnesota

State-specific code or insurance rule: Minnesota Statutes §326B.802 requires every Residential Building Contractor and Residential Remodeler to hold a Department of Labor & Industry license — and unique to Minnesota, the DLI administers a Recovery Fund that compensates homeowners up to $75,000 per project (the highest in the country) for licensed-contractor breach or fraud, with the Fund funded by per-license fees and disbursed through Department of Commerce administrative review.
