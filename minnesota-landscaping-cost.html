Landscape Installation Cost in Minnesota (2026)

Landscape installation in Minnesota typically runs $4,400–$18,400 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 3a-5a. Minnesota's combination of -30°F winter low temperatures across the entire state, 145-day growing season in interior climate zone 4a-5a, and 60-inch frost depth restricts residential turf to ultra-cold-hardy Kentucky bluegrass and creeping red fescue cool-season blends — and Minnesota's lake-effect humidity from the 10,000+ regional lakes supports lush hosta, hydrangea, and shade-perennial residential garden design while requiring aggressive fall garlic-mustard, buckthorn, and emerald-ash-borer-related ash-tree removal on every Twin Cities and Duluth landscape maintenance contract.

Minnesota climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 3a-5a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 28 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 145 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Moderate — periodic drought, advisory water-saving guidelines
  • Irrigation dependency: Low — irrigation optional, established plants survive on rainfall
  • Frost-line trench depth: 60-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

Minnesota licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Minnesota Department of Agriculture Pesticide & Fertilizer Management + Minnesota Department of Labor & Industry Residential Building Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; MDA Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care; DLI residential contractor license required above $15,000 annual gross

How landscape installation costs vary in Minnesota

Minnesota's combination of -30°F winter low temperatures across the entire state, 145-day growing season in interior climate zone 4a-5a, and 60-inch frost depth restricts residential turf to ultra-cold-hardy Kentucky bluegrass and creeping red fescue cool-season blends — and Minnesota's lake-effect humidity from the 10,000+ regional lakes supports lush hosta, hydrangea, and shade-perennial residential garden design while requiring aggressive fall garlic-mustard, buckthorn, and emerald-ash-borer-related ash-tree removal on every Twin Cities and Duluth landscape maintenance contract.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Minnesota Statute §18B.07 (Minnesota Pollinator Protection Act) imposes one of the most restrictive residential neonicotinoid restrictions in the country — every Minnesota commercial landscape contractor must label and disclose every neonicotinoid-treated plant, seed, or soil amendment delivered to residential property, and the Minnesota Department of Agriculture separately operates the Minnesota Pollinator Habitat Registry providing rebate incentives for residential conversion of cool-season turf to pollinator-friendly native prairie meadow landscaping.
