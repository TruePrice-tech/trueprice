Roof Replacement Cost in Minnesota (2026)

Roof replacement in Minnesota typically runs $8,200–$22,000 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. Twin Cities and Duluth winters combine 35-psf snow loads with hail-belt exposure (high frequency in May–August); Class 4 impact-resistant shingles plus full ice-and-water shield are the de facto standard in Hennepin, Ramsey, and St. Louis counties.

Minnesota climate & code drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 6A-7
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Hail risk tier: High — frequent severe hail seasons
  • Ground snow load: 35 psf — moderate snow load, ice-and-water shield required
  • Dominant residential roof material: asphalt shingle

Minnesota licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Minnesota Department of Labor and Industry — Residential Roofer license (Minn. Stat. §326B.802) (official site)
  • Permit: city permit required

How roof replacement costs vary in Minnesota

State-specific code or insurance rule: Minnesota Statute §325E.66 (the Roofing Contractor Statute) prohibits insurance-deductible rebating, requires written contracts with itemized scope, and mandates a five-day right of rescission on storm-damage contracts — among the most aggressive consumer-protection roofing laws in the country.
