Siding Replacement Cost in Minnesota (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Minnesota typically runs $10,800–$28,500, with vinyl panel the dominant residential cladding choice. Minnesota sits inside the second-highest annual hail-claim frequency region in the country after Colorado — Minneapolis, St. Paul, and Rochester retrofit specs increasingly default to Class 4 impact-rated fiber-cement or LP SmartSide-style engineered wood, and Twin Cities insurers offer 20-25% homeowners premium discounts on UL 2218 Class 4 cladding installations with documented hail-survival history.

Minnesota climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 6A-7
  • Dominant residential cladding: Vinyl panel — dominant low-budget retrofit choice in non-WUI, non-coastal corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate wind exposure — 110–140 mph design wind on coastal counties
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: No statewide WUI cladding mandate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: No salt-air corrosion exposure (interior continental)

Minnesota licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Minnesota Department of Labor & Industry — Residential Building Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal building permit required for full re-side and structural sheathing work

How siding replacement costs vary in Minnesota

State-specific code or insurance rule: Minnesota Statutes §326B.802 requires every Residential Building Contractor and Residential Remodeler to hold a Department of Labor & Industry license — and unique to Minnesota, the DLI administers a Recovery Fund that compensates homeowners up to $75,000 per project (the highest in the country) for licensed-contractor breach or fraud, funded by per-license fees and disbursed through Department of Commerce administrative review.
