What Minnetonka residents ask about roofing

How much does a new roof cost in Minnetonka?

Most homeowners in Minnetonka pay between $8,000 and $16,000 for a new roof, depending on size, material, and complexity.

How many layers of shingles can I have?

Most building codes allow a maximum of two layers. If you already have two layers, the old roof must be torn off before the new one is installed, adding $1,000-$3,000 to the cost.

Does a new roof increase home value?

A new roof recovers roughly 60-70% of its cost at resale and eliminates a major buyer objection. Homes with new roofs sell faster and with fewer inspection contingencies.

How long does a roof last in Minnetonka?

For Minnetonka homeowners, here are realistic roof lifespans: 3-tab asphalt shingles last 15-20 years, architectural shingles 20-25, standing seam metal 40-60, and clay or concrete tile 50+. UV degradation is the primary wear factor in this climate. Proper ventilation keeps the roof deck from overheating, and lighter material colors reflect more UV, both extending service life.

Minnetonka permits: required to replace my roof?

In most jurisdictions including Minnetonka, a building permit is required for a full roof replacement. Your contractor should pull the permit before work begins. Unpermitted roofing work can void your homeowners insurance coverage and create disclosure issues when selling the home.

When is the best time to replace a roof in Minnetonka?

For Minnetonka, aim for late summer or early fall to schedule your roof replacement. Stable weather allows proper curing and contractors are less backlogged than spring, which means better contractor availability and potential savings of 10-15% on labor. Booking right after a major storm is the worst time since every roofer in the area will be booked and prices reflect the surge demand.
