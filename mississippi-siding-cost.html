Siding Replacement Cost in Mississippi (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Mississippi typically runs $8,200–$22,500, with vinyl panel the dominant residential cladding choice. Mississippi Gulf Coast salt-haze deposition on Hancock, Harrison, and Jackson county shorelines corrodes galvanized fasteners on coastal re-sides within 5-7 years, mandating stainless on all shoreline cedar and fiber-cement — and post-Katrina BFE elevation rules drive below-grade rim-joist re-flashing on every full envelope re-side in flood-prone Gulf Coast parcels.

Mississippi climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 2A-3A
  • Dominant residential cladding: Vinyl panel — dominant low-budget retrofit choice in non-WUI, non-coastal corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: High wind exposure — 140 mph+ design wind on coastal counties
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: No statewide WUI cladding mandate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High-corrosion coastal — stainless or hot-dipped galvanized fasteners required within 3,000 ft of saltwater

Mississippi licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Mississippi State Board of Contractors (residential >$50,000) (official site)
  • Permit: city building permit required in incorporated municipalities; rural counties may have no inspection program

How siding replacement costs vary in Mississippi

Mississippi Gulf Coast salt-haze deposition on Hancock, Harrison, and Jackson county shorelines corrodes galvanized fasteners on coastal re-sides within 5-7 years, mandating stainless on all shoreline cedar and fiber-cement — and post-Katrina BFE elevation rules drive below-grade rim-joist re-flashing on every full envelope re-side in flood-prone Gulf Coast parcels.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Mississippi State Board of Contractors requires the Residential Builder license only on residential projects with total contract value above $50,000 — meaning the overwhelming majority of single-family re-sides proceed under municipal credentials only, with no state oversight in unincorporated counties without chartered inspection programs, and small-project siding work (under $50k) is effectively unregulated statewide.
