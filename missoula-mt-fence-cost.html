Missoula fencing: answers to common questions

What is the cheapest fence material?

Chain link is the cheapest at $10-$20 per linear foot installed. Pressure-treated pine is the cheapest wood option at $15-$25/ft. Vinyl and composite start at $20-$30/ft but never need painting.

What is the best fence for privacy?

A 6-foot solid board (dog-ear, board-on-board, or shadowbox) fence provides the most privacy. Board-on-board is best since it has no gaps even when boards shrink. Vinyl and composite also offer full privacy panels.

How much does a new fence cost in Missoula?

Fence costs in Missoula range from $15-$35 per linear foot for wood, $20-$40 for vinyl, $15-$30 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron, including materials and installation.

Should fence posts be set in concrete?

Yes, for most fences. Concrete-set posts are more stable and resist leaning. Some installers use compacted gravel for better drainage in wet climates, which can extend post life by reducing ground-level rot.

How deep should fence posts be in Missoula?

Missoula's freeze-thaw cycling will push shallow fence posts out of the ground within a couple of winters. Set posts 36-48 inches deep, at least 6 inches past the frost line. Bottom the hole with 8-10 inches of compacted gravel for drainage, then fill with concrete to grade. There is no shortcut on depth in this climate; it is the single most important factor in long-term fence stability.

Do I need a permit to build a fence in Missoula?

In Missoula, fence permits are standard for installations over 6 feet and sometimes required for any fence. Three things to handle before installation: call 811 for utility marking, confirm property boundaries (budget $300-$600 for a survey if needed), and review any HOA rules. The fence contractor should manage the permitting as part of the project scope.

Fence installation in Missoula typically costs $15-$30 per linear foot for wood privacy, $20-$40 for vinyl, $10-$20 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron. Local conditions. Missoula has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Missoula's deep frost line (48-60 inches) means fence post holes are essentially foundation work. Equipment rental for post-hole augers adds $100-300 to the project. Vinyl and metal fences handle the freeze-thaw cycles better than wood — cedar and pine crack and split in deep cold. Most municipalities in Missoula allow 6-foot privacy fences in rear yards and 3-4 feet in front yards without variance. Permits, codes, and licensing. Most fence installation projects in Missoula require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Missoula's measured growth has built a reliable contractor ecosystem. Lead times are manageable and pricing competition is healthy.

Local market conditions. Missoula's moderate growth keeps the contractor pipeline moving without the extreme backlogs seen in booming metros. What drives cost.
