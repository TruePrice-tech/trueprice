What Missoula residents ask about solar

How many panels do I need?

Panel count depends on your electricity usage, panel wattage, and roof sun exposure. A typical home uses 20-30 panels (400W each) to offset 80-100% of electricity consumption.

How much does solar cost in Missoula?

Most homeowners in Missoula pay $15,000-$30,000 before incentives for a 6-10kW system. After the 30% federal tax credit, net cost drops to $10,500-$21,000.

Will solar panels damage my roof?

Properly installed panels do not damage roofs. Reputable installers use flashing and sealant at every penetration point. If your roof needs replacement within 5-10 years, do it before installing solar.

How much roof space do I need for solar?

Each 400W panel needs about 18 square feet. A 8kW system (20 panels) requires roughly 360 square feet of unshaded, south-facing roof space. East and west orientations work but produce 10-15% less.

What is net metering?

Net metering credits you for excess electricity your panels send to the grid. Your meter runs backward during the day and forward at night. Policies and credit rates vary by state and utility.

Does solar work in Missoula's cold winters?

Cold weather actually improves solar panel efficiency since photovoltaic cells perform better at lower temperatures. Missoula's shorter winter days reduce seasonal output, but net metering lets you bank summer surplus for winter use. Snow typically clears from tilted panels within 1-2 days. Between higher electricity rates and incentive programs, the financial return in Missoula often matches sunbelt installations.

What solar incentives are available in Missoula?

Start with the 30% federal ITC, which applies to all Missoula solar installations with no cap. Then check DSIRE for MT-level incentives: state tax credits, utility rebates, SREC markets, property tax exemptions, and net metering terms. Each utility has different programs and rates. The combined federal-plus-state incentive stack typically reduces net cost by 40-60% in markets with strong state programs.

Best time of year to install solar in Missoula?

The best installation window in Missoula is summer, the narrow window of favorable roof conditions and you get full summer production immediately. Note that the process from quote to installation takes 6-12 weeks including permitting, utility approval, and scheduling. Start the process 2-3 months before your target installation date. The federal tax credit applies to the year of installation, so timing relative to tax year can matter.

Local Solar Guide for Missoula, MT

In Missoula, solar is a summer-heavy, winter-light equation. Production concentrates in the long daylight months, drops sharply as days shorten, and depends on net metering to bank the summer surplus for winter credit. When utility rates are high enough, this balance still pencils.

With an average home age of 32 years in Missoula, roof condition assessment is critical before any solar installation. Panels last 25-30 years, so if your roof has less than 15 years of remaining life, replace it before installing solar. Removing panels for a roof replacement later costs $1,500-$3,000 in de-install and reinstall labor. Most reputable solar companies include a roof assessment in their site survey.

Snow is a real production factor in Missoula. Panels installed at steeper tilt angles (35-45 degrees) shed snow faster, and snow guards prevent dangerous sheet slides. Most installers in this region account for snow load in their structural engineering, but confirm that your racking system is rated for Missoula's ground snow load requirements.
