Gutter Installation Cost in Missouri (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Missouri typically runs $1,100–$4,200, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 42 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Missouri's 42-inch annual rainfall combined with the Missouri and Mississippi river-valley flood-elevation overlays drives below-grade downspout-discharge re-routing on every St. Louis County, Jefferson County, and Boone County riverfront retrofit — and seasonal tornado-driven derecho events overwhelm undersized 5-inch K-style profiles on Kansas City and Springfield suburbs.

Missouri rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 42 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 20 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: Moderate — periodic ice-dam events on north-facing eaves
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: Moderate — mixed canopy, gutter guards optional

Missouri licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide gutter contractor license — Kansas City, St. Louis, Springfield issue local credentials (official site)
  • Permit: city building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction in incorporated municipalities

How gutter installation costs vary in Missouri

Missouri's 42-inch annual rainfall combined with the Missouri and Mississippi river-valley flood-elevation overlays drives below-grade downspout-discharge re-routing on every St. Louis County, Jefferson County, and Boone County riverfront retrofit — and seasonal tornado-driven derecho events overwhelm undersized 5-inch K-style profiles on Kansas City and Springfield suburbs.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Missouri remains one of fewer than ten states with no statewide residential contractor licensing for gutter installation — Kansas City and St. Louis run independent registration programs with no reciprocity, and rural Boone, Cole, and Greene counties may issue homeowner-only permits with no contractor verification, with unincorporated areas frequently having no inspection program at all.
