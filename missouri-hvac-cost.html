HVAC Replacement Cost in Missouri (2026)

HVAC replacement in Missouri typically runs $7,800–$17,900 for a complete 3-ton system replacement, with natural-gas furnaces with central AC as the dominant heating choice and roughly 5,000 heating degree days against 1,700 cooling degree days driving the seasonal load mix. Tornado-belt counties (Greene, Christian, Boone, Jackson) impose IBC anchoring on outdoor condensers (strap-and-bolt to a poured pad), and the Missouri River valley humidity drives variable-speed ECM blowers — Ameren MO and Spire utility rebates favor 95%+ AFUE gas furnaces over straight heat pumps.

Missouri climate & load drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A
  • Annual load split: Balanced — heating and cooling loads roughly equal (5,000 HDD / 1,700 CDD)
  • Dominant heating fuel: Natural gas — piped utility supply through state pipeline network
  • Dominant cooling system: Central air conditioning paired with separate heating equipment

Missouri licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: Missouri — no statewide HVAC license; St. Louis County, Kansas City, Springfield enforce municipal mechanical contractor exams (official site)
  • Permit: municipal mechanical permit required

How HVAC replacement costs vary in Missouri

State-specific code or insurance rule: Missouri has no statewide HVAC contractor license — credentialing is fully delegated to municipalities, and St. Louis County's separate Mechanical Examiners Board issues a Master Mechanical Contractor license that's reciprocal with Jackson County (Kansas City) but not with St. Louis City, creating a uniquely fragmented metro-by-metro licensure landscape.
