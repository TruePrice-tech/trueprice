Exterior Painting Cost in Missouri (2026)

Repainting a 1,500 sq ft home in Missouri typically runs $2,800–$7,800, with wood lap or shake siding the dominant residential substrate driving prep scope. Missouri's central-Midwest pre-1978 housing-stock concentration in St. Louis Central West End, Soulard, Lafayette Square, Kansas City Old Northeast, and Springfield's Walnut Street historic district triggers Missouri DHSS Lead Abatement Contractor credentialing on the majority of urban Missouri residential repaint scope — and Missouri's brick-veneer-dominant inner-city housing stock drives elastomeric masonry coatings on Missouri urban repaint specifications, with full-elevation wood-lap repaint demand concentrated in Ozark and Bootheel rural Missouri housing stock.

Missouri climate & coatings drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A
  • Dominant residential substrate: Wood lap or shake — dominant pre-1990 substrate, drives full prep + caulk + prime + 2-coat
  • Annual humidity tier: Moderate — typical continental humidity, standard topcoat formulations adequate
  • UV exposure: Moderate — typical continental UV, standard binder packages adequate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: No salt-air corrosion exposure (interior continental)
  • Freeze-thaw cycling: High — 60-100 freeze-thaw cycles drive sealant inspection on every repaint
  • VOC architectural coatings rule: Federal default — 250 g/L flat exterior topcoat limit (EPA 40 CFR Part 59)

Missouri licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide painting license — St. Louis, Kansas City MO, Springfield issue municipal contractor credentials (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for repaint; St. Louis Class B contractor registration required for residential exterior contracting above $5,000

How exterior repaint costs vary in Missouri

State-specific code or insurance rule: Missouri has no statewide residential painting contractor licensing — Missouri Revised Statutes §700 reserves construction trade regulation to municipalities, but Missouri DHSS separately maintains a Lead Abatement Contractor license under 19 CSR 30-70 for any pre-1978 housing prep that disturbs more than 2 square feet of painted surface in St. Louis, Kansas City, and St. Joseph, lowering the EPA RRP federal trigger threshold and capturing the majority of urban Missouri repaint scope.
