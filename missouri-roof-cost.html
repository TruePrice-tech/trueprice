Roof Replacement Cost in Missouri (2026)

Roof replacement in Missouri typically runs $7,500–$20,000 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. The Kansas City and St. Louis corridors sit inside the central U.S. hail belt — a Class 4 impact-resistant shingle qualifies for an automatic 10–25% premium discount under Missouri Department of Insurance Bulletin 2014-08.

Missouri climate & code drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Hail risk tier: Very high — central U.S. hail corridor, 8+ severe hail days/year
  • Ground snow load: 20 psf — light snow load
  • Dominant residential roof material: asphalt shingle

Missouri licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: No statewide roofing license — Kansas City, St. Louis, and Springfield require municipal registration (official site)
  • Permit: city permit required

How roof replacement costs vary in Missouri

State-specific code or insurance rule: Missouri Senate Bill 564 (2014) prohibits roofers from rebating insurance deductibles on storm claims and requires the contract to include explicit insurance-claim language; SB 631 (2018) added a mandatory 5-day right of rescission.
