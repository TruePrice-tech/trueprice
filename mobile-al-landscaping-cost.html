What Mobile residents ask about landscaping

How much does tree removal cost?

Small trees (under 30 feet) cost $200-$500 to remove. Medium trees (30-60 feet) cost $500-$1,000. Large trees (60-100 feet) cost $1,000-$2,000+. Stump grinding adds $100-$400 per stump.

How much does outdoor lighting cost?

Professional landscape lighting costs $2,000-$5,000 for path lights, uplights, and accent lighting. LED fixtures cost more upfront but last 50,000+ hours. Solar options cost less but provide dimmer, less reliable light.

How much does a retaining wall cost?

Retaining walls cost $20-$50 per square face foot for concrete block, $25-$60 for natural stone, and $15-$30 for timber. A 50 linear foot wall at 3 feet tall runs $3,000-$9,000 installed.

Site-specific factors around Mobile. Mobile has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Mobile's heat and humidity create both opportunities and challenges for landscaping. A complete landscaping project in Mobile typically breaks down as: plant material (30-40%), hardscape/materials (20-30%), labor (25-35%), and design (5-10%). Local factors: landscaping in Mobile, AL

Home stock in Mobile. Most Mobile homes were built about 35 years ago, which means their original systems are approaching or past their expected lifespan. landscaping projects at this stage are typically straightforward since the construction meets modern code with minimal surprises.

HOA & compliance. Few Mobile properties fall under HOA governance, which simplifies landscaping project planning. You deal only with city building codes and permits, not architectural committees.

How the local trade market looks. Mobile has a stable, lower-growth construction market, which usually means more competitive contractor pricing than fast-growing metros. The tradeoff: smaller contractor pool, so reputation and reviews matter more. A few bad actors can dominate the local landscape. Mobile's large market supports full-service landscape design firms, installation-only crews, and maintenance companies. For projects over $8,000, a dedicated design firm with installation capability typically provides better results than splitting design and installation across separate companies.

Best months to book. Mobile offers a longer landscaping season than cold-climate markets. The busiest period is March through June when spring planting demand peaks. Fall (October-December) is an excellent time for plantings, hardscape projects, and irrigation system installation at lower competition and often better pricing.

What drives cost. Lower labor costs are Mobile's advantage for landscaping project — local wages run 11% below the national average. This puts Mobile in the bottom third nationally for landscaping project labor costs. At 35 years average home age, Mobile properties are hitting their first major replacement cycle for systems and components. landscaping project demand is at peak levels in this age band, which keeps contractor schedules full but pricing competitive.

Red flags to watch for. Be cautious of landscaping project contractors in Mobile who pressure you to sign same-day. Legitimate contractors expect you to get competing bids and will hold their price for 30 days. High-pressure sales tactics correlate with inflated pricing. Any Mobile contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion.
