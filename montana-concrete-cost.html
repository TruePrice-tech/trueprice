Concrete Driveway Cost in Montana (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in Montana typically run $3,400–$8,800 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 4000 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 48-inch design frost line, and the state sits in most severe freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F3). Montana's combination of high alkaline soil sulfate exposure (S2 ACI 318 category) across central and eastern Montana, F3 (most severe) freeze-thaw exposure across the entire state, and 48-inch frost depth forces Type II/V sulfate-resistant cement plus 6%+ air-entrainment specification on every Montana residential pour — and Bozeman, Missoula, and Helena valley smoke from regional summer wildfire seasons since 2017 has measurably stressed fresh concrete cure cycles requiring extended wet-curing periods on every Montana residential pour during August-October wildfire season.

Montana climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 48-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F3 — most severe freeze-thaw + heavy de-icer use, 6-8% air-entrainment + 0.40 max w/c required
  • Typical residential mix: 4000 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: Moderate — periodic clay shrink-swell, standard footing reinforcement adequate
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S2 — severe sulfate exposure, ASTM C150 Type II/V cement required
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC C — moderate seismic risk, continuous-rebar-tie footings required
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: High — winter de-icing salt drives air-entrainment + corrosion-protected rebar

Montana licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Montana Department of Labor & Industry — Independent Contractor Registration (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential driveway pour > 200 sq ft; Montana Independent Contractor Registration required statewide
