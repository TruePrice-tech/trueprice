Attic Insulation Upgrade Cost in Montana (2026)

Attic insulation upgrades in Montana typically run $1,900–$7,000 for a 1,500 sq ft attic upgrade R-30 to R-49 blown cellulose, with blown fiberglass loose-fill the dominant residential retrofit material at the IECC R-49 attic minimum. Montana's combination of 7,800 annual heating degree days, -40°F winter low temperatures across the entire state, and 48-inch frost depth drives heating-dominant insulation upgrades statewide — R-49 blown-fiberglass attic plus R-20 wall cavity insulation plus continuous polyiso exterior on retrofits is now the dominant Montana spec, with mountain-elevation Bozeman, Big Sky, and Whitefish additionally requiring R-60 attic upgrades and triple-pane glazing on every premium ski-town residential retrofit to manage extreme winter heating costs above $500/month.

Montana energy code & climate-load drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 6B-7
  • Required attic R-value: R-49 (IECC 2021 prescriptive minimum)
  • Required wall R-value: R-20 (above-grade wood-frame)
  • Adopted energy code basis: IECC 2021 — current cycle, full mandatory R-49 attic + R-20 wall + air-sealing requirements
  • Stretch code available: No — IECC base code only
  • Annual heating + cooling load: 7,800 HDD / 400 CDD
  • Vapor barrier requirement: Warm-side vapor retarder required (typical IECC zones 5+)
  • Dominant attic insulation material: Blown fiberglass loose-fill — dominant in cooling-dominant Sun Belt
  • IRA HOMES + HEER rebate status: Planning — IRA implementation plan filed with DOE, rebates pending state legislature

Montana licensing, rebates & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Montana Department of Labor & Industry — Independent Contractor Registration (official site)
  • Permit: permit required for attic insulation retrofit per Montana State Building Code; Montana Independent Contractor Registration required statewide

How attic insulation upgrade costs vary in Montana

Montana's combination of 7,800 annual heating degree days, -40°F winter low temperatures across the entire state, and 48-inch frost depth drives heating-dominant insulation upgrades statewide — R-49 blown-fiberglass attic plus R-20 wall cavity insulation plus continuous polyiso exterior on retrofits is now the dominant Montana spec, with mountain-elevation Bozeman, Big Sky, and Whitefish additionally requiring R-60 attic upgrades and triple-pane glazing on every premium ski-town residential retrofit to manage extreme winter heating costs above $500/month.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Montana Code §50-60 makes the Montana Department of Labor & Industry Building Codes Bureau the statewide residential energy code authority — Montana adopted IECC 2021 effective 2024 statewide, the most current energy code basis in the Mountain West alongside Colorado and Maine, and the Montana Department of Environmental Quality Energy Office is the planned IRA HOMES + HEER administrator with planned 2026 launch through the existing NorthWestern Energy E+ residential weatherization program providing utility-funded insulation rebates statewide.
