Montgomery concrete FAQ

How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

How much does concrete work cost in Montgomery?

Concrete costs in Montgomery range from $6-$12 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, patios) to $15-$30/sqft for stamped or decorative finishes, including labor and materials.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

What Montgomery homeowners should know about concrete work

Why pricing varies in Montgomery. Labor rates here run roughly 16% below the national average per BLS metro data, which is the main reason this city's pricing sits below the national midpoint. Our analysis shows concrete work in Montgomery running roughly 14% below the US baseline.

Montgomery's site and climate factors. Montgomery has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Concrete in Montgomery cures differently than in dry climates — high humidity slows evaporation, which actually improves curing strength but extends the timeline. The bigger concern is expansive clay soils common in this region, which cause heave and cracking. Proper sub-base preparation (4-6 inches of compacted gravel) is non-negotiable. Homes in Montgomery averaging 37 years old typically have stable sub-grades that simplify concrete work. New pours integrate cleanly with existing slabs and foundations without the settling corrections older properties need.

Montgomery paperwork, inspections, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Montgomery require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Who's doing the work in Montgomery. Montgomery's 201,332 residents support a healthy contractor market. Competition keeps pricing fair, though the pool is not so large that you can skip due diligence on references. Montgomery's stable population base means the contractor market is not stretched thin. Homeowners benefit from competitive pricing and shorter lead times.

Montgomery, AL: skilled trade wages

The BLS reports that cement masons and concrete finishers working in the Montgomery, AL metro earn $18.92/hr at the median, putting them 24% below the national figure of $25.00/hr. Construction laborers see $17.38/hr locally, 21% below the $22.08/hr national average.

Lower labor costs are the main reason concrete work quotes in Montgomery come in below the national average. Since labor accounts for roughly 40-60% of concrete work costs, these wage differences translate directly into what you pay.

Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS), most recent annual release.

Local factors: concrete work in Montgomery, AL

Montgomery's housing profile. At an average of 37 years, Montgomery housing is squarely in the first-replacement cycle. The original components installed during construction are reaching end of life. concrete work demand is strong in this age range, and contractors are familiar with the common building patterns.

HOA and neighborhood rules. HOA involvement is uncommon in Montgomery, so most concrete work projects avoid architectural review delays. City permitting alone governs the process.

Contractor market picture. In a mature market like Montgomery, contractors rely on repeat business and referrals rather than new-construction volume. This tends to produce fair pricing and motivated service, but the smaller contractor pool means a bad choice is harder to recover from. Montgomery's large market has concrete contractors ranging from 1-person decorative specialists to large commercial outfits that also do residential. For standard flatwork (driveways, patios), mid-size companies with dedicated residential crews typically offer the best combination of pricing and quality.

Timing considerations. Montgomery allows year-round concrete work, but the ideal window is spring and fall when temperatures support proper curing without the heat-related challenges of summer. Avoid scheduling large pours during the hottest months — early morning pours are the only reliable option.

What drives cost. Lower labor costs are Montgomery's advantage for concrete work — local wages run 16% below the national average. This puts Montgomery in the bottom third nationally for concrete work labor costs. At 37 years average home age, Montgomery properties are hitting their first major replacement cycle for systems and components. concrete work demand is at peak levels in this age band, which keeps contractor schedules full but pricing competitive.

Red flags to watch for. Any Montgomery contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion. Watch for concrete work quotes in Montgomery that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Lump-sum bids hide margin and make change orders impossible to evaluate.
