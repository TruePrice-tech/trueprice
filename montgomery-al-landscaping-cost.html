Frequently asked landscaping questions

What is xeriscaping?

Xeriscaping uses drought-tolerant plants, efficient irrigation, and permeable surfaces to minimize water use. It cuts outdoor water consumption 50-75% and is increasingly required in water-restricted regions.

Do I need a permit for landscaping in Montgomery?

Most landscaping in Montgomery does not require permits. Exceptions include retaining walls over 4 feet, grading that changes drainage, connecting to city water for irrigation, and work near property lines.

How often should I replace mulch?

Replenish organic mulch annually (top off to 3 inches). Full replacement every 2-3 years prevents matting and fungal buildup. Rock and rubber mulch last 5-10 years but do not improve soil.

Montgomery, AL: landscaping cost factors

Why pricing varies in Montgomery. Labor rates here run roughly 16% below the national average per BLS metro data, which is the main reason this city's pricing sits below the national midpoint. For landscaping specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Montgomery at roughly 16% below the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Montgomery has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Montgomery's heat and humidity create both opportunities and challenges for landscaping. Native and drought-tolerant species (yaupon holly, muhly grass, lantana) thrive with minimal irrigation and cut water bills 40-60% compared to traditional lawns. Invasive species and aggressive growth mean maintenance budgets run 15-20% higher than dry-climate markets. A complete landscaping project in Montgomery typically breaks down as: plant material (30-40%), hardscape/materials (20-30%), labor (25-35%), and design (5-10%). For budget-conscious homeowners, phasing the project over 2-3 seasons lets plants establish naturally and reduces upfront costs.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most landscaping projects in Montgomery require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 201,332, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Montgomery's stable population means contractor capacity rarely tightens, so off-season pricing (typically Nov-Feb) can save 10-15% on non-emergency work.

Local factors: landscaping in Montgomery, AL

Home stock in Montgomery. With average home age near 37 years, Montgomery homes are at the typical first-replacement milestone for major systems. Original furnaces, AC units, water heaters, and roofs installed when the homes were built are aging out — landscaping is hitting peak demand in this age band.

HOA & compliance. HOA involvement is uncommon in Montgomery, so most landscaping projects avoid architectural review delays. City permitting alone governs the process.

Local market conditions. Montgomery has a stable, lower-growth construction market, which usually means more competitive contractor pricing than fast-growing metros. The tradeoff: smaller contractor pool, so reputation and reviews matter more — a few bad actors can dominate the local landscape. Montgomery's large market supports full-service landscape design firms, installation-only crews, and maintenance companies. For projects over $8,000, a dedicated design firm with installation capability typically provides better results than splitting design and installation across separate companies.

Seasonal timing. Montgomery offers a longer landscaping season than cold-climate markets. The busiest period is March through June when spring planting demand peaks. Fall (October-December) is an excellent time for plantings, hardscape projects, and irrigation system installation at lower competition and often better pricing.

What drives cost. Lower labor costs are Montgomery's advantage for landscaping project — local wages run 16% below the national average. This puts Montgomery in the bottom third nationally for landscaping project labor costs. At 37 years average home age, Montgomery properties are hitting their first major replacement cycle for systems and components. landscaping project demand is at peak levels in this age band, which keeps contractor schedules full but pricing competitive.

Red flags to watch for. Check that any Montgomery contractor doing landscaping project carries both general liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers' compensation. Request certificates directly from the insurer, not just copies the contractor provides. In Montgomery, verify your landscaping project contractor pulls the permit themselves — never pull it in your own name. If they ask you to pull the permit, they may not be properly licensed to do the work.
