Painting questions for Montgomery homeowners

How much does interior painting cost per room?

A typical room costs $300-$700 for walls and ceiling. A full interior (5-7 rooms) runs $2,500-$6,000. Trim, doors, and closets add $100-$300 per room.

Is spray painting better than brush and roller?

Spraying is faster and gives a smoother finish on large, flat surfaces. Brush and roller are better for detail work and touchups. Many pros spray first, then back-roll for adhesion.

How often should a house be painted?

Wood siding needs repainting every 5-7 years. Stucco and fiber cement last 8-12 years between coats. Vinyl and aluminum siding rarely need painting. Climate, sun exposure, and paint quality affect timing.

What should I look for in a painting quote?

A professional quote specifies: paint brand and sheen, number of coats, prep work included, surface repairs, primer usage, timeline, warranty, and what is excluded (trim, doors, ceilings).

What Montgomery homeowners should know about exterior painting

Why pricing varies in Montgomery. Labor rates here run roughly 16% below the national average per BLS metro data, which is the main reason this city's pricing sits below the national midpoint. Our analysis shows exterior painting in Montgomery running roughly 7% below the US baseline.

Montgomery's site and climate factors. Montgomery has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Mildew and algae staining are the #1 paint complaint in Montgomery. Any exterior paint job here must use 100% acrylic latex with mildewcide additive — skipping the mildewcide saves $50-100 per gallon but guarantees black streaking within 18 months. Montgomery homes averaging 37 years old typically have 1-3 paint layers in good condition. Prep is simpler — power wash, spot-prime bare spots, and apply 2 coats of finish. This keeps the overall project cost lower than older homes with heavy prep requirements.

Montgomery paperwork, inspections, and licensing. Most exterior painting projects in Montgomery require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Who's doing the work in Montgomery. Montgomery's 201,332 residents support a healthy contractor market. Competition keeps pricing fair, though the pool is not so large that you can skip due diligence on references. Montgomery's stable population base means the contractor market is not stretched thin. Homeowners benefit from competitive pricing and shorter lead times.

How the local trade market looks. In a mature market like Montgomery, contractors rely on repeat business and referrals rather than new-construction volume.
