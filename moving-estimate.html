Local moving cost

Local moves (under 50 miles) are typically charged hourly: $25–$50 per mover per hour. A 2-person crew for a typical 2-bedroom apartment: $700–$2,500 total (4–8 hours of work plus drive time).

Most movers have a 2–3 hour minimum. Tight access (3rd-floor walkup, narrow staircases, long carry from truck to door) adds 25–50% in time.

Long-distance moving cost

Long-distance moves are priced by weight (in pounds) times distance, plus fuel surcharges. Typical cost: $0.50–$0.80 per pound per 1,000 miles. A 7,500 lb 3-bedroom move from NY to FL (~1,200 miles) runs $4,500–$7,200.

Ask for a binding estimate (price won't change unless scope changes) vs non-binding (price can rise based on actual weight at destination). Always get 3 binding quotes before committing.

Full-service vs DIY moving

Full-service movers (packing + loading + transport + unloading + unpacking): $3,000–$15,000+ for a typical home. DIY truck rental + boxes + your own labor: $200–$1,500 for local, $2,500–$6,000 for cross-country.

Hybrid: Pack yourself, hire movers for loading/transport. Saves 40–60% vs full-service. PODS / mobile containers split the difference — you load on your schedule, they transport.

Moving cost factors

Distance

Local: hourly. Long-distance: per pound + per mile. Cross-country adds fuel surcharges.

Home size / volume

Studio: ~3,000 lbs. 2-BR: ~5,000 lbs. 3-BR: ~7,500 lbs. 4-BR: ~10,000+ lbs. Each box ~30–40 lbs.

Time of year

May–September is peak moving season — 20–30% more expensive. October–April is cheapest.

Day of week / month

Weekends and end-of-month are most expensive. Weekday mid-month moves are 15–25% cheaper.

Packing services

Full pack: $400–$2,500 extra. Partial pack (just kitchen, fragile): $200–$800. DIY packing saves the most.

Insurance / valuation

Basic coverage (60 cents/lb) is free but minimal. Full-value protection: 1–3% of declared value of contents.

Frequently asked questions about moving cost

How much do movers cost?
Local move (under 50 miles): $700–$2,500 for 2-bedroom. Long-distance: $3,000–$7,000 for 2-3 BR. Cross-country: $5,000–$12,000. DIY truck rental cuts cost 50–70%.
How much does it cost to move a 3-bedroom house?
Local: $1,500–$4,500. Same-state: $3,000–$7,500. Long-distance: $5,000–$11,000. Cross-country: $8,500–$18,000. Add $1,000–$3,000 for full packing service.
What's the cheapest way to move?
DIY truck rental (U-Haul, Penske, Budget): $50–$300 local, $1,000–$2,500 long-distance. Save more by recruiting friends with pizza/beer. PODS/mobile containers cost more but eliminate the driving.
Should I hire packers?
Yes if: you have less than a week, fragile/valuable items, or a long-distance move. No if: you're DIY-comfortable and have 2–3 weeks of evenings to pack. Packers save 20–40 hours but cost $400–$2,500.
When is the cheapest time to move?
October–April is 20–30% cheaper than May–September peak season. Mid-month weekdays are cheapest within the month. Avoid end-of-month and weekends — demand is 50–100% higher.
