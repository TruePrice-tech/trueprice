Frequently asked gutter questions

What is the best gutter material?

Aluminum is the best overall value: lightweight, rust-proof, paintable, and affordable. Copper is premium and maintenance-free but costs 3-5x more. Steel is strongest but rusts in humid climates. Avoid vinyl in cold regions.

Are seamless gutters worth the extra cost?

Yes. Seamless gutters cost 20-30% more than sectional but eliminate most leak points. They are custom-formed on-site to fit your home exactly, look cleaner, and require less maintenance over their lifespan.

How do I know if my gutters are failing?

Signs include: water spilling over during rain, visible sagging or pulling away from the fascia, rust spots or holes, peeling paint on fascia or soffits, and erosion or pooling at the foundation.

How long do gutters last?

Aluminum gutters last 20-30 years. Steel lasts 15-20 years. Copper lasts 50+ years. Vinyl lasts 10-15 years. Seamless gutters outlast sectional because they have fewer leak-prone joints.

Where Nashua's pricing comes from. Contractor labor rates in Nashua are roughly average for the US. Local conditions in Nashua. Nashua has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Ice dams are the primary gutter concern in Nashua. Heated gutter cables ($400-800 installed per 50 feet) prevent the ice backup that tears gutters off eaves and drives water behind siding. Ice-and-water shield membrane on the eave edge (6+ feet up from the gutter line) provides the secondary defense. Seamless aluminum gutters are the Nashua standard: $6-12 per linear foot installed. Copper ($20-40/ft) and zinc ($15-25/ft) offer 50+ year lifespans but at 3-4x the cost. For most homeowners, aluminum with a quality finish provides the best value at 20-30 years before replacement.
