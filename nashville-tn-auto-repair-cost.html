Nashville-area vehicle inspections and warranty protections

Davidson County (Nashville) participates in the state's annual vehicle emissions testing program. The test fee is $9 at state-certified inspection stations scattered throughout the metro. No companion safety inspection exists. Vehicles that fail the emissions scan must undergo documented repairs and retest; the state does not offer a repair-cost assistance or waiver program like several northern states do, so the full repair burden falls on the vehicle owner.

Under Tennessee statute, the Lemon Law covers new vehicles with persistent defects arising within the manufacturer's original express warranty period. The state Consumer Protection Act addresses deceptive repair billing and unauthorized work. Shops must furnish written estimates upon request and may not exceed the authorized amount without obtaining fresh consent. Complaints can be directed to the Tennessee Attorney General's Consumer Protection Division or the Nashville BBB chapter.
