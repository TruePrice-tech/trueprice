Healthcare in Nashville, TN: what locals should know

Hospital landscape

Nashville is served by multiple hospital systems competing for patients. Understanding which hospitals are in your insurance network before an emergency can save you thousands in out-of-network charges.

Insurance coverage

Check your plan's network carefully. In Nashville, the difference between in-network and out-of-network charges for the same procedure can be 3-5x.

Negotiation leverage

Every Nashville hospital has a financial assistance program. Ask for the self-pay rate before accepting any bill at face value. Most Nashville hospitals offer 20-40% prompt-pay discounts.

Neighborhood access

East Nashville, 12 South, Germantown residents have access to community health centers with sliding-fee scales for primary care, often at a fraction of ER costs.

Hospital systems and safety-net providers: Nashville edition

Vanderbilt University Medical Center, HCA Healthcare (headquartered in Nashville), Ascension Saint Thomas, and TriStar Health (HCA) serve the metro. Nashville is the headquarters of HCA, the largest for-profit hospital operator in the country. Nashville General Hospital at Meharry is the city-owned safety-net hospital affiliated with Meharry Medical College. Vanderbilt is the academic anchor and Level I trauma center.

Tennessee's uninsured rate is approximately 10.2%, above the national average, driven by the state's refusal to expand Medicaid under the ACA. Davidson County's rate is similar. Tennessee's repeated failure to pass Insure Tennessee (a Medicaid expansion proposal) leaves an estimated 290,000 Tennesseans in the coverage gap statewide.

Nashville average medical procedure costs

An ER visit at Vanderbilt averages $2,500-$4,200, while Nashville General charges $900-$1,900 for comparable acuity. MRI at Ascension Saint Thomas Midtown runs $1,000-$2,200; freestanding imaging centers in Brentwood and Franklin offer the same scan for $300-$600. Heart surgery at Vanderbilt ranges from $50,000 to $120,000 before insurance negotiated rates.

Vanderbilt and HCA both publish CMS-mandated price transparency files. Nashville General publishes a self-pay rate schedule. Tennessee's Division of Health Statistics publishes hospital financial data. HCA's Nashville-area transparency data covers TriStar facilities and is organized by service line.
