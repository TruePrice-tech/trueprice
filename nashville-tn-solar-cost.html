What Nashville residents ask about solar

Can I add a battery to my solar system?

Yes. Adding a home battery (10-13 kWh) costs $8,000-$15,000 installed. Batteries provide backup power during outages and can help avoid peak-rate charges in time-of-use markets.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

Do solar panels increase home value?

Owned solar systems add roughly $15,000-$20,000 to home value according to national studies. Leased systems do not add value and can complicate the sale process.

Nashville Utility Rate and Net Metering Structure

NES distributes TVA-generated power and operates under TVA's Distributed Generation policy. NES net metering credits exported energy at the retail rate for residential systems under 10 kW, which is among the most favorable structures in the Southeast. TVA's Valley Distributed Solar Program provides the framework. NES retail rates are stable and relatively low ($0.10-$0.12 per kWh) because TVA hydroelectric and nuclear generation keeps wholesale costs down. Piedmont Natural Gas service is unrelated to solar economics. The favorable NEM rate plus low retail rates create a specific sizing challenge: oversizing generates less financial return because the retail-rate credit is modest in absolute terms.

Nashville Interconnection and Grid Approval

NES interconnection for residential systems under 10 kW runs 4-8 weeks through the NES Distributed Generation application process. Williamson County (Franklin, Brentwood) maintains separate permit processes. NES's status as a TVA distributor means interconnection terms are set by TVA policy rather than state regulation.

Nashville Solar Incentives and Tax Credits

The 30 percent federal ITC is the anchor incentive. Tennessee has no state solar tax credit. Tennessee exempts solar installations from state sales tax under TCA 67-6-346. TVA's Green Power Providers program has been replaced by the Valley Distributed Solar Program with modified terms. No NES-specific solar rebate currently exists. TVA EnergyRight rebates apply to energy-efficiency improvements but not directly to solar. Tennessee does not exempt solar from property tax. Federal ITC reduces a $20,000 system to approximately $14,000. The favorable NEM retail-rate credit partially compensates for the lack of state incentives.

Nashville Solar Permitting Process

Metropolitan Nashville Department of Codes Administration issues solar permits through the online portal with 2-4 week turnaround. Tennessee Home Improvement License (tn.gov/commerce) is required. NES interconnection application should be filed in parallel with permitting. The explosive growth means permit processing times fluctuate with development volume.
