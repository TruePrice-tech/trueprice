Fence Installation Cost in Nevada (2026)

Installing 150 linear feet of fencing in Nevada typically runs $5,000–$12,800, with 6-foot vinyl panel the dominant residential choice. Concrete-set posts must clear the 18-inch design frost line. Nevada's HOA penetration is among the highest in the country — over 60% of Las Vegas and Henderson residential subdivisions enforce architectural review controls including specific approved fencing materials (typically only vinyl or stuccoed CMU), maximum heights, and color palettes — and Mojave Desert sustained 110°F+ summer heat plus termite pressure drive vinyl dominance over wood, with Las Vegas-area fence retrofits now exceeding 70% vinyl share.

Nevada climate & post-design drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 18-inch design minimum for post footings
  • Dominant residential fence material: Vinyl panel — dominant in HOA-heavy Sun Belt and termite-risk corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: Low — sub-110 mph design wind, standard 8-foot post spacing
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): High — TPCT zone 2, PT post requirement
  • HOA fencing-rule prevalence: High — large planned-community HOA penetration restricts material/color/height

Nevada licensing, HOA & boundary law

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Nevada State Contractors Board — C-15 (Fencing) classification (official site)
  • Permit: city or county permit typical for residential fence over 6 feet; HOA approval required in most master-planned communities

How fence installation costs vary in Nevada

Nevada's HOA penetration is among the highest in the country — over 60% of Las Vegas and Henderson residential subdivisions enforce architectural review controls including specific approved fencing materials (typically only vinyl or stuccoed CMU), maximum heights, and color palettes — and Mojave Desert sustained 110°F+ summer heat plus termite pressure drive vinyl dominance over wood, with Las Vegas-area fence retrofits now exceeding 70% vinyl share.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Nevada State Contractors Board issues a C-15 Fencing specialty classification under NRS Chapter 624 — making Nevada one of the few states with a dedicated fencing-specialty contractor license that requires separate examination on Nevada-specific HOA architectural review compliance, view-fence masonry design for Las Vegas-area master-planned communities, and CC&R disclosure obligations on every residential fence install.
