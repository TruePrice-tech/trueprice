Foundation Cost in Nevada (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in Nevada typically run $12,400–$28,400 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with slab-on-grade as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 18-inch design frost line. Nevada climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 18-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Slab-on-grade — dominant in Sun Belt and Southeast on warm climates with shallow frost line
  • Expansive soil risk: High — expansive clay subsoil requires geotechnical investigation + reinforced footings
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC D1 — high seismic risk + Special Inspector continuous observation required
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Water table risk: Very low — arid, no water-table exposure on residential lots
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S2 — severe sulfate exposure, ASTM C150 Type II/V cement required
  • IECC climate zone: 3B-5B
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 2 (moderate) — sub-slab depressurization rough-in recommended

Nevada licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Nevada State Contractors Board — A General Engineering, B General Building, C-8 Concrete (or C-15 Plumbing for foundation drainage) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential foundation pour; NV State Contractors Board specialty license required statewide; mandatory ASTM C150 Type II/V sulfate-resistant cement on every Las Vegas Valley residential pour; Las Vegas caliche-clay geotechnical investigation required on every Clark County residential foundation
