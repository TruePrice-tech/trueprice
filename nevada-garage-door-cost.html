Garage Door Replacement Cost in Nevada (2026)

Garage door replacements in Nevada typically run $1,300–$4,600 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 100 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Nevada climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 100 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Moderate — periodic termite pressure, PT framing recommended
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Very high — 80%+ of subdivisions enforce HOA architectural-committee pre-approval on door color/material/style
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: None — no salt-air corrosion exposure
  • IECC climate zone: 3B-5B

Nevada licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Nevada State Contractors Board — C-3 Carpentry / Doors specialty license (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening; NV State Contractors Board C-3 specialty license required statewide; replacement-only installs typically permit-exempt; HOA architectural-committee approval mandatory in 80%+ of Las Vegas Valley subdivisions

How garage door installation costs vary in Nevada

Nevada's combination of extreme summer UV exposure across the Las Vegas Valley and Reno-Tahoe corridor driving Sun-Belt-rated UV-stabilized polyester topcoats on every west-facing garage-door installation to prevent fade in 5-7 years, very-high HOA architectural-control prevalence across 80%+ of Las Vegas Valley subdivisions requiring pre-approval review on door color/material/style, and ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic design category across western Nevada from Reno to Carson City driving continuous-rebar-tie header attachment on every Washoe County residential install drives unique HOA-and-UV-driven material selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Nevada State Contractors Board §624 imposes one of the most aggressive specialty-license enforcement programs in the country with NSCB unlicensed-contractor sting operations a regular fixture across Las Vegas and Reno, requiring every Nevada garage-door contractor to hold a C-3 Carpentry / Doors specialty license, and Nevada is unusual in HOA architectural-control prevalence with the Foundation for Community Association Research documenting 80%+ of Las Vegas Valley residential subdivisions operating mandatory pre-approval committees on garage-door color, material, and style selection — the highest HOA penetration in the U.S. Mountain West.
