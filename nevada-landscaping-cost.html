Landscape Installation Cost in Nevada (2026)

Landscape installation in Nevada typically runs $5,200–$22,000 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with desert-adapted Sonoran or Mojave plant palette as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 4b-9a. Nevada's Mojave Desert combination of 10-inch annual rainfall in the Las Vegas Valley, extreme summer heat above 110°F driving plant transpiration losses, and Lake Mead Colorado River allocation pressure makes residential outdoor water use the largest discretionary residential water demand in the state — Las Vegas Valley xeriscape installs with desert-adapted Mojave Aster, Desert Willow, and Sonoran Palo Verde now dominate the typical front-yard install, with Northern Nevada Reno-Sparks adopting Sierra-Nevada-foothills xeriscape with native sagebrush and Great Basin grasses on every new residential install.

Nevada climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 4b-9a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 10 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 250 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Extreme — multi-year drought cycles drive xeriscape adoption + statewide turf restrictions
  • Irrigation dependency: Very high — 60%+ of residential outdoor water for irrigation
  • Frost-line trench depth: 24-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Desert-adapted — Sonoran or Mojave palette (cactus, palo verde, agave) on most front yards
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: Regional WUI defensible-space rule in mapped fire-hazard zones (CAL FIRE-style)

Nevada licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Nevada State Contractors Board — license C-10 (Landscape Contracting) + Nevada Department of Agriculture Pesticide Branch (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install on existing yards; NSCB C-10 specialty license required statewide on contracts above $1,000; NDA Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care

How landscape installation costs vary in Nevada

Nevada's Mojave Desert combination of 10-inch annual rainfall in the Las Vegas Valley, extreme summer heat above 110°F driving plant transpiration losses, and Lake Mead Colorado River allocation pressure makes residential outdoor water use the largest discretionary residential water demand in the state — Las Vegas Valley xeriscape installs with desert-adapted Mojave Aster, Desert Willow, and Sonoran Palo Verde now dominate the typical front-yard install, with Northern Nevada Reno-Sparks adopting Sierra-Nevada-foothills xeriscape with native sagebrush and Great Basin grasses on every new residential install.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Nevada AB 356 (2021) created the most restrictive ornamental turf removal mandate in the country — every non-functional ornamental turf area within the Southern Nevada Water Authority service area (Las Vegas, Henderson, North Las Vegas, Boulder City) must be removed by 2027, with the Nevada State Contractors Board enforcing C-10 license-holder compliance on every Las Vegas Valley residential turf-replacement contract, and SNWA additionally administering a $3-per-square-foot turf-removal rebate that has converted over 200 million sq ft of Las Vegas Valley residential turf to xeriscape since 2003.
