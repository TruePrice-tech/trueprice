Siding Replacement Cost in Nevada (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Nevada typically runs $10,500–$28,000, with three-coat hard-coat stucco the dominant residential cladding choice. Las Vegas Valley summer surface temperatures on dark exterior cladding reach 165°F+, driving stucco's continued dominance over vinyl (which buckles under sustained heat) and fiber-cement (which expands/contracts at higher rates than stucco's mineral substrate) — and Reno-Sparks WUI overlay across Washoe County has driven Class A ignition-resistant cladding adoption on Truckee Meadows retrofits since the 2017-2020 wildfire seasons.

Nevada climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 3B-5B
  • Dominant residential cladding: Three-coat hard-coat stucco — dominant in arid and Mediterranean Sun Belt
  • Hurricane wind tier: Low wind exposure — sub-110 mph design wind
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: Regional WUI overlay — ignition-resistant cladding required in mapped fire-hazard zones
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: No salt-air corrosion exposure (interior continental)

Nevada licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Nevada State Contractors Board — B (General Building) or C-13 (Wet Walls/Stucco) classification (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for full re-side

How siding replacement costs vary in Nevada

Las Vegas Valley summer surface temperatures on dark exterior cladding reach 165°F+, driving stucco's continued dominance over vinyl (which buckles under sustained heat) and fiber-cement (which expands/contracts at higher rates than stucco's mineral substrate) — and Reno-Sparks WUI overlay across Washoe County has driven Class A ignition-resistant cladding adoption on Truckee Meadows retrofits since the 2017-2020 wildfire seasons.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Nevada State Contractors Board issues a C-13 Wet Walls/Stucco specialty classification distinct from the C-3 Carpentry or B General Building credentials — Nevada stucco contractors typically also hold a C-31A Drywall classification because three-coat hard-coat stucco scratch-coat work routinely intersects with interior gypsum installation, and Las Vegas-area code requires three-coat hard-coat stucco on all new residential exteriors with synthetic acrylic finish coats permitted only on retrofits.
