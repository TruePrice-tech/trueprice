Painting questions for New Brunswick homeowners

What warranty should I expect?

Expect a minimum 1-year workmanship warranty from the contractor plus manufacturer warranties on materials. Get warranty terms in writing before signing.

Should I get multiple quotes?

Always get at least 3 written quotes. Compare line-item details, not just total price. The lowest quote often omits scope items that appear as change orders later.

How do I find a good painting contractor in New Brunswick?

Check licensing through your state's contractor board, verify insurance, read reviews from New Brunswick homeowners, and get at least 3 written quotes with line-item detail.

How much should I pay upfront?

Never pay more than 10-15% as a deposit before work begins. Standard payment structure: deposit, progress payment when materials arrive, final payment on completion.

About exterior painting in New Brunswick, NJ

Why pricing varies in New Brunswick. Labor rates here run roughly 32% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For exterior painting specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts New Brunswick at roughly 20% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. New Brunswick has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. New Brunswick's mix of humidity, moderate UV, and seasonal temperature swings means exterior paint faces all three major degradation pathways. 100% acrylic latex with mildew-resistant primer is the standard — expect 7-10 year repaint cycles with proper prep. Homes in New Brunswick averaging 57 years often have lead paint underneath existing coats. Lead testing before scraping is both a safety requirement and a cost factor — lead abatement adds $2,000-8,000 to a whole-house exterior paint job. Never dry-scrape pre-1978 paint without testing.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most exterior painting projects in New Brunswick require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. At 55,831 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. New Brunswick has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.
