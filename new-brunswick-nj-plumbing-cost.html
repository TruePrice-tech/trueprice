Plumbing in New Brunswick: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Water quality in New Brunswick is moderate. You probably won't need a softener, but flushing your water heater tank once a year prevents sediment accumulation and keeps it running efficiently.

❄

Freeze risk

New Brunswick occasionally sees freezing temperatures that can threaten exposed pipes. Basic insulation on outdoor spigots and crawlspace pipes is a smart, inexpensive precaution.

🔧

Material recommendation

Most New Brunswick plumbers now recommend PEX for whole-house repiping. It's code-compliant, durable, and significantly cheaper than copper. The main exception: some homeowners prefer copper for its longevity and perceived quality.

💡

Local tip

Many New Brunswick homes built before 1970 have galvanized steel pipes that corrode from the inside out. If your water pressure has been steadily dropping or you see rusty water, a whole-house repipe is likely needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in New Brunswick?

Plumber rates in New Brunswick typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
