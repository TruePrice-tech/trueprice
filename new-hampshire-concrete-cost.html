Concrete Driveway Cost in New Hampshire (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in New Hampshire typically run $3,600–$9,200 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 4000 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 48-inch design frost line, and the state sits in most severe freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F3). New Hampshire's combination of 48-inch frost depth in interior climate zones 5A-6A, F3 (most severe) freeze-thaw exposure across the entire state, and -25°F winter low temperatures forces 6%+ air-entrainment plus Type II cement on every New Hampshire residential pour — and North Atlantic salt-spray on the Seacoast from Hampton to Portsmouth combined with New Hampshire road de-icing salt exposure on every grade-level driveway edge drives 0.40 maximum water-cement ratio plus continuous epoxy-coated rebar tie schedules on every coastal New Hampshire residential pour.

New Hampshire climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 48-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F3 — most severe freeze-thaw + heavy de-icer use, 6-8% air-entrainment + 0.40 max w/c required

  • Expansive soil risk: Low — sandy or stable subsoil, standard slab-on-grade design
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC B — minor seismic risk, standard tie-down requirements
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: Very high — heavy de-icer use drives 0.40 max w/c + epoxy-coated rebar
