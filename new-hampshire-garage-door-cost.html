Garage Door Replacement Cost in New Hampshire (2026)

Garage door replacements in New Hampshire typically run $1,300–$4,500 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 115 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. New Hampshire's combination of 48-inch frost depth across the southern half plus deeper frost in the White Mountains, F3 freeze-thaw cycling driving aggressive sealant degradation on every garage-door bottom seal at 3-4 year intervals, persistent Atlantic salt-spray on every Rockingham county exposure driving stainless-hardware replacement at 5-7 year intervals, and NH mountain elevations above 4,000 feet driving frost-protected shallow foundation alternatives drives extreme cold-climate-driven material selection.

New Hampshire climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 115 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: Low — DASMA 108 standard rating at sub-110 mph design wind
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Low — minimal termite pressure
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Low — limited HOA garage-door restrictions outside metro subdivisions
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: Moderate — bayfront salt-spray, stainless hardware recommended
  • IECC climate zone: 5A-6A

New Hampshire licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: New Hampshire has no statewide residential builder license — NH Office of Professional Licensure & Certification oversees specialty trades only (official site)
  • Permit: town or municipal building permit required for new garage-door opening; no state license; New Hampshire State Building Code 2018 IRC + IECC mandatory; replacement-only installs typically permit-exempt; alpine elevations above 4,000 feet require frost-protected shallow foundation alternatives

How garage door installation costs vary in New Hampshire

State-specific code or insurance rule: New Hampshire has no statewide residential builder license — NH Office of Professional Licensure & Certification oversees only specialty trades like plumbers, electricians, and oil-burner installers, leaving garage-door contractor competency and bond entirely to municipal building departments and homeowner due diligence, and New Hampshire State Building Code (2018 IRC + 2018 IECC) separately mandates frost-protected shallow foundation (FPSF) detailing as an alternative to deep frost-line excavation on every alpine residential foundation pour above 4,000 feet White Mountain elevation including detached garage construction.
