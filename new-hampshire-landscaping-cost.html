Landscape Installation Cost in New Hampshire (2026)

Landscape installation in New Hampshire typically runs $4,200–$17,600 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with forest-edge native-shrub and shade-perennial design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 4a-6a. New Hampshire's combination of acidic White Mountain forest-soil substrates, Granite State boulder substrates across the Lakes Region, and 150-day growing season restricts residential lawn establishment to specialty cool-season fine fescue and creeping red fescue blends with mid-cycle overseeding — and Seacoast salt-spray on Hampton, Rye, and Portsmouth residential properties combined with intense White Mountains fall-foliage maple-tannin drip on east-facing exterior elevations measurably stresses ornamental flower beds and freshly-installed shade-tree saplings.

New Hampshire climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 4a-6a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 43 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 150 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Low — adequate residential precipitation, minimal drought stress
  • Irrigation dependency: Low — irrigation optional, established plants survive on rainfall
  • Frost-line trench depth: 48-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Forest-edge — native-shrub and shade-perennial design under mature canopy
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

New Hampshire licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: No statewide landscape license; New Hampshire Department of Agriculture, Markets & Food Pesticide Control Division (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; NHDAMF Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care

How landscape installation costs vary in New Hampshire

New Hampshire's combination of acidic White Mountain forest-soil substrates, Granite State boulder substrates across the Lakes Region, and 150-day growing season restricts residential lawn establishment to specialty cool-season fine fescue and creeping red fescue blends with mid-cycle overseeding — and Seacoast salt-spray on Hampton, Rye, and Portsmouth residential properties combined with intense White Mountains fall-foliage maple-tannin drip on east-facing exterior elevations measurably stresses ornamental flower beds and freshly-installed shade-tree saplings.

State-specific code or insurance rule: New Hampshire RSA 430:31 makes the New Hampshire Department of Agriculture, Markets & Food Pesticide Control Division the residential pesticide applicator licensor with statewide preemption over every municipal pesticide ordinance — Live Free or Die framework explicitly blocks Manchester, Concord, Nashua, and Portsmouth from adopting local pesticide restrictions on residential landscape contractors, while NHDAMF maintains the New Hampshire Pollinator Stewardship Initiative protecting commercial maple sugaring operations and honeybee yards adjacent to suburban subdivisions.
