Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in New Jersey (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in New Jersey typically run $2,700–$6,400 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2020 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Service entrance design assumes a 200A minimum on new residential construction. New Jersey Shore coastal salt-haze deposition from Cape May to Sandy Hook corrodes outdoor service equipment within 5-7 years, mandating stainless mounting hardware and NEMA 4X meter sockets on shoreline upgrades — and the Pine Barrens' high water table drowns ground-mounted meter pedestals during nor'easter rainfall events, forcing meter-base elevation above the local water table on Burlington and Ocean county properties.

New Jersey code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2020 NEC — dwelling unit AFCI on most 120V branch circuits, GFCI expanded
  • State amendments: New Jersey adopts 2020 NEC with state amendments under N.J.A.C. 13:31 — Bureau of Electrical Subcode regulates apprentices, journeymen, and electrical contractors statewide
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: Moderate — typical interior continental flash density
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 18-to-24-inch design depth on buried service conduit

New Jersey licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: New Jersey Division of Consumer Affairs — Board of Examiners of Electrical Contractors (official site)
  • Permit: municipal electrical permit required for panel upgrade and service-line work; UCC subcode official inspection

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in New Jersey

New Jersey Shore coastal salt-haze deposition from Cape May to Sandy Hook corrodes outdoor service equipment within 5-7 years, mandating stainless mounting hardware and NEMA 4X meter sockets on shoreline upgrades — and the Pine Barrens' high water table drowns ground-mounted meter pedestals during nor'easter rainfall events, forcing meter-base elevation above the local water table on Burlington and Ocean county properties.

State-specific code or insurance rule: New Jersey N.J.A.C. 13:31 makes the Electrical Contractor business license a corporate-entity credential separate from the individual Qualifier credential — the Qualifier (typically a Master Electrician) must be on the contractor's payroll for the license to remain active, and a contractor losing its Qualifier has 90 days to replace before license suspension, unique among Mid-Atlantic states.
