Gutter Installation Cost in New Jersey (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in New Jersey typically runs $1,300–$5,000, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 47 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. New Jersey's 47-inch annual rainfall combined with high mature-deciduous tree canopy across Bergen, Essex, and Morris counties drives near-universal gutter-guard retrofit demand on long-tenure suburban properties — and post-Sandy BFE elevation rules drive below-grade downspout-discharge re-routing on every Cape May, Atlantic, and Ocean county shoreline retrofit.

New Jersey rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 47 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 30 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: Moderate — periodic ice-dam events on north-facing eaves
  • Hurricane wind tier: High — 140 mph+ design wind on coastal counties
  • Tree-canopy density: High — mature deciduous canopy drives gutter-guard demand and 2x annual cleaning frequency

New Jersey licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: New Jersey Division of Consumer Affairs — Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration (official site)
  • Permit: municipal building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction

How gutter installation costs vary in New Jersey

State-specific code or insurance rule: New Jersey N.J.S.A. §56:8-136 requires every contractor performing residential exterior work above $500 to register as a Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) with the Division of Consumer Affairs — and unique to New Jersey, the HIC registration mandates $500,000 of liability insurance on file, a written contract with specific 18-point disclosures, and prohibits any payment over 33% of contract value before substantial completion.
