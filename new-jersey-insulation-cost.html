Attic Insulation Upgrade Cost in New Jersey (2026)

Attic insulation upgrades in New Jersey typically run $2,000–$7,800 for a 1,500 sq ft attic upgrade R-30 to R-49 blown cellulose, with blown cellulose loose-fill the dominant residential retrofit material at the IECC R-49 attic minimum. New Jersey's combination of 5,000 annual heating degree days, 36-inch frost depth, and Atlantic coastal humidity from Cape May to Sandy Hook creates a balanced heating-cooling insulation profile statewide — and New Jersey's pre-1978 housing-stock concentration in Newark, Jersey City, Paterson, and Elizabeth combined with Jersey Shore coastal salt-spray drives combination air-sealing + dense-pack cellulose wall cavity + blown-cellulose attic upgrades on the majority of urban New Jersey insulation retrofit scope, with closed-cell spray foam additionally specified on every Jersey Shore coastal home.

New Jersey energy code & climate-load drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A
  • Required attic R-value: R-49 (IECC 2021 prescriptive minimum)
  • Required wall R-value: R-20 (above-grade wood-frame)
  • Adopted energy code basis: IECC 2021 — current cycle, full mandatory R-49 attic + R-20 wall + air-sealing requirements
  • Stretch code available: Yes — opt-in or mandatory above-baseline pathway
  • Annual heating + cooling load: 5,000 HDD / 1,100 CDD
  • Vapor barrier requirement: Warm-side vapor retarder required (typical IECC zones 5+)
  • Dominant attic insulation material: Blown cellulose loose-fill — dominant in heating-dominant Northeast and Upper Midwest
  • IRA HOMES + HEER rebate status: Live — IRA HOMES + HEER rebates currently available to residential homeowners

New Jersey licensing, rebates & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: New Jersey Division of Consumer Affairs — Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration (official site)
  • Permit: permit required for attic insulation retrofit per New Jersey Uniform Construction Code; HIC registration required above $500 contract

How attic insulation upgrade costs vary in New Jersey

New Jersey's combination of 5,000 annual heating degree days, 36-inch frost depth, and Atlantic coastal humidity from Cape May to Sandy Hook creates a balanced heating-cooling insulation profile statewide — and New Jersey's pre-1978 housing-stock concentration in Newark, Jersey City, Paterson, and Elizabeth combined with Jersey Shore coastal salt-spray drives combination air-sealing + dense-pack cellulose wall cavity + blown-cellulose attic upgrades on the majority of urban New Jersey insulation retrofit scope, with closed-cell spray foam additionally specified on every Jersey Shore coastal home.

State-specific code or insurance rule: New Jersey N.J.S.A. 52:27D-119 imposes the New Jersey Uniform Construction Code with the New Jersey Energy Subcode (N.J.A.C. 5:23-3.18) — New Jersey adopted IECC 2021 effective 2023 statewide and was one of the first Northeast states to adopt the Stretch Code through New Jersey BPU's IRA HOMES + HEER program administration, plus New Jersey Clean Energy Program through Public Service Electric & Gas and Atlantic City Electric provides residential insulation rebates layered with IRA HEAR funding statewide.
