Landscape Installation Cost in New Jersey (2026)

Landscape installation in New Jersey typically runs $4,400–$18,800 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 6a-7b. New Jersey's combination of Coastal Plain sandy soils across the Pine Barrens and Jersey Shore, dense suburban canopy across Bergen and Morris counties, and Barnegat Bay watershed nutrient-management framework restricts standard high-nitrogen turf fertilizer programs across the state — and the state's Pinelands National Reserve overlay across Atlantic, Burlington, Cumberland, Cape May, Camden, Gloucester, Ocean counties imposes Pinelands Commission-approved native plant lists on every new residential landscape install in the 1.1-million-acre overlay zone.

New Jersey climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 6a-7b — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 47 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 195 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Low — adequate residential precipitation, minimal drought stress
  • Irrigation dependency: Low — irrigation optional, established plants survive on rainfall
  • Frost-line trench depth: 36-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

New Jersey licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection Pesticide Control Program + New Jersey Division of Consumer Affairs HIC registration (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; HIC registration required above $500 contract; NJ DEP Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care; New Jersey Fertilizer Law caps phosphorus and nitrogen application statewide

How landscape installation costs vary in New Jersey

State-specific code or insurance rule: New Jersey Fertilizer Law (NJSA 58:10A-64) imposes the most restrictive residential turf fertilizer rule in the Mid-Atlantic outside Maryland — every New Jersey commercial lawn-care contractor and homeowner must apply zero phosphorus on established turf, cap nitrogen at 0.7 lb per 1,000 sq ft per application, prohibit fall fertilizer application after November 15, and post mandatory contractor signage detailing application content, all to protect Barnegat Bay and Delaware Bay TMDL nutrient-reduction targets, with NJDEP enforcement targeting commercial landscape contractor compliance through residential complaint inspections.
