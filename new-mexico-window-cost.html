Window Replacement Cost in New Mexico (2026)

Window replacements in New Mexico typically run $5,400–$14,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR South-Central climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.32, SHGC ≤ 0.25). New Mexico's combination of extreme summer UV exposure across the Albuquerque Basin and Rio Grande Valley driving Sun-Belt-rated UV-stabilized vinyl frames on every west-facing residential window installation, ENERGY STAR South-Central climate zone driving low-SHGC (≤0.25) glazing requirement, Santa Fe Historic District adobe-style preservation requirements on every Santa Fe residential window replacement, and high-altitude UV exposure across the Sangre de Cristo Mountains drives unique Pueblo-Revival-and-arid-climate-driven glazing selection.

New Mexico climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: South-Central — mixed climate, balanced U-factor and lower SHGC (U ≤ 0.32, SHGC ≤ 0.40)
  • IECC climate zone: 3B-5B
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.32 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: 0.25
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 90 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: High — multiple state-mapped historic districts with mandatory review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Moderate — 20-40% pre-1978 housing share, EPA RRP required on selected replacements

New Mexico licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: New Mexico Construction Industries Division — GB-98 General Building Contractor + GB-2 Residential Specialty (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification; NM CID GB-98 license required statewide; replacement-only same-size installs typically permit-exempt; New Mexico Historic Preservation review required on every Santa Fe Historic District window installation

How window replacement costs vary in New Mexico

New Mexico's combination of extreme summer UV exposure across the Albuquerque Basin and Rio Grande Valley driving Sun-Belt-rated UV-stabilized vinyl frames on every west-facing residential window installation, ENERGY STAR South-Central climate zone driving low-SHGC (≤0.25) glazing requirement, Santa Fe Historic District adobe-style preservation requirements on every Santa Fe residential window replacement, and high-altitude UV exposure across the Sangre de Cristo Mountains drives unique Pueblo-Revival-and-arid-climate-driven glazing selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: New Mexico Construction Industries Division §60-13 imposes one of the most credential-segmented residential builder licensing schemes in the country — every NM window contractor must hold a GB-98 General Building Contractor license plus may need a GB-2 Residential Specialty endorsement, and Santa Fe Historic District (the only U.S. historic-preservation overlay covering an entire downtown across 6,000 acres) separately requires Historic Preservation review on every Santa Fe window installation to ensure adobe-style mass-wall fenestration, divided-light grille pattern, and authentic carved-wood lintel detailing remain visually consistent with the Pueblo Revival architectural overlay.
