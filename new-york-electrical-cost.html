Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in New York (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in New York typically run $3,400–$8,200 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2017 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Service entrance design assumes a 200A minimum on new residential construction. NYC and Long Island coastal storm surge during Sandy-class events flooded thousands of meter pedestals and service equipment — FEMA NFIP elevation-above-BFE rules now drive meter-base elevation on every coastal Brooklyn, Queens, and Long Island upgrade, and Adirondack winter ambients in St. Lawrence and Franklin counties brittle PVC fittings, requiring rigid metallic conduit on exterior service masts above 44° latitude.

New York code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2017 NEC — older adoption cycle, local amendments common
  • State amendments: New York has no statewide electrical contractor license — NYC, Yonkers, Long Island, and Buffalo issue independent municipal credentials with NYC operating its own Electrical Code (NYC EC) parallel to NEC
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: Moderate — typical interior continental flash density
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 30-inch+ design depth — buried service conduit must clear hard frost line

New York licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide electrical license — NYC Department of Buildings, Westchester, Nassau, and Suffolk issue municipal credentials (official site)
  • Permit: municipal electrical permit required; NYC requires Licensed Master Electrician on every job

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in New York

State-specific code or insurance rule: New York City operates its own Electrical Code (the NYC EC, currently 2011 cycle with substantial 2008 NEC overlay) entirely parallel to and distinct from the National Electrical Code used in the rest of the state — every NYC panel upgrade requires a Licensed Master Electrician's seal on the filing, with the LME credential issued by the NYC Department of Buildings rather than any state authority, and rigid metallic conduit is mandatory on most circuits.
