Landscape Installation Cost in New York (2026)

Landscape installation in New York typically runs $4,400–$18,800 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 3b-7b. New York's combination of Adirondack and Catskill mountain forest-soil substrates upstate, dense suburban canopy across Westchester and Long Island, and Long Island Sound and Atlantic coastal salt-spray on every Long Island residential property drives Long Island Pine Barrens Commission native-plant overlay on every new install in the 100,000-acre core protected area — and Hudson Valley and Catskills landscape installs require terraced retaining walls and drainage swales on every sloping residential lot, doubling typical hardscape labor versus flat suburban subdivision installs in Long Island Nassau and Suffolk counties.

New York climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 3b-7b — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 41 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 175 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Low — adequate residential precipitation, minimal drought stress
  • Irrigation dependency: Low — irrigation optional, established plants survive on rainfall
  • Frost-line trench depth: 48-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

New York licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide landscape license; New York State Department of Environmental Conservation Pesticide Bureau; NYC Department of Consumer & Worker Protection HIC license (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; NYC HIC license required above $200 contract within five boroughs; NYS DEC Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care

How landscape installation costs vary in New York

State-specific code or insurance rule: New York Environmental Conservation Law §33 imposes the Neighbor Notification Law on every restricted-use pesticide application on residential property — every New York commercial landscape contractor must provide 48-hour advance written notification to neighbors within 150 ft of any restricted-use pesticide application, plus the NYS Child-Safe Playing Fields Act bans synthetic pesticide application on school and daycare property, and NYC Local Law 142 of 2021 separately bans non-essential synthetic pesticide application on every NYC park and city-maintained property, the most restrictive municipal pesticide ordinance in the Northeast.
