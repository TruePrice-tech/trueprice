New York electrical FAQ

How much does an electrician charge per hour in New York?

Electricians in New York typically charge $80-$150 per hour. Master electricians and emergency calls run higher, while apprentice rates are lower.

Is aluminum wiring dangerous?

Aluminum wiring itself is not dangerous, but the connections can overheat due to oxidation and expansion. Homes with aluminum wiring should have COPALUM or AlumiConn connectors installed at every connection point.

How much does it cost to install recessed lighting?

Recessed lights cost $150-$300 per fixture installed when wiring is accessible. A typical living room with 6 lights runs $900-$1,800. LED retrofit kits for existing cans cost $20-$40 each.

What should I look for in a contractor quote?

A professional quote breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. It includes a timeline, payment schedule, warranty terms, and the contractor's license number.

Do I need a permit for electrical work in New York?

Most electrical work projects in New York require a permit. Your contractor should pull the permit and schedule the inspection. Never pull permits in your own name.

What are common red flags in contractor quotes?

Red flags include: no line-item detail, same-day signing pressure, request for more than 30% upfront, no license or insurance verification, and verbal-only warranties.

New York Utility and Code Context

Con Edison serves Manhattan, the Bronx, and most of Queens and Westchester. PSEG Long Island covers Nassau and Suffolk, and Rockaway parcels sit in a Con Ed coverage area with different meter-coordination processes. ConEd's Section 17 requires licensed master electrician coordination for any service change and the utility-side cutover can take 5-15 business days from request to completion.

NYC runs its own electrical code that overlays the 2020 NEC with significant amendments. The NYC Electrical Code requires BX (armored cable), MC cable, or EMT conduit for nearly all residential wiring. Romex (NM cable) is effectively banned in dwelling units inside the five boroughs, which alone adds 20-40% to wiring labor versus code-minimum states.

New York Home Stock and Panel Realities

Pre-war apartment buildings across the boroughs still use 40-60 amp fused services with Dual-Element cartridge fuses. Post-war co-ops and rowhouses typically have 100-amp panels that were upgraded in the 1960s-80s. Any modern renovation in a NYC dwelling will require service upgrade to 200 amps minimum, coordinated with ConEd and filed through DOB NOW.

NYC housing stock is dominated by pre-1940 tenements, brownstones, and six-story walk-ups. Manhattan buildings south of 96th Street are typically served by vertical riser feeds to individual apartment panels. Brooklyn brownstones often have combined meter cabinets in the cellar. Post-war co-ops in Queens frequently have aluminum sub-feeders that need inspection during any panel work.

New York Licensing and Permits

NYC DOB licenses Master Electricians and Special Electricians separately from state certifications. Master Electricians must hold 7.5 years of supervised experience, pass the notoriously difficult NYC exam (historical pass rate under 40%), and carry $100,000 minimum liability coverage. Verify licenses at the DOB License Verification portal before signing.

Electrical permits file through DOB NOW: Electrical. Standard residential jobs process in 2-4 weeks. Historic district work adds LPC review. Con Edison service coordination is a separate filing that can add 3-6 weeks to the real schedule for any service upgrade.
