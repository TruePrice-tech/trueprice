Garage Door questions for New York homeowners

How much does a garage door replacement cost in New York?

A new garage door in New York costs $800-$4,000 for a standard door installed, or $2,500-$8,000+ for a premium insulated or carriage-style door with a new opener.

What R-value should my garage door have?

R-8 is fine if the garage is detached or unheated. R-12 to R-16 is best for attached garages or if you use the space for a workshop. R-18+ is for extreme cold climates or heated garages.

Are smart garage door openers worth it?

Smart openers add $100-$200 over basic models. They let you open, close, and monitor the door from your phone, set schedules, and receive alerts. Useful for deliveries and forgetting to close.

What R-value garage door do I need in New York?

For New York's cold winters, an R-12 to R-16 insulated door is recommended for attached garages. If you heat your garage or use it as a workshop, step up to R-16 or R-18. The extra $200-$500 for higher insulation pays back in 3-5 years through reduced heating costs in adjacent rooms and prevents freezing issues with items stored in the garage.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in New York?

With New York homes averaging 60 years, many still have original garage doors past their useful life. A full replacement ($1,200-$3,000 with a new opener) typically returns 90-100% at resale. Repair is worth it only for newer doors with isolated issues like a single broken spring, damaged panel, or failing opener motor. If the door is original to the house, replacement is the better investment.

What's the best season to replace a garage door in New York?

The ideal window for a New York garage door replacement is late spring or early fall. Moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing, plus you get access to a wider range of styles and colors. Avoid spring if you can, since that is when winter-related spring breakages create peak demand and longer wait times.
