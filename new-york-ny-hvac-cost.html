New York homeowners rely heavily on heating from November through March. Proper furnace sizing matters — an oversized unit cycles too frequently and wastes energy. In New York, a high-efficiency gas furnace (95%+ AFUE) paired with a moderate-SEER AC (14-16) gives you the best value. Heating is your biggest expense, so prioritize furnace efficiency.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new HVAC system cost in New York?

Most homeowners in New York pay between $4,555 and $14,157 for HVAC replacement, depending on system type and efficiency.

Should I replace my AC and furnace at the same time?

Replacing both at once is usually more cost-effective than separate replacements. Matched systems run more efficiently and most contractors offer bundle pricing.

What SEER rating should I choose?

14 SEER is the minimum federal requirement. 16 SEER offers a good balance of efficiency and cost. 18-20+ SEER provides the best energy savings but costs significantly more upfront.
