Plumbing in New York: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Water quality in New York is moderate. You probably won't need a softener, but flushing your water heater tank once a year prevents sediment accumulation and keeps it running efficiently.

❄

Freeze risk

Frozen pipes happen in New York most winters. The most vulnerable spots are pipes in exterior walls, unheated basements, and garages. Pipe insulation is cheap ($0.50-1/ft) and prevents expensive burst-pipe repairs.

🔧

Material recommendation

For New York homes, PEX is the go-to repiping material. Its flexibility means it handles freeze-thaw cycles better than copper or CPVC. It's also faster to install, reducing labor costs.

💡

Local tip

Many New York homes built before 1970 have galvanized steel pipes that corrode from the inside out. If your water pressure has been steadily dropping or you see rusty water, a whole-house repipe is likely needed.

What affects plumbing cost in New York

  • Pipe material (copper, PEX, CPVC)

  • Code requirements and permits
