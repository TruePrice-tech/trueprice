Roof Replacement Cost in New York (2026)

Roof replacement in New York typically runs $11,500–$30,000 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. Long Island carries 130-mph design winds and salt exposure; the Adirondacks and Tug Hill plateau (Lewis, Oswego counties) carry some of the highest ground snow loads (70–100 psf) east of the Rockies, and ice-dam mitigation is a year-round cost driver.

New York climate & code drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A-6A
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate wind exposure — 110–140 mph design wind on coastal counties
  • Hail risk tier: Low — minimal severe hail history
  • Ground snow load: 30 psf — moderate snow load, ice-and-water shield required
  • Dominant residential roof material: asphalt shingle

New York licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Local: NYC DCWP Home Improvement Contractor; Westchester, Suffolk, Nassau, Rockland — county HIC license; no single statewide license (official site)
  • Permit: DOB/municipal permit required

How roof replacement costs vary in New York

State-specific code or insurance rule: New York General Business Law §771 voids any home-improvement contract over $500 that lacks the required written terms — and Article 36-A allows escrow-account requirements for any down-payment over 30% of contract value.
