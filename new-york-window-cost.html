Window Replacement Cost in New York (2026)

Window replacements in New York typically run $7,400–$19,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Northern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.3, no SHGC limit). New York's combination of 48-inch frost depth across the Adirondack region, F3 freeze-thaw cycling across the Adirondacks and Catskills, persistent Atlantic salt-spray on every NYC, Suffolk, Nassau, Westchester, and Long Island coastal exposure, FEMA NFIP base-flood-elevation foundation height verification on every NYC and Long Island coastal residential install post-Sandy, very-high pre-1978 housing share (60%+ statewide) driving mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification, NYC LPC review on every window replacement within NYC's 150+ designated historic districts (the most aggressive municipal historic-preservation program in the U.S.), and New York Energy Code Title 19 Part 1240 above-IECC-baseline U-factor mandate drives extreme cold-climate-and-historic-NYC-driven glazing selection.

New York climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Northern — coldest climates, U-factor priority (U ≤ 0.27 in coldest tier)
  • IECC climate zone: 4A-6A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.3 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: No limit (cold climate)
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 130 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: Mandatory ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated in coastal counties only
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Very high — extensive historic-preservation overlays + mandatory profile/glazing-pattern review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Very high — 60%+ pre-1978 housing share, mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every replacement

New York licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide window license — NYC Department of Buildings and most NY home-rule municipalities issue Building Contractor licenses (official site)
  • Permit: city or town building permit required for window opening modification; NYC DOB Construction Superintendent license required for new-construction; NY Energy Code Title 19 Part 1240 + 2020 Stretch Code mandatory; FEMA NFIP base-flood-elevation foundation height required on every NYC and Long Island coastal residential install post-Sandy; NYC LPC review required on every NYC residential window installation within an LPC-mapped historic district

How window replacement costs vary in New York

State-specific code or insurance rule: New York is one of the few large states without a statewide residential builder license — NYC Department of Buildings, Long Island municipalities, Westchester, Suffolk, and Nassau counties issue local Building Contractor credentials but no centralized state board oversees window contractor competency or bond requirements, plus NYC Landmarks Preservation Commission (LPC) separately requires review on every window replacement within NYC's 150+ designated historic districts (Greenwich Village, Park Slope, Brooklyn Heights, Upper West Side, Greenpoint, Carnegie Hill, Hamilton Heights, etc.) — the most aggressive municipal historic-preservation review program in the country, plus very-high pre-1978 housing share (60%+ statewide) driving mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification.
