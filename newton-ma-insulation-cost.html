Insulation questions for Newton homeowners

Can I add insulation over existing insulation?

Yes, in most cases. Adding blown-in cellulose or unfaced fiberglass over existing attic insulation is common and effective. Never compress existing insulation and do not add a vapor barrier on top of existing insulation.

Should I remove old insulation before adding new?

Remove if the existing insulation is wet, moldy, pest-contaminated, or vermiculite (potential asbestos). Otherwise, add new insulation on top. Removal costs $1-$2/sqft and is usually unnecessary.

What is the difference between open-cell and closed-cell spray foam?

Open-cell (R-3.7/inch) is cheaper, flexible, and better for interior walls. Closed-cell (R-6.5/inch) is rigid, waterproof, adds structural strength, and is required in flood zones and below-grade applications.

How much energy does insulation save?

Insulating an under-insulated attic to current code saves 15-25% on heating and cooling costs. In a home spending $2,000/year on energy, that is $300-$500 annual savings, paying back a $1,500-$2,500 project in 3-5 years.

What warranty should I expect?

Expect a minimum 1-year workmanship warranty from the contractor plus manufacturer warranties on materials. Get warranty terms in writing before signing.

Should I get multiple quotes?

Always get at least 3 written quotes. Compare line-item details, not just total price. The lowest quote often omits scope items that appear as change orders later.

How do I find a good insulation contractor in Newton?

Check licensing through your state's contractor board, verify insurance, read reviews from Newton homeowners, and get at least 3 written quotes with line-item detail.

How much should I pay upfront?

Never pay more than 10-15% as a deposit before work begins. Standard payment structure: deposit, progress payment when materials arrive, final payment on completion.
