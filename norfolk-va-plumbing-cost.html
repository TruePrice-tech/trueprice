Common plumbing questions in Norfolk

How long do plumbing fixtures typically last?

Faucets last 15-20 years, toilets 25-50 years, water heaters 8-12 years (tank) or 15-20 years (tankless), and supply lines should be replaced every 20-25 years.

What is the difference between copper and PEX piping?

Copper lasts 50+ years, resists bacteria, and has proven durability. PEX costs 60% less, installs faster, resists freezing better, and requires fewer joints. Both are code-compliant.

Is trenchless sewer repair worth the extra cost?

Trenchless repair costs 20-40% more but avoids excavating your yard, driveway, or landscaping. It is faster and causes less property damage, often making it cheaper overall.

How much does it cost to replace a water heater?

A standard 50-gallon tank water heater runs $1,200-$2,500 installed. Tankless units cost $2,500-$5,000 but last roughly twice as long and cut energy bills 20-30%.

How do I protect my plumbing during a hurricane in Norfolk?

Hurricane prep for plumbing in Norfolk: locate your main shutoff and test it works before storm season. Shut the main off if you leave during a hurricane warning. After the storm passes, avoid using tap water until authorities confirm safety. Check for sewer backflow damage if flooding reached your property. A backflow preventer is a worthwhile investment and becoming a code requirement locally.

What should a plumbing quote include in Norfolk?

A Norfolk plumbing estimate should detail: scope of work, material specifications, labor costs, permit fees if needed, waste disposal, and warranty. Check that the plumber's license number and insurance are included. Compare three written quotes for any project over $500; make sure the scope matches across all three.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Norfolk?

For non-emergency plumbing in Norfolk, schedule during early fall, between summer water-heater demand and winter freeze calls. Emergency calls cost 50-100% more than scheduled work, so replacing aging water heaters, supply lines, and fixtures proactively during off-peak months saves significantly versus waiting for a failure.
