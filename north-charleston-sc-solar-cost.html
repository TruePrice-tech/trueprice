Frequently asked solar questions

How many panels do I need?

Panel count depends on your electricity usage, panel wattage, and roof sun exposure. A typical home uses 20-30 panels (400W each) to offset 80-100% of electricity consumption.

How much does solar cost in North Charleston?

Most homeowners in North Charleston pay $15,000-$30,000 before incentives for a 6-10kW system. After the 30% federal tax credit, net cost drops to $10,500-$21,000.

Will solar panels damage my roof?

Properly installed panels do not damage roofs. Reputable installers use flashing and sealant at every penetration point. If your roof needs replacement within 5-10 years, do it before installing solar.

How much roof space do I need for solar?

Each 400W panel needs about 18 square feet. A 8kW system (20 panels) requires roughly 360 square feet of unshaded, south-facing roof space. East and west orientations work but produce 10-15% less.

What is net metering?

Net metering credits you for excess electricity your panels send to the grid. Your meter runs backward during the day and forward at night. Policies and credit rates vary by state and utility.

Will solar panels survive a hurricane in North Charleston?

Solar panels on properly rated racking systems handle hurricanes well, often outlasting the roof itself during high-wind events. The critical factors: racking must be certified for North Charleston's wind speed zone (130+ mph), all roof penetrations need proper flashing and sealant, and ongoing maintenance keeps connections tight. Schedule a post-storm inspection with your installer after any significant hurricane event.

What solar incentives are available in North Charleston?

Every North Charleston homeowner installing solar qualifies for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit with no dollar limit. Beyond that, SC may offer state tax credits, utility-specific rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits, and property tax exemptions. The DSIRE database has the current list for your area. These stacked incentives typically reduce net cost by an additional 10-30%.

When is the best time to install solar in North Charleston?

North Charleston homeowners should aim for fall or winter to install solar. Cooler weather makes roof work safer and the system is ready for the high-production spring and summer months. Allow 6-12 weeks from signing a contract through permitting, utility interconnection approval, and installation. Start gathering quotes 2-3 months before your target window. Remember that the 30% ITC applies to the tax year in which installation is completed.
