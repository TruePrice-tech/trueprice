Attic Insulation Upgrade Cost in North Dakota (2026)

Attic insulation upgrades in North Dakota typically run $1,800–$6,800 for a 1,500 sq ft attic upgrade R-30 to R-49 blown cellulose, with blown fiberglass loose-fill the dominant residential retrofit material at the IECC R-49 attic minimum. North Dakota's combination of 8,800 annual heating degree days, -45°F winter low temperatures across the entire state, and 60-inch frost depth drives the most heating-dominant insulation profile in the Lower 48 alongside Maine and Minnesota — R-49+ blown-fiberglass attic plus R-20 wall cavity insulation plus continuous polyiso exterior on retrofits is now the dominant North Dakota spec, with Bakken oil-patch fugitive dust contamination in the Williston, Tioga, and Watford City corridor measurably reducing attic insulation R-value over time and driving 12-15 year retrofit cycles versus 18-25 year national norms.

North Dakota energy code & climate-load drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 6A-7
  • Required attic R-value: R-49 (IECC 2021 prescriptive minimum)
  • Required wall R-value: R-20 (above-grade wood-frame)
  • Adopted energy code basis: IECC 2018 — prior cycle, R-49 attic + R-20 wall, advisory air-sealing
  • Stretch code available: No — IECC base code only
  • Annual heating + cooling load: 8,800 HDD / 600 CDD
  • Vapor barrier requirement: Warm-side vapor retarder required (typical IECC zones 5+)
  • Dominant attic insulation material: Blown fiberglass loose-fill — dominant in cooling-dominant Sun Belt
  • IRA HOMES + HEER rebate status: Planning — IRA implementation plan filed with DOE, rebates pending state legislature

North Dakota licensing, rebates & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: North Dakota Secretary of State — Contractor License (official site)
  • Permit: permit required for attic insulation retrofit per North Dakota Building Code; ND Contractor License required statewide above $4,000 contract

How attic insulation upgrade costs vary in North Dakota

North Dakota's combination of 8,800 annual heating degree days, -45°F winter low temperatures across the entire state, and 60-inch frost depth drives the most heating-dominant insulation profile in the Lower 48 alongside Maine and Minnesota — R-49+ blown-fiberglass attic plus R-20 wall cavity insulation plus continuous polyiso exterior on retrofits is now the dominant North Dakota spec, with Bakken oil-patch fugitive dust contamination in the Williston, Tioga, and Watford City corridor measurably reducing attic insulation R-value over time and driving 12-15 year retrofit cycles versus 18-25 year national norms.

State-specific code or insurance rule: North Dakota Century Code §54-21.3 makes the North Dakota Department of Commerce Division of Community Services the statewide energy code authority — North Dakota operates under IECC 2018 with state-specific cold-climate amendments, and the North Dakota Department of Commerce Energy Programs Office is the planned IRA HOMES + HEER administrator with planned 2026 launch through Otter Tail Power and Montana-Dakota Utilities residential weatherization programs providing utility-funded insulation rebates statewide.
