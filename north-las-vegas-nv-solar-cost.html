Frequently asked solar questions

What is the difference between string inverters and microinverters?

String inverters are cheaper but the whole array drops output when one panel is shaded. Microinverters optimize each panel independently, cost 15-20% more, and last longer.

Should I buy or lease solar panels?

Buying maximizes long-term savings and increases home value. Leasing eliminates upfront cost but the leasing company keeps the tax credit, and panels may complicate home sales.

Does North Las Vegas have any state or local solar incentives?

Incentives vary. Check the DSIRE database for North Las Vegas-specific rebates, SRECs, property tax exemptions, and performance-based incentives that stack on top of the federal 30% ITC.

How does the federal solar tax credit work?

The Investment Tax Credit (ITC) lets you deduct 30% of your total solar installation cost from your federal taxes. It applies to purchased systems, not leased. There is no cap on the credit amount.

How much solar energy can I produce in North Las Vegas?

North Las Vegas is one of the best locations in the country for residential solar production. A standard 8kW system produces 12,000-14,000 kWh annually, 25-35% more than average U.S. markets. Most North Las Vegas homeowners can cover 80-100% of their electricity needs with a properly sized system. Plan for future consumption increases from EVs or pool equipment when determining system size.

What solar incentives are available in North Las Vegas?

Start with the 30% federal ITC, which applies to all North Las Vegas solar installations with no cap. Then check DSIRE for NV-level incentives: state tax credits, utility rebates, SREC markets, property tax exemptions, and net metering terms. Each utility has different programs and rates. The combined federal-plus-state incentive stack typically reduces net cost by 40-60% in markets with strong state programs.

When should I install solar in North Las Vegas?

The best installation window in North Las Vegas is fall or winter, cooler temperatures make attic work comfortable and your system starts producing before peak summer rates. Note that the process from quote to installation takes 6-12 weeks including permitting, utility approval, and scheduling. Start the process 2-3 months before your target installation date. The federal tax credit applies to the year of installation, so timing relative to tax year can matter.
