Concrete questions for Norwich homeowners

How thick should a concrete driveway be?

Residential driveways should be 4 inches thick for cars and 5-6 inches for heavy vehicles like RVs. A 6-inch compacted gravel base underneath is essential. Thickened edges (6 inches) prevent cracking at the perimeter.

Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Norwich?

In Norwich, permits are typically required for driveways, sidewalks, and any concrete work near property lines or public right-of-way. Backyard patios may be exempt depending on size. Check local requirements.

What is stamped concrete?

Stamped concrete uses patterned mats pressed into wet concrete to mimic brick, stone, slate, or wood. It costs $12-$25/sqft installed. It requires resealing every 2-3 years to maintain appearance and prevent wear.

What causes concrete to crack?

Common causes: shrinkage during curing, frost heave from inadequate base prep, tree roots, heavy vehicle loads beyond design, poor control joint placement, and water pooling due to improper grading.

About concrete work in Norwich, CT

Why pricing varies in Norwich. BLS wage data shows local labor costs running about 21% above the US median. This premium is the primary factor separating Norwich pricing from national benchmarks. Norwich's concrete work pricing sits approximately 6% above national norms based on our cost analysis.

Local conditions. Norwich has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Norwich for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Standard concrete in Norwich runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Norwich require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. Norwich's population of 40,347 supports a workable contractor market. For specialized trades, you may need to expand your search to neighboring cities for a third quote. Norwich's stable population means contractor capacity rarely tightens, so off-season pricing (typically Nov-Feb) can save 10-15% on non-emergency work.
