Questions Norwich homeowners ask about garage door

Can I insulate my existing garage door?

Yes. Retrofit insulation kits ($100-$200 DIY) add R-4 to R-8. However, adding weight to a non-insulated door may require spring adjustment. A new insulated door is often a better investment.

How much does a garage door opener cost?

A new opener installed costs $250-$600 for chain or belt drive. Smart openers with Wi-Fi and battery backup run $400-$800 installed. Replacing the opener alone takes 2-3 hours.

What is the difference between chain and belt drive openers?

Chain drive is cheaper ($150-$250) but louder. Belt drive ($200-$350) is whisper-quiet and ideal when bedrooms are above or adjacent to the garage. Both have similar reliability.

Should I repair or replace my garage door?

Repair if damage is limited to one or two panels and replacements are available. Replace if the door is over 15 years old, panels are no longer manufactured, or you want better insulation and security.

Does a new garage door increase home value?

A garage door replacement recovers 90-100% of its cost at resale, making it one of the highest-ROI home improvements. It also dramatically improves curb appeal.

What R-value garage door do I need in Norwich?

In Norwich, the minimum practical insulation for an attached garage door is R-12, with R-16 being the better choice for most homeowners. Workshop or heated-garage applications should go R-16 to R-18. That extra $200-$500 investment recovers through energy savings in adjacent living spaces within a few years and keeps the garage itself above freezing through winter.

For Norwich, target late spring or early fall for your garage door replacement. Moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing, and off-peak scheduling often means 5-10% savings on installation.
