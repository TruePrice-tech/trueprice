Concrete Driveway Cost in Ohio (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in Ohio typically run $3,400–$8,800 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 4000 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 32-inch design frost line, and the state sits in severe freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F2). Ohio's combination of 32-inch frost depth across the Western Reserve and Allegheny Plateau, F2 (severe) freeze-thaw exposure across the entire state, and Lake Erie lake-effect humidity along the Cleveland-Toledo corridor drives 6%+ air-entrainment plus Type II cement on every Ohio residential pour — and Ohio road de-icing salt exposure on every grade-level driveway edge from Ohio Department of Transportation snow operations plus winter ice-melt salt application on every residential driveway forces 0.40 maximum water-cement ratio on every Ohio residential pour.

Ohio climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 32-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F2 — severe freeze-thaw + de-icer exposure, 5-7% air-entrainment required
  • Typical residential mix: 4000 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: Low — sandy or stable subsoil, standard slab-on-grade design
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC A — no seismic design considerations
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: Very high — heavy de-icer use drives 0.40 max w/c + epoxy-coated rebar

Ohio licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide concrete license — Cleveland, Columbus, Cincinnati issue municipal contractor credentials (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential driveway pour > 200 sq ft; municipal contractor registration required in Cleveland, Cincinnati, Columbus on residential exterior contracting above $5,000

How concrete driveway costs vary in Ohio

State-specific code or insurance rule: Ohio Revised Code §4740 reserves general construction trade regulation to municipalities — Ohio has no statewide residential concrete contractor licensing, but Ohio Building Code §1706.4 separately requires Special Inspector continuous observation on every residential foundation pour above 1,500 sq ft, and Ohio Department of Transportation Specifications 499 enforces statewide aggregate-source certification on every concrete pour to prevent ASR (alkali-silica reaction) given northeastern Ohio's high-reactivity glacial-till aggregate deposits affecting the Cleveland-Akron-Youngstown corridor.
