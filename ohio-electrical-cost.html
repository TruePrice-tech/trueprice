Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in Ohio (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in Ohio typically run $2,200–$5,300 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2017 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Service entrance design assumes a 200A minimum on new residential construction. Ohio code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2017 NEC — older adoption cycle, local amendments common
  • State amendments: Ohio adopts 2017 NEC under OBOC residential code with Construction Industry Licensing Board issuing statewide Electrical Contractor credentials for projects above $25k
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: Moderate — typical interior continental flash density
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 18-to-24-inch design depth on buried service conduit

Ohio licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Ohio Construction Industry Licensing Board — Electrical Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: city or county electrical permit required for panel upgrade and service-line work

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in Ohio

Lake Erie shoreline counties from Lucas to Ashtabula face lake-effect winter freeze-thaw cycling that drives ground frost depth to 42 inches and shears buried service-entrance PVC conduit annually — and the Ohio River industrial corridor's high humidity and coal-fired plant downwind sulfate deposition shorten outdoor service-mast galvanic life on Cincinnati and Steubenville-area service drops.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Ohio Construction Industry Licensing Board issues the statewide Electrical Contractor credential only for projects with total value above $25,000 — single-family residential panel upgrades and rewires under that threshold proceed under municipal credentials only, with cities like Cincinnati, Columbus, and Cleveland running independent journeyman and master examination programs that don't reciprocate with the state credential.
