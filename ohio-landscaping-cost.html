Landscape Installation Cost in Ohio (2026)

Landscape installation in Ohio typically runs $4,000–$17,000 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 5b-6b. Ohio's combination of glacial-till loam soils across the Western Reserve and Allegheny Plateau, mature deciduous canopy across older Cleveland, Cincinnati, and Columbus suburbs, and 175-day growing season supports cool-season Kentucky bluegrass-perennial ryegrass blend turf as the dominant Ohio residential lawn — and Lake Erie lake-effect snow loads on Cleveland, Toledo, and Akron landscapes drive snow-shoveling-corridor design considerations on every front-yard install plus salt-tolerant plant selection on grade-level beds adjacent to ODOT snow-operations corridors.

Ohio climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 5b-6b — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 39 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 175 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Low — adequate residential precipitation, minimal drought stress
  • Irrigation dependency: Low — irrigation optional, established plants survive on rainfall
  • Frost-line trench depth: 32-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

Ohio licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide landscape license; Ohio Department of Agriculture Pesticide Regulation; Cleveland, Columbus, Cincinnati municipal contractor credentials (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; ODA Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care; municipal contractor registration required in Cleveland, Cincinnati, Columbus on residential exterior contracting above $5,000

How landscape installation costs vary in Ohio

Ohio's combination of glacial-till loam soils across the Western Reserve and Allegheny Plateau, mature deciduous canopy across older Cleveland, Cincinnati, and Columbus suburbs, and 175-day growing season supports cool-season Kentucky bluegrass-perennial ryegrass blend turf as the dominant Ohio residential lawn — and Lake Erie lake-effect snow loads on Cleveland, Toledo, and Akron landscapes drive snow-shoveling-corridor design considerations on every front-yard install plus salt-tolerant plant selection on grade-level beds adjacent to ODOT snow-operations corridors.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Ohio Revised Code §921 grants the Ohio Department of Agriculture exclusive preemption authority over residential and municipal pesticide ordinances — Ohio is one of the strongest pesticide-preemption states in the Midwest, blocking Cleveland, Columbus, Cincinnati, and Toledo from adopting local pesticide restrictions on residential landscape contractors, with ODA Pesticide Regulation Section managing the only operational regulatory channel statewide and ODA additionally maintaining the Ohio Sensitive-Crop Registry protecting beekeeping operations and certified-organic Amish farms in Wayne, Holmes, and Stark counties.
