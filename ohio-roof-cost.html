Roof Replacement Cost in Ohio (2026)

Roof replacement in Ohio typically runs $7,800–$21,000 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. Lake-effect ice damming on northern Ohio counties (Cuyahoga, Lake, Ashtabula) drives full ice-and-water-shield specs from eave to 24 inches inside the heated wall; freeze-thaw counts (60+/winter) put valley flashing and step-flashing at the top of the long-term cost-driver list.

Ohio climate & code drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Hail risk tier: Moderate — periodic severe hail

Ohio licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: No statewide residential roofing license — Cleveland, Cincinnati, Columbus require municipal registration; commercial roofing requires Ohio Construction Industry Licensing Board (official site)
  • Permit: municipal permit required

How roof replacement costs vary in Ohio

Lake-effect ice damming on northern Ohio counties (Cuyahoga, Lake, Ashtabula) drives full ice-and-water-shield specs from eave to 24 inches inside the heated wall; freeze-thaw counts (60+/winter) put valley flashing and step-flashing at the top of the long-term cost-driver list.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Ohio Revised Code §4722 (Home Construction Service Suppliers Act) requires written contracts on any home job over $25,000 and gives consumers a 3-day right of rescission on door-to-door storm-chaser pitches, with treble damages for violations.
