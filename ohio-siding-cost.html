Siding Replacement Cost in Ohio (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Ohio typically runs $9,500–$26,000, with vinyl panel the dominant residential cladding choice. Lake Erie shoreline counties from Lucas to Ashtabula face lake-effect winter freeze-thaw cycling that drives wood-cladding rot and rainscreen-gap requirements on every coastal re-side — and the Ohio River industrial corridor's high humidity and coal-fired plant downwind sulfate deposition shorten outdoor cladding galvanic fastener life on Cincinnati and Steubenville-area properties.

Ohio climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A
  • Dominant residential cladding: Vinyl panel — dominant low-budget retrofit choice in non-WUI, non-coastal corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate wind exposure — 110–140 mph design wind on coastal counties
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: No statewide WUI cladding mandate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: No salt-air corrosion exposure (interior continental)

Ohio licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: No statewide siding license — Cleveland, Columbus, Cincinnati issue local Home Improvement Contractor credentials (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for full re-side

How siding replacement costs vary in Ohio

State-specific code or insurance rule: Ohio has no statewide residential contractor licensing for siding, roofing, or general remodeling — Ohio Construction Industry Licensing Board regulates HVAC, electrical, plumbing, hydronics, and refrigeration statewide but exempts envelope and structural work — and Cleveland, Columbus, and Cincinnati each issue independent Home Improvement Contractor registrations that don't reciprocate, with rural unincorporated counties typically having no inspection program.
