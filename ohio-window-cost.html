Window Replacement Cost in Ohio (2026)

Window replacements in Ohio typically run $5,400–$14,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Northern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.3, no SHGC limit). Ohio's combination of 32-inch frost depth across the northern half (Lake Erie shoreline counties), F2 freeze-thaw cycling, persistent Lake Erie salt-spray-equivalent (winter de-icing salt + lake-effect humidity) on every Cuyahoga, Lorain, Erie, Lake, Sandusky, Ottawa, and Lucas county exposure, very-high pre-1978 housing share (60%+ across Cleveland, Cincinnati, Toledo) driving mandatory EPA RRP plus Cleveland Lead Safe Certification Ordinance enforcement, and Ohio Residential Code OAC 4101:8 mandatory IECC 2021 adoption drives moderate Great-Lakes-and-Cleveland-lead-safe-driven glazing selection.

Ohio climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Northern — coldest climates, U-factor priority (U ≤ 0.27 in coldest tier)
  • IECC climate zone: 5A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.3 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: No limit (cold climate)
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 110 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: High — multiple state-mapped historic districts with mandatory review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Very high — 60%+ pre-1978 housing share, mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every replacement

Ohio licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: No statewide license — municipal credentials only
  • License board: Ohio Construction Industry Examining Board licenses commercial trades only; residential window work falls under municipal Building Department (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification; municipal license required; OH Construction Industry Examining Board commercial license does not cover residential; replacement-only same-size installs typically permit-exempt; Cleveland Lead Safe Certification Ordinance separately enforces RRP-equivalent on every Cleveland residential window replacement

How window replacement costs vary in Ohio

Ohio's combination of 32-inch frost depth across the northern half (Lake Erie shoreline counties), F2 freeze-thaw cycling, persistent Lake Erie salt-spray-equivalent (winter de-icing salt + lake-effect humidity) on every Cuyahoga, Lorain, Erie, Lake, Sandusky, Ottawa, and Lucas county exposure, very-high pre-1978 housing share (60%+ across Cleveland, Cincinnati, Toledo) driving mandatory EPA RRP plus Cleveland Lead Safe Certification Ordinance enforcement, and Ohio Residential Code OAC 4101:8 mandatory IECC 2021 adoption drives moderate Great-Lakes-and-Cleveland-lead-safe-driven glazing selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Ohio is unusual in operating two parallel licensing schemes — Ohio Construction Industry Examining Board licenses only commercial trades (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, hydronics, and refrigeration), while residential window work falls entirely to municipal Building Departments without a statewide residential builder license, plus City of Cleveland Lead Safe Certification Ordinance (Cleveland Code §240) separately enforces RRP-equivalent lead-safe-certification mandate on every Cleveland residential window replacement in pre-1978 housing — making Cleveland one of the most aggressive municipal lead-safe enforcement programs in the country with very-high pre-1978 housing share statewide.
