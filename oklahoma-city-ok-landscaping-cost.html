What Oklahoma City residents ask about landscaping

Should I hire a landscaper or landscape architect?

A landscaper handles installation and maintenance. A landscape architect designs complex projects and manages grading, drainage, and permits. For projects over $15,000 or involving structural elements, an architect is worthwhile.

How much does lawn installation cost?

Sod costs $1-$2 per square foot installed. Hydroseeding costs $0.05-$0.10/sqft. Traditional seeding costs $0.10-$0.25/sqft but takes 6-8 weeks to establish. A 5,000 sqft lawn costs $5,000-$10,000 in sod.

How much does a paver patio cost?

Paver patios cost $12-$25 per square foot installed, depending on paver material and pattern complexity. A 300 sqft patio runs $3,600-$7,500. Permeable pavers cost 15-20% more but help with drainage.

Oklahoma City, OK: landscaping cost factors

Why pricing varies in Oklahoma City. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. For landscaping specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Oklahoma City at roughly 14% below the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Oklahoma City has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Oklahoma City's moderate climate supports a wide range of landscaping options. The mix of native perennials and structured plantings performs well with standard irrigation. Avoid high-water exotic species that struggle with summer humidity and winter dormancy transitions. A complete landscaping project in Oklahoma City typically breaks down as: plant material (30-40%), hardscape/materials (20-30%), labor (25-35%), and design (5-10%). For budget-conscious homeowners, phasing the project over 2-3 seasons lets plants establish naturally and reduces upfront costs.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most landscaping projects in Oklahoma City require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. As a major metro of 610,613 residents, the local contractor market is deep — expect 10+ legitimate bidders on any standard residential job. Oklahoma City has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.

Labor rates in Oklahoma City, OK

According to BLS data, construction laborers in the Oklahoma City, OK metro earn a median of $19.18/hr (13% below the national median of $22.08/hr).

Lower labor costs are the main reason landscaping quotes in Oklahoma City come in below the national average. Labor typically represents 40-60% of a landscaping quote, so even a few percentage points above or below the national median shows up in your bottom line.

Local factors: landscaping in Oklahoma City, OK

Home stock in Oklahoma City. With average home age near 37 years, Oklahoma City homes are at the typical first-replacement milestone for major systems. Original furnaces, AC units, water heaters, and roofs installed when the homes were built are aging out — landscaping is hitting peak demand in this age band.

HOA & compliance. HOA prevalence in Oklahoma City is moderate. If you're in a managed community, check covenants before signing a landscaping contract — material color, visible fixture type, or warranty requirements may affect your selection.

Local market conditions. Oklahoma City has moderate construction activity. Contractors are busy but accessible with 1-3 weeks' notice. Off-season quotes (contractor-dependent) can run 8-15% below peak rates. Oklahoma City's large market supports full-service landscape design firms, installation-only crews, and maintenance companies. For projects over $8,000, a dedicated design firm with installation capability typically provides better results than splitting design and installation across separate companies.

Seasonal timing. Oklahoma City offers a longer landscaping season than cold-climate markets. The busiest period is March through June when spring planting demand peaks. Fall (October-December) is an excellent time for plantings, hardscape projects, and irrigation system installation at lower competition and often better pricing.

What drives cost. With Oklahoma City labor rates near the national median, the cost difference between a budget and premium full yard makeover comes down to materials and scope rather than labor premiums. Oklahoma City's housing stock averages 37 years — the age where original installations start failing and code requirements have evolved. Most landscaping project quotes will include some code-catch-up items that newer homes wouldn't need.

Red flags to watch for. Check that any Oklahoma City contractor doing landscaping project carries both general liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers' compensation. Request certificates directly from the insurer, not just copies the contractor provides. In Oklahoma City, verify your landscaping project contractor pulls the permit themselves — never pull it in your own name. If they ask you to pull the permit, they may not be properly licensed to do the work.
