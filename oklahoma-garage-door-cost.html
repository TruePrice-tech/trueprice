Oklahoma climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 110 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Chain-drive — dominant on detached or budget installs, durable and economical
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): High — TPCT zone 2, PT wood jamb framing required
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Moderate — suburban HOA prevalence on newer subdivisions
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: None — no salt-air corrosion exposure
  • IECC climate zone: 3A-4A

Oklahoma licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Oklahoma Construction Industries Board — Roofing Contractor only (no garage-door specialty); Oklahoma Uniform Building Code Commission (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening; Oklahoma has no statewide garage-door contractor license; tornado-shelter rough-in within attached garage commonly required under municipal codes; Oklahoma Geological Survey expansive-clay disclosure recommended on Oklahoma City and Tulsa subdivisions

How garage door installation costs vary in Oklahoma

State-specific code or insurance rule: Oklahoma is unusual in licensing only Roofing Contractors at the state level (post the 2010 hail-fraud crisis) without a parallel residential garage-door contractor license — every Oklahoma garage-door contractor operates under municipal Building Department oversight only, leaving Oklahoma City, Tulsa, Norman, Edmond, and Lawton homeowners to rely on municipal license history rather than a state-board examination, plus Oklahoma Tornado Alley exposure drives mandatory FEMA P-320 storm-shelter rough-in detailing within attached garages on every residential foundation pour with Oklahoma City Tornado Shelter Initiative documenting the highest U.S. residential tornado-shelter penetration at 35% of new construction.
