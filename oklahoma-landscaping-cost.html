Landscape Installation Cost in Oklahoma (2026)

Landscape installation in Oklahoma typically runs $3,800–$16,400 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 6b-7b. Oklahoma's combination of Cross Timbers transitional substrates across Central Oklahoma, red-clay subsoil across the Anadarko Basin and Western Oklahoma, and 215-day growing season pushes Oklahoma residential landscape design toward warm-season Bermuda and zoysia turf with regular irrigation — and central-plains hailstorms with 2-inch+ diameter routinely shred ornamental flower beds and large-leaf shrubs across the entire state during the April-June severe weather season, plus Oklahoma red-clay dust contamination during high-wind spring events.

Oklahoma climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 6b-7b — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 36 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 215 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Moderate — periodic drought, advisory water-saving guidelines
  • Irrigation dependency: Moderate — periodic irrigation needed during dry season
  • Frost-line trench depth: 18-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

Oklahoma licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food & Forestry Consumer Protection Services — Pesticide Division (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; ODAFF Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care; Oklahoma City and Tulsa municipal contractor registration required for residential exterior contracting above $5,000

How landscape installation costs vary in Oklahoma

Oklahoma's combination of Cross Timbers transitional substrates across Central Oklahoma, red-clay subsoil across the Anadarko Basin and Western Oklahoma, and 215-day growing season pushes Oklahoma residential landscape design toward warm-season Bermuda and zoysia turf with regular irrigation — and central-plains hailstorms with 2-inch+ diameter routinely shred ornamental flower beds and large-leaf shrubs across the entire state during the April-June severe weather season, plus Oklahoma red-clay dust contamination during high-wind spring events.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Oklahoma 2 OS §3-81 grants the Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food & Forestry exclusive preemption authority over residential and municipal pesticide ordinances — Oklahoma is one of the strongest pesticide-preemption states in the Southern Plains, blocking Oklahoma City, Tulsa, and Norman from adopting local pesticide restrictions on residential landscape contractors, with ODAFF Pesticide Division managing the only operational regulatory channel statewide and ODAFF additionally maintaining the Oklahoma Sensitive-Crop Registry protecting commercial pecan, peach, and beekeeping operations adjacent to suburban subdivisions.
