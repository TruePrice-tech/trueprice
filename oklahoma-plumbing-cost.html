Plumbing & Repipe Cost in Oklahoma (2026)

Plumbing repipe in Oklahoma typically runs $4,300–$10,800 for a whole-house 2-bath PEX repipe, with PEX-A or PEX-B cross-linked polyethylene the dominant retrofit choice and the International Plumbing Code (IPC) as the adopted enforcement basis. Buried service-line trench depth must clear the 18-inch design frost line. Tornado Alley counties from Tillman through Tulsa carry annual EF-3+ tornado risk that snaps residential gas-meter risers and shears water-service connections at the foundation slab — Oklahoma residential plumbing increasingly specifies excess-flow gas valves and seismic-grade flexible water connectors after the 2013 Moore EF-5, and Arbuckle-Simpson aquifer hardness across south-central counties exceeds 25 grains-per-gallon.

Oklahoma water-quality & freeze drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 3A-4A — drives freeze-protection scope on supply lines
  • Frost-line trench depth: 18-inch design minimum for buried water service
  • Water hardness profile: Very hard (10.5+ grains/gallon) — whole-house softener standard on most repipes
  • Dominant retrofit supply material: PEX-A or PEX-B cross-linked polyethylene — dominant on whole-house repipes since 2005
  • Adopted plumbing code basis: International Plumbing Code (IPC) — ICC model adopted statewide
  • Lead service line risk tier: Moderate — partial pre-1986 service line inventory under utility review

Oklahoma licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Oklahoma Construction Industries Board — Plumbing Division (Plumbing Contractor / Journeyman / Apprentice) (official site)
  • Permit: city or CIB plumbing permit required; on-site sewage through Oklahoma DEQ

How plumbing and repipe costs vary in Oklahoma

Tornado Alley counties from Tillman through Tulsa carry annual EF-3+ tornado risk that snaps residential gas-meter risers and shears water-service connections at the foundation slab — Oklahoma residential plumbing increasingly specifies excess-flow gas valves and seismic-grade flexible water connectors after the 2013 Moore EF-5, and Arbuckle-Simpson aquifer hardness across south-central counties exceeds 25 grains-per-gallon.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Oklahoma 59 O.S. §1001 places plumbing licensure under the Construction Industries Board with combined oversight of plumbing, mechanical, electrical, and inspector trades — a four-trade unified-board structure unusual outside the Plains states — and CIB enforces a $50,000 contractor-bond requirement that is among the largest residential-trade bonds in the lower Plains.
