Roof Replacement Cost in Oklahoma (2026)

Roof replacement in Oklahoma typically runs $7,000–$18,500 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. Oklahoma City and Tulsa metros are at the heart of Tornado Alley and Hail Alley overlap — Class 4 impact-resistant shingles plus six-nail high-wind installation patterns are the dominant insurance-eligible upgrade combination.

Oklahoma climate & code drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 3A-4A
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Hail risk tier: Very high — central U.S. hail corridor, 8+ severe hail days/year
  • Ground snow load: 10 psf — light snow load
  • Dominant residential roof material: asphalt shingle

Oklahoma licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Oklahoma Construction Industries Board — Roofing Contractor Registration (Title 59 §1151.21) (official site)
  • Permit: city permit required

How roof replacement costs vary in Oklahoma

State-specific code or insurance rule: Oklahoma's Roofing Contractor Registration Act (Title 59 §1151.21–1151.31) mandates statewide registration with a $1,000 surety bond and prohibits insurance-deductible rebating — the Oklahoma Insurance Department actively enforces post-storm.
