Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in Oregon (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in Oregon typically run $2,700–$6,400 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2023 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Service entrance design assumes a 200A minimum on new residential construction. Cascadia subduction zone seismic exposure across the I-5 corridor from Portland to Eugene drives flexible-conduit and seismic-anchorage requirements on every meter base, panel, and load-side connection — and Pacific Northwest coastal salt-haze across Tillamook, Lincoln, and Coos counties corrodes outdoor service equipment within 7-10 years, mandating stainless mounting hardware and NEMA 4X meter sockets on shoreline upgrades.

Oregon code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2023 NEC — current cycle with expanded GFCI on dwelling outdoor and 250V circuits, EV charging requirements
  • State amendments: Oregon adopts 2023 NEC under OAR 918 with Building Codes Division enforcement and General Supervising Electrician (GS) / General Journeyman (J) / Limited Energy (LEA/LEB) tiered licensing
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: Low — Pacific coastal and high desert minimal flash density
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 18-to-24-inch design depth on buried service conduit

Oregon licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Oregon Building Codes Division — Electrical & Elevator Board (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal electrical permit required for panel upgrade and service-line work

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in Oregon

State-specific code or insurance rule: Oregon BCD issues seven distinct electrical licenses — General Supervising Electrician, General Journeyman, Limited Energy A (high-voltage signs), Limited Energy B (low-voltage), Limited Maintenance Electrician, Plant Electrician, and Restricted Energy Technician — making Oregon the most tiered-credential state in the country and forcing PV-plus-storage installers to often hold both the GS and LEB credentials simultaneously.
