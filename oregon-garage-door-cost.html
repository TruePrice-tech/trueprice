Garage Door Replacement Cost in Oregon (2026)

Garage door replacements in Oregon typically run $1,400–$5,000 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 110 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Oregon's combination of Cascadia subduction zone seismic exposure forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic-detailed garage-door header attachment on every coastal residential install from Astoria to Brookings, persistent Pacific salt-spray on every coastal exposure driving stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track replacement at 5-7 year intervals, Pacific Northwest year-round humidity driving rapid corrosion on standard galvanized springs at 5-7 year intervals, and Oregon Reach Code 2022 above-baseline R-value optional adoption drives unique seismic-and-coastal-driven material selection.

Oregon climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 110 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating at 110-140 mph design wind
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Moderate — periodic termite pressure, PT framing recommended
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Low — limited HOA garage-door restrictions outside metro subdivisions
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High — coastal salt-spray drives stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track requirement at 5-7 year intervals
  • IECC climate zone: 4C-5B

Oregon licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Oregon Construction Contractors Board (CCB) — Residential General Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening; CCB Residential General Contractor license required above $1,000 contract; replacement-only installs typically permit-exempt; Oregon Reach Code 2022 above-baseline R-value optional; Cascadia subduction zone seismic-detailed header attachment mandatory on every coastal residential install

How garage door installation costs vary in Oregon

State-specific code or insurance rule: Oregon Construction Contractors Board (CCB) §701.026 imposes one of the most aggressive residential builder licensing schemes in the country — every Oregon garage-door contractor exceeding the $1,000 contracting threshold must hold a Residential General Contractor license plus complete a 16-hour pre-licensure education program and pass a written examination, plus Oregon Department of Geology and Mineral Industries (DOGAMI) separately publishes Cascadia subduction zone seismic-design maps requiring ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic-detailed garage-door header attachment on every Oregon coastal residential garage-door installation from Astoria to Brookings.
