Gutter Installation Cost in Oregon (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Oregon typically runs $1,300–$4,900, with 6-inch oversized K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 31 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Oregon's 31-inch state-average annual rainfall masks dramatic east-west variation — coastal Tillamook, Lincoln, and Coos counties receive 90+ inches/year primarily as winter Pacific frontal rainfall driving 6-inch oversized K-style as standard residential profile, while eastern Oregon counties from Burns to Ontario receive under 12 inches/year with 5-inch K-style adequate for low-volume runoff.

Oregon rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 31 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 6-inch oversized K-style seamless aluminum — dominant in high-rainfall and steep-pitch corridors
  • Ground snow load: 20 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: Low — occasional freeze events, minimal ice-dam history
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: High — mature deciduous canopy drives gutter-guard demand and 2x annual cleaning frequency

Oregon licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Oregon Construction Contractors Board (CCB) — Residential General Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction

How gutter installation costs vary in Oregon

Oregon's 31-inch state-average annual rainfall masks dramatic east-west variation — coastal Tillamook, Lincoln, and Coos counties receive 90+ inches/year primarily as winter Pacific frontal rainfall driving 6-inch oversized K-style as standard residential profile, while eastern Oregon counties from Burns to Ontario receive under 12 inches/year with 5-inch K-style adequate for low-volume runoff.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Oregon Construction Contractors Board (CCB) requires every contractor performing residential exterior work above $1,000 to hold a CCB license — and unique to Oregon, the CCB administers a Residential Bond pool that compensates homeowners up to $20,000 per project for licensed-contractor breach, with mandatory continuing education on Oregon-specific Reach Code energy amendments and stormwater-management requirements on every renewal.
