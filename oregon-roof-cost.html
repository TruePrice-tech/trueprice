Roof Replacement Cost in Oregon (2026)

Roof replacement in Oregon typically runs $8,500–$21,500 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. The Willamette Valley's persistent winter rain and moss growth drive zinc and copper-strip ridge installations and frequent gentle pressure-washing schedules; coastal Lincoln, Tillamook, Clatsop counties see saltwater corrosion and 110-mph Pacific-storm wind exposure.

Oregon licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Oregon Construction Contractors Board — license required for any roofing work for compensation (official site)
  • Permit: city/county permit required

How roof replacement costs vary in Oregon

The Willamette Valley's persistent winter rain and moss growth drive zinc and copper-strip ridge installations and frequent gentle pressure-washing schedules; coastal Lincoln, Tillamook, Clatsop counties see saltwater corrosion and 110-mph Pacific-storm wind exposure.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Oregon Revised Statute §701 mandates CCB licensing on any structure-altering work — even unpaid roofing for a friend triggers liability if unlicensed; the CCB Recovery Fund covers up to $20,000 per residential consumer claim against a licensed contractor.
