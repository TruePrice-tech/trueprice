Siding Replacement Cost in Oregon (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Oregon typically runs $11,000–$29,000, with fiber-cement plank (Hardie or LP SmartSide-style) the dominant residential cladding choice. Pacific Northwest coastal salt-haze plus Olympic Peninsula sustained rainfall (Tillamook 90+ inches/year) corrode galvanized fasteners on shoreline re-sides within 7-10 years, mandating stainless on all coastal cedar and fiber-cement — and the 2020 Labor Day Oregon wildfire complex drove Class A ignition-resistant cladding adoption on Marion, Lane, and Jackson county WUI retrofits, with fiber-cement plank dominating cedar lap on rebuilds.

Oregon climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4C-5B-6B
  • Dominant residential cladding: Fiber-cement plank — dominant in WUI fire-zone and high-humidity Sun Belt
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate wind exposure — 110–140 mph design wind on coastal counties
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: Regional WUI overlay — ignition-resistant cladding required in mapped fire-hazard zones
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High-corrosion coastal — stainless or hot-dipped galvanized fasteners required within 3,000 ft of saltwater

Oregon licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Oregon Construction Contractors Board (CCB) — Residential General Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal building permit required for full re-side; Oregon WUI cladding standards on coastal and forested counties

How siding replacement costs vary in Oregon

State-specific code or insurance rule: Oregon Construction Contractors Board (CCB) requires every contractor performing residential exterior work above $1,000 to hold a CCB license — and unique to Oregon, the CCB administers a Residential Bond pool that compensates homeowners up to $20,000 per project for licensed-contractor breach, with mandatory continuing education on Oregon-specific energy code (Reach Code) and WUI cladding amendments on every renewal.
