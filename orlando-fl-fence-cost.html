Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a fence cost in Orlando?

Most homeowners in Orlando pay between $3,800 and $10,900 for fence installation, depending on material and yard size.

What is the cheapest fence material?

Chain link is the most affordable fencing option at $15-$25 per linear foot installed. Wood privacy fencing is the next most affordable at $25-$50 per linear foot.

How long does a wood fence last?

A well-maintained wood fence typically lasts 15-20 years. Regular staining and sealing every 2-3 years can significantly extend its lifespan. Cedar and redwood last longer than pine.

About fence installation in Orlando, FL

Why pricing varies in Orlando. Labor rates here run roughly 11% below the national average per BLS metro data, which is the main reason this city's pricing sits below the national midpoint. For fence installation specifically, Orlando sits within a few points of the national midpoint on our cost model.

Local conditions. Orlando has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Pressure-treated pine rated for ground contact (UC4A) is the local standard; cedar rots in 5-7 years here.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most fence installation projects in Orlando require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 255,483, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Orlando's rapid recent growth has tightened the contractor market — expect longer lead times and less negotiating room than smaller markets.
