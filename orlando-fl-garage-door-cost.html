R-Value for Orlando Conditions

Orlando averages zero freeze-thaw cycles, so insulation is not a cold-weather necessity. The critical specification is wind-load rating: every residential garage door must carry Florida Product Approval (FPA) certification, and Hurricane Ian in 2022 proved that the garage door is the single most vulnerable large opening in the building envelope.

R-8 polystyrene is adequate for temperature comfort in Orlando garages since the cooling load comes from radiant heat, not conductive loss. What matters far more is wind-load reinforcement: horizontal struts at 12-inch spacing and heavy-gauge vertical tracks rated for the Orlando wind zone. OUC and Duke Energy serve the metro but neither offers garage-door insulation rebates because the energy payback in a zero-freeze climate is negligible.

Florida Building Code does not mandate R-value on garage doors but Orange County's wind-zone compliance requires doors that incidentally carry thermal performance ratings. Orlando's July-September garage temperatures routinely exceed 135F on uninsulated panels, which destroys stored electronics and chemicals. Duke Energy Florida does not offer standalone garage-door insulation rebates as of 2026, but IRA 25C federal credits apply to qualifying insulated-door upgrades.
