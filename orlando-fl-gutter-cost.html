Common Gutter Scams in Orlando

Winter Park, Thornton Park, and College Park homeowners see these same four gutter-quote issues repeatedly.

Orlando-specific undersizing

A Orlando bid that specifies 5-inch gutters without reference to 50 inches of annual rainfall or the specific Winter Park, Thornton Park, and College Park roof area being drained is underthought. Any roof plane above 1,500 sq ft emptying to a single gutter run needs 6-inch in Orlando.

Pitch errors on Orlando installs

Correct slope for Orlando gutters: 1/4 inch per 10 feet of run toward each downspout. Flat sections pool water and overflow during storms. A five-minute post-install hose test catches the problem — any standing water means the hangers need adjusting.

Orlando discharge routing skipped

Gutters collect water; downspouts deliver it. Without splash blocks, extensions, or buried drainage, all that water concentrates at your foundation. Orlando's 50 inches of annual rain turns that into foundation cracks, basement seepage, and eroded landscaping. A Orlando bid silent on discharge routing is incomplete.

Seamed gutters sold as seamless

Real seamless means the installer rolls the full gutter run on your Orlando property from continuous coil stock; seams appear only at corners, outlets, and end caps. A "seamless" Orlando bid that mentions joining factory-cut sections with splice connectors is sectional gutter at seamless pricing — the single most common install-side scam.
