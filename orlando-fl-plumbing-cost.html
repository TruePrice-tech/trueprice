Orlando plumbing FAQ

Do I need a plumbing permit in Orlando?

Most Orlando jurisdictions require permits for new lines, water heater installs, and sewer work. Minor repairs like faucet swaps and toilet replacements typically do not need permits.

How long do plumbing fixtures typically last?

Faucets last 15-20 years, toilets 25-50 years, water heaters 8-12 years (tank) or 15-20 years (tankless), and supply lines should be replaced every 20-25 years.

What is the difference between copper and PEX piping?

Copper lasts 50+ years, resists bacteria, and has proven durability. PEX costs 60% less, installs faster, resists freezing better, and requires fewer joints. Both are code-compliant.

Freeze Risk and Winterization in Orlando

Orlando essentially does not freeze, making pipe winterization a non-issue for the vast majority of homes. The rare hard-freeze event every 10-15 years catches homeowners off guard because no freeze protection exists in standard Florida construction. The January 2010 and December 2022 cold snaps each produced scattered burst-pipe reports in exposed garage plumbing and outdoor irrigation lines. Frost-free hose bibs are essentially absent from Orlando homes. The zero-freeze-thaw-cycle climate means pipe insulation is driven by energy efficiency rather than freeze protection.
