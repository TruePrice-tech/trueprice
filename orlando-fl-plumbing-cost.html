Plumbing in Orlando: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Orlando has moderately hard water which can reduce water heater lifespan over time. Consider a water softener or tankless unit with a descaling system to protect your investment.

❄

Freeze risk

Orlando rarely freezes, but when it does, exposed pipes are especially vulnerable because homes here aren't built for cold weather. Insulate outdoor pipes and know your shutoff valve location just in case.

🔧

Material recommendation

PEX piping is the most popular repipe material in Orlando. It resists mineral scale buildup better than copper in hard-water areas and typically costs 30-40% less to install.

💡

Local tip

Homes built in Orlando during the 1970s-80s often have copper pipes that may be developing pinhole leaks after 40+ years. If you're seeing green staining at joints, it's time for an inspection.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Orlando?

Plumber rates in Orlando typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
