Orlando solar FAQ

What happens to solar panels during a power outage?

Grid-tied systems without batteries shut off during outages for safety (anti-islanding). A battery backup or hybrid inverter lets you use solar power during grid failures.

How long do solar panels last?

Modern panels are warrantied for 25-30 years and continue producing at reduced efficiency beyond that. Inverters last 10-15 years (string) or 25 years (microinverters) and are the main replacement cost.

Can I add a battery to my solar system?

Yes. Adding a home battery (10-13 kWh) costs $8,000-$15,000 installed. Batteries provide backup power during outages and can help avoid peak-rate charges in time-of-use markets.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

Orlando Sun Exposure and Seasonal Production

Orlando averages 4.8 peak sun hours daily with June production at 6.4 kWh per kW per day and December at 3.5. June-September afternoon thunderstorms arrive between 2pm and 5pm like clockwork and clip 10-15 percent off theoretical summer production versus cloud-free modeling. OUC territory (city proper) and Duke Energy Florida territory (unincorporated Orange County) split the metro, and confirming which utility serves a property is the first step in any solar quote. Winter Park's heavy oak canopy reduces production 15-25 percent versus open-sky lots in Lake Nona and Dr. Phillips.

Orlando Utility Rate and Net Metering Structure

Florida's net metering rules under Florida Statute 366.91 provide 1-for-1 retail-rate credits for exported energy, one of the most favorable structures in the Southeast. OUC and Duke Energy Florida both honor the 1-for-1 retail-rate structure. OUC operates its own solar programs separate from Duke Energy, including the OUC Reliable community solar option that offers virtual subscription credits. Duke Energy Florida customers fall under PSC Order PSC-2021-0015. Florida has no state income tax, which means the federal ITC is the sole tax credit. No state solar tax credit exists.

Orlando Interconnection and Grid Approval

OUC interconnection for residential systems runs 4-8 weeks through the OUC Solar Online portal. Duke Energy Florida interconnection runs 6-10 weeks through the Duke DG portal. City of Orlando Permitting Services Division issues solar permits with 1-2 week turnaround for standard residential installs. Winter Park operates a separate building department. Hurricane Ian in September 2022 disrupted interconnection queues across Central Florida for months. OUC's smaller service territory generally clears interconnection faster than Duke Energy's larger queue.

The Orlando installer market is led by Sunrun, Freedom Forever, Sunnova, and Tesla Energy on the lease and PPA side, with SunPower dealers and local shops on the purchase side. Orlando Solar Incentives and Tax Credits

The 30 percent federal ITC (Section 25D) is the anchor incentive. Florida exempts solar equipment from state sales tax through 2037 under Florida Statute 212.08(7). Florida's property tax exemption for solar improvements (FS 193.624) prevents reassessment on the added home value through 2037. OUC and Duke Energy Florida have phased out direct solar rebates but OUC Reliable community solar provides a subscription alternative. No state solar tax credit exists. Federal ITC alone reduces a $22,000 system to approximately $15,400 effective cost. Florida Product Approval certification is required for all mounting hardware sold in the state.

Orlando Roof Types and Mounting Implications

Orlando rooftop stock is heavily concrete tile (flat and S-tile) on post-1985 CBS block construction across Dr. Phillips, Lake Nona, and Windermere, adding 15-25 percent install cost because of specialty tile-hook mounting hardware. Composition shingle appears on 1970s-80s College Park and Maitland homes at lower install cost. Metal standing-seam roofs on newer construction accept solar with S-5 clamp systems that require no penetrations. Winter Park brick-street district homes often have complex hip-roof geometry that limits solar-viable roof area. Florida Product Approval is required for all racking sold in the state.

Orlando HOA Rules and Solar Access Law

Florida Statute 163.04 is among the strongest solar access laws in the country, prohibiting HOAs and deed restrictions from preventing solar installations. HOAs can impose reasonable restrictions that do not increase cost by more than $2,000 or reduce output by more than 10 percent. Master-planned communities in Lake Nona, Windermere, and Celebration have architectural review committees that must comply with FS 163.04 but can set placement and aesthetic rules within the statutory limits. Winter Park Historic Preservation Board may review visible installations on contributing structures.

Orlando Solar Permitting Process

City of Orlando Permitting Services Division uses expedited processing for residential solar under 10 kW, clearing in 1-2 weeks. Orange County Building Division handles unincorporated-area permits. Winter Park and Kissimmee have separate portals. Florida's statewide SolarAPP+ adoption streamlines automated approvals for qualifying systems. The Florida Building Code 7th Edition governs structural and electrical requirements. Florida Product Approval (FL-number) is required for all mounting hardware, which eliminates non-approved components that might be acceptable in non-Florida markets.

Orlando Battery Storage Options and Rebates

Battery storage adoption in Orlando is strongly driven by hurricane-season outage resilience. Hurricane Ian in 2022 and Hurricane Irma in 2017 both caused extended multi-day outages across Central Florida, and the resulting solar-plus-storage demand surge continues. Tesla Powerwall 3 and Enphase IQ Battery 10 dominate the market. Federal ITC at 30 percent applies to battery. Florida has no SGIP-equivalent state battery rebate. OUC and Duke Energy Florida do not offer battery-specific incentives. The insurance value of avoiding spoiled food and temporary housing during multi-day outages drives purchasing decisions.

Orlando Climate Risks for Solar Installations

Orlando climate risks for solar are hurricane wind uplift (ASCE 7-16 design wind of 150 mph for most of Orange County), summer afternoon lightning (Central Florida is the lightning capital of the US), and occasional hail from severe thunderstorms. Florida Building Code requires Miami-Dade NOA or Florida Product Approval for all mounting hardware. Module grounding and surge protection are critical given the lightning exposure. Hurricane Ian produced documented racking failures where wind speeds exceeded design loads. Salt spray is not a factor for inland Orlando unlike coastal Florida markets.
