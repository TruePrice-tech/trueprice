Orlando window replacement: answers to common questions

How do I know my windows need replacing?

Replace when you see fog between panes (failed seal), feel drafts with windows closed, frames are rotting or warping, locks no longer engage, or energy bills are rising with no other explanation.

What is the difference between double-pane and triple-pane windows?

Triple-pane windows add 15-25% better insulation over double-pane but cost 25-35% more. They pay back faster in extreme climates (very cold winters or very hot summers).

What is argon gas fill in windows?

Argon is an inert gas sealed between panes that insulates better than air. It improves R-value by 15-20% and is standard on most quality double and triple-pane windows. It slowly leaks over 15-20 years.

Should I replace all windows at once?

Replacing all windows at once saves 10-15% on labor versus doing them individually. It also ensures uniform appearance and consistent energy performance throughout the home.

Do replacement windows increase home value?

New windows recover 65-75% of their cost at resale. They also improve curb appeal, reduce noise, and eliminate a common inspection concern for buyers.

Energy performance spec for Orlando

Orlando is IECC zone 2A, a cooling-dominant subtropical climate where air conditioning drives 75-85% of annual residential energy use and SHGC is the critical glazing metric. OUC (Orlando Utilities Commission) serves the city core while Duke Energy Florida covers most of the surrounding metro. ENERGY STAR Southern-zone targets set U-factor 0.40 and SHGC 0.25 maximum, but Orlando's latitude and solar intensity mean SHGC below 0.22 on west and south elevations delivers measurably better cooling-bill reduction. OUC's residential rebate program stacks with the federal 25C credit for qualified ENERGY STAR windows. Spectrally-selective Low-E 366 coatings reject 95% of infrared solar energy while transmitting visible daylight, and on a typical 2,000 sq ft Orlando home replacing 15 windows, that coating change alone reduces annual cooling use by 1,500-2,400 kWh. Orlando has zero freeze days in a normal year, so U-factor is secondary to SHGC for energy ROI.

Orlando cooling economics on the I-4 corridor reward aggressive SHGC reduction because subtropical humidity drives air-conditioning loads 10 months of the year. Duke Energy Florida's Home Energy Improvement Program returns roughly $35-$65 per qualified ENERGY STAR Southern-zone window, and Orlando Utilities Commission residential customers can stack OUC-specific rebates on top. The Walt Disney World and Universal Studios employment corridors in Dr. Phillips and International Drive create a unique housing market where short-term vacation rentals require impact-rated windows for both insurance compliance and tenant-turnover durability that standard residential spec does not address.

Orlando condensation and interior moisture management

Orlando condensation management addresses subtropical humidity with summer air-conditioning condensation as the primary concern. Interior condensation on cool air-conditioned window glass during 90%+ humidity days along the Butler Chain of Lakes can drip onto sills and damage surrounding trim within a single cooling season. Orlando-specific window fraud patterns

Orlando's most persistent window fraud pattern involves contractors installing products without valid Florida Product Approval. The FPA number is printed on the window frame and is verifiable at floridabuilding.org in two minutes. If the installed product lacks an FPA number, it cannot legally pass final inspection in any Florida jurisdiction, and the homeowner bears the cost of re-replacement. The second Orlando-specific scam surfaces after hurricane events: out-of-state crews arrive within 48 hours, canvass damaged neighborhoods, collect deposits for "impact-rated" replacement, and install non-impact standard glass. Verify DBPR license status on the day of contract signing at myfloridalicense.com, confirm the specific FPA number of the proposed product before signing, and never pay more than 10% deposit on a Florida window contract (Florida Statute 489.126 caps pre-work deposits).

Orlando window scam patterns and contractor verification

Orlando window scam patterns: storm-chase out-of-state contractors after hurricane near-misses along the I-4 corridor, Assignment of Benefits (AOB) insurance-fraud demands on wind-damage claims (Florida Statute §627.7152 restricts but does not eliminate AOB abuse), "Florida Product Approval" claims with products lacking actual FPA documentation, and "impact-rated" claims on windows that fail large-missile testing under ASTM E1886/E1996. Legitimate Orlando installers provide written Florida DBPR Certified General Contractor or specialty C-22 license number (verifiable at myfloridalicense.com), FPA documentation verifiable on the product, Orange County business tax receipt, and physically inspectable manufacturer labels on frames before final inspection.
