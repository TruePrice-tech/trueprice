What the parts types mean
Aftermarket — third-party brands like Bosch, Denso, or Spectra that fit your vehicle but are not made by the original supplier. Cheapest option, often perfectly fine for non-critical parts.
OEM — the same physical part the factory installed, made by the original equipment manufacturer (often Bosch, Denso, etc.) but sold in plain packaging. Mid-priced.
Dealer / Genuine — the identical OEM part in the automaker's branded box (e.g. Toyota-branded, BMW-branded). Same part as OEM, 30-60% premium for the brand. Required by some warranties.
Remanufactured — factory-rebuilt cores (alternators, starters, calipers, etc.). Cheapest option, usually carries a warranty. Not available for all parts.
