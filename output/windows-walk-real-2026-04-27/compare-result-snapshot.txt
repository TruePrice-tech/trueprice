How to compare window quotes correctly

Window quotes vary by frame material, glass package, and install method — not just brand. A vinyl insert install from one company and a full-frame fiberglass install from another aren't the same job, even for the same window count.

Low-E coatings, argon or krypton fill, and triple-pane glass all raise price but pay back in comfort and energy bills. Pay attention to U-factor and SHGC numbers — these are the real efficiency metrics, not marketing stars.

Full-frame replacement costs more than insert (pocket) replacement but addresses rot and air sealing. Capping, wrap, and interior trim work should be spelled out, along with warranty on frame, glass seals, and labor.

Most important fields to compare
Window count & sizes
Brand & frame material (vinyl/fiberglass/wood)
U-factor & SHGC ratings
Glass package (Low-E, argon, triple-pane)
Install method (full-frame vs insert)
Exterior capping & wrap
Interior trim work
Frame warranty
Glass seal warranty
