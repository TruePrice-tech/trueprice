What Overland Park residents ask about garage door

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Overland Park?

Garage door replacement in Overland Park typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

What causes a garage door to be noisy?

Common causes: worn rollers (replace nylon for steel), dry hinges (lubricate with silicone spray), loose hardware, a chain-drive opener, or worn spring coils. A tune-up costs $100-$200.

What is the best type of garage door?

Insulated steel with polyurethane foam offers the best balance of durability, energy efficiency, and cost. R-value of 12-18 keeps the garage 10-20 degrees closer to house temperature.

How often should I maintain my garage door in Overland Park?

Basic maintenance twice a year keeps your Overland Park garage door running safely. Apply silicone spray to all moving parts, test the auto-reverse safety feature with a board under the door, and inspect weatherstrip seals for damage. Vibration from daily use loosens hardware over time, so tighten bolts and brackets as part of the routine. Professional servicing every 2-3 years ($100-$200) catches spring fatigue and roller wear early.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Overland Park?

If the issue is a single spring, one panel, or the opener, repair is usually the right call. Replace when the door is past 15 years, parts are discontinued, or you want modern features like insulation or smart-home connectivity. Garage door replacement is among the highest-ROI home improvements at 90-100% cost recovery at sale.

When is the best time to replace a garage door in Overland Park?

Schedule your Overland Park garage door replacement for spring or fall, moderate temperatures and lower demand. Off-peak timing can save 5-10% on installation costs and gets you a wider selection of available styles and colors. Spring is the busiest season for garage door companies since broken springs peak after winter cold stress.
