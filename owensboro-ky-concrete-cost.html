Owensboro concrete FAQ

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

How much does a concrete foundation cost?

A full basement foundation costs $15,000-$30,000. A slab foundation for a house costs $5,000-$15,000. A garage slab costs $2,500-$6,000. Price depends on size, depth, and soil conditions.

How long before I can drive on new concrete?

Wait 24-48 hours for foot traffic, 7 days for vehicle traffic, and 30 days for full cure. Avoid deicing salt for the first winter. New concrete reaches 90% strength in 7 days and full strength in 28 days.

What is the best time to pour concrete?

Pour when temperatures stay between 50-80F for at least 3 days. Spring and fall are ideal. Avoid pouring in rain, direct hot sun, or below freezing. Cold and heat both compromise cure quality.

What warranty should I expect?

Expect a minimum 1-year workmanship warranty from the contractor plus manufacturer warranties on materials. Get warranty terms in writing before signing.

Should I get multiple quotes?

Always get at least 3 written quotes. Compare line-item details, not just total price. The lowest quote often omits scope items that appear as change orders later.

Check licensing through your state's contractor board, verify insurance, read reviews from Owensboro homeowners, and get at least 3 written quotes with line-item detail.

Never pay more than 10-15% as a deposit before work begins. Standard payment structure: deposit, progress payment when materials arrive, final payment on completion.

About concrete work in Owensboro, KY

Where Owensboro's pricing comes from. Contractor labor rates in Owensboro are roughly average for the US. The real pricing variation between quotes here comes down to scope interpretation, material tiers, and contractor overhead. On concrete work, Owensboro comes in about 5% under the national average based on our metro pricing data.

Local conditions in Owensboro. Owensboro has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Owensboro's freeze-thaw cycles and moderate rainfall both affect concrete performance. Air-entrained mix is standard for outdoor flatwork, and expansion joints every 10-12 feet prevent the cracking that temperature swings cause. Proper drainage away from slabs prevents the water infiltration that freeze-thaw cycles exploit. Older properties in Owensboro (averaging 40 years) often have settling concrete that affects drainage. Re-pouring may require sub-grade correction (compaction, drainage tile) that new construction doesn't need. Budget 10-20% extra for site preparation on established properties.

Owensboro permits and code requirements. Permits are usually required for concrete work in Owensboro; verify the specifics with the local building department before committing.

The Owensboro contractor pool. At 58,416 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. The Owensboro market has grown at a steady pace, producing a solid base of experienced contractors without the severe backlogs of booming cities.
