House Painting Cost Guide

By Geoff Lane, founder of Woogoro · Updated April 2026

A fresh coat of paint is one of the most cost-effective ways to transform a home, inside or out. But painting costs can range dramatically depending on the scope, surface condition, paint quality, and labor market in your area. This 2026 guide covers average prices for both exterior and interior painting, explains where your money goes, and gives you proven strategies for getting the best value.

Cost Breakdown: Where Your Money Goes

Painting is one of the most labor-intensive home services. A low bid usually means the crew is skipping prep work, using fewer coats, or using cheap paint. All three shortcuts will show within a year or two.

Premium paints like Benjamin Moore Regal, Sherwin-Williams Duration, and PPG Timeless cost more per gallon but cover better, last longer, and often require fewer coats. Over a 15-year cycle, premium paint can actually cost less than repainting with builder-grade every 4–5 years.

Factors That Affect Your Cost

  • Surface condition. Clean, smooth surfaces need minimal prep. Peeling paint, chalking, wood rot, or mildew require extensive scraping, sanding, patching, and priming, which can double the labor hours.
  • Number of coats. Two coats is the standard for a quality job. Going from one coat to two adds roughly 40–50% more paint but only 20–30% more labor since prep and cleanup stay the same.
  • Height and access. Two-story and three-story homes require ladders, scaffolding, or lifts. This adds $500–$2,000+ in equipment costs and slows the crew down significantly.
  • Trim, doors, and detail work. Painting trim, shutters, doors, and window sashes by hand is time-consuming. A quote that includes detailed trim work will be noticeably higher than walls-only pricing.
  • Color change. Shifting from a dark color to a light one (or vice versa) may require an extra coat of tinted primer, adding 10–15% to the project cost.
  • Region. Labor rates in the Northeast and West Coast are 15–25% above national averages. Southern and Midwestern markets tend to be at or below baseline.

How to Save Money on House Painting

Saving on painting means maximizing the longevity of every dollar spent, not just getting the cheapest bid.

  • Get at least three quotes. Compare scope carefully. Make sure each bid specifies the number of coats, paint brand and product line, and exactly which surfaces are included.
  • Do your own prep. If you have the time and ability, handling pressure washing, furniture moving, and basic taping yourself can reduce the bill by 10–20%.
  • Bundle interior and exterior. Many painters offer a discount of 5–10% when you combine interior and exterior work in a single project.
  • Choose the right paint quality for your situation. Mid-range paint is the sweet spot for most homeowners. Premium paint makes sense for harsh climates or if you plan to stay in the home long term.
  • Schedule in the off-season. Late fall and early spring are slower periods for painters in most markets. You may save 5–15% by being flexible on timing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to paint the exterior of a house in 2026?

For a typical 2,000-square-foot home, exterior painting costs $4,500–$9,000 with standard prep and two coats. Homes with extensive peeling, wood rot, or three-story sections can run $7,000–$14,000 or more.

How often should you repaint a house?

Exterior paint typically lasts 7–10 years with mid-range products and 10–15 years with premium paint. Interior paint lasts 5–10 years in most rooms. High-traffic areas like hallways and kitchens may need repainting sooner.

Is it worth paying more for premium paint?

In most cases, yes. Premium paint covers better (often needing fewer coats), resists fading and chalking longer, and is easier to clean. Over a 15-year period, the higher upfront cost is often offset by not needing to repaint as soon.

Should I paint or replace my siding?

If your siding is structurally sound with no major rot or warping, painting is far more cost-effective. Painting costs roughly one-fifth to one-third of siding replacement. If more than 20–30% of the siding is damaged, replacement is usually the better long-term investment.

How long does a house painting project take?

A full exterior paint job on a standard home takes 3–5 days for a crew of 2–3 painters. Whole-house interior painting takes 3–7 days depending on the number of rooms and level of detail. Weather delays can extend exterior timelines.

Does painting increase home value?

Fresh exterior paint is one of the highest-ROI improvements you can make. Industry data suggests it recoups 60–100% of cost at resale, primarily by boosting curb appeal. Neutral interior colors can also help a home sell faster.
