Interior painting cost

Interior painting averages $3–$8 per square foot of floor area for full coverage (walls, ceilings, trim, doors). For an average 1,500 sq ft home, that's $4,500–$12,000 total. Walls-only painting (no ceilings or trim) cuts cost roughly in half.

Per-room: a standard 12x12 bedroom runs $400–$1,000 painted top-to-bottom. Bathrooms: $300–$700. Kitchens (without cabinets): $500–$1,500. Living rooms/great rooms: $600–$1,800.

Exterior painting cost

Exterior house painting runs $3–$8 per square foot of siding area for a quality 2-coat job. A typical 1,500 sq ft single-story home runs $4,500–$12,000. Two-story homes cost 30–50% more due to ladder/scaffold work.

Wood and stucco surfaces require more prep (sanding, caulking, primer) than vinyl or fiber cement. Expect $1–$3/sq ft additional for heavy prep on a peeling or weather-damaged exterior.

Cabinet painting cost (kitchen refresh)

Painting kitchen cabinets professionally costs $1,500–$4,500 for a typical 10x10 kitchen (about 30 cabinet doors and 10 drawer fronts). This is the highest-ROI kitchen update under $5,000 — 70–90% of buyers respond positively to fresh-painted cabinets.

DIY cabinet painting saves $1,000+ but takes 30–60 hours and requires careful prep (degreasing, sanding, primer, 2–3 finish coats). Brushed finish vs sprayed finish makes a noticeable quality difference.

House painting cost factors

Square footage

Bigger projects have lower per-sq-ft cost (mobilization spread). Smallest jobs (single room) often have $300–$500 minimum.

Number of coats

Most quotes assume 2 coats. 1 coat saves ~30% but rarely covers cleanly. 3 coats add 20–30% (often required for dramatic color changes).

Prep work needed

Heavy scraping, sanding, caulking, or repair adds $1–$3 per sq ft. Lead paint testing/abatement on pre-1978 homes adds significant cost.

Paint quality

Builder-grade: $25–$45/gallon. Mid-grade (Sherwin-Williams ProClassic, Benjamin Moore Regal): $50–$70/gallon. Premium (Aura, Emerald): $80–$120/gallon. Better paint covers in fewer coats and lasts longer.

Trim, doors, windows

Add $5–$15 per linear foot of trim. Doors $75–$200 each. Windows $50–$150 per frame.

Wall texture / repair

Patching nail holes, drywall damage, or removing wallpaper adds $0.50–$3/sq ft.

Frequently asked questions about house painting cost

How much does it cost to paint a house interior?
Full interior painting (walls, ceilings, trim) for a 1,500 sq ft home costs $4,500–$12,000. Walls-only is $1,500–$4,500. Per room: $400–$1,500 depending on size and scope.
How much does exterior house painting cost?
Exterior painting for a typical 1,500 sq ft single-story home runs $4,500–$12,000 for a 2-coat job. Two-story homes add 30–50% due to ladder/scaffold work. Heavy prep (peeling, weathered surfaces) adds $1–$3/sq ft.
How much paint do I need?
1 gallon covers ~350–400 sq ft per coat on smooth surfaces, ~300 sq ft on textured surfaces. For a 12x12 room, you'll need 1–1.5 gallons for 2 coats of walls only. Always buy 10% extra for touch-ups.
Should I paint cabinets or replace them?
Painting kitchen cabinets ($1,500–$4,500) refreshes the look at 10–20% the cost of replacement ($8,000–$25,000). Painting works well if cabinet boxes are still solid. Replace if cabinet boxes are damaged, swollen, or particle board.
How long does interior painting take?
A single room: 1–2 days for a 2-person crew. Whole-house interior: 4–10 days. Crews typically paint while you live in the home, prepping and finishing 1–2 rooms per day.
