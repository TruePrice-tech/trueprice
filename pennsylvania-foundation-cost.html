Foundation Cost in Pennsylvania (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in Pennsylvania typically run $18,400–$42,800 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with full basement pour as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 36-inch design frost line. Pennsylvania's combination of statewide radon zone 1 designation across all 67 counties (PA has the second-highest residential radon concentrations in the country after Iowa) driving mandatory sub-slab depressurization rough-in pipe specification on every PA residential foundation pour, 36-inch frost depth across the northern half, F2 freeze-thaw exposure across the entire state, Marcellus shale legacy abandoned-mine subsidence risk across Westmoreland, Allegheny, Washington, Greene, and Fayette counties driving abandoned-mine subsidence disclosure under PA §52-3201, and Allegheny County mandatory passive radon mitigation makes PA one of the most radon-driven foundation regulatory environments in the U.S.

Pennsylvania climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 36-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Full basement — dominant in Upper Midwest, Northeast, and Mid-Atlantic on freeze-thaw + radon-zone-1 sites
  • Expansive soil risk: Low — sandy or stable subsoil, standard slab-on-grade design
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC B — minor seismic risk, standard tie-down requirements
  • Hurricane wind tier: Low — sub-110 mph design wind, minimal tie-down detailing
  • Water table risk: Moderate — water table below frost line in most lots
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 1 (highest) — passive sub-slab depressurization rough-in required on every basement and slab pour

Pennsylvania licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Pennsylvania Office of Attorney General — Home Improvement Contractor (HICPA) Registration (official site)
  • Permit: city or borough building permit required for residential foundation pour; HICPA registration required above $5,000 home-improvement contract; PA Construction Code Act (Act 45) mandatory IRC + IECC adoption; statewide radon zone 1 designation drives sub-slab depressurization rough-in

How foundation pour costs vary in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania's combination of statewide radon zone 1 designation across all 67 counties (PA has the second-highest residential radon concentrations in the country after Iowa) driving mandatory sub-slab depressurization rough-in pipe specification on every PA residential foundation pour, 36-inch frost depth across the northern half, F2 freeze-thaw exposure across the entire state, Marcellus shale legacy abandoned-mine subsidence risk across Westmoreland, Allegheny, Washington, Greene, and Fayette counties driving abandoned-mine subsidence disclosure under PA §52-3201, and Allegheny County mandatory passive radon mitigation makes PA one of the most radon-driven foundation regulatory environments in the U.S.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Pennsylvania Home Improvement Consumer Protection Act (HICPA, 73 P.S. §517.1) requires every Pennsylvania foundation contractor performing home-improvement work above $5,000 to register with the PA Office of Attorney General — making PA one of the most aggressive consumer-protection registration regimes in the country, with HICPA-violation civil penalties up to $1,000 per violation plus mandatory contractor disclosure of any criminal conviction or other-state disciplinary action against the contractor, plus PA Construction Code Act (Act 45 of 1999) separately mandates IRC + IECC 2018 adoption with PA-specific amendments allowing optional 2018-or-later adoption by individual municipalities, and PA Department of Environmental Protection statewide radon zone 1 designation drives sub-slab depressurization rough-in.
