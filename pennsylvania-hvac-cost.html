HVAC Replacement Cost in Pennsylvania (2026)

HVAC replacement in Pennsylvania typically runs $8,500–$19,500 for a complete 3-ton system replacement, with natural-gas furnaces with central AC as the dominant heating choice and roughly 5,700 heating degree days against 1,100 cooling degree days driving the seasonal load mix. Eastern Pennsylvania (Philadelphia, Lehigh Valley, Pocono) retains a sizable fuel-oil installed base — over 20% of homes — while Western PA (Pittsburgh, Erie) is overwhelmingly natural gas via Marcellus Shale supply; PUC Act 129 EE programs through PECO, PPL, and Duquesne Light fund $400-$1,200 HP rebates that target the oil-dependent eastern half disproportionately.

Pennsylvania climate & load drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A-6A
  • Annual load split: Heating-dominant — annual heating load exceeds cooling load (5,700 HDD / 1,100 CDD)
  • Dominant heating fuel: Natural gas — piped utility supply through state pipeline network
  • Dominant cooling system: Central air conditioning paired with separate heating equipment

Pennsylvania licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Pennsylvania Home Improvement Consumer Protection Act (HICPA) registration; municipal HVAC license in Philadelphia, Pittsburgh, Allegheny County (official site)
  • Permit: municipal mechanical permit required

How HVAC replacement costs vary in Pennsylvania

State-specific code or insurance rule: Pennsylvania has no statewide HVAC contractor license — instead, the Home Improvement Consumer Protection Act (HICPA, 2009) requires all contractors doing work over $5,000 to register with the Attorney General's Office and post a $50K bond — but Philadelphia's Code §A-602 mandates a separate City HVAC Contractor license tested through L&I, and Pittsburgh's BIRT requires its own mechanical credential.
