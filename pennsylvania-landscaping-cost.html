Landscape Installation Cost in Pennsylvania (2026)

Landscape installation in Pennsylvania typically runs $4,200–$17,800 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 5b-7a. Pennsylvania's combination of acidic forest-soil substrates across the Appalachian Plateau and Pocono Mountains, mature deciduous canopy across Philadelphia Main Line and Pittsburgh suburbs, and Susquehanna River watershed Chesapeake Bay TMDL nutrient-reduction targets pushes Pennsylvania residential landscape design toward bay-friendly natives, low-input cool-season tall fescue turf, and rain garden bioretention on every new install — and the state's pre-1900 housing-stock concentration in Philadelphia, Pittsburgh, Allentown, and Lancaster triggers lead-soil testing on landscape installs adjacent to pre-1978 painted exteriors.

Pennsylvania climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 5b-7a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 42 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 180 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Low — adequate residential precipitation, minimal drought stress
  • Irrigation dependency: Low — irrigation optional, established plants survive on rainfall
  • Frost-line trench depth: 36-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

Pennsylvania licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Pennsylvania Department of Agriculture Bureau of Plant Industry — Pesticide Programs + Office of Attorney General HIC registration (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; PDA Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care; PA HIC registration required above $5,000 annual gross

How landscape installation costs vary in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania's combination of acidic forest-soil substrates across the Appalachian Plateau and Pocono Mountains, mature deciduous canopy across Philadelphia Main Line and Pittsburgh suburbs, and Susquehanna River watershed Chesapeake Bay TMDL nutrient-reduction targets pushes Pennsylvania residential landscape design toward bay-friendly natives, low-input cool-season tall fescue turf, and rain garden bioretention on every new install — and the state's pre-1900 housing-stock concentration in Philadelphia, Pittsburgh, Allentown, and Lancaster triggers lead-soil testing on landscape installs adjacent to pre-1978 painted exteriors.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Pennsylvania Pesticide Control Act (3 P.S. §111.21) makes the Pennsylvania Department of Agriculture the residential pesticide applicator licensor and grants PDA preemption authority over municipal pesticide ordinances — Pennsylvania blocks Philadelphia, Pittsburgh, Allentown, and Erie from adopting local pesticide restrictions on residential landscape contractors, but Pennsylvania separately operates the Chesapeake Bay Pollution Reduction Plan that caps commercial nitrogen fertilizer application rates on residential property in the Susquehanna River watershed under PA Act 38 nutrient management rules.
