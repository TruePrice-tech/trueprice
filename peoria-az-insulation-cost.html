Questions Peoria homeowners ask about insulation

Is blown-in insulation better than batts?

Blown-in fills gaps and voids that batts leave around wiring, pipes, and framing. It performs 15-25% better than batts in real-world conditions. Batts are easier to install in open walls during new construction.

Do I need a permit for insulation in Peoria?

Most Peoria jurisdictions do not require permits for adding insulation to existing spaces. Permits may be needed if the work involves removing drywall or altering vapor barriers. Check local codes.

What is a vapor barrier and do I need one?

A vapor barrier prevents moisture from passing through walls and ceilings. In cold climates, install on the warm (interior) side. In hot-humid climates, install on the exterior side. Closed-cell spray foam acts as its own vapor barrier.

Where Peoria's pricing comes from. Contractor labor rates in Peoria are roughly average for the US. Local conditions in Peoria. Peoria has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Radiant barriers and reflective roof coatings outperform added attic insulation in Peoria's cooling-dominant climate. The intense solar radiation on the roof surface is the primary heat source — reflecting it before it enters the attic is more effective than trying to insulate against it. Insulation costs in Peoria vary by material: blown fiberglass ($1-1.50/sqft at R-38), blown cellulose ($1.25-2/sqft at R-38), spray foam open-cell ($1.50-2.50/sqft at R-19), and spray foam closed-cell ($2.50-4/sqft at R-21).
