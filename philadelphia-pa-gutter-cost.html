Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a gutter cost in Philadelphia?

Most homeowners in Philadelphia pay between $1,000 and $1,500 for gutter installation, depending on material and yard size.

What is the cheapest gutter material?

Aluminum gutters are the most affordable option at $6-$12 per linear foot installed. Vinyl is comparable at $5-$11 per linear foot but less durable in extreme climates.

How long does a wood gutter last?

A well-maintained wood gutter typically lasts 15-20 years. Regular staining and sealing every 2-3 years can significantly extend its lifespan. Cedar and redwood last longer than pine.

Philadelphia-specific gutter sizing

Standard 5-inch K-style gutters move about 1.2 gallons per second, adequate for moderate rainfall up to 2 inches per hour. Philadelphia rainfall intensity routinely exceeds that threshold, so 6-inch oversized gutters with 40% more capacity are worth the $2-$3 per linear foot upcharge in Fishtown, Northern Liberties, and Manayunk.

Downspout capacity and placement

Center City row houses and older Fishtown, Brewerytown, and South Philly blocks still discharge almost universally into the city's combined sewer system, and Philadelphia Water Department's stormwater grading program now pushes commercial properties and some residential renovations toward disconnected downspouts, rain gardens, or stormwater planters. On twin homes and row houses, shared party-wall gutter runs mean one neighbor's clog becomes both neighbors' problem.

Rule of thumb for Philadelphia: one downspout per 20-30 linear feet of gutter. Each 2x3-inch downspout drains roughly 600 sq ft; step up to 3x4-inch on Fishtown, Northern Liberties, and Manayunk homes with large roof planes or steep pitches that concentrate runoff.

What to Watch for in Philadelphia Gutter Bids

Here are the Philadelphia gutter-bid patterns that burn homeowners most often.

Bidding Philadelphia roofs without rainfall context

Philadelphia receives 44.5 inches of rain annually. A contractor bidding standard 5-inch gutters without mentioning Philadelphia rainfall intensity or your specific roof area is sizing for convenience, not performance. Homes in Fishtown, Northern Liberties, and Manayunk with roof areas over 1,500 sq ft draining to a single run should default to 6-inch gutters.

Philadelphia gutter slope problems

Correct slope for Philadelphia gutters: 1/4 inch per 10 feet of run toward each downspout. Flat sections pool water and overflow during storms. A five-minute post-install hose test catches the problem — any standing water means the hangers need adjusting.

No splash blocks or downspout extensions

Discharge routing is the half of a Philadelphia gutter project contractors most often skip. Given 44.5 inches of Philadelphia rainfall annually, dumping water at the foundation causes foundation movement, basement moisture, and yard erosion. A proper Philadelphia bid addresses where the water goes, not just how it gets off the roof.

"Seamless" that actually has seams

Seamless = formed on-site from one coil. Sectional = factory pieces joined with connectors. The Philadelphia scam is marketing sectional gutters as "seamless" and charging the seamless premium. Seams cause the majority of gutter failures, so paying for real seamless matters — verify the coil-forming machine showed up to your job.
