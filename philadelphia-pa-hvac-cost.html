What SEER and AFUE to Aim For in Philadelphia

Cooling efficiency target

16+ SEER

16 SEER is the sensible Philadelphia minimum because heating, not cooling, dominates your PECO (electric) bill. The 14-to-16 SEER jump delivers modest savings here; the 16-to-20 jump delivers almost none. Put the efficiency budget toward the furnace or a cold-climate heat pump instead.

Heating efficiency target

95%+ AFUE

Philadelphia's heavy heating load makes 95-96% AFUE the Philadelphia minimum on any new gas furnace. At PECO (electric)'s $1.55/therm rate, the 80-to-96% AFUE jump saves $225-$420 annually in a typical Philadelphia home. Condensing furnaces vent through 2-3 inch PVC rather than the old metal flue, which is a plus if your intown home already has a compromised brick chimney but adds cost on a first-time conversion.

Philadelphia's 2026 minimum efficiency rules. Federal floor for PA is 14 SEER2 under the DOE northern-region rule, but most Philadelphia homeowners should aim well above that because the incremental cost of higher SEER2 is modest. A Philadelphia bid showing "14 SEER" in 2026 is either clearing pre-2025 inventory or mixing up SEER2 with legacy SEER — cross-check the AHRI reference at ahridirectory.org.

Philadelphia rebate math and PA incentives. PECO's Smart Ideas program pays $100-$350 for qualifying high-SEER AC and smart thermostats; PGW's EnergySense program adds $400-$700 for high-AFUE gas furnaces. 25C heat-pump credits ended with 2025 and have no effect on 2026 Philadelphia project taxes. HEAR and HOMES IRA rebates remain live: HEAR delivers up to $8,000 for income-qualified PA residents; HOMES delivers $4,000-$8,000 based on measured performance. Both flow through PA's energy office and the installer, not through your 1040. Ground-loop geothermal is a separate path in Philadelphia: 30% Section 25D credit, uncapped, runs through 2034.
