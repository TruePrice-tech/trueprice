Plumbing in Philadelphia, PA: what locals should know

Climate & site factors

Philadelphia has moderate water hardness — not extreme, but enough to warrant annual water heater flushing. A whole-house filter can improve taste and reduce minor buildup in pipes and fixtures. For Philadelphia homes, PEX is the go-to repiping material. Its flexibility means it handles freeze-thaw cycles better than copper or CPVC. It's also faster to install, reducing labor costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Philadelphia?

Plumber rates in Philadelphia typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
