Phoenix kitchen remodel: answers to common questions

Should I hire a general contractor or manage subs myself?

A GC adds 15-25% overhead but manages scheduling, permits, and quality across trades. Self-managing saves money but requires significant time and construction knowledge to coordinate plumbing, electrical, and carpentry.

How long does a kitchen remodel take?

A cosmetic refresh takes 2-4 weeks. A mid-range remodel runs 6-10 weeks. A full gut renovation with layout changes takes 12-16 weeks. Permit delays and backordered materials can add 2-4 weeks.

Phoenix Permits and Electrical Scope

City of Phoenix Planning and Development issues permits via TRACE online system with 2-4 week review on kitchen remodels. APS or SRP panel upgrades to 200A run $2,400-$4,800 depending on whether the service drop is overhead or underground. Arizona amends the NEC to require AFCI on kitchen lighting circuits and GFCI on all receptacles. Scottsdale, Mesa, Chandler, Tempe, and Gilbert each run separate portals with different fee schedules and inspection timelines.

TRACE online review runs 2-4 weeks in Phoenix proper with longer queues in peak remodel season (October-March). Scottsdale typically runs 3-5 weeks and is stricter on green building ordinance compliance; Mesa runs 2-3 weeks. HOAs add 2-6 weeks on top of city review and many require in-person architectural committee meetings with specific cut-off dates. Summer permit submissions are often faster because Valley remodel demand drops sharply June-September.
