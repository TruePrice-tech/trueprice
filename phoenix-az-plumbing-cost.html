Climate & site factors

The extremely hard water in Phoenix causes heavy scale buildup in pipes and appliances. Water heaters, dishwashers, and faucets all suffer. A water softener is practically a necessity here. For Phoenix's hard water, PEX piping is ideal — it doesn't corrode or develop pinhole leaks like copper can in mineral-rich water. It's also the most cost-effective option for whole-house repiping.

Phoenix Water Utility and Supply

Phoenix Water Services draws from the Salt and Verde rivers via the Salt River Project (SRP) canal system plus Central Arizona Project (CAP) Colorado River deliveries. The blend varies seasonally with SRP dominating winter and spring supply and CAP dominating summer. Hardness tests at 220 to 350 ppm (very hard). The Val Vista, 24th Street, and Deer Valley Water Treatment Plants serve different pressure zones. The Arizona Department of Water Resources tracks drought-contingency allocations that affect CAP availability.

Phoenix hardness of 220 to 350 ppm is among the highest of major US metros and causes aggressive scale buildup that reduces water heater efficiency by up to 30 percent and cuts 12-year tank life to 6 to 8. Tankless Rinnai and Navien units must be descaled annually or they fail prematurely. Whole-house softeners through Kinetico, RainSoft, or Culligan are standard, and the Arizona Water Utilities Association publishes hardness data by pressure zone that helps sizing decisions.

Pipe Materials Inside Phoenix Walls

Phoenix 1950s-1970s homes used copper supply lines as standard. CPVC was common in 1990s-2000s construction and becomes extremely brittle in Phoenix's extreme heat, especially on attic runs where temperatures exceed 150F. PEX-a Uponor or PEX-b SharkBite is the current repipe standard under Arizona Plumbing Code. The alkaline soil and hard water create conditions for copper pitting corrosion from both exterior soil contact and interior scale, which drives PEX conversion on 40-plus-year-old homes.

Phoenix Plumbing Licensing and Permits

Arizona Registrar of Contractors (ROC) issues the CR-37 plumbing license for any work over $1,000. Verify at roc.az.gov. The ROC database includes complaint history, bond status, and license classification. Phoenix permits route through Planning and Development Department with Scottsdale, Mesa, Chandler, and Gilbert handling separately. Arizona also requires separate backflow prevention certification for anyone working on irrigation or fire suppression crossings. ROC bond requirements of $15,000 minimum protect homeowners.

Phoenix Water Heater Culture

Phoenix residential water heaters are typically gas tank units from AO Smith, Bradford White, or Rheem at 50 gallons, often located in garages where summer attic temperatures would otherwise degrade them. Tankless Rinnai RUR199iN units require annual descaling in the hard-water environment. Heat pump water heaters from AO Smith Voltex or Rheem ProTerra qualify for APS Efficiency Rebates plus Southwest Gas Residential Rebates on gas units, plus federal IRA credits. Solar thermal water heating qualifies for additional APS incentives.

Freeze Risk and Winterization in Phoenix

Pipe freezing is extremely rare in Phoenix proper but can occur in outlying areas like Fountain Hills, Cave Creek, Carefree, and New River during occasional December or January hard freeze events. Most Phoenix homes have zero freeze protection, so rare events cause disproportionate damage. Anthem and Queen Creek at higher elevation see 28F nights more often. The January 2023 freeze and the January 2007 deep freeze are reference events. Frost-free Woodford Model 17 hose bibs are uncommon but worthwhile for northern-Valley elevation homes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Phoenix?

Plumber rates in Phoenix typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
