Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a concrete driveway cost in Pittsburgh?

Most homeowners in Pittsburgh pay between $4,550 and $9,750 for a concrete driveway, depending on size and finish type.

Is stamped concrete worth the extra cost?

Stamped concrete costs 30-50% more than plain concrete but can mimic the look of pavers, brick, or natural stone at a fraction of the cost. It adds significant curb appeal and value.

How long does a concrete driveway last?

A properly installed and sealed concrete driveway typically lasts 25-30 years. Regular sealing every 2-3 years and prompt crack repair can extend its lifespan further.

About concrete work in Pittsburgh, PA

Why pricing varies in Pittsburgh. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. For concrete work specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Pittsburgh at roughly 5% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Pittsburgh has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained mix (5-7%) is mandatory; sealers extend service life on driveways exposed to road salt.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Pittsburgh require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 305,841, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Pittsburgh has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.
