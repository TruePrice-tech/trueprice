Pittsburgh plumbing: answers to common questions

What is the difference between copper and PEX piping?

Copper lasts 50+ years, resists bacteria, and has proven durability. PEX costs 60% less, installs faster, resists freezing better, and requires fewer joints. Both are code-compliant.

Is trenchless sewer repair worth the extra cost?

Trenchless repair costs 20-40% more but avoids excavating your yard, driveway, or landscaping. It is faster and causes less property damage, often making it cheaper overall.

How much does it cost to replace a water heater?

A standard 50-gallon tank water heater runs $1,200-$2,500 installed. Tankless units cost $2,500-$5,000 but last roughly twice as long and cut energy bills 20-30%.

What does a plumbing inspection include?

A thorough inspection covers supply lines, drain lines, water heater condition, fixture connections, shutoff valves, water pressure testing, and a visual check for leaks and corrosion.

How much does a plumber charge per hour in Pittsburgh?

Plumbers in Pittsburgh typically charge $75-$150 per hour. Emergency and after-hours calls add a $100-$200 trip fee on top of the hourly rate.

How do I prevent frozen pipes in Pittsburgh?

In Pittsburgh, burst pipes from freezing are among the costliest plumbing emergencies. Prevention measures: insulate exposed pipes with foam sleeves, install thermostat-controlled heat tape on vulnerable runs, keep cabinets under exterior-wall sinks open during cold snaps, and never let the thermostat drop below 55F. A plumber can winterize vacant properties by draining the system and protecting traps with antifreeze.

What should a plumbing quote include in Pittsburgh?

A professional plumbing quote in Pittsburgh should itemize: labor hours, material costs and brands, permit fees if applicable, cleanup, and warranty terms. The quote should include the plumber's license number and proof of insurance. Get three written quotes for any job over $500 to ensure fair pricing.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Pittsburgh?

Pittsburgh homeowners should target late spring or summer for planned plumbing work. Frozen ground complicates any excavation and emergency freeze calls dominate plumber schedules in winter, which translates to better availability and pricing. Emergency service runs 50-100% more than scheduled jobs, so proactive replacement of aging equipment is the financially smarter path.
