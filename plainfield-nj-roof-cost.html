Plainfield roofing FAQ

How long does a roof replacement take?

Most residential roofs take 1-3 days to replace. Complex roofs with multiple layers, steep pitch, or extensive damage can take 4-7 days.

What is the most affordable roofing option?

Three-tab asphalt shingles are the most affordable at $3-5 per square foot installed. Architectural shingles cost $4-7/sqft but last 10-15 years longer, making them a better value over time.

How much does a new roof cost in Plainfield?

Most homeowners in Plainfield pay between $8,000 and $16,000 for a new roof, depending on size, material, and complexity.

How many layers of shingles can I have?

Most building codes allow a maximum of two layers. If you already have two layers, the old roof must be torn off before the new one is installed, adding $1,000-$3,000 to the cost.

How long does a roof last in Plainfield?

Roof lifespan in Plainfield depends on material and maintenance: architectural shingles run 20-25 years, 3-tab shingles 15-20, metal 40-60, and tile 50 or more. UV radiation breaks down materials faster than most homeowners expect, so lighter colors and proper attic ventilation both add years. These numbers assume no major storm damage and regular maintenance.

Is a Plainfield permit needed to replace my roof?

Yes, Plainfield generally requires a building permit for full roof replacements. Your roofer should pull this as part of the job. Without a permit, your insurance coverage may be compromised and you face disclosure obligations if you sell the home.

When should I replace a roof in Plainfield?

Plainfield homeowners should target early fall for roof replacement work. After summer storms subside and before winter weather complicates scheduling, giving you the best combination of weather conditions and contractor pricing. Off-season scheduling typically saves 10-15% compared to peak demand periods, especially the weeks immediately following severe weather events.
