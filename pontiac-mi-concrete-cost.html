Concrete questions for Pontiac homeowners

How thick should a concrete driveway be?

Residential driveways should be 4 inches thick for cars and 5-6 inches for heavy vehicles like RVs. A 6-inch compacted gravel base underneath is essential. Thickened edges (6 inches) prevent cracking at the perimeter.

Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in Pontiac?

In Pontiac, permits are typically required for driveways, sidewalks, and any concrete work near property lines or public right-of-way. Backyard patios may be exempt depending on size. Check local requirements.

What is stamped concrete?

Stamped concrete uses patterned mats pressed into wet concrete to mimic brick, stone, slate, or wood. It costs $12-$25/sqft installed. It requires resealing every 2-3 years to maintain appearance and prevent wear.

What causes concrete to crack?

Common causes: shrinkage during curing, frost heave from inadequate base prep, tree roots, heavy vehicle loads beyond design, poor control joint placement, and water pooling due to improper grading.

About concrete work in Pontiac, MI

Where Pontiac's pricing comes from. At 19% above national average labor rates, Pontiac's contractor pricing starts from a higher baseline than most US markets. The gap reflects both local wages and regional demand for skilled trades. On concrete work, Pontiac runs about 9% above the US average based on our pricing data across metro areas.

Local conditions in Pontiac. Pontiac has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Pontiac for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Older properties in Pontiac (averaging 42 years) often have settling concrete that affects drainage. Re-pouring may require sub-grade correction (compaction, drainage tile) that new construction doesn't need. Budget 10-20% extra for site preparation on established properties.

Pontiac permits and code requirements. Permits are usually required for concrete work in Pontiac; verify the specifics with the local building department before committing.

The Pontiac contractor pool. At 59,887 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. The Pontiac area's flat growth keeps contractor availability high year-round. Seasonal discounts of 10-15% are realistic for flexible project timelines.
