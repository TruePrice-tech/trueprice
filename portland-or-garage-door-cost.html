Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new garage door cost in Portland?

Most homeowners in Portland pay between $1,169 and $4,830 for a new garage door installed, depending on size and material.

Should I replace the opener when I get a new garage door?

If your opener is more than 10 years old, replacing it with the door is usually more cost-effective. New openers are quieter, more secure, and include modern safety features.

How long does garage door installation take?

A standard garage door installation takes 3-5 hours. Custom doors or jobs requiring structural modifications may take a full day.

Permits, Code, and Dealers in Portland

City of Portland Bureau of Development Services handles garage door replacement permits when structural headers or opening dimensions change. Standard replacement of same-size doors generally does not require a permit.

Clopay, Amarr, and Wayne Dalton are the major brands distributed through Portland dealers. Pearl District and Lake Oswego have strong local dealer networks offering Overhead Door and C.H.I. products. Specialty carriage-house and custom wood doors are available through regional distributors.

Severe weather events in Portland occasionally damage garage doors, with thunderstorms and wind as the primary risks.

Portland's climate is characterized by marine west coast like Seattle with constant winter rain, moss growth, and rare but damaging ice storms (Feb 2021), which affects every garage door spec decision discussed above.

Local soil conditions also matter for garage slab integrity: clay-heavy with many landslide-prone hillsides in the West Hills. This affects track alignment and long-term door performance. Permit timeline in Portland: City of Portland BDS permits typically take 1-3 weeks for residential exterior work.
