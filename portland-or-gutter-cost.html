Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a gutter cost in Portland?

What is the cheapest gutter material?

Aluminum gutters are the most affordable option at $6-$12 per linear foot installed. Vinyl is comparable at $5-$11 per linear foot but less durable in extreme climates.

How long does a wood gutter last?

Common Gutter Scams in Portland

Alberta Arts, Hawthorne, and Sellwood homeowners see these same four gutter-quote issues repeatedly.

Portland-specific undersizing

A Portland bid that specifies 5-inch gutters without reference to 43 inches of annual rainfall or the specific Alberta Arts, Hawthorne, and Sellwood roof area being drained is underthought. Any roof plane above 1,500 sq ft emptying to a single gutter run needs 6-inch in Portland.

Pitch errors on Portland installs

Correct slope for Portland gutters: 1/4 inch per 10 feet of run toward each downspout. Flat sections pool water and overflow during storms. A five-minute post-install hose test catches the problem — any standing water means the hangers need adjusting.

Portland discharge routing skipped

Portland downspouts need splash blocks, extensions, or buried drain tile — without them, 43 inches of annual rain lands at the foundation each year. Foundation problems, wet basements, and landscape erosion follow. Portland bids that ignore discharge routing are missing half the job.

Seamed gutters sold as seamless

Real seamless means the installer rolls the full gutter run on your Portland property from continuous coil stock; seams appear only at corners, outlets, and end caps. A "seamless" Portland bid that mentions joining factory-cut sections with splice connectors is sectional gutter at seamless pricing — the single most common install-side scam.
