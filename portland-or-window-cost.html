Common window replacement questions in Portland

Should I replace all windows at once?

Replacing all windows at once saves 10-15% on labor versus doing them individually. It also ensures uniform appearance and consistent energy performance throughout the home.

Do replacement windows increase home value?

New windows recover 65-75% of their cost at resale. They also improve curb appeal, reduce noise, and eliminate a common inspection concern for buyers.

What type of replacement window is best?

Vinyl frames offer the best value with low maintenance and good insulation. Fiberglass is more durable and paintable. Wood has the best aesthetics but needs ongoing maintenance. Aluminum is cheapest but conducts heat.

How much energy do new windows actually save?

Replacing single-pane windows saves 25-30% on heating and cooling costs. Upgrading from older double-pane to modern Low-E double-pane saves 10-15%. Payback takes 10-15 years through energy savings alone.

How much does window replacement cost in Portland?

Window replacement in Portland costs $400-$1,200 per window installed, depending on size, frame material, glass type, and accessibility. A whole-house project (10-15 windows) runs $5,000-$15,000.

Portland-specific window fraud patterns

Portland's most common window fraud involves CCB license misrepresentation. Oregon requires the CCB license number on all advertising, contracts, and business cards, and contractors who omit it are either unlicensed or operating under a lapsed license. Check ccb.oregon.gov on the day of contract signing, not the day of the initial bid. The CCB database shows the exact license expiration date, bond amount, and complaint history. The second Portland-specific scam is bait-and-switch on Energy Trust rebate eligibility: a contractor promises Energy Trust rebate processing, quotes a net price reflecting the rebate, and the homeowner discovers after installation that the installed product does not meet Energy Trust's specific NFRC performance thresholds. Confirm the exact product model number qualifies for the Energy Trust residential window rebate at energytrust.org before signing the contract.
