Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a gutter cost in Raleigh?

What is the cheapest gutter material?

Aluminum gutters are the most affordable option at $6-$12 per linear foot installed. Vinyl is comparable at $5-$11 per linear foot but less durable in extreme climates.

How long does a wood gutter last?

Common Gutter Scams in Raleigh

Five Points, Cameron Park, and Oakwood homeowners see these same four gutter-quote issues repeatedly.

Raleigh-specific undersizing

At 46 inches of annual rain in Raleigh, a bid that defaults to 5-inch gutters without discussing Five Points, Cameron Park, and Oakwood roof area or peak rainfall rate is cutting corners. Roof planes over 1,500 sq ft draining to one run want 6-inch capacity in Raleigh's rain profile.

Pitch errors on Raleigh installs

Correct slope for Raleigh gutters: 1/4 inch per 10 feet of run toward each downspout. Flat sections pool water and overflow during storms. A five-minute post-install hose test catches the problem — any standing water means the hangers need adjusting.

Raleigh discharge routing skipped

Raleigh downspouts need splash blocks, extensions, or buried drain tile — without them, 46 inches of annual rain lands at the foundation each year. Foundation problems, wet basements, and landscape erosion follow. Raleigh bids that ignore discharge routing are missing half the job.

Seamed gutters sold as seamless

Real seamless means the installer rolls the full gutter run on your Raleigh property from continuous coil stock; seams appear only at corners, outlets, and end caps. A "seamless" Raleigh bid that mentions joining factory-cut sections with splice connectors is sectional gutter at seamless pricing — the single most common install-side scam.
