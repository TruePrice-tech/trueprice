Healthcare in Raleigh, NC: what locals should know

Hospital landscape

Raleigh is served by multiple hospital systems competing for patients. Understanding which hospitals are in your insurance network before an emergency can save you thousands in out-of-network charges.

Insurance coverage

Check your plan's network carefully. In Raleigh, the difference between in-network and out-of-network charges for the same procedure can be 3-5x.

Negotiation leverage

Every Raleigh hospital has a financial assistance program. Ask for the self-pay rate before accepting any bill at face value. Most Raleigh hospitals offer 20-40% prompt-pay discounts.

Neighborhood access

Five Points, Cameron Park, Oakwood residents have access to community health centers with sliding-fee scales for primary care, often at a fraction of ER costs.

Raleigh Hospital Systems and Safety-Net Providers

WakeMed Health & Hospitals, UNC REX Healthcare, Duke Health Raleigh Hospital, and UNC Health serve the Raleigh-Durham metro. WakeMed Raleigh is the Level I trauma center. Duke University Hospital in Durham is the academic anchor. There is no county-owned safety-net hospital; WakeMed absorbs disproportionate uncompensated care for Wake County.

Wake County's uninsured rate is approximately 8%, below the North Carolina average, reflecting the Research Triangle's employer-based coverage rates. North Carolina's 2023 Medicaid expansion is projected to further reduce the rate as enrollment ramps up.

Average Medical Procedure Costs in Raleigh

An ER visit at WakeMed averages $2,000-$3,500, while UNC REX charges $1,800-$3,200 for comparable acuity. MRI at Duke Raleigh runs $1,100-$2,400; freestanding imaging centers in Cary and Apex offer the same scan for $300-$600. Joint replacement at WakeMed costs $26,000-$44,000 before insurance negotiated rates.

WakeMed and UNC REX publish CMS-mandated price transparency files. Duke Health publishes consolidated transparency data. North Carolina DHHS publishes hospital financial reports. WakeMed's patient price estimator is among the more user-friendly tools in the Triangle market.

Emergency Room vs. Urgent Care in Raleigh

WakeMed Urgent Care and UNC Urgent Care operate 10+ locations across the Triangle. Self-pay visits run $150-$300 versus $2,000+ at a WakeMed ER. Advance Community Health operates FQHC locations in Wake County. Alliance Medical Ministry provides free primary care for uninsured working adults.
