Raleigh Water Utility and Supply

City of Raleigh Public Utilities (Falls Lake reservoir) serves Raleigh. The surface water source requires seasonal treatment adjustments. Service line from the curb stop to the building is homeowner-responsible. Annual water quality reports are published by the utility.

Raleigh water tests at 40-80 ppm (soft to slightly hard). Relatively soft water means scale buildup is not a major concern for fixtures and water heaters. The source lake water chemistry affects pipe corrosion rates and treatment needs.

Raleigh Sewer Infrastructure

Raleigh sewer infrastructure varies by neighborhood age. Sewer line inspection with video camera ($200-$400) is recommended before purchasing older homes.

Raleigh receives 46 inches of annual precipitation. Heavy rainfall regularly overwhelms aging storm drains and causes basement or crawlspace flooding. French drains, sump pumps, and proper grading protect against foundation water intrusion.

Pipe Materials Inside Raleigh Walls

Raleigh pre-1960 homes may still have galvanized supply lines and cast-iron drains. Post-1970 homes typically have copper supply. Raleigh Water Heater Culture

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Raleigh?

Plumber rates in Raleigh typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
