Common solar questions in Raleigh

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

Do solar panels increase home value?

Owned solar systems add roughly $15,000-$20,000 to home value according to national studies. Leased systems do not add value and can complicate the sale process.

How long does solar take to pay for itself in Raleigh?

Payback in Raleigh depends on electricity rates, sun hours, and incentives. Most systems pay back in 6-10 years and then generate free electricity for another 15-20 years.

What is the difference between string inverters and microinverters?

String inverters are cheaper but the whole array drops output when one panel is shaded. Microinverters optimize each panel independently, cost 15-20% more, and last longer.

Will solar panels survive a hurricane in Raleigh?

Solar panels on properly rated racking systems handle hurricanes well, often outlasting the roof itself during high-wind events. The critical factors: racking must be certified for Raleigh's wind speed zone (130+ mph), all roof penetrations need proper flashing and sealant, and ongoing maintenance keeps connections tight. Schedule a post-storm inspection with your installer after any significant hurricane event.

What solar incentives are available in Raleigh?

Every Raleigh homeowner installing solar qualifies for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit with no dollar limit. Beyond that, NC may offer state tax credits, utility-specific rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits, and property tax exemptions. The DSIRE database has the current list for your area. These stacked incentives typically reduce net cost by an additional 10-30%.

What's the best season to install solar in Raleigh?

Raleigh homeowners should aim for early spring or fall to install solar. Moderate weather conditions for installation and you avoid the peak-demand scheduling crunch. Allow 6-12 weeks from signing a contract through permitting, utility interconnection approval, and installation. Start gathering quotes 2-3 months before your target window. Remember that the 30% ITC applies to the tax year in which installation is completed.

Local Solar Guide for Raleigh, NC

Solar performs reliably in Raleigh's mixed climate, with production typical of the region rather than desert highs. The financial case is driven less by raw insolation and more by rising retail electricity rates plus available federal and state incentives. Microinverters or optimizers tolerate partial cloud cover better than string systems here.

Raleigh's hurricane exposure adds a design consideration: panel racking systems must be rated for high-wind loads, and some insurers require specific mounting certifications. Wind-rated racking adds $500-$1,500 to installation cost but is required by building code. Verify that your installer uses racking certified for the wind speed zone applicable to your location and that the installation is permitted and inspected.

With an average home age of 41 years in Raleigh, roof condition assessment is critical before any solar installation. Panels last 25-30 years, so if your roof has less than 15 years of remaining life, replace it before installing solar. Removing panels for a roof replacement later costs $1,500-$3,000 in de-install and reinstall labor. Most reputable solar companies include a roof assessment in their site survey.

Raleigh's rapid growth often strains the local utility grid, which can strengthen the case for solar-plus-battery systems. Time-of-use rate structures are increasingly common in growing markets, and solar production aligns well with peak-rate afternoon hours, maximizing the bill offset.
