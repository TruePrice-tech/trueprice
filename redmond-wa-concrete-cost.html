Concrete questions for Redmond homeowners

How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

How much does concrete work cost in Redmond?

Concrete costs in Redmond range from $6-$12 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, patios) to $15-$30/sqft for stamped or decorative finishes, including labor and materials.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

What Redmond homeowners should know about concrete work

Why pricing varies in Redmond. Labor rates here run roughly 42% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. Our data shows concrete work costs in Redmond tracking roughly 23% higher than the national baseline.

Redmond's site and climate factors. Redmond has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Redmond's constant moisture promotes moss and algae growth on concrete surfaces. Annual pressure washing and sealer application maintain appearance and prevent the biological growth that makes walkways slippery. Standard mixes work well — freeze-thaw additives are unnecessary in this mild climate. Homes in Redmond averaging 36 years old typically have stable sub-grades that simplify concrete work. New pours integrate cleanly with existing slabs and foundations without the settling corrections older properties need.

Redmond paperwork, inspections, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Redmond require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Who's doing the work in Redmond. With 57,530 people, Redmond's contractor pool is mid-sized. You will find 4-6 established shops for most trades, which provides enough competition for fair pricing. Redmond's expansion has outpaced trade workforce growth. The practical effect: longer booking windows and firmer pricing than you would find in slower-growth cities.

Timing considerations. Redmond allows year-round concrete work, but the ideal window is spring and fall when temperatures support proper curing without the heat-related challenges of summer. Avoid scheduling large pours during the hottest months — early morning pours are the only reliable option.

What drives cost. Redmond labor rates sit 42% above the US median, making labor the single largest cost factor for concrete work. Redmond's housing stock averages 36 years — the age where original installations start failing and code requirements have evolved.
