Common landscaping questions in Redmond

How much does a paver patio cost?

Paver patios cost $12-$25 per square foot installed, depending on paver material and pattern complexity. A 300 sqft patio runs $3,600-$7,500. Permeable pavers cost 15-20% more but help with drainage.

How much does landscaping cost in Redmond?

Basic landscaping in Redmond costs $3,000-$8,000 for design and installation. Full yard transformations with hardscaping, irrigation, and mature plantings run $15,000-$50,000+.

What are the best low-maintenance landscaping plants?

Native perennials, ornamental grasses, and drought-tolerant shrubs need the least care. Avoid high-maintenance options like hybrid tea roses, non-native annuals, and fast-growing trees that require frequent pruning.

Does landscaping increase home value?

Well-designed landscaping adds 5-15% to home value. Mature trees alone add $1,000-$10,000 each. Curb appeal landscaping recovers 100-200% of investment at resale, one of the highest ROI improvements.

How much does tree removal cost?

Small trees (under 30 feet) cost $200-$500 to remove. Medium trees (30-60 feet) cost $500-$1,000. Large trees (60-100 feet) cost $1,000-$2,000+. Stump grinding adds $100-$400 per stump.

More questions about landscaping in Redmond

How much does mulch cost per yard?

Bulk mulch costs $25-$50 per cubic yard delivered. Plan 3 inches deep — one cubic yard covers about 108 sq ft. A 1,000 sq ft bed needs roughly 10 yards ($250-$500 in materials) plus $30-$50/yard for professional spreading and edging.

What does a sprinkler system cost in Redmond?

A residential irrigation system in Redmond costs $2,500-$5,000 for a quarter-acre lot with 4-6 zones. Smart wifi controllers add $100-$300 and cut water usage 20-40%. Drip-irrigation zones for planting beds cost $500-$1,000 extra and are worth it on shrubs and perennials.

How much does sod installation cost?

Sod costs $1-$2 per square foot installed including soil prep and rolling. Hydroseeding runs $0.05-$0.10/sqft and establishes in 4-6 weeks. Traditional broadcast seeding is the cheapest at $0.10-$0.25/sqft but needs 6-8 weeks and frequent watering. A 5,000 sq ft lawn lands between $5,000 and $10,000 in sod.

Do I need a permit for landscaping in Redmond?

Most landscaping in Redmond — planting, mulching, sod, basic irrigation — does not need a permit. Retaining walls over 4 feet, drainage tied to the storm system, tree removal in some neighborhoods, and any grading that changes lot drainage usually do. Confirm with your landscaper before they start digging.

Redmond's mild, wet climate makes it one of the easiest landscaping environments in the country. Native rhododendrons, ferns, and conifers thrive with minimal intervention. The challenge is managing overgrowth — maintenance budgets should include seasonal pruning and weed control. A complete landscaping project in Redmond typically breaks down as: plant material (30-40%), hardscape/materials (20-30%), labor (25-35%), and design (5-10%). Local factors: landscaping in Redmond, WA

The Redmond housing picture. At an average of 36 years, Redmond housing is squarely in the first-replacement cycle. The original components installed during construction are reaching end of life. landscaping demand is strong in this age range, and contractors are familiar with the common building patterns.

Neighborhood rulebook. HOA prevalence in Redmond is moderate. If you live in a managed community, check covenants before signing a landscaping contract. Material color, visible fixture type, or warranty requirements may affect your selection.

Local market conditions. The Redmond metro's rapid expansion has created a seller's market for contractors. Booking windows stretch 3-6 weeks, premium pricing holds even in off-season, and the most experienced crews are booked months ahead. Schedule proactively. In Redmond (57,530 residents), landscaping is often handled by small local crews who do both installation and maintenance. For larger design-build projects, consider firms from the nearest larger metro that service your area.

When to schedule. Redmond offers a longer landscaping season than cold-climate markets. The busiest period is March through June when spring planting demand peaks. Fall (October-December) is an excellent time for plantings, hardscape projects, and irrigation system installation at lower competition and often better pricing.

What drives cost. The biggest factor in Redmond landscaping project pricing is labor cost, running 42% above national benchmarks. For a full yard makeover, that premium alone accounts for $1680-3360 in additional cost. Redmond's housing stock averages 36 years — the age where original installations start failing and code requirements have evolved. Most landscaping project quotes will include some code-catch-up items that newer homes wouldn't need. Redmond's rapid growth means contractors can be selective about which jobs they take. Off-season scheduling and flexible timelines give you better leverage on pricing than trying to rush a project during peak demand.

Red flags to watch for. Any Redmond contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion. Watch for landscaping project quotes in Redmond that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Lump-sum bids hide margin and make change orders impossible to evaluate. Redmond's rapid growth attracts out-of-state contractors who follow the boom. Verify any unfamiliar company's local licensing, physical address, and track record. Fly-by-night operations leave when the market cools.
