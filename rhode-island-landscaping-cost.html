Landscape Installation Cost in Rhode Island (2026)

Landscape installation in Rhode Island typically runs $4,400–$18,800 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 6a-7a. Rhode Island's combination of acidic glacial-till soils across the Blackstone Valley and South County, mature deciduous canopy across East Side Providence and Newport, and Narragansett Bay watershed nutrient-management framework restricts standard high-nitrogen turf fertilizer programs across the state — and the state's sandy outwash soils across Block Island and the Newport coastal corridor combined with North Atlantic salt-spray on every Westerly to Newport residential property require salt-tolerant beach plum, bayberry, and northern bayberry hedging on every coastal install.

Rhode Island climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 6a-7a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 50 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 195 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Low — adequate residential precipitation, minimal drought stress
  • Irrigation dependency: Low — irrigation optional, established plants survive on rainfall
  • Frost-line trench depth: 42-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

Rhode Island licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Rhode Island Department of Environmental Management Office of Agriculture + Rhode Island Contractors Registration & Licensing Board (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; CRLB Contractor Registration required statewide regardless of contract value; RI DEM Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care

How landscape installation costs vary in Rhode Island

Rhode Island's combination of acidic glacial-till soils across the Blackstone Valley and South County, mature deciduous canopy across East Side Providence and Newport, and Narragansett Bay watershed nutrient-management framework restricts standard high-nitrogen turf fertilizer programs across the state — and the state's sandy outwash soils across Block Island and the Newport coastal corridor combined with North Atlantic salt-spray on every Westerly to Newport residential property require salt-tolerant beach plum, bayberry, and northern bayberry hedging on every coastal install.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Rhode Island General Laws §23-25 imposes one of the most restrictive residential pesticide notification regimes in New England — every Rhode Island commercial landscape contractor applying restricted-use pesticides on residential property within 50 ft of an occupied structure must provide 24-hour advance written notification, plus Rhode Island's 2018 Healthy Soils Act under RI Gen. Laws §2-25.1 created statewide soil-health incentive programs targeting residential landscape contractor adoption of organic-matter-building bed prep and cover-cropping on every transitional residential install.
