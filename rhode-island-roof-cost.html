Roof Replacement Cost in Rhode Island (2026)

Roof replacement in Rhode Island typically runs $9,500–$24,000 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. Newport, Block Island, and South County coastal exposures sit on the 140-mph wind-design line and require enhanced fastening; the state's small geography means saltwater-air corrosion affects nearly all roofs within 5 miles of the shoreline.

Rhode Island climate & code drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 5A
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate wind exposure — 110–140 mph design wind on coastal counties
  • Hail risk tier: Low — minimal severe hail history
  • Ground snow load: 30 psf — moderate snow load, ice-and-water shield required
  • Dominant residential roof material: asphalt shingle

Rhode Island licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Rhode Island Contractors' Registration and Licensing Board — registration required for any roofing work (official site)
  • Permit: city/town permit required

How roof replacement costs vary in Rhode Island

Newport, Block Island, and South County coastal exposures sit on the 140-mph wind-design line and require enhanced fastening; the state's small geography means saltwater-air corrosion affects nearly all roofs within 5 miles of the shoreline.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Rhode Island General Laws §5-65 mandates registration on every home-improvement contractor, including roofers — unregistered roofers cannot pull permits or collect on contracts, and the CRB enforces with up to $5,000 fines per violation.
