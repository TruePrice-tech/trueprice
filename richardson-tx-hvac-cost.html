Questions Richardson homeowners ask about hvac

What size HVAC system do I need?

System size depends on home square footage, insulation quality, window area, and climate zone. A Manual J load calculation determines the correct size. Never accept rule-of-thumb sizing.

How long does a new HVAC system last?

A properly maintained system lasts 15-20 years. Air conditioners and heat pumps last 12-17 years, while gas furnaces can reach 20-25 years with annual maintenance.

How long does HVAC installation take in Richardson?

A standard residential HVAC replacement takes 1-2 days. Complex jobs involving ductwork modification or system-type changes can take 3-5 days.

How much does a new HVAC system cost in Richardson?

Most homeowners in Richardson pay between $4,000 and $14,000 for a new HVAC system, depending on equipment type, efficiency rating, and home size.

Is a two-stage system worth the extra cost?

Two-stage and variable-speed systems run quieter, dehumidify better, and maintain more consistent temperatures. The $1,000-$2,500 premium pays back in 5-8 years through energy savings and comfort.

Local factors: HVAC replacement in Richardson, TX

The Richardson housing picture. Richardson's housing stock averages 32 years old, relatively recent construction that was largely built to modern code. HVAC replacement projects here tend to be simpler: fewer surprise compliance issues, quicker permit turnaround, and contractors who can quote from memory.

Neighborhood rulebook. HOA oversight is the norm across Richardson's residential communities. HVAC replacement projects require advance approval of materials, colors, and sometimes contractors. Build 2-4 weeks of review time into your project schedule.

Local market conditions. The Richardson contractor market is balanced: enough work to keep shops busy, but enough competition that homeowners can still get timely service. Booking 1-3 weeks ahead typically secures your preferred contractor. Many Richardson homes still have older R-22 refrigerant systems. If yours is one of them, now is the time to upgrade — R-22 is phased out and repair costs are skyrocketing.

When to schedule. The best time to replace your HVAC in Richardson is October through February. Contractors offer off-season pricing and can schedule sooner. Avoid June-September when demand spikes.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Richardson track within a few points of the national average for HVAC replacement, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. Richardson's relatively young housing stock (32 years average) simplifies most HVAC replacement projects. Modern code compliance, standard dimensions, and accessible construction reduce both time and cost versus older homes.

Red flags to watch for. Any Richardson contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion. Watch for HVAC replacement quotes in Richardson that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Lump-sum bids hide margin and make change orders impossible to evaluate.
