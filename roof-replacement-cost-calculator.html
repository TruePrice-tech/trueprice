How This Roof Cost Calculator Works

Roof replacement pricing usually starts with actual roof size, not just home square footage. The final number then changes based on material type, roof pitch, complexity, tear off requirements, disposal, flashing work, ventilation, and local labor costs.

This calculator gives a directional range rather than a guaranteed contractor price. It is most useful for checking whether a bid feels broadly reasonable before you compare detailed roofing quotes.

Typical Factors That Raise Roofing Cost

  • Steep pitch or difficult access
  • Multiple valleys, dormers, skylights, or chimneys
  • Decking replacement
  • Premium underlayment or flashing upgrades
  • Metal roofing or designer shingles
  • Higher local labor rates or permit costs

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is this roof cost calculator?

It is a directional pricing tool. It can help you benchmark a project, but a final contractor quote still depends on exact measurements, materials, local labor, flashing details, ventilation, and deck condition.

Why is roof size different from house size?

Roof area is often larger than interior square footage because of pitch, overhangs, garages, porches, and layout complexity.

What should I do after using this calculator?

The best next step is to compare actual contractor quotes and review whether materials, flashing, tear off, ventilation, and warranty coverage are clearly included.

Cost of asphalt shingle roofing

Asphalt is on 70%+ of US homes for good reason: cheapest material, fastest install, decent lifespan. Architectural (laminated) shingles cost about 30% more than 3-tab but last 5–10 years longer and look noticeably nicer. For a typical 2,000 sq ft home with 22 squares of roof area, expect $8,500–$16,000 for full architectural shingle replacement including tear-off, underlayment, and standard accessories.

Cost of metal roof replacement

Standing seam metal roofs run $15,000–$30,000 for an average home — roughly 2–3x the cost of asphalt — but last 40–70 years and improve energy efficiency 10–25%. Corrugated metal is the cheapest metal option at $5–$12/sq ft, popular for barns, garages, and budget-conscious whole-home installs.

Insurance discounts of 10–30% are common on metal roofs in hail-prone regions.

Roof replacement cost factors

Beyond material, the biggest cost drivers are: roof pitch (steep roofs add 15–30% labor for safety), tear-off layers (each existing layer adds $1–$2/sq ft to remove), decking replacement ($2–$5/sq ft for any rotted plywood), chimney/skylight flashing (each adds $300–$1,000), and upgraded underlayment or ice-and-water shield ($0.50–$1.50/sq ft).

Roof cost factors

Roof size

Measured in 'squares' (100 sq ft each). Average single-family home: 22 squares. Multiply per-sq-ft cost by total square feet to estimate.

Pitch / slope

Steep roofs (8/12+) add 15–30% labor for safety equipment and slower work. Flat roofs need different membrane materials.

Tear-off layers

Each existing roof layer to remove adds $1–$2/sq ft. Most cities allow only 2 total layers, so most roofs need full tear-off.

Decking repair

Plywood damage uncovered during tear-off costs $2–$5/sq ft to replace. Budget 5–10% extra for surprises.

Flashing + accessories

Chimney flashing $300–$1,000. Skylight reflashing $200–$500. Drip edge, ridge vent, starter strip add $1–$3/sq ft.

Permits + dump fees

$200–$1,000 in permits depending on city. Disposal of old shingles $400–$1,000 in dumpster fees.

Frequently asked questions about roof cost

How much does a new roof cost?
For a typical 2,000 sq ft home, full roof replacement runs $8,500–$16,000 for architectural asphalt shingles. Premium metal roofs run $15,000–$30,000. Tile or slate can hit $25,000–$60,000+. Tear-off, decking repairs, and pitch all impact final pricing.
How long does a roof last?
Asphalt 3-tab: 15–20 years. Architectural shingles: 25–30 years. Metal: 40–70 years. Tile: 50+ years. Slate: 75–100 years. Manufacturer warranty is usually shorter than expected lifespan. Climate (hail, sun exposure, freeze-thaw) significantly impacts real-world life.
Should I do tear-off or roof-over?
Roof-over (installing new shingles on top of old) saves $1,500–$3,500 in tear-off costs but reduces lifespan, voids most warranties, hides decking damage, and most cities only allow 2 total layers. Tear-off is almost always the right call for permanent fix.
Is metal roofing worth the premium?
Metal costs 2–3x asphalt upfront but lasts 2–3x as long. Lifetime cost is similar; metal wins on energy efficiency (10–25% lower cooling bills), insurance discounts in hail country, and resale value. Best ROI in hot or hail-prone climates.
How long does roof installation take?
A typical asphalt shingle replacement takes 1–3 days for a 2-person crew. Metal roofs take 3–7 days. Tile or slate: 1–3 weeks. Weather delays are common — budget extra time during rainy or hot seasons.
