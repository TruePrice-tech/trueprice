Plumbing questions for Roswell homeowners

Why does my water heater make popping noises?

Popping or rumbling sounds come from sediment buildup at the bottom of the tank. Flushing the tank annually prevents this and extends the heater's lifespan by 2-3 years.

How do I know if I have a slab leak?

Signs include unexplained spikes in your water bill, warm spots on the floor, the sound of running water when fixtures are off, and cracks in the foundation or damp baseboards.

What is a backflow preventer and do I need one?

A backflow preventer stops contaminated water from flowing back into the clean supply. Most municipalities require them on irrigation systems and commercial properties. Some require annual testing.

How often should I have my plumbing inspected in Roswell?

A yearly plumbing check in Roswell prevents the kind of surprises that cost thousands. The priority items: water heater (8-12 year lifespan, check anode rod and sediment), braided steel supply lines under sinks (replace after 10 years regardless of appearance), and main sewer line condition. Camera sewer inspections ($150-$400) every 3-5 years are standard practice for older homes.

What should a plumbing quote include in Roswell?

A professional plumbing quote in Roswell should itemize: labor hours, material costs and brands, permit fees if applicable, cleanup, and warranty terms. The quote should include the plumber's license number and proof of insurance. Get three written quotes for any job over $500 to ensure fair pricing.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Roswell?

Roswell homeowners should target fall or winter for planned plumbing work. Demand drops after summer's peak water-heater season and before spring's outdoor plumbing projects, which translates to better availability and pricing. Emergency service runs 50-100% more than scheduled jobs, so proactive replacement of aging equipment is the financially smarter path.
