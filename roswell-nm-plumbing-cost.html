Frequently asked plumbing questions

What is a backflow preventer and do I need one?

A backflow preventer stops contaminated water from flowing back into the clean supply. Most municipalities require them on irrigation systems and commercial properties. Some require annual testing.

Do I need a plumbing permit in Roswell?

Most Roswell jurisdictions require permits for new lines, water heater installs, and sewer work. Minor repairs like faucet swaps and toilet replacements typically do not need permits.

How long do plumbing fixtures typically last?

Faucets last 15-20 years, toilets 25-50 years, water heaters 8-12 years (tank) or 15-20 years (tankless), and supply lines should be replaced every 20-25 years.

What is the difference between copper and PEX piping?

Copper lasts 50+ years, resists bacteria, and has proven durability. PEX costs 60% less, installs faster, resists freezing better, and requires fewer joints. Both are code-compliant.

Is trenchless sewer repair worth the extra cost?

Trenchless repair costs 20-40% more but avoids excavating your yard, driveway, or landscaping. It is faster and causes less property damage, often making it cheaper overall.

Does Roswell's hard water damage plumbing?

It does. The mineral content in Roswell's water supply leaves calcium and magnesium deposits inside pipes, tanks, and fixtures. Water heaters lose 20-30% efficiency as scale builds on heating elements. Fixture aerators clog and valves stick. A whole-house softener ($1,500-$3,000 installed) prevents the buildup. Descale your water heater yearly to extend its life by 3-5 years.

What should a plumbing quote include in Roswell?

A professional plumbing quote in Roswell should itemize: labor hours, material costs and brands, permit fees if applicable, cleanup, and warranty terms. The quote should include the plumber's license number and proof of insurance. Get three written quotes for any job over $500 to ensure fair pricing.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Roswell?

Roswell homeowners should target fall or spring for planned plumbing work. Moderate temperatures make excavation work easier and contractor schedules are less congested, which translates to better availability and pricing. Emergency service runs 50-100% more than scheduled jobs, so proactive replacement of aging equipment is the financially smarter path.
