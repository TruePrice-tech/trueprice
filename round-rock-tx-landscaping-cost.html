What Round Rock residents ask about landscaping

How much does tree removal cost?

Small trees (under 30 feet) cost $200-$500 to remove. Medium trees (30-60 feet) cost $500-$1,000. Large trees (60-100 feet) cost $1,000-$2,000+. Stump grinding adds $100-$400 per stump.

How much does outdoor lighting cost?

Professional landscape lighting costs $2,000-$5,000 for path lights, uplights, and accent lighting. LED fixtures cost more upfront but last 50,000+ hours. Solar options cost less but provide dimmer, less reliable light.

How much does a retaining wall cost?

Retaining walls cost $20-$50 per square face foot for concrete block, $25-$60 for natural stone, and $15-$30 for timber. A 50 linear foot wall at 3 feet tall runs $3,000-$9,000 installed.

Round Rock, TX: landscaping cost factors

Why pricing varies in Round Rock. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. For landscaping specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Round Rock at roughly 9% below the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Round Rock has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Round Rock's heat and humidity create both opportunities and challenges for landscaping. Native and drought-tolerant species (yaupon holly, muhly grass, lantana) thrive with minimal irrigation and cut water bills 40-60% compared to traditional lawns. Invasive species and aggressive growth mean maintenance budgets run 15-20% higher than dry-climate markets. A complete landscaping project in Round Rock typically breaks down as: plant material (30-40%), hardscape/materials (20-30%), labor (25-35%), and design (5-10%). For budget-conscious homeowners, phasing the project over 2-3 seasons lets plants establish naturally and reduces upfront costs.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most landscaping projects in Round Rock require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 109,821, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Round Rock's rapid recent growth has tightened the contractor market — expect longer lead times and less negotiating room than smaller markets.

What Round Rock, TX homeowners should know about landscaping

Home stock in Round Rock. Round Rock has an unusually young housing stock (average 18 years). Most homes are still running their original systems and components. landscaping demand skews toward upgrades (efficiency, smart controls) rather than full replacement — ask contractors about tune-ups and partial upgrades before committing to a replacement.

HOA & compliance. HOAs are common in Round Rock. For landscaping, factor in 2-4 weeks of HOA architectural review before starting, and collect pre-approval of materials in writing. HOA rejections after project start can be expensive.

Local market conditions. Round Rock's high growth rate means contractor demand consistently exceeds supply. Expect 3-6 week booking delays in season. Get on a contractor's calendar as soon as you know you need work — waiting rarely improves pricing here. Round Rock's large market supports full-service landscape design firms, installation-only crews, and maintenance companies. For projects over $8,000, a dedicated design firm with installation capability typically provides better results than splitting design and installation across separate companies.

Seasonal timing. Round Rock offers a longer landscaping season than cold-climate markets. The busiest period is March through June when spring planting demand peaks. Fall (October-December) is an excellent time for plantings, hardscape projects, and irrigation system installation at lower competition and often better pricing.

What drives cost. With Round Rock labor rates near the national median, the cost difference between a budget and premium full yard makeover comes down to materials and scope rather than labor premiums. Newer construction in Round Rock (averaging 18 years) means landscaping project projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay. Round Rock's rapid growth means contractors can be selective about which jobs they take. Off-season scheduling and flexible timelines give you better leverage on pricing than trying to rush a project during peak demand.

Red flags to watch for. Be cautious of landscaping project contractors in Round Rock who pressure you to sign same-day. Legitimate contractors expect you to get competing bids and will hold their price for 30 days. High-pressure sales tactics correlate with inflated pricing. Any Round Rock contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion. In fast-growing Round Rock, some contractors take on more work than they can handle. Ask about their current project count — a reputable landscaping project contractor runs 2-4 jobs simultaneously, not 10-15.
