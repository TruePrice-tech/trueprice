Healthcare in Sacramento, CA: what locals should know

Hospital landscape

Sacramento is served by multiple hospital systems competing for patients. Understanding which hospitals are in your insurance network before an emergency can save you thousands in out-of-network charges.

Insurance coverage

Check your plan's network carefully. In Sacramento, the difference between in-network and out-of-network charges for the same procedure can be 3-5x.

Negotiation leverage

Every Sacramento hospital has a financial assistance program. Ask for the self-pay rate before accepting any bill at face value. Most Sacramento hospitals offer 20-40% prompt-pay discounts.

Neighborhood access

East Sacramento, Land Park, Midtown residents have access to community health centers with sliding-fee scales for primary care, often at a fraction of ER costs.

Sacramento Hospital Systems and Safety-Net Providers

Sutter Health, UC Davis Health, Dignity Health (CommonSpirit), and Kaiser Permanente serve the Sacramento metro. UC Davis Medical Center is the Level I trauma center and academic anchor. Sutter Medical Center Sacramento is the largest private hospital. There is no county-owned safety-net hospital; Sacramento County contracts with UC Davis and community health centers for indigent care.

Sacramento County's uninsured rate is approximately 7%, near the California average, driven by Medi-Cal expansion and Covered California. The state's 2024 expansion of full-scope Medi-Cal to all income-eligible adults regardless of immigration status has further reduced the rate.

Average Medical Procedure Costs in Sacramento

An ER visit at UC Davis Medical Center averages $2,400-$4,000, while Sutter's ER charges $2,000-$3,500 for comparable acuity.
