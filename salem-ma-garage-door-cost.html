Salem garage door FAQ

How long does garage door installation take?

A standard single or double door replacement takes 3-5 hours. Custom doors or jobs requiring structural framing changes take 1-2 days. Most installations are completed in a single visit.

How much does a garage door replacement cost in Salem?

A new garage door in Salem costs $800-$4,000 for a standard door installed, or $2,500-$8,000+ for a premium insulated or carriage-style door with a new opener.

What R-value should my garage door have?

R-8 is fine if the garage is detached or unheated. R-12 to R-16 is best for attached garages or if you use the space for a workshop. R-18+ is for extreme cold climates or heated garages.

Are smart garage door openers worth it?

Smart openers add $100-$200 over basic models. They let you open, close, and monitor the door from your phone, set schedules, and receive alerts. Useful for deliveries and forgetting to close.

How much does garage door spring replacement cost?

Torsion spring replacement costs $200-$400 for parts and labor. Extension springs cost $150-$300. Never attempt spring replacement yourself since the stored tension is extremely dangerous.

What R-value garage door do I need in Salem?

Salem's winters demand serious insulation. An R-12 to R-16 door is the baseline for attached garages, with R-16 to R-18 recommended if you use the space as a workshop or heat it. The $200-$500 upgrade cost pays back through lower heating bills in rooms adjacent to the garage within 3-5 years.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Salem?

Given that Salem homes average 56 years old, many original garage doors are due for replacement rather than repair. A new insulated door and modern opener runs $1,200-$3,000 and recoups 90-100% at resale. Repairs make financial sense for doors under 15 years old with specific damage, but throwing money at an aging system usually delays an inevitable replacement.

What's the best season to replace a garage door in Salem?

Salem homeowners get the best pricing and selection by scheduling garage door replacement during late spring or early fall. Moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing, and you can save 5-10% versus peak season pricing. Spring demand surges from cold-weather spring failures, so plan ahead to avoid the rush.
