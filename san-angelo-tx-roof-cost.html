Frequently asked roofing questions

What time of year is cheapest for roofing in San Angelo?

Late fall and winter are typically slower seasons in San Angelo, so contractors may offer better pricing. Spring and summer are peak demand, meaning longer waits and higher labor costs.

Why do roofing quotes vary so much?

Bid-to-bid variation comes down to material grade, labor assumptions, flashing work, ventilation upgrades, and warranty tier. Lower bids often exclude items that appear as change orders later.

How do I know if my roof deck needs replacing?

Signs include sagging between rafters, soft or spongy spots when walked on, visible water stains from the attic, and rotted or delaminating plywood. Your roofer checks this during tear-off.

Do I need impact-resistant shingles in San Angelo?

Impact-resistant shingles should be high on your list for San Angelo. This area sees enough hail activity that standard architectural shingles frequently sustain damage requiring premature replacement. The 10-20% price premium for Class 4 IR-rated shingles is offset by insurance premium reductions of 15-25% in most TX markets, making them the financially smarter choice over the life of the roof.

Is a San Angelo permit needed to replace my roof?

Yes, San Angelo generally requires a building permit for full roof replacements. Your roofer should pull this as part of the job. Without a permit, your insurance coverage may be compromised and you face disclosure obligations if you sell the home.

When should I replace a roof in San Angelo?

San Angelo homeowners should target fall or early spring for roof replacement work. Summer surface temperatures make installation difficult and can affect adhesive curing, giving you the best combination of weather conditions and contractor pricing. Off-season scheduling typically saves 10-15% compared to peak demand periods, especially the weeks immediately following severe weather events.
