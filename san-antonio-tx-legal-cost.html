Legal costs in San Antonio, TX: what locals should know

Rate landscape

Attorney rates in San Antonio vary significantly by practice area and firm size. Solo practitioners and small firms typically charge 30-50% less than large firms for comparable work.

Fee structures

Many San Antonio attorneys offer flat fees for routine matters like uncontested divorces, wills, and LLC formations. Always ask about flat-fee options before accepting hourly billing.

Free legal help

San Antonio has multiple legal aid organizations offering free services for qualifying residents. Bar association referral services provide low-cost initial consultations.

Court access

Alamo Heights, Stone Oak, Olmos Park residents can access small claims court for disputes without hiring an attorney, saving thousands in legal fees for straightforward claims.

Attorney hourly rates by practice area: a San Antonio guide

Attorney hourly rates in San Antonio range from $200-$400 for general practice, $300-$550 for Alamo Heights and downtown litigation firms, $175-$350 for family law, and $150-$300 for immigration. Military law practitioners near Joint Base San Antonio bill $200-$450. San Antonio rates run 15-25% below Dallas and Houston due to lower overhead and a different competitive dynamic driven by the military and government sectors.

Most Common Legal Disputes across San Antonio

The most common legal disputes in San Antonio are family law (custody, divorce, military divorce under the SCRA), immigration (asylum, adjustment of status, DACA, removal defense through RAICES and Catholic Charities), personal injury (auto accidents on I-35/I-10/Loop 410), landlord-tenant matters (eviction defense, security deposit claims), and military-related legal matters including veterans' benefits appeals.

Bexar County's court system operates 30+ district courts. The civil backlog averages 12-16 months to trial. Family law cases average 8-12 months for contested custody, with military deployment complications adding complexity. Justice Courts move at 30-60 days for contested small claims. The Paul Elizondo Tower houses most civil courts, and the county has added judicial seats to address population growth.
