Frequently asked solar questions

What happens to solar panels during a power outage?

Grid-tied systems without batteries shut off during outages for safety (anti-islanding). A battery backup or hybrid inverter lets you use solar power during grid failures.

How long do solar panels last?

Modern panels are warrantied for 25-30 years and continue producing at reduced efficiency beyond that. Inverters last 10-15 years (string) or 25 years (microinverters) and are the main replacement cost.

Can I add a battery to my solar system?

Yes. Adding a home battery (10-13 kWh) costs $8,000-$15,000 installed. Batteries provide backup power during outages and can help avoid peak-rate charges in time-of-use markets.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

San Antonio Utility Rate and Net Metering Structure

CPS Energy is a municipal utility, which means San Antonio is NOT in the deregulated ERCOT retail market despite being in Texas. CPS Energy's SolarHost buyback program credits exported energy at a specific rate (approximately $0.07-$0.09 per kWh) against the retail rate of $0.11-$0.13. This is more favorable than most deregulated ERCOT REP solar buyback plans. CPS Energy sets its own rates through the CPS Energy Board of Trustees rather than through PUC retail competition. Sizing solar to 90-95 percent of annual consumption optimizes the SolarHost economics because exports earn less than retail.

San Antonio Solar Incentives and Tax Credits

The 30 percent federal ITC (Section 25D) is the anchor incentive. Texas has no state solar tax credit. Texas Tax Code Section 11.27 exempts solar from residential property tax. Texas Tax Code 151.355 exempts solar equipment from state sales tax. CPS Energy's SolarHost program provides the most structured municipal solar buyback in Texas, more favorable than deregulated REP offerings. No CPS Energy direct rebate exists but the buyback rate and property tax exemption improve payback significantly. Federal ITC reduces a $20,000 system to approximately $14,000 effective cost.
