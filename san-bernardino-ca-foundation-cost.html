Questions San Bernardino homeowners ask about foundation repair

Should I buy a house with foundation issues?

It depends on severity and cost. Minor cracks are normal settling. Structural issues that need pier work ($10K-$30K+) should be factored into your offer. Always get an independent structural engineer's assessment, not just a repair company's quote.

What is a foundation inspection?

A structural engineer inspects visible cracks, measures floor levelness, checks doors and windows for alignment, assesses exterior grading and drainage, and looks for signs of water intrusion. Cost: $300-$700 for an independent engineer's report.

What causes foundation problems?

The most common cause is soil movement from moisture changes. Expansive clay swells when wet and shrinks when dry, creating cyclical pressure. Poor drainage, plumbing leaks, tree roots, and inadequate compaction during construction also contribute.

About foundation repair in San Bernardino, CA

Where San Bernardino's pricing comes from. At 17% above national average labor rates, San Bernardino's contractor pricing starts from a higher baseline than most US markets. The gap reflects both local wages and regional demand for skilled trades. On foundation repair, San Bernardino runs about 13% above the US average based on our pricing data across metro areas.

Local conditions in San Bernardino. San Bernardino has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Expansive clay soils in San Bernardino cause more foundation damage than any other factor. During droughts, soil shrinks and pulls away from foundations, causing settlement. During rain events, it swells back and pushes against walls. Regular foundation watering during dry months prevents the worst movement — a soaker hose on a timer costs $50 and prevents $10,000+ in repairs. Foundation repair methods in San Bernardino fall into three tiers: cosmetic ($500-2,000 for crack sealing and waterproofing), structural ($5,000-15,000 for piering, wall anchors, and drainage), and major ($15,000-40,000+ for underpinning and total reconstruction). Get a structural engineer's assessment ($300-600) before committing to any contractor's recommended scope.

San Bernardino permits and code requirements. Permits are usually required for foundation repair in San Bernardino; verify the specifics with the local building department before committing.

The San Bernardino contractor pool. With a population of 213,708, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. The San Bernardino market has grown at a steady pace, producing a solid base of experienced contractors without the severe backlogs of booming cities.
