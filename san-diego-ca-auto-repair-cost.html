San Diego Vehicle Inspections and Warranty Protections

San Diego County participates in the statewide biennial smog-check program. Vehicles over 8 model years old must pass an OBD-II emissions scan at a BAR-certified station every two years. Fees run from $29.95 at dedicated test-only stations to $70 at full-service repair facilities. No companion safety inspection exists in California. Vehicle owners who fail the smog check can apply for the state CAP program, which reimburses up to $1,200 in qualifying emissions repairs or offers a $1,500 vehicle-retirement incentive.

California's Song-Beverly Act provides Lemon Law coverage for new vehicles with persistent defects within 18 months or 18,000 miles. San Diego-area consumers also benefit from the Automotive Repair Act, which obligates every BAR-licensed shop to furnish a written estimate, secure customer authorization before commencing work, and return replaced parts upon request. The BAR publishes enforcement actions and shop-level complaint histories searchable by license number, which is a valuable vetting tool for San Diego residents choosing between the metro's many independent operators.
