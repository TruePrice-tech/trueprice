Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a concrete driveway cost in San Diego?

Most homeowners in San Diego pay between $5,350 and $11,400 for a concrete driveway, depending on size and finish type.

Is stamped concrete worth the extra cost?

Stamped concrete costs 30-50% more than plain concrete but can mimic the look of pavers, brick, or natural stone at a fraction of the cost. It adds significant curb appeal and value.

How long does a concrete driveway last?

A properly installed and sealed concrete driveway typically lasts 25-30 years. Regular sealing every 2-3 years and prompt crack repair can extend its lifespan further.

About concrete work in San Diego, CA

Why pricing varies in San Diego. Labor rates here run roughly 24% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For concrete work specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts San Diego at roughly 21% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. San Diego has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Hot-weather concrete placement requires retarders and curing compounds; pours after 10am in summer fail without them.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in San Diego require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. As a major metro of 1,355,896 residents, the local contractor market is deep — expect 10+ legitimate bidders on any standard residential job. San Diego has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.
