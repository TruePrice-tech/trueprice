Questions San Diego homeowners ask about foundation repair

How do I prevent foundation problems?

Maintain consistent soil moisture around the foundation, ensure gutters and downspouts direct water 6+ feet away, slope grading away from the house, fix plumbing leaks promptly, and keep trees at least their mature height's distance from the foundation.

Do I need a permit for foundation repair in San Diego?

Most San Diego jurisdictions require permits for structural foundation work like pier installation, underpinning, and wall reinforcement. Minor crack sealing typically does not need a permit.

Can I fix foundation cracks myself?

Hairline cracks under 1/8 inch can be sealed with hydraulic cement or epoxy kits ($20-$50 DIY). Any crack wider than 1/4 inch, growing over time, or accompanied by wall movement needs professional structural assessment.

Local conditions in San Diego. San Diego has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Expansive clay soils in San Diego cause more foundation damage than any other factor. During droughts, soil shrinks and pulls away from foundations, causing settlement. During rain events, it swells back and pushes against walls. Regular foundation watering during dry months prevents the worst movement — a soaker hose on a timer costs $50 and prevents $10,000+ in repairs. Foundation repair methods in San Diego fall into three tiers: cosmetic ($500-2,000 for crack sealing and waterproofing), structural ($5,000-15,000 for piering, wall anchors, and drainage), and major ($15,000-40,000+ for underpinning and total reconstruction).
