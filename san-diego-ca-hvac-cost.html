What SEER and AFUE to Aim For in San Diego

Cooling efficiency target

16+ SEER

16 SEER is the sensible San Diego minimum because heating, not cooling, dominates your San Diego Gas & Electric (SDG&E) bill. The 14-to-16 SEER jump delivers modest savings here; the 16-to-20 jump delivers almost none. Put the efficiency budget toward the furnace or a cold-climate heat pump instead.

Heating efficiency target

80%+ AFUE

San Diego's minimal heating load means an 80% AFUE furnace is fine if you even keep gas. Most San Diego homeowners are better served by going all-electric with a high-SEER heat pump and skipping the gas furnace, water heater, and PGW-equivalent bill altogether. Do the math on San Diego Gas & Electric (SDG&E)'s electric rate before locking in dual-fuel.

San Diego 2026 efficiency minimums. Federal floor for CA is 15 SEER2 (roughly 15.2 legacy SEER) under the DOE southern-region rule — any San Diego bid under that is illegal for new residential install. Any "14 SEER" number on a San Diego 2026 bid is either left-over old inventory or a labeling mix-up between SEER2 and legacy SEER; the AHRI listing at ahridirectory.org is the authoritative source.
