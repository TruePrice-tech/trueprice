Healthcare in San Diego, CA: what locals should know

Hospital landscape

San Diego is served by multiple hospital systems competing for patients. Understanding which hospitals are in your insurance network before an emergency can save you thousands in out-of-network charges.

Insurance coverage

Check your plan's network carefully. In San Diego, the difference between in-network and out-of-network charges for the same procedure can be 3-5x.

Negotiation leverage

Every San Diego hospital has a financial assistance program. Ask for the self-pay rate before accepting any bill at face value. Most San Diego hospitals offer 20-40% prompt-pay discounts.

Neighborhood access

La Jolla, North Park, Hillcrest residents have access to community health centers with sliding-fee scales for primary care, often at a fraction of ER costs.

San Diego Hospital Systems and Safety-Net Providers

Scripps Health, Sharp HealthCare, UC San Diego Health, and Kaiser Permanente serve the San Diego market. Rady Children's Hospital is the regional pediatric facility. UC San Diego Health is the academic anchor, and there is no single county-owned safety-net hospital. Instead, San Diego County contracts with multiple systems and operates Family Health Centers of San Diego as the primary FQHC network for uninsured residents.

San Diego County's uninsured rate is approximately 7.5%, slightly above the California average, driven by the military-connected population transitioning between TRICARE and civilian coverage and the border-adjacent immigrant community. Covered California and Medi-Cal expansion have driven significant coverage gains since 2014.

Average Medical Procedure Costs in San Diego

An ER visit at Scripps La Jolla averages $2,400-$4,200, while UC San Diego's ER charges $2,000-$3,500 for comparable acuity. San Diego Balance Billing Protections and Patient Rights

California's AB 72 (2017) provides comprehensive balance billing protections, setting payment at the greater of 125% of Medicare or the insurer's average contracted rate. The law covers emergency and non-emergency out-of-network care at in-network facilities. California's DMHC and CDI enforce protections. The federal No Surprises Act covers ERISA plans not reached by state law.

California expanded Medi-Cal under the ACA and covers all income-eligible adults regardless of immigration status as of 2024. San Diego County's Medi-Cal enrollment exceeds 800,000 residents. The county's Health and Human Services Agency processes Medi-Cal applications and manages enrollment through contracted managed care plans including Community Health Group and Molina Healthcare.

The effective negotiation tactic in San Diego is to use California's Hospital Fair Pricing Act thresholds as the starting point and compare quotes across the Scripps, Sharp, and UC San Diego systems.

San Diego Financial Assistance and Charity Care Programs

California's Hospital Fair Pricing Act requires free care for patients under 200% FPL and discounts to 350% FPL. Scripps Health's charity care is among the most generous in the county. Sharp HealthCare's financial assistance covers facility charges with 30-day application processing. UC San Diego Health covers both facility and physician charges. Family Health Centers of San Diego provides comprehensive care on a sliding scale.
