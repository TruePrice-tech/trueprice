San Francisco fencing: answers to common questions

Do I need a permit to build a fence in San Francisco?

Most San Francisco jurisdictions require a permit for fences over 6 feet tall. Many require permits for any fence. You also need to check setback requirements, HOA rules, and call 811 for utility locates before digging.

Does a fence increase property value?

A well-built fence adds 1-5% to property value depending on material and neighborhood norms. Privacy fencing adds more value in neighborhoods where it is expected. Chain link adds minimal value.

How long does a wood fence last?

Pressure-treated pine lasts 15-20 years. Cedar lasts 15-25 years. Redwood lasts 20-30 years. All wood fences last longer with stain or sealant applied every 2-3 years.

Why pricing varies in San Francisco. For fence installation specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts San Francisco at roughly 39% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. San Francisco has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Constant moisture in San Francisco rots untreated wood fencing within 5-8 years. HOA-governed communities in San Francisco frequently dictate fence height, style, and material. Expect fence installation quotes to include mentions of upgraded electrical service, ductwork adjustments, or insulation retrofits that newer homes wouldn't need.

HOA & compliance. HOAs are common in San Francisco. For fence installation, factor in 2-4 weeks of HOA architectural review before starting, and collect pre-approval of materials in writing. HOA rejections after project start can be expensive.

Local market conditions. San Francisco has moderate construction activity. Contractors are busy but accessible with 1-3 weeks' notice. Off-season quotes (contractor-dependent) can run 8-15% below peak rates. San Francisco's large market supports both dedicated fence companies and general contractors who do fencing. Dedicated fence installers typically quote 15-25% less because they buy materials in bulk and run crews that do nothing but fence work.

Seasonal timing. In San Francisco, fence contractors are busiest March through June when homeowners want fencing before summer. Fall (September-November) offers better availability and occasionally lower prices as outdoor project demand fades.

Neighborhood patterns. In San Francisco, the highest volume of fence installation work tends to come out of Noe Valley, Mission, Sunset, and Richmond — larger lots, older homes, and higher home values all drive replacement demand. Contractors familiar with these neighborhoods often already know the typical building stock, permit nuances, and HOA quirks.
