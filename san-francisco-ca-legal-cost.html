Legal costs in San Francisco, CA: what locals should know

Rate landscape

Attorney rates in San Francisco vary significantly by practice area and firm size. Solo practitioners and small firms typically charge 30-50% less than large firms for comparable work.

Fee structures

Many San Francisco attorneys offer flat fees for routine matters like uncontested divorces, wills, and LLC formations. Always ask about flat-fee options before accepting hourly billing.

Free legal help

San Francisco has multiple legal aid organizations offering free services for qualifying residents. Bar association referral services provide low-cost initial consultations.

Court access

Noe Valley, Mission, Sunset residents can access small claims court for disputes without hiring an attorney, saving thousands in legal fees for straightforward claims.

San Francisco Attorney Hourly Rates by Practice Area

Attorney hourly rates in San Francisco range from $350-$700 for general practice, $600-$1,200+ for Financial District and SoMa litigation firms, $300-$600 for family law, and $250-$500 for immigration. San Francisco is the second most expensive legal market in the country after New York. Technology IP and venture-capital attorneys command the highest rates, often exceeding $1,000/hour at major firms.

Flat fees in San Francisco are standard for uncontested divorces ($2,000-$5,000), simple trusts ($1,000-$4,000 for a basic living trust), LLC formations ($750-$2,000), and tech startup incorporation ($3,000-$8,000 including equity plans). California does not require attorney involvement in real estate closings. Rent Board petitions and tenant protection matters are commonly flat-fee given the city's rent control framework.

San Francisco Bar Associations and Lawyer Referral Services

The State Bar of California is the mandatory licensing body. The Bar Association of San Francisco (BASF) provides lawyer referral services, CLE programs, and pro bono coordination. The Charles Houston Bar Association (historically Black bar), the Asian American Bar Association of the Greater Bay Area, and the Bay Area Lawyers for Individual Freedom (LGBTQ+ bar) provide community services.

BASF's Volunteer Legal Services Program coordinates pro bono placement. Justice & Diversity Center of the Bar Association of San Francisco manages volunteer attorneys. The Bay Area's concentration of major law firms creates one of the deepest pro bono resource pools in the country. Firms including Morrison & Foerster, Cooley, and Fenwick & West maintain extensive pro bono programs.

San Francisco Contingency Fees and Fee Structures

Court Filing Costs and Small Claims in San Francisco

San Francisco Superior Court civil filing fees start at $435 for unlimited civil cases (over $25,000) and $75 for limited cases. Small Claims Court filing fees run $30-$75. Family law petition filing costs $435. Probate filing costs $435-$470. Unlawful detainer filing costs $240-$435. SF's fee schedule mirrors the statewide California Superior Court schedule.

California Small Claims Court handles claims up to $10,000 for individuals and $5,000 for businesses. San Francisco Superior Court hears Small Claims at the Civic Center Courthouse. Filing fees run $30-$75. Attorneys are not permitted. Appeals go to Superior Court for trial de novo with attorneys allowed. The process runs 30-60 days from filing to hearing.

Mediation and Arbitration Options in San Francisco

JAMS San Francisco and AAA operate major arbitration and mediation centers in the Financial District. The San Francisco Bar Association's Lawyer Referral Service includes a mediation panel. Community Boards provides free community mediation in SF neighborhoods. The SF Superior Court's ADR program offers mediation for qualifying civil cases.

Mediation and arbitration typically resolve San Francisco disputes faster and at lower cost than full litigation. Ask any San Francisco attorney whether alternative dispute resolution is appropriate for your case before committing to a courtroom timeline.

Most Common Legal Disputes in San Francisco

The most common legal disputes in San Francisco are landlord-tenant matters (eviction defense under SF Rent Ordinance, Ellis Act evictions, tenant buyout negotiations), employment law (wage theft, discrimination, wrongful termination under FEHA), personal injury (auto accidents, bicycle/pedestrian injuries), family law (custody, divorce), and immigration (asylum, DACA, TPS).

San Francisco Superior Court's civil backlog averages 14-22 months to trial for unlimited civil cases. Family law cases average 10-14 months for contested custody. The court's relatively small size (compared to LA or Cook County) means individual judge assignments significantly affect case pace. Small Claims moves at 30-60 days. Unlawful detainer (eviction) cases have expedited timelines of 21-60 days.
