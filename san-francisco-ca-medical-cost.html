Healthcare in San Francisco, CA: what locals should know

Hospital landscape

San Francisco is served by multiple hospital systems competing for patients. Understanding which hospitals are in your insurance network before an emergency can save you thousands in out-of-network charges.

Insurance coverage

Check your plan's network carefully. In San Francisco, the difference between in-network and out-of-network charges for the same procedure can be 3-5x.

Negotiation leverage

Every San Francisco hospital has a financial assistance program. Ask for the self-pay rate before accepting any bill at face value. Most San Francisco hospitals offer 20-40% prompt-pay discounts.

Neighborhood access

Noe Valley, Mission, Sunset residents have access to community health centers with sliding-fee scales for primary care, often at a fraction of ER costs.

Hospital systems and safety-net providers throughout San Francisco

UCSF Health, Dignity Health (CommonSpirit), Sutter Health (CPMC), and Kaiser Permanente are the dominant systems. Zuckerberg San Francisco General Hospital (ZSFG) is the city-owned Level I trauma center managed through the SF Department of Public Health, serving as the primary safety net for uninsured San Franciscans. UCSF Medical Center at Mission Bay is the academic anchor.

San Francisco's uninsured rate is approximately 3.5%, among the lowest of any major US city, driven by Medi-Cal expansion, Covered California, and the city's own Healthy San Francisco program that covers residents regardless of immigration status. The city's universal coverage approach predates the ACA and continues to fill remaining gaps.

Average Medical Procedure Costs: a San Francisco breakdown

An ER visit at UCSF Medical Center averages $3,000-$5,200, while ZSFG's ER charges $1,200-$2,400 for comparable acuity. MRI at Sutter CPMC runs $1,500-$3,000; freestanding imaging centers in Daly City and San Bruno offer the same scan for $400-$800. Appendectomy at UCSF ranges from $18,000 to $38,000 before insurance negotiated rates, reflecting Bay Area labor costs.

UCSF Health publishes comprehensive CMS-mandated price transparency files. ZSFG publishes a navigable sliding-fee schedule. California's Office of Health Care Affordability publishes cost benchmarks by region, and the Bay Area's high-cost status is documented separately. Sutter Health's transparency data has been scrutinized after the state Attorney General's 2019 antitrust settlement.

NEMS (North East Medical Services) provides culturally competent care in Chinatown. Lyon-Martin Health Services specializes in care for LGBTQ+ patients. All operate on sliding-fee scales.

Balance billing protections and patient rights within San Francisco

California's AB 72 (2017) provides comprehensive balance billing protections, setting payment at the greater of 125% of Medicare or the insurer's average contracted rate. The law covers emergency and non-emergency out-of-network care at in-network facilities. California's Department of Managed Health Care and Department of Insurance jointly enforce the protections. The federal No Surprises Act covers ERISA plans.

California expanded Medi-Cal under the ACA and covers all income-eligible adults regardless of immigration status as of 2024. The SF DPH enrollment team actively seeks out uninsured residents through community outreach.

Financial assistance and charity care programs within San Francisco
