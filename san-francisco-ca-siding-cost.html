What San Francisco residents ask about siding

Do I need a permit for siding replacement in San Francisco?

Most San Francisco jurisdictions require a permit if you are changing siding material or adding insulation. Like-for-like replacement without structural changes may be exempt. Check with your local building department.

What is board-and-batten siding?

Board-and-batten uses wide vertical boards with narrow strips (battens) covering the seams. It is popular in farmhouse and modern styles. Costs $8-$16/sqft installed in fiber cement or engineered wood.

Can new siding go over old siding?

Overlaying is possible but not recommended. It hides potential water damage, adds weight, and can void warranties. Tear-off adds $1,000-$3,000 but lets you inspect and repair sheathing and moisture barriers.

Should I paint or replace my siding?

Paint if siding is structurally sound with no rot or warping. Painting costs $3,000-$7,000 and lasts 5-10 years. If paint peels repeatedly or siding has damage, replacement is the better long-term investment.
