San Jose Vehicle Inspections and Warranty Protections

California requires biennial smog checks for vehicles six model years old and newer (change-of-ownership exemption for vehicles four years old or newer). The BAR (Bureau of Automotive Repair) licenses all smog-check stations in San Jose. Test-only stations handle vehicles directed for a retest or flagged by DMV. The standard smog-check fee runs $50-$70 in San Jose. No separate safety inspection exists in California; the smog check is the sole periodic state requirement.

California's Song-Beverly Consumer Warranty Act (the state Lemon Law) is one of the strongest in the nation, covering new vehicles with substantial defects that the manufacturer cannot fix after a reasonable number of attempts. The Tanner Consumer Protection Act provides additional procedural framework. The BAR's Automotive Repair Act requires all California shops to provide written estimates before work begins and to obtain customer authorization for any amount exceeding the estimate.
