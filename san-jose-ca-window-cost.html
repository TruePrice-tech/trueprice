Window Replacement questions for San Jose homeowners

Do I need a permit for window replacement in San Jose?

In San Jose, permits are typically required when changing window size or adding new openings. Same-size replacements usually do not need permits, but check your local building department.

What U-factor should I look for?

Lower U-factor means better insulation. Aim for 0.25-0.30 in cold climates and 0.30-0.40 in mild climates. Energy Star requirements vary by region.

What is Low-E glass?

Low-E (low emissivity) coating reflects infrared heat while allowing visible light through. It reduces heat gain in summer and heat loss in winter, cutting energy costs 10-25%.

Energy performance spec for San Jose

The tech-affluent homeowner base increasingly requests triple-pane with dynamic glass (View, SageGlass) for automated tint control on west exposures, a premium specification ($200-$400 per window premium) that few US markets request as a standard consideration.

At PG&E/SJCE's $0.30+/kWh rate (among the highest residential rates in the US), window energy performance produces faster payback than almost any other market: a full-house window upgrade from single-pane to double-pane Low-E produces $400-$800 in annual energy savings on a typical 1960s-70s ranch house. The NEM 3.0 solar economics make window efficiency complementary to solar: reducing cooling load through better windows reduces the solar system size needed to achieve target consumption offset, saving $2,000-$5,000 on the solar investment. This window-solar interaction is a San Jose-specific economic consideration that window companies in non-solar markets rarely address.

San Jose condensation and interior moisture management

San Jose's mild climate produces minimal condensation compared to cold markets like Baltimore or Minneapolis. The marine-layer moisture during coastal-fog intrusion events can produce brief exterior condensation on high-performance Low-E glass (the glass surface stays cold enough to reach dew point during humid mornings), which is a sign the windows are performing well rather than a defect. Interior condensation is rare except on original single-pane windows during the handful of cold-night events each winter. The primary moisture-management concern is at the window-to-stucco interface where differential expansion can allow rain intrusion during the November-March wet season.

Wind, storm, and durability requirements for San Jose
