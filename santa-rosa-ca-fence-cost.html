Frequently asked fencing questions

Who owns the fence between two properties?

Typically the homeowner who installed it. If the fence sits exactly on the property line, both neighbors share responsibility. Always get a survey before building to confirm the boundary.

What is the difference between dog-ear and flat-top fence?

Dog-ear pickets have angled cuts at the top for water runoff and a traditional look. Flat-top pickets have a clean, modern appearance but hold slightly more water, which can accelerate rot.

Are permits required to build a fence in Santa Rosa?

Most Santa Rosa jurisdictions require a permit for fences over 6 feet tall. Many require permits for any fence. You also need to check setback requirements, HOA rules, and call 811 for utility locates before digging.

Does a fence increase property value?

A well-built fence adds 1-5% to property value depending on material and neighborhood norms. Privacy fencing adds more value in neighborhoods where it is expected. Chain link adds minimal value.

What fence types does my Santa Rosa HOA allow?

Every Santa Rosa HOA has its own fence rules, but typical restrictions include height limits (6 feet backyard, 4 feet front), material restrictions (no chain link is common), color requirements (earth tones or white), and mandatory outward-facing finished sides. You will usually need architectural review board approval before installation begins. Get the current guidelines from your HOA management company and submit your design before committing to materials.

Are permits required to build a fence in Santa Rosa?

Check with Santa Rosa's building department, but permits are generally required for fences, especially those above 6 feet. Before digging post holes, you need utility locates (call 811) and confirmed property lines. If boundaries are uncertain, a survey ($300-$600) prevents expensive neighbor disputes. Your contractor should pull the permit.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Santa Rosa?

In Santa Rosa, budget $15-$30 per linear foot for wood privacy fencing, $20-$40 for vinyl, $10-$20 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron. A 150 linear foot backyard project totals $2,250-$6,000 depending on material. Collect 3 written quotes scoped identically to compare apples to apples.
