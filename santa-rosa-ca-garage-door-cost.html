Garage Door questions for Santa Rosa homeowners

How much does garage door spring replacement cost?

Torsion spring replacement costs $200-$400 for parts and labor. Extension springs cost $150-$300. Never attempt spring replacement yourself since the stored tension is extremely dangerous.

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Santa Rosa?

Garage door replacement in Santa Rosa typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

How often should I maintain my garage door in Santa Rosa?

Twice-yearly lubrication of springs, hinges, rollers, and tracks with silicone spray keeps the mechanism running smoothly. Test the safety auto-reverse with a 2x4 under the door at least annually. Inspect weatherstripping for wear, and tighten all mounting hardware since door vibration loosens bolts gradually. Budget $100-$200 for a professional tune-up every 2-3 years.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Santa Rosa?

Given that Santa Rosa homes average 38 years old, many original garage doors are due for replacement rather than repair. A new insulated door and modern opener runs $1,200-$3,000 and recoups 90-100% at resale. Repairs make financial sense for doors under 15 years old with specific damage, but throwing money at an aging system usually delays an inevitable replacement.

When should I replace a garage door in Santa Rosa?

Santa Rosa homeowners get the best pricing and selection by scheduling garage door replacement during spring or early fall. Dry conditions for proper weatherstrip adhesion, and you can save 5-10% versus peak season pricing. Spring demand surges from cold-weather spring failures, so plan ahead to avoid the rush.
