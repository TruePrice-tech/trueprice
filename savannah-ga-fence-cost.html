Frequently asked fencing questions

When is the best time to install a fence?

Late spring through early fall offers the best conditions for concrete curing and working in the ground. Avoid frozen ground and extremely wet seasons. Many contractors offer off-season discounts.

How long does fence installation take?

A standard 150-200 linear foot residential fence takes 1-3 days to install. Hillside or rocky soil adds time. Post holes need 24-48 hours to cure if set in concrete.

How deep should fence posts be?

Standard rule: bury one-third of the total post length. For a 6-foot fence with 8-foot posts, dig 24-30 inches deep. In frost-prone areas, posts must go below the frost line to prevent heaving.

How much does vinyl fencing cost?

Vinyl privacy fencing costs $20-$40 per linear foot installed. A typical 150 linear foot backyard fence runs $3,000-$6,000. Vinyl never needs painting, resists rot, and lasts 25-30 years.

Can I install a fence on a slope?

Yes, using either stepped (stair-step) or racked (following the slope) installation. Racked looks smoother on gentle slopes. Stepped is better for steep grades and is structurally stronger.

What fence material lasts longest in Savannah's climate?

Vinyl and aluminum are the longevity champions in Savannah's humid conditions, outlasting wood by 10-15 years since they resist rot, termites, and moisture damage entirely. Wood fence lovers should choose pressure-treated pine or cedar and commit to staining or sealing every 2-3 years. Composite materials split the difference with wood aesthetics and near-vinyl durability.

Are permits required to build a fence in Savannah?

Check with Savannah's building department, but permits are generally required for fences, especially those above 6 feet. Before digging post holes, you need utility locates (call 811) and confirmed property lines. If boundaries are uncertain, a survey ($300-$600) prevents expensive neighbor disputes. Your contractor should pull the permit.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Savannah?

In Savannah, budget $15-$30 per linear foot for wood privacy fencing, $20-$40 for vinyl, $10-$20 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron. A 150 linear foot backyard project totals $2,250-$6,000 depending on material. Collect 3 written quotes scoped identically to compare apples to apples.

Local Fencing Guide for Savannah, GA

In Savannah, humidity and insects are the two failure modes that shorten fence life. Untreated lumber rots from ground contact within a few seasons, and termites work the back side of any wood fence that touches soil. Vinyl, aluminum, and pressure-treated composites all sidestep both problems.

Some Savannah neighborhoods have HOA fence restrictions. If your property is in a managed community, check the CC&Rs for height limits, material restrictions, and color requirements before committing to a design. Even without an HOA, most Savannah zoning codes restrict front-yard fence height to 3-4 feet.

As a hurricane-exposure area, Savannah homeowners should consider wind resistance when choosing a fence style. Solid privacy fences act as sails in high winds and are more likely to fail. Shadow-box or spaced-picket designs allow wind to pass through while still providing partial privacy. Post spacing of 6 feet (instead of 8) with deeper footings adds meaningful wind resistance.

Many Savannah properties (average home age 45 years) have aging or deteriorated fences that predate current building codes. Replacement often requires updated post depth, property line verification, and sometimes a survey to confirm boundaries before new fence construction begins. Budget $300-$600 for a property survey if boundary markers are not clearly visible.

Local factors: fence installation in Savannah, GA

The Savannah housing picture. Savannah's homes, averaging 45 years, are entering the window where original-equipment replacements become unavoidable. This is the sweet spot for fence installation work since systems are aging but the homes are modern enough to avoid major code-upgrade surprises.

Neighborhood rulebook. HOA rules affect some but not all Savannah homeowners. If applicable, confirm your HOA's requirements for fence installation before getting quotes. This avoids the situation where your preferred material or color gets rejected after you have already signed a contract.

Local market conditions. With steady but not explosive growth, Savannah's contractor market offers reasonable availability. Most shops can schedule work within 1-3 weeks, and shopping for quotes is straightforward. Savannah's large market supports both dedicated fence companies and general contractors who do fencing. Dedicated fence installers typically quote 15-25% less because they buy materials in bulk and run crews that do nothing but fence work.

When to schedule. In Savannah, fence contractors are busiest March through June when homeowners want fencing before summer. Fall (September-November) offers better availability and occasionally lower prices as outdoor project demand fades.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Savannah track within a few points of the national average for fence installation, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. At 45 years average home age, Savannah properties are hitting their first major replacement cycle for systems and components. fence installation demand is at peak levels in this age band, which keeps contractor schedules full but pricing competitive.

Red flags to watch for. Any Savannah contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag.
