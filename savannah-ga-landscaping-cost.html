About landscaping in Savannah, GA

Why pricing varies in Savannah. Labor rates here run roughly 9% below the national average per BLS metro data, which is the main reason this city's pricing sits below the national midpoint. For landscaping specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Savannah at roughly 12% below the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Savannah has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Savannah's heat and humidity create both opportunities and challenges for landscaping. Native and drought-tolerant species (yaupon holly, muhly grass, lantana) thrive with minimal irrigation and cut water bills 40-60% compared to traditional lawns. Invasive species and aggressive growth mean maintenance budgets run 15-20% higher than dry-climate markets. A complete landscaping project in Savannah typically breaks down as: plant material (30-40%), hardscape/materials (20-30%), labor (25-35%), and design (5-10%). For budget-conscious homeowners, phasing the project over 2-3 seasons lets plants establish naturally and reduces upfront costs.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most landscaping projects in Savannah require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 142,772, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Savannah has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.

Local factors: landscaping in Savannah, GA

Home stock in Savannah. With average home age near 45 years, Savannah homes are at the typical first-replacement milestone for major systems. Original furnaces, AC units, water heaters, and roofs installed when the homes were built are aging out — landscaping is hitting peak demand in this age band.

HOA & compliance. HOA prevalence in Savannah is moderate. If you're in a managed community, check covenants before signing a landscaping contract — material color, visible fixture type, or warranty requirements may affect your selection.

Local market conditions. Savannah has moderate construction activity. Contractors are busy but accessible with 1-3 weeks' notice. Off-season quotes (contractor-dependent) can run 8-15% below peak rates. Savannah's large market supports full-service landscape design firms, installation-only crews, and maintenance companies. For projects over $8,000, a dedicated design firm with installation capability typically provides better results than splitting design and installation across separate companies.

Seasonal timing. Savannah offers a longer landscaping season than cold-climate markets. The busiest period is March through June when spring planting demand peaks. Fall (October-December) is an excellent time for plantings, hardscape projects, and irrigation system installation at lower competition and often better pricing.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Savannah track within a few points of the national average for landscaping project, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. At 45 years average home age, Savannah properties are hitting their first major replacement cycle for systems and components. landscaping project demand is at peak levels in this age band, which keeps contractor schedules full but pricing competitive.

Red flags to watch for. In Savannah, verify your landscaping project contractor pulls the permit themselves — never pull it in your own name. If they ask you to pull the permit, they may not be properly licensed to do the work. Be cautious of landscaping project contractors in Savannah who pressure you to sign same-day. Legitimate contractors expect you to get competing bids and will hold their price for 30 days. High-pressure sales tactics correlate with inflated pricing.
