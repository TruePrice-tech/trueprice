What Scottsdale residents ask about concrete

How much does a concrete foundation cost?

A full basement foundation costs $15,000-$30,000. A slab foundation for a house costs $5,000-$15,000. A garage slab costs $2,500-$6,000. Price depends on size, depth, and soil conditions.

How long before I can drive on new concrete?

Wait 24-48 hours for foot traffic, 7 days for vehicle traffic, and 30 days for full cure. Avoid deicing salt for the first winter. New concrete reaches 90% strength in 7 days and full strength in 28 days.

What is the best time to pour concrete?

Pour when temperatures stay between 50-80F for at least 3 days. Spring and fall are ideal. Avoid pouring in rain, direct hot sun, or below freezing. Cold and heat both compromise cure quality.

How thick should a concrete driveway be?

Residential driveways should be 4 inches thick for cars and 5-6 inches for heavy vehicles like RVs. A 6-inch compacted gravel base underneath is essential. Thickened edges (6 inches) prevent cracking at the perimeter.

Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

Where Scottsdale's pricing comes from. Contractor labor rates in Scottsdale are roughly average for the US. On concrete work, Scottsdale's pricing closely mirrors the national average in our data.

Local conditions in Scottsdale. Scottsdale has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Hot-weather concrete placement in Scottsdale requires retarders and curing compounds — pours after 10am in summer months fail without them. Early morning pours (before 8am) are standard practice. The dry heat also requires aggressive curing: keep concrete moist for 7 days minimum to prevent surface cracking. Standard concrete in Scottsdale runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Scottsdale permits and code requirements. Permits are usually required for concrete work in Scottsdale; verify the specifics with the local building department before committing.

The Scottsdale contractor pool. With a population of 226,918, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. The Scottsdale area's booming growth means contractors have more work than they can handle. This shifts pricing power toward the contractor side of the table.
