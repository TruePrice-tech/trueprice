Frequently Asked Questions

How much does new siding cost in Scranton?

Most homeowners in Scranton pay between $6,000 and $20,950 for siding replacement, depending on material and home size.

Is fiber cement siding worth the extra cost?

Fiber cement (like James Hardie) costs more than vinyl but lasts 30-50 years, resists fire and termites, and adds more resale value. It is a popular mid-range upgrade.

How long does siding replacement take?

Most siding projects take 1-2 weeks depending on home size and material. Vinyl installs fastest, while fiber cement and wood take longer.

Local conditions. Scranton has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Freeze-thaw cycles in Scranton test siding at every seam and penetration. Water infiltrates behind panels, freezes, expands, and forces siding away from sheathing. Fiber cement and insulated vinyl handle this cycle best — standard vinyl can crack in deep cold, and wood expands and contracts enough to pop nails. Siding costs in Scranton vary dramatically by material: vinyl ($4-8/sqft installed), fiber cement ($8-14/sqft), engineered wood ($9-15/sqft), cedar ($10-18/sqft), and stone veneer ($15-30/sqft). For a 2,000 sqft exterior, that's $8,000-60,000 — material choice is the single biggest pricing decision.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most siding replacement projects in Scranton require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.
