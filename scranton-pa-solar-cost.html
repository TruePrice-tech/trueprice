Frequently asked solar questions

Can I add a battery to my solar system?

Yes. Adding a home battery (10-13 kWh) costs $8,000-$15,000 installed. Batteries provide backup power during outages and can help avoid peak-rate charges in time-of-use markets.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

Do solar panels increase home value?

Owned solar systems add roughly $15,000-$20,000 to home value according to national studies. Leased systems do not add value and can complicate the sale process.

Does solar work in Scranton's cold winters?

Solar works well in Scranton despite the cold. Panels convert sunlight more efficiently at lower temperatures, partially offsetting shorter winter days. Net metering lets you bank summer overproduction as credits for winter months. Snow sheds from angled panels within a day or two in most cases. Higher local electricity rates and available incentives often make the payback comparable to warmer markets.

What solar incentives are available in Scranton?

Every Scranton homeowner installing solar qualifies for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit with no dollar limit. Beyond that, PA may offer state tax credits, utility-specific rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits, and property tax exemptions. The DSIRE database has the current list for your area. These stacked incentives typically reduce net cost by an additional 10-30%.

What's the best season to install solar in Scranton?

Scranton homeowners should aim for late spring or summer to install solar. Weather cooperates for roof work and you maximize first-year production before winter. Allow 6-12 weeks from signing a contract through permitting, utility interconnection approval, and installation. Start gathering quotes 2-3 months before your target window. Remember that the 30% ITC applies to the tax year in which installation is completed.
