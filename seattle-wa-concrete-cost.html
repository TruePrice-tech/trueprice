Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a concrete driveway cost in Seattle?

Most homeowners in Seattle pay between $5,750 and $12,300 for a concrete driveway, depending on size and finish type.

Is stamped concrete worth the extra cost?

Stamped concrete costs 30-50% more than plain concrete but can mimic the look of pavers, brick, or natural stone at a fraction of the cost. It adds significant curb appeal and value.

How long does a concrete driveway last?

A properly installed and sealed concrete driveway typically lasts 25-30 years. Regular sealing every 2-3 years and prompt crack repair can extend its lifespan further.

Climate Impact on Seattle Concrete

Seattle averages about 15 freeze-thaw cycles a year. The dominant concrete threat is persistent moisture that produces efflorescence, moss growth on unsealed surfaces, and alkali-silica reaction with non-compliant aggregate. WSDOT uses anti-icer sprays only during the 3-5 hard-freeze events a typical winter produces.

The 2021 Pacific Northwest heat dome cooked freshly poured slabs that were not covered and produced a documented regional rash of plastic-shrinkage cracking. Seattle crews now treat any forecast above 90F as a cold-weather equivalent and require evaporation retarder on the surface.

Seattle Concrete Maintenance

Seattle's persistent moisture means moss and organic growth colonize unsealed concrete within 12-18 months, faster than any other major metro. Annual pressure washing at 2,000 PSI (lower than typical to avoid aggregate damage) plus penetrating silane sealer every 2-3 years is the Queen Anne and Capitol Hill standard. Moss-inhibitor additives in the sealer extend cleaning intervals.

About concrete work in Seattle, WA

Why pricing varies in Seattle. Labor rates here run roughly 42% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For concrete work specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Seattle at roughly 26% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Seattle has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Sealers prevent moss and algae growth; standard mixes work without freeze additives.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Seattle require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. As a major metro of 652,405 residents, the local contractor market is deep — expect 10+ legitimate bidders on any standard residential job. Seattle has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.
