## What shipped tonight

### 1. Roof hub overhauled (highest-impact ship)
**Why:** `roof-cost-by-material.html` was the canonical roof hub (the destination for "roof replacement cost", "roof cost by material", "roof cost" — ~56+ imp/wk per Apr 25 GSC) but was an 83-line stub with **zero schema**, no nav, no footer, no E-E-A-T byline, and a 4-row table.

Beefed it up to 440 lines using fence-cost.html as the template:
- 6-schema set: Article, Service, Dataset, BreadcrumbList, FAQPage, HowTo
- Geoff Lane byline + dateModified 2026-04-27
- 9-material pricing table (asphalt 3-tab/architectural/cedar shake/standing-seam metal/exposed-fastener metal/concrete tile/clay tile/slate/flat TPO)
- 5-bucket house-size table (1,000 sqft -> 3,500 sqft)
- 30-city pricing table linking to existing `*-roof-cost.html` city pages
- Asphalt-grade deep dive (3-tab vs architectural vs designer)
- Metal-type deep dive (standing seam vs exposed fastener vs stone-coated steel vs copper)
- Tile/slate deep dive (concrete/clay/slate/synthetic)
- Hidden costs (decking replacement, second-layer tear-off, steep-pitch surcharge, solar detach-and-reset, insurance scope mismatch, etc.)
- Permits + insurance claims section (storm-chaser red flags, "we waive your deductible" fraud warning, Xactimate scope matching)
- 9-question FAQ matching FAQPage schema
- Methodology section + related-guides chips

**Note on accuracy:** I used the same metro multipliers fence-cost.html uses (Boston/LA +22%, NYC +30%, SF +32%, Memphis -12%, etc.). These are calibrated from BLS roofers' wages + BEA RPP, but the headline architectural-shingle ranges in the city table are my projections — verify against your `cal:roof-cost:architectural:*` Redis buckets when you're back. If they're off, regenerate the table from your actual calibration data.

### 2. Killed keyword cannibalization on 15 vertical pillars
**Why:** Each of these verticals had **two indexed pillars competing for the same keyword**:
- `fence-cost.html` (hub, 423 lines) AND `fencing-cost-guide.html` (guide, 324 lines) — both self-canonical
- Same pattern on concrete, plumbing, electrical, hvac, siding, painting, solar, garage-door, kitchen-remodel, insulation, landscaping, foundation-repair, window-replacement, gutter-installation

Fix: rewrote canonicals on the 15 guide pages to point at the matching hub. Also fixed the matching `og:url` and hreflang values. Pages still render; Google consolidates ranking signal on the hub. If a guide page is still indexed in 28 days, escalate to noindex (script is checked in: `scripts/_seo-canonicalize-guides-to-hubs.js`).

The 4 verticals with no main hub (`legal-cost-guide.html`, `medical-cost-guide.html`, `moving-cost-guide.html`, `auto-repair-cost-guide.html`) were left as-is — those guides are the de facto pillars.

### 3. Hub title + description trim (13 pages)
**Why:** Most hub pages had titles >65 chars and descriptions >160 chars, which Google truncates in SERPs. landscaping-cost was 92 char title / 233 char desc. solar-cost was 86 char title.

All 13 trimmed to title ≤65 and desc ≤134 chars while preserving the lead $ range and "1,000+ U.S. cities, no email" positioning. Script: `scripts/_seo-trim-hub-titles-descs.js`.

Sample before/after (solar-cost.html):
- T(86): `Solar Panel Cost 2026: $15,000-$38,000, $10,500-$26,600 After 30% Tax Credit | Woogoro`
- T(65): `Solar Panel Cost 2026: $10,500-$26,600 After Tax Credit | Woogoro`
