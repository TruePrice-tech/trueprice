Shelton fencing FAQ

How long does fence installation take?

A standard 150-200 linear foot residential fence takes 1-3 days to install. Hillside or rocky soil adds time. Post holes need 24-48 hours to cure if set in concrete.

How deep should fence posts be?

Standard rule: bury one-third of the total post length. For a 6-foot fence with 8-foot posts, dig 24-30 inches deep. In frost-prone areas, posts must go below the frost line to prevent heaving.

How much does vinyl fencing cost?

Vinyl privacy fencing costs $20-$40 per linear foot installed. A typical 150 linear foot backyard fence runs $3,000-$6,000. Vinyl never needs painting, resists rot, and lasts 25-30 years.

How deep should fence posts be in Shelton?

In Shelton's cold climate, fence posts should extend at least 6 inches below the frost line to prevent heaving. For most of Shelton, this means 36-48 inches deep with 8-10 inches of compacted gravel at the bottom for drainage and concrete from there to grade. Shallow posts in freeze-prone areas will lean within 2-3 winters regardless of how well the rest of the fence is built.

Are permits required to build a fence in Shelton?

Fence permits are required in most Shelton jurisdictions, particularly for heights over 6 feet. Before breaking ground: call 811 for utility locates, verify property lines (a survey costs $300-$600 if boundaries are unclear), and check HOA requirements. A professional contractor handles the permit process as part of the project.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Shelton?

Per-foot costs for fence installation in Shelton: wood privacy $15-$30, vinyl $20-$40, chain link $10-$20, and aluminum or wrought iron $25-$55.
