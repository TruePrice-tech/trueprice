Frequently asked fencing questions

How long does a wood fence last?

Pressure-treated pine lasts 15-20 years. Cedar lasts 15-25 years. Redwood lasts 20-30 years. All wood fences last longer with stain or sealant applied every 2-3 years.

How tall can I build my fence?

Most municipalities allow 6 feet in backyards and 3-4 feet in front yards without a variance. Some HOAs restrict fence height further. Always check local zoning before building.

What is the cheapest fence material?

Chain link is the cheapest at $10-$20 per linear foot installed. Pressure-treated pine is the cheapest wood option at $15-$25/ft. Vinyl and composite start at $20-$30/ft but never need painting.

What is the best fence for privacy?

A 6-foot solid board (dog-ear, board-on-board, or shadowbox) fence provides the most privacy. Board-on-board is best since it has no gaps even when boards shrink. Vinyl and composite also offer full privacy panels.

How much does a new fence cost in Shreveport?

Fence costs in Shreveport range from $15-$35 per linear foot for wood, $20-$40 for vinyl, $15-$30 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron, including materials and installation.

What fence material lasts longest in Shreveport's climate?

Vinyl and aluminum are the longevity champions in Shreveport's humid conditions, outlasting wood by 10-15 years since they resist rot, termites, and moisture damage entirely. Wood fence lovers should choose pressure-treated pine or cedar and commit to staining or sealing every 2-3 years. Composite materials split the difference with wood aesthetics and near-vinyl durability.

Do I need a permit to build a fence in Shreveport?

Check with Shreveport's building department, but permits are generally required for fences, especially those above 6 feet. Before digging post holes, you need utility locates (call 811) and confirmed property lines. If boundaries are uncertain, a survey ($300-$600) prevents expensive neighbor disputes. Your contractor should pull the permit.

Local Fencing Guide for Shreveport, LA

In Shreveport, humidity and insects are the two failure modes that shorten fence life. Untreated lumber rots from ground contact within a few seasons, and termites work the back side of any wood fence that touches soil. Vinyl, aluminum, and pressure-treated composites all sidestep both problems.

As a hurricane-exposure area, Shreveport homeowners should consider wind resistance when choosing a fence style. Solid privacy fences act as sails in high winds and are more likely to fail. Shadow-box or spaced-picket designs allow wind to pass through while still providing partial privacy. Post spacing of 6 feet (instead of 8) with deeper footings adds meaningful wind resistance.

The Shreveport housing picture. With homes averaging just 32 years old, Shreveport has a relatively new building stock. Most fence installation work involves upgrades and efficiency improvements rather than end-of-life replacements. Code compliance is rarely an issue since these homes were built to recent standards.

Local market conditions. Shreveport's flat growth pattern keeps contractor pricing competitive. You will likely find more willingness to negotiate and shorter lead times than in booming cities. However, fewer contractors means fewer quotes to compare, so check references carefully. Shreveport's large market supports both dedicated fence companies and general contractors who do fencing. What drives cost. Shreveport homeowners benefit from labor rates 13% below national medians. For a 150-foot privacy fence, this translates to savings of $390-780 compared to the national average. Red flags to watch for. Any Shreveport contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag.
