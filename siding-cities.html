All cities with siding replacement cost data

738 U.S. cities across 50 states · data updated 2026

Siding replacement pricing falls into climate-driven materials, not aesthetic ones. Vinyl dominates the Midwest at $4–$7 per square foot installed; fiber-cement (James Hardie, LP SmartSide) takes the Mid-Atlantic and West Coast in the $9–$15 range; engineered wood is gaining on the rainy side of the Pacific Northwest. Hurricane-prone counties (FL, eastern NC, coastal TX) layer impact-rated nailing patterns and wind-rated panels that add $1–$2/sqft over the same product on a Kansas City rambler. Tear-off is routinely 30% of the job when the existing siding hides asbestos backer or pre-2008 vinyl on furring strips that have to come down to inspect sheathing. City-by-city pages cover material mix, climate-driven add-ons, and the tear-off load that historically lands on quotes in that area.
