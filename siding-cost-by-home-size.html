Siding Cost by Home Size (2026)

Siding replacement in 2026: $7,500-$11,000 for a 1000 sq ft single-story (vinyl), $11,000-$16,500 for 1500 sq ft, $14,500-$22,000 for 2000-2500 sq ft, $20,000-$32,000 for 3000+ sq ft. Fiber cement (Hardie) runs 40-60% higher. The price hinges on wall area, not floor area — and on whether the existing siding needs tear-off.

Siding Cost by Home Square Footage

Siding is priced by exterior wall area, which is rarely the same as home floor area. A single-story 1500 sq ft home with 9-foot ceilings typically has 1,700-2,100 sq ft of wall surface (less roughly 100-150 sq ft for windows and doors). A two-story 1500 sq ft home of the same floor plan has less total wall area but more height to access.

The numbers below assume vinyl siding (the most common 2026 choice), tear-off and disposal of existing siding, new housewrap (Tyvek or equivalent), basic trim and J-channel, and standard caulking. They don't include sheathing replacement, wood rot repair, or window/door trim upgrades.

Siding Cost for a 1500 Sq Ft House

This is the most-searched specific size. A 1500 sq ft single-story home in 2026 has roughly 1,700-2,200 sq ft of wall to side (after subtracting windows/doors). At $5-$11 per sq ft installed for vinyl, that's $9,000-$24,000 in raw range, with most quotes landing $11,000-$16,500. Fiber cement (Hardie) on the same home runs $16,000-$24,000.

The factors that move within the range: existing siding type (cement-board tear-off is harder than vinyl tear-off), how much sheathing repair shows up after tear-off, complexity of trim (lots of shutters, gables, decorative trim costs more), and the contractor's choice of vinyl product line.

Siding Cost for a 2000-2500 Sq Ft House

2000-2500 sq ft homes typically have 2,200-3,300 sq ft of wall area for single-story; less for two-story but with height-access cost increases. Vinyl: $12,500-$22,500. Hardie: $20,500-$33,500. Engineered wood (LP SmartSide): $18,000-$30,000.

Above 2000 sq ft, scope ambiguity is the biggest source of bid variance. Get all three quotes specifying the same wall square footage and the same tear-off scope before comparing — a $4,000 spread between bids often comes from one quote excluding tear-off or assuming sheathing is in good shape.

What Moves the Price Beyond Square Footage

Material choice. Vinyl: cheapest, 25-30 year life. Insulated vinyl: 25-30% more, modest energy savings. Engineered wood (LP SmartSide): 30-40 year life, ~50% more than vinyl. Fiber cement (Hardie): 40-50 year life, 60-100% more than vinyl, paintable. Cedar shake: premium look, 20-30 year life if not maintained, expensive.

Tear-off complexity. Single-layer vinyl tear-off: standard. Tear-off plus sheathing repair: +$2,000-$6,000 depending on rot extent. Stucco tear-off (not common but expensive): +$5,000-$15,000. Always ask: "What's the per-square-foot rate for sheathing replacement if rot is found?" Get the answer before signing.

Trim work. Standard J-channel and corner trim: included. Upgraded fascia and soffit: +$2,000-$5,000. Window-trim wraparound: +$50-$150 per window. Decorative trim and shutters: priced individually.

Insulation upgrade. Some homeowners add rigid foam under new siding for energy gains. Materials and labor: +$1.50-$3.50 per sq ft. Pays back over 10-20 years on heating-dominant climates; less so in mild climates.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does siding cost for a 1500 sq ft house?

$11,000-$16,500 for vinyl siding on a single-story 1500 sq ft home in 2026. Fiber cement (Hardie) runs $16,000-$24,000. Wall area is typically 1,700-2,200 sq ft.

How is siding cost calculated from home size?

Contractors price siding by exterior wall sq ft, not floor sq ft. Single-story: wall area is roughly 1.0-1.5x floor area. Two-story: 1.0-1.3x floor area. Subtract roughly 100-150 sq ft per side for windows and doors.

Is vinyl or fiber cement siding cheaper?

Vinyl is meaningfully cheaper at install ($5-$11/sq ft vs $9-$16 for fiber cement). Over 30 years, fiber cement is often cheaper on a lifetime basis (40-50 year life vs 25-30 for vinyl).

Is the cost different for a one-story vs two-story home of the same square footage?

Two-story homes have less wall area (taller, narrower) but cost more per sq ft due to scaffolding and access. Net cost is usually 5-15% lower than the same-floor-area single-story.

What's siding cost per square foot in 2026?

Vinyl: $5-$11 installed. Insulated vinyl: $7-$13. Fiber cement (Hardie): $9-$16. Engineered wood: $8-$14. Cedar shake: $11-$18. Stucco: $9-$15.
