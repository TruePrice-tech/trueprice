Sierra Vista fencing FAQ

What is the best fence for privacy?

A 6-foot solid board (dog-ear, board-on-board, or shadowbox) fence provides the most privacy. Board-on-board is best since it has no gaps even when boards shrink. Vinyl and composite also offer full privacy panels.

How much does a new fence cost in Sierra Vista?

Fence costs in Sierra Vista range from $15-$35 per linear foot for wood, $20-$40 for vinyl, $15-$30 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron, including materials and installation.

Should fence posts be set in concrete?

Yes, for most fences. Concrete-set posts are more stable and resist leaning. Some installers use compacted gravel for better drainage in wet climates, which can extend post life by reducing ground-level rot.

When is the best time to install a fence?

Late spring through early fall offers the best conditions for concrete curing and working in the ground. Avoid frozen ground and extremely wet seasons. Many contractors offer off-season discounts.

How long does fence installation take?

A standard 150-200 linear foot residential fence takes 1-3 days to install. Hillside or rocky soil adds time. Post holes need 24-48 hours to cure if set in concrete.

How long does a wood fence last in Sierra Vista?

Expect 15-20 years from pressure-treated wood in Sierra Vista, 15-25 from cedar, and only 5-8 from untreated pine. Consistent maintenance makes the difference between the low and high end of those ranges. Stain or seal every 2-3 years, and keep grass, vines, and mulch away from the base to slow ground-level rot.

Sierra Vista permits: required to build a fence?

In Sierra Vista, fence permits are standard for installations over 6 feet and sometimes required for any fence. Three things to handle before installation: call 811 for utility marking, confirm property boundaries (budget $300-$600 for a survey if needed), and review any HOA rules. The fence contractor should manage the permitting as part of the project scope.
