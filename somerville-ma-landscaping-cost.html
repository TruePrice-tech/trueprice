What Somerville residents ask about landscaping

How much does a sprinkler system cost?

A residential irrigation system costs $2,500-$5,000 for a quarter-acre lot. Smart controllers add $100-$300 and reduce water usage 20-40%. Drip irrigation zones for beds cost $500-$1,000 extra.

Should I hire a landscaper or landscape architect?

A landscaper handles installation and maintenance. A landscape architect designs complex projects and manages grading, drainage, and permits. For projects over $15,000 or involving structural elements, an architect is worthwhile.

How much does lawn installation cost?

Sod costs $1-$2 per square foot installed. Hydroseeding costs $0.05-$0.10/sqft. Traditional seeding costs $0.10-$0.25/sqft but takes 6-8 weeks to establish. A 5,000 sqft lawn costs $5,000-$10,000 in sod.

How much does a paver patio cost?

Paver patios cost $12-$25 per square foot installed, depending on paver material and pattern complexity. A 300 sqft patio runs $3,600-$7,500. Permeable pavers cost 15-20% more but help with drainage.

Understanding landscaping costs in Somerville, MA

Why pricing varies in Somerville. Labor rates here run roughly 34% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For landscaping specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Somerville at roughly 24% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Somerville has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Somerville's short growing season (May-September) compresses landscaping work into 4-5 months. Spring planting after the last frost date is critical for plant establishment before winter. Fall planting of trees and shrubs (September-October) allows root establishment before dormancy — a strategy experienced Somerville landscapers recommend for better spring survival rates. Established properties in Somerville (averaging 49 years) often have mature trees and existing landscape elements that a redesign should work around. Removing mature trees costs $500-2,000 each and eliminates decades of shade value. Design with existing assets, not against them.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most landscaping projects in Somerville require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. At 78,804 residents, the local pool is moderate; expect 4-6 active contractors for most standard residential work. Somerville has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.

Local market conditions. Somerville has moderate construction activity. Contractors are busy but accessible with 1-3 weeks' notice. Off-season quotes (contractor-dependent) can run 8-15% below peak rates. In Somerville (78,804 residents), landscaping is often handled by small local crews who do both installation and maintenance. For larger design-build projects, consider firms from the nearest larger metro that service your area.

Seasonal timing. The landscaping season in Somerville runs April through October. Spring planting (April-May) is the busiest period as homeowners and contractors rush to install before summer heat. Fall planting (September-October) is the insider move — plants establish roots before dormancy and emerge stronger the following spring.

What drives cost. Somerville labor rates sit 34% above the US median, making labor the single largest cost factor for landscaping project. This is structural — driven by local cost of living and demand — not something negotiation can erase. Somerville's housing stock averages 49 years — the age where original installations start failing and code requirements have evolved. Red flags to watch for. Check that any Somerville contractor doing landscaping project carries both general liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers' compensation.
