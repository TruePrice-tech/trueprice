Concrete Driveway Cost in South Carolina (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in South Carolina typically run $2,800–$7,600 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 3000 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 6-inch design frost line, and the state sits in no freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F0). South Carolina climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 6-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F0 — no freeze-thaw exposure, no air-entrainment required
  • Typical residential mix: 3000 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: Moderate — periodic clay shrink-swell, standard footing reinforcement adequate
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC C — moderate seismic risk, continuous-rebar-tie footings required
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: Low — minimal winter de-icer use

South Carolina licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: South Carolina Department of Labor, Licensing and Regulation — Residential Specialty (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential concrete pour; SC LLR residential license required above $5,000 contract

How concrete driveway costs vary in South Carolina

South Carolina's combination of Charleston-Summerville fault zone seismic risk forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC C-D0 design requirements on every residential foundation pour in the corridor, persistent Atlantic salt-spray on the Lowcountry from Charleston to Hilton Head, and hurricane wind uplift requirements drive continuous epoxy-coated rebar tie schedules on every coastal South Carolina residential foundation pour — and Charleston Old & Historic District and Beaufort Historic District separately impose color-palette and substrate-prep approval through HBAR review on every covered residential concrete pour.
