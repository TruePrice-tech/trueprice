Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in South Carolina (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in South Carolina typically run $2,000–$4,800 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2020 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Service entrance design assumes a 200A minimum on new residential construction. Charleston, Hilton Head, and Myrtle Beach Atlantic salt-haze deposition corrodes outdoor service equipment within 5-7 years, mandating stainless mounting hardware and NEMA 4X meter sockets on shoreline upgrades — and the Lowcountry hurricane wind exposure across Beaufort, Charleston, and Horry counties drives reinforced weatherheads and category-rated service-mast bracing on every coastal panel upgrade.

South Carolina code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2020 NEC — dwelling unit AFCI on most 120V branch circuits, GFCI expanded
  • State amendments: South Carolina adopts 2020 NEC under SC LLR — Residential Builders Commission with Specialty Contractor SP-RBE classification for residential electrical
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: High — Southeast and lower Plains lightning belt
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 12-to-18-inch design depth on buried service conduit

South Carolina licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: South Carolina Department of Labor, Licensing and Regulation — Residential Builders Commission (SP-RBE Electrical Specialty) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county electrical permit required for panel upgrade and service-line work

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in South Carolina

Charleston, Hilton Head, and Myrtle Beach Atlantic salt-haze deposition corrodes outdoor service equipment within 5-7 years, mandating stainless mounting hardware and NEMA 4X meter sockets on shoreline upgrades — and the Lowcountry hurricane wind exposure across Beaufort, Charleston, and Horry counties drives reinforced weatherheads and category-rated service-mast bracing on every coastal panel upgrade.

State-specific code or insurance rule: South Carolina SC Code §40-59 places residential electrical work under the Residential Builders Commission rather than a freestanding electrical board — Residential Specialty Contractor SP-RBE (Electrical) is the credential required to bid and execute single-family panel upgrades and rewires, and the Commission also requires the Residential Builder's General Contractor credential on any project where electrical is bundled with structural work.
