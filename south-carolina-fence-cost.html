Fence Installation Cost in South Carolina (2026)

Installing 150 linear feet of fencing in South Carolina typically runs $4,400–$11,500, with 6-foot pressure-treated southern yellow pine the dominant residential choice. Concrete-set posts must clear the 8-inch design frost line. South Carolina sits inside Termite Probability and Climatic Tier 1 (the highest TPCT zone alongside Florida and Alabama) with annual subterranean and Formosan termite pressure across the Lowcountry — every in-ground wood post requires CCA-treated southern yellow pine .60 or higher, and Charleston, Hilton Head, and Myrtle Beach Atlantic salt-haze deposition mandates stainless mounting on shoreline retrofits within 1 mile of tidewater.

South Carolina climate & post-design drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 8-inch design minimum for post footings
  • Dominant residential fence material: Pressure-treated southern yellow pine — dominant low-budget Sun Belt and Plains
  • Hurricane wind tier: High — 140 mph+ design wind drives 8-foot post spacing max and concrete sleeves
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Very high — TPCT zone 1, all in-ground wood requires PT southern yellow pine .60 CCA or alternative
  • HOA fencing-rule prevalence: Moderate — suburban HOA prevalence on newer subdivisions

South Carolina licensing, HOA & boundary law

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: South Carolina Department of Labor, Licensing and Regulation — Residential Builders Commission (Specialty Contractor SP-RBE) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county permit typical for residential fence over 6 feet; Beaufort/Charleston enforce coastal wind code

How fence installation costs vary in South Carolina

South Carolina sits inside Termite Probability and Climatic Tier 1 (the highest TPCT zone alongside Florida and Alabama) with annual subterranean and Formosan termite pressure across the Lowcountry — every in-ground wood post requires CCA-treated southern yellow pine .60 or higher, and Charleston, Hilton Head, and Myrtle Beach Atlantic salt-haze deposition mandates stainless mounting on shoreline retrofits within 1 mile of tidewater.

State-specific code or insurance rule: South Carolina SC Code §40-59 requires every Residential Builder and Residential Specialty Contractor to hold a Residential Builders Commission credential — and unique to South Carolina, the Specialty Contractor SP-RBE (Roofing/Siding/Exterior) classification authorizes envelope and fence work distinct from the full Residential Builder license, with the Commission requiring a $10,000 surety bond and 8-hour pre-license building science continuing education on Lowcountry coastal wind-design and termite-resistance topics.
