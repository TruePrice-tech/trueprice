Foundation Cost in South Carolina (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in South Carolina typically run $11,400–$32,800 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with crawl-space stem-wall as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 6-inch design frost line. South Carolina's combination of Atlantic hurricane wind exposure across the Lowcountry coastal counties (Charleston, Berkeley, Dorchester, Horry, Georgetown, Beaufort, Jasper, Colleton, Hampton) driving SC Charleston-Lowcountry Building Code hurricane tie-down requirements at 150 mph design wind on every coastal residential foundation pour, marsh-mud and pluff-mud subsoil across Lowcountry tidal-creek and barrier-island residential properties driving pier-and-beam pile-supported foundation specification instead of slab-on-grade, and ASCE 7-22 SDC C seismic design category across the entire state due to 1886 Charleston earthquake legacy drives a unique hurricane-and-seismic-driven coastal foundation regulatory environment.

South Carolina climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 6-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Crawl-space stem-wall — dominant in Southeast humid corridor and termite-pressure zones
  • Expansive soil risk: Moderate — periodic clay shrink-swell, standard footing reinforcement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC C — moderate seismic risk, continuous-rebar-tie footings required
  • Hurricane wind tier: High — 140 mph+ design wind drives continuous load-path tie-down from foundation to roof
  • Water table risk: Moderate — water table below frost line in most lots
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • IECC climate zone: 2A-3A
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 3 (lowest) — no statewide rough-in requirement

South Carolina licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: South Carolina Department of Labor, Licensing & Regulation — Residential Builders Commission (RBC) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential foundation pour; SC RBC Residential Builder license required above $5,000 contract; SC Charleston-Lowcountry Building Code mandatory hurricane wind tie-down on coastal counties; Lowcountry pier-and-beam pile-supported foundations standard on every Beaufort, Charleston, and Georgetown coastal pour

How foundation pour costs vary in South Carolina

State-specific code or insurance rule: South Carolina Residential Builders Commission §40-59 imposes one of the most credential-segmented residential builder licensing schemes in the country — every SC foundation contractor performing work above $5,000 must hold a Residential Builder license plus the South Carolina Department of Labor, Licensing & Regulation separately requires Charleston-Lowcountry Building Code hurricane wind tie-down detailing on every Charleston, Berkeley, Dorchester, Horry, Georgetown, Beaufort, Jasper, Colleton, and Hampton county residential foundation pour, with Lowcountry pier-and-beam pile-supported foundations standard on every coastal residential pour to address marsh-mud subsoil and hurricane-storm-surge water table.
