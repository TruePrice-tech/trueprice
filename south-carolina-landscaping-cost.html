Landscape Installation Cost in South Carolina (2026)

Landscape installation in South Carolina typically runs $3,800–$16,400 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 7a-9a. South Carolina's combination of warm-humid Lowcountry climate from Charleston to Hilton Head, alluvial Coastal Plain soils across the Pee Dee region, and 240-day growing season supports near-year-round residential landscape demand on warm-season Bermuda, zoysia, and centipede turf with St. Augustinegrass dominant on coastal Lowcountry — and the state's pine-belt across the Sandhills supports loblolly and longleaf pine canopy that drives fungicide-rotation programs for take-all root rot on every residential turf install in the coastal corridor plus Lowcountry historic-district landscape design with palmetto, oak, and hydrangea palette dominating Charleston single-family residential properties.

South Carolina climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 7a-9a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 50 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 240 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Moderate — periodic drought, advisory water-saving guidelines
  • Irrigation dependency: Moderate — periodic irrigation needed during dry season
  • Frost-line trench depth: 6-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

South Carolina licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: South Carolina Department of Pesticide Regulation (Clemson University) + South Carolina Department of Labor, Licensing & Regulation (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; SC DPR Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care; SC LLR residential license required above $5,000 contract

How landscape installation costs vary in South Carolina

South Carolina's combination of warm-humid Lowcountry climate from Charleston to Hilton Head, alluvial Coastal Plain soils across the Pee Dee region, and 240-day growing season supports near-year-round residential landscape demand on warm-season Bermuda, zoysia, and centipede turf with St. Augustinegrass dominant on coastal Lowcountry — and the state's pine-belt across the Sandhills supports loblolly and longleaf pine canopy that drives fungicide-rotation programs for take-all root rot on every residential turf install in the coastal corridor plus Lowcountry historic-district landscape design with palmetto, oak, and hydrangea palette dominating Charleston single-family residential properties.

State-specific code or insurance rule: South Carolina Code §46-13 grants Clemson University's Department of Pesticide Regulation exclusive authority over residential pesticide regulation statewide — South Carolina is unusual in housing pesticide regulation under a land-grant university Department of Pesticide Regulation rather than a state Department of Agriculture, with Clemson DPR operating both the licensing program and the academic Cooperative Extension continuing-education channel for every South Carolina commercial landscape contractor applying chemicals on residential property statewide.
