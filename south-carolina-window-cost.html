Window Replacement Cost in South Carolina (2026)

Window replacements in South Carolina typically run $5,200–$16,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Southern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.4, SHGC ≤ 0.25). South Carolina's combination of 150 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed across the Lowcountry coastal counties driving SC Charleston-Lowcountry Building Code ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated window mandate on every coastal residential install, persistent Atlantic salt-spray on every coastal exposure, very-high TPCT termite zone 1 pressure across the entire state, ASCE 7-22 SDC C seismic design category across the entire state due to 1886 Charleston earthquake legacy, and Charleston BAR review on every Charleston historic-district window replacement (the first U.S. local historic-preservation program, established 1931) drives unique Charleston-historic-and-coastal-and-seismic-driven glazing selection.

South Carolina climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Southern — cooling-dominant, low SHGC priority (U ≤ 0.40, SHGC ≤ 0.25)
  • IECC climate zone: 2A-3A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.4 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: 0.25
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 150 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: Mandatory ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated in coastal counties only
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: High — multiple state-mapped historic districts with mandatory review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Moderate — 20-40% pre-1978 housing share, EPA RRP required on selected replacements

South Carolina licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: South Carolina Department of Labor, Licensing & Regulation — Residential Builders Commission (RBC) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification; SC RBC Residential Builder license required above $5,000 contract; SC Charleston-Lowcountry Building Code mandatory ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated windows on coastal counties; Charleston Board of Architectural Review (BAR) review required on every Charleston historic-district install

How window replacement costs vary in South Carolina

State-specific code or insurance rule: South Carolina Residential Builders Commission §40-59 imposes one of the most credential-segmented residential builder licensing schemes in the country — every SC window contractor performing work above $5,000 must hold a Residential Builder license, plus Charleston Board of Architectural Review (BAR) separately requires review on every window replacement within Charleston's 6 designated historic districts (Old & Historic Charleston, Old City Historic District, Wragg Square, Cannonborough-Elliotborough, Hampton Park Terrace, Radcliffeborough) — one of the oldest local historic-preservation review programs in the country (established 1931, the first in the U.S.) requiring profile, glazing, and shutter-pattern continuity with the surrounding 1670-1900 architectural overlay.
