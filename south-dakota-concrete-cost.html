Concrete Driveway Cost in South Dakota (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in South Dakota typically run $2,800–$7,400 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 4000 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 60-inch design frost line, and the state sits in most severe freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F3). South Dakota's combination of high alkaline soil sulfate exposure (S2 ACI 318 category) across the western Black Hills counties, F3 (most severe) freeze-thaw exposure across the entire state, and -35°F winter low temperatures forces ASTM C150 Type V sulfate-resistant cement plus 6%+ air-entrainment specification on every South Dakota residential pour — and Black Hills wildfire-smoke seasons since 2017 measurably stress fresh concrete cure cycles requiring extended wet-curing periods on every Western South Dakota residential pour during August-October wildfire season.

South Dakota climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 60-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F3 — most severe freeze-thaw + heavy de-icer use, 6-8% air-entrainment + 0.40 max w/c required
  • Typical residential mix: 4000 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: Moderate — periodic clay shrink-swell, standard footing reinforcement adequate
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S2 — severe sulfate exposure, ASTM C150 Type II/V cement required
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC A — no seismic design considerations
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: Very high — heavy de-icer use drives 0.40 max w/c + epoxy-coated rebar

South Dakota licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: No statewide concrete license; Sioux Falls, Rapid City issue municipal contractor credentials (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential driveway pour > 200 sq ft; municipal contractor registration required in Sioux Falls and Rapid City
