Window Replacement Cost in South Dakota (2026)

Window replacements in South Dakota typically run $5,200–$13,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Northern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.3, no SHGC limit). South Dakota's combination of 48-inch frost depth across the entire state, F3 freeze-thaw cycling, Pierre shale bentonite-clay shrink-swell across the western half driving aggressive header-attachment detailing, eastern SD Tornado Alley exposure across the Sioux Falls metro driving optional impact-resistant glazing recommendation, and ENERGY STAR Northern climate zone driving low-U-factor (≤0.30) glazing requirement statewide drives moderate cold-climate-and-bentonite-and-tornado-driven glazing selection.

South Dakota climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Northern — coldest climates, U-factor priority (U ≤ 0.27 in coldest tier)
  • IECC climate zone: 5A-6A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.3 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: No limit (cold climate)
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 110 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Low — limited historic-preservation review outside specific neighborhoods
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Moderate — 20-40% pre-1978 housing share, EPA RRP required on selected replacements

South Dakota licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: South Dakota has no statewide residential builder license — SD Department of Labor & Regulation oversees specialty trades only (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification; no state license; replacement-only same-size installs typically permit-exempt; tornado-shelter rough-in detailing within attached garage common in eastern SD municipalities

How window replacement costs vary in South Dakota

State-specific code or insurance rule: South Dakota has no statewide residential builder license — SD Department of Labor & Regulation oversees only specialty trades like plumbers, electricians, and oil-burner installers, leaving window contractor competency and bond entirely to municipal building departments and homeowner due diligence, plus South Dakota Geological Survey separately publishes Pierre shale and Niobrara chalk bentonite-clay shrink-swell expansive-soil maps requiring aggressive header-attachment detailing on every Pierre, Rapid City, Sioux Falls, Brookings, and Watertown residential window installation, with eastern SD Tornado Alley exposure driving optional impact-resistant glazing recommendation.
