Fencing questions for Southaven homeowners

Do I need a permit to build a fence in Southaven?

Most Southaven jurisdictions require a permit for fences over 6 feet tall. Many require permits for any fence. You also need to check setback requirements, HOA rules, and call 811 for utility locates before digging.

Does a fence increase property value?

A well-built fence adds 1-5% to property value depending on material and neighborhood norms. Privacy fencing adds more value in neighborhoods where it is expected. Chain link adds minimal value.

How long does a wood fence last?

Pressure-treated pine lasts 15-20 years. Cedar lasts 15-25 years. Redwood lasts 20-30 years. All wood fences last longer with stain or sealant applied every 2-3 years.

What fence material lasts longest in Southaven's climate?

Southaven's humidity and heat are hard on wood fences. Vinyl and aluminum fences last 10-15 years longer because they do not rot, attract termites, or absorb moisture. If wood is your preference, pressure-treated pine or cedar hold up best but require staining or sealing every 2-3 years. Composite fencing is a middle-ground option that looks like wood but handles the humidity like vinyl.

Do I need a permit to build a fence in Southaven?

In Southaven, fence permits are standard for installations over 6 feet and sometimes required for any fence. Three things to handle before installation: call 811 for utility marking, confirm property boundaries (budget $300-$600 for a survey if needed), and review any HOA rules. The fence contractor should manage the permitting as part of the project scope.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Southaven?

Fence installation in Southaven typically costs $15-$30 per linear foot for wood privacy, $20-$40 for vinyl, $10-$20 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron. A 150 linear foot backyard fence runs $2,250-$6,000 depending on material. Getting 3 quotes with matching scope ensures fair pricing.
