Common solar questions in Spokane Valley

What happens to solar panels during a power outage?

Grid-tied systems without batteries shut off during outages for safety (anti-islanding). A battery backup or hybrid inverter lets you use solar power during grid failures.

How long do solar panels last?

Modern panels are warrantied for 25-30 years and continue producing at reduced efficiency beyond that. Inverters last 10-15 years (string) or 25 years (microinverters) and are the main replacement cost.

Can I add a battery to my solar system?

Yes. Adding a home battery (10-13 kWh) costs $8,000-$15,000 installed. Batteries provide backup power during outages and can help avoid peak-rate charges in time-of-use markets.

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

Does solar work in Spokane Valley's cold winters?

Solar works well in Spokane Valley despite the cold. Panels convert sunlight more efficiently at lower temperatures, partially offsetting shorter winter days. Net metering lets you bank summer overproduction as credits for winter months. Snow sheds from angled panels within a day or two in most cases. Higher local electricity rates and available incentives often make the payback comparable to warmer markets.

What solar incentives are available in Spokane Valley?

Every Spokane Valley homeowner installing solar qualifies for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit with no dollar limit. Beyond that, WA may offer state tax credits, utility-specific rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits, and property tax exemptions. The DSIRE database has the current list for your area. These stacked incentives typically reduce net cost by an additional 10-30%.

Best time of year to install solar in Spokane Valley?

Spokane Valley homeowners should aim for late spring or summer to install solar. Weather cooperates for roof work and you maximize first-year production before winter. Allow 6-12 weeks from signing a contract through permitting, utility interconnection approval, and installation. Start gathering quotes 2-3 months before your target window. Remember that the 30% ITC applies to the tax year in which installation is completed.
