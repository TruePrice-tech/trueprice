Spokane garage door: answers to common questions

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Spokane?

Garage door replacement in Spokane typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

What causes a garage door to be noisy?

Common causes: worn rollers (replace nylon for steel), dry hinges (lubricate with silicone spray), loose hardware, a chain-drive opener, or worn spring coils. A tune-up costs $100-$200.

What is the best type of garage door?

Insulated steel with polyurethane foam offers the best balance of durability, energy efficiency, and cost. R-value of 12-18 keeps the garage 10-20 degrees closer to house temperature.

Can I insulate my existing garage door?

Yes. Retrofit insulation kits ($100-$200 DIY) add R-4 to R-8. However, adding weight to a non-insulated door may require spring adjustment. A new insulated door is often a better investment.

How much does a garage door opener cost?

A new opener installed costs $250-$600 for chain or belt drive. Smart openers with Wi-Fi and battery backup run $400-$800 installed. Replacing the opener alone takes 2-3 hours.

What R-value garage door do I need in Spokane?

Insulation ratings of R-12 to R-16 are standard for Spokane's climate on attached garages. Heated garages or workshops should use R-16 or R-18 doors for meaningful energy savings. The incremental cost of $200-$500 for better insulation typically returns through reduced heating costs in 3-5 years, plus you avoid frozen paint, liquids, and other garage-stored items.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Spokane?

Most original garage doors in Spokane's 33-year-average housing stock have exceeded their useful life. Replacing with a new insulated door and opener ($1,200-$3,000) is typically the smarter investment, recovering 90-100% of the cost at resale. Repair only makes sense for newer doors with isolated issues like a single panel or spring replacement.

Best time of year to replace a garage door in Spokane?

Schedule your Spokane garage door replacement for late spring or early fall, moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing. Off-peak timing can save 5-10% on installation costs and gets you a wider selection of available styles and colors. Spring is the busiest season for garage door companies since broken springs peak after winter cold stress.
