Red flags include: no line-item detail, same-day signing pressure, request for more than 30% upfront, no license or insurance verification, and verbal-only warranties.

When is the best time to schedule this work in St Louis?

About concrete work in St. Louis, MO

Why pricing varies in St. Louis. Labor rates here run roughly 23% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For concrete work specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts St. Louis at roughly 12% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. St. Louis has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. St. Louis's freeze-thaw cycles and moderate rainfall both affect concrete performance. Air-entrained mix is standard for outdoor flatwork, and expansion joints every 10-12 feet prevent the cracking that temperature swings cause. Proper drainage away from slabs prevents the water infiltration that freeze-thaw cycles exploit. Standard concrete in St. Louis runs $6-10 per square foot for basic flatwork (driveways, walkways). Decorative options (stamped, colored, exposed aggregate) add $4-8 per square foot. The biggest hidden cost is demolition and removal of existing concrete — budget $2-4 per square foot for tearout of old slabs.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in St. Louis require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 318,416, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes.
