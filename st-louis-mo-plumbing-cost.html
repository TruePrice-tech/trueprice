St. Louis Plumbing Licensing and Permits

Missouri Division of Professional Registration issues state plumbing licenses through the Board of Plumber Examiners. The City of St. Louis Building Division requires a separate city plumbing license for work within city limits, similar to Chicago's dual-licensing system. Verify both at pr.mo.gov and stlouis-mo.gov. St. Louis County municipalities (Clayton, Webster Groves, Kirkwood) have their own permit processes separate from the city. Spire gas permits are required for any water heater gas-line work.

What St Louis residents ask about plumbing

How much does a plumber charge per hour in St Louis?

Plumbers in St Louis typically charge $75-$150 per hour. Emergency and after-hours calls add a $100-$200 trip fee on top of the hourly rate.

How often should I have my drains cleaned in St Louis?

Annual drain maintenance is recommended for most St Louis homes. Older homes with mature trees nearby may need semi-annual service to prevent root intrusion.

Can I do plumbing repairs myself?

Simple tasks like replacing a faucet cartridge or fixing a running toilet are DIY-friendly. Anything involving supply lines, gas connections, sewer work, or permits should be left to a licensed plumber.
