St. Louis climate and roof performance

St. Louis roofs sit on loess bluffs and Missouri River alluvium that create highly variable drainage conditions from neighborhood to neighborhood, and the city averages 60 freeze-thaw cycles per year that punish every flashing joint and valley seam. The April 2011 EF4 tornado that cut across Lambert and north St. Louis County demonstrated that severe wind events here rival anything in tornado alley proper, and insurance carriers responded by incentivizing impact-rated assemblies on north-of-I-44 properties. Lafayette Square, Soulard, and Compton Heights feature original slate and copper roofing on 1870s-1890s Second Empire and Italianate mansions, and replacing those materials with anything other than matching slate triggers Cultural Resources Office enforcement. The Hill neighborhood has a concentration of flat-roof modified-bitumen systems on brick rowhouses, and the aging SBS membranes on these 1920s-1940s structures represent the highest-volume reroof work in the city.

St. Louis sits at the convergence of Great Plains supercell corridors and Gulf moisture: the metro averages 2-4 damaging hail events annually with spring supercells tracking northeast across St. Charles County into Metro East. The April 2011 Good Friday tornado (EF4) devastated Lambert Airport and north St. Louis County, and the August 2020 derecho produced 80-100 mph straight-line winds across the entire metro. ASCE 7-22 rates St. Louis at 105 mph design wind speed. Class 4 impact-rated assemblies deliver 10-20% insurance premium reductions on most Missouri and Illinois homeowners policies.

St. Louis dominant roofing materials

St. Louis's dominant roofing materials are architectural composition on post-1950 ranch and split-level homes, flat-roof modified bitumen (SBS and APP systems) on The Hill rowhouses and Soulard commercial-residential mixed structures, and original slate and copper on Lafayette Square and Compton Heights historic mansions. The loess-bluff geology creates expansive soil movement in south-city neighborhoods like Tower Grove and Holly Hills, which stresses roof framing and requires extra attention to ridge-line straightness during tear-off inspection. Ice-and-water-shield extends 3 feet minimum from eaves in the St. Louis climate zone, and valley flashing on the steep-pitch Victorian rooflines in Compton Heights demands step-flashed metal rather than woven composition.

St. Louis residential roofing splits along the city-county divide: City of St. Louis homes in the Central West End, Lafayette Square, and Soulard carry architectural composition on 1880s-1920s brick Second Empire, Italianate, and Victorian four-square construction, while newer St. Louis County construction in Chesterfield, Wildwood, and Ballwin runs dimensional composition on 1970s-2000s ranch and contemporary. Slate appears on Lafayette Square and Compton Heights landmarked mansions. The Hill neighborhood's dense 1920s brick construction features flat-roof modified bitumen and EPDM systems similar to Chicago three-flats.

GAF ships to St. Louis through the Kansas City and Indianapolis distribution hubs with 1-3 day lead times. Owens Corning manufactures at the Irving TX and Kansas City plants. CertainTeed ships from Shakopee MN and Corsicana TX. Atlas Roofing operates the Daingerfield TX plant. Malarkey ships from Portland OR. IKO ships from Canadian plants via Great Lakes rail. St. Louis distributors include ABC Supply (multiple metro locations), Beacon Roofing Supply, SRS Distribution, and Allied Building Products. St. Louis permits, codes, and licensing

St. Louis is an independent city entirely separate from St. Louis County, which means city permits, inspections, and code enforcement operate under a completely different jurisdiction than Ladue, Clayton, or Kirkwood just across the line. City of St. Louis Building Division enforces 2018 IRC with local amendments, and permits must be pulled before any work begins on residential replacements. The Cultural Resources Office reviews all exterior alterations in Lafayette Square, Compton Heights, Soulard, and Shaw historic districts, adding 4-8 weeks for approval of materials and color profiles. Missouri has no statewide residential roofing contractor license, so verification relies on city business license, insurance certificates verified with the issuer, and Missouri Attorney General Consumer Protection complaint history.

City of St. Louis Building Division permits residential reroofs within 5-10 business days at $150 minimum. St. Louis County processes separately with 3-5 day turnaround. Missouri has no statewide contractor licensing for roofers; both jurisdictions require local contractor registration and insurance verification. Historic districts under the City of St. Louis Cultural Resources Office include Lafayette Square, Benton Park, Fox Park, Soulard, Tower Grove East, Compton Heights, and Shaw. Landmark review adds 4-8 weeks and typically requires matching original slate, composition profiles, or specific clay tile on contributing structures.

St. Louis has 30+ locally designated historic districts and 200+ National Register-listed properties under the City of St. Louis Cultural Resources Office. Districts include Lafayette Square, Benton Park, Compton Heights, Fox Park, Soulard, Tower Grove East, Tower Grove South, Shaw, Forest Park Southeast, Central West End, Skinker-DeBaliviere, West End, North Central, Hyde Park, Old North St. Louis, and the Gaslight Square area. Landmark review requires matching original material profiles: slate on Second Empire and Italianate, specific composition on vernacular brick four-squares, and clay tile on Tudor Revival.

St. Louis roofing insurance and claims

St. Louis homeowners insurance covers sudden hail and wind damage but treats gradual wear as homeowner responsibility, and the April 2011 EF4 tornado drove major underwriting tightening on north-county properties. Document damage within 72 hours of any storm event because Missouri carriers push back aggressively on deferred claims. Ameren Missouri offers modest rebates on energy-efficient roofing as part of whole-home envelope upgrades, and reflective cool-roof coatings on flat-roof modified-bitumen systems in The Hill and Soulard qualify for those credits. Slate and copper replacements in Lafayette Square run $25,000-$45,000 depending on roof geometry, while standard composition on a typical South City bungalow runs $8,000-$14,000.

Missouri carriers handle hail and wind damage under standard homeowners policies, and the metro's spring hail frequency means roof-condition underwriting is a major factor. Most carriers require roof inspection on homes with 15+ year roofs, and several apply ACV settlement on roofs over 20 years. Class 4 impact-rated assemblies yield 10-20% premium reductions on most Missouri policies. Missouri Revised Statutes Section 375.1007 prohibits unfair claim settlement practices. Document hail damage within 30 days of any spring supercell event with dated exterior and interior photos.

St. Louis reroof upsells to accept and decline

St. Louis reroof upsells worth accepting: upgrade to Class 4 impact-rated composition (10-20% Missouri insurance premium reduction), upgrade to ice-and-water-shield extending 6 feet from eaves on low-slope sections (cheap insurance against freeze-thaw ice dams), upgrade to synthetic underlayment from organic felt (survives Missouri seasonal cycling 25+ years vs 15-20 on felt), and upgrade to reinforced starter course. Upsells to decline: excessive ridge ventilation beyond balanced intake-exhaust, unnecessary skylight additions, and proprietary "Gateway-rated" products without independent UL 2218 or FM 4473 certification.

Roofing questions for St Louis homeowners

What is a roofing square?

A roofing square equals 100 square feet. A typical 2,000 sqft roof is about 20 squares. Contractors price materials and labor per square.

Should I repair or replace my roof?

If damage covers less than 30% of the roof and the roof is under 15 years old, repair is usually sufficient. Beyond that, replacement provides better long-term value.

Should I choose the lowest roofing quote?

Not always. Lower bids sometimes exclude flashing, ventilation upgrades, or disposal costs that appear later as change orders. Compare scope, not just price.

How long does a roof replacement take?

Most residential roofs take 1-3 days to replace. Complex roofs with multiple layers, steep pitch, or extensive damage can take 4-7 days.
