St. Louis frame material selection

St. Louis condensation and interior moisture management

St. Louis condensation management addresses the Mississippi Valley's distinctive humidity pattern where winter relative humidity averages 72-78% due to river-proximity moisture. Interior surface temperature on single-pane at 15F ambient drops below 38F in Central West End brick four-family flats, causing interior condensation that damages century-old plaster walls and ornamental wood trim. Dual-pane argon-filled IGUs with warm-edge spacer systems (Super Spacer, Edgetech, Cardinal) raise interior surface temperature above 52F and reduce condensation damage. Summer humidity-driven condensation on cool air-conditioned window glass is a secondary St. Louis concern; HVAC dehumidification through properly sized equipment is essential during the July-August humidity peak that averages 76F dew point.
