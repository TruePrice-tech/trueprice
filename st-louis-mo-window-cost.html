What St Louis residents ask about window replacement

Should I replace all windows at once?

Replacing all windows at once saves 10-15% on labor versus doing them individually. It also ensures uniform appearance and consistent energy performance throughout the home.

Do replacement windows increase home value?

New windows recover 65-75% of their cost at resale. They also improve curb appeal, reduce noise, and eliminate a common inspection concern for buyers.

What type of replacement window is best?

Vinyl frames offer the best value with low maintenance and good insulation. Fiberglass is more durable and paintable. Wood has the best aesthetics but needs ongoing maintenance. Aluminum is cheapest but conducts heat.

How much energy do new windows actually save?

Replacing single-pane windows saves 25-30% on heating and cooling costs. Upgrading from older double-pane to modern Low-E double-pane saves 10-15%. Payback takes 10-15 years through energy savings alone.

How much does window replacement cost in St Louis?

Window replacement in St Louis costs $400-$1,200 per window installed, depending on size, frame material, glass type, and accessibility. A whole-house project (10-15 windows) runs $5,000-$15,000.

St. Louis-specific window fraud patterns

The most common St. Louis window fraud exploits the city/county jurisdictional confusion. A contractor pulls a county permit for work performed in the city (or vice versa), the inspection never happens, and the homeowner discovers the issue years later during a sale. Always confirm the permit jurisdiction matches your property address. The second St. Louis-specific scam targets post-storm insurance claims: crews from out of state canvass North County and South City neighborhoods after severe weather events, collect deposits, and disappear. Missouri's lack of a statewide contractor license makes verification harder than in licensed states, so St. Louis due diligence requires checking the city occupational license database, confirming a permanent local business address (not a P.O. box), and cross-referencing at least three local references from jobs completed more than 18 months ago.
