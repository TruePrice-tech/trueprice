Concrete questions for St Petersburg homeowners

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

Cement is an ingredient in concrete. Concrete is a mix of cement (10-15%), water (15-20%), and aggregates (sand and gravel, 60-75%). Concrete is the finished building material; cement is the binding powder.

How much does a concrete foundation cost?

A full basement foundation costs $15,000-$30,000. A slab foundation for a house costs $5,000-$15,000. A garage slab costs $2,500-$6,000. Price depends on size, depth, and soil conditions.

How long before I can drive on new concrete?

Wait 24-48 hours for foot traffic, 7 days for vehicle traffic, and 30 days for full cure. Avoid deicing salt for the first winter. New concrete reaches 90% strength in 7 days and full strength in 28 days.

What is the best time to pour concrete?

Pour when temperatures stay between 50-80F for at least 3 days. Spring and fall are ideal. Avoid pouring in rain, direct hot sun, or below freezing. Cold and heat both compromise cure quality.

More questions about concrete in St. Petersburg

What does a concrete driveway cost per square foot?

Plain 4-inch concrete runs $6-$12 per square foot installed in 2026. Stamped concrete adds $4-$8/sqft for pattern and color. Heavy-duty 6-inch driveways for RVs or trucks cost $9-$15/sqft. A 600 sq ft two-car driveway typically lands between $5,800 and $8,100.

How much does a 1,000 sq ft concrete patio cost?

Typical 2026 ranges for a 1,000 sq ft project: plain broom-finish $8,000-$12,000, exposed-aggregate $9,500-$14,500, stamped $11,000-$16,500. Tearing out an existing slab adds $1,500-$3,000. Reinforcing wire mesh and rebar are usually $0.50-$1.00/sqft and worth it on driveways, optional on patios.

What does a slab foundation cost in St. Petersburg?

A monolithic slab foundation for a typical single-family house in St. Petersburg runs $5,000-$15,000 depending on square footage and soil conditions. A garage slab is $2,500-$6,000. Full basement foundations are a different scope entirely and run $15,000-$30,000.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in St. Petersburg?

Driveways, patios over a certain size, and any structural slab in St. Petersburg require a permit and an inspection of the base prep and rebar before the pour. Sidewalks, repairs, and resurfacing usually do not. Your contractor pulls the permit — never pull it in your own name.

About concrete work in St. Petersburg, FL

Where St. Petersburg's pricing comes from. St. Petersburg's labor market runs about 9% below the national median for skilled trades. This wage differential is the primary driver of the area's below-average project costs. On concrete work, St. Petersburg comes in about 7% under the national average based on our metro pricing data.

Local conditions in St. Petersburg. St. Petersburg has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Concrete in St. Petersburg cures differently than in dry climates — high humidity slows evaporation, which actually improves curing strength but extends the timeline. The bigger concern is expansive clay soils common in this region, which cause heave and cracking. Proper sub-base preparation (4-6 inches of compacted gravel) is non-negotiable. Homes in St. Petersburg averaging 33 years old typically have stable sub-grades that simplify concrete work. New pours integrate cleanly with existing slabs and foundations without the settling corrections older properties need.

St. Petersburg permits and code requirements. Permits are usually required for concrete work in St. Petersburg; verify the specifics with the local building department before committing.

The St. Petersburg contractor pool. With a population of 249,688, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. The St. Petersburg market has grown at a steady pace, producing a solid base of experienced contractors without the severe backlogs of booming cities.
