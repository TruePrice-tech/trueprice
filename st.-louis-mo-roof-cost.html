Roof Replacement Cost in St. Louis, MO

The average roof replacement cost in St. Louis typically ranges between $9,100 and $18,000 depending on roof size, material, pitch, tear off, and complexity.

Typical St. Louis Roofing Prices

  • Architectural shingles: $500 to $750 per square
  • Metal roofing: $1,000 to $1,600 per square
  • Tile roofing: $1,500 to $3,000 per square

How to Compare Roofing Quotes

Do not compare roofing quotes by price alone. A lower quote may exclude flashing replacement, ventilation upgrades, underlayment quality, tear off, or disposal.

What Affects Roof Replacement Cost in St. Louis?

  • Roof size and number of stories
  • Pitch and jobsite complexity
  • Material selection
  • Local labor and permit conditions
  • Whether old roofing must be removed

Local Roofing Guide for St. Louis, MO

St. Louis gets the full range of weather stress on a roof: summer heat and humidity, occasional severe storms, and enough cold-weather cycling to test flashing and sealant integrity over time.

St. Louis in the greater metro area experiences extreme seasonal temperature swings, spring severe weather including tornadoes and hail, and winter ice storms that stress building envelopes.

Impact-resistant materials are a smart investment in St. Louis given the spring hail risk, and any material selection should account for the freeze-thaw cycling from November through March.

The City of St. Louis operates independently from St. Louis County with separate building departments; verify which jurisdiction your property falls in before starting work.

A building permit from the City of St. Louis Building Division is required before any major home improvement project can begin.

Hail is an occasional but real risk in St. Louis. While not as frequent as cities in the central plains, impact-resistant materials are still worth the 10-15% cost premium for homeowners planning to stay in their home for 10+ years.

With an average home age of 55 years, many St. Louis roofs are on their second or third replacement. Older homes frequently need decking repairs, upgraded ventilation to current code, and sometimes structural reinforcement before new roofing goes on. Budget an additional 10-20% contingency for homes built before 1985.
