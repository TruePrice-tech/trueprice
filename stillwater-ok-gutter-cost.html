Gutter questions for Stillwater homeowners

How much do new gutters cost in Stillwater?

Gutters in Stillwater cost $6-$15 per linear foot for aluminum seamless, $8-$20 for steel, and $15-$30 for copper. A typical home (150-200 linear feet) runs $1,200-$3,000 for aluminum.

What causes gutters to pull away from the house?

Common causes: rotted fascia board behind the gutter, failed or spaced-too-far-apart hangers, ice and snow weight, clogged gutters holding heavy water, and improper original installation.

Do gutters protect the foundation?

Yes. Gutters prevent concentrated roof runoff from eroding soil next to the foundation and saturating the ground. Homes without gutters have 3-5x more foundation moisture problems than homes with properly functioning gutter systems.

How Stillwater pricing is built. BLS data puts Stillwater's skilled trade wages near the national midpoint. What Stillwater's local context means for pricing. Stillwater has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Standard 5-inch K-style gutters handle most rain events in Stillwater. Add gutter guards ($5-12 per linear foot) where overhanging trees drop heavy seasonal debris. Leaf debris in gutters promotes standing water and mosquito breeding — seasonal cleaning or guards are important for both function and health. Seamless aluminum gutters are the Stillwater standard: $6-12 per linear foot installed. Copper ($20-40/ft) and zinc ($15-25/ft) offer 50+ year lifespans but at 3-4x the cost. For most homeowners, aluminum with a quality finish provides the best value at 20-30 years before replacement.
