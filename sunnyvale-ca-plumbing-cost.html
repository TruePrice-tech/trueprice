Questions Sunnyvale homeowners ask about plumbing

How much does it cost to replace a water heater?

A standard 50-gallon tank water heater runs $1,200-$2,500 installed. Tankless units cost $2,500-$5,000 but last roughly twice as long and cut energy bills 20-30%.

What does a plumbing inspection include?

A thorough inspection covers supply lines, drain lines, water heater condition, fixture connections, shutoff valves, water pressure testing, and a visual check for leaks and corrosion.

How much does a plumber charge per hour in Sunnyvale?

Plumbers in Sunnyvale typically charge $75-$150 per hour. Emergency and after-hours calls add a $100-$200 trip fee on top of the hourly rate.

How often should I have my drains cleaned in Sunnyvale?

Annual drain maintenance is recommended for most Sunnyvale homes. Older homes with mature trees nearby may need semi-annual service to prevent root intrusion.

How often should I have my plumbing inspected in Sunnyvale?

Annual inspections are the standard recommendation for Sunnyvale homes. Key things your plumber should check: water heater age and condition (failure typically comes at 8-12 years), supply line connections at every fixture, and drain performance. Homes older than 20 years benefit from a sewer camera inspection ($150-$400) every few years since underground lines deteriorate silently.

What should a plumbing quote include in Sunnyvale?

A Sunnyvale plumbing estimate should detail: scope of work, material specifications, labor costs, permit fees if needed, waste disposal, and warranty. Check that the plumber's license number and insurance are included. Compare three written quotes for any project over $500; make sure the scope matches across all three.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Sunnyvale?

For non-emergency plumbing in Sunnyvale, schedule during late spring or early fall, dry weather simplifies any excavation and contractor availability is better. Emergency calls cost 50-100% more than scheduled work, so replacing aging water heaters, supply lines, and fixtures proactively during off-peak months saves significantly versus waiting for a failure.
