Fencing questions for Tamarac homeowners

How long does fence installation take?

A standard 150-200 linear foot residential fence takes 1-3 days to install. Hillside or rocky soil adds time. Post holes need 24-48 hours to cure if set in concrete.

How deep should fence posts be?

Standard rule: bury one-third of the total post length. For a 6-foot fence with 8-foot posts, dig 24-30 inches deep. In frost-prone areas, posts must go below the frost line to prevent heaving.

How much does vinyl fencing cost?

Vinyl privacy fencing costs $20-$40 per linear foot installed. A typical 150 linear foot backyard fence runs $3,000-$6,000. Vinyl never needs painting, resists rot, and lasts 25-30 years.

What fence material lasts longest in Tamarac's climate?

Tamarac's humidity and heat are hard on wood fences. Vinyl and aluminum fences last 10-15 years longer because they do not rot, attract termites, or absorb moisture. If wood is your preference, pressure-treated pine or cedar hold up best but require staining or sealing every 2-3 years. Composite fencing is a middle-ground option that looks like wood but handles the humidity like vinyl.

Do I need a permit to build a fence in Tamarac?

In Tamarac, fence permits are standard for installations over 6 feet and sometimes required for any fence. Three things to handle before installation: call 811 for utility marking, confirm property boundaries (budget $300-$600 for a survey if needed), and review any HOA rules. The fence contractor should manage the permitting as part of the project scope.

Fence installation in Tamarac typically costs $15-$30 per linear foot for wood privacy, $20-$40 for vinyl, $10-$20 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron.
