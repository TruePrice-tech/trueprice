Questions Tampa homeowners ask about siding

How long does siding installation take?

A full siding replacement on an average home takes 5-10 days. Larger homes or complex designs with multiple gables, dormers, and trim details can take 2-3 weeks.

How do I maintain fiber cement siding?

Repaint every 12-15 years, caulk joints and seams every 5-7 years, keep vegetation trimmed 6 inches from the wall, and wash annually with a garden hose. Avoid power washing at high pressure.

Do I need a permit for siding replacement in Tampa?

Most Tampa jurisdictions require a permit if you are changing siding material or adding insulation. Like-for-like replacement without structural changes may be exempt. Check with your local building department.

What is board-and-batten siding?

Board-and-batten uses wide vertical boards with narrow strips (battens) covering the seams. It is popular in farmhouse and modern styles. Costs $8-$16/sqft installed in fiber cement or engineered wood.

Can new siding go over old siding?

Overlaying is possible but not recommended. It hides potential water damage, adds weight, and can void warranties. Tear-off adds $1,000-$3,000 but lets you inspect and repair sheathing and moisture barriers.

Tampa climate impact on siding

Tampa siding endures Gulf-side tropical storms tracking across warm Bay water, karst-sinkhole terrain that shifts foundations and propagates stucco cracks unpredictably, and relentless UV that chalks south-facing stucco finishes in 8-12 years. The dominant Tampa failure pattern is distinct from South Florida: post-tensioned slab movement on karst limestone in Seffner, Temple Terrace, and north Hillsborough opens hairline stucco cracks that admit wind-driven rain behind the CBS assembly, leading to concealed moisture damage that surfaces years later. Pinellas beach communities face additional salt-corrosion cycling that accelerates fastener and flashing failure on any metal component.

Pinellas County beach communities (Clearwater Beach, Indian Rocks Beach, Treasure Island, Pass-a-Grille, St. Pete Beach) and Hillsborough Gulf-adjacent zones (Davis Islands, Bayshore Boulevard, Harbour Island) see severe salt corrosion. Stainless 316 fasteners are the only durable spec; standard galvanized pits within 3-4 years in the Gulf salt environment. Marine-coated aluminum J-channel and soffit resist salt better than raw aluminum but still degrade faster than copper or bronze. Gulf-side installations also face periodic red-tide aerosol exposure that accelerates surface staining on light-colored stucco and fiber cement.
