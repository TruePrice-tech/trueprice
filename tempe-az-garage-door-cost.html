Questions Tempe homeowners ask about garage door

How much does garage door spring replacement cost?

Torsion spring replacement costs $200-$400 for parts and labor. Extension springs cost $150-$300. Never attempt spring replacement yourself since the stored tension is extremely dangerous.

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Tempe?

Garage door replacement in Tempe typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

Local factors: garage door replacement in Tempe, AZ

Home stock in Tempe. Tempe's housing stock averages 24 years old — relatively recent construction that was largely built to modern code. garage door replacement projects here tend to be simpler: fewer surprise compliance issues, quicker permit turnaround, and contractors who can quote from memory.

HOA & compliance. HOAs are common in Tempe. For garage door replacement, factor in 2-4 weeks of HOA architectural review before starting, and collect pre-approval of materials in writing. HOA rejections after project start can be expensive.

Local market conditions. Tempe has moderate construction activity. Contractors are busy but accessible with 1-3 weeks' notice. Off-season quotes (contractor-dependent) can run 8-15% below peak rates. Tempe's large market has dedicated garage door companies, big-box retailer installation services, and independent contractors. Dedicated garage door companies typically offer the widest selection and fastest service but price 10-20% above big-box options for equivalent products.

Seasonal timing. Garage door replacement in Tempe isn't highly seasonal — it's a 4-6 hour indoor/outdoor job that works year-round. However, spring (March-May) is the busiest period as homeowners address winter damage and start curb-appeal projects. Off-peak months (July-September, November-January) offer faster scheduling.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Tempe track within a few points of the national average for garage door replacement, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. Tempe's relatively young housing stock (24 years average) simplifies most garage door replacement projects. Red flags to watch for. Any Tempe contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion. Watch for garage door replacement quotes in Tempe that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Lump-sum bids hide margin and make change orders impossible to evaluate.
