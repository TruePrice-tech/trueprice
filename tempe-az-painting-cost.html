Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to paint a house exterior in Tempe?

Most homeowners in Tempe pay between $5,200 and $10,600 for exterior painting, depending on home size and paint quality.

How many coats of paint does an exterior need?

Most exterior painting jobs require at least two coats for proper coverage and durability. A single coat may work for touch-ups or if the existing paint is in good condition with a similar color.

How long does exterior paint last?

Quality exterior paint typically lasts 5-10 years depending on climate, sun exposure, and paint quality. Premium paints like Sherwin-Williams Duration can last 10-15 years.

About exterior painting in Tempe, AZ

Why pricing varies in Tempe. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. For exterior painting specifically, Tempe sits within a few points of the national midpoint on our cost model.

Local conditions. Tempe has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Tempe's dry heat causes paint to crack and peel differently than humid climates — the wood substrate dries out and contracts, pulling paint films apart at joints and edges. Proper caulking and flexible primers matter more here than in temperate regions. Tempe homes averaging 24 years old typically have 1-3 paint layers in good condition. Prep is simpler — power wash, spot-prime bare spots, and apply 2 coats of finish. This keeps the overall project cost lower than older homes with heavy prep requirements.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most exterior painting projects in Tempe require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 168,228, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Tempe has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.

Local factors: exterior painting in Tempe, AZ

Home stock in Tempe. Tempe's housing stock averages 24 years old — relatively recent construction that was largely built to modern code. exterior painting projects here tend to be simpler: fewer surprise compliance issues, quicker permit turnaround, and contractors who can quote from memory.

HOA & compliance. HOAs are common in Tempe. For exterior painting, factor in 2-4 weeks of HOA architectural review before starting, and collect pre-approval of materials in writing. HOA rejections after project start can be expensive.

Local market conditions. Tempe has moderate construction activity. Contractors are busy but accessible with 1-3 weeks' notice. Off-season quotes (contractor-dependent) can run 8-15% below peak rates. Tempe's large market supports both premium and budget painting contractors. Prices vary 30-50% for the same scope — the difference is usually prep quality, not paint brand. Ask every bidder to detail their prep process in writing.

Seasonal timing. In Tempe, the best painting conditions are October through March — cooler temperatures and lower humidity allow proper curing. Summer painting is possible but requires early-morning starts and careful attention to direct sun exposure during application.

What drives cost. With Tempe labor rates near the national median, the cost difference between a budget and premium whole-house exterior paint job comes down to materials and scope rather than labor premiums. Newer construction in Tempe (averaging 24 years) means exterior painting projects rarely encounter the hidden-scope surprises common in older markets. What you see in the quote is usually what you pay.

Red flags to watch for. Any Tempe contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion. Watch for exterior painting quotes in Tempe that lack line-item detail. A professional estimate breaks out labor, materials, permits, and cleanup separately. Lump-sum bids hide margin and make change orders impossible to evaluate.
