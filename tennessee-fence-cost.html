Fence Installation Cost in Tennessee (2026)

Installing 150 linear feet of fencing in Tennessee typically runs $4,400–$11,500, with 6-foot pressure-treated southern yellow pine the dominant residential choice. Concrete-set posts must clear the 12-inch design frost line. Tennessee sits inside Termite Probability and Climatic Tier 2 with annual subterranean termite pressure across the entire state — every in-ground wood post requires CCA-treated southern yellow pine .60 or higher per Tennessee Building Code, and the Tennessee River and Cumberland River valley flood-elevation overlays drive concrete-encased steel or treated wood posts on every Memphis, Nashville, and Knoxville riverfront retrofit.

Tennessee climate & post-design drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 12-inch design minimum for post footings
  • Dominant residential fence material: Pressure-treated southern yellow pine — dominant low-budget Sun Belt and Plains
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — 110–140 mph design wind drives 8-foot post spacing
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): High — TPCT zone 2, PT post requirement
  • HOA fencing-rule prevalence: Moderate — suburban HOA prevalence on newer subdivisions

Tennessee licensing, HOA & boundary law

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Tennessee Board for Licensing Contractors (residential >$25,000) (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal permit typical for residential fence over 6 feet

How fence installation costs vary in Tennessee

State-specific code or insurance rule: Tennessee Board for Licensing Contractors requires the Contractor license (BC, BC-A, BC-B classifications) on any residential project with total contract value above $25,000 — and unique to Tennessee, the Home Improvement license is a separately tested credential required for residential remodeling work between $3,000 and $25,000, with the Tennessee Department of Commerce & Insurance enforcing dual credentialing on contractors operating across both thresholds.
