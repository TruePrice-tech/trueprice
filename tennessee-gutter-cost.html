Gutter Installation Cost in Tennessee (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Tennessee typically runs $1,100–$4,100, with 6-inch oversized K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 53 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Tennessee's 53-inch annual rainfall concentrated in spring tornado-driven and summer thunderstorm-driven downbursts pushes 6-inch oversized K-style as standard residential profile — and high mature-deciduous canopy across the Tennessee Valley drives gutter-guard retrofit demand on long-tenure Nashville, Knoxville, and Chattanooga suburbs within 2-3 years of original installation.

Tennessee rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 53 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 6-inch oversized K-style seamless aluminum — dominant in high-rainfall and steep-pitch corridors
  • Ground snow load: 10 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: Low — occasional freeze events, minimal ice-dam history
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: High — mature deciduous canopy drives gutter-guard demand and 2x annual cleaning frequency

Tennessee licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Tennessee Board for Licensing Contractors (residential >$25,000) (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction

How gutter installation costs vary in Tennessee

Tennessee's 53-inch annual rainfall concentrated in spring tornado-driven and summer thunderstorm-driven downbursts pushes 6-inch oversized K-style as standard residential profile — and high mature-deciduous canopy across the Tennessee Valley drives gutter-guard retrofit demand on long-tenure Nashville, Knoxville, and Chattanooga suburbs within 2-3 years of original installation.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Tennessee Board for Licensing Contractors requires the Contractor license (BC, BC-A, BC-B classifications) on any residential project with total contract value above $25,000 — and unique to Tennessee, the Home Improvement license is a separately tested credential required for residential remodeling work between $3,000 and $25,000, with the Tennessee Department of Commerce & Insurance enforcing dual credentialing on contractors operating across both thresholds.
