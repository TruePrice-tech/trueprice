Window Replacement Cost in Tennessee (2026)

Window replacements in Tennessee typically run $4,800–$13,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR South-Central climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.32, SHGC ≤ 0.4). Tennessee's combination of New Madrid Seismic Zone influence across 14 west Tennessee counties from Memphis to Jackson forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC C-D0 seismic-detailed window header attachment on every west Tennessee residential install, F2 freeze-thaw cycling across the eastern half, ENERGY STAR South-Central climate zone driving moderate U-factor (≤0.32) and SHGC (≤0.40) requirements, Memphis Heritage Conservation district review on every Memphis historic-district window replacement, and Middle Tennessee karst limestone subsoil drives moderate seismic-and-historic-driven glazing selection.

Tennessee climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: South-Central — mixed climate, balanced U-factor and lower SHGC (U ≤ 0.32, SHGC ≤ 0.40)
  • IECC climate zone: 3A-4A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.32 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: 0.4
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 115 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Moderate — selected metro historic districts with review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Moderate — 20-40% pre-1978 housing share, EPA RRP required on selected replacements

Tennessee licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Tennessee Board for Licensing Contractors — Residential Contractor License (BC-A.1) for new construction (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification; TN Residential Contractor BC-A.1 license required above $25,000 contract; New Madrid Seismic Zone Special Inspection required in 14 west TN counties for window header attachments; Memphis Heritage Conservation district review required

How window replacement costs vary in Tennessee

Tennessee's combination of New Madrid Seismic Zone influence across 14 west Tennessee counties from Memphis to Jackson forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC C-D0 seismic-detailed window header attachment on every west Tennessee residential install, F2 freeze-thaw cycling across the eastern half, ENERGY STAR South-Central climate zone driving moderate U-factor (≤0.32) and SHGC (≤0.40) requirements, Memphis Heritage Conservation district review on every Memphis historic-district window replacement, and Middle Tennessee karst limestone subsoil drives moderate seismic-and-historic-driven glazing selection.
