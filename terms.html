4. Methodology

For transparency, here is exactly how Woogoro generates auto repair, home services, and other cost estimates. We believe users deserve to know the inputs behind any number we show.

Auto repair labor pricing. Labor rates are derived from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS) survey, May 2024 release, occupation code SOC 49-3023 (Automotive Service Technicians and Mechanics). We use the published mean or median hourly mechanic wage for each Metropolitan Statistical Area, then apply industry-standard shop overhead multipliers (approximately 3.0x for independent shops, 3.5x for chain shops, and 4.5x for dealerships) to arrive at the customer-facing labor rate. Where no MSA data exists for a user's location, we fall back to the state median wage, then to the national median.

Auto repair labor hours. Labor hour ranges are seeded from industry-typical values for each repair category. These are estimates of how long an average mechanic spends on an average vehicle in average condition. Modified, lifted, rusted, salvage-title, or non-stock vehicles often take significantly longer.

Auto repair parts pricing. Parts are priced from industry-typical retail ranges by tier: Aftermarket (third-party brands), OEM (original equipment manufacturer in plain packaging), Dealer/Genuine (the same OEM part in automaker-branded packaging at a 30-60% markup), and Remanufactured (factory-rebuilt cores). Auto parts are mostly nationally-distributed commodities, so a brake caliper costs roughly the same in San Francisco as in rural Georgia. We apply only about 25% of the local geographic factor to parts pricing (capturing shop-markup variance and local fees) rather than the full mechanic wage delta we apply to labor.

Shop supplies, taxes, and fees. Shop supplies are estimated at approximately 6% of labor (industry standard). Sales tax is computed using each state's sales tax rate applied to parts and supplies. Hazmat, disposal, refrigerant recovery, and environmental fees are added per repair category where applicable. Diagnostic fees, towing, programming charges, and after-hours premiums are NOT included in our totals.

Crowdsourced calibration (the flywheel). When users submit real shop quotes through Woogoro analysis tools, we anonymize the data (price, repair type, city, state, vehicle category -- no personal information) and aggregate it into a per-metro per-repair calibration store. When at least 3 real user quotes exist for a specific repair in a specific metro, we display the real average alongside our seeded estimate so users can see how the benchmark compares to actual local prices.

Home services and other verticals. Similar methodologies apply: federal labor data where available, industry-typical material and parts ranges, regional adjustments, and crowdsourced calibration from user-submitted quotes. Methodology specifics vary by vertical and are continuously refined.

What our estimates are NOT. Our estimates are benchmarks, not appraisals. They are not insurance appraisals, court-admissible expert opinions, binding quotes, or substitutes for licensed professional assessments. They should not be used as the sole basis for legal disputes, insurance claims, warranty contests, or major financial decisions. Always obtain at least three quotes from licensed local professionals.

DIY safety. Where Woogoro offers a "DIY parts only" mode for auto repair, the displayed savings reflect parts cost alone. They do not account for the value of your time, the cost of specialized tools, EPA certifications required for refrigerant work, or the safety risks of attempting repairs you are not qualified to perform. Brakes, fuel systems, suspension, airbag systems, and load-bearing components carry serious safety risk when done incorrectly. Know your limits.

Federal labor data. Auto repair labor rates are derived from the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS) survey, currently the May 2024 release. BLS publishes this data annually. Between releases, the figures may not reflect the latest market conditions, wage movements, or local labor shortages.

Industry-typical seed data. Labor hour ranges, parts pricing tiers, and method comparisons are seeded from published industry surveys, trade publications, manufacturer catalogs, and our own research. They are estimates of typical conditions for typical projects on typical vehicles or homes. Modified, lifted, rusted, salvage-title, historic, or otherwise non-stock items will not match these defaults.

Crowdsourced calibration. Crowdsourced averages are not independently verified, may be biased by self-selection, and may include data months or years old. They are presented as additional context, not as authoritative pricing.

AI-generated extraction. AI extraction can produce errors including missing line items, misreading numbers, hallucinating fields, or misclassifying services. Always cross-reference our extracted data against the original document before relying on it.
