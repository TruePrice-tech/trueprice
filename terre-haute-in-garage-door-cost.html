Terre Haute garage door FAQ

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Terre Haute?

Garage door replacement in Terre Haute typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

What causes a garage door to be noisy?

Common causes: worn rollers (replace nylon for steel), dry hinges (lubricate with silicone spray), loose hardware, a chain-drive opener, or worn spring coils. A tune-up costs $100-$200.

What is the best type of garage door?

Insulated steel with polyurethane foam offers the best balance of durability, energy efficiency, and cost. R-value of 12-18 keeps the garage 10-20 degrees closer to house temperature.

What R-value garage door do I need in Terre Haute?

For Terre Haute's cold winters, an R-12 to R-16 insulated door is recommended for attached garages. If you heat your garage or use it as a workshop, step up to R-16 or R-18. The extra $200-$500 for higher insulation pays back in 3-5 years through reduced heating costs in adjacent rooms and prevents freezing issues with items stored in the garage.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Terre Haute?

With Terre Haute homes averaging 45 years, many still have original garage doors past their useful life. A full replacement ($1,200-$3,000 with a new opener) typically returns 90-100% at resale. Repair is worth it only for newer doors with isolated issues like a single broken spring, damaged panel, or failing opener motor. If the door is original to the house, replacement is the better investment.

When should I replace a garage door in Terre Haute?

The ideal window for a Terre Haute garage door replacement is late spring or early fall. Moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing, plus you get access to a wider range of styles and colors. Avoid spring if you can, since that is when winter-related spring breakages create peak demand and longer wait times.
