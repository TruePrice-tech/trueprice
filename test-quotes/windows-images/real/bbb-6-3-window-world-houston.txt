SOURCE: BBB complaint - Window World

Window World of Houston incorrectly measured and installed undersized windows in my home. They concealed the gaps with trim instead of properly replacing them, violating both City of Houston building code (2021 IRC R609.1, R703.4) and the manufacturers installation guidelines. As a result, the windows leaked and caused over $4,000 in water damage.Despite reinstalling the windows, the leaks persist. I have left the interior gypsum board removed, making the visible water intrusion evident from inside the home. I have attached a screenshot of a video taken March 29th from inside my home that shows water droplets on the interior side of the windows 24 hours after a rain storm. The water intrusion would be hidden if I allowed them to install decorative interior trim as before. The field manager repeatedly stated that they do not need to follow building code because this is not new construction. I provided the code stating otherwise. When provided with manufacturer installation guidelines, they refused to follow them. They also refused to provide contact information for escalation beyond a field supervisor who lacks knowledge of building codes and proper installation.I have documented proof, including photos of the improper installation, visible leaks, and water damage.Requested Resolution:Full refund of my purchase price due to the repeated failure to install the windows properly.$4,000 reimbursement for property damage repairs caused by the leaks.I do not trust Window World to reinstall the windows a third time and will be hiring another company to fabricate new windows and install them correctly.Window World of Houston has demonstrated a disregard for building code compliance and proper installation practices. I expect them to take financial responsibility for the damage they caused.
