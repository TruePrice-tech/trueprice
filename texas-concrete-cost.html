Concrete Driveway Cost in Texas (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in Texas typically run $3,000–$8,200 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 3500 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 6-inch design frost line, and the state sits in no freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F0). Texas climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 6-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F0 — no freeze-thaw exposure, no air-entrainment required
  • Typical residential mix: 3500 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: Very high — bentonitic clay subsoil drives post-tension foundation requirement
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S2 — severe sulfate exposure, ASTM C150 Type II/V cement required
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC A — no seismic design considerations
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: Low — minimal winter de-icer use

Texas licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: No statewide concrete license; Texas Department of Licensing & Regulation issues no concrete-specific credential (official site)
  • Permit: city building permit required for residential driveway pour > 200 sq ft; municipal contractor registration required in Houston, Dallas, San Antonio, Austin, Fort Worth on residential exterior contracting
