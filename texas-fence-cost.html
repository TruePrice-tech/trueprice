Fence Installation Cost in Texas (2026)

Installing 150 linear feet of fencing in Texas typically runs $4,200–$11,500, with 6-foot pressure-treated southern yellow pine the dominant residential choice. Concrete-set posts must clear the 10-inch design frost line. Texas spans hurricane-exposed Gulf Coast (Tier 1 TWIA counties), Tornado Alley central plains, and arid Trans-Pecos west — Houston, Galveston, and Corpus Christi retrofits face high-wind engineered post-spacing under 6 feet with concrete-set requirements, while Oklahoma-border North Texas and Panhandle properties face annual EF1-EF3 hail with reinforced post-bracing now standard on Lubbock, Amarillo, and Wichita Falls retrofits — and Texas Hill Country and South Texas sit inside Termite Probability and Climatic Tier 1.

Texas climate & post-design drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 10-inch design minimum for post footings
  • Dominant residential fence material: Pressure-treated southern yellow pine — dominant low-budget Sun Belt and Plains
  • Hurricane wind tier: High — 140 mph+ design wind drives 8-foot post spacing max and concrete sleeves
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Very high — TPCT zone 1, all in-ground wood requires PT southern yellow pine .60 CCA or alternative
  • HOA fencing-rule prevalence: High — large planned-community HOA penetration restricts material/color/height

Texas licensing, HOA & boundary law

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: No statewide fencing contractor license — municipal building department oversight only (official site)
  • Permit: city or municipal permit typical for residential fence over 6 feet; Texas Windstorm Insurance Association (TWIA) rules apply on Tier 1 coastal counties

How fence installation costs vary in Texas

Texas spans hurricane-exposed Gulf Coast (Tier 1 TWIA counties), Tornado Alley central plains, and arid Trans-Pecos west — Houston, Galveston, and Corpus Christi retrofits face high-wind engineered post-spacing under 6 feet with concrete-set requirements, while Oklahoma-border North Texas and Panhandle properties face annual EF1-EF3 hail with reinforced post-bracing now standard on Lubbock, Amarillo, and Wichita Falls retrofits — and Texas Hill Country and South Texas sit inside Termite Probability and Climatic Tier 1.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Texas Property Code §202.018 (the Texas HOA Fencing Statute, 2021) preempts HOA architectural review controls that prohibit storm-resistant fencing materials — making Texas the only state where homeowners in HOA-governed subdivisions can install impact-rated metal or storm-resistant composite fencing notwithstanding HOA color or material restrictions, in response to post-Harvey hurricane-driven fence loss in Houston-area master-planned communities, with the law allowing reasonable HOA height and setback rules but prohibiting outright material bans.
