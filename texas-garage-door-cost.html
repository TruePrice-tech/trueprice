Garage Door Replacement Cost in Texas (2026)

Garage door replacements in Texas typically run $1,100–$5,400 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 130 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Texas's combination of Gulf Coast hurricane wind exposure driving Texas Coastal Building Code DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating mandate at 130-150 mph design wind on every coastal residential install, Texas Black Prairie expansive clay subsoil shrink-swell across the Dallas-Fort Worth-Austin-San Antonio metropolitan corridor driving aggressive header-attachment detailing on every metro residential install, very-high TPCT termite zone 1 pressure across the entire state, very-high HOA architectural-control prevalence across 75%+ of Texas metro subdivisions, and extreme summer UV exposure across the Sun Belt drives unique hurricane-and-Black-Prairie-and-HOA-driven material selection.

Texas climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 130 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: High — DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating at 140-150 mph design wind
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Chain-drive — dominant on detached or budget installs, durable and economical
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Very high — TPCT zone 1, all wood jamb framing requires PT southern yellow pine .60 CCA
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Very high — 80%+ of subdivisions enforce HOA architectural-committee pre-approval on door color/material/style
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High — coastal salt-spray drives stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track requirement at 5-7 year intervals
  • IECC climate zone: 2A-4B

Texas licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Texas Department of Licensing & Regulation oversees specialty trades only; Texas Real Estate Commission Residential Construction Liability Act (RCLA) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening on new construction; Texas has no statewide residential builder license; Texas Coastal Building Code mandatory hurricane wind tie-down on Gulf Coast counties; HOA architectural-committee approval mandatory in 75%+ of Dallas-Fort Worth, Houston, Austin, and San Antonio subdivisions

How garage door installation costs vary in Texas

State-specific code or insurance rule: Texas Residential Construction Liability Act (RCLA, Texas Property Code Chapter 27) is one of the most homeowner-favorable construction defect laws in the country — every Texas residential garage-door contractor must comply with 60-day pre-suit notice provisions, with affected homeowners able to recover up to $200,000 in damages plus attorney fees for installation defects, and Texas is unusual among large states in not requiring a statewide residential builder license — Texas Department of Licensing & Regulation oversees only specialty trades like plumbers, electricians, and HVAC installers, plus Texas HOA architectural-committee prevalence across 75%+ of Dallas-Fort Worth, Houston, Austin, and San Antonio subdivisions drives mandatory pre-approval review on every garage-door color/material/style.
