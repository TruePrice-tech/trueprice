Gutter Installation Cost in Texas (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Texas typically runs $1,100–$4,200, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 28 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Texas's 28-inch state-average annual rainfall masks dramatic east-west variation — Houston receives 50+ inches/year requiring 6-inch oversized K-style on Gulf Coast residential properties, while El Paso and the Trans-Pecos receive under 10 inches/year and routinely defer gutter installation entirely — and Houston, Galveston, and Corpus Christi hurricane wind exposure drives stainless mounting hardware on every Tier 1 coastal retrofit.

Texas rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 28 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 5 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: No ice-dam exposure
  • Hurricane wind tier: High — 140 mph+ design wind on coastal counties
  • Tree-canopy density: Moderate — mixed canopy, gutter guards optional

Texas licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: No statewide gutter contractor license — municipal building department oversight only (official site)
  • Permit: city or municipal building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction; Texas Windstorm Insurance Association (TWIA) inspection on Tier 1 coastal counties

How gutter installation costs vary in Texas

Texas's 28-inch state-average annual rainfall masks dramatic east-west variation — Houston receives 50+ inches/year requiring 6-inch oversized K-style on Gulf Coast residential properties, while El Paso and the Trans-Pecos receive under 10 inches/year and routinely defer gutter installation entirely — and Houston, Galveston, and Corpus Christi hurricane wind exposure drives stainless mounting hardware on every Tier 1 coastal retrofit.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Texas has no statewide residential contractor licensing for gutter installation — making Texas one of the most lightly regulated construction-licensing states in the country — but Texas Insurance Code Chapter 2210 (Texas Windstorm Insurance Association) requires WPI-8 certificate of inspection on every roofing or envelope project including gutter installation in 14 Tier 1 coastal counties from Cameron to Jefferson, without which residential properties cannot obtain TWIA windstorm coverage.
