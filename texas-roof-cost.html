Roof Replacement Cost in Texas (2026)

Roof replacement in Texas typically runs $7,400–$20,000 for a 2,000 sq ft asphalt re-roof, with asphalt shingle as the dominant residential covering. Texas spans hurricane wind zones (Houston, Galveston, Corpus Christi 140-mph) and the heart of Hail Alley (Dallas–Fort Worth, San Antonio, Austin) — Class 4 impact-resistant shingles plus six-nail high-wind installation are the dominant combined insurance-driven upgrade.

Texas climate & code drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 2A-2B-3A-3B-4B
  • Hurricane wind tier: High wind exposure — 140 mph+ design wind on coastal counties
  • Hail risk tier: Very high — central U.S. hail corridor, 8+ severe hail days/year
  • Ground snow load: 5 psf — minimal snow load
  • Dominant residential roof material: asphalt shingle

Texas licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: No statewide roofing license — no exam, no bond requirement; some cities (Houston, Dallas, Austin) require local registration (official site)
  • Permit: city permit required

How roof replacement costs vary in Texas

Texas spans hurricane wind zones (Houston, Galveston, Corpus Christi 140-mph) and the heart of Hail Alley (Dallas–Fort Worth, San Antonio, Austin) — Class 4 impact-resistant shingles plus six-nail high-wind installation are the dominant combined insurance-driven upgrade.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Texas Insurance Code §707 (the Roofing Insurance Anti-Rebating Statute) prohibits roofers from waiving or rebating insurance deductibles on residential storm claims; Texas Department of Insurance Order 2013-1 caps a contractor's cosmetic-versus-functional damage assessment exposure on hail claims.
