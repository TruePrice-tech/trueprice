Siding Replacement Cost in Texas (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Texas typically runs $9,500–$26,000, with brick veneer over wood frame the dominant residential cladding choice. Texas spans hurricane-exposed Gulf Coast, Tornado Alley central plains, and arid Trans-Pecos west — Houston, Galveston, and Corpus Christi retrofits face Notice of Acceptance fastener schedules and stainless component requirements, while Oklahoma-border North Texas and Panhandle properties face annual EF1-EF3 hail with Class 4 impact-rated cladding now standard on Lubbock, Amarillo, and Wichita Falls retrofits.

Texas climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 2A-3A-3B-4B
  • Dominant residential cladding: Brick veneer — dominant in tornado-alley and historic-district overlays
  • Hurricane wind tier: High wind exposure — 140 mph+ design wind on coastal counties
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: No statewide WUI cladding mandate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High-corrosion coastal — stainless or hot-dipped galvanized fasteners required within 3,000 ft of saltwater

Texas licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: No statewide siding contractor license — municipal building department oversight only (official site)
  • Permit: city or municipal building permit required for full re-side; Texas Windstorm Insurance Association (TWIA) inspection on Tier 1 coastal counties

How siding replacement costs vary in Texas

State-specific code or insurance rule: Texas has no statewide residential contractor licensing for siding, roofing, or general remodeling — making Texas one of the most lightly regulated construction-licensing states in the country — but Texas Insurance Code Chapter 2210 (Texas Windstorm Insurance Association) requires WPI-8 certificate of inspection on every roofing or envelope project in 14 Tier 1 coastal counties from Cameron to Jefferson, without which residential properties cannot obtain TWIA windstorm coverage.
