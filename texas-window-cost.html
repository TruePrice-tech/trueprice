Window Replacement Cost in Texas (2026)

Window replacements in Texas typically run $5,200–$16,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR South-Central climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.32, SHGC ≤ 0.25). Texas's combination of Gulf Coast hurricane wind exposure driving Texas Coastal Building Code ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated window mandate on every coastal residential install at 130-150 mph design wind, Texas Black Prairie expansive clay subsoil shrink-swell across the Dallas-Fort Worth-Austin-San Antonio metropolitan corridor driving aggressive header-attachment detailing, ENERGY STAR South-Central climate zone driving low-SHGC (≤0.25) glazing requirement, very-high HOA architectural-control prevalence across 75%+ of Texas metro subdivisions, and TWIA Wind Loss Mitigation insurance-discount eligibility for impact-rated glazing drives unique TWIA-and-Black-Prairie-driven glazing selection.

Texas climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: South-Central — mixed climate, balanced U-factor and lower SHGC (U ≤ 0.32, SHGC ≤ 0.40)
  • IECC climate zone: 2A-4B
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.32 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: 0.25
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 130 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: Mandatory ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated in coastal counties only
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Moderate — selected metro historic districts with review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Low — sub-20% pre-1978 housing share, RRP rarely triggered

Texas licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Texas Department of Licensing & Regulation oversees specialty trades only; Texas Real Estate Commission Residential Construction Liability Act (RCLA) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification on new construction; Texas has no statewide residential builder license; Texas Coastal Building Code mandatory ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated windows on Gulf Coast counties; HOA architectural-committee approval mandatory in 75%+ of Dallas-Fort Worth, Houston, Austin, and San Antonio subdivisions

How window replacement costs vary in Texas

Texas's combination of Gulf Coast hurricane wind exposure driving Texas Coastal Building Code ASTM E1996 large-missile impact-rated window mandate on every coastal residential install at 130-150 mph design wind, Texas Black Prairie expansive clay subsoil shrink-swell across the Dallas-Fort Worth-Austin-San Antonio metropolitan corridor driving aggressive header-attachment detailing, ENERGY STAR South-Central climate zone driving low-SHGC (≤0.25) glazing requirement, very-high HOA architectural-control prevalence across 75%+ of Texas metro subdivisions, and TWIA Wind Loss Mitigation insurance-discount eligibility for impact-rated glazing drives unique TWIA-and-Black-Prairie-driven glazing selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Texas Residential Construction Liability Act (RCLA, Texas Property Code Chapter 27) is one of the most homeowner-favorable construction defect laws in the country — every Texas residential window contractor must comply with 60-day pre-suit notice provisions, with affected homeowners able to recover up to $200,000 in damages plus attorney fees for installation defects, and Texas is unusual among large states in not requiring a statewide residential builder license — Texas Department of Licensing & Regulation oversees only specialty trades like plumbers, electricians, and HVAC installers, plus Texas Department of Insurance Texas Windstorm Insurance Association (TWIA) Wind Loss Mitigation discount eligibility for ASTM E1996 impact-rated windows on every Gulf Coast residential property.
