Thornton garage door: answers to common questions

How much does garage door spring replacement cost?

Torsion spring replacement costs $200-$400 for parts and labor. Extension springs cost $150-$300. Never attempt spring replacement yourself since the stored tension is extremely dangerous.

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Thornton?

Garage door replacement in Thornton typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

What R-value garage door do I need in Thornton?

In Thornton, the minimum practical insulation for an attached garage door is R-12, with R-16 being the better choice for most homeowners. Workshop or heated-garage applications should go R-16 to R-18. That extra $200-$500 investment recovers through energy savings in adjacent living spaces within a few years and keeps the garage itself above freezing through winter.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Thornton?

Repair if the damage is limited to one or two panels, a single spring, or the opener mechanism. Replace when the door is over 15 years old, panels are discontinued, the door lacks insulation you want, or you are planning to sell. A garage door replacement recovers 90-100% of its cost at resale, making it one of the highest-ROI home improvements available.

When should I replace a garage door in Thornton?

For Thornton, target late spring or early fall for your garage door replacement. Moderate temperatures allow proper weatherstrip sealing and caulk curing, and off-peak scheduling often means 5-10% savings on installation. Spring is the peak rush since cold weather causes spring failures that flood installers with emergency calls.
