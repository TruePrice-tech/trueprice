Roofing questions for Thornton homeowners

What is the difference between architectural and 3-tab shingles?

Architectural shingles are thicker, have a dimensional look, and last 25-30 years. 3-tab shingles are flat, thinner, and last 15-20 years but cost 30-40% less upfront.

Does homeowners insurance cover roof replacement?

Insurance covers sudden damage (storms, falling trees) but not wear-and-tear or maintenance neglect. Verify your policy covers replacement cost, not depreciated value.

What time of year is cheapest for roofing in Thornton?

Late fall and winter are typically slower seasons in Thornton, so contractors may offer better pricing. Spring and summer are peak demand, meaning longer waits and higher labor costs.

Why do roofing quotes vary so much?

Bid-to-bid variation comes down to material grade, labor assumptions, flashing work, ventilation upgrades, and warranty tier. Lower bids often exclude items that appear as change orders later.

Do I need impact-resistant shingles in Thornton?

Absolutely. Hail damage is one of the most frequent roof insurance claims in Thornton. Class 4 impact-resistant shingles absorb hail strikes that would crack or dislodge standard materials. While they cost 10-20% more upfront, the 15-25% insurance discount most CO carriers offer for IR-rated roofs typically pays back the difference in 3-5 years, not counting the avoided replacement costs.

Is a Thornton permit needed to replace my roof?

In most jurisdictions including Thornton, a building permit is required for a full roof replacement. Your contractor should pull the permit before work begins. Unpermitted roofing work can void your homeowners insurance coverage and create disclosure issues when selling the home.

When should I replace a roof in Thornton?

For Thornton, aim for late spring or early fall to schedule your roof replacement. Should I repair or replace my roof?
