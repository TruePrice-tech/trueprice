Garage Door questions for Tigard homeowners

How wide is a standard garage door?

A single-car door is 8-9 feet wide. A double-car door is 16 feet wide. Heights are typically 7-8 feet. Custom sizes are available but cost 20-40% more than standard dimensions.

Do I need a permit for a garage door in Tigard?

Garage door replacement in Tigard typically does not require a permit if you are not changing the opening size. If you widen or add a new opening, a structural permit is required.

What causes a garage door to be noisy?

Common causes: worn rollers (replace nylon for steel), dry hinges (lubricate with silicone spray), loose hardware, a chain-drive opener, or worn spring coils. A tune-up costs $100-$200.

What is the best type of garage door?

Insulated steel with polyurethane foam offers the best balance of durability, energy efficiency, and cost. R-value of 12-18 keeps the garage 10-20 degrees closer to house temperature.

How often should I maintain my garage door in Tigard?

Lubricate all moving parts (hinges, rollers, springs, tracks) with silicone spray twice a year. Test the auto-reverse by placing a 2x4 under the door -- it should reverse within 2 seconds of contact. Check weatherstripping for gaps and cracks annually. Tighten all hardware since vibration loosens bolts over time. A professional tune-up ($100-$200) every 2-3 years catches worn parts before they fail.

Should I repair or replace my garage door in Tigard?

With Tigard homes averaging 29 years, many still have original garage doors past their useful life. A full replacement ($1,200-$3,000 with a new opener) typically returns 90-100% at resale. Repair is worth it only for newer doors with isolated issues like a single broken spring, damaged panel, or failing opener motor. If the door is original to the house, replacement is the better investment.

What's the best season to replace a garage door in Tigard?

The ideal window for a Tigard garage door replacement is spring or early fall. Dry conditions for proper weatherstrip adhesion, plus you get access to a wider range of styles and colors. Avoid spring if you can, since that is when winter-related spring breakages create peak demand and longer wait times.
