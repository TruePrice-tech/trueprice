Questions Tucson homeowners ask about insulation

Can I add insulation over existing insulation?

Yes, in most cases. Adding blown-in cellulose or unfaced fiberglass over existing attic insulation is common and effective. Never compress existing insulation and do not add a vapor barrier on top of existing insulation.

Should I remove old insulation before adding new?

Remove if the existing insulation is wet, moldy, pest-contaminated, or vermiculite (potential asbestos). Otherwise, add new insulation on top. Removal costs $1-$2/sqft and is usually unnecessary.

What is the difference between open-cell and closed-cell spray foam?

Open-cell (R-3.7/inch) is cheaper, flexible, and better for interior walls. Closed-cell (R-6.5/inch) is rigid, waterproof, adds structural strength, and is required in flood zones and below-grade applications.

How much energy does insulation save?

Insulating an under-insulated attic to current code saves 15-25% on heating and cooling costs. In a home spending $2,000/year on energy, that is $300-$500 annual savings, paying back a $1,500-$2,500 project in 3-5 years.

More questions about insulation in Tucson

What does spray foam cost per square foot?

Open-cell spray foam runs $1.50-$3.50 per square foot installed nationally. Closed-cell, with a higher R-value and built-in vapor barrier, runs $2.50-$5.50 per square foot. Walls and crawl spaces add 15-20% over attic-floor pricing.

How much does a 1,500 sq ft attic cost to insulate?

Typical 2026 ranges for a 1,500 sq ft attic: blown-in cellulose $1,500-$3,500, fiberglass batts $1,500-$3,000, open-cell spray foam $2,500-$5,000, closed-cell spray foam $4,000-$8,000. Removing old insulation adds about 25%; air sealing top plates and penetrations adds $300-$700 and pays back within a few heating seasons.

Do I need a permit for insulation in Tucson?

Most blown-in attic jobs in Tucson do not require a permit. Spray foam projects often do, especially in walls or crawl spaces where ignition-barrier code applies. Confirm with your contractor — pulling the permit is the contractor's job, not yours.

Where Tucson's pricing comes from. Tucson's labor market runs about 9% below the national median for skilled trades. This wage differential is the primary driver of the area's below-average project costs. On insulation, Tucson comes in about 7% under the national average based on our metro pricing data.

Local conditions in Tucson. Tucson has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Radiant barriers and reflective roof coatings outperform added attic insulation in Tucson's cooling-dominant climate. The intense solar radiation on the roof surface is the primary heat source — reflecting it before it enters the attic is more effective than trying to insulate against it. Homes in Tucson averaging 24 years old were built to more recent energy codes and typically have adequate attic and wall insulation. Air sealing ($300-600) delivers better ROI than adding insulation depth in these homes.
