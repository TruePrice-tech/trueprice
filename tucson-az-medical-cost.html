Hospital systems and safety-net providers in Tucson

Banner - University Medicine Tucson, TMC HealthCare (Tucson Medical Center), Northwest Healthcare (Community Health Systems), and Carondelet Health Network (Tenet) serve the Tucson metro. Banner - UMC Tucson is the Level I trauma center and academic anchor tied to the University of Arizona. TMC HealthCare is the locally governed nonprofit hospital. There is no county-owned safety-net facility; Pima County contracts with El Rio Health for primary care.

Pima County's uninsured rate is approximately 10.5%, above the national average, driven by the concentration of seasonal workers, University of Arizona student population gaps, and border-adjacent communities with limited coverage options. Arizona's Medicaid expansion (AHCCCS) has reduced the rate significantly from pre-ACA levels.

Average Medical Procedure Costs: a Tucson breakdown

An ER visit at Banner - UMC Tucson averages $2,000-$3,500, while TMC's ER charges $1,600-$2,800 for comparable acuity. MRI at Northwest Medical Center runs $900-$2,000; freestanding imaging centers on the Eastside offer the same scan for $280-$550. Hip replacement at TMC costs $24,000-$42,000 before insurance negotiated rates.
