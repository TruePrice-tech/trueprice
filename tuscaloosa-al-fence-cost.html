Tuscaloosa fencing FAQ

Who owns the fence between two properties?

Typically the homeowner who installed it. If the fence sits exactly on the property line, both neighbors share responsibility. Always get a survey before building to confirm the boundary.

What is the difference between dog-ear and flat-top fence?

Dog-ear pickets have angled cuts at the top for water runoff and a traditional look. Flat-top pickets have a clean, modern appearance but hold slightly more water, which can accelerate rot.

Do I need a permit to build a fence in Tuscaloosa?

Most Tuscaloosa jurisdictions require a permit for fences over 6 feet tall. Many require permits for any fence. You also need to check setback requirements, HOA rules, and call 811 for utility locates before digging.

Does a fence increase property value?

A well-built fence adds 1-5% to property value depending on material and neighborhood norms. Privacy fencing adds more value in neighborhoods where it is expected. Chain link adds minimal value.

What fence material lasts longest in Tuscaloosa's climate?

For maximum lifespan in Tuscaloosa's climate, vinyl and aluminum fences are the clear winners, lasting 10-15 years longer than wood due to immunity from rot, termites, and moisture. Cedar and pressure-treated pine are the best wood options but need sealing every 2-3 years. Composite fencing offers wood-grain appearance with weather resistance closer to vinyl.

Do I need a permit to build a fence in Tuscaloosa?

Most Tuscaloosa jurisdictions require permits for fences, especially those over 6 feet. Check with your local building department before starting. You must call 811 for utility locates before digging, verify property lines (consider a survey if boundaries are unclear), and check any HOA rules. Your contractor should handle permitting as part of the project.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Tuscaloosa?

Tuscaloosa fence installation rates: wood privacy $15-$30/ft, vinyl $20-$40, chain link $10-$20, and ornamental metal $25-$55. For a typical 150 linear foot backyard, that works out to $2,250-$6,000 depending on material choice. Three quotes with identical scope give you a reliable pricing range.
