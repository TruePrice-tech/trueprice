Plumbing in Union City: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Union City has moderate water hardness — not extreme, but enough to warrant annual water heater flushing. A whole-house filter can improve taste and reduce minor buildup in pipes and fixtures.

❄

Freeze risk

Union City occasionally sees freezing temperatures that can threaten exposed pipes. Basic insulation on outdoor spigots and crawlspace pipes is a smart, inexpensive precaution.

🔧

Material recommendation

Most Union City plumbers now recommend PEX for whole-house repiping. It's code-compliant, durable, and significantly cheaper than copper. The main exception: some homeowners prefer copper for its longevity and perceived quality.

💡

Local tip

Older homes in Union City (pre-1970) often have galvanized or cast iron drain pipes that deteriorate over decades. A camera inspection ($150-300) can reveal hidden corrosion before it causes a major leak or backup.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Union City?

Plumber rates in Union City typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
