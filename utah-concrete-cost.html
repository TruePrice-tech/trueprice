Concrete Driveway Cost in Utah (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in Utah typically run $3,200–$8,400 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 4000 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 30-inch design frost line, and the state sits in severe freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F2). Utah's Wasatch Front Fault System earthquake risk forces ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic design category requirements on every residential foundation pour from Brigham City to Nephi — and Utah's high alkaline soil sulfate exposure across the Great Salt Lake watershed forces ASTM C150 Type V sulfate-resistant cement specification on every Salt Lake City and Provo residential pour, plus Park City and Deer Valley high-altitude exposure above 7,000 ft additionally drives 6%+ air-entrainment plus extended cure-time specifications on every premium ski-town residential pour.

Utah climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 30-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F2 — severe freeze-thaw + de-icer exposure, 5-7% air-entrainment required
  • Typical residential mix: 4000 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: Moderate — periodic clay shrink-swell, standard footing reinforcement adequate
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S2 — severe sulfate exposure, ASTM C150 Type II/V cement required
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC D1 — high seismic risk + Special Inspector continuous observation required
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: High — winter de-icing salt drives air-entrainment + corrosion-protected rebar

Utah licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Utah Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing — S210 (Concrete Contractor) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential driveway pour > 200 sq ft; DOPL S210 Concrete Contractor specialty license required statewide on contracts above $3,000

How concrete driveway costs vary in Utah

State-specific code or insurance rule: Utah Code §58-55 requires every Utah concrete contractor exceeding the $3,000 residential threshold to hold a Utah Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing S210 Concrete Contractor specialty license — Utah is one of only a handful of Mountain West states with concrete as its own specialty trade license, and Utah's Wasatch Front Fault System earthquake risk forces ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic design category requirements on every residential foundation pour from Brigham City to Nephi, with mandatory continuous-rebar-tie footings plus minimum 4000 psi compressive strength on every Wasatch Front residential foundation pour.
