Fence Installation Cost in Utah (2026)

Installing 150 linear feet of fencing in Utah typically runs $4,600–$12,000, with 6-foot vinyl panel the dominant residential choice. Concrete-set posts must clear the 30-inch design frost line. Utah's 30-inch frost line drives mandatory deep-set concrete-anchored posts on every residential fence installation — and Wasatch Front HOA penetration across Salt Lake, Davis, and Utah counties is among the highest in the West, with most master-planned communities enforcing architectural review controls that favor uniform vinyl over weathered cedar, driving rising vinyl market share over wood on suburban retrofits.

Utah climate & post-design drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 30-inch design minimum for post footings
  • Dominant residential fence material: Vinyl panel — dominant in HOA-heavy Sun Belt and termite-risk corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: Low — sub-110 mph design wind, standard 8-foot post spacing
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Moderate — periodic termite pressure
  • HOA fencing-rule prevalence: High — large planned-community HOA penetration restricts material/color/height

Utah licensing, HOA & boundary law

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Utah Department of Commerce — Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing (B100 General Building or S210 Fencing specialty) (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal permit typical for residential fence over 6 feet; HOA approval required in most master-planned communities

How fence installation costs vary in Utah

Utah's 30-inch frost line drives mandatory deep-set concrete-anchored posts on every residential fence installation — and Wasatch Front HOA penetration across Salt Lake, Davis, and Utah counties is among the highest in the West, with most master-planned communities enforcing architectural review controls that favor uniform vinyl over weathered cedar, driving rising vinyl market share over wood on suburban retrofits.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Utah DOPL issues an S210 Fencing specialty contractor classification distinct from the B100 General Building license — and unique to Utah, the DOPL administers a Construction Trades Recovery Fund that compensates homeowners up to $40,000 per project for licensed-contractor breach, funded by per-license fees, with claims reviewed by the Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing under Utah Code §38-11-201.
