Foundation Cost in Utah (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in Utah typically run $16,400–$38,800 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with full basement pour as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 30-inch design frost line. Utah's combination of Wasatch Fault near-fault seismic exposure forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic design category requirements on every Salt Lake Valley, Utah Valley, and Davis County residential foundation pour, expansive Salt Lake Valley clay subsoil driving aggressive 4-to-6-inch crushed-aggregate capillary break under every Salt Lake County, Utah County, and Davis County slab and basement pour, statewide radon zone 1 designation forcing sub-slab depressurization rough-in on every residential foundation, and high alkaline soil sulfate exposure (S2 ACI 318 category) across the Salt Lake Valley forcing ASTM C150 Type II/V sulfate-resistant cement specification drives aggressive seismic-and-sulfate-driven foundation engineering.

Utah climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 30-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Full basement — dominant in Upper Midwest, Northeast, and Mid-Atlantic on freeze-thaw + radon-zone-1 sites
  • Expansive soil risk: High — expansive clay subsoil requires geotechnical investigation + reinforced footings
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC D1 — high seismic risk + Special Inspector continuous observation required
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Water table risk: Moderate — water table below frost line in most lots
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S2 — severe sulfate exposure, ASTM C150 Type II/V cement required
  • IECC climate zone: 5B-6B
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 1 (highest) — passive sub-slab depressurization rough-in required on every basement and slab pour

Utah licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Utah Division of Occupational & Professional Licensing (DOPL) — General Building Contractor (B-100) license (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential foundation pour; DOPL B-100 General Building Contractor license required above $1,000 contract; Wasatch Front fault-zone seismic detailing mandatory; Utah Geological Survey expansive-clay disclosure recommended on every Salt Lake Valley foundation pour

How foundation pour costs vary in Utah

State-specific code or insurance rule: Utah Division of Occupational & Professional Licensing (DOPL) §58-55 requires every Utah foundation contractor to hold a B-100 General Building Contractor license — one of the lowest-threshold mandatory contractor licensing schemes in the Mountain West at $1,000 contract value, plus Utah is one of the only Mountain West states requiring ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic design category detailing on every Salt Lake Valley residential foundation pour due to Wasatch Fault near-fault design requirements, with the Utah Geological Survey separately publishing expansive-clay maps for the Salt Lake Valley, Utah Valley, and St. George Basin requiring geotechnical investigation on every Salt Lake County, Utah County, Davis County, and Washington County residential foundation pour.
