Garage Door Replacement Cost in Utah (2026)

Garage door replacements in Utah typically run $1,200–$4,400 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 100 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Utah's combination of Wasatch Fault near-fault seismic exposure forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic-detailed garage-door header attachment on every Salt Lake Valley, Utah Valley, and Davis County residential install, expansive Salt Lake Valley clay subsoil driving aggressive header-attachment detailing, and high HOA architectural-control prevalence across Salt Lake County subdivisions drives unique seismic-and-Wasatch-driven material selection.

Utah climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 100 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Moderate — periodic termite pressure, PT framing recommended
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: High — 60-80% of subdivisions enforce HOA architectural-committee pre-approval
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: None — no salt-air corrosion exposure
  • IECC climate zone: 5B-6B

Utah licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Utah Division of Occupational & Professional Licensing (DOPL) — General Building Contractor (B-100) license (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening; DOPL B-100 license required above $1,000 contract; Wasatch Front fault-zone seismic detailing mandatory on every Salt Lake Valley residential install; Utah HOA architectural-committee approval common on Salt Lake County subdivisions

How garage door installation costs vary in Utah

Utah's combination of Wasatch Fault near-fault seismic exposure forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic-detailed garage-door header attachment on every Salt Lake Valley, Utah Valley, and Davis County residential install, expansive Salt Lake Valley clay subsoil driving aggressive header-attachment detailing, and high HOA architectural-control prevalence across Salt Lake County subdivisions drives unique seismic-and-Wasatch-driven material selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Utah Division of Occupational & Professional Licensing (DOPL) §58-55 requires every Utah garage-door contractor to hold a B-100 General Building Contractor license — one of the lowest-threshold mandatory contractor licensing schemes in the Mountain West at $1,000 contract value, plus Utah is one of the only Mountain West states requiring ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic-detailed garage-door header attachment on every Salt Lake Valley residential install due to Wasatch Fault near-fault design requirements, with the Utah Geological Survey separately publishing expansive-clay maps for the Salt Lake Valley, Utah Valley, and St. George Basin requiring geotechnical investigation on every residential install above 0.5 acre lot.
