Gutter Installation Cost in Utah (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Utah typically runs $1,100–$4,200, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 14 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Utah's 14-inch state-average annual rainfall masks dramatic Wasatch Front vs eastern-Utah variation — Wasatch Front and Great Salt Lake counties receive 18-25 inches/year primarily as winter snow that drives 30+ psf snow-load and ice-dam exposure — while eastern Utah and the Uintah Basin receive under 10 inches/year and routinely defer gutter installation on smaller residential structures.

Utah rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 14 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 30 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: High — sustained sub-freezing winter requires ice-and-water shield + heat-cable retrofit budget
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: Moderate — mixed canopy, gutter guards optional

Utah licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Utah Department of Commerce — Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing (B100 General Building or S350 Siding specialty) (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction

How gutter installation costs vary in Utah

Utah's 14-inch state-average annual rainfall masks dramatic Wasatch Front vs eastern-Utah variation — Wasatch Front and Great Salt Lake counties receive 18-25 inches/year primarily as winter snow that drives 30+ psf snow-load and ice-dam exposure — while eastern Utah and the Uintah Basin receive under 10 inches/year and routinely defer gutter installation on smaller residential structures.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Utah DOPL issues an S350 Siding specialty contractor classification that includes gutter work distinct from the B100 General Building license — and unique to Utah, the DOPL administers a Construction Trades Recovery Fund that compensates homeowners up to $40,000 per project for licensed-contractor breach, funded by per-license fees, with claims reviewed by the Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing under Utah Code §38-11-201.
