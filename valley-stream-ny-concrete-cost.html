How much does a concrete driveway cost?

A standard 600 sqft concrete driveway costs $3,600-$7,200 for plain finished concrete. Stamped or colored concrete adds $3-$10/sqft. Removing the old driveway adds $1,000-$2,000.

Should I seal my concrete?

Yes. Sealing prevents water penetration, staining, freeze-thaw damage, and salt damage. Apply a penetrating sealer every 2-3 years. It costs $0.50-$2.00 per square foot for professional application.

What is the difference between concrete and cement?

What Valley Stream homeowners should know about concrete work

Why pricing varies in Valley Stream. Labor rates here run roughly 18% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. Our data shows concrete work costs in Valley Stream tracking roughly 8% higher than the national baseline.

Valley Stream's site and climate factors. Valley Stream has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in Valley Stream for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Older properties in Valley Stream (averaging 53 years) often have settling concrete that affects drainage. Re-pouring may require sub-grade correction (compaction, drainage tile) that new construction doesn't need. Budget 10-20% extra for site preparation on established properties.

Valley Stream paperwork, inspections, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in Valley Stream require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Who's doing the work in Valley Stream. With 37,659 people, Valley Stream's contractor pool is mid-sized. You will find 4-6 established shops for most trades, which provides enough competition for fair pricing. Valley Stream's measured growth has built a reliable contractor ecosystem. Lead times are manageable and pricing competition is healthy.
