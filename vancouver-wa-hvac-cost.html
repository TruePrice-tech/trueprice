HVAC in Vancouver: what locals should know

🌡

Climate and your HVAC

In Vancouver, you rarely see temperature extremes, making a heat pump the most cost-effective HVAC choice. Many homeowners here skip gas furnaces entirely.

⚙

System recommendation

In Vancouver's mild climate, a heat pump is the clear winner. With moderate temperatures year-round, heat pumps operate at peak efficiency. Look for a 15+ SEER model with a good HSPF rating.

📅

Best time to buy

In Vancouver, HVAC demand is more spread out than in extreme climates. Still, fall is the best time for heat pump installations before the wet, cool winter season arrives.

💡

Local tip

Vancouver utilities often offer significant heat pump rebates. Check with your local utility before buying — incentives can reduce the upfront cost by $1,000-3,000 or more.

What affects HVAC cost in Vancouver

  • System type (AC only, heat pump, furnace, full system)
  • SEER efficiency rating (14-20+)
  • Home size and tonnage requirement
  • Ductwork condition and modifications
  • Equipment brand (Carrier, Trane, Goodman, etc.)
  • Local labor rates in Vancouver, WA

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new HVAC system cost in Vancouver?

Most homeowners in Vancouver pay between $8,755 and $25,070 for HVAC replacement, depending on system type and efficiency.

Should I replace my AC and furnace at the same time?

Replacing both at once is usually more cost-effective than separate replacements. Matched systems run more efficiently and most contractors offer bundle pricing.

What SEER rating should I choose?

14 SEER is the minimum federal requirement. 16 SEER offers a good balance of efficiency and cost. 18-20+ SEER provides the best energy savings but costs significantly more upfront.
