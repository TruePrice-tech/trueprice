Vancouver window replacement: answers to common questions

How do I know my windows need replacing?

Replace when you see fog between panes (failed seal), feel drafts with windows closed, frames are rotting or warping, locks no longer engage, or energy bills are rising with no other explanation.

What is the difference between double-pane and triple-pane windows?

Triple-pane windows add 15-25% better insulation over double-pane but cost 25-35% more. They pay back faster in extreme climates (very cold winters or very hot summers).

What is argon gas fill in windows?

Argon is an inert gas sealed between panes that insulates better than air. It improves R-value by 15-20% and is standard on most quality double and triple-pane windows. It slowly leaks over 15-20 years.

Should I replace all windows at once?

Replacing all windows at once saves 10-15% on labor versus doing them individually. It also ensures uniform appearance and consistent energy performance throughout the home.

Do replacement windows increase home value?

New windows recover 65-75% of their cost at resale. They also improve curb appeal, reduce noise, and eliminate a common inspection concern for buyers.

Local trade economics. The Vancouver contractor market is balanced: enough work to keep shops busy, but enough competition that homeowners can still get timely service. Seasonal timing. Vancouver allows year-round window installation, but spring and fall are optimal — moderate temperatures mean less thermal stress during the swap and more comfortable working conditions for installers. Summer heat can cause sealants to cure too fast.

What drives cost. Labor is the dominant cost driver for window replacement in Vancouver — local wages run 32% above the national average, which adds 16% or more to a typical full-house window swap. Vancouver's housing stock averages 35 years — the age where original installations start failing and code requirements have evolved. Most window replacement quotes will include some code-catch-up items that newer homes wouldn't need.

Red flags to watch for. Any Vancouver contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag. Standard practice is 10-15% deposit, materials-on-delivery payment, and final payment on completion. Watch for window replacement quotes in Vancouver that lack line-item detail.
