Gutter Installation Cost in Vermont (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Vermont typically runs $1,100–$4,200, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 43 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Vermont's 50 psf ground snow load combined with Green Mountain elevation winter snow drives heavier-gauge aluminum K-style and steel-bracket spacing under 18 inches on Caledonia, Essex, and Orleans county residential installations — and ice-dam formation on every north-facing eave drives near-universal heat-cable retrofit with budgets averaging $700-1,100 per home, with proactive ice-shield underlayment now standard.

Vermont rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 43 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 50 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: High — sustained sub-freezing winter requires ice-and-water shield + heat-cable retrofit budget
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: High — mature deciduous canopy drives gutter-guard demand and 2x annual cleaning frequency

Vermont licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide registration required (no exam)
  • License board: Vermont Office of Professional Regulation — Residential Contractor Registration (official site)
  • Permit: municipal building permit required where adopted; many small towns have no inspection program

How gutter installation costs vary in Vermont

Vermont's 50 psf ground snow load combined with Green Mountain elevation winter snow drives heavier-gauge aluminum K-style and steel-bracket spacing under 18 inches on Caledonia, Essex, and Orleans county residential installations — and ice-dam formation on every north-facing eave drives near-universal heat-cable retrofit with budgets averaging $700-1,100 per home, with proactive ice-shield underlayment now standard.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Vermont 26 V.S.A. Chapter 109 (effective 2021) requires every residential contractor to register with the Office of Professional Regulation if performing more than $10,000 of work per year on Vermont property — making Vermont one of the most recently established residential contractor registries in the country, with mandatory written contracts, $250,000 of liability insurance, and 3-day right of cancellation provisions.
