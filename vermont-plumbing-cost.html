Plumbing & Repipe Cost in Vermont (2026)

Plumbing repipe in Vermont typically runs $5,100–$13,200 for a whole-house 2-bath PEX repipe, with Type-L copper the dominant retrofit choice and the International Plumbing Code (IPC) as the adopted enforcement basis. Buried service-line trench depth must clear the 60-inch design frost line. Vermont water-quality & freeze drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 6A — drives freeze-protection scope on supply lines
  • Frost-line trench depth: 60-inch design minimum for buried water service
  • Water hardness profile: Soft (under 3.5 grains/gallon) — minimal scaling, no softener required
  • Dominant retrofit supply material: Type-L copper — dominant on retrofits in high-soft-water and pre-1990 housing stock
  • Adopted plumbing code basis: International Plumbing Code (IPC) — ICC model adopted statewide
  • Lead service line risk tier: Moderate — partial pre-1986 service line inventory under utility review

Vermont licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Vermont Department of Public Safety — Division of Fire Safety (Plumbers' Licensing Board: Master / Journeyman / Apprentice / Specialty) (official site)
  • Permit: DPS plumbing permit required statewide outside delegated municipalities
