Window Replacement Cost in Vermont (2026)

Window replacements in Vermont typically run $5,800–$14,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Northern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.27, no SHGC limit). Vermont's combination of 60-inch frost depth across the Northeast Kingdom (Caledonia, Essex, and Orleans counties), F3 freeze-thaw cycling driving aggressive sealant degradation, -30°F winter low temperatures driving Vermont Residential Building Energy Standards U-0.27 maximum-U-factor mandate, very-high pre-1978 housing share driving mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification, and Green Mountain alpine elevations above 3,000 feet drives extreme cold-climate-and-Vermont-RBES-driven glazing selection.

Vermont climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Northern — coldest climates, U-factor priority (U ≤ 0.27 in coldest tier)
  • IECC climate zone: 6A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.27 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: No limit (cold climate)
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 105 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Moderate — selected metro historic districts with review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Very high — 60%+ pre-1978 housing share, mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification on every replacement

Vermont licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: Vermont has no statewide residential builder license — Vermont Office of Professional Regulation oversees specialty trades only (official site)
  • Permit: town or municipal building permit required for window opening modification; no state license; Vermont Residential Building Energy Standards (RBES) above-IECC-baseline U-factor mandatory at U-0.27 maximum window U-factor; replacement-only same-size installs typically permit-exempt

How window replacement costs vary in Vermont

State-specific code or insurance rule: Vermont has no statewide residential builder license — Vermont Office of Professional Regulation oversees only specialty trades like plumbers, electricians, and oil-burner installers, leaving window contractor competency and bond entirely to municipal building departments and homeowner due diligence, plus Vermont Residential Building Energy Standards (RBES) §30 V.S.A. §51 separately mandates above-IECC-2021-baseline U-0.27 maximum window U-factor on every Vermont residential installation, making Vermont one of the few small states with mandatory Stretch Code-equivalent window U-factor with very-high pre-1978 housing share driving mandatory EPA RRP lead-safe certification.
