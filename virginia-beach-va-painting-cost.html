Frequently asked painting questions

How often should a house be painted?

Wood siding needs repainting every 5-7 years. Stucco and fiber cement last 8-12 years between coats. Vinyl and aluminum siding rarely need painting. Climate, sun exposure, and paint quality affect timing.

What should I look for in a painting quote?

A professional quote specifies: paint brand and sheen, number of coats, prep work included, surface repairs, primer usage, timeline, warranty, and what is excluded (trim, doors, ceilings).

How much does exterior painting cost in Virginia Beach?

Exterior painting in Virginia Beach costs $3,000-$8,000 for an average home. Price depends on home size, siding material, number of stories, prep work required, and paint quality.

How long does exterior paint last?

Quality acrylic latex lasts 7-10 years on properly prepped surfaces. Budget paint lasts 3-5 years. South-facing walls fade faster. Two coats over primer significantly outlast a single coat.

Climate and paint durability near Virginia Beach

Virginia Beach's paint-failure environment is dominated by the Chesapeake Bay and Atlantic maritime climate: summer humidity with dew points in the mid-to-upper 70s promotes mildew on north-facing and shaded surfaces within 12-18 months, while nor'easter wind events (60-80 mph) drive rain horizontally into every paint-film failure point from October through March. The salt-air exposure at the Oceanfront and Sandbridge adds corrosion-driven failure on metal and wood-to-metal transitions that inland Kempsville and Great Neck properties never experience. UV intensity is moderate at Virginia Beach's latitude but sufficient to fade south-facing paint within 5-7 years. The combination of humidity, wind-driven rain, and salt creates more simultaneous degradation pathways than most inland markets face.

The Oceanfront and Shore Drive corridors face the worst conditions because the Atlantic and Bay moisture combines with the urban-canyon shading effect of resort-corridor buildings.
