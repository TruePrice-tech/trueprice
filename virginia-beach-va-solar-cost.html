Common solar questions in Virginia Beach

How long does solar take to pay for itself in Virginia Beach?

Payback in Virginia Beach depends on electricity rates, sun hours, and incentives. Most systems pay back in 6-10 years and then generate free electricity for another 15-20 years.

What is the difference between string inverters and microinverters?

String inverters are cheaper but the whole array drops output when one panel is shaded. Microinverters optimize each panel independently, cost 15-20% more, and last longer.

Should I buy or lease solar panels?

Buying maximizes long-term savings and increases home value. Leasing eliminates upfront cost but the leasing company keeps the tax credit, and panels may complicate home sales.

Will solar panels survive a hurricane in Virginia Beach?

Solar panels on properly rated racking systems handle hurricanes well, often outlasting the roof itself during high-wind events. The critical factors: racking must be certified for Virginia Beach's wind speed zone (130+ mph), all roof penetrations need proper flashing and sealant, and ongoing maintenance keeps connections tight. Schedule a post-storm inspection with your installer after any significant hurricane event.

What solar incentives are available in Virginia Beach?

Every Virginia Beach homeowner installing solar qualifies for the 30% federal Investment Tax Credit with no dollar limit. Beyond that, VA may offer state tax credits, utility-specific rebates, Solar Renewable Energy Credits, and property tax exemptions. The DSIRE database has the current list for your area. These stacked incentives typically reduce net cost by an additional 10-30%.

When should I install solar in Virginia Beach?

Virginia Beach homeowners should aim for early spring or fall to install solar. Moderate weather conditions for installation and you avoid the peak-demand scheduling crunch. Allow 6-12 weeks from signing a contract through permitting, utility interconnection approval, and installation. Start gathering quotes 2-3 months before your target window. Remember that the 30% ITC applies to the tax year in which installation is completed.
