Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in Virginia (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in Virginia typically run $2,200–$5,300 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2020 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Service entrance design assumes a 200A minimum on new residential construction. Chesapeake Bay coastal salt-haze deposition across Hampton Roads, Virginia Beach, and the Eastern Shore corrodes outdoor service equipment within 5-7 years, mandating stainless mounting hardware and NEMA 4X meter sockets on shoreline upgrades — and Blue Ridge winter ice storms on the western Virginia counties drive overhead service-drop failures with reinforced weatherheads and copper service conductors increasingly written into rewires.

Virginia code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2020 NEC — dwelling unit AFCI on most 120V branch circuits, GFCI expanded
  • State amendments: Virginia adopts 2020 NEC under VA USBC with DPOR Electrical contractor credentialing — Class A (any size), Class B ($10k-$120k), Class C (under $10k) tiers
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: Moderate — typical interior continental flash density
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 18-to-24-inch design depth on buried service conduit

Virginia licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Virginia Department of Professional and Occupational Regulation — Board for Contractors (Electrical) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county electrical permit required for panel upgrade and service-line work

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in Virginia

Chesapeake Bay coastal salt-haze deposition across Hampton Roads, Virginia Beach, and the Eastern Shore corrodes outdoor service equipment within 5-7 years, mandating stainless mounting hardware and NEMA 4X meter sockets on shoreline upgrades — and Blue Ridge winter ice storms on the western Virginia counties drive overhead service-drop failures with reinforced weatherheads and copper service conductors increasingly written into rewires.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Virginia DPOR issues Class A (any project size), Class B ($10,000-$120,000), and Class C (under $10,000) as three contracting tiers — and unique to Virginia, the Master Electrician credential is a personal certification separate from the contractor credential, with the contractor required to designate a Master Electrician as the Designated Employee on every panel upgrade or service-line project.
