Fence Installation Cost in Virginia (2026)

Installing 150 linear feet of fencing in Virginia typically runs $4,600–$12,000, with 6-foot vinyl panel the dominant residential choice. Concrete-set posts must clear the 24-inch design frost line. Virginia sits inside Termite Probability and Climatic Tier 2 with annual subterranean termite pressure across the Coastal Plain — every in-ground wood post requires CCA-treated southern yellow pine .60 or higher per Virginia Building Code, driving rising vinyl and aluminum market share over wood — and Northern Virginia HOA penetration across Fairfax, Loudoun, and Prince William counties drives architectural review controls favoring uniform vinyl over weathered cedar.

Virginia climate & post-design drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 24-inch design minimum for post footings
  • Dominant residential fence material: Vinyl panel — dominant in HOA-heavy Sun Belt and termite-risk corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — 110–140 mph design wind drives 8-foot post spacing
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): High — TPCT zone 2, PT post requirement
  • HOA fencing-rule prevalence: High — large planned-community HOA penetration restricts material/color/height

Virginia licensing, HOA & boundary law

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Virginia Department of Professional and Occupational Regulation — Board for Contractors (Class A/B/C residential) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county permit typical for residential fence over 6 feet; HOA approval required in most master-planned communities

How fence installation costs vary in Virginia

Virginia sits inside Termite Probability and Climatic Tier 2 with annual subterranean termite pressure across the Coastal Plain — every in-ground wood post requires CCA-treated southern yellow pine .60 or higher per Virginia Building Code, driving rising vinyl and aluminum market share over wood — and Northern Virginia HOA penetration across Fairfax, Loudoun, and Prince William counties drives architectural review controls favoring uniform vinyl over weathered cedar.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Virginia DPOR issues Class A (any project size), Class B ($10,000-$120,000), and Class C (under $10,000) as three contracting tiers — and unique to Virginia, the Board for Contractors issues separately tested specialty designations (HIC Home Improvement, BLD Building, RBC Residential Building) within each class, with fence work falling under either RBC Residential Building or HIC Home Improvement depending on project scope.
