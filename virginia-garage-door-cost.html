Garage Door Replacement Cost in Virginia (2026)

Garage door replacements in Virginia typically run $1,300–$4,600 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 115 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Virginia's combination of moderate Chesapeake Bay salt-spray on every Tidewater coastal exposure driving stainless-hardware replacement at 7-10 year intervals, F2 freeze-thaw cycling, Virginia Chesapeake Bay Preservation Act 100-foot Resource Protection Area buffer-zone setback requirement on every Tidewater residential install, high TPCT termite zone 2 pressure across the southern half, and high HOA architectural-control prevalence across northern Virginia counties (Loudoun, Fairfax, Prince William, Arlington) drives moderate coastal-and-HOA-driven material selection.

Virginia climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 115 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating at 110-140 mph design wind
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): High — TPCT zone 2, PT wood jamb framing required
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: High — 60-80% of subdivisions enforce HOA architectural-committee pre-approval
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: Moderate — bayfront salt-spray, stainless hardware recommended
  • IECC climate zone: 4A

Virginia licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Virginia Department of Professional & Occupational Regulation (DPOR) — Class A / B / C Contractor License (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening; DPOR Class C license required above $1,000 (up to $10,000), Class B above $10,000, Class A above $120,000; Chesapeake Bay Preservation Act buffer-zone review required on every Tidewater install

How garage door installation costs vary in Virginia

State-specific code or insurance rule: Virginia Department of Professional & Occupational Regulation (DPOR) §54.1-1100 imposes one of the most contract-value-segmented residential builder licensing schemes in the country — every Virginia garage-door contractor performing work above $1,000 must hold a Class C Contractor license, above $10,000 a Class B license, and above $120,000 a Class A license, with the DPOR Contractor Recovery Fund compensating affected homeowners up to $20,000 per project for DPOR-contractor abandonment, plus Virginia Chesapeake Bay Preservation Act §10.1-2100 separately requires environmental review on every Tidewater residential garage-door installation within Resource Protection Area or Resource Management Area buffer zones across all 84 Tidewater Virginia localities.
