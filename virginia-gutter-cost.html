Gutter Installation Cost in Virginia (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Virginia typically runs $1,100–$4,200, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 44 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Virginia's 44-inch annual rainfall combined with high mature-deciduous tree canopy across Northern Virginia, Richmond, and Hampton Roads drives near-universal gutter-guard retrofit demand on long-tenure suburban properties — and Chesapeake Bay coastal salt-haze deposition corrodes standard galvanized hardware within 5-7 years, mandating stainless mounting on shoreline retrofits.

Virginia rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 44 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 25 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: Moderate — periodic ice-dam events on north-facing eaves
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — 110–140 mph design wind
  • Tree-canopy density: High — mature deciduous canopy drives gutter-guard demand and 2x annual cleaning frequency

Virginia licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Virginia Department of Professional and Occupational Regulation — Board for Contractors (Class A/B/C residential) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction

How gutter installation costs vary in Virginia

Virginia's 44-inch annual rainfall combined with high mature-deciduous tree canopy across Northern Virginia, Richmond, and Hampton Roads drives near-universal gutter-guard retrofit demand on long-tenure suburban properties — and Chesapeake Bay coastal salt-haze deposition corrodes standard galvanized hardware within 5-7 years, mandating stainless mounting on shoreline retrofits.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Virginia DPOR issues Class A (any project size), Class B ($10,000-$120,000), and Class C (under $10,000) as three contracting tiers — and unique to Virginia, the Board for Contractors issues separately tested specialty designations (HIC Home Improvement, BLD Building, RBC Residential Building) within each class, with gutter work falling under either RBC Residential Building or HIC Home Improvement depending on project scope.
