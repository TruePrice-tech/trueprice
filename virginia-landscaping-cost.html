Landscape Installation Cost in Virginia (2026)

Landscape installation in Virginia typically runs $4,200–$17,800 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 5b-8a. Virginia's combination of Chesapeake Bay watershed-protection nutrient caps under the Lawn Fertilizer Law, mature deciduous canopy across the Northern Virginia I-95 corridor and Tidewater, and 200-day growing season pushes Virginia residential landscape design toward bay-friendly natives, low-input cool-season tall fescue turf, and rain garden bioretention on every new install — and Virginia's Tidewater coastal salt-spray plus the Virginia Beach Coastal Erosion Control Zone restrict shoreline residential landscaping to salt-tolerant beach plum, sea oats, and bayberry hedging on every coastal Virginia property.

Virginia climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 5b-8a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 44 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 200 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Low — adequate residential precipitation, minimal drought stress
  • Irrigation dependency: Low — irrigation optional, established plants survive on rainfall
  • Frost-line trench depth: 18-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

Virginia licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Virginia Department of Agriculture & Consumer Services Office of Pesticide Services + Virginia Department of Professional and Occupational Regulation (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; VDACS Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care; DPOR Class C Contractor license required above $1,000 contract

How landscape installation costs vary in Virginia

Virginia's combination of Chesapeake Bay watershed-protection nutrient caps under the Lawn Fertilizer Law, mature deciduous canopy across the Northern Virginia I-95 corridor and Tidewater, and 200-day growing season pushes Virginia residential landscape design toward bay-friendly natives, low-input cool-season tall fescue turf, and rain garden bioretention on every new install — and Virginia's Tidewater coastal salt-spray plus the Virginia Beach Coastal Erosion Control Zone restrict shoreline residential landscaping to salt-tolerant beach plum, sea oats, and bayberry hedging on every coastal Virginia property.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Virginia Code §3.2-3939.1 imposes the Virginia Lawn Fertilizer & Lawn Maintenance Code statewide — every Virginia commercial lawn-care contractor must apply zero phosphorus on established turf except at establishment, comply with seasonal blackout periods on phosphorus and nitrogen application, and post mandatory contractor signage detailing application content, all to protect Chesapeake Bay TMDL nutrient-reduction targets, with VDACS enforcement targeting commercial landscape contractor compliance through residential complaint inspections.
