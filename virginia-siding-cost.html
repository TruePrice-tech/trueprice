Siding Replacement Cost in Virginia (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Virginia typically runs $9,200–$25,500, with vinyl panel the dominant residential cladding choice. Chesapeake Bay coastal salt-haze deposition across Hampton Roads, Virginia Beach, and the Eastern Shore corrodes galvanized fasteners on shoreline re-sides within 5-7 years, mandating stainless on all coastal vinyl, fiber-cement, and cedar — and Blue Ridge winter ice storms on the western Virginia counties drive wood-cladding rot and rainscreen-gap requirements on every full envelope re-side.

Virginia climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4A-5A
  • Dominant residential cladding: Vinyl panel — dominant low-budget retrofit choice in non-WUI, non-coastal corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate wind exposure — 110–140 mph design wind on coastal counties
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: No statewide WUI cladding mandate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: Moderate-corrosion coastal — stainless fasteners recommended on bayfront and tidal exposures

Virginia licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Virginia Department of Professional and Occupational Regulation — Board for Contractors (Class A/B/C residential) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for full re-side

How siding replacement costs vary in Virginia

Chesapeake Bay coastal salt-haze deposition across Hampton Roads, Virginia Beach, and the Eastern Shore corrodes galvanized fasteners on shoreline re-sides within 5-7 years, mandating stainless on all coastal vinyl, fiber-cement, and cedar — and Blue Ridge winter ice storms on the western Virginia counties drive wood-cladding rot and rainscreen-gap requirements on every full envelope re-side.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Virginia DPOR issues Class A (any project size), Class B ($10,000-$120,000), and Class C (under $10,000) as three contracting tiers — and unique to Virginia, the Board for Contractors issues separately tested specialty designations (HIC Home Improvement, BLD Building, RBC Residential Building) within each class, with siding work falling under either RBC Residential Building or HIC Home Improvement depending on project scope.
