Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a paver patio cost in Waco?

Most homeowners in Waco pay between $5,400 and $10,750 for a paver patio, depending on size and materials chosen.

How long does a paver patio last?

A properly installed paver patio can last 25-50 years with minimal maintenance. Pavers can be individually replaced if damaged, making them a long-lasting investment.

Do I need a permit for landscaping work?

Permits are typically required for retaining walls over 4 feet tall, major grading changes, and drainage work that affects neighboring properties. Check with your local building department in Waco.

About landscaping in Waco, TX

Why pricing varies in Waco. Labor rates here run roughly 12% below the national average per BLS metro data, which is the main reason this city's pricing sits below the national midpoint. For landscaping specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Waco at roughly 14% below the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Waco's Central Texas location makes it vulnerable to severe hailstorms from spring supercells, while summer temperatures regularly exceeding 100°F stress roof materials Waco sees high hail risk — all of which contractors here have to plan for. Native and drought-tolerant species (yaupon, muhly grass, lantana) cut irrigation costs and survive both flood and drought.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most landscaping projects in Waco require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 129,030, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Waco has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.
