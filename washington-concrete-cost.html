Concrete Driveway Cost in Washington (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in Washington typically run $3,800–$10,000 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 3500 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 18-inch design frost line, and the state sits in moderate freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F1). Washington's combination of Cascadia Subduction Zone earthquake risk plus Seattle Fault Zone risk driving ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic design category requirements west of the Cascade Range, persistent Pacific Northwest mildew growing conditions slowing fresh concrete cure rates, and Cascade Range wood-substrate moisture cycling forces continuous-rebar-tie schedules plus Special Inspector continuous observation on every Washington residential foundation pour above 1,500 sq ft — and Washington Coast salt-spray on Aberdeen, Long Beach, and Anacortes additionally drives epoxy-coated rebar specifications on every coastal Washington pour.

Washington climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 18-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F1 — moderate freeze-thaw, 4.5-6% air-entrainment recommended
  • Typical residential mix: 3500 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: Moderate — periodic clay shrink-swell, standard footing reinforcement adequate
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S1 — moderate sulfate exposure, ASTM C150 Type II cement required
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC D1 — high seismic risk + Special Inspector continuous observation required
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: Moderate — periodic de-icer use

Washington licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Washington State Department of Labor & Industries — Contractor Registration (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential driveway pour > 200 sq ft; L&I Contractor Registration required statewide regardless of contract value
