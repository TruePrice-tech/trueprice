Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in Washington (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in Washington typically run $2,700–$6,500 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2020 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Service entrance design assumes a 200A minimum on new residential construction. Cascadia subduction zone seismic exposure across the I-5 corridor from Bellingham to Vancouver drives flexible-conduit and seismic-anchorage requirements on every meter base, panel, and load-side connection — and Puget Sound coastal salt-haze plus Olympic Peninsula sustained rainfall corrode outdoor service equipment within 7-10 years, mandating stainless mounting hardware on shoreline panel upgrades.

Washington code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2020 NEC — dwelling unit AFCI on most 120V branch circuits, GFCI expanded
  • State amendments: Washington adopts 2020 NEC under WAC 296-46B with L&I Electrical Section issuing Administrator (01-04), Master (01-04), Journeyman, and Specialty (06/07/etc.) credentials
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: Low — Pacific coastal and high desert minimal flash density
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 18-to-24-inch design depth on buried service conduit

Washington licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Washington State Department of Labor & Industries — Electrical Section (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal electrical permit required for panel upgrade and service-line work

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in Washington

State-specific code or insurance rule: Washington L&I issues an Administrator credential at four levels (01 General, 02 Residential, 03 Pump & Irrigation, 04 Sign) plus separate Master Electrician credentials at the same four levels — making Washington the most heavily tiered electrical-licensing state in the country, with separate testing required for each scope category and the Administrator credential mandatory for every contractor business entity statewide.
