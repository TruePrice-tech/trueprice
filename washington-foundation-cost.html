Foundation Cost in Washington (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in Washington typically run $21,400–$51,400 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with full basement pour as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 18-inch design frost line. Washington's combination of Cascadia subduction zone seismic exposure forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic design category requirements on every coastal residential foundation pour from Cape Flattery to Long Beach, ASCE 7-22 SDC D0-D1 seismic detailing across the Puget Sound including Seattle, Tacoma, and Olympia, statewide radon zone 1 designation across eastern Washington counties (Spokane, Pend Oreille, Stevens, Ferry) forcing sub-slab depressurization rough-in on every residential foundation, Pacific Northwest high water table across the Coastal Range and Puget Sound driving sump-pump and capillary-break specification, and WSEC above-IECC-baseline foundation insulation R-value mandate drives aggressive seismic-and-energy-code-driven foundation engineering.

Washington climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 18-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Full basement — dominant in Upper Midwest, Northeast, and Mid-Atlantic on freeze-thaw + radon-zone-1 sites
  • Expansive soil risk: Moderate — periodic clay shrink-swell, standard footing reinforcement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC D1 — high seismic risk + Special Inspector continuous observation required
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — 110-140 mph design wind, standard tie-down
  • Water table risk: High — periodic water table within footing depth, sump-pump rough-in standard
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • IECC climate zone: 4C-5B
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 2 (moderate) — sub-slab depressurization rough-in recommended

Washington licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Washington State Department of Labor & Industries — Contractor Registration (General or Specialty) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential foundation pour; WA L&I Contractor Registration required for all residential work; WA State Energy Code WSEC 2021 above-IECC-baseline foundation R-value mandatory; Cascadia subduction zone seismic detailing mandatory on coastal Washington residential pours

How foundation pour costs vary in Washington

State-specific code or insurance rule: Washington State Department of Labor & Industries §18.27 requires every Washington foundation contractor to register with the WA L&I Contractor Registration program plus carry a $12,000 General Contractor or $6,000 Specialty Contractor surety bond — one of the most aggressive consumer-protection bond regimes in the country, with the WA L&I Construction Contractor Registration Section operating active enforcement against unregistered contractors statewide, plus WA State Energy Code (WSEC, WAC 51-11C) separately mandates above-IECC-2021-baseline foundation insulation R-value (R-15 continuous below-grade plus R-19 cavity) on every Washington residential foundation pour, and Cascadia subduction zone seismic detailing mandatory on every Washington coastal residential pour.
