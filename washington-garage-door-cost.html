Garage Door Replacement Cost in Washington (2026)

Garage door replacements in Washington typically run $1,400–$5,000 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 110 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Washington's combination of Cascadia subduction zone seismic exposure forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic-detailed garage-door header attachment on every coastal residential install from Cape Flattery to Long Beach, persistent Pacific salt-spray on every coastal exposure driving stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track replacement at 5-7 year intervals, Pacific Northwest year-round humidity driving rapid corrosion on standard galvanized springs at 5-7 year intervals, and Puget Sound saltwater immersion on every Seattle, Tacoma, and Olympia waterfront residential install drives unique seismic-and-coastal-driven material selection.

Washington climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 110 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — DASMA 108 wind-pressure rating at 110-140 mph design wind
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Moderate — periodic termite pressure, PT framing recommended
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Moderate — suburban HOA prevalence on newer subdivisions
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: High — coastal salt-spray drives stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track requirement at 5-7 year intervals
  • IECC climate zone: 4C-5B

Washington licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Washington State Department of Labor & Industries — Contractor Registration (General or Specialty) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening; WA L&I Contractor Registration required for all residential work; WA State Energy Code WSEC 2021 above-IECC-baseline R-value optional; Cascadia subduction zone seismic detailing mandatory on every coastal residential install

How garage door installation costs vary in Washington

Washington's combination of Cascadia subduction zone seismic exposure forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic-detailed garage-door header attachment on every coastal residential install from Cape Flattery to Long Beach, persistent Pacific salt-spray on every coastal exposure driving stainless-hardware-and-zinc-coated-track replacement at 5-7 year intervals, Pacific Northwest year-round humidity driving rapid corrosion on standard galvanized springs at 5-7 year intervals, and Puget Sound saltwater immersion on every Seattle, Tacoma, and Olympia waterfront residential install drives unique seismic-and-coastal-driven material selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Washington State Department of Labor & Industries §18.27 requires every Washington garage-door contractor to register with the WA L&I Contractor Registration program plus carry a $12,000 General Contractor or $6,000 Specialty Contractor surety bond — one of the most aggressive consumer-protection bond regimes in the country, with the WA L&I Construction Contractor Registration Section operating active enforcement against unregistered contractors statewide, plus WA State Energy Code (WSEC, WAC 51-11C) separately encourages above-IECC-2021-baseline R-value on garage doors as part of optional Stretch Code adoption, and Cascadia subduction zone seismic-detailed header attachment mandatory on every coastal residential install.
