Attic Insulation Upgrade Cost in Washington (2026)

Attic insulation upgrades in Washington typically run $2,200–$8,400 for a 1,500 sq ft attic upgrade R-30 to R-49 blown cellulose, with blown cellulose loose-fill the dominant residential retrofit material at the IECC R-60 attic minimum. Washington's Puget Sound combination of 5,300 annual heating degree days, persistent Pacific Northwest mildew growing conditions, and Cascade Range wood-substrate moisture cycling drives heating-dominant insulation upgrades statewide — R-60 blown-cellulose attic plus R-21 wall cavity insulation plus continuous polyiso exterior on retrofits is now the dominant Washington spec, with Washington Coast salt-spray homes additionally requiring closed-cell spray foam at the roofline plus marine-grade fastener replacement on every coastal Washington retrofit.

Washington energy code & climate-load drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 4C-5B-6B
  • Required attic R-value: R-60 (IECC 2021 prescriptive minimum)
  • Required wall R-value: R-21 (above-grade wood-frame)
  • Adopted energy code basis: State-specific code (deviates from IECC baseline — typically more restrictive)
  • Stretch code available: Yes — opt-in or mandatory above-baseline pathway
  • Annual heating + cooling load: 5,300 HDD / 300 CDD
  • Vapor barrier requirement: Warm-side vapor retarder required (typical IECC zones 5+)
  • Dominant attic insulation material: Blown cellulose loose-fill — dominant in heating-dominant Northeast and Upper Midwest
  • IRA HOMES + HEER rebate status: Live — IRA HOMES + HEER rebates currently available to residential homeowners

Washington licensing, rebates & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Washington State Department of Labor & Industries — Contractor Registration (official site)
  • Permit: permit required for attic insulation retrofit per Washington State Energy Code (WSEC); L&I Contractor Registration required statewide regardless of contract value

How attic insulation upgrade costs vary in Washington

Washington's Puget Sound combination of 5,300 annual heating degree days, persistent Pacific Northwest mildew growing conditions, and Cascade Range wood-substrate moisture cycling drives heating-dominant insulation upgrades statewide — R-60 blown-cellulose attic plus R-21 wall cavity insulation plus continuous polyiso exterior on retrofits is now the dominant Washington spec, with Washington Coast salt-spray homes additionally requiring closed-cell spray foam at the roofline plus marine-grade fastener replacement on every coastal Washington retrofit.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Washington RCW §19.27A imposes the Washington State Energy Code (WSEC) the most aggressive statewide residential energy code in the Pacific Northwest — Washington requires state-specific R-60 attic + R-21 wall + mandatory blower-door testing on every new residential construction, plus the Washington State Building Code Council adopted the 2024 WSEC requiring electric-ready hot water heating + electric-ready space heating on every new residential construction, and the Washington State Energy Office administers IRA HOMES + HEER funding layered with Puget Sound Energy, Seattle City Light, and Tacoma Power utility weatherization rebates.
