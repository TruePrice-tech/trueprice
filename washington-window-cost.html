Window Replacement Cost in Washington (2026)

Window replacements in Washington typically run $6,400–$16,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Northern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.3, no SHGC limit). Washington's combination of Cascadia subduction zone seismic exposure forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic-detailed window header attachment on every coastal residential install from Cape Flattery to Long Beach, persistent Pacific salt-spray on every coastal exposure driving aluminum-clad-wood frame replacement at 5-7 year intervals, Pacific Northwest year-round humidity driving rapid sealant degradation on every window perimeter caulk at 4-6 year intervals, and WA State Energy Code WSEC 2021 above-IECC-baseline U-0.27 mandate drives unique seismic-and-WSEC-driven glazing selection.

Washington climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Northern — coldest climates, U-factor priority (U ≤ 0.27 in coldest tier)
  • IECC climate zone: 4C-5B
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.3 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: No limit (cold climate)
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 110 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Moderate — selected metro historic districts with review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): Moderate — 20-40% pre-1978 housing share, EPA RRP required on selected replacements

Washington licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Washington State Department of Labor & Industries — Contractor Registration (General or Specialty) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification; WA L&I Contractor Registration required for all residential work; WA State Energy Code WSEC 2021 above-IECC-baseline U-factor mandatory at U-0.27 maximum window U-factor; Cascadia subduction zone seismic detailing mandatory on every coastal residential install

How window replacement costs vary in Washington

Washington's combination of Cascadia subduction zone seismic exposure forcing ASCE 7-22 SDC D1 seismic-detailed window header attachment on every coastal residential install from Cape Flattery to Long Beach, persistent Pacific salt-spray on every coastal exposure driving aluminum-clad-wood frame replacement at 5-7 year intervals, Pacific Northwest year-round humidity driving rapid sealant degradation on every window perimeter caulk at 4-6 year intervals, and WA State Energy Code WSEC 2021 above-IECC-baseline U-0.27 mandate drives unique seismic-and-WSEC-driven glazing selection.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Washington State Department of Labor & Industries §18.27 requires every Washington window contractor to register with the WA L&I Contractor Registration program plus carry a $12,000 General Contractor or $6,000 Specialty Contractor surety bond — one of the most aggressive consumer-protection bond regimes in the country, plus WA State Energy Code (WSEC, WAC 51-11C) separately mandates above-IECC-2021-baseline U-0.27 maximum window U-factor on every Washington residential installation — one of the lowest mandatory U-factor mandates outside Alaska, Minnesota, Montana, and Vermont, and Cascadia subduction zone seismic-detailed window header attachment mandatory on every coastal residential install.
