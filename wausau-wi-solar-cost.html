Questions Wausau homeowners ask about solar

Do solar panels work on cloudy days?

Yes. Panels produce 10-25% of their rated output on overcast days. Annual production matters more than any single day. Systems are sized to account for regional cloud cover patterns.

Do solar panels increase home value?

Owned solar systems add roughly $15,000-$20,000 to home value according to national studies. Leased systems do not add value and can complicate the sale process.

How long does solar take to pay for itself in Wausau?

Payback in Wausau depends on electricity rates, sun hours, and incentives. Most systems pay back in 6-10 years and then generate free electricity for another 15-20 years.

What is the difference between string inverters and microinverters?

String inverters are cheaper but the whole array drops output when one panel is shaded. Microinverters optimize each panel independently, cost 15-20% more, and last longer.

What drives cost. Labor costs in Wausau track within a few points of the national average for solar installation, so material selection and project scope are the bigger pricing levers for homeowners. Red flags to watch for. Any Wausau contractor who asks for more than 30% upfront before materials are ordered is a red flag.
