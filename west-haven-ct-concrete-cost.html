Questions West Haven homeowners ask about concrete

Is concrete or asphalt better for a driveway?

Concrete lasts 30+ years vs. 15-20 for asphalt. Concrete costs more upfront ($6-$12/sqft vs. $3-$6/sqft) but needs less maintenance. Asphalt needs resealing every 3-5 years and is softer in extreme heat.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in West Haven?

In West Haven, permits are typically required for driveways, sidewalks, and any concrete work near property lines or public right-of-way. Backyard patios may be exempt depending on size. Check local requirements.

What is stamped concrete?

Stamped concrete uses patterned mats pressed into wet concrete to mimic brick, stone, slate, or wood. It costs $12-$25/sqft installed. It requires resealing every 2-3 years to maintain appearance and prevent wear.

More questions about concrete in West Haven

What does a concrete driveway cost per square foot?

Plain 4-inch concrete runs $6-$12 per square foot installed in 2026. Stamped concrete adds $4-$8/sqft for pattern and color. Heavy-duty 6-inch driveways for RVs or trucks cost $9-$15/sqft. A 600 sq ft two-car driveway typically lands between $5,800 and $8,100.

How much does a 1,000 sq ft concrete patio cost?

Typical 2026 ranges for a 1,000 sq ft project: plain broom-finish $8,000-$12,000, exposed-aggregate $9,500-$14,500, stamped $11,000-$16,500. Tearing out an existing slab adds $1,500-$3,000. Reinforcing wire mesh and rebar are usually $0.50-$1.00/sqft and worth it on driveways, optional on patios.

What does a slab foundation cost in West Haven?

A monolithic slab foundation for a typical single-family house in West Haven runs $5,000-$15,000 depending on square footage and soil conditions. A garage slab is $2,500-$6,000. Full basement foundations are a different scope entirely and run $15,000-$30,000.

Do I need a permit for concrete work in West Haven?

Driveways, patios over a certain size, and any structural slab in West Haven require a permit and an inspection of the base prep and rebar before the pour. Sidewalks, repairs, and resurfacing usually do not. Your contractor pulls the permit — never pull it in your own name.

Why pricing varies in West Haven. Labor rates here run roughly 24% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. West Haven's site and climate factors. West Haven has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Air-entrained concrete mix (5-7% air content) is mandatory in West Haven for freeze-thaw resistance. Standard mixes without air entrainment scale and spall within 2-3 winters. Road salt tracking from driveways accelerates surface degradation — apply concrete sealer annually to driveways and walkways exposed to de-icing chemicals. Older properties in West Haven (averaging 53 years) often have settling concrete that affects drainage. Re-pouring may require sub-grade correction (compaction, drainage tile) that new construction doesn't need. Budget 10-20% extra for site preparation on established properties.

West Haven paperwork, inspections, and licensing. Most concrete work projects in West Haven require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Who's doing the work in West Haven. With 55,046 people, West Haven's contractor pool is mid-sized. You will find 4-6 established shops for most trades, which provides enough competition for fair pricing. West Haven's measured growth has built a reliable contractor ecosystem. Lead times are manageable and pricing competition is healthy.
