Questions West Palm Beach homeowners ask about window replacement

How much does window replacement cost in West Palm Beach?

Window replacement in West Palm Beach costs $400-$1,200 per window installed, depending on size, frame material, glass type, and accessibility. A whole-house project (10-15 windows) runs $5,000-$15,000.

What is the difference between full-frame and insert replacement?

Insert replacement fits a new window into the existing frame, is faster, and costs less. Full-frame replacement removes everything down to the studs, costs 30-50% more, but fixes hidden damage.

Should I replace window screens too?

New windows almost always include new screens. If reusing inserts, check screen mesh for holes and frame corners for separation. Replacement screens cost $25-$75 each.

West Palm Beach, FL: window replacement cost factors

Why pricing varies in West Palm Beach. Labor rates here track within a few points of the national average, so most of the price variation comes from material costs and local market dynamics rather than wage differentials. For window replacement specifically, West Palm Beach sits within a few points of the national midpoint on our cost model.

Local conditions. West Palm Beach has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Window performance in West Palm Beach is dominated by solar heat gain. Spec Low-E coatings tuned for low SHGC (under 0.30) — this matters more than U-factor in cooling-dominant climates. Impact-rated glass adds 25-40% to cost but is increasingly required by insurance carriers in storm-prone areas. Full-house window replacement (8-12 windows) in West Palm Beach runs $4,500-12,000 for vinyl frames and $8,000-20,000 for fiberglass or wood-clad. Frame material drives the price more than glass specs — vinyl is the most cost-effective for most homeowners, with fiberglass offering a 10-15% energy improvement at 40-60% higher cost.

Permits, codes, and licensing. Most window replacement projects in West Palm Beach require a permit; confirm with the local building department before signing.

Contractor market. With a population of 102,436, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. West Palm Beach has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.
