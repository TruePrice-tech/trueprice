Fence Installation Cost in West Virginia (2026)

Installing 150 linear feet of fencing in West Virginia typically runs $4,200–$11,000, with 4-to-6-foot galvanized chain-link the dominant residential choice. Concrete-set posts must clear the 24-inch design frost line. West Virginia's Allegheny Plateau and coal-country reclaimed mine subsidence creates unpredictable ground settlement that disrupts post-footing concrete annually on properties built over abandoned mine works — and Allegheny Plateau winter ice storms drive vinyl panel brittleness on exposed installations, with chain-link and engineered wood retaining dominant share over composite alternatives across rural Wyoming and McDowell county properties.

West Virginia climate & post-design drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 24-inch design minimum for post footings
  • Dominant residential fence material: Galvanized chain-link — dominant in rural and utility-yard applications
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate — 110–140 mph design wind drives 8-foot post spacing
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Moderate — periodic termite pressure
  • HOA fencing-rule prevalence: Low — limited HOA fencing restrictions outside metro subdivisions

West Virginia licensing, HOA & boundary law

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: West Virginia Division of Labor — Contractor Licensing Board (residential >$2,500) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county permit typical for residential fence over 6 feet

How fence installation costs vary in West Virginia

West Virginia's Allegheny Plateau and coal-country reclaimed mine subsidence creates unpredictable ground settlement that disrupts post-footing concrete annually on properties built over abandoned mine works — and Allegheny Plateau winter ice storms drive vinyl panel brittleness on exposed installations, with chain-link and engineered wood retaining dominant share over composite alternatives across rural Wyoming and McDowell county properties.

State-specific code or insurance rule: West Virginia Code §21-11 requires every contractor performing residential or commercial work above $2,500 to hold a Contractor License issued by the Division of Labor's Contractor Licensing Board — making West Virginia one of the lowest-threshold mandatory-licensing states for fencing work, with separate Residential, Commercial, Subcontractor, Roofing, and Specialty classifications and a mandatory $10,000 surety bond on every license tier.
