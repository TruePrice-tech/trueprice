Foundation Cost in West Virginia (2026)

New-construction foundation pours in West Virginia typically run $12,400–$29,400 for a full-house 2,000 sq ft new-construction foundation pour, with full basement pour as the dominant residential foundation type. Buried footings must clear the 30-inch design frost line. West Virginia's combination of abandoned-coal-mine subsidence risk across 22 coal-belt counties from McDowell to Marion driving WV Code §22-3 mandatory abandoned-mine subsidence disclosure on every residential property transfer in the affected counties, eastern panhandle karst limestone subsoil across Berkeley, Jefferson, Hampshire, Hardy, Grant, Mineral, and Pendleton counties driving karst-sinkhole disclosure-recommended on every residential property, statewide radon zone 1 designation forcing sub-slab depressurization rough-in on every residential basement pour, and 30-inch frost depth across the entire state drives aggressive subsidence-and-karst-driven foundation engineering.

West Virginia climate, soil, seismic & hydrologic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 30-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Dominant residential foundation type: Full basement — dominant in Upper Midwest, Northeast, and Mid-Atlantic on freeze-thaw + radon-zone-1 sites
  • Expansive soil risk: Low — sandy or stable subsoil, standard slab-on-grade design
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC B — minor seismic risk, standard tie-down requirements
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Water table risk: Moderate — water table below frost line in most lots
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • IECC climate zone: 5A
  • EPA radon zone: Zone 1 (highest) — passive sub-slab depressurization rough-in required on every basement and slab pour

West Virginia licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: West Virginia Division of Labor — Contractor License (general or specialty) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential foundation pour; WV Contractor License required for any residential contract; WV Karst-Sinkhole Disclosure recommended in eastern panhandle counties; abandoned-mine subsidence disclosure required across coal-belt counties; statewide radon zone 1 designation drives sub-slab depressurization rough-in

How foundation pour costs vary in West Virginia

West Virginia's combination of abandoned-coal-mine subsidence risk across 22 coal-belt counties from McDowell to Marion driving WV Code §22-3 mandatory abandoned-mine subsidence disclosure on every residential property transfer in the affected counties, eastern panhandle karst limestone subsoil across Berkeley, Jefferson, Hampshire, Hardy, Grant, Mineral, and Pendleton counties driving karst-sinkhole disclosure-recommended on every residential property, statewide radon zone 1 designation forcing sub-slab depressurization rough-in on every residential basement pour, and 30-inch frost depth across the entire state drives aggressive subsidence-and-karst-driven foundation engineering.
