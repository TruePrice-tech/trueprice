Gutter Installation Cost in West Virginia (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in West Virginia typically runs $1,000–$3,800, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 45 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. West Virginia's 45-inch annual rainfall combined with Allegheny Plateau elevation snow-load and dense mature canopy across the Monongahela and Allegheny National Forests drives near-universal gutter-guard retrofit demand on long-tenure homes — and reclaimed mine subsidence creates unpredictable ground settlement that disrupts downspout-discharge infiltration trenches on properties built over abandoned mine works.

West Virginia rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 45 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 25 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: Moderate — periodic ice-dam events on north-facing eaves
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: High — mature deciduous canopy drives gutter-guard demand and 2x annual cleaning frequency

West Virginia licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: West Virginia Division of Labor — Contractor Licensing Board (residential >$2,500) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction

How gutter installation costs vary in West Virginia

West Virginia's 45-inch annual rainfall combined with Allegheny Plateau elevation snow-load and dense mature canopy across the Monongahela and Allegheny National Forests drives near-universal gutter-guard retrofit demand on long-tenure homes — and reclaimed mine subsidence creates unpredictable ground settlement that disrupts downspout-discharge infiltration trenches on properties built over abandoned mine works.

State-specific code or insurance rule: West Virginia Code §21-11 requires every contractor performing residential or commercial work above $2,500 to hold a Contractor License issued by the Division of Labor's Contractor Licensing Board — making West Virginia one of the lowest-threshold mandatory-licensing states for gutter installation, with separate Residential, Commercial, Subcontractor, Roofing, and Specialty classifications and a mandatory $10,000 surety bond on every license tier.
