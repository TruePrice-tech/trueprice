Window Replacement Cost in West Virginia (2026)

Window replacements in West Virginia typically run $4,800–$12,400 for a 10-window 1,500 sq ft single-story full-house window replacement, with vinyl-frame double-hung the dominant residential choice and ENERGY STAR Northern climate-zone glazing requirements (U-factor ≤ 0.3, no SHGC limit). West Virginia climate, wind & energy-code drivers

  • ENERGY STAR climate zone: Northern — coldest climates, U-factor priority (U ≤ 0.27 in coldest tier)
  • IECC climate zone: 5A
  • Maximum U-factor: 0.3 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Maximum SHGC: No limit (cold climate)
  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 105 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane impact-rated requirement: No statewide impact-rated requirement
  • Dominant residential frame material: Vinyl frame — dominant in most climates, lowest cost-per-window
  • Historic-preservation overlay prevalence: Moderate — selected metro historic districts with review
  • EPA RRP lead-safe trigger (pre-1978 housing share): High — 40-60% pre-1978 housing share, EPA RRP required on most replacements

West Virginia licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: West Virginia Division of Labor — Contractor License (general or specialty) (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for window opening modification; WV Contractor License required for any residential contract; replacement-only same-size installs typically permit-exempt; abandoned-mine subsidence disclosure required across coal-belt counties

How window replacement costs vary in West Virginia
