Questions Weston homeowners ask about plumbing

How much does it cost to replace a water heater?

A standard 50-gallon tank water heater runs $1,200-$2,500 installed. Tankless units cost $2,500-$5,000 but last roughly twice as long and cut energy bills 20-30%.

What does a plumbing inspection include?

A thorough inspection covers supply lines, drain lines, water heater condition, fixture connections, shutoff valves, water pressure testing, and a visual check for leaks and corrosion.

How much does a plumber charge per hour in Weston?

Plumbers in Weston typically charge $75-$150 per hour. Emergency and after-hours calls add a $100-$200 trip fee on top of the hourly rate.

How often should I have my drains cleaned in Weston?

Annual drain maintenance is recommended for most Weston homes. Older homes with mature trees nearby may need semi-annual service to prevent root intrusion.

How do I protect my plumbing during a hurricane in Weston?

Plumbing protection during Weston hurricanes requires advance preparation. Test your main water shutoff valve annually so it works when needed. Close it before evacuating. After the storm, follow all boil-water advisories and inspect for sewer backflow if your area flooded. Installing a backflow preventer ($300-$800) is increasingly required by local code and protects against contaminated floodwater entering your home's pipes.

What should a plumbing quote include in Weston?

A professional plumbing quote in Weston should itemize: labor hours, material costs and brands, permit fees if applicable, cleanup, and warranty terms. The quote should include the plumber's license number and proof of insurance. Get three written quotes for any job over $500 to ensure fair pricing.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Weston?

Weston homeowners should target fall or winter for planned plumbing work. Demand drops after summer's peak water-heater season and before spring's outdoor plumbing projects, which translates to better availability and pricing. Emergency service runs 50-100% more than scheduled jobs, so proactive replacement of aging equipment is the financially smarter path.
