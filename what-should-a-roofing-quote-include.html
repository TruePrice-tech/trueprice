What should a roofing quote include

A strong roofing quote should explain exactly what the contractor will install, remove, protect, replace, and warranty. If those details are missing, the price alone is not enough to judge value.

The most important items in a roofing estimate

Roof size and measurement

The estimate should clearly show the roof area, number of squares, or both. Without measurements, pricing is hard to compare.

Material brand and product

The quote should name the actual shingle, metal panel, or tile product, not just say “new roof.”

Tear off details

It should state whether the old roof is removed, how many layers are being torn off, and whether disposal is included.

Underlayment

A good quote identifies the underlayment type and where leak barrier products will be installed.

Flashing and penetrations

Look for chimney flashing, wall flashing, pipe boots, drip edge, and other transition details.

Ventilation

The quote should mention ridge vent, intake, or any attic ventilation improvements if needed.

Cleanup

Cleanup, debris haul away, and magnet sweep should be explicit, not assumed.

Warranty

There should be separate information for workmanship warranty and manufacturer warranty.

Other items that should be clear

Permit responsibility

Who pulls the permit and whether permit fees are included should be obvious.

Wood replacement policy

If damaged decking is found, the quote should explain whether wood replacement is included or billed separately.

Payment schedule

A legitimate estimate should show deposit terms, progress payments if any, and final payment timing.

Project timeline

The contractor should explain expected start timing and how weather delays are handled.

What is often missing from weak quotes

Weak estimates often leave out flashing, ventilation, cleanup, disposal, starter materials, or warranty details. That is one reason low quotes can look attractive at first and then become more expensive later.
