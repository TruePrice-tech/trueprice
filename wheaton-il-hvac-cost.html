HVAC in Wheaton: what locals should know

🌡

Climate and your HVAC

Wheaton's cold winters make heating efficiency the top priority. A high-efficiency furnace (95%+ AFUE) can save $400-800/year compared to older 80% models.

⚙

System recommendation

In Wheaton, a high-efficiency gas furnace (95%+ AFUE) paired with a moderate-SEER AC (14-16) gives you the best value. Heating is your biggest expense, so prioritize furnace efficiency.

📅

Best time to buy

Schedule furnace replacements in Wheaton between August and October. Waiting until the first cold snap means competing with every other homeowner whose system just failed.

💡

Local tip

Wheaton homeowners should consider a programmable or smart thermostat with their new system. Setting back temperatures while away or sleeping can save 10-15% on heating bills.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new HVAC system cost in Wheaton?

Most homeowners in Wheaton pay between $9,138 and $26,220 for HVAC replacement, depending on system type and efficiency.

Should I replace my AC and furnace at the same time?

Replacing both at once is usually more cost-effective than separate replacements. Matched systems run more efficiently and most contractors offer bundle pricing.

What SEER rating should I choose?

14 SEER is the minimum federal requirement. 16 SEER offers a good balance of efficiency and cost. 18-20+ SEER provides the best energy savings but costs significantly more upfront.
