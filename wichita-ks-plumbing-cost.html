What Wichita residents ask about plumbing

How long do plumbing fixtures typically last?

Faucets last 15-20 years, toilets 25-50 years, water heaters 8-12 years (tank) or 15-20 years (tankless), and supply lines should be replaced every 20-25 years.

What is the difference between copper and PEX piping?

Copper lasts 50+ years, resists bacteria, and has proven durability. PEX costs 60% less, installs faster, resists freezing better, and requires fewer joints. Both are code-compliant.

Is trenchless sewer repair worth the extra cost?

Trenchless repair costs 20-40% more but avoids excavating your yard, driveway, or landscaping. It is faster and causes less property damage, often making it cheaper overall.

How much does it cost to replace a water heater?

A standard 50-gallon tank water heater runs $1,200-$2,500 installed. Tankless units cost $2,500-$5,000 but last roughly twice as long and cut energy bills 20-30%.

Does Wichita's hard water damage plumbing?

Wichita's hard water absolutely affects plumbing systems. Scale accumulation reduces water heater efficiency by 20-30%, narrows pipe openings over time, and wears out fixtures faster than normal. Installing a water softener ($1,500-$3,000) eliminates the source problem. Between that and annual water heater flushing, you can add years of life to your entire plumbing system.

What should a plumbing quote include in Wichita?

A Wichita plumbing estimate should detail: scope of work, material specifications, labor costs, permit fees if needed, waste disposal, and warranty. Check that the plumber's license number and insurance are included. Compare three written quotes for any project over $500; make sure the scope matches across all three.

When is the best time to schedule plumbing work in Wichita?

For non-emergency plumbing in Wichita, schedule during spring or fall, moderate temperatures and lower demand than summer peak. Emergency calls cost 50-100% more than scheduled work, so replacing aging water heaters, supply lines, and fixtures proactively during off-peak months saves significantly versus waiting for a failure.
