Plumbing in Wilmington: what locals should know

💧

Water quality

Wilmington's soft water means less mineral buildup in your plumbing system. You generally don't need a water softener, though a basic sediment filter can improve water quality and protect fixtures.

❄

Freeze risk

Wilmington occasionally sees freezing temperatures that can threaten exposed pipes. Basic insulation on outdoor spigots and crawlspace pipes is a smart, inexpensive precaution.

🔧

Material recommendation

Most Wilmington plumbers now recommend PEX for whole-house repiping. It's code-compliant, durable, and significantly cheaper than copper. The main exception: some homeowners prefer copper for its longevity and perceived quality.

💡

Local tip

Many Wilmington homes built in the 1970s-80s have polybutylene (gray plastic) water pipes, which are prone to unexpected failures. If your home has them, most plumbers recommend proactive repiping before a catastrophic leak.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a plumber cost in Wilmington?

Plumber rates in Wilmington typically range from $85-$150/hour. Total project costs depend on the scope — a simple drain cleaning may cost $200-$450 while a water heater replacement runs $1,500-$4,500.

Should I repair or replace my water heater?

If your water heater is over 10 years old or the repair cost exceeds 50% of replacement, replacing is usually the better investment. Tankless water heaters last 20+ years and can reduce energy costs.

Is PEX or copper better for repiping?

PEX is 30-40% cheaper than copper and faster to install. Copper lasts longer (50+ years vs 25-30 for PEX) and has higher resale value. Both are code-compliant in most areas.
