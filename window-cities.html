All cities with window replacement cost data

738 U.S. cities across 50 states · data updated 2026

Window replacement pricing is a per-opening number that swings on U-factor target (ENERGY STAR Most Efficient list shifts roughly every 18 months), frame material (vinyl vs fiberglass vs aluminum-clad wood), and historic-district context. Pacific Northwest IECC Zone 4-Marine pushes triple-pane more aggressively than the national average; Sun Belt buyers hit SHGC numbers harder than U-factor. Wood-frame historic homes in pre-1925 districts often need NPS-compatible wood-frame replacements that run 2–3x vinyl, plus a preservation-board review process that delays the install 6–12 weeks. New construction sets a different baseline — track-builder allowances per opening run $400–$700 vs $1,200–$2,500 for a quality retrofit. City pages below hold the U-factor target by climate zone, dominant frame material, and the historic-district exposure homeowners need to plan around.
