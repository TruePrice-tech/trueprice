Concrete Driveway Cost in Wisconsin (2026)

Concrete driveway pours in Wisconsin typically run $3,400–$8,800 for a 600 sq ft 4-inch concrete driveway pour at 4000 psi compressive strength. Buried footings must clear the 48-inch design frost line, and the state sits in most severe freeze-thaw exposure (ACI F3). Wisconsin's combination of 48-inch frost depth across the entire state, F3 (most severe) freeze-thaw exposure across the Upper Midwest, and Lake Michigan and Lake Superior lake-effect humidity drives 6%+ air-entrainment plus Type II cement on every Wisconsin residential pour — and Wisconsin road de-icing salt exposure on every grade-level driveway edge from Wisconsin Department of Transportation snow operations plus winter ice-melt salt application on every residential driveway forces 0.40 maximum water-cement ratio on every Wisconsin residential pour.

Wisconsin climate, soil & seismic drivers

  • Frost-line trench depth: 48-inch design minimum for buried footings
  • Freeze-thaw exposure (ACI 201.2R): F3 — most severe freeze-thaw + heavy de-icer use, 6-8% air-entrainment + 0.40 max w/c required
  • Typical residential mix: 4000 psi compressive strength
  • Expansive soil risk: Low — sandy or stable subsoil, standard slab-on-grade design
  • Sulfate exposure (ACI 318): S0 — no sulfate exposure, Type I/II cement adequate
  • Seismic design category (ASCE 7-22): SDC A — no seismic design considerations
  • Winter de-icer salt exposure: Very high — heavy de-icer use drives 0.40 max w/c + epoxy-coated rebar

Wisconsin licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Wisconsin Department of Safety & Professional Services — Dwelling Contractor Certification (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for residential driveway pour > 200 sq ft; WI Dwelling Contractor Certification required for any one- and two-family dwelling exterior contracting

How concrete driveway costs vary in Wisconsin

Wisconsin's combination of 48-inch frost depth across the entire state, F3 (most severe) freeze-thaw exposure across the Upper Midwest, and Lake Michigan and Lake Superior lake-effect humidity drives 6%+ air-entrainment plus Type II cement on every Wisconsin residential pour — and Wisconsin road de-icing salt exposure on every grade-level driveway edge from Wisconsin Department of Transportation snow operations plus winter ice-melt salt application on every residential driveway forces 0.40 maximum water-cement ratio on every Wisconsin residential pour.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Wisconsin Statute §101.654 requires every Wisconsin concrete contractor performing exterior coating work on one- or two-family dwellings to hold a Wisconsin Department of Safety & Professional Services Dwelling Contractor Certification — and Wisconsin Department of Transportation Specifications 501 enforces statewide aggregate-source certification on every concrete pour for ASR (alkali-silica reaction) and ACR (alkali-carbonate reaction) prevention given Wisconsin Driftless Area and Door County high-reactivity dolomitic aggregate deposits, with University of Wisconsin Construction Materials Lab providing mandatory aggregate testing on every residential foundation pour above 1,000 sq ft.
