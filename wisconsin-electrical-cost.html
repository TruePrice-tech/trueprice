Electrical & Panel Upgrade Cost in Wisconsin (2026)

Panel upgrades and rewires in Wisconsin typically run $2,400–$5,700 for a 200A service panel upgrade, with the 2020 NEC as the adopted enforcement basis. Service entrance design assumes a 200A minimum on new residential construction. Northern Wisconsin winter ambients across Vilas, Iron, and Bayfield counties sustain -30°F to -40°F readings that brittle PVC conduit fittings and force rigid metallic conduit on exterior service masts — and Lake Michigan and Lake Superior lake-effect freeze-thaw drives ground frost depth to 60 inches in the North Woods, requiring service-entrance conduit buried below the frost line.

Wisconsin code & environmental drivers

  • Adopted code basis: 2020 NEC — dwelling unit AFCI on most 120V branch circuits, GFCI expanded
  • State amendments: Wisconsin adopts 2020 NEC under SPS 316 (Wisconsin Electrical Code) with Department of Safety and Professional Services issuing Master, Journeyman, and Industrial Electrician credentials
  • Service entrance minimum: 200A for new residential construction
  • Lightning flash density: Moderate — typical interior continental flash density
  • Buried-conduit frost depth: 30-inch+ design depth — buried service conduit must clear hard frost line

Wisconsin licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Wisconsin Department of Safety and Professional Services — Electrical License (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal electrical permit required for panel upgrade and service-line work

How electrical and panel upgrade costs vary in Wisconsin

State-specific code or insurance rule: Wisconsin SPS 316 (the Wisconsin Electrical Code) makes substantial state-specific amendments to the NEC including different AFCI applicability rules and Master Electrician (residential and commercial scope), Journeyman (residential, industrial, and commercial scope), Industrial Journeyman, and Apprentice as separately tested credentials — and unique to Wisconsin, the Industrial Electrician credential is restricted to in-house facility work and prohibited from public contracting.
