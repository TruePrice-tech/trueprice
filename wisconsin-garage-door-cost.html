Garage Door Replacement Cost in Wisconsin (2026)

Garage door replacements in Wisconsin typically run $1,300–$4,600 for a 16x7 single-bay or 18x7 double-bay steel-insulated garage door + opener replacement, with steel-insulated panel the dominant residential choice and 105 mph ASCE 7-22 design wind speed driving DASMA wind-pressure rating selection. Wisconsin's combination of 48-inch frost depth across the entire state, F2-F3 freeze-thaw cycling driving aggressive sealant degradation on every garage-door bottom seal at 3-5 year intervals, persistent Lake Michigan and Great Lakes lake-effect humidity on every Door, Kewaunee, Manitowoc, Sheboygan, Ozaukee, Milwaukee, Racine, and Kenosha county exposure driving stainless-hardware replacement at 7-10 year intervals, and -30°F winter low temperatures driving R-16 voluntary insulation upgrade common on Wisconsin residential installs drives moderate cold-climate-and-Great-Lakes-driven material selection.

Wisconsin climate, wind & HOA drivers

  • ASCE 7-22 design wind speed: 105 mph (Risk Category II)
  • Hurricane wind tier: No hurricane wind-design exposure
  • Required garage-door R-value: R-13 (IECC 2021 + state amendments)
  • Dominant residential door material: Steel-insulated panel — dominant in most regions, polyurethane or polystyrene foam core
  • Dominant residential opener type: Belt-drive — dominant in attached-garage residential, quietest operation
  • Termite pressure (TPCT zone): Low — minimal termite pressure
  • HOA architectural-control prevalence: Moderate — suburban HOA prevalence on newer subdivisions
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: Moderate — bayfront salt-spray, stainless hardware recommended
  • IECC climate zone: 6A-7

Wisconsin licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Wisconsin Department of Safety & Professional Services (DSPS) — Dwelling Contractor Certification + Dwelling Contractor Qualifier (official site)
  • Permit: city or county building permit required for new garage-door opening; DSPS Dwelling Contractor Certification required for any 1-or-2-family new construction; replacement-only installs typically permit-exempt; WI Uniform Dwelling Code mandatory

How garage door installation costs vary in Wisconsin

State-specific code or insurance rule: Wisconsin Department of Safety & Professional Services (DSPS) §101.654 requires every Wisconsin garage-door contractor performing 1-or-2-family new-construction work to hold a Dwelling Contractor Certification plus a Dwelling Contractor Qualifier — making Wisconsin one of the few states with dual-credential mandatory licensing for residential garage-door work, with the DSPS Dwelling Contractor Examination requiring written demonstration on Wisconsin Uniform Dwelling Code (UDC) plus Wisconsin Energy Code SPS 363 garage-door insulation requirements.
