Gutter Installation Cost in Wisconsin (2026)

Whole-house gutter installation in Wisconsin typically runs $1,200–$4,400, with 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum the dominant residential profile and roughly 33 inches of annual precipitation driving sizing. Wisconsin's 35 psf ground snow load combined with Lake Michigan and Lake Superior lake-effect winter snow drives heavier-gauge aluminum K-style and steel-bracket spacing under 18 inches on Vilas, Iron, and Bayfield county residential installations — and ice-dam formation on every north-facing eave drives near-universal heat-cable retrofit with budgets averaging $700-1,200 per home in the Northwoods.

Wisconsin rainfall & drainage drivers

  • Annual rainfall: 33 inches/yr — drives K-style sizing and downspout count
  • Dominant residential gutter profile: 5-inch K-style seamless aluminum — dominant low-rainfall retrofit profile
  • Ground snow load: 35 psf — drives gutter bracket spacing and ice-shield requirements
  • Ice-dam risk tier: High — sustained sub-freezing winter requires ice-and-water shield + heat-cable retrofit budget
  • Hurricane wind tier: None
  • Tree-canopy density: High — mature deciduous canopy drives gutter-guard demand and 2x annual cleaning frequency

Wisconsin licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Wisconsin Department of Safety and Professional Services — Dwelling Contractor (DC) and Dwelling Contractor Qualifier (DCQ) (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal building permit typical for gutter installation on new construction

How gutter installation costs vary in Wisconsin

State-specific code or insurance rule: Wisconsin Statutes §101.654 requires every contractor performing residential exterior work on one- or two-family dwellings to hold both a Dwelling Contractor (DC) certification (the contracting business entity credential) AND a Dwelling Contractor Qualifier (DCQ) certification (the personal credential held by the responsible individual) — and unique to Wisconsin, the DCQ requires 12 hours of pre-certification continuing education and 12 hours per renewal cycle on Wisconsin energy-code, building-code, and consumer-protection topics.
