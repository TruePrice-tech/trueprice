Landscape Installation Cost in Wisconsin (2026)

Landscape installation in Wisconsin typically runs $4,200–$17,600 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with cool- or warm-season turf-dominant front-yard design as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 3b-5b. Wisconsin's combination of glacial-till loam soils across the Driftless Area and Door County, mature deciduous canopy across older Milwaukee and Madison neighborhoods, and 160-day growing season supports cool-season Kentucky bluegrass-perennial ryegrass blend turf as the dominant Wisconsin residential lawn — and Wisconsin's Lake Michigan and Lake Superior lake-effect snow loads on Milwaukee, Green Bay, and Superior landscapes drive snow-shoveling-corridor design considerations on every front-yard install plus salt-tolerant plant selection on grade-level beds adjacent to Wisconsin DOT snow-operations corridors.

Wisconsin climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 3b-5b — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 32 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 160 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Low — adequate residential precipitation, minimal drought stress
  • Irrigation dependency: Low — irrigation optional, established plants survive on rainfall
  • Frost-line trench depth: 48-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Turf-dominant — cool- or warm-season residential lawn as primary front-yard ground cover
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: No WUI defensible-space requirement

Wisconsin licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Wisconsin Department of Agriculture, Trade & Consumer Protection Pesticide Section + Wisconsin Department of Safety & Professional Services Dwelling Contractor (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; DATCP Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care; WI Dwelling Contractor Certification required for any one- and two-family dwelling exterior contracting

How landscape installation costs vary in Wisconsin

Wisconsin's combination of glacial-till loam soils across the Driftless Area and Door County, mature deciduous canopy across older Milwaukee and Madison neighborhoods, and 160-day growing season supports cool-season Kentucky bluegrass-perennial ryegrass blend turf as the dominant Wisconsin residential lawn — and Wisconsin's Lake Michigan and Lake Superior lake-effect snow loads on Milwaukee, Green Bay, and Superior landscapes drive snow-shoveling-corridor design considerations on every front-yard install plus salt-tolerant plant selection on grade-level beds adjacent to Wisconsin DOT snow-operations corridors.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Wisconsin Statutes §94.701 grants the Wisconsin Department of Agriculture, Trade & Consumer Protection exclusive preemption authority over residential and municipal pesticide ordinances — Wisconsin is one of the strongest pesticide-preemption states in the Upper Midwest, blocking Milwaukee, Madison, Green Bay, and Eau Claire from adopting local pesticide restrictions on residential landscape contractors, with DATCP Pesticide Section managing the only operational regulatory channel statewide and DATCP additionally maintaining the Wisconsin Sensitive-Crop Registry protecting commercial cranberry, apple, and beekeeping operations.
