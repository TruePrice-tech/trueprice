Exterior Painting Cost in Wisconsin (2026)

Repainting a 1,500 sq ft home in Wisconsin typically runs $2,800–$7,800, with vinyl panel (trim painting only on most homes) the dominant residential substrate driving prep scope. Wisconsin's 110+ annual freeze-thaw cycles and -25°F winter low temperatures across the entire state combined with Lake Michigan and Lake Superior lake-effect humidity drive aggressive wood-substrate cycling on every Milwaukee, Madison, Green Bay, and Eau Claire exterior — and Wisconsin road-salt chloride contamination on grade-level exterior wood-trim drives chloride-rinse prep on every grade-level wood-trim repaint statewide, with Driftless Area and Door County wood-lap residential stock requiring caulk-and-recaulk on every repaint.

Wisconsin climate & coatings drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 6A-7
  • Dominant residential substrate: Vinyl panel — paint scope reduced to wood trim, doors, and shutters on most homes
  • Annual humidity tier: Moderate — typical continental humidity, standard topcoat formulations adequate
  • UV exposure: Low — Pacific NW or high-latitude diffuse light, fade not the primary failure mode
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: No salt-air corrosion exposure (interior continental)
  • Freeze-thaw cycling: Very high — 100+ annual freeze-thaw cycles drive caulk-and-recaulk on every repaint
  • VOC architectural coatings rule: Federal default — 250 g/L flat exterior topcoat limit (EPA 40 CFR Part 59)

Wisconsin licensing, lead-safe & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Wisconsin Department of Safety & Professional Services — Dwelling Contractor Certification; WI DHS Lead Abatement Contractor for pre-1978 work (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for repaint; WI Dwelling Contractor Certification required for any one-and two-family dwelling construction; DHS Lead Abatement Contractor required for pre-1978 prep above 2 sq ft disturbed

How exterior repaint costs vary in Wisconsin

State-specific code or insurance rule: Wisconsin Statute §101.654 requires every Wisconsin painting contractor performing exterior coating work on one- or two-family dwellings to hold a Wisconsin Department of Safety & Professional Services Dwelling Contractor Certification — and the Wisconsin Department of Health Services Lead Abatement Contractor credential under Wisconsin Administrative Code DHS 163 separately requires lead-safe certification on every pre-1978 housing repaint that disturbs more than 2 square feet of painted surface, lowering the EPA RRP federal trigger threshold.
