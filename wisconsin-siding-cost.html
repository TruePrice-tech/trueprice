Siding Replacement Cost in Wisconsin (2026)

Re-siding a 1,500 sq ft home in Wisconsin typically runs $9,500–$25,500, with vinyl panel the dominant residential cladding choice. Northern Wisconsin winter ambients across Vilas, Iron, and Bayfield counties sustain -30°F to -40°F readings that drive vinyl panel brittleness on south-facing elevations — and Lake Michigan and Lake Superior lake-effect freeze-thaw cycling drive wood-cladding rot and rainscreen-gap requirements on every coastal re-side, with cedar lap and engineered wood retaining significant market share over composite alternatives in the Northwoods.

Wisconsin climate & cladding drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 5A-6A-7
  • Dominant residential cladding: Vinyl panel — dominant low-budget retrofit choice in non-WUI, non-coastal corridors
  • Hurricane wind tier: Moderate wind exposure — 110–140 mph design wind on coastal counties
  • Wildland-urban interface (WUI) cladding rule: No statewide WUI cladding mandate
  • Salt-air corrosion zone: Low salt-air corrosion — standard galvanized fasteners adequate

Wisconsin licensing & permits

  • License status: Statewide license required
  • License board: Wisconsin Department of Safety and Professional Services — Dwelling Contractor (DC) and Dwelling Contractor Qualifier (DCQ) (official site)
  • Permit: state or municipal building permit required for full re-side

How siding replacement costs vary in Wisconsin

State-specific code or insurance rule: Wisconsin Statutes §101.654 requires every contractor performing residential exterior work on one- or two-family dwellings to hold both a Dwelling Contractor (DC) certification (the contracting business entity credential) AND a Dwelling Contractor Qualifier (DCQ) certification (the personal credential held by the responsible individual) — and unique to Wisconsin, the DCQ requires 12 hours of pre-certification continuing education and 12 hours per renewal cycle on Wisconsin energy-code, building-code, and consumer-protection topics.
