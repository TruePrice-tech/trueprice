Woogoro vs HomeAdvisor: An Honest Comparison

By Geoff Lane, founder of Woogoro · Updated April 2026

HomeAdvisor and Angi are owned by the same parent company and operate the same lead-generation business. If you have used HomeAdvisor before, here is what Woogoro does differently and which tool fits your situation.

HomeAdvisor and Angi Are the Same Company

Worth stating up front: HomeAdvisor was acquired by Angi Inc. (formerly Angie's List) in 2017 and the two products have been progressively merged. Submitting a project on HomeAdvisor distributes the lead through the same contractor network as Angi. The site interfaces are different, but the underlying business model and pro database are now shared.

If you are looking for a true alternative, the choice is between the lead-generation marketplace model (Angi/HomeAdvisor/Thumbtack) and tools that don't sell your information at all.

What Each Site Does

HomeAdvisor is a contractor matching marketplace. You submit a project; HomeAdvisor sells your contact info to multiple contractors in their network; those contractors call, text, and email you to compete for the work.

Woogoro is a free quote analyzer. You upload a contractor quote you already have (or describe a project), and Woogoro checks the price against city-specific cost data, verifies the scope, and flags red flags. No contact info collected, no leads sold.

The Common Complaints About HomeAdvisor

The bulk of online complaints about HomeAdvisor and Angi center on the lead-distribution model:

  • Volume of follow-up. 5 to 10 contractor contacts within hours of submitting; sporadic follow-ups for weeks afterward.
  • Difficulty unsubscribing. Contractors who already paid for your lead continue to follow up even after you opt out of HomeAdvisor itself.
  • Paid placement. Featured contractors may have paid for top placement rather than earned it through quality.
  • Lead quality from contractors' side. Contractors complain about paying for leads that don't convert; this leads them to follow up more aggressively to recoup the cost.

None of this means HomeAdvisor is useless. It means the business model has costs to homeowners that other tools don't have.

Where HomeAdvisor Is Actually Useful

  • Pre-screened contractor verification. HomeAdvisor pre-checks contractor licensing in many states. If you don't want to verify yourself via state licensing boards, this is a real convenience.
  • Project guarantees. The Happiness Guarantee covers some projects (terms apply).
  • Unified booking and project management. For homeowners who want everything handled in one app.

Where Woogoro Is Better

  • Quote analysis. If you already have a quote, Woogoro tells you if the price is fair, the scope is complete, and what red flags to push back on. HomeAdvisor has no equivalent.
  • No contact info required. Use the analyzer 5 times in an hour without anyone knowing.
  • Better cost data. 739 cities with BLS wage data and BEA cost-of-living indices, vs. HomeAdvisor's national averages.
  • No upsell, no premium tier. Free is fully free.

The Bottom Line

HomeAdvisor (and Angi) is useful if you want a contractor list and accept the calls. Woogoro is better if you already have a quote and want to know if it is fair, or if you want to estimate a project before contacting anyone. Many homeowners use both: HomeAdvisor to find contractor candidates, Woogoro to analyze the quotes those contractors send.
