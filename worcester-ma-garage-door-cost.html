Frequently asked garage door questions

How long does a garage door last?

Steel garage doors last 20-30 years. Wood doors last 15-20 years with maintenance. Aluminum lasts 20+ years. Springs last 7-12 years (torsion) or 5-7 years (extension), depending on usage cycles.

How long does garage door installation take?

A standard single or double door replacement takes 3-5 hours. Custom doors or jobs requiring structural framing changes take 1-2 days. Most installations are completed in a single visit.

How much does a garage door replacement cost in Worcester?

A new garage door in Worcester costs $800-$4,000 for a standard door installed, or $2,500-$8,000+ for a premium insulated or carriage-style door with a new opener.

What R-value should my garage door have?

R-8 is fine if the garage is detached or unheated. R-12 to R-16 is best for attached garages or if you use the space for a workshop. R-18+ is for extreme cold climates or heated garages.

garage door replacement pricing explained: Worcester, MA

Why pricing varies in Worcester. Labor rates here run roughly 33% above the national average per BLS metro data, which is the single biggest driver of how this city's pricing differs from the national midpoint. For garage door replacement specifically, our cost-multiplier model puts Worcester at roughly 15% above the national midpoint.

Local conditions. Worcester has local climate conditions that affect project scheduling and material choices. Insulated garage doors (R-12 minimum, R-18+ preferred) make a measurable difference in Worcester's winters, especially for attached garages with living space above. Uninsulated doors allow cold air infiltration that increases heating costs and can freeze exposed plumbing in the garage. A standard 2-car garage door in Worcester costs $800-2,500 for the door and $300-600 for professional installation. Opener replacement adds $250-500. The biggest pricing lever is material: stamped steel ($800-1,200), insulated steel ($1,200-1,800), wood composite ($1,800-3,000), and real wood ($2,500-5,000+).

Contractor market. With a population of 182,544, the local market has enough contractors to drive real competition on price, but you should still get three quotes. Worcester has seen steady growth, which means a healthy supply of established contractors with multi-year track records.
