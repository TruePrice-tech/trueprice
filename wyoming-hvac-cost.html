HVAC Replacement Cost in Wyoming (2026)

HVAC replacement in Wyoming typically runs $8,400–$18,800 for a complete 3-ton system replacement, with natural-gas furnaces with central AC as the dominant heating choice and roughly 8,200 heating degree days against 400 cooling degree days driving the seasonal load mix. Cheyenne (6,000 ft), Casper (5,150 ft), and Jackson (6,200 ft) sit at high elevation where combustion-equipment derate exceeds 10% and -20°F winter design temps make natural-gas or LP furnaces the only practical primary heat — heat pumps remain niche and require cold-climate inverter compressors paired with significant electric resistance or fossil-fuel backup.

Wyoming climate & load drivers

  • IECC climate zone: 6B-7B
  • Annual load split: Heating-dominant — annual heating load exceeds cooling load (8,200 HDD / 400 CDD)
  • Dominant heating fuel: Natural gas — piped utility supply through state pipeline network
  • Dominant cooling system: Central air conditioning paired with separate heating equipment

Wyoming licensing & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: Wyoming — no statewide HVAC license; Cheyenne, Casper, Laramie, Jackson require municipal mechanical contractor registration (official site)
  • Permit: municipal mechanical permit required

How HVAC replacement costs vary in Wyoming

State-specific code or insurance rule: Wyoming has no statewide HVAC contractor license — the Department of Workforce Services does not credential mechanical work — and municipal models vary widely: Cheyenne requires a separately-tested Master Mechanical exam, while Jackson and Casper accept any registered general contractor for mechanical work, creating one of the loosest licensure environments in the Mountain West.
