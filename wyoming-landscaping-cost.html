Landscape Installation Cost in Wyoming (2026)

Landscape installation in Wyoming typically runs $4,000–$17,000 for a front-and-back-yard residential landscape install, with xeriscape with drought-tolerant perennials and gravel mulch as the dominant residential approach across USDA hardiness zone 3a-6a. Wyoming's high-altitude combination of 130-day growing season in interior climate zones 3a-5a, the highest-elevation residential development in the continental U.S. across Jackson Hole and Cody at 6,000+ ft elevation, and 13-inch annual rainfall supports xeriscape, native shortgrass-prairie, and short-grass perennial palettes as the dominant Wyoming residential landscape aesthetic — and chinook wind events plus Bighorn Basin and Powder River Basin oil-and-gas patch fugitive dust contamination measurably stress freshly-installed shade-tree saplings and ornamental flower beds, with native sagebrush and Great Basin grasses dominating premium Wyoming residential installs.

Wyoming climate, water & plant-palette drivers

  • USDA hardiness zone: 3a-6a — drives plant-palette selection and frost-survival ratings
  • Annual rainfall: 13 inches/yr — drives irrigation system sizing
  • Growing season: 130 frost-free days per year
  • Drought tier: Severe — recurring drought drives turf-replacement rebate programs
  • Irrigation dependency: High — 40-60% of residential outdoor water for irrigation
  • Frost-line trench depth: 48-inch design minimum for irrigation backflow + bed prep
  • Dominant residential aesthetic: Xeriscape — drought-tolerant perennials with gravel or decomposed-granite mulch
  • Wildfire defensible-space rule: Advisory — state forestry recommends defensible-space cleanup in mapped fire zones

Wyoming licensing, pesticide & permits

  • License status: No statewide trade license
  • License board: No statewide landscape license; Wyoming Department of Agriculture Technical Services Pesticide Program (official site)
  • Permit: no permit required for landscape install; WDA Commercial Pesticide Applicator required for any chemical lawn care; municipal contractor registration required in Cheyenne and Jackson

How landscape installation costs vary in Wyoming

Wyoming's high-altitude combination of 130-day growing season in interior climate zones 3a-5a, the highest-elevation residential development in the continental U.S. across Jackson Hole and Cody at 6,000+ ft elevation, and 13-inch annual rainfall supports xeriscape, native shortgrass-prairie, and short-grass perennial palettes as the dominant Wyoming residential landscape aesthetic — and chinook wind events plus Bighorn Basin and Powder River Basin oil-and-gas patch fugitive dust contamination measurably stress freshly-installed shade-tree saplings and ornamental flower beds, with native sagebrush and Great Basin grasses dominating premium Wyoming residential installs.

State-specific code or insurance rule: Wyoming Statute §35-7 makes the Wyoming Department of Agriculture the residential pesticide applicator licensor and grants WDA preemption authority over every municipal pesticide ordinance — Wyoming is one of the strongest pesticide-preemption states in the Mountain West, blocking Cheyenne, Casper, Jackson, and Laramie from adopting local pesticide restrictions on residential landscape contractors, while WDA additionally operates the Wyoming Noxious Weed and Pest Control Program providing cost-share for residential property owners managing leafy spurge, knapweed, and Russian olive infestations.
