Questions Yonkers homeowners ask about fencing

How tall can I build my fence?

Most municipalities allow 6 feet in backyards and 3-4 feet in front yards without a variance. Some HOAs restrict fence height further. Always check local zoning before building.

What is the cheapest fence material?

Chain link is the cheapest at $10-$20 per linear foot installed. Pressure-treated pine is the cheapest wood option at $15-$25/ft. Vinyl and composite start at $20-$30/ft but never need painting.

What is the best fence for privacy?

A 6-foot solid board (dog-ear, board-on-board, or shadowbox) fence provides the most privacy. Board-on-board is best since it has no gaps even when boards shrink. Vinyl and composite also offer full privacy panels.

How deep should fence posts be in Yonkers?

Yonkers's freeze-thaw cycling will push shallow fence posts out of the ground within a couple of winters. Set posts 36-48 inches deep, at least 6 inches past the frost line. Bottom the hole with 8-10 inches of compacted gravel for drainage, then fill with concrete to grade. There is no shortcut on depth in this climate; it is the single most important factor in long-term fence stability.

Do I need a permit to build a fence in Yonkers?

In Yonkers, fence permits are standard for installations over 6 feet and sometimes required for any fence. Three things to handle before installation: call 811 for utility marking, confirm property boundaries (budget $300-$600 for a survey if needed), and review any HOA rules. The fence contractor should manage the permitting as part of the project scope.

How much does fence installation cost per foot in Yonkers?

Fence installation in Yonkers typically costs $15-$30 per linear foot for wood privacy, $20-$40 for vinyl, $10-$20 for chain link, and $25-$55 for aluminum or wrought iron. A 150 linear foot backyard fence runs $2,250-$6,000 depending on material. Getting 3 quotes with matching scope ensures fair pricing.
